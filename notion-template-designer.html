The Solution

A Complete Design Business System in Notion

FreelanceOS Pro connects every part of your freelance design business — from the first client brief to the final invoice — in one searchable, linkable Notion workspace.

Project Brief Tracker

A structured brief format capturing deliverables, scope, timeline, revision rounds included, target audience, brand guidelines, and client approval checkpoints. Build a library of briefs per client so repeat work takes minutes to set up, not hours.

Client Mood Board Links

Design Revision Log

Track every round of feedback with version number, date, specific changes requested, and your delivery confirmation. When a client claims you didn't implement their feedback — or adds requests on round five — you have a complete paper trail tied to the project record.

Asset Library Index

Catalogue every font, icon set, color palette, stock image, and brand kit with tags, source links, license type, and license expiry. Never lose a purchased asset again. Filter by type, client, or availability for commercial use in seconds.

Feedback Collector

A structured format for capturing client feedback that goes beyond "make it pop." Each feedback entry logs what was said, what it means in design terms, and how it was resolved. Useful for pattern-spotting across clients over time.

Invoice Tracker

Every invoice logged with client, project, amount, send date, due date, and payment status. Rollup formulas give you monthly revenue, outstanding balances, and revenue by client — your financial picture at a glance without a separate accounting tool.

Portfolio Pipeline

A database of all completed work tagged by style, industry, deliverable type, and whether it's portfolio-ready. When updating your site or putting together a pitch, filter to find exactly the projects that fit the context. Stop sifting through old hard drives.

Style Guide Storage

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Designers

Can I track design revision rounds inside Notion?

Yes. The Design Revision Log tracks every round of client feedback with date, version number, specific changes requested, and your response. Linked to the project record, so you have a complete revision history for every project — useful for scope creep disputes and portfolio notes.

How do I store client mood boards and references in this template?

Each project record in the Client Brief database has a dedicated section for mood board links, reference URLs, and uploaded inspiration images. You can embed Figma files, Pinterest boards, or any web link directly into Notion so your references are always in context.

Does the template help me manage my design portfolio pipeline?

Yes. The Portfolio Pipeline is a database of completed and in-progress projects tagged by style, industry, and type. When you're updating your website or applying for a job, you can filter to find exactly the projects that fit what you're showcasing.

Is there an asset library for storing design files?

Yes. The Asset Library Index lets you catalogue fonts, icon sets, color palettes, stock images, and brand kits with tags, source links, and license info. Store the metadata in Notion and link to the actual files wherever they live — Drive, Dropbox, or locally.

Can I store client style guides in Notion?

Yes. Each client record has a Style Guide section for brand colors (with hex values), typography, logo usage rules, do's and don'ts, and tone of voice notes. When you start a new project for a returning client, everything is there without hunting through old emails.

Does the template include invoice tracking?

Yes. The Invoice Tracker logs every invoice with client, project, amount, due date, and payment status. Rollup formulas calculate your monthly and annual revenue, outstanding totals, and revenue per client — so you have a real financial picture without a separate accounting app.

Is this template suitable for UX/UI designers or just graphic designers?

It works for both. The project brief and feedback structures are tool-agnostic — whether you're working in Figma, Illustrator, or Canva, the template adapts. UX designers can track user research links, wireframe iterations, and handoff notes in the same project record as brand designers track files and rounds.
