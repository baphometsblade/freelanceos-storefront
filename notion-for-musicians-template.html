Every original song you've ever written, in one place. The Music Catalog is the backbone of the entire system — every other database links back to it, from setlists to income from sync licensing deals.

Track every song from first demo to commercial release with status fields: Demo, Recorded, Released, or Shelved. Practice Log

The musicians who improve fastest aren't necessarily the ones practicing the most — they're the ones practicing with intention and tracking what they're working on. The Practice Log makes every session count.

Log each practice session with the date, total duration, and focus area: technique, repertoire, new songs, or improvisation. Write what you actually worked on, note the wins (that chord change finally feels natural), and flag the struggles (still stumbling on the bridge of that new tune). Come back next session with a specific starting point instead of just noodling.

A built-in streak tracker formula shows your current streak and longest streak, giving you the accountability loop that keeps consistency going. Over time, your Practice Log becomes a record of your growth — and on hard days when nothing feels right, you can scroll back and see how far you've come.

Venue & Promoter CRM

Relationships are the engine of a live music career. The Venue & Promoter CRM keeps every important contact organized so you can nurture relationships strategically — and never let a warm lead go cold because you forgot to follow up.

Track every venue and industry contact with fields for venue name, capacity, genre fit, booker contact info, last contacted date, last performed date, typical fee range, relationship status (Cold / Warm / Regular / Headliner), and notes. Add the details that matter: the booker who likes to be emailed on Tuesdays, the venue that's perfect for your acoustic set but too loud for your originals, the promoter who said "check back in the fall."

Filter by relationship status to see who you should be reaching out to this week, sort by last contacted to identify cold contacts worth re-engaging, and build the venue network that turns a gigging musician into a working musician with a full calendar.

Music Income Tracker

A musician's income rarely comes from one place — and that's actually a strength, as long as you can see the full picture. The Music Income Tracker separates every revenue stream so you know exactly what's working and what to grow.

Track income by category: gig pay, streaming royalties (Spotify, Apple Music, etc.), sync licensing placements, music teaching income, and merchandise sales. On the expense side, separate out instrument purchases, recording studio costs, touring and travel expenses, and marketing spend. Monthly rollup formulas calculate your net music income automatically.

When tax season arrives, you'll have a complete record ready — not a frantic search through bank statements. More importantly, you'll know whether that sync licensing hustle is actually worth your time, which venues pay fairly, and how much your music is really earning you.
