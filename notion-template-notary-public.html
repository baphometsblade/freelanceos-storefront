Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Notaries and Loan Signing Agents

How does the signing appointment log work for notaries?

The signing appointment log tracks every signing with date, signing type (mortgage, refinance, HELOC, reverse mortgage, general notarial, estate, POA, I-9, apostille, vehicle title), document source and signing service, location, duration, fee, payment status, and whether corrections were required. Filter by signing type to understand your revenue mix, by signing service to track payment patterns, or by payment status to follow up on outstanding invoices. The log captures your business-level signing record — your state's notary journal requirements for individual notarial act documentation should be managed separately per your jurisdiction's requirements.

How do I track signing services and title companies?

The signing service tracker stores every client with payment terms, average fee per signing type, total signings assigned, on-time payment rate, package quality rating, platform used, and outstanding balance. Not all signing services are equally valuable — some pay in 2 weeks, others take 60 days; some send complete, well-organized packages, others send disorganized packages that add time to every signing. Tracking payment terms and package quality by service over time gives you the data to prioritize your best relationships, identify which services to reduce dependence on, and negotiate better terms when you have a demonstrated track record with a high-volume client.

How does the RON platform tracker work?

The RON platform tracker records every platform you are certified on — states you are certified in, certification date, annual renewal requirement, platform fee structure, number of RON signings completed, average fee, and tech requirements status. RON certifications are state-specific and platform-specific — tracking them in one place prevents certification lapses that would disqualify you from the RON assignments you have invested time to establish. The performance tracking also makes your RON revenue per platform visible, so you can prioritize the platforms and states where you are generating the most volume and highest average fees.

How do I track mileage and expenses for tax purposes?

The mileage and expense log tracks every trip with date, signing location, round-trip miles, and calculated deduction at the current IRS standard mileage rate — plus recurring notary business expenses: print costs per package (laser toner and paper are significant recurring expenses for active notaries), platform fees, NNA membership and certification renewal costs, E&O insurance premium, and supply expenses. Consistent expense logging throughout the year makes Schedule C preparation straightforward rather than a year-end reconstruction. Most active notaries significantly underreport deductible expenses when they rely on year-end bank statement review instead of in-year tracking.
