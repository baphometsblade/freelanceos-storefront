The Problem

Every Writer Knows This Frustrating Feeling

You have the story inside you. But between the scattered notes, the forgotten character details, the plot holes you only notice 40,000 words in, and the total absence of a system — finishing feels impossible. Here's what's really slowing you down.

🕳️

No System to Track Plot Holes and Story Arcs

You introduced a subplot in Chapter 4 that completely contradicts your Act 3 twist. Now you're rereading 200 pages just to find where things went wrong. Without a structured chapter outline tied to your plot beats, continuity errors multiply silently — and you only discover them in the painful revision stage.

📋

Character Details Scattered Everywhere

What colour were Elena's eyes in Chapter 2? Does Marcus limp on his left or right leg? These details are somewhere — in a notebook, a Google Doc, a sticky note, your memory. Character inconsistency is one of the top complaints in book reviews, and it happens to even experienced authors without a centralised character bible.

📉

Missing Daily Word Count Targets

You wrote 2,400 words on Monday and nothing for the next six days. Your momentum shattered, your streak broke, and now opening the document feels like returning to a crime scene. Without daily tracking and a visible streak, it's too easy to let "just one missed day" become three weeks of avoidance.

🗺️

No Roadmap from First Draft to Published

Writing the first draft is only 30% of the journey. You still need beta readers, developmental editing, line editing, copyediting, a query letter, agent submissions, cover design, ISBN registration, and a launch plan. Without a publishing pipeline to guide you through these stages, most finished drafts never become books.

What's Inside

8 Powerful Modules — Everything an Author Needs

From the first spark of an idea to the moment your book goes live, every stage of the writing and publishing journey has a dedicated workspace. No more context-switching between apps. Your entire authorial life lives in one Notion workspace.

Module 01
📊

Manuscript Dashboard

Your Author's Command Center. The Manuscript Dashboard is the first thing you see every writing session — a high-level view of your book's vital statistics and progress. Set your project title, genre, target word count, and writing deadline, then let the dashboard do the math.

  • Book title, genre, target audience, and elevator pitch fields
  • Auto-calculated word count progress with visual progress bar
  • Deadline tracker showing days remaining and daily word target
  • Current writing streak counter with milestone badges
  • Quick-access links to every other module in the system
  • Project status tags: Drafting, Revising, Querying, Publishing
Module 02
📖

Chapter Outline Builder

Structure your story before you write a single scene. The Chapter Outline Builder lets you map your entire manuscript from beginning to end — chapter by chapter, scene by scene — so you always know where your story is going and what each chapter needs to accomplish.

  • Chapter-by-chapter database with number, title, and POV character
  • Scene cards within each chapter with purpose, tension level, and conflict notes
  • Plot point tags: Inciting Incident, Rising Action, Midpoint, Climax, Resolution
  • Status tracking: To Do, Outlined, Drafted, Revised, Final
  • Chapter word count targets and actual counts logged automatically
  • Story arc connectors to link scenes across chapters for subplot tracking
Module 03
👤

Character Bible

Never forget a character detail again. The Character Bible is a comprehensive database of every person in your story — protagonists, antagonists, supporting cast, and even minor characters who appear in a single scene. Reference it mid-chapter to stay consistent and deepen your characterisation.

  • Full character sheets with physical description, age, occupation, and backstory
  • Character arc tracker from story introduction to final transformation
  • Relationship map showing how characters connect to each other
  • Voice and speech pattern notes for authentic dialogue
  • Motivation, internal conflict, and fatal flaw fields
  • Character role tags: Protagonist, Antagonist, Mentor, Foil, Love Interest
Module 04
🗺️

World-Building Database

Whether you're writing high fantasy, near-future sci-fi, or a contemporary thriller, your fictional world needs rules — and those rules need to be consistent. The World-Building Database stores every detail of your story's setting so you can reference it instantly without breaking your flow.

  • Location entries with physical descriptions, atmosphere notes, and sensory details
  • World rules: magic systems, technology limits, political structures, economies
  • Historical timeline with key events that happened before Chapter 1
  • Culture and society notes: traditions, religion, class systems, taboos
  • Glossary of invented terms, names, and concepts unique to your world
  • Map upload support and visual reference links for world visualisation
Module 05
✏️

Daily Writing Log

Consistency beats inspiration every single time. The Daily Writing Log transforms your writing practice into a measurable, trackable habit. Log each session, watch your cumulative word count grow, and protect the writing streak that keeps you showing up day after day.

  • Per-session log: date, session start/end time, word count written
  • Cumulative word count automatically totalled across all sessions
  • Daily streak tracker with visual calendar-style view
  • Session mood and focus rating (1-5) to identify your best writing conditions
  • Monthly word count summary for NaNoWriMo or challenge tracking
  • Personal best session display and average daily words calculation
Module 06
🔍

Research & Reference Hub

Great books are grounded in truth — even fantasy novels. The Research Hub is where you store everything you've learned, read, watched, or discovered in service of your manuscript. Every note, source, and image stays organised and searchable so you can find what you need in seconds.

  • Categorised research notes: History, Science, Location, Culture, Medical, Legal
  • Source tracking: book titles, URLs, interview notes, and access dates
  • Inspiration board with image embeds, quotes, and reference screenshots
  • Chapter linkage: attach research notes directly to the chapters that use them
  • To-Research queue for questions that arise while drafting
  • Reading list tracker for books and articles informing your project
Module 07
📬

Query Tracker

The query process is notoriously brutal — hundreds of submissions, long silences, and form rejections. The Query Tracker transforms the chaos into a system. Every agent query you send is logged, tracked, and organised so you always know the status of your manuscript's journey toward representation.

  • Agent database: name, agency, email, genre specialties, and submission guidelines
  • Submission log: query letter version, materials sent, submission date
  • Response tracking: Pending, Request for Pages, Full Request, Rejection, Offer
  • Response time calculator to identify agent response patterns
  • Query letter version control — store multiple versions with notes
  • Simultaneous submission tracking and exclusivity period management
Module 08
🚀

Publishing Pipeline

Whether you're pursuing traditional publishing or self-publishing, the Publishing Pipeline gives you a clear, stage-by-stage roadmap from completed manuscript to book in readers' hands. Stop guessing what comes next — every task is mapped, sequenced, and checklist-ready.

  • Editorial stages: Developmental Edit, Line Edit, Copyedit, Proofread
  • Beta reader management: reader names, timeline, feedback received, action taken
  • Cover design tracker: brief, designer hired, revisions, final approval
  • Self-publishing checklist: ISBN, formatting, interior design, metadata
  • Launch plan with tasks for pre-launch, launch week, and post-launch
  • Marketing assets tracker: ARC list, press kit, author bio, social media content

FAQ

Questions Authors Ask Before They Buy

We've answered every question that comes up in the author community. If yours isn't here, the answer is almost certainly "yes — here's how it works."

Does this Notion book writing template work for both fiction and non-fiction? +
Absolutely. The template is designed to flex across genres and formats. Fiction writers use the Chapter Outline Builder, Character Bible, and World-Building Database to manage plot arcs, character relationships, and settings. Non-fiction writers use the same chapter structure for argument mapping, the Research Hub for source organisation, and the Publishing Pipeline for proposal writing, agent querying, and launch planning. Memoir writers, essayists, business book authors, and self-help writers all use this system effectively. Both fiction and non-fiction tracks share the Manuscript Dashboard and Daily Writing Log — so whether you're writing a fantasy trilogy or a guide to personal finance, the system adapts to what you need.
Can I manage multiple books in the same Notion workspace? +
Yes. The template is architected so each book is its own project container with its own Manuscript Dashboard, Chapter Outline, Character Bible, and Writing Log. You can duplicate the book project structure for each new title and switch between them from a master Author Hub view. Series writers can link character and world-building entries across books so your recurring characters and settings stay consistent across multiple volumes without duplication. Whether you're writing a standalone novel, a trilogy, or managing a backlist of ten books, the system scales with you.
Does this replace Scrivener? +
For most writers, yes — and it adds features Scrivener doesn't have. The FreelanceOS Notion book template includes everything Scrivener offers: chapter structure, character sheets, and research storage. But it also includes a built-in Query Tracker for literary agents, a Publishing Pipeline with beta reader management, daily writing session logs with streak tracking, and a collaboration layer for co-authors and writing partners. Notion also runs natively on every device — web, desktop app, iOS, Android — with real-time sync. No $99 license required. The one thing Scrivener does that Notion doesn't is compile manuscript formatting for submission — but most authors use a separate tool (Vellum, Atticus) for that final step anyway.
Is this Notion template good for NaNoWriMo? +
It was built for NaNoWriMo-style writing sprints. The Daily Writing Log tracks your session word counts, cumulative total, daily targets, and visual streak. The Manuscript Dashboard shows your real-time progress against your 50,000-word goal. You can set your November 30th deadline and the template calculates your required daily word count to hit the target. Dozens of NaNoWriMo participants have used this system to hit their targets — including several who went on to revise and commercially publish their NaNo novels. Even if you don't participate in the official NaNoWriMo event, the daily discipline structure the template builds is exactly the kind of writing habit that finishes books.
Can I collaborate with a co-author in this template? +
Yes. Since the template lives in Notion, you can share it with a co-author via Notion's native sharing. Both writers can see the same Chapter Outline, edit the Character Bible simultaneously, log individual writing sessions, and leave comments on any page. You can assign chapter ownership using Notion's person property so it's always clear who is drafting which section. The template includes Collaboration Notes sections in each chapter card for handoff notes between writing partners. Many writing duos who co-author fiction and non-fiction books use this exact system to stay synchronised across time zones.
Does the template include a query tracker for literary agents? +
Yes — the Query Tracker is one of the most detailed modules in the system. It stores each literary agent's name, agency, submission guidelines, query letter version sent, materials requested (synopsis, sample pages, full manuscript), submission date, response date, response type (offer, rejection, request for more), and your personal notes. You can filter by response status, sort by submission date, and see at a glance which agents currently have your manuscript. It also tracks simultaneous submission status and exclusivity periods. It removes all the spreadsheet juggling that querying authors dread — and it's used by writers who have gone on to sign with major literary agencies.
