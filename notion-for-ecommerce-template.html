Sound familiar?

Running a store without a system feels like this

Small ecommerce operators don't lack hustle — they lack a single place to see everything. Here's what that actually costs you.

📤
Inventory and supplier contacts scattered everywhere

Your supplier's WhatsApp is in one app, their MOQ is in a spreadsheet, your inventory count is in Shopify, and your reorder notes are on a sticky note. The first stockout you miss is not a surprise — it's a system failure. Every order you can't fulfill because a supplier changed their lead time and you didn't notice costs you real revenue and ranking.

🗓️
No system to track product launches and campaigns

Product launches, seasonal campaigns, Black Friday prep, and new listing rollouts are all in your head or scattered across email threads. You end up launching reactively instead of proactively, miss the window for peak demand, and repeat the same frantic "did we prep the ads?" conversation every quarter. A structured launch calendar changes that permanently.

💬
Customer feedback lost in review tabs

Your Amazon reviews, Etsy messages, Shopify support tickets, and email complaints are siloed across five platforms. You can't see that 30% of your candle returns mention "weak scent throw" because those three-star reviews are buried in notifications. Surfacing patterns from customer feedback is how great operators improve products — doing it manually at scale is almost impossible.

📊
Profitability per product unknown without digging

Shopify shows you revenue. Amazon shows you units. But neither tells you that after your COGS, platform fees, ad spend, and return rate, your best-selling product is actually your worst-margin product. Without a profit tracker that accounts for every cost category, you're scaling the wrong products. The stores that grow profitably are the ones that know their real numbers every month.

What's inside

8 modules that run your entire store

Every database is linked. Click a supplier and see every product they supply. Click a product and see its launch history, revenue data, and customer feedback — all connected.

Module 01
📦

Product Catalog

Your master product database — every SKU, variant, and listing in one place. Know your margins, track your inventory, and see which products are active, under review, or retired without opening five different apps.

  • SKU, product name, description, and variant tracking (size, color, material)
  • COGS, selling price, platform fees, and auto-calculated gross margin %
  • Linked supplier field — click any product to see its vendor record
  • Current inventory level with reorder trigger threshold
  • Product status: Testing / Active / Low Stock / Retired
  • Sales channel tags: Shopify, Amazon, Etsy, WooCommerce, own site
  • Photography checklist and listing copy status per product
Module 02
🏭

Supplier & Vendor Hub

Every manufacturer, print partner, dropship supplier, and logistics vendor in one database. Know your lead times before you need them. Spot reliability issues before they become stockouts. Reorder at the right moment, every time.

  • Supplier name, company, contact person, email, WhatsApp, and timezone
  • Average lead time (days) and fastest lead time (rush orders)
  • Minimum order quantity (MOQ) and typical order quantity
  • Payment terms: net-30, upfront, 50/50, letter of credit
  • Reliability rating (1–5 stars) with notes on past issues
  • Active reorder triggers — linked to product catalog inventory levels
  • Linked products field — see every SKU this supplier provides
Module 03
🚀

Launch & Campaign Planner

Map every product launch, seasonal sale, and ad campaign from concept to live. Stop launching reactively. Build a 90-day runway of promotions, coordinate creative assets and inventory, and execute every campaign with a repeatable checklist.

  • Campaign type: Product Launch / Seasonal Sale / Platform Ad / Email Blast / Influencer
  • Target platform, launch date, and campaign end date
  • Linked products field — which SKUs are featured in this campaign
  • Pre-launch checklist: inventory confirmed, listing optimized, ads scheduled, email drafted
  • Budget field: total spend allocated vs. actual spend
  • Post-campaign notes: revenue generated, ROAS, what to repeat or avoid
  • Calendar view and Kanban board (Planning → In Progress → Live → Complete)
Module 04
💰

Revenue & Profit Tracker

Your monthly financial picture — not just revenue, but the real number after every cost. See net profit per product, per channel, and per month. Know which products deserve more inventory investment and which ones you're subsidizing with your margins on others.

  • Monthly revenue entries by product or by sales channel
  • COGS, platform fees (seller fees, fulfillment, transaction %), ad spend, and returns
  • Net profit calculated automatically per product per month
  • Gross margin % and net margin % tracked side by side
  • Year-to-date revenue and profit rollup formula
  • Month-over-month comparison: revenue growth, margin trend, return rate
  • Linked to Product Catalog — click any monthly entry to see which products drove it
Module 05
💬

Customer Feedback Database

Centralize reviews, returns, support tickets, and product improvement ideas from every channel into one searchable database. Stop losing insights to notification feeds. Surface patterns that let you improve products before a small problem becomes a reputation issue.

  • Feedback source: Amazon review, Etsy message, Shopify ticket, email, social comment
  • Linked product field — attach every piece of feedback to its product
  • Sentiment tag: Positive / Neutral / Negative / Return / Refund Request
  • Issue category: Product quality, Shipping, Packaging, Description mismatch, Other
  • Product improvement notes — flag recurring complaints for supplier or listing action
  • Trending complaints view — filter by product to spot patterns fast
  • Response status: No Action Needed / Replied / Refund Issued / Product Updated
Module 06
✍️

Content & SEO Hub

Manage every product description, listing copy block, keyword target, and A/B test note in one place. Great listings aren't written once — they're optimized over time. This module is the creative layer that makes your products findable and compelling across every platform.

  • Product description drafts, bullet points, and platform-specific listing copy
  • Primary keyword, secondary keywords, and long-tail targets per product
  • A/B test notes: title variants, image order, bullet copy — with results logged
  • Photography checklist: hero shot, lifestyle, detail, size reference, infographic
  • Copy review status: Draft / In Review / Live / Needs Update
  • SEO score notes and competitor listing analysis
  • Linked to Product Catalog — every copy entry connects to its product record
Module 07
📅

Operations Calendar

Never miss a restock deadline, a platform policy change, or a holiday shipping cutoff again. The Operations Calendar is the heartbeat of your store — every recurring task, external deadline, and business milestone in one timeline view so you're always operating ahead of the curve.

  • Restock dates: calculated from current inventory, reorder trigger, and supplier lead time
  • Shipping deadlines: platform carrier cutoffs for peak seasons (BFCM, Christmas, Prime Day)
  • Holiday prep timeline: 8, 4, and 2 weeks out tasks for Q4
  • Platform policy and fee change dates: Amazon, Etsy, Shopify annual schedule
  • Task types: Reorder / Deadline / Launch Prep / Review / Admin / Supplier Follow-up
  • Linked to Supplier Hub — restock tasks auto-reference supplier lead times
  • Weekly and monthly calendar views plus a simple Kanban board
Module 08
🎯

Business Goals & KPIs

Set your monthly and quarterly targets and track them against reality. Ambitious ecommerce operators who write down their goals hit them at significantly higher rates than those who keep them mental. This module makes goal-tracking a 10-minute monthly ritual instead of a quarterly scramble.

  • Monthly revenue target vs. actual — per channel and total
  • Conversion rate goal vs. actual (store-level and per listing)
  • Average order value (AOV) target with monthly tracking
  • Return rate benchmark — set acceptable % and flag months that exceed it
  • Net margin target per product category vs. actual monthly margin
  • New product launch goals: number of new SKUs per quarter
  • Quarterly retrospective template: wins, misses, key lessons, and Q+1 priorities

Common questions

Everything you want to know before buying

Real answers, not vague feature lists.

Does this Notion ecommerce template work for both physical and digital products?
Yes — the product catalog and modules work equally well for physical goods (inventory-tracked products with suppliers and shipping) and digital products (ebooks, courses, presets, downloads with no inventory). Physical products use the inventory level, restock trigger, and supplier fields. Digital products use the listing copy, keyword targets, and A/B test fields in the Content and SEO Hub. You can run mixed stores — selling both physical and digital — with the same workspace. Each product record includes a Product Type field (Physical / Digital / Subscription) that filters views so you're only seeing what's relevant to that category at any time.
Does this template replace Shopify, WooCommerce, or my ecommerce platform?
No — this template is your business operations layer that sits alongside your ecommerce platform, not instead of it. Shopify and WooCommerce handle the storefront, checkout, and order processing. This Notion workspace handles everything those platforms are not designed for: knowing which products are actually profitable after COGS and ad spend, tracking supplier lead times before you run out of stock, planning campaign launches weeks in advance, recording customer feedback from reviews in one searchable place, and reviewing your real net margin per product every month. Think of it as the strategic command center your store has always been missing — not a replacement for your tech stack, but the brain that ties it together.
Is this Notion ecommerce template good for Amazon FBA sellers?
Absolutely. The template was designed with FBA sellers in mind. The Product Catalog includes fields for ASIN, COGS, Amazon selling price, FBA fees (estimated), and net margin so you see true profitability. The Supplier and Vendor Hub tracks your manufacturer contacts, lead times, and MOQ so you can plan purchase orders well ahead of your reorder triggers. The Revenue and Profit Tracker lets you log monthly revenue, platform fees, ad spend (PPC budget), and net profit per ASIN. The Operations Calendar flags critical FBA deadlines including holiday inventory cutoffs and carrier deadline changes. Several Amazon FBA sellers report that the supplier lead-time tracking alone prevented multiple stockouts that would have tanked their search ranking.
Can I use this template to manage multiple online stores?
Yes — the template is built to support multiple stores or sales channels from one workspace. Each product record includes a Sales Channel field (Shopify, Amazon, Etsy, WooCommerce, own site, Gumroad, etc.) and each revenue entry tracks the channel as well. You can filter your product catalog to show only Amazon ASINs, only Etsy listings, or cross-channel products. The Business Goals and KPIs module lets you set revenue targets per channel so you can track each store separately. Several sellers use one workspace to manage an Etsy shop, a Shopify store, and an Amazon listing for the same product line — with complete visibility across all three from a single dashboard.
Does this Notion ecommerce template have a profit calculator?
Yes — the Revenue and Profit Tracker module includes a margin calculation system built directly into the database. For each product you enter your selling price, COGS (cost of goods sold), estimated platform fees, and average ad spend per unit sold. A formula property calculates your gross margin percentage and net margin per unit automatically. The monthly revenue entries include total revenue, total COGS, platform fees, ad spend, and net profit so your real monthly profitability is visible at a glance. The Business Goals module lets you set target margin percentages by product category so you can immediately see when a product drops below your acceptable threshold — before a margin problem becomes a cash flow problem.
Is this Notion ecommerce template good for dropshippers?
Yes — dropshippers get particular value from the Supplier and Vendor Hub (tracking supplier reliability, lead times, and average fulfillment speed to identify quality partners), the Product Catalog (COGS from supplier, retail price, and margin calculated automatically so you know before you scale which products have real margins), the Launch and Campaign Planner (coordinating new product test launches across paid ads and organic content), and the Customer Feedback Database (tracking return and refund reasons by product to identify supplier quality issues before they compound). If you are testing many products quickly, the Product Status field (Testing / Active / Retired) lets you track which products are in validation versus scaling versus being cut — and the linked revenue data shows you exactly why.
