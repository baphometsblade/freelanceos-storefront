Turkey's Growing Freelance Market

Istanbul has emerged as a major regional tech and creative hub, with Turkish freelancers increasingly serving international clients across Europe, the Middle East, and North America.

Turkey's freelance market has grown significantly in recent years, particularly in Istanbul which has emerged as a regional tech and creative hub. Key freelance sectors include software development (Istanbul and Ankara), graphic design and UI/UX, content creation and copywriting in both English and Turkish, translation services, digital marketing, video production, and architectural design. Istanbul's tech district in Maslak and Ataşehir hosts major tech employers, while a growing number of Turkish developers work remotely for European and US companies.

The Turkish lira's volatility has made USD/EUR billing attractive for international-facing freelancers. Many Turkish professionals prefer to invoice foreign clients in hard currency, receiving payment via Payoneer or Wise, then converting to TL as needed. This creates unique accounting requirements: income from foreign sources must be declared in Turkish Lira at the TCMB (Central Bank of Turkey) exchange rate on the date of receipt.

Turkish freelancers working with foreign clients should note that income from foreign sources is generally taxable in Turkey as Turkish tax residents are taxed on worldwide income. The stopaj (withholding tax) mechanism applies when companies pay professional fees — the company withholds a percentage before payment. Gelir &Iddot;daresi Başkanlığı (G&Iddot;B) is Turkey's tax authority: vergi.gov.tr.

Platforms popular among Turkish freelancers include Bionluk.com (Turkey's leading domestic freelance marketplace), Upwork and Toptal for international clients, and LinkedIn for professional networking. Bionluk operates similarly to Fiverr and is ideal for connecting with Turkish clients paying in TL; international platforms offer access to higher-budget USD/EUR projects from global clients.

Popular digital payment platforms: Papara (Turkey's most popular digital wallet for domestic transactions), Garanti BBVA and &Iddot;ş Bankası for bank transfers, and Wise and Payoneer for international income. Many Turkish freelancers maintain a multi-currency Payoneer or Wise account for USD/EUR income and a Papara or bank account for TL spending.

On the tax side, self-employed professionals in Turkey must register with their local vergi dairesi (tax office), obtain a vergi numarası (tax ID), and decide whether to operate under Basit Usul (simplified method) or the general tax regime. SGK (Sosyal Güvenlik Kurumu) social security contributions are compulsory for registered self-employed individuals. Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from Turkish freelancers about taxes, platforms, and getting started with FreelanceOS Pro.

Do Turkish freelancers pay taxes on international income?

Yes. Turkish tax residents are taxed on their worldwide income under the Income Tax Law (Gelir Vergisi Kanunu). Income earned from foreign clients — in USD, EUR, or other currencies — must be declared in Turkish Lira at the TCMB exchange rate on the date of receipt. Consult a Mali Müşavir for advice specific to your situation. This is general information only and not professional tax advice.

What is the serbest meslek makbuzu?

The serbest meslek makbuzu (professional service receipt) is the official invoice document used by freelance professionals (serbest meslek erbabı) in Turkey. It must be issued for each professional service rendered and includes: the professional's name and vergi numarası (tax ID), client details, service description, gross amount, stopaj (withholding tax) amount, and net amount payable. The FreelanceOS Pro template includes a complete tracker for logging all issued serbest meslek makbuzu.

What is Basit Usul and who qualifies?

Basit Usul (Simple Tax Method) is a simplified income tax regime for sole traders with low annual turnover. Eligible taxpayers declare total annual income and pay tax on the net profit without the obligation to issue a formal receipt for every transaction. Qualification thresholds are set annually by G&Iddot;B (Gelir &Iddot;daresi Başkanlığı) and depend on business type and location. Consult a Mali Müşavir to confirm your eligibility.

Do Turkish freelancers need to pay KDV (VAT)?

KDV (Katma Değer Vergisi) is Turkey's Value Added Tax. The standard rate is 20% (increased from 18% in 2024) for most services. Many small freelancers below registration thresholds may be exempt from KDV. Freelancers registered for KDV must file monthly KDV declarations (KDV Beyannamesi) with G&Iddot;B. Freelancers providing services to foreign clients may benefit from different rules on export of services. This template includes a KDV tracking module — consult a Mali Müşavir for registration advice.

What is Bionluk and how does it compare to Upwork?

Bionluk (bionluk.com) is Turkey's leading domestic freelance marketplace, operating a gig-based model similar to Fiverr. It connects Turkish freelancers with Turkish clients and pays in TL. Upwork is the dominant international platform for higher-budget USD/EUR projects. Many Turkish freelancers use both: Bionluk for local clients and Upwork or Fiverr for international income. The FreelanceOS Pro platform income dashboard tracks income from all platforms in one view.

How do Turkish freelancers receive international payments?

Popular international payment methods for Turkish freelancers include: Payoneer (widely used, multi-currency USD/EUR accounts), Wise (lower fees, real exchange rates), PayPal (available but with limited USD withdrawal options), and SWIFT bank transfers via Garanti BBVA, &Iddot;ş Bankası, or Yapı Kredi. Papara is Turkey's leading domestic digital wallet but mainly used for TL. Most serious international freelancers prefer Payoneer or Wise for hard currency income.
