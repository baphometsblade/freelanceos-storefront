Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a time-tracking app or a task manager?

Neither — and both. FreelanceOS Pro is a Notion workspace template, which means it combines task management, time-block planning, session logging, and business data in one place. It's not a replacement for a dedicated time-tracker like Toggl if you need billing-precise time entries, but it gives you the productivity visibility and task management that most freelancers actually need without paying for a second or third app.

How long does it take to set up the productivity system?

Most freelancers are up and running within an hour. You duplicate the template (one click), enter your current open projects and tasks, and configure your working-hours preferences. The weekly review and session tracker are ready to use immediately — no configuration needed. The included setup guide walks you through every step with screenshots.

Can I use this alongside existing tools like Toggl or Google Calendar?

Yes. FreelanceOS doesn't try to replace every tool in your stack. Many users run Toggl for billing-accurate time entries while using FreelanceOS for task planning, project management, and weekly reviews. Notion also supports Google Calendar embeds on paid plans, so you can view your schedule without switching tabs. The system is designed to be the hub, not a walled garden.

What if my productivity needs are different from the template defaults?

Notion is fully customizable, and so is FreelanceOS Pro. Every database, view, and property can be renamed, restructured, or extended. If you want to track energy levels differently, add a new task category, or build a custom dashboard view, you have complete control. The template gives you the best starting point — you make it yours from there. See also our Notion workspace setup guide for customization tips.

Does this work on mobile?

Yes. Notion's mobile app is fully functional and all FreelanceOS Pro databases and views work on iOS and Android. The daily planner and task views are particularly useful on mobile for quick task captures and status updates between meetings or client calls. The weekly review is best completed on desktop given its depth, but everything else works seamlessly on phone.
