The Solution

A Complete Recruiting Business OS in Notion

FreelanceOS Pro for Recruiters connects your candidate pipeline, job requisitions, interview scorecards, offer tracking, hiring manager CRM, sourcing analytics, and commission log — all in one relational Notion workspace built for the full operational reality of professional recruiting and HR.

Candidate Pipeline Kanban — Applied → Screened → Interview → Offer → Hired / Rejected

A full-lifecycle candidate tracking database with a kanban view organized by pipeline stage — Applied, Phone Screened, Hiring Manager Screen, First Interview, Second Interview, Final Round, Offer Extended, Hired, and Rejected or Withdrawn. Each candidate card holds their name, current title and employer, the specific job requisition they are being considered for, the sourcing channel where you found or received them, their resume link, current compensation, availability date, and all interview notes and scorecard results linked directly to their record. Filter views let you instantly see every candidate for a specific role, every candidate currently at the Offer stage across all open searches, or every candidate sourced from LinkedIn in the past thirty days — without rebuilding a query each time. Linked databases connect each candidate to their specific job requisition and to the interviewers who scored them, so navigating from a role to its full candidate pool takes a single click rather than a tab-switching expedition. Moving candidates through stages is as simple as dragging their card across the board, and the pipeline view gives you and your hiring managers a shared, real-time picture of where every search stands without a single status-update email.

Job Requisitions Database — Role, Client, Salary Range, Priority & Status

A master database of every open, on-hold, filled, and cancelled job requisition you manage, with properties for role title, department or client organization, hiring manager name, location and remote policy, salary or rate range, seniority level, date opened, target fill date, current status, and authorized headcount. The primary view is filtered to Active requisitions sorted by target fill date so the most time-sensitive searches are always at the top of your attention without manual sorting. Board views by priority — Critical, High, Medium, or Low — let you make intentional decisions about where to invest your sourcing hours when you have competing demands across concurrent searches. A gallery view of all open roles with salary range and client visible on each card helps you quickly identify roles that share qualification profiles, so a candidate who is not quite right for one search can immediately be evaluated for another without starting a new process from scratch. Each requisition links directly to its full candidate pool, so you navigate from the req to every candidate in that search in one click.

Interview Scorecard Templates — Competency Ratings, Notes & Hiring Decision Log

A standardised interview scorecard template that lives within each candidate record, scoring the candidate across competencies defined for the role — communication, technical or functional skills, culture alignment, problem-solving approach, leadership or collaboration style, and any role-specific criteria the hiring manager cares about most. Each competency receives a numerical rating from one to five and a free-text notes field where the interviewer records specific observations, examples given by the candidate, and direct quotes that support the rating. The scorecard closes with a structured hiring recommendation — Strong Yes, Yes, Leaning No Decision, No, or Strong No — accompanied by a brief written rationale the interviewer must complete before submitting. Standardised scorecards solve one of the most persistent problems in recruiting: hiring decisions made on memory and gut feel rather than documented, comparable evidence from every interview conducted. When multiple interviewers complete the same scorecard template for a candidate, you can compare their ratings side by side, identify where evaluators agreed and disagreed, and bring structured data to the hiring decision conversation instead of relying on whoever has the strongest opinion in the room.

Offer Tracking & Negotiation Log — Offer Details, Counteroffers & Acceptance Status

A dedicated offer management database tracking every offer extended across all your active searches, with properties for the candidate name, role and client, offer date, base salary or rate, bonus structure, equity or commission if applicable, benefits summary, offer expiration date, and current acceptance status — Pending, Negotiating, Accepted, Declined, or Withdrawn. The negotiation log section within each offer record captures the full back-and-forth: each counteroffer received, the client's response, your recommended approach as the recruiter facilitating the negotiation, and the timeline of key decision points with dates. Tracking offer negotiations in a structured log rather than email threads means you have the full context of where a negotiation stands the moment you pick up the phone for an update call — you know exactly what was offered, what was countered, what was conceded, and what the candidate said was their true priority. When an offer falls through or a candidate declines, the documented negotiation history also helps you advise future clients on competitive compensation benchmarks for similar roles based on real market data from your own placement history.

Client & Hiring Manager CRM — Contact History, Brief Notes & Relationship Score

A client relationship database tracking every hiring organization and individual hiring manager you work with, including company name, primary contact, secondary contacts, preferred communication cadence and channel, historical placement count, average time-to-fill for past searches, fee structure and payment terms, your subjective relationship health score, last contact date, and any specific preferences or sensitivities about how they like to work. Log every substantive interaction — calls, job brief meetings, candidate submissions, interview feedback sessions, offer discussions — as a timestamped note on the client record so the full relationship history is always one click away and you never walk into a client call unprepared. Connect each client record to their open and historical job requisitions so you can navigate from any client to their complete search history and see at a glance which roles you have filled for them, what those placements earned, and where their current open searches stand. Strong client relationships are the foundation of a sustainable recruiting practice, and having all of that context structured and accessible means you show up to every client conversation informed, prepared, and capable of a strategic discussion rather than a routine status update.

Onboarding Checklist Generator — Role-Specific Checklists & First-90-Days Plans

A template system for generating role-specific onboarding checklists and first-90-days plans that you can deliver to clients as a value-added service with every successful placement, or use internally to support HR teams who want to improve their new hire retention and ramp time. Each onboarding checklist template covers pre-start logistics — system access, equipment, workspace setup, and paperwork completion — Day 1 activities including introductions, orientation scheduling, and first-week goal setting, Week 1 milestones covering key stakeholder meetings, training modules, and role clarity conversations, and the 30-60-90 day milestone framework with suggested goals for the hiring manager to adapt for their specific role and team context. Providing clients with a structured onboarding framework after every placement positions you as a full-cycle recruiting partner rather than a transactional resume supplier — and placements that are well-onboarded are far less likely to fall off in the first 90 days, protecting your guarantee period, your placement record, and your long-term client relationship simultaneously.

Sourcing Channel Tracker — LinkedIn, Indeed, Referrals, Job Boards & Response Rates

A sourcing analytics database tracking every channel you use to find candidates — LinkedIn Recruiter, LinkedIn outreach, Indeed, Glassdoor, niche job boards, employee referral programs, professional associations, university career centers, direct sourcing from competitor companies, and your own network — with the number of candidates sourced per channel per role, the number who responded to outreach, the number who progressed past a phone screen, the number who reached the offer stage, and the number who were ultimately hired. Calculating response rates and progression rates by channel for each role type gives you the sourcing intelligence that most recruiters never develop because they never track it systematically across searches. You will quickly discover that LinkedIn outreach for senior engineering roles produces a 12% response rate and a 40% screen-to-offer rate from respondents, while referrals produce a 60% response rate and a 55% screen-to-offer rate — which tells you exactly where to invest your limited sourcing hours on the next similar search rather than repeating the same channel mix out of habit without evidence.

Placement Commission Tracker — Fees, Split Arrangements & Billing Milestones

A placement and commission tracking database with a record for every completed placement, linked to the relevant job requisition and candidate record so the full context of each placement is always one click away from your commission data. Each placement record captures the candidate placed, the client organization, the agreed placement fee structure — percentage of first-year compensation or flat fee — the gross fee amount, any split arrangement with a co-recruiter or colleague along with the agreed split percentage and each party's net amount, the invoice date, payment due date, actual payment received date, and payment status flagged as Invoiced, Partial Payment, Paid in Full, or Overdue. Formula properties automatically calculate your net commission after any splits and highlight overdue invoices so nothing falls past due without an active follow-up. A rollup view organized by month shows your total placements, total gross fees, total net commissions, and any outstanding receivables at a glance — giving you a real-time picture of your recruiting business performance without reconstructing your numbers from email threads and a side spreadsheet every time you want to understand where your income stands for the quarter.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Recruiters & HR Professionals

How do recruiters use Notion?

Recruiters use Notion as an all-in-one recruiting workspace that replaces scattered spreadsheets, sticky notes, and ad-hoc email folders. In practice, this means building a candidate database with a kanban view that shows every candidate's pipeline stage at a glance, a job requisitions database that tracks every open role across multiple clients or departments, a sourcing tracker that logs which channels produce the strongest candidates and at what response rates, and an interview management system with scorecard templates so every hiring decision is documented and defensible. Notion's relational databases allow you to connect candidates to specific roles and link job reqs to hiring managers, meaning your entire recruiting operation lives in one cohesive workspace rather than being spread across five disconnected tools. For independent recruiters and small agencies, Notion also handles the business side — commission tracking, client relationship notes, and billing milestones — that purpose-built ATS platforms typically ignore entirely, making it the most complete single workspace available for independent recruiting businesses.

Can Notion work as an ATS for small recruiting agencies?

Yes, with important caveats about scale. Notion works extremely well as a lightweight applicant tracking system for independent recruiters, boutique staffing agencies, and small in-house recruiting teams handling up to a few dozen open roles at a time. The candidate pipeline kanban, job requisitions database, and interview scorecard templates replicate the core functionality of entry-level ATS platforms without monthly per-seat fees that add up quickly as your team or client roster grows. What Notion does not do natively is automated job board posting, bulk email sequences to candidates, or compliance documentation workflows required by regulated industries — for those needs, a dedicated ATS like Greenhouse, Lever, or Workable is the right tool alongside Notion rather than instead of it. The sweet spot is using Notion as the operational hub for your recruiting business — pipeline visibility, candidate notes, sourcing analytics, client relationships, and commission tracking — while using a job board or email outreach tool separately for outreach volume at scale.

How do I track candidates in Notion?

The most effective approach is a Notion database with a kanban view organized by pipeline stage: Applied, Phone Screened, Hiring Manager Screen, First Interview, Second Interview, Final Round, Offer Extended, Hired, and Rejected. Each candidate record stores their name, current role and employer, the specific job requisition they applied to, the source channel where you found or received them, a resume link, current salary or rate, availability date, and all interview notes and scorecard results linked directly to their record. Filter views let you see all candidates for a specific role, all candidates at the Offer stage across all open roles, or all candidates sourced from a specific channel in the past 30 days — without rebuilding the query each time you need that view. Linked databases connect candidates directly to job requisitions and to the interviewers who scored them, so you can navigate from any role to its full candidate pool or from any candidate to their complete interview history in a single click rather than a tab-switching expedition through multiple files.

What is the best way to organise job requisitions in Notion?

Build a job requisitions database with properties for role title, department or client name, hiring manager, location and remote policy, salary range, job level, date opened, target fill date, current status — Active, On Hold, Filled, or Cancelled — and authorized headcount. Add a board or gallery view filtered to Active roles so you always have a live overview of your current workload without manual refreshing each morning. Connect each job req to its candidate pool using Notion relation properties so you can click any open role and immediately see every candidate in that pipeline in a single view. For agencies serving multiple clients, add a client field linked to a separate Clients database tracking each client relationship, their hiring preferences, fee terms, and historical placements. This structure gives you a real-time view of your total requisition load, the priority ranking across concurrent searches, and your progress toward fill dates — the overview that makes the difference between a reactive recruiter and a strategic one who can have a confident business conversation with any client at any time.

Can I use Notion to score interview candidates?

Yes. Build a Notion interview scorecard template as a structured page within each candidate record, scoring the candidate across competencies relevant to the role — communication, technical or functional skills, culture alignment, problem-solving approach, leadership or collaboration style, and any role-specific criteria the hiring manager has defined in the job brief. Each competency gets a numerical rating from one to five and a free-text notes field for specific observations, examples the candidate gave, and direct quotes that support the rating. The scorecard closes with a structured hiring recommendation — Strong Yes, Yes, Leaning No, No, or Strong No — with a brief written rationale required from every interviewer before the result is submitted. Standardised scorecards mean that when multiple interviewers evaluate the same candidate, their ratings can be compared side by side in the debrief rather than relying on whoever speaks most confidently in the room, leading to better, more equitable hiring decisions and a documented audit trail for every hire that protects both you and your client.

What Notion views work best for recruiting pipelines?

The kanban board view is the primary view for day-to-day pipeline management — columns represent pipeline stages and cards represent individual candidates, so you can drag candidates forward as they progress and immediately see the distribution of your pipeline across all open searches in one visual. The table view is better for filtering and sorting across large candidate pools: sorting by application date to find the oldest unreviewed candidates, filtering by role to produce a full candidate list for a hiring manager status update, or filtering by stage to build a quick pipeline report for a client. Gallery view works well for visually reviewing candidate profiles when a role requires a strong portfolio or personal brand component. Timeline view is useful for tracking target fill dates across all open requisitions and spotting which searches are at risk of missing their promised delivery window before you have a difficult client conversation. Most experienced Notion recruiters use a combination of all four views depending on the task: kanban for pipeline flow, table for reporting and cross-role analysis, gallery for profile review, and timeline for capacity planning and deadline management.

How do I manage client relationships as a recruiter in Notion?

Build a dedicated clients database tracking each hiring organization and individual hiring manager as a separate record with fields for company name, primary contact, secondary contacts, preferred communication cadence and channel, historical placement count, average time-to-fill for past searches, fee structure and payment terms, your relationship health score — a subjective rating of how strong and active the relationship currently is — last contact date, and any specific preferences or sensitivities about how they like to work or receive candidate submissions. Log every substantive interaction — job brief calls, candidate submissions, interview scheduling and feedback, offer negotiations, post-placement check-ins — as a timestamped note on the client record so the full relationship history is always one click away and you never walk into a client call without complete context. Connect each client record to their open and historical job requisitions so you can navigate from any client to their complete search history, see which roles you have filled for them, what those placements earned, and where their current open searches stand in your pipeline. Showing up to every client conversation with that full context — knowing their history, their preferences, their current pain points, and the status of their active searches — differentiates you fundamentally from transactional recruiters who treat every call as a fresh start.
