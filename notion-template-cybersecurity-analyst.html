Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Cybersecurity Professionals

How do cybersecurity consultants use Notion?

Cybersecurity consultants use Notion as their practice management hub — client engagements, findings logs (for authorized tests only), methodology checklists, certification tracking, and a business dashboard all in one workspace. FreelanceOS Pro is a business management tool, not a security testing platform. All testing must be performed only on systems for which you have explicit written authorization — this is non-negotiable and the template's authorization tracking is designed to enforce this discipline.

Can I track penetration testing engagements in Notion?

Yes, for authorized engagements only. The Client Engagement Tracker stores the engagement type (external pentest, internal pentest, web app pentest, mobile app security review, social engineering, red team, GRC audit, risk assessment), scope notes (general reference only — detailed scope lives in your secure engagement documentation), authorization status (written authorization received — critical field), start date, report due date, and status. Notion is for managing the business of the engagement, not for storing sensitive test data.

How does the OSCP / CISSP certification tracker work?

The Certification and CPE Tracker stores each certification (OSCP, CISSP, CEH, GPEN, GWAPT, CISM, CompTIA Security+/CySA+/CASP+, eJPT, PNPT, CRTP, and others) with fields for expiration date, CPE/CPD credits required for renewal, credits earned this period, renewal fee, and study resources. You can filter to see certifications expiring in the next 12 months and track CPE progress toward each renewal requirement.

Is the findings tracker secure for storing vulnerability data?

The Findings Tracker is designed for business tracking of authorized engagement findings — severity, category, affected system (general description only), status, and remediation confirmation. It is NOT a secure vault for sensitive vulnerability details, exploitation code, or client-specific technical data. Detailed technical findings belong in your secure engagement delivery system (encrypted reports, client-approved storage). Notion is your business management layer, not your sensitive data store.

Does this template work for GRC consultants, not just pentesters?

Yes. The Client Engagement Tracker includes GRC audit, security assessment, risk assessment, and security awareness training as engagement types alongside penetration testing. The Business Dashboard tracks revenue by engagement type so you can see whether your GRC consulting hours or your pentest projects generate better revenue per hour — useful for deciding where to specialize.

One-time purchase — $29, no recurring fees. Duplicate the template into your Notion workspace once and it's yours permanently, including all future updates.
