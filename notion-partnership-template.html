The BD Process

Managing a BD Process: From Prospect to Active Partner

Most partnership conversations fail not from strategic misalignment but from operational atrophy — nobody owned the next step. Here is how the template structures the process so deals do not die quietly.

Prospect
Identified, no contact yet
Exploring
Initial conversations, alignment check
LOI
Letter of intent or term sheet
Active
Signed, live, generating value
Paused
On hold, not dead
Ended
Completed or terminated
1

Identifying Strategic Partners and Warm Intro Paths

The pipeline's Source field tracks how you identified each prospect: warm intro, conference, inbound, or strategic outreach. After 12 months of data, you see which sourcing channels produce partnerships that actually reach Active stage.

2

First Conversation to Signed Agreement

Exploring to LOI is where most deals stall. Each pipeline card has a discovery checklist: alignment confirmed, exec sponsor identified, success metrics agreed, legal review initiated. When all boxes are checked, you move to LOI. When they are not, you know exactly what is missing.

3

Partner Launch and Ongoing Success Tracking

Moving to Active triggers a link to the Partner Enablement Hub for immediate onboarding. Ongoing tracking includes a monthly touchpoint log, quarterly review dates, and a health score that decays without interaction — an early warning for partnerships that are drifting.

Different Partner Types, Different Tracking Needs

Not all partners are the same. Type-specific views ensure you always see the metrics that matter for each relationship model.

Strategic Alliances

Long-term, executive-level relationships with complementary vendors or market leaders. Relationship-intensive and slow-moving but highest potential value.

  • Exec sponsor relationship health
  • Joint initiative pipeline
  • Quarterly strategic review cadence
  • Co-innovation project tracker

Technology Integrations

API and product integrations with complementary SaaS tools. Track technical requirements, integration health, and the co-sell pipeline these integrations generate.

  • API documentation status
  • Integration install base growth
  • Co-sell deal registration
  • Technical support escalation log

Resellers & VARs

Channel partners who resell your product to their customer base. Operationally intensive with deal registration, training, quota, and commission management.

  • MDF allocation and usage

Affiliates

Performance-based referral partners. Track unique links, commission rates, content performance, and payout status in purpose-built affiliate tracking views.

  • Unique tracking link log
  • Commission rate and structure

  • Payout history and status

Co-Marketing Partners

Partners with overlapping audiences for joint content, events, and webinars. Track campaign commitments, delivery status, and audience reach.

  • Campaign calendar and deadlines
  • MDF budget and spend
  • Audience reach and lead attribution
  • Campaign ROI by partner

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Notion Partnership and BD template.

How do I build a partnership pipeline in Notion?

Create a partnerships database with a Status select property: Prospect, Exploring, LOI, Active, Paused, Ended. Board view grouped by Status gives visual pipeline management. Add partnership type, primary contact, signed date, revenue attributed, and a relation to co-marketing campaigns. The FreelanceOS partnership template has this structure pre-built — duplicate and start adding partners immediately.

How do I track co-marketing commitments and make sure both sides deliver?

The Co-Marketing Campaign Tracker gives every campaign its own record with a status workflow and owner assignment. Each campaign links to the partner record. Add a "partner deliverables" section with checkbox completion tracking so you always know whether your partner has fulfilled their side — and you have documentation if they have not.

Can Notion handle different partnership types like affiliates and channel partners simultaneously?

Yes. The Partnership Type select covers strategic alliance, technology integration, reseller/VAR, affiliate, and co-marketing. Each type has dedicated filtered views with type-relevant metrics: commissions for affiliates, deal registration for resellers, install base for integrations. All in one database — not five separate systems.

How does revenue attribution to partners work in Notion?

Log every deal sourced or influenced by a partner: value, attribution type, close date. Rollup properties aggregate total pipeline sourced, influenced, and closed revenue. A quantified ROI score for every partner — essential for QBRs and budget justification.

Is this template suitable for a solo founder doing BD, or is it only for dedicated partnership teams?

Both. Solo founders: start with the Pipeline and Partner Directory for immediate visibility. Dedicated teams: activate all eight modules for full coverage. The template grows with you — use what you need today and expand as your program matures.
