Frequently Asked Questions

How is a Notion freelance business tracker different from a spreadsheet?

A spreadsheet stores data in flat rows and columns. A Notion database stores data in pages with rich properties, and crucially — multiple databases can be related to each other. This means your invoice knows which client it belongs to, and your client page shows all their invoices automatically via a rollup. Spreadsheets require you to maintain these connections manually, which breaks constantly. Notion's relational structure keeps everything consistent with zero manual syncing.

Can I use FreelanceOS Pro on mobile?

Yes. Notion has iOS and Android apps that are fully functional for database work. You can add a new client, update a project status, or check your dashboard from your phone. The template is designed for desktop-first use (most freelancers do admin from a laptop), but all core functionality is accessible on mobile without limitation.

I'm just starting out. Should I set this up now or wait until I have more clients?

Set it up now. The effort to migrate 15 existing clients into a new system later is much higher than entering your first 3 clients when you're getting started. The best freelancers in any discipline are organized from day one — it creates habits that compound. The rate calculator alone is worth using before you price your next proposal, regardless of how many clients you have.

Does this track time, or just projects?

FreelanceOS Pro tracks projects, not individual time entries. It's intentionally scoped to business-level tracking — clients, projects, invoices, and revenue — rather than minute-by-minute time logging. If you need time tracking, tools like Toggl or Harvest integrate well alongside FreelanceOS. Many freelancers use Toggl for time logging and FreelanceOS for everything else, and the two work independently without conflict.
