Staff Scheduling and Shift Notes Tracker

A planning and communication layer for your team schedule — who is working which shift, shift swap notes, callout tracking, and manager notes per service. Not a payroll or time-clock replacement, but the operational planning layer that keeps your FOH and BOH coordination visible in one place. Supplier and Vendor Contact Database with Order History

Every supplier in one searchable database — produce, proteins, dairy, alcohol, packaging, cleaning supplies, equipment service providers, and linen services. For each vendor: contact info, typical order days, delivery schedule, pricing notes, contract terms, and renewal dates. Never scramble for a vendor's number during a delivery dispute or lose track of when a supplier contract is up for renegotiation. Recipe and Menu Management System with Cost Notes

A menu database where every item has a recipe link, key ingredient cost notes, estimated plate cost, current menu price, and rough margin estimate. Not a full-featured restaurant costing platform — but a fast-reference layer that tells you which items on your current menu are your highest-margin dishes and which are costing more than you thought. Invaluable for menu engineering decisions during reprints, seasonal rotations, and price increase conversations with yourself.

Weekly P&L and Revenue Tracking

A simplified weekly financial dashboard that you update in 15 minutes every Monday morning from your POS data and expense records. Track weekly revenue, food cost, labor cost, and gross margin estimates week over week. Spot trends early — a food cost spike in week three, a revenue dip that correlates with a new competitor — rather than discovering problems three weeks later when your bookkeeper reconciles the month. This is not your accounting software; it is your operational gut-check dashboard.

Inventory Checklist Templates

Reusable inventory count checklists for your key product categories — proteins, produce, dry goods, dairy, alcohol, cleaning supplies. Run the same structured checklist every order cycle, track par levels versus on-hand counts, and reduce the combination of over-ordering (waste) and under-ordering (86'd menu items) that erodes margins in most independent restaurants. Assign inventory counts to specific staff with clear instructions rather than informal walk-arounds.

Marketing and Events Calendar

A unified calendar for all your restaurant marketing and community activities — email newsletter send dates, Instagram and Facebook post plans, local advertising placements, community event sponsorships, and seasonal promotion launches. Plan 60 days ahead, assign content creation tasks with deadlines, and maintain a consistent local marketing presence without it requiring a marketing department. Independent restaurants that market consistently outperform those that only market reactively when business is slow.

Event and Private Dining Booking Pipeline

A booking pipeline for private events, buyouts, and large party reservations — the highest-margin revenue category in most independent restaurants. Track each inquiry from first contact through deposit collected, menu confirmed, and event completed. Log event size, date, menu selections, deposit amount, total value, and follow-up notes. Never let a private dining inquiry fall through the cracks because you forgot to call them back within 24 hours.

Health Inspection Prep Checklist and SOP Library

A library of SOPs mapped to common health inspection criteria — temperature log procedures, dry goods storage labeling, handwashing station compliance, pest control documentation, allergen protocols, and cleaning schedule templates. Run through the master checklist before any planned or unplanned inspection, assign corrective actions to staff with clear deadlines, and build a food safety culture that is always inspection-ready rather than scrambling when the inspector walks in.

Questions From Restaurant Owners

How does the staff scheduling tracker work?

The staff scheduling and shift notes tracker is a planning and communication layer for your team — who is scheduled which shift, shift swap notes, callout tracking, and manager notes per service. It is not a payroll or time-clock system; those stay in your dedicated restaurant software. It is the operational planning layer that keeps your FOH and BOH coordination visible in one place rather than fragmented across texts and whiteboards.

Can I manage my suppliers and vendors in Notion?

Yes. The supplier and vendor database stores every vendor you work with — produce, proteins, alcohol, packaging, cleaning supplies, equipment services — with contact information, typical order days, pricing notes, contract terms, and renewal dates. Searchable and always accessible when you need to reorder, find a backup vendor in an emergency, or renegotiate a contract that is coming up for renewal.

How does the recipe and menu management system work?

Each menu item has a record with its recipe link, key ingredients with rough cost notes, plate cost estimate, menu price, and margin. It is not a full-featured costing platform but it gives you a quick-reference view of which items are your highest-margin dishes and which are costing you more than you realized. This is particularly useful for menu engineering decisions during reprints, seasonal rotations, and when you are deciding whether a price increase is warranted.

No. The weekly P&L and revenue tracking in Notion is a simplified operational dashboard — weekly revenue, key cost categories, and gross margin estimates that you update from your POS and accounting data. Your actual bookkeeping stays in QuickBooks, Restaurant365, or whatever accounting platform you use. This is the weekly pulse check you run in 15 minutes every Monday morning to catch problems early rather than discovering them weeks later when the books are reconciled.

How does the health inspection prep feature work?

Teams that run this checklist regularly score significantly higher than teams that only prepare reactively.
