Practical Workflow

How to Plan a Full Week of Meals in 20 Minutes

This is the exact Sunday workflow built into the template. Most users complete it in 18–22 minutes their first time and under 15 minutes by week three, once their recipe database has 15–20 saved meals.

1

Open the Recipe Database and Browse This Week's Options (5 min)

Start in the Recipe Database filtered to your dietary goals. Sort by "last cooked" to surface meals you haven't had recently, or filter by prep time if this week is busy. Gallery view shows photos and ratings at a glance — browsing feels like a personal cookbook, not a spreadsheet. Identify 7–10 candidate meals. You don't need to decide yet, just narrow the field.

~5 minutes
2

Drag Meals into the 7-Day Grid (4 min)

The weekly planner grid shows Monday–Sunday with slots for breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks. Click any slot, type the recipe name, and it auto-links to the database entry. Fill dinners first (hardest decisions), then lunches (often repurposed leftovers), then breakfasts. Snacks surface from pantry tracker suggestions. Drag-and-drop lets you shift meals between days in one move.

~4 minutes
3

Review the Auto-Generated Grocery List (3 min)

Switch to the Smart Grocery List view. Every ingredient from every planned recipe has auto-populated — two recipes needing onions are merged into one entry with the combined quantity. The list is pre-sorted by store section: produce, proteins, dairy, pantry staples, frozen. Adjust quantities if you're cooking for more or fewer than the default serving size.

~3 minutes
4

Cross-Check Against Your Pantry Tracker (4 min)

Toggle "Have it" on the grocery list for anything already in your kitchen — the list shortens in real time. Check expiry dates: items approaching expiry show in orange, nudging you to use them this week instead of letting them waste. Most households find 30–40% of each week's grocery list is already on hand. That's where the template saves the most money.

~4 minutes
5

Finalize the Shopping List and Flag Prep Day (4 min)

What remains after the pantry check is your actual shopping list. Add household staples running low (the Pantry Tracker has a "household" category for coffee, cleaning supplies, etc.). Open the Meal Prep Schedule and flag which meals you'll batch-cook Sunday vs. cook fresh. The template calculates required Sunday prep time so you can adjust the plan if needed. Lock it in and you're done.

~4 minutes

Week 1 vs Week 6 — what changes

Week 1: 20–25 minutes because you're building your recipe database as you plan. Add 5–8 recipes you already know. Week 3: Down to 15 minutes — enough recipes that planning is mostly selecting from a trusted list. Week 6: Under 12 minutes. A rotation of 20–25 recipes, an accurate pantry tracker, and Sunday planning that feels effortless. The habit sticks because it feels like control, not like a chore.
