Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Data Scientists

How does the ML project tracker work?

Each ML project gets its own record in the tracker with fields for dataset version, model type, key hyperparameters, accuracy metrics, experiment notes, and links to notebooks or GitHub repos. You can compare runs side by side and maintain a clean experiment log without relying on scattered notebook comments or trying to remember which version was the good one.

Can I use this for freelance data science consulting?

Yes. The client consulting pipeline works like a lightweight CRM for your consulting engagements — track each client from proposal through delivery, log deliverables, deadlines, and revenue per project. It connects to your project tracker so you can navigate from client to technical work in two clicks. Multiple consultants have told us it paid for itself on the first project they ran through it cleanly.

How do I track research papers in Notion?

The research paper database lets you save papers with title, authors, publication venue, tags by topic and method, and your personal notes and key takeaways. You can filter by topic — transformers, causal inference, reinforcement learning — and find relevant papers instantly instead of digging through downloads folders or Zotero without notes. Your own annotations stay attached to every paper permanently.

Does the template help with Kaggle competitions?

Yes. The Kaggle and competition tracker logs each competition with its deadline, prize structure, your current ranking, approach notes, and links to your notebooks. It also tracks past competitions so you can review what worked and apply lessons to future entries. Serious Kaggle competitors often find reviewing their own historical approach notes more valuable than reading others' writeups.

How is the code snippet library organized?

The code snippet and function library is an index of your most reusable code — it doesn't store the code in Notion (that belongs in GitHub or a notebook) but keeps a searchable index of what you have, what it does, the language or framework, and a direct link to the source. Find your custom data cleaning functions or evaluation utilities in seconds rather than trawling through old notebooks.

Can I plan my data science career and skill development?

Yes. The skills development roadmap lets you map the technologies and methods you want to master — ML frameworks, cloud certifications, new statistical techniques — with learning resources, estimated timelines, and progress notes. Pair it with the conference and publication calendar to build a complete career development system that you actually run instead of just aspirationally thinking about.

FreelanceOS Pro connects projects to clients, papers to projects, and skills to career goals out of the box. You get a working, relational system in under an hour.
