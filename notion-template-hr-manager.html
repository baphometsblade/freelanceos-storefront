The Problem

You Build Systems for Your Organization. What About Your Own Professional System?

HR professionals design the operating systems that other people's careers run on — onboarding experiences, development programs, performance frameworks, and recognition systems. Yet most HR practitioners manage their own professional work from a combination of email folders, shared drives full of outdated SOPs, and notes scattered across meeting tools and personal notebooks.

📋

SOPs exist but are not findable

The onboarding checklist is in a shared drive folder that has not been updated since 2023. The performance review process exists in a deck from a team meeting last year. When someone asks for the process, you spend twenty minutes searching before sending something you are not certain is current.

🎓

SHRM credits tracked manually or not at all

Your SHRM-CP recertification is coming up and you are piecing together your credit history from conference emails, webinar completion certificates in your downloads folder, and your own imperfect memory. This is not the standard you hold the rest of the organization to for compliance tracking.

🔧

HR projects managed across too many tools

The benefits renewal project is in one spreadsheet, the ATS implementation is tracked in another tool, the DEI initiative has a shared Google Doc, and the manager training program is in someone else's email thread. You are the connective tissue and nothing is connected.

📊

HR metrics only exist at reporting time

Time-to-fill, offer acceptance rate, turnover by department — you pull these numbers quarterly when leadership asks for them. There is no living view of how your HR function is performing between formal reporting cycles, which means problems compound before they become visible.

Interview Process Templates — Structured Guides & Evaluation Rubrics

Structured interview guides and competency-based evaluation rubrics stored by role type and seniority level — no candidate PII stored in Notion. Structured interviewing with documented rubrics reduces bias, improves hiring consistency across interviewers, supports fair hiring practices, and produces better outcomes for both candidates and organizations. Track your full L&D portfolio, monitor budget utilization against allocation, compare program completion rates across offerings, and build a historical record of every initiative your function has delivered. HR Project & Initiative Tracker — DEI, Benefits Renewals & System Implementations

See every HR project on a single dashboard instead of tracking them across disconnected tools and email threads. HR leaders who manage their project portfolio visibly are more effective at prioritizing competing demands and communicating function-level progress to leadership.

SHRM/HRCI CPD & Certification Tracker

A complete professional development tracker for your SHRM-CP, SHRM-SCP, PHR, SPHR, GPHR, or other HR certification recertification requirements. Build institutional memory about vendor relationships that survives individual team member transitions.

HR Metrics Dashboard — Turnover, Time-to-Fill & Offer Acceptance (Aggregate)

A workforce metrics dashboard template for tracking key HR function performance indicators at a non-identifying aggregate level — turnover rate by department (department-level, not individual), voluntary versus involuntary separation split, time-to-fill by role category, offer acceptance rate, cost-per-hire, eNPS aggregate score, headcount trend by quarter, and L&D program completion rates.
