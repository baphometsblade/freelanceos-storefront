Client Time Zone Tracking

When you're in Chiang Mai but your clients are in New York and London, keeping track of who's available when is a daily puzzle. The travel planner keeps time zone references visible at the day level of your itinerary so you never accidentally schedule a call at 3 AM your time or miss a deadline because you forgot to account for the date line.

Time zones Client calls Deadline tracking
Real scenario: Flying from Berlin (UTC+2) to Tokyo (UTC+9) with US East Coast clients (UTC-4). A 13-hour gap means your morning is their previous evening. The itinerary builder shows this offset so you plan check-in calls accordingly — not after you've already missed a window.

Workation Planning

A workation is not a vacation. You have deliverables, client expectations, and revenue targets to hit — the beach backdrop is incidental. The itinerary builder lets you block non-negotiable work hours before you plan anything else, so your capacity is protected from day one. See your actual available hours before you commit to any tourist activities or social plans.

Work hours block Coworking spaces Focus time
Real scenario: 3-week workation to Porto. You need 6 billable hours per day Mon–Fri. Block those first in the itinerary, mark them as deep work. What's left — evenings, weekends, a Friday afternoon — is genuinely free time. No guilt, no stress, no missed client emails.
