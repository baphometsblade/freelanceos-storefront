Frequently Asked Questions

Can I manage brand guidelines and assets in one place?
Yes. FreelanceOS Pro includes a Brand Guidelines Hub for tone of voice, messaging pillars, and visual standards, plus a separate Brand Asset Library for logos, color palettes, fonts, and downloadable files. Both are linked throughout your workspace so your campaign pages can reference the exact assets and guidelines they follow. You can share either page with teammates or agencies via Notion's sharing settings.
How do I track campaign performance across multiple channels?
The Campaign Tracker lets you create one entry per campaign with fields for budget, channels (social, email, paid, PR, events), KPIs, launch date, status, and results. Each campaign page can hold detailed notes, linked assets, and post-campaign results. You can filter and sort by status, channel, budget, or date — so whether you're running one campaign or fifteen, you always have a clear view of what's active and how it's performing.
Does it work for freelance brand consultants with multiple clients?
Absolutely. The Client CRM lets you manage each client relationship separately, with contact info, project status, next action, and invoice status. The Campaign Tracker, Asset Library, and Brand Guidelines Hub can all be filtered by client — so your work for each brand stays clean and separate. The Invoice Tracker makes it easy to track billable projects and payments across your roster.
How do I monitor competitor activity in Notion?
The Competitor Monitor gives you a structured database for tracking each competitor with fields for their positioning, recent campaigns, social moves, and share of voice observations. You can add a date-stamped log entry each time something notable happens — a rebrand, a major campaign launch, a new product line — and tag it by competitor. Over time it becomes a valuable archive of the brand landscape you compete in.
Can I use it to manage brand feedback from stakeholders?
Yes. The Stakeholder Feedback Log is specifically built for capturing brand direction and feedback from leadership, sales, investors, and customers. Each entry includes the source, the feedback itself, priority, status (open, in review, resolved), and notes on how it was addressed. This prevents brand feedback from living in email threads or Slack messages where it gets lost — everything is tracked, owned, and resolved in one place.
