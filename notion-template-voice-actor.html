Frequently Asked Questions

Is this Notion template designed specifically for voice actors and VO artists?
Yes — every database and workflow is built around the voice actor's business cycle: auditioning, booking, delivering scripts, tracking sessions, managing client relationships, logging revenue and royalties, and maintaining a demo reel. It works for commercial VO, audiobook narration, video game voice acting, eLearning, and animation work.
Can I track both commercial VO bookings and audiobook narration projects?
Yes. The Audition Tracker includes a genre field covering commercial, audiobook, video game, eLearning, animation, and narration. The Revenue Tracker logs income by genre so you can see which type of work is most profitable and make strategic decisions about where to focus your demo reel updates and marketing efforts.
How does the audition tracker help me improve my booking rate?
The Audition Tracker logs every submission — date, project, client or casting director, character or role, script excerpt link, self-tape submitted, callback received, and whether you booked the job. Over time, you can calculate your audition-to-booking rate by genre, identify which casting directors or agencies you book most frequently through, and spot patterns in what types of auditions convert into paid work.
Can I track SAG-AFTRA residuals and royalty payments?
Yes. The Revenue and Royalties Tracker distinguishes SAG-AFTRA union work from non-union projects and logs residuals and royalties by project — commercials, audiobooks, video games — separately from session fees. You can track cumulative royalty income by client and genre over time, which is especially useful for commercial spots or audiobooks with ongoing earnings.
