Interview Preparation

Use Notion to Prepare for Interviews Like a Consultant, Not a Candidate

Most candidates walk into interviews hoping they'll remember their good stories. This system ensures you walk in knowing exactly what you're going to say — and why.

1

Build your STAR answer library

The Interview Question Bank structures every behavioral answer in STAR format. Writing all four components forces you to include specific numbers and outcomes. Tag each story with skills it demonstrates, rate confidence 1–5, and build a "greatest hits" view of your most versatile answers.

2

Run the pre-interview research checklist

The Company Research Database includes a pre-interview checklist: business model, recent funding news, product walkthrough, major competitors, and Glassdoor culture signals. Prepare a specific "why this company?" answer and identify one challenge you'd be positioned to address in the role.

3

Prepare questions to ask — and capture the answers

The template includes a curated question bank by category: role clarity, team dynamics, success metrics, and culture. It also captures the actual answers you receive — often more revealing than the job description. Strong closers: "What does success look like in 90 days?", "What are the biggest unsolved challenges right now?"

4

Track salary research and negotiation floor

Log posted range, Glassdoor research, your target, and walkaway floor. Walk in able to anchor on a specific, justified number. Negotiate total comp — equity, signing bonus, and remote stipend are all movable. Log every round so you have context throughout.

7-Step Job Search Strategy with Notion as Your HQ

A job search is a sales process — you are the product, companies are prospects, interviews are demos. The fastest job seekers aren't the most qualified; they're the most organized and strategic.

  1. Define your target first. Write down target industries, company size, role level, remote preference, and minimum comp. Screen every application against this — without it, three months pass going nowhere.
  2. Build a proactive target company list. Best searches are 50% inbound, 50% outbound. Build a list of 30–50 targets in the Company Research Database and track funding news and headcount growth.
  3. Optimize for quality over volume. 30 tailored applications outperform 100 generic ones. Customize each resume headline and cover letter. 45 minutes of research per application dramatically lifts response rates.
  4. Run a daily 90-minute session. 20 min follow-ups, 40 min new applications, 20 min networking, 10 min analytics. Consistency beats intensity.
  5. Build the referral before applying. Referred applications get 4–5x more interviews. For each target company, find a LinkedIn connection and send a brief message asking for 15 minutes.
  6. Interview every week to stay sharp. Take interviews with lukewarm companies early to practice and build your Question Bank. Best performance comes after several reps.
  7. Run a weekly analytics review. Check response rate, interview conversion, and where you're losing in the pipeline. Treat it like a conversion funnel and keep optimizing.

Pro Tip: The Follow-up Email That Works

Send a follow-up email 7–10 days after applying if you haven't heard back. Under four sentences: reference the specific role, include one concrete achievement, end with a clear ask. This generates interviews on ~15–20% of applications that would have gone silent.
