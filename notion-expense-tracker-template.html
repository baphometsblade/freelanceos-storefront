Frequently Asked Questions

What expenses can freelancers track in this template?
The FreelanceOS expense tracker covers every major freelance expense category: software subscriptions (Adobe, Figma, Notion, Zoom), equipment purchases (laptop, camera, microphone), home office costs (a calculated percentage of rent or mortgage based on office square footage, plus internet and utilities), professional development (courses, books, conferences), travel (client meeting transportation, mileage, flights, hotels), and business services (accountant fees, legal review, banking charges, contractor payments). Each entry includes a deductibility flag so you instantly know what actually reduces your tax bill versus what is just a personal cost that happens to live in your business account.
How does this Notion expense tracker help with tax preparation?
The template generates automatic monthly and annual summaries grouped by IRS Schedule C categories. At tax time you hand your accountant a clean breakdown — total deductible software expenses, total equipment, total home office, total travel — instead of a shoebox of receipts or an unsorted bank export. The built-in deductible vs. non-deductible flag means you never accidentally claim a personal expense, and the partial-use percentage field correctly handles mixed-use items like your phone or home internet. Most FreelanceOS Pro users report their tax preparation appointment is 30–60 minutes shorter than it used to be, which more than covers the cost of the template. If you also use the Notion revenue tracker, your accountant gets a complete picture of income and expenses from one source.
Can I link expenses to specific client projects?
Yes — this is one of the most powerful features for freelancers who want to understand their real profit margins. Every expense entry has a Client / Project relation field that connects directly to your client database in FreelanceOS Pro. This lets you see the true cost of delivering each project, calculate accurate profit margins after tools and time costs, and surface reimbursable expenses to invoice back to clients without losing track of them. The project profitability view shows revenue minus all directly attributed expenses at a glance. If you have a client that pays well but requires expensive software, travel, or subcontractor costs, this view makes that visible so you can price your next project more accurately or negotiate better rates.
Does this Notion expense tracker replace accounting software like QuickBooks or FreshBooks?
For most solo freelancers and solopreneurs, yes — this template handles everything you actually need without a monthly subscription. QuickBooks and FreshBooks are designed for businesses with employees, inventory, payroll, and complex multi-account reconciliation. If your business is you plus a few contractors and your expenses flow through one or two accounts, the FreelanceOS expense tracker paired with your bank's transaction export is genuinely sufficient. That said, if your accountant specifically requires books in QBO format, or if you have payroll to run, keep your accounting software and use this template as a companion tool for project-level tracking that QBO doesn't do well. Many freelancers use both — QBO for formal bookkeeping, FreelanceOS for day-to-day visibility — and find the combination more useful than either alone. This template also integrates naturally with the Notion invoice tracker and freelance budget template already in the system.
How do I separate business expenses from personal expenses in Notion?
The template includes a three-way Business / Personal / Mixed selector on every expense entry. Business entries flow into your deduction totals automatically. Personal entries are still logged (useful for understanding your full cash flow) but are excluded from all tax summary views. Mixed expenses — the most common situation for items like a phone plan used 70% for work — include a Business Use % field. Enter the percentage once, and the template calculates the deductible portion correctly every month. A dedicated "Personal Only" filter view hides non-deductible items so your workspace stays clean, while the "All Expenses" view gives you the complete picture when you want it. The key discipline is logging everything as it happens rather than sorting retroactively — once you build that habit, separating business from personal takes seconds per entry.
