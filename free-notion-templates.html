How to Duplicate a Notion Template

New to Notion templates? Here's how to duplicate any free template into your own workspace:

  1. Click the "Get Free Template" button for the template you want.
  2. The template opens in Notion. If you're not logged in, you'll see a "Sign In" prompt — use your existing Notion account or create a free one.
  3. Click the "Duplicate" button in the top-right corner of the page.
  4. Choose the workspace and page location where you want the template saved.
  5. The template is now in your workspace — customize it however you like.

The whole process takes about 30 seconds. Notion's free plan supports unlimited pages, so you can duplicate as many templates as you want without upgrading.

Frequently Asked Questions About Free Notion Templates

Do I need a paid Notion account to use free templates?

No. All 12 free templates work on Notion's free plan. The free plan supports unlimited pages and blocks for personal use — more than enough to run any of these templates.

Can I customize these free templates?

Yes. Once you duplicate a template, it's yours to edit completely. Add properties, change views, remove sections you don't need, and make it work for your specific situation.

Are these templates compatible with Notion on mobile?

Yes. All templates work in Notion's iOS and Android apps. Some complex views display better on desktop, but the core functionality — adding entries, checking tasks, reviewing your calendar — works well on mobile.

What's the difference between these free templates and paid templates like FreelanceOS Pro?

The free templates are simplified, standalone databases. The paid templates use relational databases where everything is connected: tasks link to projects, projects link to clients, clients link to invoices. This cross-linking enables rollups, automated counts, and advanced views that the free templates don't support.

Can I share these templates with my team?

Yes. Once you duplicate a template to your workspace, you can share the page with guests or teammates. Notion's free plan allows up to five guest users. For larger teams, you'll need a Plus plan.
