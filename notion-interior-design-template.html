Sound Familiar?

The way most interior designers run their business — juggling client messages across platforms, spec sheets in scattered folders, project statuses living only in memory — is a liability at scale.

📱

Client feedback scattered everywhere

Approvals come in via email, text, Instagram DMs, WhatsApp, and verbal comments on site. When a client asks "didn't I already approve this?", you have no single record to check — and no way to prove what was agreed.

📦

No single place for FF&E specs and vendors

The sofa specs are in one email thread, the tile vendor's contact is in your phone, the lead time estimate is in a PDF somewhere in Downloads. When procurement gets chaotic, deliveries get delayed — and clients blame you.

🗂

Losing track of where each project stands

With three or four active projects, you're constantly context-switching and reconstructing status from memory. You arrive at a client meeting not completely sure what's been approved and what's still pending their sign-off.

💸

Invoicing and billing across multiple projects

Tracking which clients have paid deposits, which have outstanding design fees, and which procurement invoices need to be billed through — all while managing the actual design work — is an enormous admin drain on a small practice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before purchasing.

Can I use this Notion interior design template for multiple client projects at once?
Yes. The template is built around a multi-project pipeline. Each project gets its own page with its own phase tracker, budget, FF&E log, and client communication history. A master dashboard shows all active projects in one view — which phase each is in, upcoming milestones, and which items are waiting on client approval — so nothing slips through when you're juggling five projects at once.
What exactly does the FF&E tracker include?
The FF&E log tracks every item from specification to installation: vendor name, model/SKU, unit cost, quantity, lead time, procurement status (specified / ordered / delivered / installed), and any substitutions made. It calculates total budget vs. actual spend automatically and can be filtered by room, status, or vendor. It's the single source of truth for your procurement process.
Does this template help with client approval and revision tracking?
Yes. Every design decision that goes to a client has an approval status field: Pending, Approved, Revision Requested, or Rejected. When a client requests revisions, you log what they asked for, which round it is, and when the revised version was sent. This creates a clean paper trail that protects you in scope disputes and helps you bill for additional rounds beyond what your contract covers.
Can design studios use this template with multiple team members?
Absolutely. Notion's sharing model lets you add team members with different permission levels. You can assign projects to specific designers, create a shared vendor database that everyone contributes to, and run design review meetings directly from the Notion workspace. The template includes a team section with project assignments and design review checklists built for studio workflows.
How does the mood board and concept tracker work in Notion?
Each project has a Concept & Mood Board section where you embed images, save links from Pinterest or Houzz, attach PDFs, write design direction narratives, and tag concepts by room or zone. You can share this page directly with clients for feedback. It works best as a centralized reference that links your inspiration to the actual specification decisions — a single source of design intent for the project.
