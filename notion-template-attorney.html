Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Attorneys and Legal Professionals

Can attorneys use Notion for case management?

Notion is suitable for the administrative and business management layer of legal practice — matter tracking, deadline calendars, time logs, CLE compliance, and business development. It is not a substitute for a certified legal practice management system (Clio, MyCase, PracticePanther) and must not be used to store privileged communications, confidential case strategy, or sensitive client information. FreelanceOS Pro for attorneys is designed specifically for the administrative workflows that sit alongside your primary practice management software.

Is Notion safe to use for attorney-client privilege?

Notion must not be used to store attorney-client privileged communications, confidential case strategy, work product, or any sensitive client information. Notion is a cloud-based productivity tool, not a secure legal practice management system, and does not meet the data security and confidentiality standards required for privileged legal materials in most jurisdictions. FreelanceOS Pro for attorneys is built strictly for administrative, scheduling, and business management purposes. All privileged information must remain in your certified, compliant practice management system.

How do solo attorneys track billable hours in Notion?

The FreelanceOS time tracking log lets you record each time entry with the matter number, date, work description, time spent, billing rate, and billable status. Log entries at the point of work — during or immediately after a call, research session, or document review — rather than reconstructing at end-of-week. Run a filtered view by matter to see total time logged against retainer or estimate. This complements, rather than replaces, your formal billing software for invoice generation.

How do I track court deadlines in Notion?

The deadline tracker database includes fields for deadline type (court date, filing deadline, response due, statute of limitations), matter link, date, urgency flag, and a description field. Create a filtered view showing only deadlines in the next 30 days, sorted by date. Critical deadlines should always be mirrored in your primary calendar system (Outlook, Google Calendar) — Notion serves as the structured, context-rich record while your calendar provides the alert.

What are the bar compliance requirements for using Notion?

Bar association rules on technology use in legal practice vary by jurisdiction. Most require attorneys to ensure that client information is stored securely and confidentially, to vet third-party software for data security standards, and to understand where data is stored and who has access to it. Because FreelanceOS Pro is designed for administrative use only (no PHI, no privileged information), the compliance burden is significantly lower than for practice management software. That said, always consult your state or national bar association's guidance on cloud software use before adopting any new tool in your practice.

How do I track CLE requirements in Notion?

Log each completed CLE course with: provider name, course title, credit hours, credit category (general, ethics, professionalism, specialisation), delivery format, completion date, and certificate reference number. Create a summary view that calculates total hours by category and compares them to your jurisdiction's requirements for the current compliance period. For attorneys licensed in multiple jurisdictions, tag each entry with the relevant jurisdiction so you can filter by requirement independently.

Can I use Notion to track client referrals?

Yes — the referral network and business development tracker is one of the highest-value sections of FreelanceOS Pro for attorneys. Log every referral source (attorney, accountant, financial advisor, former client), the date of each referral, the matter it led to, your follow-up actions, and the relationship status. Create a view sorted by last contact date to surface relationships that need maintenance. Building and maintaining your referral network systematically is one of the highest-ROI activities for a solo practice, and most attorneys have no system for it at all.

Is Notion better than Clio for solo attorney practice management?

Clio and similar legal practice management platforms (MyCase, PracticePanther) are built specifically for legal practice and include court calendaring, IOLTA trust accounting, client portal features, and secure document management that Notion cannot and should not replicate. FreelanceOS Pro for attorneys is not a Clio replacement — it's a complement to your practice management software, handling the business development, administrative organisation, CLE compliance, and professional planning layers that dedicated legal software often handles poorly. Most solo attorneys find they need both: a certified PMS for client matters and a flexible organisational tool for everything else.
