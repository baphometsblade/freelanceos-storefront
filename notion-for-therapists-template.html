Frequently Asked Questions

Everything Therapists Ask Before Purchasing

Therapists have specific, important questions about HIPAA compliance, clinical scope, multi-state licensing, and whether this tool genuinely fits their practice situation. Here are direct, complete answers — including the most important answer about what this template explicitly does not do.

Is this Notion template HIPAA-compliant for client records? Can I use it for session notes or patient files? +
No — and this is the single most important thing to understand before purchasing. This Notion template contains absolutely zero functionality for client or patient records, session notes, progress notes, treatment plans, PHI (Protected Health Information), diagnoses, case conceptualizations, or any clinical data of any kind. It is not designed for clinical records and is not HIPAA-compliant for that purpose.

What this template IS for: tracking your own CE credits by category and license, managing your license renewal timelines with automated countdown reminders, logging your own supervision hours for professional documentation and licensure tracking, tracking your professional reading and training goals, managing your referral source relationships and practice business development activities, and monitoring your practice's financial health at an aggregate non-PHI level.

All client records — session notes, progress notes, treatment plans, diagnoses, intake forms, any document with PHI — must remain in your HIPAA-compliant EHR or EMR system such as SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, Jane App, or TheraNest. This Notion workspace is purely for the therapist as a professional, not for any client-related information.
I am licensed in two or more states with completely different CE requirements. Does this template handle multi-state licensure? +
Yes — the License and Credential Hub is specifically designed for therapists with multiple licenses across different states, which is increasingly common as telehealth expands and therapists serve clients in multiple jurisdictions. Each license is a fully separate database entry with its own state, license type, license number, renewal date, total CE hour requirement, and categorical CE requirements specific to that state's board.

You can hold an LCSW license in California requiring 36 hours with 6 in law and ethics and 3 in cultural competency alongside an LCSW license in New York with completely different categorical requirements — each license has its own independent progress dashboard and renewal countdown. CE courses that qualify toward multiple licenses can be tagged to multiple license entries simultaneously. The template tracks each license's CE progress independently so you can see at a glance that your California license is on track while your New York license needs two more ethics hours before an earlier renewal deadline.
I am a pre-licensed therapist accumulating supervision hours toward my first independent license. Is this template designed for me? +
Absolutely. The Supervision Log module is one of the most valuable tools in the template specifically for pre-licensed therapists working toward their independent licensure upgrade. Whether you are accumulating hours toward your LCSW, LPC, MFT, LPCC, or another credential, you log each supervision session with the date, your supervisor's name and professional credentials, the duration in hours, the modality (individual, dyadic, or group supervision), and any thematic professional learning notes from the session.

The module tracks your running total of supervision hours against your state board's requirement and displays your percentage complete — so you always know exactly how many hours you have accumulated, how many remain toward the requirement, and whether you are on pace to meet your target licensure upgrade date. This is your own professional record only; it contains no identifying information about any clients you may have discussed thematically in supervision sessions.
I own or work in a group practice with multiple therapists. Can we use this template collectively as a practice? +
Yes, with an important structural recommendation: each therapist in the practice should maintain their own individual Notion workspace for their personal CE credits, supervision hours, professional reading, and individual development plan — since those data points are specific to each clinician's license, professional history, and individual career trajectory and should not be commingled.

A group practice owner or clinical director can effectively use the Private Practice Business Hub and Revenue Dashboard modules for the practice's shared business operations — tracking referral source relationships at a practice level, monitoring aggregate revenue and business expenses, and managing shared marketing initiatives. Notion's page-sharing and permission features let you share the business-facing sections with practice partners or administrators while keeping each clinician's individual professional development workspace private and separately managed. Many group practices use one shared workspace for business operations and provide each clinician with individual professional development modules separately.
Does the CE Credit Tracker actually handle different mandatory credit categories like ethics, cultural competency, and suicide prevention? +
Yes — handling categorical CE requirements is a core, purpose-built feature of the CE Credit Tracker, not an afterthought. Most state licensing boards do not simply require a total number of CE hours; they require specific minimum hours in designated mandatory categories. Getting your total hour count right but failing a categorical requirement can result in a renewal application being rejected even when you have completed significantly more than enough total hours overall.

The CE Credit Tracker includes a full category system covering: Ethics and Professional Conduct, California Law and Ethics or equivalent state law requirement, Cultural Competency and Diversity, Suicide Prevention and Crisis Intervention, Substance Abuse and Chemical Dependency, Domestic Violence and Intimate Partner Violence, Child Abuse and Mandated Reporting, Aging and Long-Term Care where required by state board, and General or Specialty CE. Each CE course is tagged to its applicable category at the time of logging. The dashboard then shows your progress in each category independently versus your state's specific categorical requirement — so you can see immediately that you have completed 5.5 of 6 required ethics hours and need exactly one more ethics-designated course, independently of your total CE hour count.
I am a licensed therapist who also provides clinical supervision to pre-licensed associates. Is this template useful for my supervisor role too? +
Yes. The Consultation and Peer Support Network module adapts well for licensed therapists who provide clinical supervision to others. You can track your supervisees' names and credentials, log the topics covered in supervision sessions at an aggregate thematic level with absolutely no client-identifying information, document your own supervisor training hours, and note your supervision methodology goals and ongoing professional development as a clinical supervisor.

The Professional Goals module is particularly valuable for therapists actively working toward a formal supervisor credential such as AAMFT Approved Supervisor, BBS-Certified Supervisor in California, or similar state-specific designations. You can track the specific training requirements, hours of consultation with a supervisor of supervisors, and your career milestones on the path to becoming a credentialed supervisor. The CE Credit Tracker also supports supervisor training CE hours as a distinct category, since many supervisor credential pathways require their own ongoing training hours that are separate from your standard clinical CE requirements.
