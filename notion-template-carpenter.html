Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use this for both residential and commercial carpentry work?
Yes. The Project Tracker and Client CRM work for homeowners, general contractors, and commercial clients alike. You can tag projects by client type and filter your pipeline to see residential vs. commercial work at any time.
How does the materials reference help me quote faster?
The Materials Reference stores your commonly used materials — lumber species, sheet goods, hardware, finishes — with supplier, cost per unit, and markup notes. When building a quote, you're pulling from real pricing data you've collected rather than estimating from memory or calling your supplier mid-meeting.
Can I track subcontractors working on my jobs?
Yes. The Subcontractor Log lets you track painters, electricians, tile setters, and other subs by project — contact details, scope, agreed price, timeline, and payment status. Keeping this in the same workspace as your project tracker makes coordination much easier when multiple trades are on the same job.
Can I use the tool inventory for an insurance claim?
The Tool Inventory stores serial numbers, purchase dates, warranty expiry, service dates, and replacement cost for each tool. This is exactly the information needed to file a job-site theft or loss claim with your insurer — and it's much easier to maintain in Notion than in a spreadsheet or paper list you can't find when you need it.
Does this work on my phone on the job site?
Yes. Notion works on iOS and Android. You can log project notes, check a materials reference, or update a quote status from your phone on the job site. All changes sync instantly to your desktop workspace at the shop or home office.
