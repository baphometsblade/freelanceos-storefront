FreelanceOS vs Easlo

Easlo's "Notion Everything" bundle is genuinely excellent — it's one of the most polished Notion template products available. But at $79–$149, it's priced for productivity enthusiasts who want an entire life OS, not specifically for freelance business management. It includes habits, journal, reading lists, and dozens of pages you'll never use as a freelancer.

FreelanceOS is purpose-built for one thing: running a freelance business. Every feature maps directly to a real freelancer need — client CRM, project management, invoice tracking, revenue dashboards. There's no bloat, no learning overhead for features you won't use, and the free tier lets you try it before spending anything.

FreelanceOS vs August Bradley's PPV System

August Bradley's Pillars, Pipelines & Vaults (PPV) system is a sophisticated life design and productivity methodology with an associated Notion implementation. It's genuinely powerful for people who want to manage their entire life — goals, habits, projects, areas — in one philosophical framework.

For most freelancers, it's overkill. The learning curve alone (10–20 hours minimum to understand and implement properly) costs more in time than the template costs in money. FreelanceOS is operational in 15 minutes.

FreelanceOS vs Generic Notion Templates

There are hundreds of cheap Notion templates on Gumroad and Etsy for $5–$25. Most are built by individual creators as side projects — they look nice in screenshots but lack the relational database architecture that makes a real business system work. They typically have a client list that doesn't connect to projects, projects that don't connect to invoices, and no revenue tracking.

FreelanceOS was built specifically as a business management system. Its databases are fully relational — your clients link to their projects, which link to invoices, which roll up into your revenue dashboard. It's the difference between a digital filing cabinet and a real business operating system.

FreelanceOS vs Spreadsheets

DIY spreadsheets are the most common "solution" for freelancers, and they work well enough until they don't. The problem isn't the spreadsheet — it's the fragmentation. Your client list is in one file, your projects in another, your invoices in a third, and your revenue tracking somewhere else entirely. Cross-referencing is manual, nothing updates automatically, and you spend time doing data entry instead of client work.

FreelanceOS replaces all of those spreadsheets with a single, connected Notion workspace. When you update a client's name, it updates everywhere. When you mark an invoice paid, your revenue dashboard updates automatically. It's everything a spreadsheet does, but connected.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything freelancers ask before choosing a Notion template system.

Is FreelanceOS actually free, or is it a limited trial? ▼
FreelanceOS Lite is permanently free — no trial period, no expiry, no credit card required. It's a fully functional subset of the Pro system that includes a client CRM, project tracking, invoice list, and basic revenue tracker. You can use it indefinitely. The Pro version ($29, one-time) adds advanced features like the full revenue dashboard, proposal templates, and contractor management.
How does FreelanceOS compare to building my own Notion system? ▼
Building your own system from scratch in Notion typically takes 5–15 hours for a freelancer who's comfortable with the platform, or much longer if you're still learning. You'll also spend time fixing architecture issues you didn't anticipate — like client databases that don't connect to invoices properly. FreelanceOS gives you a pre-built, battle-tested architecture that you can customize rather than starting from scratch. Most freelancers find they can add and remove fields from a working template much faster than designing one from zero.
Do I need to pay for Notion to use FreelanceOS? ▼
No. Both FreelanceOS Lite and Pro work on the Notion free plan. The free plan supports all the database features, views, and linking that FreelanceOS uses. You would only need a paid Notion plan if you want to add team members with edit access (Notion Plus) or if you exceed the free plan's block limits (unlikely for most freelancers).
Can I customize the template after duplicating it? ▼
Yes — it's just Notion, so you can modify anything. Add fields, change views, rename databases, delete sections you don't use, and add new pages. The template is a starting point with a proven structure, not a locked system. Most freelancers customize their copy within the first few days of using it as they figure out their specific workflow.
I'm a freelancer who also does content creation. Which template is right for me? ▼
If client work is your main income source, start with FreelanceOS — it's purpose-built for managing clients, projects, invoices, and freelance revenue. If content creation and brand deals are your primary business, CreatorHQ is the better fit. If you do a mix of both at significant scale, the Pro versions of each are $29 each — many creators start with one and add the other when needed.
How is FreelanceOS different from a project management tool like Asana or Monday? ▼
Asana, Monday, and similar tools are great for project management but they don't handle the financial side of a freelance business — no invoice tracking, no revenue dashboards, no client CRM with deal stages. FreelanceOS combines project management with business management in a single Notion workspace. You also own the data entirely, with no recurring SaaS subscription required beyond Notion itself (which is free).
What makes Notion better than a dedicated CRM like HubSpot? ▼
For freelancers, a dedicated CRM like HubSpot is usually overkill — it's built for sales teams with complex pipelines, not for individual freelancers with 5–30 clients. HubSpot's free plan also has significant limitations, and the paid plans are expensive. Notion lets you build a CRM that's exactly as simple or complex as you need, connected to your project management and invoicing in a single workspace. You're not paying for features you'll never use.
Do I get updates when FreelanceOS is improved? ▼
FreelanceOS Pro includes lifetime updates. When we release improvements, new templates, or new modules, Pro users get access to them. Because Notion templates are duplicated to your workspace, updates work by sharing the updated template — you choose when to incorporate changes so nothing is forced on you mid-project.
