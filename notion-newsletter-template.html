How It Works

Plan → Draft → Publish & Track

Three clear steps replace the chaos of "I should write a newsletter this week" with a repeatable system you can run on autopilot.

1

Plan Your Editorial Calendar

Every Monday, spend five minutes reviewing your Ideas Inbox, choose topics for the next two to four issues, and drop them onto your Editorial Calendar with publish dates. Your pipeline is always visible and your future self is always prepared.

2

Draft With Structure & Momentum

Open an Issue Template, pull in your chosen topic from the Ideas Inbox, and write inside a pre-built structure — headline, hook, body, CTA. No blank page paralysis. Move the issue from Draft to Ready when writing is done and editing is signed off.

3

Publish, Track & Improve

After you send on Substack, Beehiiv, or ConvertKit, log your open rate, clicks, and list growth in Subscriber Metrics. The issue moves to Published and auto-archives. Next week you start with better data, clearer patterns, and a stronger newsletter.
