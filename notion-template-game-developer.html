The Problem

Your Game Dev Workflow Lives in 10 Different Places. None of Them Talk to Each Other.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Game Developers

How do game developers use Notion?

Indie developers and solo studios use Notion as their game development headquarters — a GDD workspace linked to a sprint backlog, a bug tracker, a playtesting log, and a release checklist all in one place. Unlike Jira or Trello, Notion lets you combine the narrative design doc with the technical task board so your entire project lives in one connected system. FreelanceOS Pro gives you a pre-built version of this setup ready to use in under an hour.

Can Notion replace Jira for indie game dev?

For solo developers and small indie teams (1–5 people), Notion is usually a better fit than Jira. Jira is built for large engineering organisations and carries significant setup overhead. Notion's Kanban boards, linked databases, and flexible views handle sprint backlogs, bug queues, and release checklists effectively without requiring a project manager to configure and maintain the system. For teams of 10+ with dedicated producers, Jira or Shotgrid may offer advantages Notion can't match.

How do I write a game design document in Notion?

The most effective GDD structure in Notion separates the document into linked databases rather than one long page. Core mechanics, story beats, art direction, and platform requirements each live in their own section, linked to relevant feature cards in your sprint backlog. This means when a mechanic changes, you update one record and every linked feature reflects the change. FreelanceOS Pro includes a pre-built GDD workspace with this relational structure already set up.

How do I track bugs in Notion?

Create a database where each record is a bug report with fields for: title, severity (Critical/High/Medium/Low), affected platform, current status (Open/In Progress/Fixed/Won't Fix), steps to reproduce, and a video or screenshot attachment. Sort by severity to work on the most important bugs first. Filter by platform to run targeted regression tests. FreelanceOS Pro includes a pre-built bug tracker with all these fields and a priority-sorted default view.

Is Notion good for solo game developers?

Notion is arguably better for solo developers than for teams. When you're the only person who needs to understand the system, you can optimise it entirely for your own workflow without negotiating with other team members. Solo developers benefit most from the GDD-to-backlog link (keeps design and development in sync), the playtesting feedback log (structured solo playtesting discipline), and the release milestone checklist (prevents the chaotic last-week-before-launch scramble).

How do I manage game assets in Notion?

Use a Notion database as your asset registry — not to store assets (those stay in your file system, Google Drive, or Perforce) but to catalog them. Each record has: asset name, type (sprite/audio/3D model/UI), source (original/purchased/free), licence, file location link, version status (draft/final/placeholder), and notes. This gives you a searchable inventory without moving your actual files. FreelanceOS Pro's asset inventory is pre-built with these fields.

Can I use Notion for playtesting sessions?

Yes. Set up a Notion database for playtesting sessions with fields for date, testers, session type, key observations, and linked bug reports from issues found. You can use a Notion form to capture feedback during or after sessions — testers fill in the form and responses flow directly into your database. This is dramatically better than collecting feedback in Discord or Google Forms that aren't connected to your bug tracker.

What's the best Notion template for game development sprints?

The most effective sprint setup in Notion uses a Kanban board view of your feature backlog with columns for Idea, In Dev, Testing, and Done, filtered to show only the current sprint's tasks. Pair it with a milestone tracker that links sprint completion to your alpha/beta/launch gates. FreelanceOS Pro is built around this structure — the sprint backlog, milestone tracker, and bug queue are all linked so completing a sprint task can automatically surface the next milestone gap.
