The Solution

A Complete Real Estate Development OS in Notion

FreelanceOS Pro for Real Estate Developers connects your project tracker, deal pipeline, cost log, contractor directory, portfolio dashboard, and finance notes in one relational workspace built for independent developers managing multiple projects and deals simultaneously.

🏗️ Development Project Tracker — Budget, ARV, Status, and Projected Profit

Every development project gets a structured record: property address, project type (fix-and-flip, new construction, BRRR, commercial, buy-and-hold), acquisition cost, after-repair value estimate, renovation budget, actual costs to date, target completion date, projected net profit, and current status (Acquiring, In Development, Listing, Sold, Refinancing). The filtered dashboard view shows all active projects with budget vs. actual variance visible at a glance so you always know which projects are on budget and which need immediate attention. Navigate to any project's full detail including the line-item cost log in two clicks.

💼 Deal Pipeline — Evaluation, Due Diligence, and Acquisition Tracking

Every property under consideration gets a deal record: address, deal source (MLS, wholesaler, off-market, auction, direct mail), asking price, estimated repair cost, offer made status and offer price, due diligence stage, earnest money amount, target close date, your decision to pursue or pass, and deal notes. The pipeline view shows all active deals side by side so you can compare opportunities, prioritize follow-ups, and never let a promising property fall through the cracks during a busy period. Filter by deal source to see which channels produce the most viable opportunities in your market.

💰 Project Cost & Budget Log — Line-Item Budget vs. Actual by Trade

Per-project cost tracking at the line-item level organized by cost category: acquisition cost, carrying costs (property taxes, insurance, utilities, HOA, financing interest), hard costs by trade (demolition, foundation, framing, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, drywall, insulation, roofing, windows, flooring, cabinets, countertops, paint, landscaping), and soft costs (permits, architect, engineer, survey, closing costs). Each line item has a budgeted amount and an actual amount so you can calculate variance in real time as invoices come in. The project cost log is the single most important tool for protecting margins on development projects — most overruns are caught too late because developers are not tracking line by line.

🔨 Contractor & Vendor Directory — Rates, Ratings, and Trade Reference

A searchable contractor database organized by trade — one record per contractor with their business name, contact info, license number, insurance status and expiry, typical rates and payment terms, reliability rating (1-5), quality of work rating (1-5), projects you have worked on together, and detailed notes on strengths and weaknesses. Filter by trade to find your best plumber, electrician, or tile setter in seconds for any new project. The directory accumulates institutional knowledge across every project you run — a competitive advantage that compounds over time and eliminates the expensive mistake of hiring unknown contractors without a reference history.

📊 Portfolio Dashboard — Equity, Cash Flow, and Active Project Overview

A portfolio-level dashboard view covering: total properties owned, in development, and sold this year; estimated portfolio equity across all current holdings; active project count with status summary; annualized return calculations by completed project; monthly cash flow across any rental units in the portfolio; and financing maturity dates sorted by urgency so you always see the nearest balloon payment or hard money maturity first. The portfolio dashboard is what moves you from managing individual deals reactively to managing a property portfolio strategically — with enough visibility to plan capital deployment, timing of sales, and refinancing decisions ahead of deadlines rather than at them.

🏦 Finance & Lending Reference — Deal-by-Deal Financing Notes

Per-deal financing reference notes for your own organizational use: lender name, loan type (hard money, conventional, bridge, DSCR, private), interest rate, LTV, draw schedule notes, loan maturity date, personal guarantee noted, and balloon payment amount and date. This is your personal organizational reference — not a formal underwriting or loan tracking system. Having all financing details in one place means you can quickly assess which deals have hard money maturing in the next 90 days, which are eligible for conventional refinancing, and what your total debt service obligation across the portfolio looks like at any given time.

How does the development project tracker work?

The filtered dashboard view shows all active projects at once with budget vs. actual variance visible so you always know which projects are on track and which need attention. How does the deal pipeline help me evaluate new properties?

The deal pipeline tracks every property under consideration with address, deal source, asking price, estimated repairs, offer status, due diligence stage, and your pursue or pass decision. Having all active deals in a single view means you can compare opportunities side by side, prioritize follow-ups, and never lose track of a promising property during a busy period. How does the contractor directory work across projects?

The contractor directory stores a profile for every GC and sub you work with — trade, contact, license, insurance status, typical rates, payment terms, and a reliability and quality rating from past projects. Filter by trade to find your best electrician or tile installer in seconds when starting a new project. The directory accumulates expertise across every project, turning contractor knowledge from an asset that lives in your head into one that lives in a searchable system that compounds over time.

How does the cost log track budget vs. actual?

The project cost and budget log breaks every project into line items by cost category — acquisition, carrying costs, hard costs by trade, and soft costs. Each line item has a budget amount and an actual amount you update as invoices arrive. You can see your variance in real time at the line level so you catch overruns while there is still time to make adjustments — rather than discovering at project completion that you missed your margin target by 12%.
