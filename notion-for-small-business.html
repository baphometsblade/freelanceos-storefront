Setup Guide

Building Your Notion Business OS: 7 Core Databases

These 7 databases, properly linked, form a complete small business operating system. Each database is a full Notion database with related properties, views, and filters. FreelanceOS Pro includes all 7 pre-built and pre-connected.

1

Team Directory

Properties: Name, Role, Department, Start Date, Skills (multi-select), Email, Slack Handle, Status (Active/On Leave/Alumni). One record per team member. Linked to Tasks (assignee), SOPs (owner), and Projects (team member). This becomes your single source of truth for who does what at your company.

2

Client CRM

Properties: Client Name, Industry (select), Status (Active/Past/Prospect/Partner), ARR (number), Primary Contact (text), Account Manager (relation to Team Directory), Linked Projects (relation to Projects). One record per client. Add a formula for “months active” and a rollup for total billed from the invoice log. Filter by status for different pipeline views.

3

Project Tracker

Properties: Project Name, Client (relation to CRM), Type (Retainer/Fixed Project/Internal/R&D), Status (Planning/Active/Review/Delivered/On Hold), Start Date, End Date, Budget (number), Team Members (relation to Team Directory), Linked Tasks (relation to Tasks). Board view by Status is your sprint board. Table view with client filter is your per-client project history.

4

Tasks Database

Properties: Task Name, Linked Project (relation), Assignee (relation to Team Directory), Due Date, Priority (High/Medium/Low/Urgent), Status (Not Started/In Progress/Blocked/Done), Time Estimate (number, hours), Notes. Each person can filter to “My Tasks” as their personal to-do list. Board view by Assignee is your team workload overview.

5

SOP Library

Properties: Process Name, Department (select), Version (number), Last Reviewed (date), Owner (relation to Team Directory), Status (Draft/Active/Archived), Linked Roles (multi-select), Steps (page content). Gallery view organized by department. Linked to Onboarding Checklist so new team members see only the SOPs relevant to their role.

6

Revenue & Invoice Log

Properties: Invoice Number, Client (relation to CRM), Amount (number), Date Issued, Due Date, Status (Unpaid/Paid/Overdue/Partial), Payment Method (select), Notes. Rollup to Client CRM shows total revenue per client. Monthly grouped view shows P&L by month. Filter by “Overdue” status for accounts receivable follow-up. Export periodically for your accountant.

7

Hiring Tracker

Properties: Role Title, Department, Status (Planning/Actively Hiring/On Hold/Closed), Salary Range, Target Start Date, Candidates (sub-pages or linked database), Hiring Manager (relation to Team Directory), Job Description (page content). Each open role has a Kanban view of candidates by interview stage. Archived roles become institutional knowledge for future hires.

Tool Comparison

Notion vs Other Small Business Tools: Honest Comparison

The businesses that succeed with Notion are those that invest one weekend in building a custom system rather than adopting a rigid tool’s opinionated structure. Here’s an honest breakdown of when Notion wins and when it doesn’t.

Notion vs Monday.com

Monday.com is better when: Your workflow is primarily visual project tracking with heavy automation. Monday.com’s native automations (if status changes to Done, notify the next person in the workflow), Gantt charts, and workload views are more polished out of the box. If project tracking is 90% of what you need, Monday.com onboards faster.

Notion is better when: You need docs, wiki, and knowledge management alongside your projects. When you want full customization without paying for feature tiers. When you’re a team of 2–15 people where tool cost is meaningful ($12–$22/user/month vs $15/user/month with much more functionality). When your team does a mix of project management, client communication, SOP documentation, and internal knowledge-sharing — Notion handles all of it in one place.

Notion vs ClickUp

ClickUp is better when: You need native time tracking, goals, and project automations. ClickUp has more built-in project management features — time tracking, OKR goals, Gantt dependencies, and native automations — than Notion. For teams that live in detailed project management workflows, ClickUp offers more out of the box.

Notion is better when: Your team values integrated documentation and knowledge management as much as task management. ClickUp’s docs feature is less capable than Notion’s native page and database system. Notion also wins on UI simplicity — ClickUp’s feature density can be overwhelming for small teams who just want a clean, flexible workspace. If you regularly write proposals, SOPs, client-facing docs, and internal guides alongside your project work, Notion is the better home.

Notion vs Asana

Asana is better when: Your primary need is sophisticated project management with Gantt charts, task dependencies, portfolio management, and native reporting. Asana is purpose-built PM software with a more mature, polished project management experience and better native collaboration features for task-heavy workflows.

Notion is better when: You need docs and knowledge alongside project tracking. Many teams that use Asana also need Confluence or Google Docs for documentation — Notion replaces both tools in one workspace, significantly simplifying your tool stack and eliminating the context-switching between your project tracker and your wiki.

Notion vs HubSpot Free CRM

HubSpot Free CRM is better when: Your primary need is a dedicated CRM with email tracking, deal pipeline management, meeting scheduling integration, and marketing automation. HubSpot’s free CRM is genuinely excellent as a pure CRM — it tracks email opens, syncs with your inbox, and manages contacts with features no Notion CRM can match.

Notion is better when: You also need projects, docs, SOPs, and internal knowledge linked to your client records. A HubSpot CRM plus Notion docs plus a separate project tracker is three tools with three logins. A Notion CRM is less powerful as a pure CRM but wins when you want your client record to directly link to their projects, invoices, meeting notes, and shared deliverables.

Notion vs Confluence + Jira

Confluence + Jira is better when: You’re an engineering-led team at 25+ people that needs enterprise-grade ticketing, sprints, story points, and deep development workflow integrations. Confluence + Jira is the gold standard for software engineering teams at scale.

Notion is better when: You’re a sub-25-person company that doesn’t want Atlassian’s complexity, cost (Jira Standard $8.15/user/month + Confluence Standard $5.75/user/month = $13.90/user/month for a 10-person team), and administrative overhead. The vast majority of small businesses — including early-stage software startups — are better served by a well-configured Notion workspace than by the Atlassian suite.

Bottom line: The businesses that succeed with Notion are those willing to invest one focused weekend in building a custom system that fits exactly how they work — rather than adopting a rigid tool’s opinionated structure. The long-term ROI of a well-built Notion OS is significant: team members actually use it because it was built for their workflow, not a generic workflow a software company decided was “best practice.”

Frequently Asked Questions

Notion for Small Business: FAQ

Is Notion good for small businesses?
Yes — Notion is one of the most popular small business operating systems in 2026. Its flexibility lets you build exactly the system your business needs: CRM, project tracker, SOP library, HR database, financial dashboard, and team wiki all in one workspace. The cost savings compared to running separate tools are significant, especially for teams of 2–15 people. The main trade-off is that Notion requires an upfront investment of time to configure — purpose-built tools like Asana or HubSpot are faster to start using out of the box but charge more and keep you locked into their opinionated structure.
Can Notion replace project management software for small teams?
Yes, for most small teams. Notion has database views (Board/Kanban, Table, Calendar, Timeline/Gantt, Gallery) that cover the core project management use cases. It lacks native time tracking, advanced automations, and dependency management that tools like Asana or ClickUp offer. But for teams that primarily need task assignment, status tracking, client deliverable visibility, and linked documentation — Notion handles all of this in one place. Many 2–20 person teams find Notion’s all-in-one approach more practical than paying for separate PM software plus a wiki tool.
How do I set up a CRM in Notion for my small business?
Create a Contacts/Clients database (name, company, email, phone, status: lead/prospect/active client/past client, ARR, account manager linked to Team Directory) and a Deals database (deal name, linked client, stage: discovery/proposal/negotiation/closed-won/closed-lost, value, close date, linked project). Connect them with Relation properties. Add a linked database view on your main dashboard filtered to “Active Deals” sorted by close date for a live pipeline view. FreelanceOS Pro includes a pre-built Notion CRM template with all these databases already connected and pre-populated with example records.
What is Notion Business Plan and is it worth it for small teams?
Notion Business ($15/user/month, $12/user/month billed annually) adds unlimited file uploads, advanced permission controls, private teamspaces, PDF exports, and SAML SSO. For most small teams of 2–10 people, the free plan or Notion Plus ($8/user/month) is sufficient to start. Business plan makes sense when you need advanced permission controls (restricting team members from editing certain sensitive databases), SAML SSO for security compliance, or you’re hitting file storage limits. A 5-person team on Business pays $60/month — still significantly cheaper than running Asana + Confluence + a basic CRM separately.
Can Notion replace Monday.com?
For many small businesses, yes. Notion can replicate Monday.com’s Board, Table, and Timeline views with its database system. What Monday.com does better: native automations (if status changes to Done, notify the assignee), built-in time tracking, and a more polished out-of-box project management experience with less setup required. What Notion does better: integrated docs and wiki alongside your projects, full flexibility to build custom systems, significantly lower cost for larger teams, and no feature-tier paywalls. Teams that primarily use Monday.com for visual task boards without heavy automations often find Notion works equally well at a fraction of the cost.
How do small businesses use Notion for SOPs?
Create an SOP Library database with properties: Process Name, Department, Version, Last Reviewed (date), Owner (relation to Team Directory), Status (Draft/Active/Archived). Each SOP is a full Notion page with structured content: Purpose, When to Use, Step-by-Step Process, Tools Required, Common Mistakes to Avoid, and Related Processes. Link your SOP Library to your Team Directory (each SOP has an assigned owner) and to your Onboarding Checklist (new employees read the SOPs relevant to their role). This creates a living documentation system that improves continuously as your team adds institutional knowledge over time.
Is Notion free for small businesses?
Notion has a free plan that covers a single user with unlimited pages and blocks. For teams, the Plus plan ($8/user/month billed annually) adds unlimited file uploads, version history (90-day), and guest invites — this is the most popular plan for small businesses. The free plan’s main limitation for teams is the cap on guest invites and file sizes. Most 1–3 person businesses run comfortably on the free plan; teams of 4+ people typically need Plus. FreelanceOS Pro templates work fully on the free Notion plan for individual users or very small teams.
