Grant Management

The Full Grant Lifecycle — Tracked From First Lead to Final Report

Grant management is where many nonprofits feel the most pain. Deadlines missed. Relationships not nurtured. Reporting requirements forgotten. The Grant Tracker module covers every stage of the lifecycle so nothing falls through.

🔍
Stage 1 — Prospect Research
Identify & Qualify Funders

Log foundations, government agencies, and corporate giving programs you're researching. Capture eligibility criteria, typical grant size, application windows, and your initial assessment of fit. Link each prospect to your Donor CRM for relationship tracking.

📝
Stage 2 — LOI & Pre-Application
Letter of Inquiry Tracking

Track Letters of Inquiry separately from full proposals. Note the submission date, LOI requirements, funder response deadline, and your relationship contact. The LOI stage filters help you see what's pending a response before you invest time in a full application.

📤
Stage 3 — Application
Proposal Tracker with Deadline Alerts

Each grant application gets its own record with submission deadline, requested amount, assigned staff writer, required attachments checklist, and internal review status. A Notion formula field calculates days remaining so you can prioritize the grant calendar view by urgency.

🏆
Stage 4 — Award & Acceptance
Award Notifications & Grant Agreements

Log award amounts, grant periods, allowable use restrictions, and grant agreement notes. Link awarded grants to the programs they fund in the Program Tracker. A single view shows all active grants, their total funding, and their end dates — critical for budget planning.

📊
Stage 5 — Reporting
Reporting Deadlines & Deliverables

Track interim and final report deadlines for every active grant. Link reporting requirements to your Program Impact Tracker so the outcomes data you need is already collected. Never scramble for numbers at report time — they've been building all year.

📈
Ongoing — Analytics
Success Rate & Relationship Intelligence

A gallery view of your grant history shows approval rates by funder type, average grant size, and year-over-year trends. Funder relationship notes capture conversations, site visit prep, and cultivation history. This is the institutional knowledge that typically lives only in the grants manager's head.

Grant Calendar View

The Grant Tracker includes a calendar view filtered to show all upcoming deadlines — application deadlines, reporting due dates, and LOI response windows — in a single monthly grid. Your entire grant calendar, visible in 30 seconds.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before setting up Notion for your nonprofit.

Is Notion free for nonprofits?
Yes. Notion offers a free plan that supports all the core features needed to run a nonprofit workspace — databases, linked views, templates, and sharing with guests. Notion also has a nonprofit discount program for registered 501(c)(3) organizations that can significantly reduce or eliminate the per-member cost on paid plans. FreelanceOS Pro is a one-time $29 template that works on both free and paid Notion plans with no ongoing subscription required from us.
Can I track donations and donor relationships in Notion?
Yes. The Donor & Funder CRM module lets you log individual and corporate donors, track gift amounts and dates, note communication history, set follow-up reminders, and segment your donor base by giving level or engagement. Notion isn't a payment processor, so it works as a relationship management and tracking layer alongside your donation platform — Stripe, PayPal Giving Fund, Donorbox, or any other tool you use to accept online gifts.
How do I manage multiple programs in Notion?
The Program & Impact Tracker module includes a program database where each program is its own record with linked beneficiary data, KPIs, staff leads, budget notes, funder connections, and outcome tracking. You can filter and group by program status, funder, or impact area. Board members and staff can view a high-level dashboard or drill into individual program pages. This replaces scattered spreadsheets and gives everyone a single source of truth.
Can volunteers access the workspace?
Yes. Notion lets you share specific pages with guests at no extra cost on most plans. You can give volunteers view or comment access to their scheduling pages, training materials, and shift sign-up boards without exposing sensitive donor or financial data. The Volunteer Database module includes a public-friendly onboarding page template you can share directly with new volunteers as part of their orientation process.
Is this Notion nonprofit template HIPAA-compliant?
Notion is not HIPAA-compliant and does not sign Business Associate Agreements (BAAs). If your nonprofit handles protected health information (PHI) — for example, a healthcare nonprofit, community health center, or social services organization that stores medical data — you should not store PHI in Notion. This template is suitable for program data, volunteer records, donor relationships, grant tracking, and organizational operations that do not involve PHI. If you need HIPAA-compliant document storage, Notion can still serve as your management hub while PHI lives in a compliant system.
How do I share reports with the board?
The Board Meeting Hub module includes a shareable board portal page that you can give board members view access to. It contains the current and past meeting agendas, minutes archive, action item tracker, and program dashboards. You can also export individual Notion pages as PDFs for email distribution or printing for formal board packets. For annual reporting, the Annual Report Data Collection module helps you gather all impact data in one place before you format or export it for external publication.
