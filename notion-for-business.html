Setup Guide

Business Notion Setup: 5 Core Databases

These five databases form the backbone of a Notion business OS. Once linked, everything flows together — tasks to projects, projects to clients, meetings to actions.

1

Projects Database

Linked to tasks — every project has its own filtered view, team, status, client, and deadline. The master hub your whole team references daily. Add a gallery view for visual project boards or a timeline view for deadline planning.

2

Team Tasks Database

A universal task board — filtered by project, person, due date, and priority. Each task is linked back to its parent project. Build personal filtered views for each team member so everyone sees only what's theirs.

3

Client & Deals CRM

Contact info, deal stage, ARR, communication history, and next action — all linked to the active projects for that client. Replace your CRM subscription with a custom Notion view that fits your exact sales process.

4

Company Wiki

SOPs, policies, playbooks, and how-to guides — permission-controlled by department. Your entire team's knowledge lives here, searchable and always up to date. Structure by department: Operations, Sales, Marketing, Product, HR.

5

Meeting Notes Database

Date, attendees, decisions, and action items — auto-linked to tasks and projects. Every meeting generates accountable to-dos that appear in the right person's task board automatically.

When Notion Is the Right Choice for Business

Notion works great for: small teams where people wear multiple hats, knowledge-heavy work (consulting, agencies, content teams), startups moving fast and iterating on processes, businesses that want one tool instead of five subscriptions.

Notion is less suited for: large enterprise PMOs that need complex task dependencies, time-tracking, and Gantt charts across hundreds of people; teams in heavily regulated industries with strict data residency needs; engineering teams deeply embedded in Jira workflows with CI/CD integrations.

The math is clear: Notion's Plus plan at $10/user/month replaces what would otherwise cost $11 (Asana Starter) + $5 (Confluence) + a CRM subscription. For a 10-person team, that's typically $2,000–4,000/year in savings — plus the productivity gain of having everything in one searchable workspace.

Notion for Remote and Distributed Teams

Remote work exposed a fundamental problem with most business tool stacks: information is scattered across Slack, email, Google Drive, and three different PM tools, with no single source of truth. Notion solves this by making one workspace the canonical home for all business knowledge.

Async-first teams thrive in Notion. Instead of scheduling a meeting to share an update, team members write it in Notion — where it is searchable, timestamped, and linked to the relevant project. Leadership can see the status of every initiative without anyone having to report it in a sync.

Teams that adopt Notion as their central operating system consistently report up to 30% fewer meetings. When your SOPs, project status, meeting decisions, and team tasks all live in one place — and everyone knows where to look — the "quick sync to get everyone on the same page" becomes unnecessary.

Transparent decision-making is another remote-team superpower. In Notion, decisions live on pages with context: why the decision was made, who was involved, and what alternatives were considered. New team members can read through the decision log and understand how the company thinks, without needing someone to brief them.

Remote team tip: Create a "Company Updates" page in your Notion wiki and link it from your team homepage. Weekly updates, decision logs, and company announcements in one place means your distributed team never feels out of the loop — no matter what time zone they're in.

Frequently Asked Questions: Notion for Business

Can Notion be used for business management?
Yes — Notion is one of the most versatile business management tools available. Small businesses and startups use it for project management, team wikis, CRM, meeting notes, HR processes, and executive dashboards. Its linked database system lets you build a fully connected business operating system for a fraction of the cost of separate tools.
What size business is Notion best for?
Notion works best for businesses with 1–100 people. Solopreneurs use it as a personal OS, small teams (2–20) use it for project and knowledge management, and agencies use it for client project delivery. At enterprise scale (500+ people), more purpose-built tools with advanced permission and compliance controls are often needed.
Can Notion replace Asana or ClickUp for a small team?
For most small teams (under 20 people), yes. Notion handles tasks, projects, timelines, and team collaboration. It lacks some advanced automation and Gantt features that heavy PM shops need, but for the majority of small business workflows it is more than sufficient — and the added benefit of having wikis, docs, and databases in the same tool is significant.
Is Notion secure enough for business data?
Notion uses AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS in transit. The Business plan adds SAML SSO, advanced permissions, and audit logs. It is SOC 2 Type II compliant. For most small businesses and startups, Notion's security is more than sufficient. Highly regulated industries (healthcare, finance) should consult their compliance requirements before storing sensitive data.
Can I build a CRM in Notion?
Absolutely. A Notion CRM is one of the most popular business use cases. You can build a contacts database with deal stage, ARR, communication history, next action, and linked projects. Many small businesses and agencies save thousands per year by replacing $300–500/month CRM tools with a custom Notion CRM that fits their exact workflow.
Does Notion work for remote teams?
Notion was built with async, remote collaboration in mind. Unlimited team members can co-edit pages, comment, and leave mentions in real time. As a single source of truth for remote teams, it dramatically reduces meeting time — teams report up to 30% fewer meetings once their SOPs, meeting notes, and project updates are all in one searchable Notion workspace.
What is the best Notion plan for businesses?
The Notion Plus plan ($10/user/month) is ideal for most small businesses — it unlocks unlimited pages, unlimited file uploads, full database access, and version history. The Business plan ($15/user/month) adds SAML SSO, advanced analytics, and audit logs for teams that need them. Both are significantly cheaper than equivalent combinations of PM tools, wikis, and CRMs.
