Why Agencies Are Moving to Notion

The average boutique agency runs on five to eight separate tools: a CRM for business development, a project management platform for delivery, a separate docs tool for SOPs and wikis, a client portal for stakeholder communication, a time-tracking tool for retainer management, and a spreadsheet for revenue forecasting. Each subscription adds cost and context-switching overhead; critical client information ends up scattered across multiple platforms.

Notion replaces most of that stack. Your business development pipeline, active project boards, SOP library, client portals, retainer tracker, and revenue dashboard can all live in one interconnected workspace. When a prospect converts to a client, you move their record forward without any data migration. When a project kicks off, the client brief is already linked to the project task board. When it's time for a retainer renewal conversation, your full client history, delivered value, and upcoming scope are all in the same place.

The public sharing feature is particularly transformative for agency client relationships. Instead of sending status updates via email or maintaining a separate client-facing tool, you share a Notion page directly with clients. They see live project status, deliverable files, meeting notes, and upcoming milestones — without accessing your internal workspace. Clients consistently report that this transparency improves their satisfaction and reduces the volume of "what's the status?" emails.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can agencies use Notion instead of Asana or Monday.com?
For boutique agencies and small to mid-size teams (1–15 people), Notion can fully replace Asana or Monday.com for project management while also handling documentation, wikis, SOPs, and client portals in the same workspace. Large agencies with complex approval workflows, resource scheduling across 50+ team members, or deep integrations with ad platforms may find dedicated PM tools more appropriate. The key advantage of Notion is consolidation: projects, docs, client notes, and SOPs all in one place at lower cost.
How do agencies use Notion for client management?
Agencies build a Client database in Notion with properties for contract type, retainer amount, billing cycle, primary contact, assigned account manager, and renewal date. Each client entry links to all active and historical projects. For client-facing communication, agencies share specific Notion pages as client portals — clients see project status, deliverable files, and meeting notes without accessing the full internal workspace. This replaces the need for a separate client portal software subscription.
Is Notion good for tracking agency retainers?
Yes. A Retainer Tracker database with properties for client, monthly value, renewal date, service scope, and status (active / at risk / churned) gives agencies a clear view of recurring revenue. Notion's rollup formula property can calculate total MRR from all active retainer entries automatically. Renewal date reminders can be set on database entries. Many agencies also track retainer hours in a separate log to ensure service delivery stays profitable relative to the monthly fee.
How does Notion compare to ClickUp for agencies?
ClickUp has more built-in task management features — native time tracking, goal tracking, workload charts, and more native integrations with design tools and communication platforms. Notion has significantly better documentation, wikis, and client-facing pages. Agencies that prioritize task management power and reporting often prefer ClickUp; agencies that need strong internal documentation, SOPs, and low-friction client portals lean toward Notion. Many agencies find the sweet spot is using ClickUp for task tracking and Notion for documentation and client-facing communication.
Can Notion replace an agency CRM?
For boutique agencies doing mostly inbound or referral business, Notion functions effectively as a lightweight CRM — tracking leads, proposals, contract stages, and full client history. For agencies with active outbound sales requiring email sequence automation, call logging, or advanced pipeline forecasting with team quotas, a dedicated CRM (HubSpot, Pipedrive) is more appropriate. The advantage of Notion as a CRM is that client records link directly into the project workspace with no handoff friction — the moment a lead converts, their project workspace is already connected.
What agency types use Notion?
Notion is most popular among digital marketing agencies, SEO agencies, content production agencies, web design and development studios, social media agencies, PR agencies, creative studios, video production companies, and brand strategy consultancies. It is particularly well-suited for boutique agencies (1–15 people) and solopreneur agency owners who want one workspace for their entire operation rather than a complex multi-tool stack.
