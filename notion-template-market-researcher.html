Frequently Asked Questions

Is this Notion template built specifically for market researchers and consumer insights consultants?
Yes — every database is designed around the market researcher's workflow: managing a pipeline of quantitative, qualitative, and mixed-method research projects across multiple clients and categories, maintaining a reusable questionnaire and discussion guide library, tracking fieldwork operations and data quality, synthesizing insights into client-ready deliverables, and running the business operations of an independent research practice. It works for online surveys, focus groups, in-depth interviews, ethnography, conjoint analysis, brand tracking, concept testing, NPD research, and secondary research.
Does this cover both quantitative and qualitative research methods?
Yes. The Research Project Tracker, Methodology & Analysis Notes, and Questionnaire & Discussion Guide Library all distinguish between quantitative (online survey/telephone survey/conjoint/MaxDiff/TURF/cluster analysis/A/B test analysis), qualitative (focus group/IDI/ethnography/online community/shop-along), and mixed-method approaches. The Methodology & Analysis Notes includes your statistical software toolkit (SPSS/R/Python/Q Research/Displayr) alongside qualitative analysis approaches (thematic analysis/grounded theory/framework analysis/axial coding).
How does the questionnaire and discussion guide library work?
The Questionnaire & Discussion Guide Library stores your reusable research instrument components — screener questions, warm-up sections, main batteries, classification questions, and qualitative projective techniques — organized by topic area and platform. Build it up over engagements and reuse tested components across clients instead of writing every instrument from scratch. Over time it becomes one of your most valuable intellectual property assets as an independent researcher.
How does the fieldwork and data quality tracking work?
The Fieldwork & Recruitment Tracker logs recruitment method, screening criteria summary, panel provider, recruitment status (n recruited vs. target), fieldwork dates, data collection platform, and data quality checks performed (speeder removal, straight-liner detection, attention check pass rate, open-ended quality review). Tracking the clean sample size after quality removal is essential — it affects your effective n for analysis and reporting, and clients increasingly ask about data quality protocols as panel quality challenges have grown.
