What's Inside

Every System a Mediation Practice Needs

From case intake and session logging to referral network management and practice dashboards — FreelanceOS Pro is built for how professional mediators actually manage caseloads and run a sustainable practice.

📁 Case Pipeline & Intake Overview (Administrative)

Case reference number (avoid full party names in a non-secure system), case type (family: divorce/custody/parenting plan/elder care; commercial: contract dispute/business partnership/real estate; workplace: employment dispute/harassment complaint/team conflict; community: neighbor/HOA; ODR; environmental/public policy), referral source (court-referred/attorney referral/self-referred/mediation panel/organizational partner), parties count, attorneys involved, case status (inquiry/intake completed/sessions scheduled/in mediation/agreement reached/impasse/withdrawn), estimated total sessions, intake date, and target resolution date. Your entire administrative case pipeline at a glance — filterable by case type, referral source, and status.

📅 Session & Scheduling Log (Administrative)

A billing and administrative record for each session — not a record of mediation content: case reference, session date, session format (in-person/videoconference/phone/shuttle diplomacy), duration, session number in case, parties present by role (Party A/Party B/attorneys — not names in a general tool), mediator fee for session, payment received, and administrative outcome (session ongoing/continued/impasse reached/agreement reached/cooling-off period). Your administrative log for invoicing and practice analytics — designed to track what is billable without storing anything from the confidential mediation process itself.

📋 Agreement Tracking (Administrative)

Per case: type of agreement reached (full settlement/partial settlement/parenting plan/term sheet/MOU/impasse), date of agreement, number of issues resolved versus total issues, whether agreement was reduced to writing, whether parties signed same session or deferred, administrative follow-up scheduled, referral back to legal counsel for drafting required, and court filing required (for family matters). Track which cases resolve and which reach impasse — your closure rate and resolution rate by case type are the most important quality metrics in a mediation practice, and they only exist if you track them consistently.

🤝 Referring Attorney & Professional Network

Your referral partner ecosystem: attorney or organization name, type (family law attorney/employment attorney/commercial litigation attorney/HR department/court ADR program/insurance company/mediation panel/community organization), number of referrals received from this source to date, last referral date, relationship warmth (cold/active/champion), cultivation activity (lunch scheduled/CLE presentation/newsletter/webinar), and specialty focus relevant to your practice. Mediation practices grow almost entirely through referral relationships — this database turns that reality into a manageable, data-informed system.

📚 Continuing Education & Certification Log

Certifications held (IMI Certified Mediator, FINRA Mediation Panelist, AAA Commercial Mediator Panel, CEDR Accredited Mediator, court-connected ADR panel accreditations by state and jurisdiction), continuing education requirement (hours per renewal cycle — most certification bodies require 20 to 40 hours per cycle), CE hours completed this cycle, trainings attended (advanced mediation skills/negotiation theory/subject matter expertise courses), conferences (ACR Annual Conference, IIAM, ABA Section on Dispute Resolution), peer review group participation, and co-mediation observations logged. Your professional standing record — the credential maintenance system that keeps your panel memberships and certifications current.

💼 Mediation Practice Dashboard

Active cases in pipeline, cases resolved this quarter, average sessions per resolved case, resolution rate (percentage of cases reaching agreement versus impasse), monthly revenue by case type, income by referral source, average case value, new referral sources added this year, continuing education hours year-to-date, certifying body renewal dates, and annual revenue goal. The complete operational and financial picture of your mediation practice — showing you not just how busy you are, but how effective and how profitable your practice is across each specialty area.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Professional Mediators

What does the mediation case pipeline track?

The case pipeline tracks your administrative and business information for each case: case reference number, case type (family, commercial, workplace, community, ODR), referral source, parties count, case status from inquiry through agreement or impasse, estimated sessions, intake date, and target resolution date. It is strictly an administrative business record — it does not store any information from the mediation sessions themselves, which is protected by confidentiality obligations in most jurisdictions. The design deliberately uses case reference numbers rather than party names in the main tracker to support appropriate data hygiene for a general-purpose business tool.

How does the session log protect mediation confidentiality?

The session log is designed as a billing and administrative record only. It tracks case reference (not party names), session date, format, duration, mediator fee, and administrative outcome (continued, impasse, agreement reached). It contains no party statements, mediator proposals, offers, concessions, or any content from the mediation itself. Mediation communications protected by privilege or confidentiality statutes must never be stored in any general-purpose tool — they belong in your secure, confidential mediation file maintained according to your professional obligations and jurisdiction requirements. FreelanceOS Pro handles the business side of your practice; your confidential mediation records require a separate, appropriately secure system.

Can I track different mediation specialties in the same workspace?

Yes. The case pipeline uses case type tags (family: divorce/custody/parenting plan/elder care; commercial: contract dispute/partnership/real estate; workplace: employment/harassment/team conflict; community: neighbor/HOA; ODR) so you can filter your entire caseload by specialty at any time. If you practice across family and commercial mediation, the dashboard shows revenue and resolution rates by case type separately — which is useful data for deciding where to focus your practice development and whether specializing more narrowly would improve your referral positioning and practice economics.

How does the referring attorney tracker help grow my practice?

The referring attorney and professional network tracker logs every referral relationship: attorney or organization name, type (family law attorney, employment attorney, commercial litigator, HR department, court ADR program, insurance company, mediation panel), number of referrals received, last referral date, relationship warmth, and cultivation activity. After 12 to 24 months of consistent logging, patterns emerge: which attorneys refer consistently, which referral sources produce the most complex high-value cases, and which relationships have gone cold and deserve re-engagement. Mediation practices grow almost entirely through referral networks — understanding the mechanics of your referral ecosystem turns relationship cultivation from a vague intention into a deliberate, data-informed practice.
