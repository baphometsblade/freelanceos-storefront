The Solution

A Complete Nutrition Practice OS in Notion

FreelanceOS Pro for Dietitians connects your client database, session notes reference, meal plan library, practice dashboard, referral source tracking, and CEU log in one organized workspace built for private practice and group nutrition professionals.

👤 Client Profile Database — Goals, Dietary Pattern, and Practice Reference

A searchable reference database for each client covering the information you need before every session: name or client ID, age, sex, primary health goal (weight management, sports performance, gut health, prenatal, chronic disease management), dietary pattern (omnivore, vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, Halal, Kosher), food intolerances and allergies, relevant medications, session frequency, referral source, start date, and session package purchased. Filter by goal to see all your weight management clients, or filter by referral source to see who your primary care physician partners are sending. This is a practice management reference — not a clinical record system.

📋 Session Notes Reference Log — Practice Management Documentation

A lightweight session reference log for your own practice management use — not clinical billing documentation. Fields include session date, client, session number, goals reviewed, key discussion points, recommendations made, behavior change commitment, and next session date. This is your practice-side working memory, distinct from any formal clinical notes in your EHR system. Review the log before each session to recall where you left off, what the client committed to, and what to address in the upcoming appointment — without digging through a separate system.

🍽️ Meal Plan & Resource Library — Organized by Condition and Goal

A centralized library of reusable meal plan frameworks organized by health goal: weight management, heart health, sports performance, GI health and low-FODMAP, prenatal, anti-inflammatory, and condition-specific eating patterns. Each entry includes the plan framework, modification notes for common dietary patterns and intolerances, a link to the full plan document in your preferred file storage, and notes on which client populations this plan works best for. Also includes your handout and educational resource library sorted by topic — the organized foundation for consistent, efficient client education across your entire caseload.

📈 Progress & Counseling Goal Tracker

Per-client qualitative goal tracking for your practice reference: behavior change goals set at intake, specific obstacles identified during counseling, milestone goals achieved session by session, and behavior change notes over time. This is designed for your counseling approach reference, not for tracking clinical measurements like weight or lab values — those belong in your compliant EHR. The goal tracker helps you remember where each client started, what they are working toward, and what counseling approaches have been most effective so every follow-up session moves the relationship forward purposefully.

💰 Practice Operations Dashboard — Caseload, Revenue, and Referral Sources

A business-level view of your practice: total active caseload, new inquiry pipeline with follow-up status, session type breakdown (individual, group, corporate wellness, telehealth), monthly revenue against goals, referral source breakdown by volume, and client retention rate notes. Tracking referral sources systematically reveals which relationships generate the most clients — primary care physicians, fitness studios, social media, word of mouth — so you can invest your relationship-building time strategically. The revenue dashboard helps you make informed decisions about capacity, pricing, and which program types to expand or retire.

🎓 CEU & Credential Tracker — CDR Portfolio, State Licensure, Specialty Certs

A complete credential management system tracking every professional requirement: CDR Professional Development Portfolio (75 CEU hours per 5-year cycle, 1 ethics hour required) with completed credits logged by date and source; state dietitian licensure renewal dates and hours required; specialty certifications including CSSD for sports nutrition, CDCES for diabetes education, and IFNCP for integrative and functional nutrition; CPR and AED certification expiry; and professional liability insurance renewal date. Never approach a renewal deadline unsure of where you stand. The tracker gives you a single dashboard view of every credential with renewal dates sorted so the most urgent appear first.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Dietitians

Is this template HIPAA-compliant?

No. FreelanceOS Pro is a practice management and business organization tool — it is not a HIPAA-compliant EHR system. For formal clinical recordkeeping, insurance billing, and compliant patient data storage, use dedicated healthcare software such as SimplePractice, Healthie, or Kareo. This template supports practice operations, business tracking, resource organization, and credential management — not formal clinical documentation or insurance billing.

How does the client profile database work?

The client profile database stores a practice management reference card for each client — name or client ID, age, primary health goal, dietary pattern, intolerances and allergies noted, session frequency, referral source, start date, and package purchased. It is a quick-reference tool for your practice management use, not a formal clinical record. Search and filter across all clients by goal, dietary pattern, or referral source to understand your caseload composition at a glance and prepare efficiently for each session.

How is the meal plan library organized?

The meal plan library stores your reusable frameworks organized by condition and goal — weight management, heart health, sports performance, GI health, prenatal, low-FODMAP, and anti-inflammatory — with modification notes for common dietary patterns and intolerances. Each entry links to your full plan document stored wherever you prefer. The library is the index that helps you find the right starting point for a new client without rebuilding from scratch every time a client arrives with a familiar goal.

How does the practice dashboard track referral sources?

The practice operations dashboard includes a referral source breakdown showing how many clients came from each source — primary care physicians, fitness studios, self-referral, social media, insurance panel, or other channels. Tracking this systematically lets you identify which relationships to invest in, which channels are growing organically, and where to focus relationship-building energy for the highest return on your practice development time.

How does the CEU tracker work for RD renewal?

The CEU and credential tracker logs your CDR Professional Development Portfolio requirements — 75 hours per 5-year cycle with 1 ethics hour required — alongside completed credits, dates, and sources. It also tracks state dietitian licensure renewal dates, specialty certifications (CSSD, CDCES, IFNCP), CPR renewal, and professional liability insurance expiry. Never approach a renewal deadline without knowing exactly where you stand on your required credit count and which requirements are still outstanding.
