FAQ

Questions From Journalists

Can I manage multiple stories in different stages at once?

Yes. The Story Pipeline Tracker uses a Kanban board with columns for Idea, Pitching, Researching, Writing, Editing, and Published. You can see every story at a glance, drag cards between stages, and filter by publication or beat.

How does the source contact database work?

Each source is a record with name, title, organization, contact details, beat relevance, and notes on past interactions. Sources link to the stories they contributed to, so you can quickly see who you've used for what and surface the right expert when a new story breaks.

Is this template useful for staff journalists, not just freelancers?

Absolutely. Staff journalists use it to manage their personal beat, track pitches to editors, organize research, and maintain a source Rolodex the newsroom doesn't control. It works alongside your CMS, not instead of it.

How does the pitch tracker help with rejection and follow-ups?

The Pitch Tracker logs every pitch with the publication, editor contact, pitch date, status (sent, rejected, accepted, assigned), and follow-up date. You'll never lose track of where a pitch stands or forget to follow up after two weeks of silence.

Can I store interview recordings and transcripts?

You can link to recordings and paste or attach transcripts directly in each interview note record. The Interview Notes System organizes entries by story, source, and date, with a summary field for key quotes and a follow-up section for unanswered questions.

How does the research archive prevent duplicate work?

The Research Archive is a searchable database of documents, URLs, and notes tagged by topic, geography, and story. Before you start a new investigation, search the archive to see what background research already exists — saving hours of re-reporting.

Does this work with my existing tools like Google Docs or Airtable?

FreelanceOS Pro lives in Notion and links to external tools via URL. You can link directly to Google Docs drafts, Airtable databases, or any cloud resource from within your workspace. Many journalists use it as the hub that connects everything else.
