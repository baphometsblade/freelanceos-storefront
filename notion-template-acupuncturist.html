The Problem

Running a Private Acupuncture Practice Means Managing Business Operations Alongside Clinical Work

Your HIPAA-compliant EMR handles your clinical notes. But who handles your scheduling overview, herbal inventory, package revenue, and practice growth metrics? Here is what the unmanaged business side looks like.

📋

Scheduling overview lives in your appointment software only

Your scheduling platform (Acuity, Jane, Calendly) tells you when appointments are booked — but it doesn't tell you how many sessions a patient has used on their 10-session package, whether their package expires next month, or which patients are at risk of dropping off because they've gone 6 weeks without a visit. A practice roster with session-count and package-status visibility gives you that business intelligence at a glance without opening the EMR.

🌿

Herbal dispensary stockouts cost you revenue and trust

A patient comes in asking for the Bu Zhong Yi Qi Wan they've been taking for two months. You're out of stock and won't have more for 10 days. This isn't just a lost sale — it's a gap in continuity of care that erodes confidence in your practice. An inventory system with reorder points and supplier contacts means you catch stockouts before they happen, not when a patient is standing at your dispensary.

💰

Practice revenue tracked in accounting software — but not by type

You know your total monthly revenue. But you don't know what percentage came from individual acupuncture sessions versus herbal sales versus packages versus community acupuncture versus workshops. Without income broken out by type, you can't identify which services drive disproportionate revenue per hour of your time — and you can't make informed decisions about how to grow the practice.

🎓

NCCAOM CEU requirements tracked in a spreadsheet or not at all

NCCAOM license renewal requires 60 PDA (Professional Development Activity) points every four years, including specific category requirements. Most acupuncturists track these in a spreadsheet or don't track them until renewal approaches — then spend hours reconstructing records. A CEU log with category tracking and renewal date visibility eliminates the scramble and ensures compliance documentation is always current.

How does the herbal dispensary inventory help me manage product sales and reordering?
The herbal dispensary inventory tracks each product you sell — herb name, format (granule, teapill, raw herb, tincture, topical), supplier, unit cost, retail price, quantity in stock, reorder point, reorder quantity, last ordered date, and month-to-date revenue per product. This is a product inventory and revenue tracking system, not a clinical formulary or prescription record. When a product drops below your reorder threshold, the inventory view surfaces it automatically so you can place a supplier order before running out mid-week. The revenue column tracks which product categories generate the most retail income — some practices find that a small number of top-selling formulas drive the majority of herbal retail revenue, which informs purchasing and display decisions. For practices with a meaningful herbal dispensary component, tracking this inventory reduces stockouts (which erode patient trust) and overstock (which ties up cash in products that move slowly).
What does the administrative session log track, and what does it deliberately NOT track?
The administrative session log tracks billing and scheduling information only: patient identifier (initials or practice-assigned code — never full name in a non-HIPAA system), session date, session type (individual acupuncture, cupping, gua sha, moxibustion, tuina, herbal consultation, community acupuncture), duration, rate, payment method, payment received status, and if applicable, insurance carrier and authorization status. This is a billing record, not a clinical record. The log deliberately does NOT include TCM diagnostic patterns, point selection or needling specifics, tongue and pulse findings, formula rationale, treatment progress notes, or any clinical information. Those records belong in your HIPAA-compliant EMR. The boundary matters both for HIPAA compliance and for professional standards — FreelanceOS Pro handles your administrative billing documentation, while your EMR handles your clinical documentation.
How does the package and membership manager help me grow recurring revenue?
The package and membership manager tracks each recurring revenue product you offer: package name, sessions included, price, discount versus single-session rate, active clients on this package, revenue generated from this package this month, package sold date, expiration if applicable, renewal rate, and client satisfaction notes. Packages and memberships represent the most predictable revenue in a private acupuncture practice — a client on a 10-session package generates known forward revenue, while a single-session-only practice has variable monthly income that depends entirely on whether existing clients rebook and new clients arrive. Tracking renewal rate by package type shows which offerings retain clients and which may need pricing or structure adjustment. Practices that shift even 40% of active clients to a package or membership model often report significantly reduced income volatility month to month.
