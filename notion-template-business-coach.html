👤 Client Business Overview

📋 Strategy Session Log

Per session: client, date, session number, primary topic (sales pipeline / marketing strategy / hiring / pricing / product development / quarterly planning / obstacle solving / mindset), key decisions made, action items with owner and due date, revenue or metric discussed, progress vs. previous session goals, and coach prep notes for next session. Track business metrics over each coaching engagement: monthly revenue, new clients or customers acquired, CAC if tracked, churn or refund rate, team size, MRR or ARR for recurring-revenue businesses, ROAS if running ads. Clients who can see their business progress during coaching are far more likely to renew, refer, and give strong testimonials.

🔍 Business Audit Framework Library

Your proprietary frameworks stored in Notion: business model canvas notes, SWOT analysis template, pricing audit (current vs. value-based pricing calculation), sales funnel audit (leads / conversions / revenue per stage), team capacity audit, offer suite review. Between-session accountability check-ins (weekly or biweekly, async via Notion or Voxer): wins since last check-in, action items from last session completed (yes/no), blockers or decisions needed, revenue or metric update, next priority. Your async touchpoint system that keeps clients on track between sessions and ensures momentum does not stall during the week when you are not on a call together.

How is this different from a CRM or generic coaching template?

Most CRMs are built for sales pipelines, not coaching relationships. FreelanceOS Pro for business coaches is built around the unique workflow of a coaching engagement — client business context including revenue stage, business model, and primary focus area; session logs with action items and metric updates; an accountability check-in system between sessions; and a revenue and KPI tracker that documents client business growth over the engagement. It is the operating system for a business coaching practice, not a contact database or a sales tool designed for something else.

Can I use this to track client business metrics and results?

Yes — the Client Revenue and KPI Tracker is one of the most valuable sections for business coaches. You can log each client's monthly revenue, new customers acquired, team size, MRR for recurring-revenue businesses, and other key metrics over the course of the coaching engagement. Seeing measurable business progress within the coaching relationship is what justifies premium coaching rates and generates the strongest testimonials and referrals. Coaches who can point to a documented track record of client results command significantly higher prices than coaches who rely on qualitative feedback alone.

The accountability check-in system works particularly well for group programs where you need to collect async weekly updates from multiple participants between live group calls without it becoming a logistical burden.
