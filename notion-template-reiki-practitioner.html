The Problem

A Reiki Practice Is a Business — And the Business Side Needs Its Own System

Your healing work happens in the session room. But the business behind a thriving reiki practice — managing client appointments, teaching training programs, tracking distance healing, selling wellness products — needs its own organized system. Here's what an unmanaged reiki business looks like.

📋

Appointment overview scattered across text, email, and calendar apps

You have sessions booked in Calendly, intake forms in a Google Form, payment records in Venmo and PayPal, and new client inquiries in your email inbox. None of these talk to each other. A centralized administrative session overview — client, session type, date, payment status, follow-up timing — gives you the business clarity that scattered apps cannot. This is not a clinical record; it's a business operations log.

🎓

Reiki training students tracked in a notebook or nowhere

You teach Reiki Level I, Level II, and Master classes. Each student has a payment plan, needs their manual sent, receives their attunement, and gets a certificate. Managing 10+ students across multiple training levels in your email inbox means things fall through — manuals not sent, certificates delayed, follow-up check-ins forgotten. Your teaching lineage deserves a proper tracker, not an inbox search.

🙏

Distance healing treated as informal — not tracked as a revenue line

Distance healing represents real income and requires real workflow management. Scheduling across time zones, confirming session completion, sending follow-up messages, confirming payment — these steps are easy to let slip when distance healing isn't tracked as formally as in-person sessions. The result: uncollected payments, missed follow-ups, and clients who feel unserved after their session.

💰

Revenue from sessions, training, and products tracked nowhere together

You earn from in-person sessions, distance healing, reiki training classes, crystal healing kits sold on Etsy, and a beginner reiki online course on Teachable. Each revenue stream exists in a separate dashboard. Without a unified view, you cannot answer: which service line generates the most revenue per hour of your time? Where should you invest energy to grow? The practice business dashboard exists to answer exactly these questions.

Is FreelanceOS Pro a HIPAA-compliant system for reiki practitioners?
No. FreelanceOS Pro is NOT a HIPAA-compliant electronic health record (EHR), clinical notes system, or healthcare billing platform. Reiki is a complementary wellness modality — most reiki practitioners are not subject to HIPAA as defined healthcare providers unless they are also licensed healthcare professionals in a clinical setting. However, the fundamental principle applies regardless: never store client health history, symptoms, medical conditions, diagnoses, treatment outcomes, or any health-related session observations in this tool. The client session overview in FreelanceOS Pro is strictly administrative: appointment scheduling, payment tracking, service type, and business analytics. If your reiki practice operates under any healthcare licensing or in a clinical setting where HIPAA applies, use an appropriate HIPAA-compliant system for any protected health information and consult your compliance advisor.

The student tracker covers everything from initial enrollment through certificate issuance: student name, level pursuing (Reiki Level I, Level II, Advanced Reiki Training, Reiki Master, Master Teacher), lineage system (Usui Shiki Ryoho, Jikiden Reiki, Holy Fire Reiki, Karuna Reiki, Komyo Reiki), class date, format (in-person, virtual, hybrid), payment plan and status, manual and materials sent, attunements completed, certificate issued, and whether the student went on to practice professionally. Teaching reiki is a significant income stream for many Master Teachers — a full Level I weekend class, Level II series, and Master program represents substantial revenue per student cohort. Tracking students through the entire pipeline from inquiry to certificate also builds your lineage record, which is professionally meaningful in the reiki community.

Distance healing has grown substantially as a business model for reiki practitioners, often representing 30-50% or more of total session revenue for established practitioners. Tracking it as its own service line gives you accurate revenue data and follow-up workflow management for remote clients who may be in any time zone.
