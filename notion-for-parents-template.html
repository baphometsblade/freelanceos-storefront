Module 06

Family Budget Dashboard

You make a solid income. You're not extravagant. But every month ends the same — the account is lower than expected, and you're not sure why. The Family Budget Dashboard answers "where does all the money go?" once and for all.

The dashboard tracks monthly income against every major spending category: housing, groceries, utilities, kids' activities, childcare, medical copays, entertainment, clothing, and savings. Each month gets its own column. You enter actuals, see immediately where you're over or under.

The annual view is where the real insights live. Summer activity spending spikes in June–July. Back-to-school hits hard in August. Holidays show up in November–December. See the whole year, plan for spikes instead of being surprised by them.

Activity fees from the Activity Tracker feed directly here. You always know that soccer, ballet, piano, and t-ball cost $310/month — and can decide deliberately whether that's right for your family.

How does the family calendar management work in this Notion template? +

Each entry includes date, event type, who's involved (tagged by name), location, and a reminder note. Filter by child, by week, or by event type. Both parents view and edit in real time, ending the "did you know about this?" problem for good.
Can I track multiple kids' activities with this template? +
Yes. The Activity Tracker is built for families with multiple children. Each entry is tagged to a specific child — filter by child for their schedule or view the whole family. Fields: activity name, child, day/time, location, coach contact, equipment needed, monthly fee, season dates, and carpool notes. You'll never confuse Tuesday soccer with Wednesday gymnastics again.
How does this template help with school document organization? +
The Kids' School Hub gives each child their own page linked from the family dashboard. The Household Task Manager adds a school-year section for registration deadlines, supply lists, and permission slips — so nothing gets lost in the kitchen counter pile.

Always consult medical professionals for health decisions and use appropriate secure systems where regulatory compliance is required. This template is not HIPAA-compliant.

Check off when done. Nothing falls through the cracks — not the oil change, not the permission slip, not the annual registration.
How does the family budget dashboard help parents track spending? +
