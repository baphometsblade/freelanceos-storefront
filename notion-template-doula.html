The Problem

Managing a Doula Practice Means Tracking Clients, On-Call Windows, Visits, and Revenue Simultaneously

Doulas juggle multiple active clients at different stages — prenatal meetings, on-call windows, active birth support, and postpartum visits — plus certification renewal, marketing, and business finances. Here is what the unmanaged version looks like.

📅

On-call windows and due dates tracked in memory

Three clients due within the same 6-week window. Your on-call commitment starts at 37 weeks for each. Prenatal meeting schedules overlap. One has a backup doula arrangement you set up but haven't formalized in writing. Managing simultaneous on-call windows without a clear tracker is how doula professionals get into double-booking situations — not through negligence, but through tracking complexity that grows faster than memory can manage.

🤝

Referral sources unknown — you don't know what's actually working

You have 8 bookings this year. Three came from midwife referrals, two from past clients, two from Instagram, and one from Doula Match. But you're not sure — you didn't write it down. Without referral source tracking, you might be investing energy in networking with OBs who have never sent a referral, while neglecting midwives who have sent three. Six months of data changes how you grow your practice.

💸

Income by service type is a mystery

You do birth doula support, postpartum doula support, and occasional childbirth education classes. You know your total annual income — but you don't know what percentage is birth support versus postpartum versus education. You've been wondering whether to expand your postpartum practice or focus on births. Without income-by-type tracking, that decision is guesswork instead of data.

🎓

Certification renewal tracked informally

Your DONA certification renewal requires continuing education, attending births, and submitting documentation. Your CEs are scattered across email confirmations, printed certificates, and memory. Your next renewal date is somewhere in your inbox. A certification and CEU log with renewal date visibility and continuing education tracking means you're never scrambling to reconstruct documentation under a deadline.

Your client pipeline from first inquiry to final postpartum visit — all in one place, with every active client's on-call window visible at a glance.

📞 Prenatal & Postpartum Visit Log (Administrative)

Per meeting/visit: client name, meeting number, date, meeting type (prenatal meeting, birth support, postpartum visit, phone check-in, text support session, childbirth education class), duration in hours, labor hours for births (total hours in attendance), and administrative notes covering logistics and scheduling — not clinical birth notes. This is a business-level visit log: it answers how many hours you've invested per client, how many prenatal meetings remain before the on-call window, and whether postpartum visit hours are on track against the package agreement. Never clinical content — that boundary is fundamental to the doula professional role.

🍼 Postpartum Visit Schedule

For postpartum support clients: client name, estimated date for postpartum support to begin, postpartum support package (hours or visits), visits per week, total weeks, start date, end date, visits completed, visits remaining, and general support focus areas (infant feeding support, household help, emotional support, sibling adjustment — the client's stated priorities, not clinical assessments). Managing 2 to 4 concurrent postpartum clients with different schedules requires a clear view of who has visits this week, who is mid-package, and who is approaching their final visit so you can discuss renewal or completion thoughtfully rather than abruptly.

📚 Childbirth Education & Workshop Tracker

For doulas who also teach: class or workshop name, curriculum or method (Lamaze, HypnoBirthing, Bradley Method, your own curriculum), venue, date and time, class size, price per person, total revenue, materials provided, and follow-up resources shared with attendees. Track your education programs as a distinct revenue stream with its own performance metrics — revenue per class, average attendance, repeat attendees, and referrals to your doula services. The most successful doulas who teach often find that 20 to 30% of their birth doula clients first encountered them in a childbirth education class.

📣 Lead & Referral Network

Per inquiry: lead source (OB office referral, midwife referral, past client, Instagram, The Doula Network, Doula Match, Facebook birth group, local parenting group), client name, due date, inquiry date, package interest, conversion result, and if not booked, the reason (price, timing, location, chose a hospital doula). Plus referral partnerships: OBs, midwives, pediatricians, lactation consultants, and childbirth educators who refer clients your way, with last referral date. Six months of this data transforms how you think about practice growth — from marketing broadly to deepening the relationships that actually convert.

💼 Doula Business Dashboard

Active clients (prenatal plus postpartum), births attended this year, postpartum clients currently active, monthly revenue, income by service type (birth doula, postpartum doula, childbirth education, online courses), referral source distribution, certification status (DONA, CAPPA, ProDoula, or other — renewal date and CEU hours logged), continuing education log, reviews collected (Google, Yelp, Doula Match), and annual revenue goal. The income-by-type breakdown tells you which service drives your business — and whether your time allocation matches your revenue priorities.

Common Questions

Is FreelanceOS Pro intended to store clinical or medical information about birth clients?
No. FreelanceOS Pro is a business management tool for doula professionals and is not intended to store clinical health information, labor or delivery notes, medical observations, lactation assessments, or any information that could constitute a medical record. Doulas are non-medical support providers — their role is emotional and practical, not clinical. FreelanceOS Pro handles doula business logistics: client management, scheduling, invoicing tracking, and business analytics. Never store clinical, health, or medical information in this template. Doulas should consult their certifying organization (DONA International, CAPPA, TOBI, ProDoula, and others) regarding professional standards for record-keeping.
How does the client and birth tracker help me manage clients from inquiry through postpartum?
The client and birth tracker gives each client a complete record from inquiry to final postpartum visit: client name, estimated due date, birth type preference, primary care provider type, support partner involvement, prenatal meetings scheduled, on-call start date, birth date recorded after, birth location, package type, contract signed status, deposit paid, and balance due. Having every client's timeline in one view — upcoming due dates, on-call windows, prenatal meetings needed — means you never lose track of where each client is in your support arc. For doulas who manage three to six active clients simultaneously, this visibility is essential to ensuring no client slips through the gaps between inquiry and the on-call period.
How does the backup doula arrangement tracker work?
The backup doula arrangement tracker records your backup arrangements: backup doula name, certification, contact information, geographical coverage area, fee arrangement (flat fee, percentage of your package fee, reciprocal coverage agreement), current availability periods, any client-specific notes about the arrangement, and whether a specific client has been introduced to or approved the backup. Having this information organized matters most in the moments you need it least at leisure — when you're attending a birth that runs long, feeling ill, or have a family emergency. Professional doulas who have backup arrangements in place but haven't organized them clearly often find themselves searching for a phone number or fee agreement under stress. A clear backup tracker means the handoff, when it happens, is professional and rapid.
How does the lead and referral network tracker help me grow my doula practice?
The lead and referral network tracker records every inquiry with source, conversion result, and if not booked, the reason. It also tracks your referral partnerships — OBs, midwives, pediatricians, lactation consultants, and childbirth educators who refer clients to you, with last referral date and relationship notes. After 6 to 12 months of data, this tracker answers the most important growth question: where do my booked clients actually come from? Most doulas who track this discover that 70 to 80% of their bookings trace back to two or three referral sources — which tells you exactly where to invest relationship-building energy.
