The Irish Freelancer's Tax Guide

Irish self-employed people face a three-part tax system — Income Tax (Cáin Ioncaim), USC (Universal Social Charge), and PRSI — that applies to net profit from self-employment. Here is a complete overview of your obligations as an Irish sole trader or personal service company contractor.

Income Tax — Standard Rate and Higher Rate

Ireland uses a two-rate income tax system. For a single person in 2024: the Standard Rate of 20% applies to income up to €42,000 (the standard rate cut-off point). Income above this is taxed at the Higher Rate of 40%. Married couples and civil partners have higher cut-off points. Tax credits — including the Personal Tax Credit (€1,875), Earned Income Tax Credit (€1,875 for self-employed), and PRSI credit where applicable — reduce your tax liability euro for euro. FreelanceOS's tax tracker calculates your approximate Income Tax, USC, and PRSI on each payment received.

Universal Social Charge (USC)

The USC is a tax on gross income (not just profit) payable by everyone earning above €13,000 per year. For 2024: 0.5% on the first €12,012; 2% on €12,013–€25,760; 4% on €25,761–€70,044; 8% on income above €70,044. Self-employed people earning over €100,000 pay an additional 3% USC surcharge on income above €100,000. The USC applies to all income including PAYE employment income, self-employment profit, and rental income — so if you also hold a PAYE job alongside freelancing, your combined income determines your USC rate.

Revenue Online Service (ROS)

ROS (ros.ie) is the Irish Revenue Commissioners' online portal for self-assessment filing, VAT3 returns, and tax payments. All self-employed people are required to file via ROS. ROS registration typically takes 5–10 business days to fully activate (you receive credentials by post). Filing and paying your Form 11 liability via ROS by the mid-November online deadline avoids the surcharges applied to late paper returns. FreelanceOS's ROS tracker covers registration steps, credential renewal, and all filing deadlines.

Irish Freelance Business Structures

  • Sole Trader: Simplest structure — register with Revenue, get a tax registration number, file Form 11 annually. No CRO registration required. Full personal liability for business debts. Income taxed at personal Income Tax rates. Suitable for most freelancers earning under €80,000/year.
  • Limited Company (DAC or private company limited by shares): Incorporate through the CRO (Companies Registration Office — cro.ie). Separate legal entity — limited liability. Profits taxed at 12.5% corporate tax rate. Can pay yourself a salary (subject to personal Income Tax) and dividends (taxed at higher personal rates). More admin: annual CRO returns, Companies Registration Office filing, accounts preparation, directors' duties. Commonly used by tech contractors billing high-earning contracts.
  • Partnership: Two or more people trading together — each partner pays personal tax on their share of profits. Suitable for husband/wife or business partner arrangements. Register with Revenue; no CRO requirement for general partnerships.

Preliminary tax — your estimate of the current year's tax — is also due 31 October.
