Origin story

Why we built FreelanceOS

We were running a freelance business out of 7 spreadsheets.

In 2024, we started mapping out everything it took to run a solo freelance operation. Not just the work itself — the business of freelancing: tracking who owes you money, remembering which lead you sent a proposal to three weeks ago, knowing at a glance whether this month's revenue is on track.

Here's what we were using:

✕ Client list (Google Sheet)
✕ Project tracker (Trello board)
✕ Invoice log (another Sheet)
✕ Revenue tracker (third Sheet)
✕ Lead pipeline (Airtable free)
✕ Weekly tasks (Notion, loosely)
✕ Proposals (Google Docs folder)

None of these talked to each other. You'd close a project and have to manually update four different places. You'd send an invoice and forget to mark the lead as won. You'd plan your week without knowing which client was most overdue.

We looked at the Notion templates that existed. Easlo made beautiful systems — but they were personal productivity tools, not business management. Thomas Frank's Ultimate Brain was incredible for note-taking and knowledge management, but it wasn't built around client work and billing. We tried building our own — and it took weeks to get right.

So we built FreelanceOS: one Notion workspace where every database is connected. Close a project, and your revenue dashboard updates. Mark an invoice paid, and your client record reflects it. Your weekly planner knows which clients have deadlines this week. We wanted the system we wished we'd had in year one of freelancing — and we made it cheap enough that it's a no-brainer.

🚀
Easlo
$49–$79 one-time — various bundles available

Easlo is one of the most well-known Notion template creators, and for good reason. The design quality is high, the systems are thoughtful, and the community around his work is strong. His main products (Hive, Notion OS) are personal productivity systems — task management, habit tracking, goals, journaling, second brain.

For freelancers specifically, there are elements that help — a tasks database, project tracking, some contact management. But Easlo's templates aren't designed around invoicing, client billing, or revenue tracking. You get a beautiful personal operating system; you'd still need to layer on the business infrastructure yourself.

Strengths
  • Outstanding design quality
  • Excellent task and habit systems
  • Strong community and documentation
  • Good for personal productivity
Limitations
  • Not freelance-specific
  • No invoice or revenue tracking
  • Higher price point
  • More complex to set up

Best for: People who want an all-in-one personal operating system that also handles work. If you're a freelancer who also wants life tracking, habits, journaling, and a second brain in one workspace, Easlo delivers that in a way FreelanceOS doesn't.

📚
Thomas Frank Notion Templates
$50–$100 one-time — Ultimate Brain is the flagship

Thomas Frank has built some of the most polished Notion templates available, and his YouTube channel has introduced millions of people to Notion. His flagship product, Ultimate Brain, is genuinely impressive — a comprehensive personal knowledge management and task system with deep Notion integrations, formulas, and views.

Where it falls short for freelancers: it's a note-taking and productivity system, not a business system. There's no CRM, no invoice tracking, no client billing, no revenue dashboard. It's excellent for the "knowledge work" side of freelancing — capturing ideas, managing research, long-form writing. For the business operations side, you'd be on your own.

Strengths
  • Best-in-class Notion craftsmanship
  • Excellent documentation & video tutorials
  • Strong for note-taking & PKM
  • Regular updates, active community
Limitations
  • No business or billing features
  • Not designed for client management
  • Premium price for non-business use
  • Complex setup for new Notion users

Best for: Writers, researchers, creators, and knowledge workers who want an exceptional personal knowledge base and productivity system. If content creation and note-taking are central to your work, Thomas Frank's templates are hard to beat. Many freelancers use Ultimate Brain alongside a business-focused tool like FreelanceOS.

📈
DIY Spreadsheets
$0 cost — significant time investment

Building your own system from scratch in Google Sheets, Airtable, or Notion has a real appeal: you get exactly what you need, nothing more. And for very simple freelance operations — one or two clients, straightforward projects — it can work well.

The problem shows up at scale. The moment you have 5+ active clients, overlapping deadlines, and multiple income streams, the spreadsheet cracks start showing. Things don't cross-reference. You find yourself copying data between tabs. You spend Friday evening updating four different trackers when you should be done for the week.

Strengths
  • Zero cost
  • Completely customizable
  • Familiar tools (Sheets, Excel)
  • Full ownership of your data
Limitations
  • No cross-database linking
  • Weeks to build well
  • Breaks as you scale
  • Ongoing maintenance burden

Best for: Freelancers in their first few months who have simple needs and want to understand their workflow before committing to a system. Once you know what you need, templating that understanding into a purpose-built tool saves hours every week.
