Client Roster — Engagement Type, Filing Deadlines & Renewal Dates

A master business-side client database tracking each client relationship — name, engagement type (bookkeeping, tax prep, advisory, payroll, audit), engagement tier, billing frequency, key annual deadline dates for your planning purposes, and renewal or engagement review date. CPE/CPD Tracker — Hours, Categories, License Expiry & Provider Log

A complete continuing professional education tracker logging every course completed with provider name, course title, delivery format (in-person, webinar, self-study), credit hours earned, credit category (tax, audit, accounting, advisory, ethics, management), completion date, and which license or certification those hours apply to. Never scramble to complete ethics hours in the final weeks of a renewal period again — the dashboard shows your gap at any point in the year.

Business Development Pipeline — Referral Sources, Networking Contacts & Proposal Status

A structured business development pipeline tracking every prospect from first contact to signed engagement letter. Each record holds the prospect name, referral source (attorney, financial planner, client referral, networking event, online inquiry), engagement type they need, estimated annual engagement value, current pipeline stage (Prospect / Qualified / Proposal Sent / Negotiating / Won / Lost), last follow-up date, and the specific next action with a due date. Accounting practices grow primarily through professional referrals and trusted networks — having a structured pipeline ensures you follow up with warm leads systematically even during your busiest filing seasons rather than losing opportunities to the chaos of peak workload.

Seasonal Deadline Calendar — Tax Season Prep Checklist & Key Dates by Month

A month-by-month view of every key date in the accounting calendar — individual filing deadlines, estimated payment due dates, extended filing deadlines, payroll tax deposits, state-specific deadlines, and quarterly regulatory milestones. Pair the deadline calendar with a tax season prep checklist that tracks document collection status, review milestone, e-file confirmation, and client communication for each engagement. Knowing exactly where every client engagement stands during peak season — who has delivered all documents, who is still outstanding, who is on extension — means you can manage a full client roster through busy season without missing deadlines or making errors driven by administrative confusion rather than technical problems.

Service Menu and Pricing Tracker — Packages, Tiers & Client Assignments

A service catalog documenting every service package you offer — monthly bookkeeping retainer tiers, annual tax preparation packages, advisory retainer options, payroll services, fractional CFO engagements — with the price, scope summary, deliverables included, and which client relationships are currently assigned to each tier. Knowing exactly which clients are on which service packages helps you identify natural upsell conversations, standardize your scope conversations with new clients, make confident pricing decisions when onboarding similar prospects, and understand immediately if you have priced any service below market based on actual scope delivered versus what was agreed.

Marketing & Thought Leadership Content Calendar

A content planning calendar for your professional visibility activities — LinkedIn articles, newsletter editions, webinar topics, podcast appearances, speaking engagements, and community contributions. Plan your content quarterly around tax season rhythms, regulatory updates, and the questions your ideal clients ask most often. Your content calendar connects your practice knowledge to your practice growth in a measurable, repeatable way.

Vendor & Software Subscription Tracker for Practice Management

A complete log of every software subscription, vendor relationship, and practice tool you pay for — tax software, bookkeeping platforms, document management systems, client portal tools, e-signature software, time tracking tools, billing systems, and professional membership dues. Track the vendor name, service category, annual or monthly cost, renewal date, and whether the subscription is actively used by your practice. Accounting practices accumulate software subscriptions quickly, and an unreviewed stack costs real money in tools you have outgrown or duplicated without realizing it. Review your vendor log quarterly to make intentional decisions about your practice technology stack.

Annual Revenue Goal & Client Acquisition Dashboard

A business-level revenue and growth dashboard tracking your annual revenue goal against your current client roster's total engagement value, new client acquisition versus client attrition through the year, average revenue per client by service type, and progress toward practice growth milestones. See whether your practice is on track, plateauing, or showing warning signs that need to be addressed before they compound. Know your concentration risk — if one or two large clients represent too much of your total revenue — and track your new client pipeline value against your growth targets. Sole-practitioner accountants who track their own practice metrics as rigorously as they track their clients' financial data build more resilient, profitable, and sellable practices over time.
