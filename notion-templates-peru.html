Getting Started as a Professional Freelancer in Peru

  • Register your RUC: Free at sunat.gob.pe. Required for issuing invoices and conducting formal business. Your RUC number appears on all client-facing documents.
  • Set up electronic invoicing (recibos por honorarios electrónicos): SUNAT's portal at sunat.gob.pe allows you to issue electronic recibos por honorarios. Many freelancers also use Alegra or Siigo for more convenient invoicing management.
  • International payments — Wise first, then Payoneer: Wise offers excellent USD-to-PEN conversion rates for international transfers. Payoneer is ideal if you work on Upwork or Fiverr specifically. BCP, BBVA Peru, and Interbank all support international wire transfers in USD.
  • Domestic payments — Yape or Plin: For Peruvian clients, Yape (BCP) or Plin (Interbank/BBVA/Scotiabank) are the fastest domestic payment methods. Log each Yape/Plin receipt in your income tracker with date and amount.
  • Quarterly pagos a cuenta: Estimated quarterly tax payments to SUNAT are required for cuarta categoría income. Your contador will help you calculate the correct amounts based on your projected annual income.

Frequently Asked Questions — Peruvian Freelancers

How do Peruvian freelancers pay taxes?
Peruvian freelancers pay income tax (Impuesto a la Renta de Cuarta Categoría) to SUNAT on independent professional income. Register for an RUC at sunat.gob.pe — free and mandatory. Quarterly pagos a cuenta (estimated tax payments) are required based on your projected annual income. Electronic recibos por honorarios must be issued for all professional services rendered. The Régimen Especial de Renta (RER) is an option for small businesses. A Peruvian contador público experienced with cuarta categoría freelance income is strongly recommended for initial setup and ongoing compliance.
What is RUC and why do Peruvian freelancers need it?
RUC (Registro Único de Contribuyentes) is Peru's universal taxpayer ID administered by SUNAT. Every person or business with economic activity in Peru needs one — including freelancers and independent professionals. Registration is free at sunat.gob.pe or at a SUNAT office. Your RUC number appears on all invoices and receipts. Without RUC, you cannot legally issue recibos por honorarios electrónicos (electronic professional receipts) to clients, which is a requirement for formal professional income in Peru.
How do Peruvian freelancers receive USD payments?
Peruvian freelancers use several international payment methods: Wise (excellent USD-to-PEN conversion rates, widely used), Payoneer (standard for Upwork and Fiverr — USD balance with PEN withdrawal to Peruvian bank accounts), and direct wire transfers. Best Peruvian banks for international payments: BCP (Banco de Crédito del Perú — largest Peruvian bank), BBVA Peru, Interbank, and Scotiabank Peru. Peru has no capital controls for normal freelance income. Many Peruvian freelancers maintain USD savings accounts at Peruvian banks to hold earnings and convert at favorable rates.
Why is Lima a growing digital nomad and tech destination?
Lima offers a compelling combination for digital professionals: high-quality infrastructure in Miraflores and San Isidro, excellent coworking spaces (WeWork, Comunal, Selina), reliable fiber internet, a globally renowned food scene that makes the quality-of-life calculus easy, and a cost of living well below US/European equivalents. Lima is at UTC-5 (US Eastern time), making collaboration with US clients seamless. Peru's growing startup ecosystem (StartUp Perú, UTEC, PUCP innovation hubs) and major multinational presence (IBM, Accenture, SAP, Oracle) create diverse professional opportunities.
What is Workana and how do Peruvian freelancers use it?
Workana is Latin America's leading freelance marketplace, founded in Argentina and dominant across Peru, Argentina, Colombia, Mexico, and Chile. It operates in Spanish and connects freelancers with regional LatAm clients who prefer working with Spanish-speaking professionals in similar time zones. For Peruvian freelancers, Workana is the primary platform for regional LatAm client acquisition, while Upwork and Fiverr are used for US and global clients. Workana handles payments in local currencies or USD depending on the arrangement between client and freelancer.
What skills are most in demand for Peruvian freelancers?
Peruvian freelancers are competitive in: software development (Python, Java, React, Node.js — Peru's engineering schools produce strong technical talent), UI/UX design, digital marketing and SEO in Spanish, graphic design, content writing in Spanish and English, bilingual customer service and virtual assistance (highly valued for US companies serving Hispanic markets), data analysis, e-commerce management, and video/animation production. The creative economy around food (Peru's global culinary prestige), tourism, and music also generates demand for media production and content freelancers.
