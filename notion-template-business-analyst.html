Frequently Asked Questions

Can I track both functional and non-functional requirements?
Yes. The Requirements Database includes a type field for functional, non-functional, technical, and business requirements. You can filter, sort, and group by type, and tag each requirement with priority, status, and the stakeholder responsible for sign-off.
How do I map stakeholders in Notion?
The Stakeholder Map database captures name, role, department, influence level, interest level, and communication preferences for every stakeholder. You can view it as a table, group by influence level, or filter by project to quickly see who needs what information and how to reach them.
Does it work for Agile vs. Waterfall projects?
Yes — the template is methodology-agnostic. For Agile, organize the Use Case Library by epic and sprint. For Waterfall, structure requirements phases with phase-gate sign-offs and traceability matrices. The databases are flexible enough to match any delivery approach your client uses.
Can I share requirements with clients who don't use Notion?
Yes. Notion pages can be shared publicly or with specific guests via a link, with no Notion account required for view access. You can share a read-only requirements view or workshop summary with clients so they can review and comment without needing a paid Notion seat.
How do I track requirement sign-offs?
The Requirements Database includes a Sign-Off Status field where you can record who approved each requirement and when. Add a date property and a stakeholder relation to create a complete approval audit trail for each item — useful for both Agile demos and formal Waterfall sign-off gates.
