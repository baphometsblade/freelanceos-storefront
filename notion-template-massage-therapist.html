The Problem

Running a Massage Practice Means Managing the Business Behind the Bodywork

Licensed massage therapists balance client relationships, CE requirements for license renewal, package tracking, supply inventory, and business financials alongside the physical demands of the work itself. Here is what the unmanaged version looks like.

📋

CE hours tracked in a folder somewhere

Your license renews in 9 months and you need 24 CE hours. You've completed three courses but the certificates are in a folder on your laptop and a paper certificate from a workshop in a drawer somewhere. How many hours do you actually have completed? You're not certain without an audit that should take 20 minutes but keeps getting pushed back until it suddenly can't.

🎁

Package sessions tracked in your head

Eight clients have prepaid packages at various points in their redemption. Two have 5-session packages, three have 3-session packages, and three purchased packages that include a 90-minute and two 60-minute sessions. When a client asks how many sessions they have remaining, you check your notes app, your booking system, or just hope your memory is accurate. It occasionally isn't.

🧴

Supplies ordered reactively rather than proactively

You ran out of your preferred massage oil mid-week last month. You discovered it when you reached for the bottle at the start of a session and found it nearly empty. A supply inventory that takes 5 minutes to review weekly would have caught that shortage 10 days earlier and prevented the scramble to a local supplier at retail markup when your usual order hasn't arrived yet.

💰

Income undocumented until tax season

A massage therapy practice income comes from multiple streams: session fees, tips, package purchases, gift certificate sales, and possibly retail product sales. None of it is documented systematically week to week. At tax time you download bank statements and try to reconstruct a year of income from transaction descriptions — which takes hours and still produces an approximation rather than a documented record.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Licensed Massage Therapists & Bodywork Professionals

Is this template HIPAA-compliant for storing client health information?

No — and this template is explicitly NOT designed for that purpose. FreelanceOS Pro is a business management tool, not a clinical documentation or SOAP note system, and it is NOT HIPAA-compliant. Never store client health information (PHI), SOAP notes, clinical intake forms, or treatment records in this template. All clinical documentation must be handled through your practice's HIPAA-compliant clinical management system (Jane App, MindBody, SimplePractice, or your state board-required practice management software). The client preference notes in this template are strictly for non-clinical service business information: modality preferences, pressure preferences, session frequency goals, how they heard about you, package balance, birthday for marketing purposes, and last service date. If you are uncertain whether a piece of information constitutes PHI, err on the side of keeping it in your clinical system rather than here.

How does the CE tracker help me stay on track for license renewal?

CE requirements for massage therapists vary significantly by state — some states require 24 CE hours per two-year renewal cycle, others require 16 hours, and the specific approved providers and topic requirements differ further. The CE tracker records your license type, license number, state licensing board, license expiration date, total CE hours required per renewal cycle (entered for your specific state requirement), and each completed CE course (course name, provider name, hours awarded, date completed, certificate of completion link). The tracker calculates completed hours against your requirement, showing you whether you are on track for renewal or falling behind — surfacing this before the renewal deadline rather than discovering a deficit with 30 days remaining. The specialty training section captures certifications beyond your base license: pregnancy massage, myofascial release, Thai massage, lymphatic drainage, hot stone — each with provider, hours, and certification date. Professional memberships (AMTA, ABMP) with renewal dates are tracked in the same record, since membership benefits including liability insurance have their own renewal deadlines that should not lapse.

How do I use the business income tracker for quarterly tax planning?

The business income tracker captures session count by week, revenue by service type (60-minute Swedish, 90-minute deep tissue, prenatal, sports, package redemptions, gift certificates redeemed, add-ons), tip totals by week and month, product retail sales if you sell massage oils or other retail, and monthly totals. For quarterly tax planning, you need your estimated annual income to calculate quarterly estimated tax payments — a therapist making $65,000 in massage income owes quarterly estimated tax payments to the IRS and likely your state, and those payments should be calculated from documented income rather than guessed. The income tracker gives you the year-to-date total at each quarterly due date (April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15) so you can calculate the payment accurately. The breakdown by service type also shows which modalities and session lengths generate the most revenue per time invested — the data behind scheduling and marketing decisions.

How do I track packages and gift certificates so I never lose count of remaining sessions?

The package and gift certificate tracker records each package or certificate separately: the client, the package type (number of sessions and modality), purchase date and price, sessions included, sessions used (updated after each redemption), sessions remaining, and an expiry date if your packages have one. When a client books using their package, you update sessions used and the remaining count is visible at a glance before the next appointment. For gift certificates, the tracker records the certificate number, the recipient, the purchase amount, the amount redeemed, and the remaining balance — covering both session-count packages and dollar-value certificates. For a therapist managing 8 to 12 active packages at different points in their redemption cycle, the tracker eliminates the risk of losing count of remaining sessions or discovering during checkout that a certificate balance was lower than expected.

How does the service menu tool help me during client consultations?

The service menu database records every service you offer: service name, duration, price, modalities included, a brief description for use in conversations and marketing, package availability and pricing, gift certificate availability, and available add-ons. The primary use is as a reference during new client consultations and when someone calls or texts to ask about services — having every service, duration, and price in one place means you give consistent, accurate information rather than approximating from memory. The menu database also creates the foundation for your website service descriptions and intake form service options, ensuring what's listed on your website exactly matches what you offer and price. When you adjust pricing or add a new service, updating the database updates your single source of truth rather than requiring updates across multiple platforms that may fall out of sync.
