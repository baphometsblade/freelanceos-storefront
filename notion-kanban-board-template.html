Why Freelancers Use Kanban

Visual project management for a multi-client workload

Freelancers don't manage one project at a time. On any given week you might be finishing a web design deliverable for one client, waiting on feedback from a second, and kicking off a copywriting project for a third. Linear to-do lists fail this kind of work because they hide context — a task named "write homepage" tells you nothing about which client it's for, what stage it's in, or whether it's waiting on someone else.

A kanban board solves this by making project status visual. The column a card sits in — To Do, In Progress, Review, Done — tells you instantly where that work stands. You scan the board and understand your entire workload in seconds. No mental overhead. No digging through project management apps to figure out what to work on next.

The FreelanceOS kanban template is designed specifically for the way freelancers work — not for development teams or marketing departments. The default stages reflect a typical freelance project lifecycle: you scope it, you work on it, the client reviews it, and you deliver it. That's the flow baked into the board. Web designers working through multiple iterations, copywriters managing feedback rounds, and consultants tracking engagement milestones all use the same structure with minor customizations.

Unlike a standalone kanban app like Trello, this template lives inside Notion alongside your client database, project tracker, income dashboard, and time log. Everything links together. When a task moves to Done, you can jump straight to the linked invoice record and mark the milestone billable. When a client asks for a status update, you filter the board to their projects and screenshot the current state. It's a system, not just a board.

FreelanceOS also uses Notion's native database features to give you multiple ways to look at the same data. The kanban view is the default, but you can flip to a table view to sort tasks by deadline, a timeline view to see how projects overlap, or a gallery view to browse deliverable previews. One database, every view you need.
