Database 01

ASIN Profitability Tracker

The core of the template. For every ASIN you sell, this database calculates your true net margin by stacking all the real costs: Amazon referral fee, FBA fulfillment fee, monthly storage fee (per unit sold), your COGS including shipping to FBA, and your average PPC spend per unit sold. What's left is your actual profit — not the number Seller Central shows you, the number that tells you whether you should keep selling this product or kill it.

This is where most sellers have their uncomfortable realization. A product generating $8,000/month in revenue might net $540 after all costs — a 6.75% margin that evaporates if PPC gets more competitive or Amazon raises FBA fees. Running this across your catalog tells you which ASINs to scale, which to optimize costs on, and which to exit quietly. Database 02

Product Research Database

Every serious Amazon seller has a product research pipeline — but most track it in a mess of browser tabs, spreadsheet rows, and Helium 10 or Jungle Scout exports that never get reviewed. This database gives you a structured, filterable record of every ASIN you've researched: the niche, estimated monthly sales volume (pulled from your research tools), BSR across the main category, competition level assessment, an opportunity score you assign, and a final decision field where you mark each product as pursue, pass, or monitor.

The monitor column is where hidden value lives. Products you passed on six months ago might become viable as competition shifts or your COGS improve. Instead of re-researching from scratch, you revisit the row, update the BSR and sales estimate, and make a fresh decision with historical context attached. Database 03

Supplier Hub

Your supplier relationships are one of the most critical and most disorganized parts of a growing Amazon business. Communication happens across WeChat, email, Alibaba messages, and WhatsApp. Lead times get quoted verbally and forgotten. Payment terms change between orders. A supplier you rated reliable six months ago had a quality issue on the last shipment that you never formally logged anywhere.

The Supplier Hub consolidates everything: the supplier's name, country, primary contact and platform, the date and cost of your sample order, their MOQ, production lead time, transit time to your 3PL or Amazon warehouse, payment terms, and a reliability rating you update after each completed order. Linked to your Inventory Planner, you can see at a glance which supplier produces each ASIN and what their lead time is — critical when you're calculating your reorder trigger date. The notes field holds observations that don't fit anywhere else: "always ships 5 days later than quoted," "quality slipped Q4 2025," "accepts Alibaba Trade Assurance only."

Database 04

Inventory & Restock Planner

Stockouts kill momentum. A 3-day stockout in a category you've been ranking in can cost you your BSR position and three weeks of recovery time. Overstock means FBA storage fees that quietly eat margin every month, and if you're not careful, long-term storage fees on top of that. Most sellers manage this reactively — checking inventory when something feels low, guessing reorder quantity, hoping the boat arrives before units run out.

The Inventory Planner replaces guesswork with a formula. For each ASIN, you log current FBA inventory and daily sales velocity (pulled from Seller Central's unit sales by day view). The "days of stock remaining" field calculates automatically. Combined with the supplier lead time from the Supplier Hub, you get a reorder trigger date — the actual calendar date you need to place the PO to avoid stockout, accounting for production time, shipping, and Amazon check-in delays. Set the reorder quantity using a configurable safety stock multiplier. The result: you open this database once a week, sort by days remaining, and know exactly what to act on.

Database 05

PPC Campaign Notes

Amazon's ad console is built for making changes, not for tracking the reasoning behind those changes. You lower a bid, ACoS improves, you don't know why. You add negative keywords, something breaks, you can't trace it. You launch an auto campaign, harvest keywords into exact match, never go back to check if the exact match campaigns are actually outperforming the auto. Strategic PPC management requires a layer above the campaign interface — a place to think, record, and review decisions over time.

The PPC Campaign Notes database is that layer. Each row covers one campaign: the ASIN it's running on, campaign type (Sponsored Products / Brands / Display), target keywords or ASINs, bid strategy, your ACoS target, the current ACoS pulled from the last weekly review, and a notes field that's the most valuable column in the database. This is where you write "Lowered bids 15% on 5 KWs on June 3 — check June 17 for ACoS impact." Next time you open it, you have context. Over time, you build a record of what actually moves ACoS on your catalog — and that institutional knowledge stays in your system, not lost in your head.

Database 06

Launch Tracker

The honeymoon period is real, and you only get it once. In the first 30–90 days after a new ASIN goes live, Amazon's algorithm is actively watching your conversion rate, click-through rate, and sales velocity to determine where you belong in search rankings. Sellers who launch without a structured plan waste the honeymoon period on disorganized promotions, late Vine enrollment, and inconsistent PPC spend — and then wonder why ranking stalls after week 3.

The Launch Tracker keeps every new product launch on a defined timeline. Before launch day, there's a pre-launch checklist: listing optimized, A+ content live, Vine enrolled (if eligible), initial inventory at FBA, launch pricing confirmed, PPC campaigns loaded and paused. On launch day, you record the date, initial BSR, starting review count (usually 0), and the review velocity target for 30/60/90 days. Post-launch fields track actual BSR trajectory, review count at each milestone, ranking positions for your main 3–5 keywords, and a notes field for honeymoon period observations. When the next launch comes, you have a full record of what your last launch looked like to calibrate against.

FAQ

Common Questions from Amazon Sellers

Can I track product research for multiple ASINs at once?
Yes. The Product Research Database is built to handle your full research pipeline. You can log every ASIN you're evaluating with columns for niche, estimated monthly sales from Helium 10 or Jungle Scout, BSR, competition level, opportunity score, and a decision field (pursue / pass / monitor). Filter by niche or decision status to keep your pipeline organized without losing historical research you've already done.
Does the template help me compare FBA vs FBM profitability?
Yes. The ASIN Profitability Tracker has dedicated columns for FBA fulfillment fees and storage costs. You can duplicate a row and swap in FBM shipping costs to see the margin difference side by side. This makes FBA vs FBM decisions data-driven rather than guesswork, especially useful for oversized products or slow-moving inventory where FBA storage costs add up.
How does the supplier management section work?
The Supplier Hub stores each supplier's contact info, country, MOQ, lead time, payment terms, sample cost and date, and a reliability rating you update after each completed order. When you're reordering, all the info you need — lead time, payment terms, last order date — is in one place instead of buried in email threads or Alibaba message history.
Can I track my PPC campaign performance and notes in this template?
Yes. The PPC Campaign Notes database covers campaign name, ASIN, target keywords, bid strategy, ACoS target vs. current ACoS, and a notes field for what's working and what isn't. It's not a replacement for your Seller Central ad console — it's the strategic layer where you record decisions, track changes over time, and build institutional knowledge about what actually moves ACoS in your catalog.
Does this template help with review monitoring and velocity tracking?
Yes. The Launch Tracker includes fields for initial review count targets, review velocity goals, honeymoon period notes, and ranking targets. You can track your Vine enrollment status and monitor review growth at 30, 60, and 90 days post-launch. Having a structured record of each launch means your next launch benefits from the data of every previous one.
How does the inventory and restock planning work?
The Inventory & Restock Planner tracks current FBA inventory, daily sales velocity, days of stock remaining (calculated automatically from those two fields), reorder quantity, supplier lead time, and the reorder trigger date. Instead of logging into Seller Central and doing mental math, you open the planner and immediately see which ASINs need a PO placed and when. Sort by days remaining and handle the urgent ones first.
