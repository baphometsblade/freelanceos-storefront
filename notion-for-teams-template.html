Configuration Guide

Set Up for Your Team Size

The template scales from a two-person freelancer duo to a 25-person agency. Here is how to configure it at each stage.

Solo → Duo (Freelancer + VA)

2 People: The Minimum Viable Team Workspace

When it is just you and a virtual assistant, the biggest gain is not in project tracking — it is in documented SOPs. Your VA can only do work at the level your processes are documented. Start here:

  • Activate Team Hub, Resource Library (SOPs only), and Onboarding Checklist
  • Build 3–5 SOP pages for the tasks you hand off most (inbox management, invoice sending, content scheduling)
  • Use the Team Directory as a "contacts + context" page for your VA and key contractors
  • Keep the Project Pipeline simple: kanban only, no timeline view until you have 5+ concurrent projects
  • Notion free plan is sufficient — no paid tier needed at this stage
Small Team (3–10 People)

3–10 People: Where the Team Workspace Earns Its Keep

At this size, communication overhead becomes the number one productivity killer. Information shared in Slack evaporates. Decisions made in meetings are forgotten. This is exactly when a central team workspace pays off most.

  • Activate all 8 modules — this is the sweet spot the template was designed for
  • Use Team Hub announcements for weekly priorities; remove the need for a Monday all-hands
  • Set up OKRs at the company level first, then add individual key results in the second quarter
  • Assign Meeting Notes as a rotating responsibility — whoever called the meeting writes the notes
  • Use Notion's comment feature on project cards instead of Slack threads about specific tasks
  • Consider Notion Plus plan ($8/member/month) for unlimited version history and audit logs
Growing Agency (10–25 People)

10–25 People: Scaling Without Losing the Signal

At agency scale, the workspace becomes a system of record. New people join and need to ramp up fast. Clients need visibility. Leadership needs a bird's-eye view without being in every Slack channel.

  • Split the Project Pipeline by department (Design, Development, Strategy) with separate filtered views
  • Create a client-facing portal page (view-only share link) per client from your Project Pipeline
  • Expand the Team Wiki into department-level sections — each team owns and maintains their sub-pages
  • Build a "Who Owns What" page in the Team Directory to resolve ambiguity as headcount grows
  • Use Notion's Business plan for SAML SSO, advanced permissions, and audit logs
  • Create a "New Client Kickoff" template page in the Resource Library — run every new client through it
  • Designate one Notion admin per department to maintain data hygiene and archive old content quarterly

Migration Guide

Move Your Team Into Notion in One Week

A realistic day-by-day plan for teams migrating from Slack + email + Google Drive. Each day has a clear deliverable so momentum never stalls.

Day
1

Set Up the Workspace Structure

Duplicate the FreelanceOS team template into your Notion workspace. Rename it with your company name. Configure top-level permissions: decide who gets full access vs. comment-only vs. view-only. Invite your team as members. Do not migrate any content yet — just get everyone oriented in the space and confirm they can log in. End of Day 1: your team has access to an empty-but-structured workspace.

Day
2

Migrate Your Active Projects

Open your current project tracking tool (Trello, Asana, ClickUp, or spreadsheet) and add every active project to the Project Pipeline database. Fill in status, owner, deadline, and priority. Do not migrate completed or archived projects — they create noise and never get looked at. Focus on the 5–15 things your team is actively working on right now. End of Day 2: your current workload is visible in one place.

Day
3

Build the Team Wiki Foundation

Identify your top 5 "frequently asked" processes — the things new team members ask about most, or that you find yourself explaining over and over. Write one SOP page for each. Keep them short: one purpose statement, numbered steps, and a "gotchas / edge cases" section at the bottom. Perfect is the enemy of done here. A rough SOP in Notion is infinitely more useful than a perfect SOP still living in someone's head.

Day
4

Move Your Resources & Brand Assets

Upload your core brand assets (logos, brand guide, color palette, fonts) to the Resource Library. Add links to your shared Google Drive or Dropbox folders for large files rather than uploading everything. Create a "Contracts & Templates" section with your standard contract, proposal template, and invoice template. This is the day you replace the "can you send me the logo?" Slack messages forever.

Day
5

Set Up OKRs & Goals

Enter this quarter's company objectives and key results into the OKR tracker. If you don't have formal OKRs yet, use this day to define 1–3 priorities for the next 90 days. Connect each active project in your pipeline to the relevant OKR. This single step — linking daily work to quarterly goals — is what most teams never do, and it is the difference between a busy team and a directional team.

Day
6

Run Your First Async Standup

Have every team member fill in their first async standup entry. Send a message to your team Slack channel: "Starting Monday, all standups happen in Notion. Link is in the Team Hub. No more morning Zoom calls." Make this the day you officially deprecate the daily standup meeting. If there is resistance, explain the timezone and flexibility benefits — and point out that async standups are searchable, while meeting recordings are not.

Day
7

Archive the Old Tools & Go Live

Send one final communication: "Our team workspace is now Notion. [Link to Team Hub]. The old Trello/spreadsheet/Confluence is read-only for 30 days, then archived." Update your Team Hub with today's date as the official "go live" date. Run a 15-minute walkthrough call (optional but recommended) for any team members who are not yet comfortable. The 30-day archive window gives people confidence — nothing is deleted immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions About Notion for Teams

How many users can share one Notion workspace? +

On Notion's free plan, up to 10 guests can be added to a workspace as collaborators. Notion's Plus plan (formerly Personal Pro) allows unlimited full members. For true team use where everyone needs to create and edit pages, the Plus or Business plan is recommended. The FreelanceOS template works on any plan — including the free tier for small teams of 2–5 where one person is the primary workspace owner and others are invited as guests.

Is Notion free for small teams? +

Yes — Notion's free plan supports unlimited pages and blocks for individuals and allows up to 10 guests (collaborators with limited rights). For teams of 3–10 who need full member permissions, unlimited version history, and admin controls, Notion Plus costs $8–$10 per member per month billed annually. There is no per-seat fee for the FreelanceOS template itself — you pay $29 once and use it forever with as many team members as your Notion plan allows.

How do we handle permissions in a shared Notion workspace? +

Notion lets you set permissions at the workspace, page, and block level. You can make pages full-access (edit), comment-only, view-only, or private to specific members. The FreelanceOS team template ships with a recommended permission structure: Team Hub and Announcements are view-only for most members, the Project Pipeline is full-access for the team, and the Resource Library is view-only by default with edit rights granted per folder. This stops accidental deletions while keeping the workspace collaborative.

Can we use Notion for client collaboration? +

Yes. Notion supports guest access — you can invite a client as a guest with view-only or comment-only permissions on specific pages, without giving them access to your internal workspace. You can also share a public link to a page that anyone can view without a Notion account at all. The FreelanceOS template includes a client-facing project status page design that you can clone per client and share as a read-only link. Clients get real-time project visibility without needing logins or paying for a Notion seat.

How do we prevent team members from accidentally breaking things? +

Notion has a page lock feature that prevents any edits to a page — ideal for finalized SOPs, policies, and the onboarding checklist template. For databases, you can restrict them to comment-only for most members so people can leave feedback without altering the data structure. Notion also has a 30-day version history (90 days on the Business plan) so any accidental deletion or unwanted edit can be restored with one click. The FreelanceOS template documentation page covers which pages to lock and which to leave open as part of your setup checklist.

Is Notion secure for business data? +

Notion encrypts data at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+). The Business plan adds SAML SSO and audit logs for enterprise-grade access control. Notion is SOC 2 Type II certified and GDPR compliant. For most small teams and agencies, Notion is entirely appropriate for project management, internal documentation, and team coordination. It is not recommended as primary storage for highly regulated data such as medical records or financial transaction logs where specific compliance frameworks (HIPAA, PCI-DSS) apply — but for the vast majority of business workflows, it is secure and compliant.
