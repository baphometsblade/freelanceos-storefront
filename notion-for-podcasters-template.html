Sound Familiar?

Running a Podcast Is Harder Than It Should Be

Every podcaster hits the same walls. Not because podcasting is hard — but because the workflow tools were never designed for how shows actually get made.

📝

Ideas Scattered Everywhere

Episode ideas live in six different notes apps, voice memo recordings, Twitter bookmarks, and random texts to yourself. Half of them never get used because you can't find them when you need them. The best episode ideas die in your camera roll at 2am.

📥

Guest Leads Lost in Your Inbox

You DM'd a great potential guest three months ago, got a tentative yes, and now can't find the thread. Your outreach is reactive rather than systematic. There's no pipeline, no follow-up reminder, no record of who you pitched and what happened. Opportunities disappear into the inbox void.

⚙️

Production Is Inconsistent Every Week

Sometimes an episode takes 4 hours from recording to publishing. Other times it takes 12. There's no standard checklist, so different tasks get done in different orders, steps get missed, show notes go out half-finished, and the whole thing feels chaotic even when the content is great.

📊

Growth Metrics Are Never Reviewed

You know downloads are going up — kind of — but you've never sat down to compare episode performance, identify your best topics, or understand which distribution channels are actually driving growth. You're making decisions based on gut feel instead of a 60-second data review.

8 Powerful Modules

Everything Your Show Needs, In One Workspace

FreelanceOS Pro includes eight fully built-out databases and templates that cover every part of running a professional podcast — from the first spark of an idea to the last repurposed clip.

Module 01

🎙️ Episode Pipeline

The beating heart of your show. Every episode moves through a clearly defined kanban pipeline from raw idea all the way to published. You always know exactly where each episode stands, what needs attention, and what's coming next — no more episodes falling through the cracks or publish dates being missed because someone forgot to finish the show notes.

  • Six-stage kanban: Idea → Outlined → Scheduled → Recorded → Editing → Published
  • Episode number, title, format (solo / interview / panel), and topic category fields
  • Target publish date with calendar view integration and overdue episode alerts
  • Episode outline notes field with templated structure for consistent episode prep
  • Status tracking linked to Production Tracker for editing team coordination
  • Linked Guest field pulls in guest details from the Guest CRM automatically
  • Episode performance field linked to Growth Dashboard for post-publish tracking
Module 02

🤝 Guest CRM

Your guest pipeline is a full customer-relationship-management system built specifically for podcast hosts. Whether you're a solo interviewer running one guest per week or an industry show booking twenty guests a month, this database keeps every relationship organized, every follow-up triggered, and every guest feeling well-taken-care-of from first pitch to post-air promotion.

  • Guest name, bio, headshot link, and one-line intro summary stored per record
  • Contact information, email, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram social handles
  • Pitch status field: Prospected → Pitched → Response Received → Confirmed → Recorded → Aired
  • Recording date, episode number linked, and scheduled calendar integration
  • Follow-up checklist: pre-show brief sent, post-episode social share, thank-you sent, clip shared
  • Notes field for personal context, talking points, and interview preparation
  • Referral tracking: who referred this guest and guest referrals they've made
Module 03

📄 Show Notes Template

Show notes are one of your most powerful SEO and listener-retention assets, yet most podcasters write them rushed and inconsistently. This module gives every episode a structured template that produces professional show notes every single time — the kind that rank in Google, get highlighted in podcast apps, and turn first-time listeners into subscribers.

  • Episode summary paragraph template: hook, main topic, key argument, takeaway
  • 3–5 key takeaways pulled from the episode in bullet format for skimmability
  • Timestamped chapter markers with auto-formatting for podcast apps and YouTube
  • Resources mentioned section: books, tools, links, and guest recommendations
  • Guest bio and social links section for interview episodes with copy-paste format
  • Call-to-action block: subscribe, review, newsletter, sponsor mention
  • SEO title and meta description field for podcast website and RSS optimization
Module 04

⚙️ Production Tracker

Consistency in production is what separates shows that grow from shows that stagnate. The Production Tracker is a per-episode checklist that standardizes your entire post-recording workflow. It works whether you're a solo host editing your own audio or running a team with a remote editor, a show notes writer, and a social media manager handling different deliverables.

  • Recording date logged with audio file location and raw file length noted
  • Editor assigned field with expected delivery date and editing status dropdown
  • Audio quality notes: any sections that need re-recording or cleanup flagged
  • Per-episode publishing checklist: edited audio, show notes, artwork, RSS, social posts
  • Quality review gate before marking as ready to publish with reviewer sign-off
  • Publishing platform checklist: Spotify, Apple Podcasts, YouTube, website post
  • Time-per-episode tracking to identify and reduce production bottlenecks over time
Module 05

♻️ Content Repurposing Planner

One podcast episode contains enough raw material for a week of content across every platform you're on — if you have a system to extract it. Most podcasters publish their episode and move on, leaving 80% of the value on the table. This planner turns your episode into a full content calendar without requiring significantly more time or creative energy.

  • Clip identification: 3–5 highlight moments tagged with timestamps for short-form video
  • Newsletter blurb template pre-structured for email digest with key insight and CTA
  • Social post drafts: tweet thread, LinkedIn post, Instagram caption, Facebook post
  • LinkedIn long-form article angle: which insight could be expanded into a 500-word piece
  • YouTube upload status: thumbnail, title, description, and chapter markers tracked
  • TikTok/Reels clip status with aspect ratio, caption, and hook text fields
  • Repurposing completion status per episode to ensure every piece gets extracted
Module 06

📈 Audience & Growth Dashboard

Data without a review habit is useless. This dashboard gives you the structure to do a monthly performance review in under 10 minutes — so you always know which episodes are resonating, which topics to create more of, and how your show is trending over time. Stop flying blind and start making decisions that compound your growth episode by episode.

  • Downloads per episode logged in a sortable database for quick performance ranking
  • Review count tracker by platform: Apple Podcasts, Spotify, Goodpods
  • Subscriber milestone log with date achieved to visualize growth acceleration
  • Best-performing episodes table with title, topic, format, and download delta
  • Month-over-month growth percentage field with trend sparkline visualization
  • Episode type performance comparison: solo episodes vs. interviews vs. panel episodes
  • Monthly growth reflection template: what worked, what to repeat, what to test next
Module 07

💰 Sponsor & Monetization Hub

Monetizing a podcast involves tracking more moving parts than most CRMs are built for. This module organizes every sponsor relationship, deal term, ad placement, and payment in one place — so you never miss an ad read, accidentally double-book a placement, or lose track of a renewal conversation. It also functions as a pitch tracker for cold sponsor outreach.

  • Sponsor company name, contact person, email, and pitch status per relationship
  • Deal terms: campaign duration, CPM or flat rate, ad type (pre-roll, mid-roll, post-roll)
  • Ad placements per episode with episode number linked and read script attached
  • Payment tracking: amount invoiced, payment due date, payment received confirmation
  • Renewal date and renewal conversation notes to proactively manage relationships
  • Sponsor performance notes: click-through feedback and campaign results to share
  • Revenue dashboard: monthly sponsorship income totaled and forecasted
Module 08

📚 Research & Resource Library

Every great podcast episode starts with research. This library is where you collect, organize, and surface the background knowledge that makes your content authoritative. It replaces the scattered browser bookmarks, email newsletters, and dog-eared books with a single searchable knowledge base that gets smarter as your show grows.

  • Topic research database: article links, summaries, and relevance notes by topic category
  • Expert sources directory: recurring guests, potential future guests, and academic experts
  • Background reading list with read status, key insights extracted, and episode linkage
  • Recurring segment prep: templates and frameworks for your show's regular segments
  • Industry news tracker: publications and newsletters you monitor with key stories flagged
  • Quote and stat library: memorable quotes and compelling data points ready to cite
  • Research request workflow: queue topics for research before scheduling episodes
How It Works

From Idea to Published in Three Clear Stages

The Podcast OS is built around one simple loop: capture ideas consistently, move them through production reliably, and extract maximum value from every episode you publish.

1

Capture Every Episode Idea

Log ideas from anywhere — voice memos, showers, Twitter threads, newsletter inspirations — directly into your Episode Pipeline as Idea-stage cards. Each idea gets a title, format tag (solo, guest, panel), and a topic category. Nothing is lost. Your best episodes start as rough ideas captured in the right place at the right time, not genius strokes of planning that happen at your desk.

2

Move Through Your Production Pipeline

When an idea is ready to develop, move it to Outlined, then Scheduled, then Recorded. The Guest CRM coordinates your guest booking while the Production Tracker keeps editing and publishing on a consistent schedule. Every episode follows the same path through the same checkpoints — so your production effort goes down as your episode count goes up and muscle memory takes hold.

3

Repurpose Every Episode for Maximum Reach

The day you hit publish is the beginning of an episode's content life, not the end. The Content Repurposing Planner extracts clips, newsletter blurbs, social posts, and LinkedIn articles from every episode. The Growth Dashboard tells you which episodes are performing. The Sponsor Hub tracks the revenue you're generating. Every piece compounds on the next.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You Want to Know Before You Buy

Does the Notion podcast template work for both solo and interview-format shows? +
Yes, absolutely. The system is designed to serve every podcast format. Solo hosts use the Episode Pipeline to move from raw idea through outline, recording, editing, and publishing without dropping the ball. Interview shows get the most out of the Guest CRM, which tracks pitch status, recording dates, follow-up needed, and social handles for post-episode promotion. Co-hosted shows use the Production Tracker to coordinate between hosts and editors. The system is modular — use the pieces that fit your format and ignore the ones that don't. You get all eight modules regardless of your format, and most podcasters find they're using six or seven of them within the first month.
Does the Notion podcast planner help with guest outreach and management? +
Yes — the Guest CRM is one of the most-praised modules in the entire system. Each guest gets their own dedicated record with name, bio, contact info, LinkedIn and social handles, pitch status (which moves from Prospected → Pitched → Response Received → Confirmed → Recorded → Aired), the recording date, the episode they're linked to, and a follow-up checklist for post-episode promotion. You will never lose a guest lead in your inbox again. You'll always know exactly where every guest relationship stands, who needs a nudge, and which confirmed guests haven't gotten their pre-show brief yet. Several interview-show hosts told us this module alone was worth the purchase price.
Is this Notion podcast template good for a new podcast with only 10 episodes? +
It is actually the perfect time to set up the system — before the disorganization scales. The Episode Pipeline, Guest CRM, and Production Tracker are immediately useful from your very first episode and prevent the disorganization that kills most new shows before episode 20. The Growth Dashboard and Sponsor Hub become more powerful as your catalogue grows, but even a 10-episode show can use them to establish baselines and set targets. The podcasters who start organized from episode one consistently grow faster because they can see what's working and double down on it, rather than spending years experimenting by feel and then trying to retrofit structure onto an already messy operation.
Can I manage multiple podcasts with this template? +
Yes. The system is built on Notion's relational database architecture, which means you can duplicate the core workspace for each show and keep them completely separate, or run a unified multi-show dashboard that aggregates episodes, guests, and metrics across all your projects. Several users manage three to five shows simultaneously using the FreelanceOS Podcast OS as their central command center. The Sponsor Hub is especially valuable for multi-show podcasters who are managing different deal terms, ad placements, and payment schedules across separate feeds. You purchase once and can use it across as many shows as you run.
Does the template include a publishing checklist for each episode? +
Yes. The Production Tracker includes a per-episode publishing checklist that covers every step from recording through publication: audio file delivered to editor, editing completed, quality review done, show notes written, timestamps and chapters added, episode artwork uploaded, RSS feed submitted, social posts drafted, newsletter blurb written, and guest notification sent. Each item is a checkbox so you can see at a glance where every episode is in the production pipeline. Nothing gets published half-finished, and you never have to hold the "did I send the guest their clip?" thought in your head again — it lives in the checklist.
Does the Notion podcast system work for video podcasts and YouTube? +
Yes. The Content Repurposing Planner includes a YouTube upload status field alongside clip identification, newsletter blurbs, and social post drafts. Whether you're recording video-first and stripping the audio for your podcast feed, or doing audio-first and uploading the video version to YouTube as a secondary channel, the system tracks both workflows. Many FreelanceOS users run active video podcast channels on YouTube with subscribers in the tens of thousands and use the repurposing planner to systematically turn every episode into a full week of content. The Production Tracker also includes separate checklist items for audio delivery and video upload so neither falls through the cracks.
