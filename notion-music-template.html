Built for Every Kind of Music Creator

Whether you write alone or collaborate, record at home or in a studio — this system adapts to how you work.

✍️

For Songwriters

Your song is more than chords and a melody. The songwriter section is designed to capture everything that goes into a song — and the business behind it.

  • Lyric drafts with version history — keep every version of your lyrics, labeled by date, so you can always go back to that better line from verse 2.
  • Co-writer credit tracking — log every collaborator, their contribution percentage, and publishing split so there's never an argument about who wrote what.
  • Publishing split documentation — record PRO affiliations, publisher assignments, and split percentages per song for accurate royalty reporting.
  • Inspiration board — save reference tracks, mood images, lyric fragments, voice memos, and artist references that inspired each song.
  • Chord progression and key notes — document the musical structure alongside the lyrics so covers, live performances, and demo sessions run smoothly.
🎛️

For Producers & Beatmakers

Your work is technical, collaborative, and often client-driven. The producer section handles the business side of your sessions so you can stay focused on the music.

  • Project file tracker — log every session project with DAW type, file path, BPM, key, linked stems, and revision history so nothing gets lost.
  • Client beat request log — track every beat request by client, deadline, reference tracks, agreed style, and payment status.
  • Sample clearance notes — document every sample used, its source, clearance status, and any license fees or split agreements.
  • Collaboration credit tracker — log producer credits, mixing engineer credits, and featured artists per track for accurate metadata and liner notes.
  • Beat pricing and licensing tiers — document your lease and exclusive license pricing, terms, and usage rights per beat or beat pack.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about using Notion for your music career.

Can I use Notion to manage my music career?
Yes — Notion is one of the most flexible tools available for musicians because it combines database views, linked pages, and free-form notes in one place. You can track song ideas with status tags, plan releases, log rehearsals, manage gig contacts, and track every revenue stream. This template is purpose-built for musicians so you don't have to build the system from scratch — duplicate it and start using it today.
Is this template good for both songwriters and producers?
Yes. The template includes separate sections optimized for each workflow. Songwriters get lyric drafts with version history, co-writer credit tracking, and publishing split documentation. Producers and beatmakers get a project file tracker with DAW links, a client beat request log, sample clearance notes, and a beat pricing system. Both workflows share the Release Planner and Income Tracker.
Can my whole band use this template?
Yes. The band workspace is designed for shared use — invite all members to a shared Notion workspace. Everyone can see rehearsal schedules, setlists, tour dates, merch inventory, and band finances. It works on Notion's free tier, so there's no extra cost to add bandmates.
How does the Release Planner work?
The Release Planner is a Notion database with Kanban and timeline views for albums, EPs, and singles. Each release has status (planning, recording, mixing, mastering, delivered, released), release date, distribution platform, budget, and linked tracks. It also includes a pre-release marketing checklist — press release, playlist pitching, social media, and distributor submission — linked to each release record.
Does this template help me track sync and licensing opportunities?
Yes. The Sync & Licensing Pitch Tracker lets you log every pitch to music supervisors, ad agencies, and licensing libraries. Each record tracks the contact, the track pitched, status (sent, in review, placed, declined), any license fee agreed, and follow-up dates. Filter by placement type — TV, film, ads, games — and see your placement rate over time.
What income streams does the Income & Royalty Tracker cover?
The tracker covers all major musician revenue streams: streaming royalties (Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube), live performance fees, sync and licensing placements, teaching income, merchandise, Bandcamp and direct sales, Patreon and fan support, and session work. Each entry is tagged by income type so you can generate quarterly reports and spot which revenue streams are growing. For full tax management, pair it with our Notion Freelance Tax Template.
