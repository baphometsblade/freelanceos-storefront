Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a replacement for shop management software like Mitchell or Shop-Ware?
No. FreelanceOS Pro is a business organization and practice management tool, not a full shop management system. It does not handle automated invoicing, parts lookup integration, or real-time inventory. It's best for independent mechanics and small shops who want to organize their jobs, customers, and parts orders outside of — or before committing to — dedicated shop software.
Can I track parts orders from multiple suppliers?
Yes. The Parts Log ties parts to each job — supplier, part number, cost, ordered date, and received date. The Supplier Directory stores your go-to vendors separately. Together they give you a clear picture of what's on order, from whom, and which job each part belongs to.
Can I use this for fleet account management?
Yes. The Fleet Account Manager is a dedicated section for managing commercial fleet clients — vehicle list, service intervals, billing contact, and account notes. It's designed for mechanics managing recurring work from companies with multiple vehicles.
How does the ASE Certification Tracker work?
Each certification gets its own entry — test area (A1–A9, L1, etc.), certification date, renewal date, and test prep notes. You can filter by upcoming renewal date to see which certifications need attention in the next 12 months. No more surprise lapses.
Does this work if I run a mobile mechanic service?
Yes. The job tracker and customer vehicle database work just as well for mobile mechanics as for shop-based technicians. Notion's mobile app lets you access the workspace from your phone while on-site — log job notes, check a customer's service history, or record parts ordered, all from the driveway.
