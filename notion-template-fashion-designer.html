Frequently Asked Questions

Is this Notion template designed specifically for fashion designers?
Yes — every database and workflow is built around the fashion designer's creative and business cycle: collection development, fabric sourcing, client fittings, lookbook planning, supplier relationships, and business financials. It works for ready-to-wear designers, bespoke tailors, accessories designers, and independent fashion brands.
Can I track both bespoke client orders and collection development at the same time?
Yes. The Collection Tracker handles multi-look seasonal collections from concept through production, while the Client CRM manages individual bespoke clients with measurement notes, fitting appointments, and order history. Both databases live in the same workspace so you can manage a private fitting while simultaneously developing a full seasonal collection.
How does the fabric sourcing log help with procurement?
The Fabric and Material Sourcing Log tracks every fabric, trim, and hardware item with supplier, item description, minimum order quantity, lead time, price per meter or unit, and colorways in stock. Organized by type and supplier, it lets you search your sourcing history to find fabrics from past collections, compare supplier pricing, and plan procurement timelines for a new season based on known lead times.
Can I plan a full lookbook shoot from this template?
Yes. The Lookbook and Campaign Planning section tracks shoot date, photographer, location, stylist, hair and makeup, model, shot concepts, garment list per look, and post-production status. Managing a lookbook shoot involves coordinating multiple creative professionals simultaneously — having all logistics in one tracker prevents the scheduling confusion that delays shoot dates and inflates production costs.

One-time purchase of $29. You get lifetime access to the template and all future updates with no recurring fees. No monthly subscription, no per-collection fees, and no annual renewal required.
