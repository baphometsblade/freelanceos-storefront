Per application: critical deadline categories — office action response window (typically 3 months statutory, extendable to 6 months with extension fees), PCT national phase entry (30 months from priority date for US national phase), maintenance fee windows (3.5/7.5/11.5 year from grant date with 6-month grace period), Paris Convention priority deadline (12 months from provisional filing date). Administrative awareness tool only — your primary docketing system must independently track all statutory deadlines. Never rely on a general tool as sole deadline tracker for prosecution.

Can this replace my patent docketing software?
No — and it's important to understand why. Patent docketing software (CPI/Dennemeyer/Anaqua/IPfolio/FoundationIP/Clio) is purpose-built for statutory deadline management, secure document storage, and USPTO rules compliance. FreelanceOS Pro manages your business operations: application pipeline overview, client billing, business analytics, and professional reference notes. How does the USPTO deadline calendar work?
The USPTO Deadline Calendar is an administrative awareness tracker — it gives you a high-level view of critical deadline categories across your prosecution portfolio: office action response windows (typically 3 months statutory, extendable to 6 months), PCT national phase entry (30 months from priority date), maintenance fee windows (3.5/7.5/11.5 year from issue), and Paris Convention priority deadlines (12 months from provisional filing). This is a supplemental awareness tool — your docketing system tracks the official deadlines with the precision that statutory compliance requires.
What technology areas does this template cover?
The application tracker includes technology area fields covering mechanical, electrical, software, chemical, biotech, materials, medical device, consumer products, and automotive.
