Brazil has one of the largest freelance economies in Latin America. With over 30 million self-employed workers and the MEI (Microempreendedor Individual) regime making it easier than ever to formalize, more Brazilians are choosing freelance and solopreneur life every year. Platforms like Workana, 99Freelas, GetNinjas, and Freelancer.com.br connect millions of jobs annually — and the digital economy opens the door to international clients paying in USD or EUR.

The challenge? Brazilian freelancers deal with administrative complexity that freelancers in other countries rarely face: Nota Fiscal emissions, DAS monthly payments, IRPF (imposto de renda) tracking, and the legal and contractual differences between CLT employment and PJ (Pessoa Jurídica) contract work. Managing all of this across spreadsheets, WhatsApp, and paper is inefficient and error-prone.

Notion has become the tool of choice for organized Brazilian professionals because it's flexible, free to start, and handles the complex administrative needs of MEI management without the monthly subscription cost of dedicated accounting software. You build the system once, and it runs your business indefinitely.

What Brazilian Freelancers Actually Need in Notion

A generic productivity template — even a good one — doesn't cover the Brazilian-specific administrative layer that every freelancer here has to manage. Here's what a Brazil-optimized Notion workspace needs to include:

MEI (Microempreendedor Individual) Management

The MEI regime (for annual income up to R$81,000/year as of 2026) is the most popular legal structure for Brazilian freelancers. It's low-cost, low-complexity, and protects you legally as a formal business entity. Your Notion workspace needs to track the key MEI obligations throughout the year:

  • Monthly DAS payment status — The Documento de Arrecadação do Simples Nacional, currently around R$71,60/month for general MEI (varies by category). Mark as Paid, Pending, or Late each month.
  • Annual MEI declaration (DASN-SIMEI) — Due by May 31 each year, this reports total revenue for the prior year. Late filing results in fines. Notion can show a countdown to the deadline.
  • Revenue tracking against the R$81,000 cap — If you approach the cap, you need to plan the transition to Simples Nacional or Lucro Presumido, which requires opening a CNPJ (Empresa Individual or Ltda.). Notion's running revenue total makes this visible before it becomes a crisis.
  • Annual revenue summary for DASN — A filtered rollup of monthly income provides the exact number you need for the annual declaration.
🏢 MEI Manager Database (suggested structure) Properties: Month (Date), DAS Status (Select: Paid/Pending/Late), DAS Value (Number), Revenue This Month (Number), Notes (Text), Cumulative Revenue YTD (Formula) Useful views: - Calendar: DAS due dates highlighted - Table: Monthly revenue summary with YTD total - Board: Status (Paid / Pending / Late) — your compliance at a glance
Important: DAS values change annually based on Brazil's minimum wage (salário mínimo). Always check the current value at gov.br/mei before setting up your tracker. The 2026 general MEI rate shown above is approximate — confirm the current figure for your CNAE category.

Nota Fiscal Organization

Regardless of whether you're operating as MEI or a registered PJ company under Simples Nacional, organizing your Notas Fiscais (NF-e for products, NFS-e for services) is essential for tax compliance and professional credibility.

Notion helps you organize:

  • Issued NFs — by client, service type, CNAE code, and value. Track whether each NF has been paid against.
  • Received NFs from suppliers — for expense documentation and deductibility analysis.
  • NF status — Emitida / Cancelada / Devolvida / Em disputa.
  • Service category — which CNAE code applies to each job. This matters if you have multiple CNAE codes registered.
MEI Note on Nota Fiscal: Standard MEI contractors (excluding MEI Caminhoneiro and some transport MEIs) do not issue Nota Fiscal. However, corporate clients often require a formal receipt (recibo) or an invoice from the MEI's CNPJ. Organize all receipts and recibos in Notion just as you would NFs — the database structure is identical.

Pix Payment Log

Pix has transformed freelance payments in Brazil since its launch in November 2020. Instant, free, 24/7 — it's now the default payment method for Brazilian client relationships. As a freelancer receiving Pix, your Notion workspace should include a dedicated payment log:

  • Log each Pix received (value, sender/client, date, project reference)
  • Cross-reference each payment against issued recibos or NFs
  • Monthly reconciliation: total Pix received vs invoices issued — catch missing payments
  • Annual summary: total income received via Pix by client and month
Tax alert: Pix transactions above R$5,000/month from the same sender may be flagged by the Receita Federal for review under the e-Financial system. This is not necessarily a problem if your income is properly declared — but maintaining a Notion log of all transactions means you have clean records if questions arise.

IRPF — Imposto de Renda Tracking

Brazilian freelancers earning above the annual IRPF exemption threshold (R$30,639.90/year for 2026 — confirm current year's value) must file their imposto de renda. Notion income tracking throughout the year dramatically reduces the stress of declaration season in April:

  • Track all income sources by category — serviços prestados, aluguéis, dividendos, rendimentos de aplicações
  • Carnê-Leão compliance — self-employed income above the monthly exemption limit requires monthly Carnê-Leão payments. Your Notion income log feeds directly into this calculation.
  • Deductible expenses — education (R$3,561.50 annual limit for 2026), health (unlimited), dependents (R$2,275.08 per dependent). Track these throughout the year.
  • Year-end summary for your contador — a filtered, date-ranged view of all income by category is exactly what your accountant needs in March.

Getting Paid as a Brazilian Freelancer

Domestic Payments (BRL)

Pix is the default for Brazilian client relationships. Set your Pix key using CPF for individual clients (personal) or CNPJ for your MEI (professional, better for accounting purposes). Pix is instant 24/7/365, has no fees for individuals, and is universally accepted. Log every Pix received in your Notion payment tracker as described above.

TED/DOC transfers are older bank transfer methods still used by some corporate accounting departments, especially for larger payments that go through formal purchase order systems. TED settles same-day if sent before the bank's cutoff time. DOC settles the next business day. Both typically require an invoice or NF as supporting documentation.

Boleto payments are still used for some clients, particularly in B2B relationships where the client's accounts payable department processes invoices on a fixed schedule. Log boleto payments separately with issue date, due date, and confirmation date (they can take 1–3 business days to process after payment).

International Clients — USD and EUR Payments

Brazilian freelancers working with international clients face additional complexity beyond just currency exchange. Every foreign payment entering Brazil is subject to IOF (Imposto sobre Operações Financeiras) and must be declared in your annual IRPF as foreign income converted to BRL at the PTAX rate on the receipt date.

Payoneer

Popular with Brazilian freelancers for USD/EUR receipt from platforms like Upwork, Fiverr, and direct clients. Your Notion log should capture: original currency amount, PTAX rate on date of Payoneer receipt, BRL equivalent, IOF applied (0.38% on inflow), net BRL received, and the date transferred to your Brazilian bank account (which is the date for IRPF purposes).

Wise (TransferWise)

Increasingly popular for better exchange rates and lower fees than traditional wire transfers. Same tracking applies: foreign amount, conversion rate at receipt, BRL equivalent, fees paid, net received. Wise provides detailed transaction reports that can be copied to Notion for year-end reconciliation.

💱 International Payment Log (Notion template structure) Properties: - Client (Relation → Clients DB) - Invoice Number (Text) - Currency (Select: USD / EUR / GBP / AUD) - Foreign Amount (Number) - PTAX Rate (Number — from Banco Central on receipt date) - Amount BRL (Formula: Foreign Amount × PTAX Rate) - IOF (Formula: Amount BRL × 0.0038) - Platform Fee (Number) - Net BRL Received (Formula: Amount BRL - IOF - Platform Fee) - Receipt Date (Date — for IRPF tax year assignment) - Status (Select: Pending / Received / Declared) Views: - Table: All international income, sortable by year - Board: Status (Pending / Received / Declared) - Filter: Year = current year → IRPF annual total
IRPF on foreign income: Dollar-denominated freelance income must be converted to BRL using the PTAX rate published by Banco Central do Brasil for the date of receipt. If you're using Payoneer or Wise as an intermediary, the receipt date is when the payment arrives in those systems, not when you transfer to your Brazilian bank. Track this carefully — the difference can affect which tax year the income falls in.
