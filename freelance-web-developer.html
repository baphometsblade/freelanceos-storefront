FAQ

Questions from web developers specifically

Does this replace my project management tool (like Linear or Trello)?

For most solo freelancers and small teams, yes. FreelanceOS Pro's Project Manager handles deliverables, milestones, and status tracking in a way that's purpose-built for client work — not internal product development. If you're doing complex sprint planning for a product team, you'd still want a dedicated tool. But for managing 3–8 client projects simultaneously, this is more than enough.

Can I track hourly and fixed-price projects in the same system?

Yes. The Invoice Generator and Time Blocker both support hourly and fixed-rate billing. You can flag a project as hourly or fixed, log time against it, and the invoice auto-populates with the right totals. Retainer clients are tracked separately in the Finance Dashboard so you can see true MRR.

I already use Notion but my workspace is a mess. Is this a full migration?

You get a clean duplicate link on purchase. You can start fresh in a new Notion workspace or import it into your existing one — it doesn't overwrite anything. Most developers keep it as a separate "Business OS" workspace alongside their personal notes, which is the recommended approach.

What if I'm just starting out and don't have many clients yet?

The Proposal Builder and Client CRM are actually most valuable when you're early-stage — they help you look professional and land clients faster. Many buyers say they landed their first $3–5k client within weeks of setting up their proposal templates and CRM pipeline.
