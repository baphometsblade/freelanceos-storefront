UK Freelance Context: What Makes It Different

Self Assessment and the January 31 Deadline

Every UK freelancer and sole trader must file a Self Assessment tax return with HMRC. The online filing deadline is 31 January each year, covering the previous tax year (which runs 6 April to 5 April). The chaos that arrives in late January for the unprepared — hunting through bank statements, chasing client invoices for confirmation, trying to reconstruct six months of mileage logs — is entirely avoidable with proper income tracking from day one.

FreelanceOS Pro includes an income tracker with client, project, and payment date fields. At year end, filter by tax year and you have your gross income figure ready. Add your allowable expenses (tracked separately in the expenses log) and you have everything an accountant or self-filing freelancer needs.

IR35: Contract Consideration for UK Freelancers

IR35 is the UK's off-payroll working legislation. If HMRC determines that your working relationship with a client looks like employment rather than genuine self-employment, you may owe additional income tax and National Insurance. The rules changed significantly in 2021 for medium and large private-sector engagements, with responsibility shifting to the end client to determine IR35 status.

Good contract management is part of IR35 defence. Keeping clear records of deliverables, project scope, right of substitution clauses, and the fact that you work for multiple clients simultaneously all support outside-IR35 status. FreelanceOS Pro's contract notes and client database give you a structured place to maintain this documentation.

VAT Registration: The £90,000 Threshold

As of 2026, UK freelancers must register for VAT once their taxable turnover exceeds £90,000 in any rolling 12-month period. Many freelancers choose to register voluntarily before this threshold — particularly if their clients are VAT-registered businesses who can reclaim the VAT, making the registration cost-neutral for both parties.

Tracking your rolling 12-month revenue accurately is essential to avoid a surprise mandatory registration. An income tracker that shows month-by-month totals with a rolling 12-month view makes this straightforward.

UK Freelancer Tax Fundamentals (2026)

Understanding your tax obligations as a UK sole trader or limited company director is non-negotiable. Here is a brief overview of what UK freelancers need to track:

  • Income Tax: Charged on profits above the Personal Allowance (£12,570 in 2026/27). Basic rate 20% up to £50,270; higher rate 40% above that.
  • National Insurance (Class 2 and 4): Sole traders pay Class 4 NIC at 9% on profits between £12,570 and £50,270, then 2% above. Class 2 contributions are now voluntary but still relevant to State Pension entitlement.
  • Self Assessment deadline: Online filing by 31 January. Tax payment also due 31 January, with a payment on account on 31 July.
  • Allowable expenses: Equipment, software subscriptions (including Notion), home office costs (simplified or actual), professional development, and professional fees are all potentially deductible.
  • VAT: Mandatory registration once taxable turnover exceeds £90,000 in any rolling 12-month period.

Note: This is general information only and does not constitute tax advice. Consult a qualified UK accountant for advice specific to your situation.
