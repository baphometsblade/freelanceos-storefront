For producers working with 5-10 artists simultaneously, this view replaces the mental juggling act with a clean operational picture updated in real time.

🎼 Beat Catalog — Lease, Exclusive, and Revenue by Track

Your complete beat catalog in one searchable database: every beat and instrumental with title, genre, mood, BPM, key, date created, current license availability (exclusive, non-exclusive lease, sold out), price for each tier, which platforms the beat is listed on, how many times it has been licensed in total, and cumulative revenue generated per beat. Every producer should know which 20% of their catalog generates 80% of their beat sales — the catalog view makes that data visible and actionable.

👤 Artist & Client CRM — Relationships, Rates, and Collaboration History

A full relationship database for every artist, ghost-production client, sync agency, indie label, and film or TV music supervisor you work with: contact info, preferred genres and sonic references, past collaborations and project history, agreed rates and payment terms, payment history, NDA status, referral source, and a relationship quality rating. For ghost producers managing multiple artist clients with different rates and confidentiality requirements, the CRM keeps every relationship organized and legally protected. Sync contacts get their own notes on submission preferences and the types of tracks they are actively looking for.

💰 Revenue & Royalties Tracker — Income by Type and PRO Statements

A complete income log organized by revenue type: beat lease fees, exclusive beat sale income, custom production fees, mixing or mastering service revenue, sync licensing fees, recording studio session income, and PRO royalty payments from ASCAP, BMI, or SESAC. Royalty entries include payment date, statement period, total amount, and per-track breakdown when available. Quarterly and annual totals calculated automatically from entries. At tax time, the revenue tracker gives you a clean, organized income record across every revenue stream — instead of reconstructing income from BeatStars exports, PayPal statements, and a stack of PRO royalty PDFs.

📝 Licensing Log — Every License Issued, Terms, and Duration

A per-license record for every license you have ever issued: date, licensee name, beat title, license type (lease, exclusive, sync), media type licensed for (music video, film, TV show, game, commercial, advertisement), territory, license duration, fee paid, and contract file reference. The licensing log is your legal paper trail showing who has what rights to which beats under what terms and for what period. When a dispute arises, a rights inquiry comes in, or an exclusive buyer asks if their purchase was honored, the log gives you a factual, timestamped answer in seconds rather than an email search.

🎛️ Studio & Equipment Notes — Gear Inventory and Plugin License Tracker

A gear inventory log for studio insurance purposes: every microphone, audio interface, monitor, keyboard, outboard piece, and hardware unit with make, model, purchase price, and current estimated value — the information your insurance broker needs for an accurate equipment rider. The plugin license tracker logs every plugin and virtual instrument with the vendor, license type (subscription vs. perpetual), renewal date, and whether the license is active — so you never lose a key plugin mid-project to a lapsed subscription. Studio session booking log tracks room utilization if you rent studio time to other artists.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Music Producers

How does the beat catalog work in Notion?

The beat catalog stores every beat and instrumental with fields for title, genre, mood, BPM, key, date created, current availability (exclusive, lease, non-exclusive, or sold out), price for each license tier, which platforms the beat is listed on, how many times it has been licensed, and total revenue generated. Filter by genre to see your trap or R&B catalog, or sort by revenue to identify your top-earning tracks. Every producer should know which beats are driving their income — the catalog makes that data visible and actionable.

How does the licensing log protect me legally?

The licensing log records every license issued with the date, licensee name, beat title, license type, media licensed for, territory, license duration, fee paid, and contract reference. This log is your paper trail showing who has rights to which beats under what terms and for how long. When a dispute arises or a rights inquiry comes in, you have a factual, timestamped answer in seconds rather than an email search that may not surface what you need in time.

Can I track PRO royalties in Notion?

Yes. The revenue and royalties tracker logs PRO royalty payments from ASCAP, BMI, or SESAC with the payment date, statement period, total amount, and per-track breakdown when available. Tracking royalty statements over time helps you identify which tracks generate the most performance royalties and ensures you can reconcile your PRO account history for tax and accounting purposes. It also gives you a running picture of passive royalty income across your catalog.

How does the artist CRM work for ghostproducing?

The artist and client CRM tracks every relationship with the artist name, contact info, preferred genres, past collaborations, agreed rates, payment history, and NDA status. For ghost producers managing multiple artist clients with different confidentiality requirements, the CRM keeps each relationship's context clear and legally organized. NDA status is tracked per client so you never accidentally disclose a ghost production relationship, and payment history gives you a full financial record of each collaboration.

How does the studio equipment tracker help with insurance?

The studio and equipment notes log your gear inventory with make, model, purchase price, and current estimated value — the information your insurance broker needs to write an accurate equipment rider on your home studio or professional studio policy. Most producers are significantly underinsured because their gear value is never formally documented. The plugin license tracker also logs renewal dates for subscription tools across Waves, Native Instruments, FabFilter, and Splice so you never lose access to a key plugin mid-project from a lapsed subscription.
