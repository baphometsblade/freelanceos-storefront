The Problem

Why Pet Business Owners Lose Clients and Income

For Pet Sitters & Dog Walkers

Daily Reports and Client Management for Care Professionals

Pet sitting and dog walking businesses run on trust. The FreelanceOS template gives you the tools to deliver professional, consistent communication that builds long-term client loyalty.

1

Daily Report Template

Every visit gets a quick-fill report: food and water given, potty trips and results, activity level and duration, mood and energy notes, and any observations worth flagging to the owner. The template pre-fills the pet name and date — you only fill in the visit specifics. Takes 90 seconds to complete and owners love receiving it.

2

Key and Access Tracker

Log every client's access method — key on file, lockbox code, garage code, building fob — in a secure note linked to their pet profile. Track which keys you have physically in your possession versus which are in storage. When a client moves or changes their code, you update one record and everything is current.

3

Emergency Contacts Per Pet

Every pet profile has an emergency contact section with owner's primary and backup phone numbers, the regular vet's name and number, the nearest emergency vet, and any specific health instructions to relay in an emergency. This is the information you need in 30 seconds when something is wrong.

4

Recurring Client Scheduling

For recurring clients on a weekly schedule, the appointment database has a recurring booking view that shows your regular clients by day of week. Monday morning walkers, Tuesday afternoon visits, Friday overnight stays — all visible in a single filtered view so you can confirm your week at a glance every Sunday evening.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Notion to run my dog training business?

Yes — Notion is an excellent system for dog trainers. You can maintain detailed profiles for every client dog, track skill progress session by session, manage your appointment calendar, monitor package usage, and track business income all in one place. The FreelanceOS Pet Business Template is pre-built for trainers, sitters, walkers, and groomers.

Does the template include a training progress tracker?

Yes. The Training Progress Tracker is one of the core modules. Each dog has a skills database where you log which commands and behaviors have been introduced, are in progress, or have been mastered. Each training session gets a notes entry with what was worked on, how the dog responded, what to focus on next, and any behavior patterns to flag for the owner.

How does the service package tracker work?

The Service Package Tracker shows each client's purchased package — 5-session basic obedience, 10-session reactivity program, unlimited monthly sitting — with the number of sessions purchased, sessions used, and sessions remaining. When a package is running low you see a visual indicator so you can reach out about renewing before the client realizes they are almost out.

Can I use this template for pet sitting and dog walking, not just training?

Absolutely. The template includes dedicated sections for pet sitters and dog walkers: a daily report template with food, water, potty, mood, and activity fields; a key and access tracker; emergency contacts per pet; daily photo and video log structure; and recurring client scheduling. You can run your sitting business and your training business side by side in the same workspace.

Does the template track vaccination and health records?

Yes. The Vaccination and Health Record Reminders module stores each pet's vaccination history with the vaccine name, date administered, and expiry date. A formula flags any vaccinations expiring within 30 days so you know which clients need to update their records before the next appointment — essential for groomers and boarding facilities that require proof of vaccination.

What does the business income section track?

The Business Income and Expenses module tracks revenue by service type — training sessions, package sales, sitting visits, grooming appointments — and business expenses by category. Monthly and annual views show your revenue trends, your top-earning services, and your profit margin. This makes tax season straightforward and helps you identify which services to promote or phase out.
