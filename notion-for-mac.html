Notion's Mac app is built for Apple Silicon, integrates natively with macOS, and pairs perfectly with tools like Raycast, Alfred, and Apple Shortcuts. Notion Mac App vs Browser: Which Should You Use?

If you are a freelancer, consultant, or creator who lives in Notion all day, the native Mac app is the clear winner over the browser tab. The app delivers offline access, native macOS notifications, Dock badge counts, multi-window support, and meaningfully better performance on Apple Silicon machines. The browser is fine for occasional access, but anyone serious about Notion as a daily operating system should install the Mac app and make it their default.

⚡ Native Mac App

  • ✓ Native arm64 build on Apple Silicon — faster loads, lower CPU usage
  • ✓ True offline mode — cached pages work without internet
  • ✓ System notifications via macOS Notification Center
  • ✓ Dock icon with unread mention badge count
  • ✓ Multi-window support — open multiple pages simultaneously
  • ✓ macOS keyboard shortcut integration (Cmd+Tab switching)
  • ✓ Notification icon in menu bar
  • ✓ Stage Manager and Mission Control compatible
  • ✗ Slightly slower cold start vs a pinned browser tab

🌐 Browser (Chrome / Safari / Arc)

  • ✓ Easy sharing — copy URL from the address bar
  • ✓ Tab groups keep Notion with related project tabs
  • ✓ Developer tools available for inspection
  • ✓ Works without any installation
  • ✗ No offline support whatsoever
  • ✗ Slower on Apple Silicon — web rendering overhead
  • ✗ No macOS Dock icon or badge counts
  • ✗ Notifications go through browser, not macOS system
  • ✗ Competes with other tabs for browser memory

Apple Silicon Performance: The Real Difference

Notion shipped a native arm64 build for Apple Silicon Macs — M1, M2, M3, and M4 chips. This is not just a marketing claim. Running native means the app executes instructions directly on Apple's architecture without going through Rosetta 2 translation. In practice, you get noticeably faster page load times, smoother scrolling through large databases with hundreds of rows, and significantly less battery drain during long working sessions. On an M-series MacBook, Notion can sit open all day alongside Figma, VS Code, or a browser heavy with tabs, consuming minimal CPU. That performance headroom matters when your entire business runs through one tool.

To download the native Mac app, visit notion.so/desktop and grab the macOS installer. It is also available on the Mac App Store. On Intel Macs the app runs fine — you just will not see the Apple Silicon performance advantages. If you are on an older Mac and Notion feels sluggish, that is a hardware ceiling rather than a software problem.

Multi-Window Support: An Underused Mac Superpower

Multi-window mode is one of the most underutilized Notion Mac features. Right-click any page in the sidebar and select "Open in New Window" to open it independently. Hold Cmd while clicking any internal link to open it in a new window. You can have as many Notion windows open as your RAM supports, and each maintains its own navigation history with Cmd+[ and Cmd+] working independently per window.

For multi-monitor setups, pin your Client CRM or Project Dashboard on a secondary screen as a permanent workspace view while your main display handles active editing. For single-monitor MacBook users, Split View (long-press the green traffic light) tiles Notion beside another app — your email client, a PDF reference, a browser tab — for side-by-side working without constant app switching. On a large iMac or Pro Display XDR, three or four Notion windows can be arranged in a tiled grid covering every major area of your work at once.

💡

Mission Control tip: Swipe up with three fingers (or press F3) to see every open Notion window at once. Assign different Notion windows to different Spaces — one Space for client work, one for admin, one for personal — and switch between them with a three-finger horizontal swipe. Each Space remembers its window layout and restores it when you return.

Setting Up Your Notion Workspace on Mac

The Mac app gives you a blank canvas, but a thoughtful initial setup transforms Notion from a flexible note tool into the central operating system for your freelance business. Here is how experienced Notion power users structure their workspace from day one.

Sidebar Organization: The 3-Level Hierarchy

The most scalable Notion structure uses a three-level hierarchy that mirrors the way real work is organized. Rather than a flat list of pages or a deeply nested maze, this approach keeps navigation fast and every page findable within two clicks:

1

Areas (Top-Level Pages)

Areas represent permanent, always-on domains of your work and life: Client Work, Business Admin, Personal, Learning, Health. These never get archived or deleted. In the sidebar, pin your Areas as top-level pages and give each a distinct emoji so you can identify them by icon alone without reading. Aim for four to six Areas maximum — more than that and the sidebar becomes difficult to scan quickly.

2

Projects (Sub-Pages Under Each Area)

Projects are time-bounded initiatives living inside a parent Area. A Website Redesign for Acme Co. lives under Client Work. A Q3 Tax Filing lives under Business Admin. Projects have a defined start and an expected completion. When a project wraps, move it to an Archive sub-folder rather than deleting it — archived projects build a searchable history of your work over time, invaluable for proposals, case studies, and personal reflection.

3

Resources (Reference Databases)

Resources are evergreen reference material that supports multiple Areas and Projects: your Client Database, Invoice Templates, Knowledge Base, Brand Asset Library, Reusable Copy Snippets. Keep Resources in their own top-level Area called "Resources" or "Reference." Link to them from Projects using Notion's @ mention system — this prevents duplication and keeps your authoritative information in one place that stays current.

Quick Capture with Notion Web Clipper

The Notion Web Clipper extension for Chrome, Safari, and Firefox lets you clip articles, web pages, screenshots, and selected text directly into any Notion database with one click. Install it and configure it to send all clips to a dedicated Inbox database — a staging area you process weekly. During your weekly review, go through the Inbox, tag each item by topic, and move it to its permanent home in your Knowledge Base or relevant Project. This GTD-inspired capture pattern keeps your main workspace clean while ensuring nothing you find on the web disappears.

macOS Widgets: What Actually Works Today

As of 2026, Notion does not offer a native macOS Desktop or Notification Center widget. The practical workarounds for Mac users:

  • Raycast Floating Notes: Raycast's persistent floating note panel is always accessible with a hotkey and serves as a fast capture surface before you process notes into Notion later.
  • Third-party Notion widget apps: Apps available in the Mac App Store can pull Notion page content and surface it as a macOS widget, though they require API access configuration.
  • Scriptable (free on Mac): Power users can write JavaScript in Scriptable to create custom widgets that query the Notion API and display today's tasks, current project status, or upcoming deadlines directly on the Desktop.

Full-Screen vs Split-Screen: Choosing Your Mode

For writing-heavy sessions — drafting proposals, documenting processes, writing SOPs — go full-screen (Ctrl+Cmd+F) and combine it with Cmd+\ to hide the sidebar. The result is a clean writing environment as distraction-free as any dedicated writing app. Activate dark mode with Cmd+Shift+L to reduce eye strain during long sessions.

For research and reference sessions — cross-checking client notes while writing a report, reviewing finances while processing invoices — use Split View to tile Notion beside a browser, PDF reader, or your email client. The ability to see two Notion contexts simultaneously, or Notion alongside another tool, is where the Mac app's multi-window capability transforms your daily workflow in ways a single-window setup simply cannot replicate.

Sidebar state memory: Notion remembers your sidebar state between sessions. If you prefer a minimal default, collapse the sidebar with Cmd+\ before closing the app — it reopens collapsed next time. Notion also remembers the last page you had open, so ending each day on your Daily Note or Dashboard means it greets you first thing the next morning.

Notion Mac Integrations That Actually Save Time

The Mac ecosystem contains a set of productivity tools that integrate with Notion in ways unavailable on other platforms. From keyboard launchers to system-level automation, these are the integrations where Mac + Notion users pull ahead in workflow efficiency.

Raycast Most Recommended

Raycast is a free Mac launcher that has become the go-to productivity tool for developers, designers, and independent professionals who want to control everything from the keyboard. The official Notion extension is available free in the Raycast Store — open Raycast, go to Preferences, click Extensions, then Store, and search "Notion." Connect it using a Notion API integration token created at Notion Settings → Connections → Develop or manage integrations.

With Raycast and the Notion extension configured, your daily workflow changes meaningfully:

  • Search Notion from anywhere: Press your Raycast hotkey, type "notion" followed by your search term. Results appear in under a second from any app without switching to Notion.
  • Create new pages instantly: The "Create Notion Page" command lets you specify a title and parent page or database without touching the Notion app. Assign it a direct hotkey like Option+N for capture in under three seconds.
  • Quick capture to Inbox: Configure a Raycast command that always creates a new page in your Inbox database. Press the hotkey, type your thought, press Enter — captured without breaking your current focus.
  • Open recent documents: "Recent Notion Pages" shows your last ten visited pages in two keystrokes — faster than navigating the Notion sidebar, especially in large workspaces.
  • Append to daily note: The extension's "Append to Page" command lets you add text to your current Daily Note from any app — ideal for capturing a thought mid-meeting without opening Notion.

Raycast is entirely free for individual users. If you use Notion seriously on Mac and have not installed Raycast yet, this integration delivers the single highest-impact workflow improvement available.

Alfred Power Users

Alfred is the original Mac launcher and remains the choice for power users with years of invested custom workflows. The Alfred Powerpack (one-time license) is required for Workflows, but it unlocks deep automation capabilities including Notion integration. If you have already bought the Powerpack and built Alfred workflows over the years, there is no reason to switch to Raycast — the capability depth is comparable.

For Notion + Alfred, the best community resources are:

  • Packal.org — the community Alfred workflow repository. Search "Notion" for maintained workflows with documentation and version history.
  • GitHub — search "Alfred workflow Notion" for open-source workflows whose underlying code you can read, fork, and adapt to match your exact workspace structure and naming conventions.

Common Alfred + Notion capabilities include searching Notion pages from the Alfred launcher, creating a page in a specific database via keyword trigger, jumping to frequently-used Notion pages through Alfred Favorites, and chaining Apple Shortcuts that interact with the Notion API for more complex automations.

Apple Shortcuts App Automation

Apple's Shortcuts app — built into macOS Monterey and later — can interact with Notion via the Notion API using the "Get Contents of URL" action for HTTP requests. Once configured, these automations run natively across Mac, iPhone, and iPad simultaneously, creating a unified Notion automation layer across your entire Apple device fleet — a capability no third-party tool can fully replicate.

The most valuable automations to build with Apple Shortcuts + Notion:

  • Daily Note Creator: A Shortcut that runs each morning — creates a new page in your Daily Notes database with today's date as the title, sets the date property automatically, and optionally pulls your calendar events into the page body using Shortcuts' calendar integration. Trigger it from the Mac Dock or assign a keyboard shortcut for one-tap daily setup.
  • Log Time Entry: A one-tap Shortcut that prompts for project name and duration, then creates a time tracking entry in a Notion database. Run it from the Mac menu bar, iPhone, or Apple Watch after completing any work block. Over weeks this builds an accurate record of where your hours actually go.
  • Share Sheet Capture: A Shortcut in the macOS Share Sheet — highlight any text or right-click any web page and send it to your Notion Inbox with the source URL and a timestamp attached automatically. Replaces the Web Clipper extension for power users who prefer a keyboard-driven flow.
  • Weekly Review Prompt: A Shortcut scheduled to run every Friday at 4pm that opens Notion to your Weekly Review template and sends a notification. The automation removes the need to remember — your workspace is ready when you are.

Shortcuts on Mac also supports folder watch triggers and Focus filter automations — meaning you can automatically create a Notion page when your Work Focus activates, or when a file drops into a watched folder. The depth of macOS system integration is Apple Shortcuts' unique advantage over third-party automation tools.

Apple Calendar & Date Sync Workaround Required

Notion does not offer native two-way sync with Apple Calendar — this is the most frequently requested missing feature. The practical options available in 2026:

  • Notion Calendar (formerly Cron): Notion's official companion app displays your Google Calendar or iCloud Calendar events alongside Notion tasks in a unified calendar view. Free for individuals, available at notion.so/product/calendar. This is the closest thing to official Apple Calendar integration and the recommended starting point.
  • Notion Database Calendar Views: Set any database with Date properties to Calendar view inside Notion itself. A full monthly calendar of your tasks, projects, and milestones — not Apple Calendar, but extremely functional for planning within Notion.
  • Fantastical: Add Notion page URLs into Fantastical event notes (fantastical.app) so you can jump from a calendar event directly to the Notion page in one click — a simple manual bridge that works surprisingly well in practice.
  • Zapier or Make automations: A Zapier automation can watch a Notion database for new entries with dates and push them into Apple Calendar as events via the iCloud Calendar integration. Reliable one-way sync from Notion to your system calendar for deadline visibility.
📅

The Notion Calendar app is the official Notion team's answer to calendar integration. It connects to Google Calendar and iCloud Calendar and shows both alongside your Notion tasks in one view. Install it as a companion to the main Notion Mac app — together they form a strong two-app productivity system for anyone who lives between a calendar and a knowledge base.

iCloud Drive & Files Best Practice

Notion supports file attachments — PDFs, images, videos, and spreadsheets can all be attached directly to pages. However, Notion is not a file storage system, and treating it as one leads to a bloated workspace, slower page loads, and eventual storage limits. The recommended approach treats Notion and iCloud Drive as complementary systems with distinct, non-overlapping roles:

  • Store files in iCloud Drive, reference from Notion: Keep actual files — design source files, contracts, project archives, deliverables — in an organized iCloud Drive folder structure. In your Notion Client CRM or Project pages, add a URL or text property that links directly to the corresponding iCloud Drive folder. One click opens it in Finder.
  • Attach only small, frequently-viewed reference files: A signed contract PDF for quick reference, a one-page spec document, a brand guide screenshot — these make sense as Notion attachments because you need them inline with the page content. Large video files, PSD source files, and archive zips belong in iCloud Drive.
  • Client folder strategy: Create one top-level iCloud Drive folder per client. In your Notion Client database, add a "Files" property linking to that client's iCloud folder. Everything about the client — information in Notion, files in iCloud — is accessible from one Notion record with one click.

This dual-system approach keeps Notion fast and purposeful. iCloud Drive provides Spotlight-indexed, version-controlled file storage with automatic backup. Notion provides structured information management, databases, and linked context. Each does what it does best — neither tries to replace the other.

Spotlight & Quick Entry Workflows

A common frustration for new Notion Mac users: Notion pages do not appear in Spotlight search results. You cannot search your Notion knowledge base with Cmd+Space the way you would search local files. Understanding why — and knowing the best alternatives — is essential for building a fast Mac workflow around Notion.

Why Notion Does Not Appear in Spotlight

Spotlight indexes local files, Mail messages, Calendar events, Contacts, and applications that build native Spotlight extensions. Notion is a cloud-based application that stores all content on Notion's servers — there are no local files for Spotlight to index. Notion has not built a Spotlight extension as of 2026. The good news: Raycast and Alfred both offer Notion search that is genuinely faster than Spotlight would be, since they query the Notion API directly and return results in real time rather than depending on a local index that can be stale.

Quick Note Workflow with Raycast

Once Raycast is installed with the Notion extension, the fastest quick-capture flow on Mac becomes:

  1. Press your Raycast hotkey (default Option+Space, or replace Spotlight entirely by setting it to Cmd+Space in Raycast Preferences)
  2. Type your configured alias for the "Create Notion Page" command — something short like "qn" works well
  3. Press Tab to enter the page title
  4. Type your note title or thought
  5. Press Enter — the page is created in your configured Notion database instantly

This complete capture flow takes under four seconds from any application. Pre-configure your Raycast Notion command to always target your Inbox database so you never have to choose a destination mid-capture. The decision of where something belongs happens during your weekly review, not during capture. Separating capture from organization is the key cognitive habit that makes quick-capture systems actually work in practice.

Daily Journal Automation with Apple Shortcuts

An Apple Shortcut that creates your Daily Note automatically is one of the highest-leverage automations you can build. The basic architecture:

  1. Open Shortcuts on Mac and create a new Shortcut called "Create Daily Note"
  2. Add a Get Current Date action to capture today's date
  3. Add a Format Date action to output YYYY-MM-DD (e.g., "2026-06-17")
  4. Add a Get Contents of URL action with Method: POST, targeting your Notion database API endpoint, with a JSON body that sets the page title to the formatted date and populates your date property
  5. Add an Open URL action using the page ID from the API response, prefixed with notion://www.notion.so/ to open the new page in the Mac app automatically
  6. Set a Focus automation to run this Shortcut when your Work Focus activates each morning, or when you connect to your work Wi-Fi network

The result: a fresh, pre-dated Daily Note appears in Notion before you open the app each morning. Over months this builds a chronological journal of your work — fully searchable, linkable to projects, and queryable by Notion AI for patterns and retrospectives.

Focus Modes + Notion: The Right Configuration

macOS Focus Modes (System Settings → Focus) let you define context-specific notification rules. The right setup for Notion users:

  • Work Focus: Allow Notion notifications for @mentions and reminders only. Silence page comment and general activity notifications. You stay reachable for urgent collaboration while blocking routine noise.
  • Deep Work Focus: Silence all Notion notifications without exception. Activate for 90-minute focus blocks when you need uninterrupted concentration. Enable Focus Status Sharing so collaborators see you are unavailable.
  • Personal Focus: Silence all work-Notion notifications. Allow personal Notion reminders if you keep personal projects in a separate workspace or section.

Stage Manager Compatibility

macOS Ventura and later include Stage Manager, which groups windows by app and task into discrete clusters on the left edge of your screen. Notion is fully compatible and works well within Stage Manager groups. A practical Stage Manager setup for freelancers: create a "Client Work" group with Notion open to the active project page alongside your email client; a "Writing" group with Notion in full-screen distraction-free mode; an "Admin" group with Notion's Finance Dashboard alongside your invoicing tool. Clicking any group in Stage Manager instantly restores that entire context — all windows at their last size and position. Context-switching between work modes becomes a single click rather than a multi-step window rearrangement.

Menu Bar & Notification Tips for Mac

Notion's Mac app integrates with macOS notifications, but the default settings tend toward too many alerts. For anyone doing focused knowledge work in Notion, notification configuration is as important as any workflow tip — the wrong defaults create a constant stream of interruptions that undermine the concentration Notion is designed to support.

Configuring Notion in macOS Notification Settings

Open System Settings → Notifications → Notion to control how Notion alerts appear on your Mac. Recommended settings for most knowledge workers:

  • Alert Style: Set to Banners (appear briefly and auto-dismiss) rather than Alerts (require manual dismissal). Banners let you register a notification and ignore it if not urgent — Alerts block your screen until you click them away.
  • Show in Notification Center: Enable. Notifications that arrive during Focus mode accumulate in Notification Center for batch review via a left-edge trackpad swipe.
  • Sounds: Disable for Notion. Visual banners are sufficient, and notification sounds have a disproportionate impact on concentration even when you intellectually know to ignore them.
  • Badges: Enable badge counts on the Dock icon. The red number showing unread mention count lets you gauge at a glance whether anything needs attention without opening Notion.
  • Show Previews: Set to "When Unlocked" rather than "Always." This prevents sensitive client mentions from appearing on screen during screen sharing or when working in a public space.

The Dock Badge: Using It as a Triage Signal

The Notion Dock icon badge shows your total unread @mentions and comment notifications as a red number. The productive way to use it: develop the habit of glancing at the badge count during natural transition moments — between tasks, before a break, at the start of a communication window — rather than responding to each notification immediately as it arrives. Batch your Notion responses into two or three dedicated windows per day rather than distributing them across all your focus blocks. This single behavioral change typically recovers 45-60 minutes of focused working time per day for Notion-heavy users.

Notion In-App Notification Controls

Beyond macOS system settings, Notion has its own notification controls. Go to Notion → Settings (Cmd+,) → Notifications to configure which events trigger notifications at all. You can toggle individually:

  • Direct @mentions: High priority — keep on. Someone specifically needs your attention.
  • Page comments: Medium priority — consider batching. Review during scheduled communication windows rather than immediately.
  • Reminders: High priority — keep on. You set these intentionally as commitments to yourself.
  • Database activity: Low priority for most solo users — consider disabling entirely to dramatically reduce notification volume without losing anything important.

Focus Modes: The Master Notification Strategy

The most powerful approach combines Notion's in-app controls with macOS Focus Modes for time-aware notification behavior. Set up a Deep Work Focus Mode in System Settings → Focus. Under Allowed Notifications, remove Notion entirely. When you activate Deep Work Focus — via the menu bar, a keyboard shortcut, or an automatic schedule tied to your calendar — all Notion notifications are silenced without any manual setting changes. The Focus reverts automatically when your scheduled work block ends. Add the Focus item to your menu bar (System Settings → Control Center → Focus → Show in Menu Bar) for one-click activation from anywhere on your Mac.

Enable Focus Status Sharing so that collaborators in Notion and compatible apps see you are in a Focus mode and know not to expect an immediate response. This passive communication eliminates manual status posting when you go into a concentration block — it sets expectations automatically without requiring you to context-switch to tell people you are unavailable.

Recommended rhythm: Keep notifications fully silent during your morning focus block (first 90 minutes of the day), banners only during collaborative working hours with batch-review every 60-90 minutes, and completely silent during evenings. This structure honors deep work and collaboration without constant negotiation with your notification settings every day.

Frequently Asked Questions: Notion for Mac

Is Notion free on Mac?
Yes, Notion has a generous free plan available on Mac. The free plan includes unlimited pages, unlimited basic blocks, and collaboration with up to 10 guests. You can create databases, boards, calendars, and use all standard block types without paying anything. Paid plans — Plus, Business, and Enterprise — add unlimited version history so you can restore any page to any previous state, Notion AI for generating and summarizing content, advanced team permission controls, and larger file upload limits. Importantly, templates like FreelanceOS work fully on the free Notion plan. You do not need a paid subscription to get complete value from a well-built template system.
Does Notion have a native Mac app?
Yes. Notion offers a native macOS app available from notion.so/desktop or from the Mac App Store. On Apple Silicon Macs (M1, M2, M3, M4 chips), it runs as a native arm64 application compiled specifically for Apple's architecture — not through Rosetta 2 translation. This delivers faster page loads, smoother database scrolling, and meaningfully lower battery drain compared to running Notion in a browser on the same machine. The Mac app also includes features not available in the browser version: offline mode, macOS Notification Center integration, Dock badge counts, and multi-window support. On Intel Macs, the app also runs natively with all the same Mac-exclusive features, minus the Apple Silicon performance advantages.
How do I use Notion offline on Mac?
The Notion Mac app includes offline mode. Pages and databases you have previously visited are cached locally and remain accessible without an internet connection. Changes you make while offline are queued and sync automatically when your Mac reconnects to the internet. For reliable offline access, visit your most important pages — your main dashboard, active project pages, and frequently-used databases — while connected to the internet so they populate the local cache. Note that Notion AI features require an internet connection and do not work offline. Very large databases with thousands of rows may not cache completely, so test your offline access before depending on it during a flight or event with unreliable connectivity.
Can I use Notion with Raycast on Mac?
Yes. Raycast has an official Notion integration available free in the Raycast Store. Open Raycast, press Cmd+Comma to open Preferences, click Extensions, then Store, and search "Notion." Install the extension and connect it to your Notion workspace using a Notion API integration token — create one at Notion Settings → Connections → Develop or manage integrations. Once configured, you can search Notion pages, create new pages in specific databases, open recent documents, and trigger quick-capture workflows — all from the Raycast launcher without switching to the Notion app. Raycast is entirely free for individual users and the Notion extension costs nothing extra.
Does Notion work with Apple Calendar?
Notion does not offer native two-way sync with Apple Calendar. Within Notion, you can set any database with Date properties to Calendar view, giving you a full monthly calendar inside Notion based on your own data. For a connection to your actual Apple Calendar, the best options are: Notion Calendar (the official companion app from Notion, formerly Cron) which displays Apple Calendar events alongside Notion tasks in a unified view; Zapier or Make automations that push Notion database entries with dates into Apple Calendar as events; or manually linking Notion page URLs into calendar event descriptions in Fantastical or BusyCal so you can jump directly from a calendar event to the Notion page. None of these are perfect two-way sync solutions, but Notion Calendar is the closest official offering.
What is the best Notion keyboard shortcut for Mac users?
Cmd+P (Command Palette) is the most powerful shortcut in Notion on Mac — it provides a searchable list of every available action without touching the mouse. Use it to change block types, insert any embed, adjust formatting, manage database views, and navigate anywhere in your workspace. Cmd+K (Quick Find) is a close second — the fastest way to jump between pages in a large Notion workspace, meaningfully faster than clicking through the sidebar hierarchy. For writers and long-form content creators, Cmd+Backslash (toggle sidebar) is essential for instantly entering a distraction-free writing environment. Learn these three first, then add others as natural opportunities arise.
How do I open multiple Notion windows on Mac?
Right-click any page in the Notion sidebar and select "Open in New Window." You can also hold Cmd while clicking any internal link or page reference in Notion to open it in a new independent window. Notion supports as many simultaneous windows as your Mac's RAM allows. This is especially valuable with multiple monitors: pin your Client CRM or Project Dashboard on a secondary screen while your main display handles active editing. Each window maintains its own independent navigation history, so Cmd+[ and Cmd+] move backward and forward separately per window. Multi-window support is exclusive to the Notion Mac app — the browser version does not support it.
Is Notion better on Mac than Windows?
Notion is cross-platform and the core feature set is identical on Mac and Windows. However, Mac users benefit from several ecosystem advantages that improve the day-to-day experience: tighter macOS integration with system notifications and Dock badges; the Raycast and Alfred launchers which have deep Notion integrations not matched on Windows; Apple Shortcuts automation that creates a unified Mac, iPhone, and iPad workflow from a single automation; Stage Manager compatibility for sophisticated multi-context window management; Apple Silicon performance on M-series MacBooks and desktops; and the general polish of native macOS applications. If you are already in the Apple ecosystem, the cross-device Notion experience — identical workspace visible on Mac, iPhone, and iPad with real-time sync — is particularly seamless and cohesive.
