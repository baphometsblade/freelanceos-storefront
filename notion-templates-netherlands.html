The Netherlands: Europe's Strongest Freelance Market

The Netherlands has one of the highest rates of self-employment in the EU. The combination of strong digital infrastructure, an English-speaking professional culture, and access to EU clients makes the Netherlands an exceptional environment for freelancers.

But operating as a ZZP'er comes with real administrative complexity: BTW registration, quarterly aangifte, KvK obligations, debteurs tracking, and increasingly complex EU cross-border billing. Frequently Asked Questions

Can ZZP'ers use Notion for administration?

Yes. Notion works well as the operational layer for ZZP'er administration: income tracking, client management, project oversight, and BTW logging. It does not replace accountancy software for official BTW aangifte filing, but most ZZP'ers use it alongside their boekhouder's tools. The combination is cleaner and cheaper than trying to do everything in one rigid accounting platform.

How do I track BTW in Notion?

FreelanceOS includes a BTW tracker database where you log each invoice with the applicable rate (21% standard, 9% reduced, or 0% reverse charge for EU clients). The template calculates BTW collected, tracks BTW paid on business expenses, and generates a quarterly totals view to share with your boekhouder or use for aangifte preparation. It handles both outgoing (omzetbelasting) and incoming (voorbelasting) BTW.

Is Notion GDPR compliant?

Notion Inc. is GDPR compliant and provides a Data Processing Addendum (DPA) for business users. Notion's EU data residency option (for Teams plan) stores data in European data centres. For ZZP'ers processing basic client contact data, most privacy professionals consider standard Notion usage acceptable under GDPR. If you process sensitive personal data (healthcare records, financial data) at scale, consult a privacy professional for your specific situation.

What tools do Dutch freelancers use?

The most common Dutch ZZP'er tech stack: Moneybird or e-Boekhouden for invoicing and BTW aangifte, a boekhouder (often via online services like Boekhoudgemak or accountant.nl) for annual tax returns, Notion or Trello for project management, Slack or Teams for client communication, and Calendly or Microsoft Bookings for scheduling. FreelanceOS replaces the project management and client relationship layer with a single, integrated Notion workspace.

How to invoice EU clients as a ZZP'er?

For EU clients outside the Netherlands, you typically apply the btw verlegd (reverse charge) mechanism — 0% BTW on your invoice with a mandatory note: "BTW verlegd / VAT reverse charged" plus the client's EU VAT number. You must verify the client's VAT number via the EU VIES system before issuing the invoice. FreelanceOS includes an EU billing checklist covering required invoice fields, VIES verification, and payment term notes. Always confirm with your boekhouder for your specific situation.

Can I use Notion instead of Exact Online?

Notion is not a replacement for Exact Online or Moneybird for official Dutch tax and bookkeeping purposes. Exact Online handles your official BTW aangifte, annual accounts, and regulatory reporting. FreelanceOS handles client relationships, project management, income visibility, and operational oversight. Most ZZP'ers who use FreelanceOS continue using their existing accountancy software alongside it — the two serve different purposes.

How do Dutch freelancers manage pension?

As a ZZP'er, pension is entirely self-directed — there is no AOW supplement for self-employed income (AOW is the state pension, which ZZP'ers do receive at retirement age, but it is rarely sufficient alone). The most common approaches are lijfrente (tax-deductible annuity), banksparen (savings-based pension), or contributions to a commercial pension provider like Brand New Day or Aegon. FreelanceOS includes a pensioenopbouw tracker to set monthly savings targets and monitor contributions. Consult a financial planner for the optimal structure for your income level and age.
