The Netherlands: Europe's ZZP Economy

The Netherlands has one of the most robust freelance economies in Europe. With over 1.2 million ZZP'ers — Zelfstandige Zonder Personeel, meaning "self-employed without personnel" — independent professionals represent a significant share of the Dutch working population. The ZZP model has become the default career structure for professionals in IT, creative services, consulting, healthcare, and engineering who prefer flexibility, autonomy, and the ability to work with multiple clients simultaneously.

The Dutch freelance market is characterised by high average day rates (particularly in IT and finance), a strong demand from corporates and the public sector for specialist contractors, and a well-structured but demanding administrative framework: KVK registration, BTW filings, IB-aangifte (income tax return), and the ongoing debate around AOV (disability insurance) for ZZP'ers.

ZZP Registration with KVK

Every ZZP'er in the Netherlands must register their business with the KVK (Kamer van Koophandel — Chamber of Commerce) before commencing business activities. The KVK registration fee is approximately €75 (one-time). After registration, you receive a KVK number (8 digits), which must appear on all your invoices and official business communications.

The KVK registration automatically triggers registration with the Belastingdienst (Dutch Tax Authority). Within a few weeks, you receive your BTW-identificatienummer (VAT identification number) in the format NL000000000B01. Both numbers — KVK and BTW-id — are required on every invoice you issue. Registration is straightforward and can be done online or at a KVK office.

BTW (Dutch VAT) for ZZP'ers

Standard BTW Obligations

Most ZZP'ers are required to charge BTW (Belasting Toegevoegde Waarde) on their services. The standard rate is 21% for most professional services. A reduced rate of 9% applies to certain categories (food, books, some healthcare), but most freelance professional services fall under the 21% rate. You must file BTW-aangifte (VAT returns) quarterly via Mijn Belastingdienst, collecting BTW from clients and remitting it minus any BTW you paid on business expenses (input tax).

For international B2B services within the EU, the reverse charge mechanism (verlegging) typically applies — you invoice without BTW and the client self-assesses. For services to non-EU clients (e.g., US companies), no BTW applies. KOR: Kleineondernemersregeling

The KOR (Kleineondernemersregeling) is the Dutch small business VAT exemption. If your annual turnover stays below €20,000, you can apply for the KOR with the Belastingdienst. Under the KOR: you issue invoices without BTW, you file no BTW returns, and you cannot reclaim BTW on business expenses. The exemption is valid for a minimum of 3 years once chosen.

The KOR is attractive for ZZP'ers with primarily consumer clients (who cannot reclaim BTW), side businesses, or those who want to minimise administrative burden in their early years. For ZZP'ers whose clients are businesses that reclaim BTW anyway, being BTW-plichtig (BTW-registered) is often cost-neutral and preferable.

Income Tax: IB-aangifte for ZZP'ers

ZZP'ers in the Netherlands pay Inkomstenbelasting (income tax) through the annual IB-aangifte (income tax return). Self-employed income falls in Box 1 (income from work and home). Key tax considerations for Dutch ZZP'ers include:

  • Zelfstandigenaftrek: A deduction for self-employed professionals of approximately €5,030 per year (2026 figure — this deduction has been reduced annually and will continue declining). Requires meeting the urencriterium (minimum 1,225 hours worked as ZZP per year).
  • Mkb-winstvrijstelling: An additional 13.31% deduction from net profit, applicable after other deductions. Available to all self-employed without the urencriterium requirement.
  • Startersaftrek: Extra deduction of approximately €2,123 for new ZZP'ers in the first three years of registration.
  • Progressive rates: Box 1 income tax rates in 2026 are approximately 36.97% up to €73,031 and 49.5% above. The effective rate after deductions is substantially lower for most ZZP'ers.
  • Voorlopige aanslag: The Belastingdienst may issue a voorlopige aanslag (provisional assessment) requiring quarterly advance income tax payments based on estimated profit.

Note: This is general information only. Consult a Dutch boekhouder or belastingadviseur for advice specific to your situation.

AOV: Disability Insurance for ZZP'ers

One of the most debated topics in the Dutch ZZP world is AOV — arbeidsongeschiktheidsverzekering (disability insurance). Unlike employees, ZZP'ers have no employer-provided safety net if they become unable to work. Private AOV premiums are expensive (often €200–€500+ per month depending on coverage and profession) and have historically been a major reason ZZP'ers are financially vulnerable.

As of 2026, mandatory AOV coverage for ZZP'ers remains under political discussion in the Netherlands. Regardless of the regulatory outcome, having a clear picture of your monthly income (for premium calculation and coverage needs) is essential — which is exactly what FreelanceOS Pro's income tracker provides.

Dutch Invoice Requirements

BTW-registered ZZP'ers must include the following on every invoice (factuur) under Dutch law:

  • Your full name and business address
  • Your KVK number
  • Your BTW-identificatienummer
  • The date of invoice
  • A unique, sequential invoice number
  • The name and address of your client (and their BTW number for B2B)
  • A description of the services provided
  • The period over which services were delivered
  • The net amount, BTW rate, BTW amount, and gross total

KOR-exempt ZZP'ers include a note on invoices: "BTW-vrijgesteld op basis van de KOR" and omit the BTW fields. FreelanceOS Pro's invoice tracker maintains sequential invoice numbering and all mandatory fields in one structured view.
