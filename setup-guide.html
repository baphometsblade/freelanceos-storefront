FreelanceOS Pro

The all-in-one Notion business OS for freelancers

A. First 10 Minutes

After duplicating the template, do these 3 things before anything else:

1
Rename the workspace to your business name Click the page title at the top and replace "FreelanceOS Pro" with your business name. This personalizes the dashboard and all linked views.
2
Add your first real client Open the Client CRM, click "New," and fill in a real client's name, email, status, and project type. Real data makes the system click immediately.
3
Set your preferences in the Rate Calculator Enter your hourly rate, monthly expenses, and income goal. The Rate Calculator auto-calculates your minimum viable rate — this is the most eye-opening feature in the whole system.
B. Core Databases Explained
Database
Client CRM

Your central contact list — every client, past, present, and potential, lives here.

NameEmailStatus Project TypeRateNotes

Connects to: Project Tracker (one client → many projects), Invoice Log (one client → many invoices)

💡 Set Status to Active/Lead/Past so you can filter your pipeline at a glance. Add every email address — you'll thank yourself at invoice time.
Database
Project Tracker

Track every project from proposal to completion — deadlines, budgets, and progress in one view.

ClientStatusDeadline BudgetType

Connects to: Client CRM (relation), Invoice Log (relation)

💡 Create a Board view grouped by Status (Proposal → In Progress → Review → Complete) for a visual kanban pipeline.
Database
Invoice Log

Every invoice in one place with automatic overdue tracking and revenue totals.

ProjectAmountDue Date Paid DateStatus

Connects to: Project Tracker (relation), Client CRM (via rollup)

💡 Add a formula property: dateBetween(now(), prop("Due Date"), "days") to auto-flag invoices that are overdue.
Database
Rate Calculator

Enter your expenses and income goal — the calculator tells you the exact hourly rate you need to charge.

Your RatesExpensesIncome Goal

Auto-calculates: minimum hourly rate, annual revenue needed, hours needed per week

💡 Run this quarterly. Your rates should go up each year as you gain experience — the calculator shows you exactly when you're undercharging.
Database
Onboarding Checklist

A reusable template for every new client — ensures a consistent, professional onboarding every time.

TaskClientStatusDue

Connects to: Client CRM (per-client checklist), Project Tracker

💡 Use the Notion template feature inside this database to create a pre-filled checklist that appears with one click for each new client.
C. Customization Tips
Add custom properties by clicking "+" next to any property — useful for niche-specific fields like "platform," "content type," or "retainer hours"
Rename columns to match your workflow — change "Project Type" to "Service Category" or whatever matches how you think
Create views: filter by Client, group by Status, or sort by Deadline — use "Add a View" in each database to build these
Use database templates for recurring tasks — click the dropdown arrow on "New" to create templates for common project types
Set up recurring tasks by duplicating checklist templates and adjusting the due date — no automation required
D. Advanced Features

🔗 Linked Views

Display a filtered view of your Project Tracker inside a Client CRM page — so each client page shows only their projects. Use "Create linked database" in a page, then filter by the client relation.

📐 Formulas

Auto-calculate totals and overdue days. Common ones: dateBetween(now(), prop("Due Date"), "days") for days until due, and prop("Budget") - prop("Invoiced") for remaining budget.

⚡ Notion Automations

Set up triggers like: when Status changes to "Paid" → set Paid Date to today. Find this in database settings → Automations. No external tools needed.

🌐 Client Sharing

Share a filtered Project Tracker view with clients via a public Notion link. They see only their projects — not your entire workspace. Use "Share" → "Share to web" with view-only access.

E. Monthly Maintenance (5 minutes)
✓
Archive completed projects by changing Status to "Archived" — keeps the active view clean
✓
Update invoice statuses — mark paid invoices, chase overdue ones
✓
Review your client pipeline — who needs a follow-up or proposal?
✓
Update your rates in the Rate Calculator if anything has changed
✓
Export key revenue data to a spreadsheet for quarterly tax review

CreatorHQ

The complete Notion command center for content creators

A. First 10 Minutes
1
Rename the workspace to your creator name or channel Replace "CreatorHQ" with your handle or channel name. This makes the dashboard feel personal and sets the right context for your content workflow.
2
Add your first 3 content ideas to the Idea Vault Open the Idea Vault, add the ideas already floating in your head. Set the platform and priority. Real data makes the system feel alive immediately.
3
Set your platform(s) and posting schedule In the Content Calendar, set up your recurring time slots — Mon/Wed/Fri for YouTube, daily for TikTok, etc. Your currency and timezone can be set in the Analytics Log.
B. Core Databases Explained
Database
Content Calendar

Your master publishing schedule — every piece of content, every platform, every date.

PlatformDateFormat TopicStatusCTA

Connects to: Idea Vault (pull from ideas), Brand Deals CRM (tag sponsored content)

💡 Use a Calendar view to see your posting schedule visually. Create a filter for each platform so you can view Instagram vs YouTube separately.
Database
Idea Vault

Never lose an idea again — this is your swipe file, inspiration log, and content backlog in one.

PlatformTypeNotes StatusPriority

Connects to: Content Calendar (promote ideas to scheduled posts)

💡 Set Status to Idea → Developing → Scheduled → Published to track the lifecycle of every idea. Filter by Priority High for your weekly planning session.
Database
Brand Deals CRM

Manage every sponsorship from first email to final payment — never lose track of a deal again.

BrandStatusRate DeliverablesDeadline

Connects to: Content Calendar (tag sponsored posts), Analytics Log (track deal performance)

💡 Add a "Rate History" note property to track how your sponsorship rates grow over time — essential for negotiation conversations.
Database
Analytics Log

Track your growth numbers week-by-week across every platform — so you always know what's working.

PlatformMetricWeekly Values

Connects to: Content Calendar (correlate posts with growth spikes)

💡 Log every Monday. 15 minutes of data entry per week gives you a 52-week growth chart by year-end — invaluable for brand pitch decks.
C. Customization Tips
Add platform-specific properties to the Content Calendar — e.g., "Thumbnail URL," "Script Link," "Hook Line"
Create a Gallery view in the Idea Vault using cover images for visual inspiration boards
Build a filtered view of the Content Calendar showing only sponsored posts for brand deal reporting
Use database templates for your recurring content formats — e.g., a "YouTube Long-form" template that pre-fills Format, Status, and includes a script outline
Add a "Content Pillars" property to tag ideas and posts by theme — lets you track content mix balance over time
D. Advanced Features

🔗 Linked Views

Embed a filtered Content Calendar view inside each Brand Deals page — so each sponsor page shows all content you've created for that brand. Use "Create linked database," then filter by brand relation.

📐 Formulas

Auto-calculate engagement rate: prop("Likes") / prop("Views") * 100. Track days since last post: dateBetween(now(), prop("Published Date"), "days").

⚡ Notion Automations

When Status changes to "Published" → set Published Date to today, move to "Published" filter view automatically. Find in database settings → Automations.

🌐 Sharing with Brands

Share a filtered Brand Deals view with sponsors as a public Notion link — they can see their deal status and deliverables without seeing your full workspace.

E. Monthly Maintenance (5 minutes)
✓
Archive published content older than 3 months to keep the calendar clean
✓
Update brand deal statuses — mark completed deals, chase outstanding payments
✓
Review your idea vault — promote top ideas to the content calendar for next month
✓
Log your monthly analytics summary and note any growth spikes
✓
Update your sponsorship rates if your audience has grown since last quarter

AgencyOS

Notion workspace built for small agencies and freelance teams

A. First 10 Minutes
1
Rename the workspace to your agency name Replace "AgencyOS" with your agency name. Every team member and client who sees the workspace should see your brand — not a template name.
2
Add your first client to the Client Portal Create a client entry with their name, status, NDA status, and start date. This is the foundation everything else builds from.
3
Add your team members and set their permissions Invite team members to the Notion workspace and set page-level sharing so each person sees what they need. Set your timezone and currency in workspace settings.
B. Core Databases Explained
Database
Client Portal

One record per client — the master view of your entire client roster with NDA, contract, and status tracking.

Client NameStatusNDA Start Date

Connects to: Team Tasks (filter by client), Project Tracker (one client → many projects), Retainer Tracker

💡 Each client record can contain sub-pages for meeting notes, brief documents, and assets — keep everything about a client in one place.
Database
Team Tasks

Every task assigned to every team member, linked to clients and projects — your team's single source of truth.

AssigneeClientDeadlineStatus

Connects to: Client Portal (relation), Project Tracker (relation)

💡 Create a personal view filtered to "Assignee = Me" for each team member's daily task list. Group by client for a client-by-client workload view.
Database
Project Tracker

Every active and upcoming project — phase, deliverables, and deadline tracked in one view.

ClientPhaseDeliverablesDeadline

Connects to: Client Portal (relation), Team Tasks (relation)

💡 Use a Timeline view to see all project deadlines at once — invaluable for capacity planning and avoiding over-booking.
Database
Retainer Tracker

Track monthly retainer clients — hours included, hours used, rollover balance, and renewal date.

ClientMonthly RateHours IncludedRollover

Connects to: Client Portal (relation), Team Tasks (hours rollup)

💡 Add a formula to auto-calculate hours remaining: prop("Hours Included") - prop("Hours Used"). Flag negative values in red with conditional formatting.
C. Customization Tips
Add a "Service Type" property to Project Tracker to filter by design, development, marketing, etc.
Create a client-facing view of the Project Tracker with sensitive columns hidden — share this URL with clients
Build a "Weekly Capacity" view in Team Tasks that shows each person's task count and deadline spread
Use Notion templates in Team Tasks for common task types — e.g., "New Project Kickoff" creates 8 standard tasks automatically
Add a "Profit Margin" formula to Project Tracker: (prop("Revenue") - prop("Cost")) / prop("Revenue") * 100
D. Advanced Features

🔗 Linked Views

Inside each Client Portal page, embed linked views of their projects, tasks, and invoices. Clients who have Notion access see a filtered view of only their data.

📐 Formulas

Calculate days overdue: if(prop("Status") != "Done", dateBetween(now(), prop("Deadline"), "days"), 0). Auto-flag anything negative as overdue.

⚡ Notion Automations

When Project Phase changes to "Review" → create a new task "Review sign-off" assigned to the project manager. Reduces missed handoff steps significantly.

🌐 Client Portals

Share a public Notion page with each client that shows their project status, deliverables, and next steps. Update it weekly for a professional client experience.

E. Monthly Maintenance (5 minutes)
✓
Archive completed projects and move them to a "Completed" filtered view
✓
Update retainer hours — confirm this month's hours are logged correctly
✓
Review client pipeline — who needs a renewal conversation or upsell proposal?
✓
Update team task assignments for the upcoming month
✓
Review project profitability — are retainer rates still right given hours used?

YouTube Empire

The complete Notion system for serious YouTube creators

A. First 10 Minutes
1
Rename the workspace to your channel name Replace "YouTube Empire" with your channel name. This connects you to the system emotionally and makes it feel like yours from day one.
2
Add your current top 3 video ideas to the Video Planner Open the Video Planner, add the ideas you're most excited about right now. Set format (Long-form, Short, Live), status, and target publish date.
3
Set up your channel's baseline analytics In the Analytics Dashboard, enter your current subscriber count, average views, and CTR. This becomes your baseline — every week you'll see your growth from here.
B. Core Databases Explained
Database
Video Planner

Your full production pipeline — every video from idea to published, with deadlines and status tracking.

TitleStatusPublish Date FormatPlatform

Connects to: Script Templates (link to template used), Sponsor Tracker (tag sponsored videos)

💡 Use a Board view grouped by Status: Idea → Scripting → Filming → Editing → Scheduled → Published. Move cards through the pipeline visually.
Database
Script Templates

Reusable script frameworks for your recurring video formats — cut scripting time in half.

TypePurposeTemplate Text

Connects to: Video Planner (link to template for each video)

💡 Build one script template per video format you make regularly. Each template should include your hook structure, main points format, and CTA section.
Database
Sponsor Tracker

Manage every YouTube sponsorship — from first pitch to final payment — with exclusivity and deadline tracking.

BrandStatusRate DeliverablesExclusivity

Connects to: Video Planner (tag sponsored videos), Analytics Dashboard (track sponsor ROI)

💡 Always log the exclusivity category — prevents accidentally working with a competitor's brand within a restricted window. This mistake is costly.
Database
Analytics Dashboard

Track views, watch time, CTR, and subscriber growth for every video — so you know what actually works.

VideoViewsWatch Time CTRSubs Gained

Connects to: Video Planner (relation to video record)

💡 Log analytics 30 days after publish, not immediately. This gives accurate performance data once the algorithm has had time to distribute the video.
C. Customization Tips
Add a "Thumbnail Concept" text property to Video Planner to capture thumbnail ideas while scripting
Create a "High Performer" filtered view in Analytics Dashboard showing videos with CTR above your channel average
Build a "Content Series" property to group related videos — useful for series planning and cross-linking
Add a "Hook" property to each video entry — forces you to nail the hook before filming begins
Use Notion database templates for your video formats: one for "Tutorial," one for "Story-driven," one for "Shorts" — pre-fills the right script template and checklist
D. Advanced Features

🔗 Linked Views

Inside each Sponsor record, embed a linked view of the Video Planner filtered by that sponsor — shows all sponsored content for that brand at a glance. Useful for brand reporting.

📐 Formulas

Calculate average view duration: prop("Watch Time (mins)") / prop("Views"). Or revenue per view: prop("Revenue") / prop("Views") * 1000 for RPM tracking.

⚡ Notion Automations

When Status changes to "Published" → set Publish Date to today, create a reminder to log analytics in 30 days. Keeps your analytics database consistently up to date.

🌐 Sharing with Sponsors

Create a sponsor-facing page showing their deal status and deliverables. Share via public Notion link. Professional, transparent, and saves you reply emails.

E. Monthly Maintenance (5 minutes)
✓
Archive videos from 3+ months ago to keep the active Video Planner clean
✓
Update sponsor deal statuses — mark completed deals, follow up on outstanding payments
✓
Log analytics for videos published 30 days ago
✓
Review high-performer analytics — what patterns made those videos work?
✓
Update your sponsorship rates if subscriber count or views have grown significantly
