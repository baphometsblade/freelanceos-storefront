A Notion template for immigration lawyers is a pre-built workspace inside Notion that organizes your entire immigration practice — visa case pipelines, client intake workflows, country-specific deadline calendars, fee agreement tracking, paralegal delegation logs, CLE credit management, and a practice dashboard — all in one connected system.

FreelanceOS Pro is built specifically for solo immigration attorneys and small immigration firms who want to eliminate the chaos of spreadsheets, sticky notes, and disconnected email threads. Instead of reconstructing context every time you open a case, everything is linked, searchable, and current.
The case pipeline tracker uses a Notion database where each case record includes fields for visa category (H-1B, L-1, EB-2, EB-3, DACA, asylum, family-based, naturalization, and any custom types you add), current stage (intake through approval or denial), assigned attorney or paralegal, client name, next action date, and linked documents.

You can filter the entire pipeline by visa type, stage, or deadline to instantly see — for example — every H-1B cap case in the RFE stage, or every asylum case scheduled for an interview in the next 30 days. Custom views let you build the filters that match how you actually run your caseload.

The deadline calendar module allows you to log USCIS filing deadlines, priority dates by country of birth and visa preference category, interview appointment dates, RFE response windows (typically 87 days from issuance), motion to reopen deadlines, and bar-mandated client notification deadlines. Each deadline entry links back to the relevant case record so you never need to search two places.

You can also set priority flags on time-sensitive dates to surface them in your daily dashboard view. Many attorneys add a 14-day and 7-day pre-warning entry for critical filing deadlines so they have buffer time for document collection.
The fee agreement tracker logs each client engagement's retainer amount, billing milestones (initial consultation, petition preparation, filing, RFE response, interview preparation, etc.), invoice dates and amounts, payments received, and outstanding balances per client. A running total view shows your aggregate receivables across all active matters.

For solo and small firm attorneys who don't run separate billing software, this provides meaningful financial visibility without the complexity of a full practice management system. The tracker also logs fee arrangement type — flat fee, hourly, or hybrid — so you can filter by billing model across your caseload.
The paralegal delegation log is a task database where every delegated case action is recorded with the assigned paralegal's name, the linked case record, the task description (collect I-94, prepare cover letter, obtain certified translations, compile exhibit list, etc.), the due date, and completion status.

Paralegals can be shared the Notion workspace with edit access on the Plus plan. Attorneys see all open tasks across all paralegals in a single filtered view — making it easy to identify which paralegal is overloaded, which tasks are overdue, and which cases are blocked waiting on a document. This replaces the half-dozen email threads attorneys typically use to track paralegal work.
The CLE tracker maintains a database of every completed CLE course — course name, provider, date, credit hours, CLE category (ethics, professionalism, immigration law, skills, etc.), and which state bar requirements it satisfies. For attorneys licensed in multiple jurisdictions, you tag each course with the applicable bars.

Filter by state bar to see your current credit balance and renewal deadline for each jurisdiction — without logging into three separate bar portal systems to reconstruct your compliance status three months before renewal. You can also log AILA chapter events, ILRC trainings, and webinars as they happen rather than scrambling to remember them at renewal time.
