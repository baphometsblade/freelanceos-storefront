Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Court Reporters and CART Providers

How does the assignment log work for court reporters?

The assignment log tracks every deposition, arbitration, hearing, and CART job with job type, attorney or scheduling agency, case reference, date, location or videoconference platform, estimated length, whether exhibits or real-time streaming are ordered, appearance fee, per-page rate, pages produced, and total invoice. Filter by status to see what is confirmed, in production, or awaiting payment. For reporters handling 15 to 40 jobs per month across multiple agencies and direct clients, having every assignment in a searchable log eliminates scheduling conflicts and missed billing that come from managing everything across email threads and paper calendars.

How does the transcript delivery pipeline work?

The transcript delivery pipeline tracks each transcript from field completion through scopist, proofreading, formatting, delivery, and payment — with dates at each stage and a status flag. You can see at a glance which transcripts are with your scopist, which are in your proofreading queue, which are formatted and ready to deliver, and which invoices are outstanding. Transcript turnaround is one of the most critical metrics attorneys use to evaluate and retain court reporters — a pipeline tracker keeps every job moving and prevents the scenario where a rush job sits undelivered because it fell through the cracks between field work and office production.

How does the attorney and agency client tracker work?

The client tracker stores every attorney, law firm, and scheduling agency you work with — contact information, scheduling preference, typical case type, per-page rate, payment terms, average jobs per month, transcript delivery preference, and outstanding balance. Know which agencies pay in 30 days versus 90, which attorneys book direct, which clients generate the most revenue, and what your established rate is with each client so you can negotiate increases and identify which relationships are worth cultivating. Your client database is a core business asset that most court reporters never organize systematically — and that shows in their ability to grow and retain high-value client relationships.

Can I track CART captioning jobs separately from court reporting assignments?

Yes. The CART captioning log tracks each real-time captioning job separately — client or institution code, subject or session type (college lecture, business meeting, conference, legal proceeding), platform, hourly rate, hours completed, transcript requested, delivery status, and invoice status. CART and court reporting have different billing models, different clients, and different workflow timelines. The separate CART log keeps both service lines organized without mixing deposition per-page billing with CART hourly billing, so your revenue reporting by service type is accurate at the end of every month.
