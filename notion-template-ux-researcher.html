Frequently Asked Questions

Can I store interview recordings in Notion?
Notion supports embedded links to recordings stored in Google Drive, Loom, or other platforms, and you can upload audio or video files directly to Notion pages. For each interview session in the Participant Database, you can link the recording alongside your notes, screener responses, and consent status in one organized place.
How do I synthesize insights from many interviews?
The Insight Synthesis Board lets you tag each observation with themes as you review session notes, then cluster tagged items into patterns across all sessions. You can filter by theme to see how frequently each insight appears — making it straightforward to identify your strongest findings before writing up the report.
Does it include usability test templates?
Yes. The Interview and Observation Notes section includes structured templates for moderated usability tests, with task scenario fields, think-aloud observation notes, post-task question prompts, and satisfaction rating captures. You can duplicate the template for each participant session and keep everything linked to the study in your Research Planner.
Can I share findings with teams who don't have Notion?
Yes. Notion allows you to publish any page as a public web page with a shareable link — no account required to view. You can share your Findings Report pages or the Research Repository directly with product managers, designers, and stakeholders who don't use Notion.
How do I build a research repository over time?
The Research Repository database is designed as a long-term archive. Each study gets an entry with method, date, product area, key findings, and links to the full report. You can search and filter by topic, date range, or product area — so past insights stay findable and reusable months or years after the study was completed.
