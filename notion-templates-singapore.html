The Singapore Freelance Economy in 2026

Singapore has quietly become one of Asia's most vibrant freelance markets. According to the Ministry of Manpower (MOM), self-employed persons now make up a growing share of Singapore's workforce, with solo consultants and platform-based gig workers rising steadily. The Freelance Association Singapore (FLAS) reports that demand for independent skilled workers — from tech and finance to creative services — has grown sharply post-pandemic as companies right-size their permanent headcounts and tap flexible talent instead.

Being a freelancer in Singapore comes with genuine advantages. The country's strong rule of law means contracts are enforceable, late payment is addressable, and businesses generally operate with professional standards. Singapore's position as a regional hub means local freelancers often serve clients across APAC — attracting project fees in USD, MYR, AUD, and HKD. Platforms like Upwork, Fiverr, and Toptal all have active Singapore-based freelancer communities, and LinkedIn has become the de facto sales channel for B2B consultants in the CBD corridor.

Tax compliance for Singapore freelancers centres on IRAS, the Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore. Self-employed persons must declare all income in their annual income tax return, track allowable business deductions, and — if annual turnover exceeds SGD $1 million — register for GST. MediSave contributions are mandatory for self-employed persons who earn above SGD $6,000 per year. Voluntary CPF contributions are increasingly popular as a tax-efficient retirement savings strategy. Keeping clean, organised financial records is not optional — it's fundamental to operating as a professional freelancer in Singapore.

Pro tip: Track all client income in Notion before filing with IRAS each year. Log every invoice the month it is issued, note the payment date, and categorise by project type. A clean Notion income tracker takes minutes to set up and saves hours at tax time — plus it makes you look professional if IRAS ever asks to verify your reported income.

Key Singapore Compliance Points to Track in Notion

  • GST registration threshold: If your annual taxable turnover exceeds SGD $1 million, you must register for GST with IRAS. Use your Notion income tracker to monitor your cumulative revenue and flag when you approach this threshold.
  • MediSave contributions: Self-employed persons earning more than SGD $6,000 per year must contribute to MediSave annually. Log these contributions in the Expense Log for accurate net income calculation.
  • Professional invoicing requirements: Singapore clients — especially corporates — expect invoices to include your full name or business name, the client's details, description of services, GST registration number (if applicable), and payment terms. The FreelanceOS invoice template covers all of these by default.
  • Allowable deductions: Track business expenses like software subscriptions, coworking memberships (e.g., WeWork or The Great Room), home office utilities, and professional development courses — all potentially deductible against your IRAS income tax.
