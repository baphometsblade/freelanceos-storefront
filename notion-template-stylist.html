Questions From Stylists

Does this work for both personal styling and editorial or commercial styling?

Yes. FreelanceOS Pro covers both sides of the styling business. The Client CRM and Wardrobe Inventory are designed for ongoing personal styling relationships — from the initial wardrobe audit through seasonal refresh appointments and retainer clients. The Editorial Pull Log handles loan tracking, return deadlines, and damage notes for commercial shoots, brand lookbooks, film, and television work. You can use both sections simultaneously if your practice spans personal and editorial styling.

How does the wardrobe inventory work in Notion?

Each wardrobe item is a record in a database linked to a specific client. You log category, brand, color, size, condition, location (in-closet, in-storage, on-loan), and last worn date, and tag each item with a keep, donate, alter, or sell recommendation. You can filter the inventory by client, category, condition, or recommendation. When a new client comes on, duplicate a blank inventory template and build theirs from scratch at the first session. Over time your inventory becomes a complete record of each client's wardrobe history.

Can I track editorial pulls and item returns in Notion?

Yes. The Editorial Pull Log is a dedicated database for commercial styling work. For each pull, you log the brand or showroom, every item borrowed with descriptions and quantities, the project the items were pulled for, the agreed return deadline, confirmation of return, and any damage notes. Keeping this log current is important for protecting yourself professionally — if a showroom claims an item was not returned or came back damaged, your pull log is your record. It takes about five minutes per pull to set up and saves significant time and stress later.

How do I organize lookbooks for different clients?

The Lookbook Planner is organized by client and season. Each lookbook contains outfit combinations built from the client's wardrobe inventory — items are linked by reference rather than duplicated, so your wardrobe database stays as the single source of truth. Each outfit entry includes the occasion (work Monday / casual weekend / evening / travel), image reference links, and client approval status. You can create as many seasonal lookbooks as needed per client, and publish any lookbook as a shareable Notion page to send to the client for review and approval before moving to the next season.
