Workspace Setup

Your VA Command Center Setup

Here is how to structure your Notion workspace to handle multiple clients without losing your mind — starting from the top level down.

1

Master Clients Table

Create one Clients database at the top of your workspace. Every client has a dedicated record with their name, status (Active / Onboarding / Offboarded), retainer amount, communication channel, tools they use, and a relation to their tasks and invoices. This is your bird's-eye view of your entire business.

2

Per-Client Dashboards

Each client record contains a linked sub-page that acts as their command center: filtered views of their tasks, their SOPs, their file links, and their communication log. When you open a client's page you see everything related to them and nothing else. Clients who get access to their own page see a clean, professional workspace.

3

Universal Task Inbox

One Tasks database for all clients. Properties include Client (relation), Priority (High / Medium / Low), Due Date, Status (To Do / In Progress / Done), and Estimated Hours. Use a filtered board view as your daily task inbox, sorted by due date. At the start of each day you know exactly what needs to happen.

4

SOP Library

Every process you repeat more than twice gets its own SOP page in your library. Tag each SOP with the client it was created for and the service category (Social Media / Email / Admin / Research). When you onboard a new client with similar needs, duplicate and adapt the relevant SOPs in minutes instead of hours.

Client Onboarding

Client Onboarding in Notion

A smooth onboarding process makes you look professional and sets clear expectations. Here is how to run the entire flow inside Notion.

When a new client signs on, you trigger a three-step Notion flow that takes under 30 minutes to complete. By the end the client has a clear view of what you are working on, and you have all the information you need to start delivering great work.

📝 Intake Form
Collect goals, tools, access needs
→
📄 Client Page Created
Duplicate template, populate details
→
✅ Kickoff Checklist
First-week tasks, access setup, intro call notes

The Intake Form is a Notion form (or a Typeform linked to your database) that collects the client's business goals, current tools, communication preferences, and required access. Once submitted, you duplicate your Client Page template, fill in their details, and share it with the client. They can view their project status, leave comments on deliverables, and see upcoming milestones at any time without needing to message you for updates.

The Kickoff Checklist is a templated sub-page inside every new client's page. It covers everything from getting access to their tools, setting up the first invoice, scheduling a kickoff call, and completing the first two weeks of deliverables. When every item is checked you are fully operational for that client.
