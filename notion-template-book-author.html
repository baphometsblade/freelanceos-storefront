Questions From Book Authors

How do book authors use Notion?

Book authors use Notion as their writing and publishing headquarters — manuscript progress tracking, chapter outlines, query submissions to literary agents, ARC reader management, book launch coordination, and royalty income tracking all in one connected workspace. Instead of managing a book launch across 12 different tools and Google Sheets, everything lives in one searchable system. FreelanceOS Pro gives you a pre-built version of this setup ready to use in under an hour.

Can Notion help me track queries to literary agents?

Yes. The Query Tracker in FreelanceOS Pro stores each agent submission with fields for agent name, agency, query date, response date, response type (no response/form rejection/personalized rejection/partial manuscript request/full manuscript request), manuscript sent (y/n), and notes. You can filter to see all open queries, all manuscript requests, and all agents who gave personalized feedback — without losing track of where you are in the querying process.

How does the ARC reader management system work?

The ARC and Review Management tracker stores each ARC reader's name, platform (NetGalley, BookSirens, direct email), ARC sent date, review due date, review posted (y/n), review link (Amazon/Goodreads/BookTok/blog), star rating, and a review quote for marketing use. You can filter to see which ARC readers haven't posted yet and how many reviews are live ahead of launch day.

Can Notion track royalties from KDP and IngramSpark?

Yes. The Author Business Dashboard includes royalty tracking by platform (KDP, IngramSpark, Draft2Digital, direct sales) and by book. You can see your monthly royalty income per book, total all-time sales by title, and how your income mix shifts across platforms over time. Particularly useful for self-published authors comparing print-on-demand vs. ebook vs. direct sales revenue.

Does this work for fiction authors, non-fiction authors, and self-help writers?

Yes. The Manuscript Tracker works for any genre — fiction, non-fiction, self-help, memoir, romance, thriller, or literary fiction. The Chapter Outline Database includes a POV character field useful for fiction authors and a scene goal field that works for both plot-driven fiction and structured non-fiction chapters. The Series Bible is included for fiction series authors tracking world-building, character names, and plot timelines across multiple books.
