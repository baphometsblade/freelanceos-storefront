Frequently Asked Questions

Questions from customer success professionals

How do customer success managers use Notion?

Customer success managers use Notion as a portfolio management system — tracking account health scores, renewal dates, QBR notes, onboarding milestones, and customer outcomes in one searchable workspace. Notion works especially well for CSMs who manage 20-50 accounts and need to see account health at a glance without maintaining five separate spreadsheets. FreelanceOS Pro gives CSMs a pre-built system with all core databases connected.

Can I track account health scores in Notion?

Yes. Notion's select property with color coding (red/yellow/green) is an effective way to track health scores — you update the score after each significant interaction or on a weekly cadence. Notion works well as a manually-maintained health dashboard for CSMs who don't have access to a dedicated CS platform, or who want a personal tracking layer alongside their company's tooling.

How do I prepare QBRs in Notion?

The QBR notes template in FreelanceOS Pro gives you a structured record for each account review: business goals discussed, KPIs reviewed, product utilization vs. targets, success stories, challenges, and agreed action items. Over multiple QBRs per account you build a searchable history of every review — valuable for renewals, expansions, and handoffs to a new CSM.

How do fractional CSMs use Notion?

Fractional customer success consultants use Notion to manage multiple client relationships from a single workspace — tracking active clients, monthly retainer revenue, onboarding progress per client, QBR cadences, and invoices. The FreelanceOS Pro template includes a freelance invoice and retainer tracker alongside the account management modules, making it a complete OS for a fractional CS practice.

What's the difference between Gainsight and Notion for CS?

Gainsight and ChurnZero are enterprise CS platforms with live data integrations, automated health scoring, and workflow automation — ideal for teams managing hundreds of accounts. Notion works best for individual CSMs or small CS teams who need to organize their own work without the cost and complexity of a full CS platform. Many CSMs use both: Gainsight for company-wide account data, and Notion for personal QBR prep, career management, and cross-account planning.
