Freelancing in France: What Every Auto-Entrepreneur Needs to Know

France has one of the most active freelance ecosystems in Europe, driven by the simplified statut d'auto-entrepreneur (also called micro-entreprise), which launched in 2009 and now has more than 3.7 million registered participants. For new and established freelancers alike, this status offers a frictionless way to start: you register through the INPI (Institut National de la Propriété Industrielle) portal, receive your SIRET number, and begin invoicing clients immediately — all without needing an accountant.

The appeal of the auto-entrepreneur régime lies in its simplicity: social charges (cotisations URSSAF) are calculated as a flat percentage of revenue — approximately 22% for service activities in 2026 — with no minimum if you earn nothing. This means low risk and low overhead for freelancers just starting out. The trade-off is the annual revenue ceiling: €77,700 for service activities (prestations de services) in 2026. Exceed this two consecutive years and you transition to a different legal structure (entreprise individuelle au régime réel simplifié).

The most popular freelance platform in France is Malt, which focuses exclusively on the French and European market and handles contract security, payment escrow, and dispute resolution. For high-profile digital and tech profiles, Comet and Crème de la Crème offer curated placement. International platforms like Upwork and Fiverr supplement local work, especially for English-language projects. Key freelance sectors in France include development and tech, design and digital creation, marketing and communications, translation and content writing (French is a highly demanded language globally), consulting, and photography/video production.

Payment infrastructure for French freelancers runs primarily through SEPA virement bancaire (bank transfer) — the dominant B2B payment method — alongside PayPal for international work, and increasingly Stripe for online clients. Key freelance communities include the Malt Community, Indépendants & Co, and the MoiJeSuis collective, which advocates for the rights and interests of independent workers in France.

En tant qu'auto-entrepreneur : Vous êtes exempté de TVA tant que votre CA reste sous le seuil de franchise en base (€36,800 pour les prestations de services en 2026). Une fois ce seuil dépassé, vous devez facturer la TVA et faire des déclarations trimestrielles ou mensuelles. Notion facilite ce suivi grâce à un tableau de bord de CA cumulé — vous saurez exactement quand vous approcherez du seuil bien avant de le dépasser.

Régime Micro-Entreprise vs. Entreprise Individuelle

Many French freelancers start under the micro-entreprise régime for its simplicity, then switch to entreprise individuelle au régime réel as their revenue grows past the ceiling. The key difference: under régime réel, you pay tax on actual profit (revenue minus deductible expenses) rather than on revenue with a flat abattement. If your expenses are high (equipment, software, travel, subcontractors), the régime réel often results in lower tax. What are the mandatory elements on a French freelance invoice?
French law requires invoices to include: your full name and address, SIRET number, TVA number (if applicable), client's name and address, invoice number (sequential), invoice date, description of services rendered, unit price HT, TVA rate and amount, total TTC, and payment terms with due date. If you are under the franchise en base, add the mention: 'TVA non applicable, article 293 B du CGI'. FreelanceOS Pro includes a pre-formatted invoice template covering all mandatory fields.
What is the auto-entrepreneur income ceiling in France?
In 2026, the annual revenue ceiling for the micro-entreprise régime is €77,700 for service activities (prestations de services) and €188,700 for commercial activities (vente de marchandises). If you exceed the service ceiling two years in a row, you are automatically moved to the régime réel simplifié. The FreelanceOS income tracker includes a yearly revenue dashboard so you can monitor your CA in real time against this threshold.
How do URSSAF social charges work for French freelancers?
Auto-entrepreneurs pay URSSAF social contributions as a percentage of their declared revenue — approximately 22% for services in 2026. These are paid monthly or quarterly depending on your election. Notion can track your cumulative revenue per period so you can estimate your upcoming URSSAF payment in advance and avoid cash flow surprises. A 'Charges Prévues' column in your income tracker that automatically calculates 22% of each invoice amount makes this instant.
