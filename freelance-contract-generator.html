      Contract Essentials
      
What Every Freelance Contract Needs

      
Miss any of these and you're leaving yourself exposed. Here's what the pros always include.

    

    

      

        
1

        

          
Party Identification

          
Full legal names and contact details for both the freelancer and the client — not just first names. If the client is a company, include the legal entity name and address.

        

      

      

        
2

        

          
Scope of Work

          
A specific description of what is included — and just as importantly, what is explicitly excluded. Vague scopes are the #1 cause of client disputes and scope creep.

        

      

      

        
3

        

          
Deliverables List

          
An itemized list of every tangible output you will provide. Format, quantity, and quality standards should be specified. "Design files" is not a deliverable. "5 Figma screens in the provided style guide" is.

        

      

      

        
4

        

          
Timeline & Deadlines

          
Start date, milestone dates (if applicable), final delivery date, and what happens if the client causes delays by not providing feedback or materials on time.

        

      

      

        
5

        

          
Payment Terms

          
Total fee, payment structure (upfront/milestone/on-completion), invoice due date, accepted payment methods, and the late fee rate. Never start work without at least a deposit.

        

      

      

        
6

        

          
Revision Policy

          
How many rounds of revisions are included, what constitutes a "revision" vs. a new request, and what additional revisions cost. Without this, "one quick change" can become infinite unpaid work.

        

      

      

        
7

        

          
Intellectual Property

          
Who owns the work — during the project, after delivery, and if the contract is terminated early. Specify whether you retain portfolio rights, whether the client needs source files, and when ownership transfers.

        

      

      

        
8

        

          
Kill Fee & Termination

          
What happens if the client cancels. A kill fee (typically 25–50% of remaining balance) compensates you for blocked calendar time and work already completed. Without it, late cancellations cost you real money.

        

      

    

  







  

    

      Avoid These
      
5 Common Freelance Contract Mistakes

      
These mistakes have cost freelancers thousands of dollars. Don't repeat them.

    

    
    
      
  • 
        
    🚫
    
        
    
          
    Starting Work Before the Contract is Signed
    
          
    You agree on the project verbally or over email, feel the excitement, and dive in. Then the client changes their mind, disputes the scope, or simply disappears. Without a signed contract, you have no legal standing and no guarantee of payment.
    
          
    Fix: Send the contract before doing a single hour of work. No exceptions.
    
        
    
      
    • 
      
  • 
        
    🚫
    
        
    
          
    Vague Deliverables — "A Website" Instead of Specific Pages
    
          
    Clients will always interpret a vague deliverable in the way that benefits them most. "A website" can mean 5 pages to you and 25 pages to them. Every deliverable needs a format, quantity, and measurable quality standard.
    
          
    Fix: List every deliverable as specifically as possible. Include file formats, number of iterations, and what "done" looks like.
    
        
    
      
    • 
      
  • 
        
    🚫
    
        
    
          
    No Revision Limit
    
          
    "Unlimited revisions" sounds like great client service. In practice, it gives the client infinite leverage to keep requesting changes without additional payment. Some clients will abuse this indefinitely, keeping the project open for months.
    
          
    Fix: Define revision rounds explicitly (2–3 rounds is standard). Charge your hourly rate for revisions beyond the included rounds.
    
        
    
      
    • 
      
  • 
        
    🚫
    
        
    
          
    No Late Payment Clause
    
          
    Without a financial consequence for late payment, some clients will simply delay until you chase them. Invoices sit unpaid for 60, 90, 120+ days. A late fee — even 1.5%/month — creates incentive to pay on time and compensates you for the delayed cash flow.
    
          
    Fix: Include a 1.5% per month late fee starting the day after the invoice due date. State it clearly in the contract so it's not a surprise.
    
        
    
      
    • 
      
  • 
        
    🚫
    
        
    
          
    No Termination Clause
    
          
    Projects get cancelled. Clients run out of budget. Priorities change. If your contract has no termination clause, you may have nothing to show for weeks of blocked calendar time and completed work — especially if you haven't invoiced yet.
    
          
    Fix: Include a kill fee (25–50% of the remaining balance) payable immediately if the client cancels. This protects your time and income.
