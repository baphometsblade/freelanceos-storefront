How to use Notion templates (3 steps)

Duplicating and using a Notion template takes less than 2 minutes. Here's exactly how:

1

Duplicate

Click the template link. In Notion, click the "Duplicate" button in the top-right corner. Choose your workspace. The entire template copies to your account in seconds.

2

Customize

Rename properties to match your workflow. Delete any sections you won't use. Add your branding, rates, and existing client data. Every field is editable.

3

Use daily

Add it to your Notion sidebar favorites. Add the mobile app to your phone. Update it after every client interaction — within a week it becomes your business brain.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these Notion templates really free?
Yes. The free tools — Rate Calculator, Onboarding Checklist, Scope of Work Builder, Retainer Calculator, Project Estimator, and more — are completely free with no signup required. FreelanceOS Pro ($29, currently $17.40 with FLASH40) gives you the full relational system with 8 connected databases.
Do I need Notion Pro to use these templates?
No. All FreelanceOS templates work on the Notion Free plan. Notion Pro (paid) unlocks extra features like unlimited version history and bigger file uploads, but every database, view, and property in FreelanceOS runs perfectly on the free tier. You don't need a paid Notion plan.
How do I duplicate a Notion template?
Open the template link in your browser. In the top-right corner of the Notion page, click the "Duplicate" button. A dialog will ask you to choose your workspace — select it and click Duplicate. The entire template, including all databases, views, and example data, copies to your Notion account. It usually takes under 30 seconds.
Can I customize these templates?
Absolutely. Every template is fully customizable. Rename database properties, add new columns, create additional views (kanban, calendar, gallery), delete sections you don't need, and add your own data. There are no locked sections or read-only areas. You own the duplicate entirely.
What's the difference between the free templates and FreelanceOS Pro?
Free templates are standalone tools — each one does one specific job well. FreelanceOS Pro connects everything into a relational system: clients link to projects, projects link to tasks and time logs, tasks link to invoices, and invoices roll up to a revenue dashboard. The result is that updating one record automatically updates everything else. That connected layer is what makes it a business OS rather than a collection of templates.
