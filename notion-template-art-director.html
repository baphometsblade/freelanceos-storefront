Frequently Asked Questions

Is this Notion template built for freelance art directors and creative directors?
Yes — every database and workflow is built around the art director's and creative director's practice: campaign and project management, creative brief writing, vendor and talent relationships, brand asset organization, portfolio case studies, and business financials. It works for freelance art directors, in-house creative leads, and independent creative directors across advertising, editorial, brand, and content.
How does the creative brief database work across multiple clients?
The Creative Brief Database stores a structured brief for every project — objective, target audience, tone of voice, key message, deliverables, reference styles, mandatories, and restrictions. All briefs are searchable across clients, so you can find a brief from 18 months ago in seconds, reference how a client framed a previous campaign when scoping a new project, and maintain creative consistency across multiple campaigns for the same brand over time.
Can I manage a team of vendors and subcontractors from this template?
Yes. The Vendor and Talent Directory tracks every creative collaborator — photographers, illustrators, retouchers, copywriters, set designers, studio directors — with contact details, specialty, rate range, past collaboration notes, availability, and quality notes. Art directors who maintain a well-organized vendor database pull together production teams faster, make better creative decisions under time pressure, and build the kind of working relationships that attract referrals back.
How does the portfolio case study tracker work?
The Portfolio and Case Study Tracker logs every project you want to feature with campaign title, client, category, results and outcomes, photography credits, publication or award credits, and whether it's included in your current portfolio. This structured record makes updating your site, pitch decks, and case studies dramatically faster — you're pulling from organized notes rather than rebuilding every story from scratch each time you refresh your portfolio.
