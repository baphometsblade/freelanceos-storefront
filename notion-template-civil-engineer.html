Questions From Civil Engineering Consultants

How do civil engineers use Notion for project management?

Civil engineering consultants use Notion as their practice management hub — a project tracker linked to deliverable logs, site visit reports, code compliance references, and a business dashboard all in one connected workspace. Unlike Excel or a dedicated PM tool like Procore (built for large contractors), Notion is flexible enough for a solo or small consulting practice to customize without IT support. FreelanceOS Pro gives you a pre-built version of this system ready to use in under an hour.

Can Notion track engineering project deliverables and submittals?

Yes. The Deliverable and Submittal Log in FreelanceOS Pro tracks each submittal with fields for deliverable name (30% plans, 60% plans, 90% plans, specs, geotech report, drainage report, SWPPP, permit application), due date, status (not started/in progress/QC review/submitted/under agency review/approved with comments/approved/revised), agency reviewer, review cycle, comments received, and resubmittal date. Filter by project to see all outstanding submittals for a single client.

How do I track professional engineering (PE) license renewals in Notion?

The Professional Development and PE License Tracker stores each state license separately with fields for license number, expiration date, CPD/PDH hours required for renewal, hours completed this period, and notes on specific course requirements. For engineers licensed in multiple states, you can filter to see every renewal coming up in the next 6 months without maintaining a separate spreadsheet.

Can I track subconsultant costs and invoices in Notion?

Yes. The Subconsultant and Cost Management tracker stores each sub's firm name, service type (survey/geotech/MEP/environmental/traffic/structural/landscape), contract value with the sub, invoices received, whether the sub's cost is marked up in your prime contract, markup percentage, and status. This helps you monitor subconsultant budget burn rate before it becomes an overrun problem.

Is this template appropriate for structural engineers or only civil engineers?

It works for both. The project type field includes structural engineering alongside transportation, water resources, geotechnical, environmental, drainage, site development, and construction management. Structural engineering consultants use the same project tracking, deliverable log, site visit report, and business dashboard functions as civil engineering generalists.
