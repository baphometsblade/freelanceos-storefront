Freelancing as an Autónomo in Spain: What You Need to Know

Spain's freelance sector is growing rapidly — with over 3 million registered autónomos (self-employed workers), Spain is one of Europe's largest freelance markets. The autónomo regime is the standard legal structure for self-employed professionals, and it brings with it a set of quarterly administrative obligations that make organization essential.

Key Administrative Obligations for Autónomos

Spanish autónomos face four major quarterly tax filings each year:

  • Modelo 303 — Quarterly IVA (VAT) declaration, filed January, April, July, and October
  • Modelo 130 — Quarterly IRPF self-assessment (if you pay income tax quarterly rather than through invoice retention)
  • Modelo 390 — Annual IVA summary declaration, filed in January for the prior year
  • Renta Anual (Modelo 100) — Annual income tax return, filed April through June

Beyond tax filings, autónomos pay monthly Social Security contributions (cuota de autónomo). Under the 2023 reform, contributions are now income-based rather than flat-rate, ranging from approximately €200 to €590/month depending on annual income. New autónomos qualify for tarifa plana — a reduced €80/month contribution for the first 12 months of registration.

The Spanish Freelance Market in 2026

Spain's freelance market is concentrated in several key sectors and cities. Barcelona is the startup and tech hub, with a strong UX/UI design and software development community. Madrid is dominant in marketing, consulting, and financial services freelancing. Valencia and Seville are growing hubs for content creation, photography, and digital marketing. Remote-first culture accelerated significantly post-pandemic, enabling autónomos to serve international clients while living outside major cities.

Top Platforms for Spanish Freelancers

Spanish freelancers find clients through a mix of domestic and international platforms:

  • Malt España — fastest-growing European freelance platform, strong in tech and consulting
  • Infojobs Freelance — dominant in tech roles and digital marketing
  • LinkedIn — essential for B2B freelancing in consulting, marketing, and finance
  • Fiverr / Upwork — for serving international clients in design, development, and writing
  • Freelancer.com — general platform with active Spanish-speaking user base

Yes. You can build an Income & Expense database in Notion that separates IVA collected on invoices (21%, 10%, or 4% depending on services) from IVA paid on business expenses. Quarterly rollup formulas calculate your net IVA balance automatically, making Modelo 303 preparation significantly faster.

How do I track Modelo 303 and Modelo 130 in Notion?
Build a quarterly income tracker in Notion where each invoice entry records the amount, IVA rate applied, and IRPF retention percentage. What is tarifa plana for autónomos in Spain?
Tarifa plana is the reduced social security contribution available to new autónomos for the first 12 months of registration, set at €80/month as of 2024 regulations. After 12 months, contributions become income-based under the cuota de autónomo system introduced in the 2023 Social Security reform.
