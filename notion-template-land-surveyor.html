Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Land Surveyors and Geomatics Professionals

How does the survey project tracker work?

The survey project tracker holds every active and archived project with project ID, client, property parcel number (internal tracking — not a legal record), survey type, status from inquiry through invoiced and paid, field crew, contract amount, and key dates. Filter by survey type to see all ALTA projects in progress, sort by field date to plan crew deployment, or filter by status to identify every project that is field-complete and waiting on office processing. For a practice handling 20 to 60 simultaneous projects, a unified tracker is the difference between controlled growth and operational chaos where jobs fall silent between fieldwork and delivery.

How does the deliverable production pipeline help manage turnaround?

The production pipeline tracks each project from field data through office processing, QC review by a licensed surveyor, drafting, client review, final delivery, county recording (for plats), and invoice. Each stage has a completion flag so you can filter to see exactly where every project stands — which are in drafting, which are awaiting QC sign-off, which are ready to invoice. Survey deliverables often stall silently between field completion and office delivery while new field work continues to pile up. The pipeline makes stalled projects visible before they become late projects that damage client relationships.

How does the equipment inventory work for a survey practice?

The equipment inventory tracks every instrument and piece of software — name, type, serial number, purchase date, last calibration date, next calibration due, and license renewal date for CAD software. For a surveying practice, calibration schedules and instrument maintenance are professional and liability requirements. An instrument with an expired calibration can produce legally suspect deliverables. The inventory makes calibration schedules visible as a checklist rather than a mental burden, and the field kit checklist ensures every required item is verified before crew deployment — not discovered missing when the crew is already on site an hour from the office.

How do I track different client types in Notion?

The client and property request log tags each client by type — property owner, real estate attorney, title company, lender, developer, engineer, municipality, or government agency. Different client types have different urgency levels, communication preferences, and deliverable format expectations. Title companies want ALTA compliance documented in specific formats, developers need construction-staking flexibility and rapid turnaround, and individual property owners often need education about what a survey produces and how long it takes. Knowing your client type before you pick up the phone — along with their prior history, referral source, and any special property considerations — makes every conversation more efficient and more professional.
