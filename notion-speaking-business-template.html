The Gig Lifecycle

From First Inquiry to Post-Event Follow-Up

Every speaking gig follows the same lifecycle. The template structures each stage so nothing falls through the cracks — inquiry through rebook.

1

Inquiry Intake — Capturing Every Opportunity

Get every inquiry into the pipeline within 24 hours. Capture event name, organizer, audience type, size, date, budget, and talk of interest. A linked view sorted by event date helps you prioritize fast-approaching opportunities and respond with full context every time.

2

Proposal — From Template to Confirmed Booking

The proposal template pulls your talk description, bio, and fee automatically — proposals take 20 minutes to customize, not two hours from scratch. Each proposal links to the gig record so you track send date, follow-up, and conversion.

3

Confirmed — Contract, Logistics, and Pre-Event Prep

When confirmed, the gig record becomes a checklist: contract signed, deposit received, A/V confirmed, travel booked, pre-event call scheduled, customization notes captured. One record for everything logistical — no more hunting across email and calendar.

4

Day-Of Prep — Arriving Ready

The day-of checklist: logistics confirmation, A/V check time, slide deck final version link, customization notes review, post-event follow-up pre-staged. You arrive prepared — not scrambling through emails that morning.

5

Post-Event — Thank You, Feedback, and Rebook Identification

Within 48 hours: thank-you email, testimonial request, feedback in the talk log, invoice submitted, rebook opportunity flag on the organizer's CRM record. The best time to get rebooked is immediately after a great engagement — the template makes this a system, not an afterthought.

How do I track which version of a talk I delivered at each event?

The Talk Library's Talk Deliveries view logs each delivery: event, date, slide deck version, audience customizations, and reflection notes. Over time this builds a history of how each talk evolved. When you rebook with an audience that saw a previous version, you know exactly which slides to refresh.

Yes. The Speaking Income Dashboard shows all confirmed and completed gig fees by month and quarter. A rollup formula calculates annual revenue against your target. The Talk Development Workspace uses four layers: Problem/Insight, Framework, Stories, and Proof. Each layer links to your research notes. Develop the talk in Notion, link slide drafts, and record post-delivery reflections for each new version.
