The Gig Lifecycle

From First Inquiry to Post-Event Follow-Up

Every speaking gig follows the same lifecycle. The template provides structure for each stage so nothing falls through the cracks from initial inquiry through to rebook conversation.

1

Inquiry Intake — Capturing Every Opportunity

When a speaking inquiry lands in your inbox, the first task is getting it into your pipeline within 24 hours. The gig pipeline intake captures: event name, organizer, audience type, audience size, event date, budget range, and the specific talk they are interested in. A linked view shows open inquiries sorted by event date so you can prioritize fast-approaching opportunities. You respond to every inquiry with full context, not a frantic inbox search.

2

Proposal — From Template to Confirmed Booking

The proposal template in the template pulls your talk description from the Talk Library, your bio from the Media Kit Hub, and your fee from the rate card — so proposals take 20 minutes to customize, not two hours to write from scratch. Each proposal is linked to the gig record so you can track when it was sent, whether it was followed up, and how it converted.

3

Confirmed — Contract, Logistics, and Pre-Event Prep

When a booking is confirmed, the gig record becomes a logistics checklist: contract signed, deposit received, A/V requirements confirmed with the venue, travel booked, pre-event call scheduled, and talk customization notes captured. The event database stores all logistics details so you have a single record for everything related to that engagement — no more hunting across your email and calendar.

4

Day-Of Prep — Arriving Ready

The day-of preparation checklist in each gig record includes: logistics confirmation (venue, time, contact), A/V check time, slide deck final version link, customization notes review, key audience context, and the post-event follow-up sequence pre-staged. You arrive at every engagement with complete preparation, not a frantic morning of emails.

5

Post-Event — Thank You, Feedback, and Rebook Identification

Within 48 hours of an engagement, the post-event checklist triggers: thank-you email to the organizer, testimonial request (linked to the Testimonial Tracker), feedback form results captured in the talk delivery log, invoice submitted (if not pre-paid), and a rebook opportunity flag on the organizer's CRM record. The best time to get rebooked is immediately after a great engagement — the template makes this a system, not an afterthought.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Notion Speaker Business template.

How do I organize my speaking business in Notion?

A complete Notion speaking business system needs four linked databases: a Speaking Gig Pipeline (tracking every inquiry from first contact to completed engagement), a Talk Library (each talk with title, description, audience, slide deck link, and delivery log), an Event Database (organizer, date, fee, audience size, and travel details), and a Speaker CRM (organizers, bureaus, and past clients). The FreelanceOS Speaker Business template has all four databases pre-built and linked — duplicate it into Notion and start adding your existing gigs and talks in under an hour.

How do I track which version of a talk I delivered at each event?

The Talk Library in this template has a Talk Deliveries linked view where each delivery is logged as a separate record: the event, the date, which slide deck version was used (with a direct link), what customizations were made for that audience, and post-delivery reflection notes on what landed and what to change. Over time this builds a rich history showing how each talk has evolved. When you book a return engagement with an audience that has seen a previous version, you know exactly which version they saw and which stories or slides to refresh.

Can I track speaking income and set annual revenue targets in Notion?

Yes. The Speaking Income Dashboard is a dedicated view showing all confirmed and completed gigs with their fees, broken down by month and quarter. A rollup formula calculates total annual speaking revenue compared to your target. You can also see revenue by talk topic and by engagement type (keynote vs. workshop vs. corporate training) to identify which talks are commanding the highest fees — valuable intelligence for deciding where to invest your talk development and BD time.

How do I develop a new talk in Notion from concept to stage-ready?

The Talk Development Workspace uses a structured four-layer framework: Problem/Insight (what non-obvious insight does your audience leave with), Framework (the structure that makes the insight actionable), Stories (personal and third-party stories that make the insight memorable), and Proof (data and demonstrations that make the framework credible). Each layer is a sub-section linked to your research notes database. You develop the talk in Notion, link your slide deck drafts, and record post-delivery reflection notes that inform each subsequent version.

How does this template help me get rebooking from past clients?

The Speaker CRM has a Rebook Likelihood score for every organizer and client, plus a Last Touchpoint date that shows when you last had meaningful contact. A filtered view called "Rebook Opportunities" surfaces organizers who scored highly on your post-event evaluation, where it has been 6+ months since last engagement, and where no follow-up is logged. It is a built-in warm outreach queue. The best time to get rebooked is within 60 days of a great engagement — this view shows you who to contact before that window closes.
