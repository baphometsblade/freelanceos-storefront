A Complete Acting Career OS in Notion

FreelanceOS Pro for Actors connects your audition tracker, agent CRM, headshot and reel log, script library, credit log, and income dashboard in one relational workspace built for professional performers across every medium.

🎭 Audition Tracker — Callback Rates, Booking Ratios, and Prep Notes

Every audition gets its own record: date, project and production, role and character type, medium (film/TV/commercial/theater/VO), casting director, sides or script worked, format (self-tape vs. in-person), whether you got a callback, whether you booked, fee if booked, and your prep notes. Over time this data tells you which media generates your highest callback rate, which casting offices are your warmest relationships, and whether your booking ratio is improving. Most actors who track this data are surprised by what it reveals about where their career momentum actually is versus where they assume it is.

👤 Agent, Manager & Casting Director CRM

A full relationship database for every professional contact in your career: agents with agency name, contract dates, contact info, and current submission strategy; managers with their approach and focus areas; casting directors with past submission history, preferred contact method, and relationship notes; coaches, directors, and industry contacts organized by relationship type. For actors with multiple reps — a commercial agent and a theatrical agent with separate strategies — the CRM keeps each relationship's context clear and accessible so every communication is informed and professional.

📸 Headshot & Demo Reel Tracker

Track every current headshot set with the date taken, photographer, type (commercial, theatrical, character, editorial), file storage location, which agent has which version submitted, and notes on when an update is due. The demo reel section logs your current reel link, last updated date, scenes included with timestamps, and total runtime. Never walk into a meeting unsure which version of your headshot is being submitted, when your reel was last refreshed, or how long your current reel runs — details that matter in casting and agent conversations.

📚 Sides & Script Library

An organized archive of all scripts, sides, and performance materials: audition sides with the role, production, and which sides you worked for which callback round; full scripts for booked projects; character analysis notes from your preparation; director's vision notes from the breakdown or pre-audition research; and callback performance notes documenting what changed and what worked. Having the complete preparation history for every audition in one place means you can reconstruct your character thinking months later if the project goes into development, and you can analyze your preparation depth across roles you booked versus roles you did not.

🏆 Credit Log & IMDb Tracker

A master credit record for every role: role name, production title, medium, director, production company, airing or release date, IMDb link to update, festival selections, and awards received. Filter by medium to build your theatrical resume vs. commercial resume vs. voice-over resume. Track progress toward IMDb Pro credentialing and SAG-AFTRA union membership milestones with the credit count and contract type fields. Never let a booking go unlogged or an IMDb credit go unupdated after the project airs.

💰 Income & Residuals Dashboard

A complete income log organized by project and medium: session fees, buy-outs, residual payments from commercial airings and streaming, theatrical box office residuals, and voice-over usage fees. The SAG-AFTRA residuals log tracks each payment with the originating project, airing market or streaming platform, payment date, and amount — so you can spot delayed or missing residual payments and always have clean records for quarterly estimated taxes and year-end filing. Union status tracking notes your current standing and the next milestone in your union progression.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Actors

How does the audition tracker work in Notion?

The audition tracker gives every audition its own record with fields for date, project, role, medium, casting director, sides worked, format (self-tape vs. in-person), callback received, booked or not, and fee if booked. Over time you can calculate your audition-to-callback ratio and callback-to-booking ratio — data that helps you identify which submission types, media, and casting offices are your strongest and where to focus your energy. Most actors find the data reveals patterns they never would have noticed without a formal log.

Can I manage my agents and casting director relationships in Notion?

Yes. The agent, manager, and casting director CRM tracks each representative with their agency, contact info, contract dates, current pitch focus, and relationship notes. The casting director section logs past submissions, preferred contact method, and notes on each relationship. For actors with multiple reps — a commercial agent and a theatrical agent — the CRM keeps each relationship's context organized so nothing important gets missed and every conversation is professionally informed.

How do I organize my headshots and demo reel?

The headshot and reel tracker logs each headshot set with the date taken, photographer, type (commercial, theatrical, character), file location, which agent uses which version, and a next update reminder. The demo reel section tracks the current reel link, last updated date, scenes included, and total runtime. Never go into a meeting unsure which version of your headshot is being submitted or when your reel was last refreshed — details that matter in agent and casting conversations.

How does the credit log work for my resume?

The credit log stores every credit with role name, production title, medium, director, production company, airing or release date, IMDb link to update, festival selections, and awards. Filter by medium to pull your theatrical credits, commercial credits, or voice-over credits for different resume versions. The log also tracks progress toward IMDb Pro credentialing and SAG-AFTRA qualification milestones with the credit count and contract type fields.

Can I track SAG-AFTRA residuals in Notion?

Yes. The income and residuals dashboard logs every residual payment by project and payment date — commercial airings, streaming residuals, theatrical residuals, and reuse payments. You can see total residual income by year and by contract type. While you will still receive official union statements, your own running log lets you spot delayed or missing payments faster and always have clean income records for estimated taxes and year-end filing with your accountant.
