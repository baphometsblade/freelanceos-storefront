How it works

From Raw Idea to Published Story — a System in Three Steps

The workflow is simple. Every story you touch moves through the same system, building your institutional knowledge with every piece you publish.

1

Log Every Story Idea Immediately

The moment you spot a story — from a tip, a tweet, a phone call, or a hunch — it goes into the Story Pipeline as an "Idea." Add the beat, the potential outlet, any source leads, and any initial notes. Nothing leaves your workspace. Nothing gets forgotten. Your idea graveyard becomes a living, searchable pipeline of publishable stories.

2

Track It from Pitch to Publication

As the story develops, it moves through each pipeline stage — Research, Reporting, Draft, Edit, Submitted, Published. Every pitch you send is logged in the Pitch Tracker. Every piece of research is filed in the archive. Every interview prep sheet is linked to the story. When your editor asks for a status update, you have a complete history of every decision and every conversation in one place.

3

Build Your Source Database with Every Story

After every story — whether it ran or not — update your Source CRM with the contacts you made or reconnected with. Add their beat specialty, how they like to be reached, and how useful they were. Over months, you build an irreplaceable contact database. The next time a story breaks on your beat, you know exactly who to call — and they already know who you are.

Common questions

Everything You Need to Know

Does this template work for both freelance journalists and staff reporters?
Yes — the template is built to serve both freelancers and staff reporters equally well. Freelance journalists will find the pitch tracker, income log, and invoicing system particularly valuable, as these address the business side of freelancing that staff reporters don't deal with. Staff reporters will get the most value from the story pipeline, source CRM, and beat knowledge base — keeping their reporting operation organized under a heavy story load. The system is modular: you use what applies to your role and skip what doesn't.
Can I use this template to protect source confidentiality?
This template is designed for general story organization and business management only. For confidential sources, sensitive investigations, or whistleblower contacts, always follow your organization's security protocols and consult your editor or legal team before using any cloud-based tool. Notion stores data in the cloud, which may not be appropriate for highly sensitive or legally protected source information. Use your organization's approved secure communication tools for those records. This template handles the editorial workflow side of journalism — story tracking, pitch management, income logging, and research organization — not secure communications.
Is this template useful for newsletter writers and independent journalists?
Absolutely. Newsletter writers and independent journalists are one of the biggest groups using this system. The editorial calendar module is built specifically for recurring deadlines and issue planning. The story pipeline adapts naturally to episode or issue-based publishing. Independent journalists running their own newsletter can track subscriber growth milestones, sponsor commitments, issue deadlines, story angles, and source outreach all in the same connected workspace. You don't need an editor or a newsroom to benefit — the system works even better when you're the entire operation.
Can I track multiple beats with one workspace?
Yes. The Beat Knowledge Base module is explicitly designed for multi-beat coverage. Each beat gets its own section — complete with key players, ongoing story threads, background context, regulatory timelines, and beat-specific contacts. The Source CRM uses beat specialty tags so you can instantly filter your contacts by beat when a new story breaks. The story pipeline uses beat tags so you can view all your active stories on a specific beat without manually searching. Whether you cover two beats or eight, the system scales with you.
Does the template include a tracker for FOI or FOIA requests?
Yes. The Research & Clip Archive module includes a dedicated FOI/FOIA tracker with fields for: the agency or body you submitted to, the exact request submitted, the submission date, the statutory response deadline, current status (submitted, acknowledged, delayed, responded, appealed), and what was ultimately received. For investigative journalists and data reporters managing multiple outstanding public records requests across different agencies simultaneously, this tracker becomes one of the most valuable parts of the system.
Is this template useful for podcast and audio journalists?
Yes. Podcast and audio journalists can adapt the entire system to their format. The Story Pipeline becomes an episode pipeline — Idea → Research → Booking → Recording → Editing → Published. The Source CRM manages interview guests, their contact details, and previous appearances. The Interview Prep Notes module is purpose-built for audio journalists who need structured research per conversation. The Editorial Calendar handles recording dates, editing deadlines, and release schedules. Many podcast journalists using the system also track sponsorship commitments and episode income in the publication log.
