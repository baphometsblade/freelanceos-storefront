$47/month for tools that weren't talking to each other. And four different places to check every time she wanted a simple answer: what's the status of this client?

The real cost wasn't the software subscriptions. It was time:

  • "I was spending 12+ hours a week on admin" — that's the equivalent of 3 full work days every week, just on paperwork and tool-switching.
  • Invoice delays were routine. She'd invoice 2 weeks after project completion because she'd forget, or lose track in email.
  • She missed a follow-up with a prospect worth $3,500 — the lead went cold because it fell through the cracks between her spreadsheet and inbox.
  • No visibility on profitability. She had no idea which clients were actually worth keeping vs. which ones were time-drains disguised as income.
