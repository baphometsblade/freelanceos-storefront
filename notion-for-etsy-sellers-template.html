Sound Familiar?

Running an Etsy shop without a system
is leaving money on the table

Most Etsy sellers make decisions based on gut feel, Etsy's star ratings, and a rough sense of how busy they are. These are the four pain points that hold shops back from real profitability.

📊

Production costs unknown — profitability is pure guesswork

You know your sale price. But after materials, packaging, labor time, Etsy's 6.5% transaction fee, payment processing, and shipping costs — do you actually know what you net? Most sellers are shocked when they run the numbers for the first time. Some "bestsellers" are barely breaking even.

💡

No system for new product research and ideas

Product ideas come in the shower, on Pinterest, at craft fairs, in Facebook groups. But without a capture system, 80% of good ideas evaporate before you act on them. The ones you do pursue often skip the market research step — leading to beautiful products that don't sell because demand was never verified.

🔍

SEO keyword research scattered and untracked across listings

You might update a listing title once and never revisit it. Keywords that were trending last year might be dead now. Without a central keyword hub, you're flying blind on which search terms are actually driving your traffic versus which ones you guessed at two years ago and forgot to update.

🎄

Order spikes before holidays with no prep system

Q4 is Etsy's Olympics. If you're not ready — inventory stocked, production ramped up, shipping cutoff dates communicated, new seasonal listings live — you'll leave your biggest revenue window of the year half-open. The panic of "I'm sold out and it's December 10th" is entirely preventable with a holiday prep timeline.

How It Works

Up and running in three steps

You don't need to fill in every field on day one. Most sellers start with the Product Catalog, add real cost data for their top 10 listings, and immediately discover which products are actually profitable. Everything else builds from there.

1

Add your products with real cost data

Start with your top 10 listings. Enter the materials cost per unit, your labor time, and the current sale price. The Product Catalog automatically calculates your true margin after Etsy's fees. Most sellers are surprised — a few listings jump out as winners, a few as problems that need repricing. This single step gives you more clarity than months of guessing.

2

Track revenue and profit margin monthly

At the end of each month, spend 15 minutes copying your key numbers from Etsy's Stats page into the Revenue Dashboard. Gross sales, units sold, Etsy fees, ads spend. The template calculates your net profit and updates your best-sellers list. Over three months, patterns become obvious — which products drive the most profit, which months are slow, and where your ads budget is actually working.

3

Plan new products from what actually sells

Use the Product Idea Pipeline to capture and research your next listings. With real margin and sales data in hand, you can make evidence-based decisions: expand the product lines with high margins and proven demand, retire the ones with thin margins and stagnant views, and use the SEO Hub to optimize titles and tags for everything in between. Your shop grows smarter, not just bigger.

Common Questions

Everything Etsy sellers ask
before buying

These are the real questions we get in our inbox and support channel. Answered fully, no marketing fluff.

Does this work for both handmade AND digital product Etsy shops?

Yes — the template handles every Etsy business model. For handmade sellers, the Product Catalog tracks physical materials costs, labor time per unit, and packaging to give you a true COGS figure. For digital product sellers, you log design time as a one-time sunk cost and then capture near-100% margin on every subsequent sale. The Revenue Dashboard and SEO Hub work identically across both models, and you can run a mixed shop with both handmade and digital listings side by side with no extra setup.

Does this replace Etsy's built-in analytics and Stats page?

No — and it's not meant to. Etsy's analytics give you views, visits, and conversion rates directly from the platform's data. This template complements those stats by adding the financial layer Etsy doesn't provide: actual profit per listing after materials, labor, Etsy fees, payment processing, and ads spend. You copy key numbers from Etsy's Stats page into the Revenue & Profit Dashboard monthly — the whole process takes about 15 minutes — and immediately see which products are genuinely worth your time and which are generating volume but thin margins.

Is this good for a brand-new Etsy shop with only 10 listings?

It's actually ideal for small shops — that's when the habits matter most. New sellers often price by copying competitors and discover months later that their bestselling item barely covers costs. Setting up your Product Catalog with real cost data from day one means every pricing decision is grounded in numbers from the start. The Product Idea Pipeline also helps you think strategically about which 10 listings to build next, so you grow a tight, profitable catalog rather than a sprawling unfocused one that's hard to manage.

Can I track multiple Etsy shops with one template?

Yes. The template is structured so you can either duplicate sections or add a "Shop" property to any database, letting you filter and view data by shop. Many sellers who started with one handmade shop later opened a second for digital products or print-on-demand — the template scales to support both without a second purchase. Just duplicate the relevant databases and tag entries by shop name. Your Revenue Dashboard can show combined or per-shop views depending on how you prefer to analyze your business.

Does this Notion template actually help with Etsy SEO?

Yes — the SEO & Keywords Hub is consistently one of the most-used modules. For each listing you log your primary target keyword, secondary keywords, all 13 Etsy tags with character counts, estimated search volume from eRank or Marmalead, and competitor research notes. Over time you build a keyword bank that shows which terms are driving traffic and which listings need a title refresh. Many sellers report a noticeable increase in views within 30–60 days of systematically working through their listings using the keyword data they've organized here.

Is this a good Notion template for print-on-demand (POD) Etsy sellers?

Absolutely — POD is one of the best use cases for this template. Your cost structure is different from handmade: you pay per order to a supplier like Printify or Printful, so the Materials & Supplier Tracker logs your base costs per product type, and the Product Catalog captures your Etsy sale price minus the POD fulfillment cost and Etsy fees to give you real margin per design. The Holiday & Seasonal Prep module is especially popular with POD sellers who need to time new design launches 6–8 weeks before peak seasons like Christmas, Valentine's Day, and Mother's Day to capture trending search traffic.
