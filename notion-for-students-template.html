How It Works

Duplicate the template into Notion, add your classes, professors, meeting times, and grade weights. Takes 20 minutes the first time. Ten minutes at the start of each new semester after that.

FAQ

Questions Students Actually Ask

Before you buy, here's everything you need to know about how the Notion Student OS works in the real world.

Does this work for both high school and university?

Yes — the Notion Student OS works for high school, undergraduate, graduate, and even online learners. All databases are adaptable: you can track AP classes and GPA just as easily as college courses, research papers, or law school casebooks. The structure flexes to your level of education.

Does this replace Canvas, Blackboard, or Google Classroom?

It complements those platforms rather than replacing them. Your school's LMS handles submission and official grades. This Notion template is your personal command center — tracking deadlines, study sessions, grade goals, and the bigger picture your LMS doesn't show you. Think of it as mission control for your academic life.

Can I use this across multiple semesters?

Absolutely. The semester-based architecture lets you archive completed terms and start fresh each semester in seconds. Your entire academic history stays searchable in Notion, so you can pull up notes and grades from any prior semester whenever you need them. Many students have used it for 3+ years continuously.

I have a part-time job. Can this handle both school and work?

Yes. The Internship & Job Hub and Study Planner work alongside each other. You can block out work shifts in the weekly schedule, track job applications separately from coursework, and keep everything in a single system so nothing falls through the cracks — whether it's a shift swap or a project deadline.

How does the GPA calculation actually work?

The Grade Calculator uses weighted averages based on the grade weights you enter per assignment category (exams, homework, quizzes, projects). It projects your final course grade and cumulative GPA dynamically, and lets you run what-if scenarios — "what do I need on the final to get an A?" — so you always know exactly where you stand and what it takes.

Does this work for online learners and non-traditional students?

Yes. Online students often juggle more platforms and have less structure than on-campus peers — which makes a unified Notion hub even more valuable. The system works on any device and keeps your async coursework, deadlines, and study blocks in one place regardless of time zone, institution, or schedule type.
