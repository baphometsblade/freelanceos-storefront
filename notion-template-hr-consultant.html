Questions From HR Consultants

Is this a compliant HR or HRIS system?

No. FreelanceOS Pro is a business management tool for HR consultants — not a regulated HRIS, not an employment records system, and not legal advice. Formal employee records for your clients must be maintained in appropriate HR systems (BambooHR, Rippling, Gusto, Workday, ADP, etc.). This template is for managing your consulting practice: clients, projects, your reusable resource library, and your business development pipeline. Think of it as the operating system for your HR consulting business — not a platform for storing your clients' people data.

Can I use this for fractional HR clients?

Yes — this template is particularly well-suited to fractional HR consultants managing multiple client retainers simultaneously. The Client and Project Tracker lets you see all active engagements with current deliverables, phases, and deadlines in one view. The Business Dashboard shows your monthly recurring revenue from retainers, income split across project types, and new client pipeline at a glance. Most fractional HR consultants find they can manage twice as many clients systematically compared to running everything from memory and scattered notes.

How does the HR resource library work?

The HR Resource and Template Library is your growing collection of reusable HR materials — job description templates organized by role family, interview question banks organized by competency, onboarding checklists for different role types, performance review frameworks, and compensation band reference templates. You build it once and then adapt each asset for new clients rather than starting from scratch on every engagement. Most HR consultants find the library saves three to six hours per new client setup within the first few months of actively populating it. The more you use it, the more valuable it becomes.

Does the template include compliance reference information?

The template includes a Compliance Reference Notes section where you can build your own private knowledge base — your notes on federal and state leave laws, classification guidance for FTE vs. contractor roles, wage and hour reference points, benefits compliance summaries, and EEOC guidance notes. This section is a personal reference for your use as an experienced HR professional — not legal advice, and not a substitute for qualified employment counsel. Always confirm jurisdiction-specific guidance with employment attorneys and check authoritative regulatory sources before advising clients on compliance matters.
