Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Supply Chain Consultants

Can I use this for both project and retainer supply chain engagements?

Yes. The Engagement Tracker captures both fixed-fee project engagements — procurement optimization, logistics network design, supplier consolidation — and time-and-materials retainer agreements. You can track contract type, project phase, key deliverables, and status for every active engagement regardless of fee structure. The Invoice and AR Tracker handles both milestone-based billing for fixed-fee projects and monthly billing for T&M retainers.

How does the supplier assessment database work?

The Supplier Assessment Database holds your client's supplier intelligence for the duration of the engagement — one row per supplier evaluated. You record supplier name, commodity or category, geography, tier classification, assessment scores across quality, delivery, cost, financial stability, ESG, and capacity, overall risk rating, strategic classification (preferred, approved, conditional, disqualified), and any corrective action plans open. This is your working database during the engagement, not a permanent supplier management system. For ongoing supplier performance management after the engagement, clients should use dedicated procurement platforms.

Does this work for different supply chain specializations like procurement, logistics, or S&OP?

Yes — the Engagement Tracker includes an engagement type field that covers procurement optimization, inventory reduction, logistics network design, supplier consolidation, S&OP implementation, 3PL RFP support, ERP advisory, resilience assessment, nearshoring advisory, and digital transformation. The Process Improvement Log and KPI Baseline Tracker are flexible enough to work across all of these specializations by letting you define the specific processes and metrics relevant to each individual engagement.

How do I demonstrate ROI to clients with this template?

The KPI and Baseline Tracker is specifically designed to capture before-and-after metrics that prove the value of your engagement. Track on-time delivery percentage, inventory turnover, days of supply, OTIF, supplier lead time average, procurement cycle time, freight cost per unit, customer fill rate, and forecast accuracy at the start of the engagement — then update at project closeout to document the improvement. The Process Improvement Log also tracks estimated annual savings per improvement recommendation. Together these give you a quantified ROI story for each engagement that becomes your most powerful marketing asset for the next client.
