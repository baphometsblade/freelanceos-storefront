Yes, the usability test scheduler covers the full lifecycle of a usability study from initial planning and participant recruitment through synthesis and stakeholder presentation. It is designed to be the operational backbone of any usability testing program, whether you are running a quick guerrilla test with five participants over two days or a formal moderated remote study across multiple weeks. Both scenarios are supported by the same flexible structure.

For each usability test study, you create a parent record with the study name, the associated project, the specific research questions you are trying to answer, your participant criteria (screener requirements, target demographics), the planned number of participants, the testing method (remote moderated, in-person moderated, or unmoderated asynchronous), and the planned timeline from recruitment to report delivery. Under that parent record, you log individual sessions with fields for participant name, recruitment status (recruited, screened, confirmed, completed, no-show), the scheduled date and time, the note-taker assigned to that session, and observation notes captured during the session. Session observations flow automatically into the user research repository so all usability test findings are searchable alongside your interview and survey data.

The synthesis side of the scheduler includes milestone tracking with assigned owners and due dates: affinity mapping session, insight prioritization, report first draft, report review and revision, and final stakeholder presentation. Setting these milestones at the start of the study prevents the common post-testing problem where research findings sit unprocessed for weeks because no clear owner or deadline was established for the synthesis work. When synthesis milestones have owners and due dates, findings reach design and product teams while decisions are still being actively debated, rather than arriving after the direction has already been locked. The scheduler also tracks participant incentive amounts and payment status to ensure recruiting commitments are fulfilled on schedule.
