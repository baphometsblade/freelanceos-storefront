Frequently Asked Questions

Is this Notion template built specifically for animators and motion graphics artists?
Yes — every database and workflow is built around the animation professional's business cycle: scoping projects, managing storyboard and animatic stages, tracking revisions, delivering files, managing client relationships, and tracking income by animation type. It works for 2D animation, 3D animation, motion graphics, character animation, and explainer video work.
Can I manage multiple animation projects at different stages at once?
Yes. The Project Tracker is designed to show all active projects and their current stage simultaneously — storyboard approved, animatic delivered, first draft, final delivery, invoice sent. You can filter by client, animation type, or stage to get a clear picture of your full workload at any point in the week.
How does the revision log prevent miscommunication with animation clients?
The Revision and Feedback Log tracks every revision round per project — what feedback was received, what changes were made, and when approval was confirmed. This creates a clear paper trail that prevents scope creep disputes, documents what was agreed at each stage, and lets you reference exactly what was approved if a client later claims the work doesn't match their brief.
Can I track my software licenses for After Effects, Cinema 4D, and plugins?
Yes. The Software and License Tracker logs every tool — After Effects, Cinema 4D, Blender, DaVinci Resolve, Lottie tools, Premiere Pro, and individual plugins — with license type, renewal date, annual cost, and notes. Reviewing this tracker quarterly helps you identify subscriptions you've outgrown and keep renewal dates organized well in advance.
