Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know before you buy.

How do real estate investors use Notion?

Real estate investors use Notion to track deal pipelines, log property analysis data, manage contractor relationships, and organise documents for tax season — replacing scattered spreadsheets and email folders with one searchable workspace.

Can I analyse deals in Notion?

Yes. The property analysis worksheet provides a structured layout for entering purchase price, ARV, rehab estimates, and calculating ROI and cap rate fields manually. It's a consistent starting framework for evaluating every deal.

How do I track multiple rental properties in Notion?

The portfolio dashboard gives every property its own entry with linked cash flow data, occupancy status, lease information, and expenses. Filter and sort across your entire portfolio in seconds.

Is Notion good for property management admin?

For high-level administrative tracking, yes — lease end dates, rent amounts, and unit status. For full property management (maintenance requests, work orders, tenant communication logs), a dedicated property management platform is better suited.

How do I track renovation budgets in Notion?

The renovation project tracker logs scope, contractor, budget, actual spend, and milestones for each project. You can see budget vs actual at a glance and track whether a rehab is on time and on budget.

Can I use Notion to manage contractors?

Yes. The contractor and vendor CRM stores contact details, trade type, ratings, availability notes, and past job records. Link contractors directly to renovation projects for full context.

What's the best Notion database for a real estate portfolio?

A linked database structure works best: a Properties master database linked to Deals, Expenses, Renovation Projects, and Tenants. The FreelanceOS template pre-builds these relationships so you don't have to start from scratch.

How do I prepare for tax season as an investor using Notion?

The tax document organiser lets you log receipts, expenses, and documents by property and tax year. Share the relevant section with your accountant or use it to pull numbers together for your Schedule E.
