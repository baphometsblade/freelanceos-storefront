The Problem

Your Documentation Work Is More Complex Than Any Generic Task Manager Can Handle

Jira was built for software engineers. Trello was built for general project management. Confluence is a documentation publishing tool, not a documentation project management tool. None of these was designed for the structured, multi-stakeholder, deadline-sensitive work of planning, tracking, and reviewing technical documentation. Writers end up building their own fragile hybrid systems — and those systems break under the weight of real workloads across multiple clients and review stakeholders.

📋

Projects Fall Through the Cracks

When you're managing five documentation projects across three clients, all with different review cycles and release dates, something always gets forgotten. Without a centralized tracker, you're relying on calendar reminders and email threads — and that doesn't scale past two concurrent clients.

🔄

Reviews Disappear Into Inboxes

Sending a document into a review cycle feels like dropping it into a black box. You don't know if the SME got it, whether legal has touched it, or what round of revisions you're currently on. Documents sit quietly in people's inboxes for weeks while release dates close in.

📖

Style Guides Live in Too Many Places

Your house style is in a Google Doc. The client has a different guide in Confluence. Your personal terminology notes are in a spreadsheet. When a new writer joins a project, finding the right rules for the right client requires hours of email archaeology before a word gets written.

💸

Invoicing Is a Reconstruction Project

Freelance technical writers often discover at tax time that they've been undercharging a client, have an unpaid invoice from four months ago, or can't reconstruct their year-to-date revenue without pulling records from three different places. There has to be a better way to run the business side.

🕰️

Stale Documentation Accumulates Silently

Documentation written for a feature that was redesigned six months ago doesn't announce itself. It just sits in your knowledge base, quietly misleading users and damaging trust in your product. Without a systematic audit process, you find out about stale docs from frustrated support tickets — not proactively.

🎤

CFP Deadlines Slip Past Unnoticed

You meant to submit a proposal to Write the Docs this year. The deadline passed while you were finishing a release cycle. Conference speaking is one of the most effective ways to build your professional reputation as a technical writer — but only if you have a system that catches deadlines before they pass.
