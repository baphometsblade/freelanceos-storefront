OKRs in Notion — Why It Works

OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) is the goal-setting framework used by Google, Intel, and thousands of high-performing teams. The structure is simple: set a bold Objective, then define 2–4 measurable Key Results that prove you reached it.

Notion is ideal for OKRs because of linked databases and formula properties. You can store your Objectives and Key Results in one database, link Key Results to Projects, and use a formula to calculate overall progress as a percentage — automatically.

🎯

Objective

A qualitative, ambitious goal. Example: “Grow freelance revenue to $10K/month.” Inspirational, not a metric itself.

🔑

Key Result

A quantitative measure proving you hit the Objective. Example: “Land 3 new retainer clients at $2K+/month.” Measurable, binary, or progress-tracked.

📊

Progress Formula

Notion formula: round(prop("Completed") / prop("Total") * 100) — shows your percentage progress automatically as you update Key Results.

Quarterly Review Template

The quarterly review is the most powerful ritual in any goal system. Schedule 90 minutes at the end of each quarter and use these four sections to close the loop and set up for the next quarter:

🏆

Wins

What went well this quarter? List OKR achievements, new clients, revenue milestones, skill gains, and personal victories. Celebrate specifically — vague wins don't stick.

❌

Misses

Which Key Results did you miss, and why? Be honest but not harsh. Was it the wrong goal, bad execution, or external factors? Understanding misses is how you avoid repeating them.

💡

Lessons

What did this quarter teach you about your business, your habits, or your priorities? Write 3–5 specific lessons. These become your operating principles for next quarter.

🎯

Focus for Next Quarter

What is the single most important Objective for next quarter? What are your 3 Key Results? Write them now, while the lessons from this quarter are fresh in your mind.
