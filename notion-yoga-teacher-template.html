The Problem

Why Yoga Teachers Stay Disorganized Despite Good Intentions

Teaching yoga requires creativity, physical presence, and genuine connection. The admin around it — sequence planning, student management, income tracking, continuing education — quietly drains the energy that belongs on the mat.

🔮

Rebuilding Sequences From Scratch Every Week

You have taught a hundred vinyasa classes, but Monday morning still finds you staring at a blank page trying to construct a 60-minute flow. Without a sequence library, every class starts from zero — even when you have excellent material from last month that could be reused, adapted, or remixed for a different level.

📋

Student Attendance and Memberships in Different Apps

Your studio tracks class passes in one system, private clients pay via Venmo, workshop registrations live in a Google Form, and your handwritten notes about who has tight hips are in a notebook you cannot find. When a student asks about their package balance or what you worked on last week, you have no quick answer.

🏭

Workshop Planning Chaos

A weekend workshop requires a theme, detailed sequence, prop list, marketing copy, registration link, pricing, and follow-up emails — all moving simultaneously. Most yoga teachers plan workshops inside their head and in a swarm of sticky notes, which leads to important details falling through right before showtime.

💹

No Clear View of Income Across All Streams

Studio per-class pay lands in your bank on one schedule. Private client payments arrive whenever they remember to pay. Workshop income comes in a burst, then disappears. Online class revenue trickles in from a separate platform. Without a central Revenue Tracker you have no idea whether your teaching business is actually growing or whether you are just staying busy.

How It Works

Building a Reusable Sequence Library in Notion

The Class Sequence Library is the heart of the template. Here is exactly how to build a library that saves you hours every week and keeps getting better the more you teach.

Tagging System That Actually Works

Each sequence record has five tags that let you filter across your entire library instantly:

  • → Level: Beginner, All-Levels, Intermediate, Advanced — teach to the room, not to an average
  • → Style: Vinyasa, Yin, Restorative, Hatha, Power, Gentle, Prenatal, Chair
  • → Duration: 30, 45, 60, 75, or 90 minutes — critical for last-minute sub slots
  • → Peak Pose Theme: hip openers, backbends, inversions, twists, standing balances
  • → Seasonal / Holiday: New Year, spring renewal, summer energy, autumn grounding, winter stillness
The Adaptation Workflow

Every class record has a "Taught Count" and "Last Taught" date. When a sequence hits four or five uses, duplicate it, note the adaptation (swap the peak pose, shorten the warm-up, add a pranayama), and save the new version. Your library grows organically from your actual teaching history.

Adapting for Different Audiences

The Audience Notes field lets you log how you modify a sequence for specific groups: remove the jumpbacks for a seniors class, add hip prep for corporate desk workers, slow the pace and emphasize breath for prenatal. One base sequence becomes three without rebuilding anything.
