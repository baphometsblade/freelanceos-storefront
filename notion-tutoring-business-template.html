The tutor problem

Teaching is the easy part.
Running the business is the chaos.

Every tutor hits a point where the administrative overhead starts eroding the time and energy that should go to students.

📄

Session notes in random docs with no student history

Notes from last month's algebra session are in a Google Doc somewhere. Last week's are in a different notebook. When a student comes back after a break, you have no idea where you left off.

💸

Billing across multiple students is impossible to track

Who owes you for last week? Did the Chen family pay for their package? Does Marcus still have 3 sessions left or 2? Every billing conversation starts with you frantically checking three different places.

📊

No system to show parents concrete progress

Parents are paying premium rates and want to know their child is improving. "It's going well" doesn't cut it when they ask for specifics. You know the student has improved — but you can't show it.

📅

Scheduling chaos across platforms and students

Your Calendly link, your texting with parents, your recurring bookings — all separate. When a student cancels, you're not sure whether the makeup session ever got rescheduled or fell through a crack.

Session preparation

Reusable session templates that make every lesson better

The best tutors don't improvise session-to-session — they build a repeatable preparation system that lets them focus on the student, not what to do next. FreelanceOS Pro's Session Planner gives you a template structure that works across every subject and student level.

Each session plan links to the student's profile, pulling in their current learning goals and the previous session's notes automatically. When you sit down to prepare, all the context is already there.

The session template structure

Learning objectives for this session — review questions from last session's homework — warm-up activity and materials — main instruction block with content notes — practice problems or exercises assigned — time estimate and difficulty level — feedback to share with parents — prep notes for the next session.

After the session

Update the session log with what was actually covered, what the student struggled with, what clicked, and what needs reinforcement. This becomes the foundation for the next session plan and for the monthly parent progress report.

Common questions

Frequently asked questions

Everything tutors ask before getting started with FreelanceOS Pro.

Can I manage multiple students with different subjects in this template?
Yes. The Student Roster is a relational database where each student profile stores their grade level, subject areas, learning goals, session schedule, and billing rate. Every session log, progress note, and parent communication links back to the individual student. You can filter your entire view by subject, grade level, session status, or billing status — making it straightforward to run 15+ students across different subjects without losing track of anyone.
How do I track student progress and share it with parents?
FreelanceOS Pro's Progress Tracker logs skill checkpoints, test score milestones, and learning objective completion per student. After each session you log what was covered, what the student demonstrated mastery of, and what needs more work. At the end of each month you can generate a progress summary from your logged session data and share it with parents — showing concrete growth, upcoming focus areas, and score improvements. Parents see real data instead of vague reassurances.
How does billing work for students with different rates and packages?
Each student in FreelanceOS Pro has their own billing rate — hourly, session package, or both. The Billing & Payment Tracker links every session to that student's rate and shows you invoice status (sent/paid/outstanding) across all students at once. Package billing tracks the remaining session balance per student. You can see a full outstanding balance view in one click rather than checking individual logs per student.
Does this work for online tutoring or only in-person?
FreelanceOS Pro works equally well for online, in-person, and hybrid tutoring practices. Each student profile includes a session format field (online/in-person/hybrid), and the session planner includes a platform link field for recurring Zoom or Google Meet links. The resource library is especially useful for online tutors — it stores digital resources, shared document links, and worksheet files that you can pull up quickly during sessions.
I tutor for SAT/ACT test prep. Is there anything specific for that?
Yes. The Progress Tracker includes a standardized test prep module with score logging across practice test dates, section-by-section breakdown (Math, Reading, Writing, Science for SAT; Math, English, Reading, Science, Writing for ACT), target score tracking, and test date countdown. You can see a student's score trajectory across multiple practice tests and adjust your instruction to their weakest sections. The session planner includes a test prep template with practice problem sets, timed sections completed, and error pattern notes.
Can I build a reusable lesson library by subject?
Yes. FreelanceOS Pro includes a Resource Library — a searchable database of lesson materials, worksheets, explanation frameworks, practice problem sets, and reference links organized by subject, grade level, and topic. When you build a great explanation for solving quadratic equations, a reliable Spanish verb conjugation warm-up, or a strong reading comprehension strategy lesson, it goes into the library and is reusable for every future student at that level. Build once, use it for every student — stop rebuilding the same lessons from scratch.
