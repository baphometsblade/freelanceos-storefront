The Problem

You Create for a Living. Your Business Systems Should Match Your Ambition.

Music schools teach craft — theory, technique, performance, production, songwriting. They rarely teach independent musicians how to run a structured music career that turns creative output into sustainable income. The business side of being a musician is a skill set in itself, and most artists are figuring it out from scratch without a system.

🎤

Gigs tracked in texts and DMs

Booking conversations scattered across Instagram DMs, text threads, and email chains. You are not sure whether the venue confirmed the deposit, what the load-in time is, or whether you have a contract signed — and you find out the hard way when something goes wrong on a Friday night.

🎸

Songs live in disconnected places

Song ideas in a voice memo app, demos in a DAW folder on an old laptop, lyrics in a notes app on your phone, and half-finished projects scattered across several hard drives. You have more material than you realize but no way to see it all in one place and prioritize what to develop next.

📢

Releases get launched without a plan

You finish a song, upload it to your distributor, post about it twice, and watch the streams plateau at 200. You know you should be pitching playlists, sending press releases, and running a coordinated campaign — but with no system, it never happens the way you intended.

💸

Music income is a mystery

Streaming royalties from three distributors, gig fees paid in cash and Venmo, a sync check from last year, and sporadic teaching income. You have no real picture of what your music career actually earns across all its income streams or which activities generate the most financial return for your time.

The Solution

A Complete Music Career OS in Notion

FreelanceOS Pro for Musicians connects your gig database, song tracker, release pipeline, venue contacts, revenue tracker, practice log, press tracker, and social media content calendar in one relational Notion workspace built for independent artists who take their music career seriously.

Gig & Performance Database — Venue, Pay, Setlist & Status

A master database of every gig, show, and performance opportunity — venue name and address, date and time, pay and payment terms, setlist link, travel and accommodation notes, equipment checklist, and status from first inquiry through booking confirmation to post-show completion. Sort by date to see your upcoming performance schedule clearly, filter by pay to review which venues offer the best financial return, and look back at your complete performance history when assembling a press kit, booking pitch, or grant application. Link every gig to your venue and promoter database so the booking contact is always two clicks away when you need to confirm a load-in time or chase an outstanding payment.

Song & Project Tracker — Status, Collaborators & Production Notes

A complete project database for every piece of music in your creative pipeline — song title, genre, current production status (Idea / Demo / Recording / Mixing / Mastering / Released), collaborators and co-writers involved, songwriter split percentages if applicable, recording location and session notes, and the linked release it is destined for. Filter to see everything currently in the recording stage, sort by priority to focus your limited studio time on the right projects, and navigate from any song record to its full production history in one click. Having every project visible in a single organized database means nothing slips through the creative cracks during busy touring periods and you always know exactly where every piece of music stands in your production pipeline.

Release Pipeline — Album/EP/Single, Distributor & Marketing Plan

A release management system where each album, EP, or single has its own record with the planned release date, distributor platform, marketing plan checklist, playlist pitching tracker by curator and platform, press and review outreach log, social media promotion schedule, and promotional asset checklist. Build your release campaign plan four to six weeks in advance and execute each step systematically rather than scrambling to pitch playlists the night before release. Independent musicians who approach every release with a documented pipeline generate more first-week streams, more editorial playlist placements, more press coverage, and more social momentum than equally talented artists who release without a structured campaign behind the music.

Venue & Promoter Contact Database — History, Booking Notes & Relationships

A contact database for every venue, promoter, booking agent, festival organizer, and event contact you have ever worked with or want to work with. Each record holds the contact name, organization, phone and email, venue capacity, typical pay range, relationship history, the dates you have played there before, and notes on what made each booking successful or challenging. Your venue database is one of the most valuable long-term professional assets you build as a gigging musician — the difference between consistently getting booked at quality venues and perpetually starting from scratch is the depth and quality of your professional network and how systematically you maintain those relationships over time.

Music Business Revenue Tracker — Gigs, Streaming, Sync & Teaching

A complete income tracker logging every revenue stream in your music career — gig fees and performance payments, streaming royalties by distributor, sync licensing fees, master use fees, merchandise sales, Bandcamp and direct sales, private event and wedding performance fees, music teaching income, and grants or commissions received. See your total monthly income across all streams, track each source quarter over quarter to identify trends, and understand your actual income mix so you can make intentional decisions about which activities to invest more time and energy in based on real financial data rather than assumptions about what the music industry pays.

Practice & Skill Development Log — Focus Areas & Progress Notes

A structured practice log tracking every session with date, duration, focus area (technical exercises, repertoire, improvisation, music theory, ear training, composition, production, performance preparation), your observations about what clicked and what needs more work, and specific areas to prioritize in the next session. Tracking your practice intentionally over weeks and months reveals where you improve fastest, which skills need more deliberate attention, and how your creative output connects to your practice habits. Musicians who log their development tend to improve significantly faster because documented progress creates positive feedback loops that sustain discipline through inevitable creative plateaus and periods where improvement feels invisible.

Press & Media Tracker — Reviews, Playlists, Radio & Interviews

A complete media and press tracking database for every review submission, interview request, playlist pitch, radio station submission, blog feature inquiry, and sync licensing opportunity. Each record holds the publication or platform name, editorial contact, submission date, current status (Submitted / Under Review / Published / Rejected / No Response), and any coverage or response received with a link to the published piece. Independent musicians who pitch systematically with tracked follow-up dates land dramatically more coverage than artists who pitch reactively and forget which outlets they have already contacted. Your press tracker becomes your growing media footprint and a reference document for booking agents, labels, and brand partnership inquiries.

Social Media & Marketing Content Calendar for Music Promotion

A content planning calendar organizing your social media and marketing activities across Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Facebook, and newsletter platforms. Plan content in advance around your release schedule — behind-the-scenes recording content in the production phase, teaser clips in the two weeks before release, launch day content, and community engagement content between releases. Musicians with a consistent content presence between releases maintain audience attention and algorithm momentum instead of going silent for months and having to rebuild from zero with every release. Batch your content planning once a week and execute against the calendar daily instead of making reactive content decisions in the moment while also managing a full gigging and recording schedule.
