The T-90 Day Countdown: Your Event Planning Backbone

The most valuable part of any event planning system isn't the day-of checklist — it's the runway. The T-90 countdown timeline inside FreelanceOS breaks your planning into four distinct phases:

  • T-90 to T-60 — Strategy & Foundation: Define event goals, confirm the format and date, secure your venue or platform, set your budget ceiling, and identify any speakers or collaborators you need to reach out to early.
  • T-60 to T-30 — Logistics & Promotion: Lock in vendors, sign contracts, build the landing page, open registration, start your email warm-up sequence, and draft your promotional content calendar.
  • T-30 to T-7 — Final Prep: Finalize the run-of-show, confirm all vendor deliverables, send attendee communications, prepare materials and slide decks, do a full technical rehearsal.
  • T-7 to Day-Of — Event Week: Send final reminders, prep day-of logistics, confirm all access and technology, and execute with confidence because the hard work is already done.

For shorter lead times, the timeline compresses gracefully. Planning a webinar in three weeks? The same phases still apply — they're just tighter. The template makes this easy because tasks are tagged by phase, not hard-coded to specific calendar dates.

Managing Vendors, Speakers, and Collaborators Without Losing Your Mind

Every event involves other people. The bigger the event, the more external parties: guest speakers who need tech specs and green-room instructions, AV vendors who need access to the venue, photographers who need a shot list, sponsors who need logo placements confirmed by a deadline. Each of these relationships has its own communication thread, contract, and payment schedule.

The Vendor & Speaker Hub in FreelanceOS centralizes all of it. Each contact gets a record with their category, payment terms, contract status (draft, signed, paid), key deliverables, and a notes field for anything that doesn't fit a structured column. You can filter to see all unpaid invoices, all unsigned contracts, or all speakers who haven't confirmed their availability — right from your event dashboard, without digging through email.

If you're running a multi-speaker virtual summit, this database becomes indispensable. It replaces the messy spreadsheet most event organizers use, and because it's inside Notion, you can link speaker records directly to their sessions in the run-of-show.
