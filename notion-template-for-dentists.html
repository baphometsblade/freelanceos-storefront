FreelanceOS Pro for dentists is a practice administration workspace — not a clinical records system. It includes a patient lifecycle tracker, recall campaign planner, staff scheduling overview, CE credit and license tracker, vendor and supply management database, practice KPI dashboard, and treatment plan acceptance tracker. It is designed to organize the business operations of a dental practice inside Notion.
Administrative management only. FreelanceOS Pro organizes the business operations layer of a dental practice — patient tracking, recall outreach, scheduling, supplies, finances, and team management. Clinical records, charting, and treatment notes belong in your practice management software (Dentrix, Eaglesoft, Open Dental, etc.). This template works alongside your clinical system, not instead of it.
The recall campaign planner is a database of patients due for hygiene appointments organized by overdue status — 0–30 days overdue, 31–60 days, 61–90 days, and 90+ days. You track contact attempts, the method used (text, call, email), the patient's response, and whether an appointment was booked. It gives your front desk team a systematic recall workflow rather than working from a random list.
Yes — the CE Credit and License Tracker has a record per team member, with their license type, state board requirements, renewal date, credits completed, courses attended, and certificates logged. You can see at a glance which team members are on track and which have upcoming renewal deadlines. This is especially useful for practices with multiple hygienists, assistants, and associate dentists.
Yes — the Vendor and Supply Management module lets you maintain a supplier database with contact information, products ordered, pricing, reorder thresholds, and order history. You can flag items below a reorder level and maintain a running log of what you ordered and when. It is not connected to supplier systems — it is a manual tracking layer that gives you visibility over your supply chain.
The Treatment Plan Acceptance Tracker logs every treatment plan presented to a patient — treatment type, estimated value, presentation date, acceptance status, and any follow-up contacts made. By reviewing acceptance rates over time, you can identify patterns: which treatment types get accepted, which are frequently declined, and which patients need follow-up. This visibility helps you improve case presentation and find untapped production opportunities.
