Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Immigration Consultants

Is this suitable for licensed immigration consultants or immigration attorneys?

This template is designed as a practice management and case tracking tool for registered immigration consultants — RCICs in Canada, OISC-registered advisers in the UK, registered migration agents in Australia, and similar licensed practitioners in other jurisdictions. It is not a legal case management system and does not replace compliant recordkeeping required by your licensing body. Immigration attorneys can use this alongside their firm's legal practice management software for the practice operations and business development elements, but should not use it as a substitute for their regulated case management system.

How does the document checklist system work?

The Document Checklist Library contains reusable master checklists for each visa type and destination country you work with — H-1B US work visa, UK Skilled Worker, Canada Express Entry, Australia Skilled Migration, EU Blue Card, spouse or family visa, student visa, and others you add as you encounter them. For each new client case, you link or duplicate the relevant master checklist and then track document collection status item by item — missing, received, reviewed, submitted — so you always know exactly what is outstanding. As your library grows, new cases start with a complete, tested checklist rather than a blank page.

Can I track cases across multiple destination countries?

Yes. The Case Tracker stores destination country and visa type for every case, so you can filter your entire active caseload by country, visa category, or case stage at any time. If you work across US, Canada, UK, and Australia simultaneously — which many boutique immigration practices do — you can view cases grouped by country, sorted by next deadline, or filtered by current stage. The Visa Reference Library is also organized by destination country, so your reference notes and knowledge base stay organized as your practice expands geographically.

How do I stay on top of case deadlines when managing many clients?

The Deadline and Appointment Tracker is a unified database of all critical dates across every active case — application submission deadlines, biometrics appointments, visa expiry dates, permit renewal reminders, appeal windows, and follow-up dates. You sort by urgency or filter by client to see what is due this week and this month. For larger caseloads, most immigration consultants check this tracker at the start of each working day and at the beginning of each week to stay ahead of every approaching deadline before it becomes a crisis. Missing an immigration deadline can have serious consequences for clients, so this tracker is designed to surface urgency clearly.
