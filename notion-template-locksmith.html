Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Locksmiths and Security Consultants

How does the service call log work for locksmiths?

The service call log tracks every job with date and time, call source, job type (residential lockout through security assessment), service completed, time on job, materials used, call fee plus labor plus materials breakdown, total invoice, payment method, and payment received status. Filter by job type to see how much revenue automotive programming generates versus commercial rekeying, or by payment status to follow up on outstanding invoices. Your complete, searchable service record for every job you run — which also becomes your evidence trail if a client or dispatcher ever disputes a charge or completion.

How does the commercial account tracker work?

The commercial account tracker stores every commercial client with property type, number of locations, key control system, master key system status, access control system, last rekey date, service frequency, annual account value, primary contact, contract status, and after-hours emergency contact. Commercial accounts are the most valuable recurring revenue in a locksmith business — they generate predictable income from rekeying cycles, access control maintenance, and emergency calls at premium rates. Knowing which accounts have a contract on file, which key control system they use, and when they last rekeyed keeps your commercial relationships organized, defensible, and positioned for upselling new security services.

How does the automotive log help with documentation?

The automotive log records vehicle year, make, model, key type, programming equipment used, blank availability, programming success, VIN verified, and proof of ownership verified — for every automotive key job. Consistent VIN verification and ownership documentation is critical professional practice for automotive locksmiths. It creates an evidence trail confirming the customer established ownership before you provided service. Keeping this documentation in a searchable log rather than on inconsistent paper tickets or memory makes your practice defensible and professional across every job, not just the ones that feel high-risk in the moment.

How do I track key inventory and hardware stock?

The key and hardware inventory tracks key blank manufacturer and series, quantity on hand, machine compatibility, authorization notes for high-security blanks (Medeco, Mul-T-Lock, ABLOY, Schlage Primus), automotive transponder blanks and chips, smart lock inventory for sales, deadbolt and lock hardware by grade, and panic hardware. Knowing your current stock by blank series and hardware type before accepting a call — rather than discovering you are out of a critical blank when you are already on site an hour from the shop — is the difference between a completed job and a return trip that costs you two hours of billable time. The inventory also makes truck restocking a systematic checklist rather than a guessing game.
