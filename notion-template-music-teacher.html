What's Inside

Six Modules Built for Private Music Teachers

🎵

Student Roster & Lesson Tracker

Student name, instrument, lesson day/time (recurring), lesson duration (30/45/60 min), level (beginner/intermediate/advanced), age group (child/teen/adult), current piece, technique focus this term, lesson rate, payment method, payment due date, makeup lessons owed, notes. Every student's current status at a glance before their next lesson.

📋

Lesson Plan & Assignment Log

Per lesson per student: date, what was covered (technique drills, scales, pieces worked on), assignment for next week with specific instructions, next lesson focus, parent communication sent (for child students). The substitute teacher's best friend — and your own memory when a student claims they forgot what you assigned.

🎼

Repertoire & Material Library

Pieces tagged by title, composer, difficulty level (1-10), instrument, style (classical/jazz/pop/film/folk/original), appropriate age and level, PDF source or edition link, and your teaching notes (common trouble spots, fingering suggestions, how you teach it). Your curated teaching catalog built over your career, searchable in seconds.

🎭

Recital & Performance Planner

Event name, date, venue, type (recital/studio showcase/competition/masterclass/ensemble), students performing, piece each student is performing, parent notification sent, accompanist needed, program printed, run-through scheduled, post-recital notes. One place for every detail from announcement to applause.

💰

Billing & Payment Tracker

Per student: monthly tuition, payment method (cash/Venmo/Zelle/check/auto-debit), payment due date, payment received (yes/no), late payment follow-up sent, year-to-date income per student. Your small business billing system without a spreadsheet — see who has paid and who is overdue at a glance every month.

💼

Music Studio Business Dashboard

Active students, weekly lesson hours, monthly income, income by instrument, student retention rate, new student inquiries, summer lesson retention rate (track the inevitable summer dip), referral sources (parent word of mouth, school, social media, website), and annual income goal — the full picture of your teaching business.
