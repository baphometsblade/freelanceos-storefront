Questions From Occupational Therapists in Private Practice

Is it safe to use Notion for my OT practice management?

Notion is appropriate for the business management and professional development functions covered in this template, provided you maintain a strict boundary around protected health information. The FreelanceOS OT Practice OS is explicitly designed for non-clinical content only: business metrics, referral relationships, CE tracking, professional development, general resource organization, and marketing planning — none of which involves patient names, diagnoses, session notes, evaluation results, or any other PHI. For all clinical documentation, patient records, scheduling, and billing involving patient-specific data, you must use a HIPAA-compliant EHR/EMR system such as WebPT, Therabill, SimplePractice, TheraNest, or another platform that provides a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) and meets the technical, physical, and administrative safeguards required by HIPAA. Notion does not provide a BAA and is not appropriate for PHI storage regardless of how the workspace is configured. Many OTs use Notion for their business operating layer — the management, development, and administrative functions that support clinical work — while maintaining their HIPAA-compliant clinical system for all patient-related activity. This two-system approach is standard and appropriate.

How does the CE tracker handle NBCOT renewal requirements specifically?

The CE tracker is designed around NBCOT's continuing competency structure. NBCOT renewal requires 36 Professional Development Units (PDUs) over a three-year certification period, with specific PDU values for different activity types and caps on certain categories. The tracker's CE category field maps to NBCOT's activity categories, and the hours awarded field captures the PDU value for each completed activity. Filtering by the current renewal cycle and summing PDUs by category gives you a running total that shows your current standing against NBCOT's requirements without manual calculation. The tracker also accommodates state license renewal, which runs on a different cycle (typically one or two years) and may have different hour requirements or category specifications than NBCOT renewal — you note the applicable requirement in each CE record. For OTs with specialty certifications like CHT (which has its own continuing education requirements through HTCC), SIPT, or BCPR, separate certification records track those independent renewal cycles alongside NBCOT and state license requirements. The certificate storage location field links each CE record to the PDF certificate in your cloud storage, creating a documented record that satisfies NBCOT audit requirements without searching through email or download folders when a compliance audit occurs.

How does the referral source tracker help me build stronger physician relationships?

The referral source tracker operationalizes physician relationship development by creating a systematic follow-up practice rather than relying on the good intentions that caseload pressure consistently crowds out. The last contact date and next follow-up date fields work together: each week you filter the tracker by next follow-up date and make the contacts on your list, then update the last contact date and set the next follow-up date before closing the record. This creates a consistent cadence of professional outreach to your most important referral relationships that continues whether your schedule is full or you have capacity to fill. The relationship strength field (Active / Warm / Lapsed / Developing) drives the content of outreach: Active referrers with consistent volume get substantive professional engagement — in-service presentations on OT approaches, clinical consultation offers, research summaries relevant to their patient population. Warm referrers who have sent referrals in the past but less recently get a personal check-in and a clinical education update that reconnects the relationship. Developing referrers who have expressed interest but have not yet sent a referral get a formal introduction to your practice services and an invitation to observe or consult. The relationship notes field is where you capture the professional interests and context that make outreach personal rather than transactional — knowing that a specific pediatrician has particular interest in sensory processing disorder and engaging them with relevant clinical content builds a relationship that generates referrals from professional respect rather than proximity.

How does the self-care tracker support burnout prevention practically?

The self-care and burnout prevention tracker is designed around the recognition that burnout monitoring for healthcare professionals must be a structured practice, not a reaction to crisis. The weekly check-in record takes five minutes to complete — rating your caseload sustainability, noting specific stressors, and documenting what occupational balance activities are in your coming week. The caseload sustainability rating (Green / Yellow / Red) is the early warning system: a Yellow rating triggers proactive review of what specific aspect of the current caseload is unsustainable and what adjustment is available — whether that is reducing new referral intake, restructuring session scheduling, increasing supervision support, or adding specific rest activities to the week. Waiting until a Yellow becomes a Red before making adjustments is the burnout pathway; the tracker is designed to surface Yellow earlier and consistently enough to allow Green-restoring adjustments before the threshold into burnout territory. The recovery practice notes field creates accountability for the specific activities that buffer against burnout: writing "30-minute walk three times this week, Tuesday cooking with partner, Thursday yoga class" is a different level of commitment than intending to take better care of yourself generally. For supervisors and practice owners, the self-care tracker also models the monitoring practice you want to cultivate in your supervisees: demonstrating that burnout prevention is a structured professional practice, documented with the same intentionality as clinical work, communicates organizational values about sustainable practice more effectively than any policy statement.

How does the professional development log support specialty certification applications?

Specialty certifications in OT — CHT, SIPT, BCPR, and others — require documentation of professional development activities, supervision hours, and clinical experience that must be assembled for certification applications and subsequent renewal cycles. The professional development log provides the ongoing documentation infrastructure that makes certification applications straightforward rather than requiring retroactive reconstruction of years of professional activity. The supervision hours tracking is particularly important for CHT candidates, who must document a specific number of supervised hand therapy hours with a qualified supervisor before becoming eligible to sit for the CHT exam — tracking these hours with supervisor names, dates, and session notes in the professional development log creates the audit-ready record that the Hand Therapy Certification Commission requires. The publications and presentations fields document the scholarly and educational activity that NBCOT and some specialty certifications value as professional development evidence beyond CE hours. Conference presentations, guest lectures in OT academic programs, peer-reviewed publications, and clinical education webinars all belong in the professional development log with enough detail to be cited precisely in a certification portfolio or CV. The log also supports AOTA specialty certification programs — Board Certifications and Specialty Certifications offered through AOTA — that require documented evidence of advanced practice in specific clinical areas.
