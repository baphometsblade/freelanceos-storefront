The Problem

Pregnancy is joyful — but also a lot to keep track of

Between 40 weeks of development milestones, a full calendar of OB visits and scans, a mountain of baby gear to research and buy, and the emotional journey of preparing for a new life — it's easy to feel scattered. Most parents-to-be end up with scattered notes, missed appointment details, and the nagging feeling that important information is slipping through the cracks.

📝

Weekly milestones tracked in scattered notes

A sticky note here, a voice memo there, a photo buried in your camera roll. Your Week 12 ultrasound memory deserves better than a misplaced screenshot. Every week of pregnancy is a milestone worth capturing properly — with context, dates, and your actual feelings in the moment.

🏥

Forgetting the details from doctor appointments

You walk out of every OB appointment with a head full of information — blood pressure numbers, fundal height measurements, test results, the doctor's exact words — and by the time you get to the car, half of it is fuzzy. A structured appointment log changes everything about how you track your prenatal care.

🛒

No system for baby shopping and nursery planning

Car seat options in one browser tab, crib reviews in another, Amazon wish list growing uncontrollably, registry spreadsheet last updated two months ago. Baby gear research is overwhelming without a single organized system that tracks what you need, what you've bought, what it cost, and where it's coming from.

🌪️

Overwhelmed by pregnancy information everywhere

Apps, books, Facebook groups, Reddit threads, blog posts, podcasts, your mother-in-law's advice — pregnancy information comes from everywhere at once. Without a personal system to capture, organize, and reference what actually matters to you and your unique pregnancy, the flood of information becomes noise instead of support.

Daily tracking for your physical and emotional experience of pregnancy. FAQ

Common questions about the Pregnancy Tracker Template

Is this Notion pregnancy tracker only for pregnancy, or does it work postpartum too?

⌄
The FreelanceOS Notion pregnancy tracker template is designed to carry you from the moment you find out you're expecting all the way through the first six months postpartum. The Postpartum Recovery Tracker and Newborn Essentials Checklist modules activate seamlessly once baby arrives, turning the same workspace into your postpartum command center. Many users report using it well into their baby's first year — tracking feeding schedules, healing milestones, pediatric appointments, and developmental checkpoints all in one place. This is one of the most frequently mentioned benefits in customer reviews: you don't need to start over with a new system when your baby is born.

Can my partner access and edit the pregnancy tracker in Notion?

⌄
Absolutely. Notion's sharing system means you can invite your partner (or any support person) with full edit access at no extra cost. They can add their own notes to the Symptom Journal, vote on baby names in the Name Brainstorm Database, check off items in the shopping lists, and view appointment details. Many couples use it as a shared digital pregnancy journal — a living record of this special season they can revisit together for years to come. You can also invite a doula, a family member, or anyone else involved in your pregnancy journey with the appropriate level of access.

Does the template include a birth plan builder?

⌄
Yes — the Birth Plan Builder is one of the most-loved modules in the template. It walks you through all the key decisions: pain management preferences, labor environment (lighting, music, who's in the room), pushing and delivery preferences, immediate postpartum wishes (skin-to-skin, cord cutting, delayed bathing), and emergency scenario notes. It includes a pre-formatted layout you can share with your OB or midwife ahead of time, and an Emergency Contacts section so everyone on your care team is one click away. Many users say building the birth plan helped them feel calmer and more prepared going into labor, even when things didn't go exactly as planned — because they'd thought through their priorities in advance.

Is this Notion pregnancy tracker suitable for all three trimesters?

⌄
Yes, the template spans all 40 weeks plus postpartum. The Weekly Milestone Tracker includes a dedicated entry for every week of pregnancy, with space to note baby's development (your own observations and feelings — this is an organizational tool, not medical content), your symptoms, energy levels, and emotional experience. First-trimester users appreciate the early appointment log and symptom journal. Second-trimester users love the baby shopping lists, nursery planner, and Name Brainstorm Database. Third-trimester users rely heavily on the birth plan builder, hospital bag checklist, and the countdown feel of the milestone tracker in those final weeks. You can start using it at any point in your pregnancy and immediately catch up on earlier weeks.

Can I track multiple pregnancies or use this for a second pregnancy?

⌄
Definitely. Each time you start a new pregnancy, simply duplicate the template in Notion — it takes about 30 seconds. Your previous pregnancy data stays archived in the original copy, while the new duplicate starts completely fresh. Many parents use this system to compare their pregnancies side by side. You can also customize the template for each pregnancy, removing modules you don't need and adding new ones (like a sibling-prep section to help older children get ready for the new baby) as your family's needs evolve. The one-time $29 purchase covers all your future pregnancies — no need to buy it again.
