Frequently Asked Questions

Is this Notion template built specifically for wedding planners?
Yes — every database and workflow is purpose-built for the wedding planning business: client and event tracking, vendor management across every category, per-wedding budget tracking, wedding day timeline building, and a 12-month planning checklist system. It works for day-of coordinators, partial planners, full-service wedding planners, and bridal consultants handling elopements through large ballroom events.
How does the Vendor Management Database work when I'm running multiple weddings at once?
The Vendor Management Database is organized per wedding. Every vendor — from venue and caterer to florist and DJ — is linked to the specific wedding they're contracted for. You can filter by wedding date to see all vendors for an upcoming event, or filter by vendor category across your whole portfolio. Contract amount, deposit paid, final payment due date, deliverables, and insurance on file are tracked per entry. Running 5 weddings in a busy fall season means 60+ vendor entries — the database keeps them organized by event so nothing goes unpaid.
Can I share the wedding day timeline with my clients and vendors?
Yes. Each wedding's day-of timeline is built as a Notion page that can be shared via a public link. The timeline tracks every event from hair and makeup start through the final send-off, with scheduled time, responsible vendor, and room or area noted for each. Share the link directly with the couple, the venue coordinator, the caterer, and the photography team so everyone works from the same master schedule. One link replaces emailing multiple PDF versions and chasing confirmations from five different vendor contacts.
Does the template help me track new leads and convert inquiries to bookings?
Yes. The Inquiry and Lead Tracker captures every new inquiry with the couple's names, event date, venue they're considering, package interest, budget range, inquiry source, and follow-up stage. The Business Dashboard shows your lead conversion rate — total inquiries vs. contracts signed — broken out by referral source so you can see whether your venue partnerships, The Knot listings, or past couple referrals are your best lead generators. Knowing where bookings come from is how you decide where to invest your marketing time each season.
