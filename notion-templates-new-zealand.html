New Zealand's freelance market has grown significantly since the pandemic, with a large proportion of the workforce now engaged in contract, freelance, or self-employed work. GST Tracker — NZD $60,000 Threshold

New Zealand GST is a single flat rate of 15% — much simpler than Ireland's multi-rate VAT or Australia's mixed-supply GST. Registration becomes compulsory once your taxable turnover exceeds NZD $60,000 in any 12-month period. Below this, registration is voluntary. Provisional Tax Calendar — 3 Instalments

If your residual income tax (RIT) in the previous year exceeded NZD $5,000, you become a provisional taxpayer and must make three instalments during the current year. Standard uplift method dates: August 28 (first instalment), January 15 (second instalment), and May 7 (third instalment). Each instalment is approximately one-third of 105% of last year's RIT. FreelanceOS pre-populates all three provisional tax due dates, tracks each payment made, and includes a variation log if you opt to use the estimate method (useful when current-year income has dropped significantly from the prior year).

ACC Levy Tracker — CoverPlus & CoverPlus Extra

ACC (Accident Compensation Corporation) is New Zealand's unique no-fault accident compensation scheme — all self-employed people pay an annual levy and are covered for injuries that prevent them from working. There are two main options: ACC CoverPlus provides weekly compensation of up to 80% of your previous year's income if injured. ACC CoverPlus Extra lets you negotiate a fixed weekly compensation amount (useful if your freelance income is highly variable year to year). The New Zealand Freelancer's Tax Guide

New Zealand's tax system is simpler than most English-speaking countries — no capital gains tax on most assets, a single flat GST rate, and a relatively straightforward self-employment regime. Here is a complete overview of your obligations as a NZ sole trader or company freelancer.

Income Tax Rates for New Zealand Freelancers (2024–25)

NZ income tax is progressive with no separate self-employment tax. Rates: 10.5% on the first $14,000; 17.5% on $14,001–$48,000; 30% on $48,001–$70,000; 33% on $70,001–$180,000; and 39% on income above $180,000. Your taxable income is gross self-employment income minus allowable business expenses. New Zealand's independent earner tax credit (IETC) may reduce your liability at lower income levels. No tax return is required if your only income is PAYE employment — but as a freelancer, you must file an IR3 regardless.

New Zealand Tax Year: April 1 to March 31

New Zealand's tax year is April 1 to March 31 — not the calendar year and not the same as Australia's July–June financial year. Your IR3 return for the year ending March 31, 2026 is due July 7, 2026 if you don't have a tax agent. If you use a registered tax agent (a New Zealand tax agent or accountant), the deadline is typically extended to March 31 of the following year. FreelanceOS's NZ financial year calendar tracks the April–March cycle with all key dates pre-populated.

IRD Number and Business Registration

Every New Zealand resident who earns income needs an IRD number — this is your tax identity number used for all IRD interactions. If you're a new freelancer, apply online at ird.govt.nz — the process takes a few days for NZ residents. If you're not a NZ resident but working in NZ, you must still obtain an IRD number before starting to earn. If incorporating a company, you need a separate IRD number for the company (different from your personal IRD number) and an NZBN (New Zealand Business Number) from the Companies Register.
