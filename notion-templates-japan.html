Freelancing in Japan: The 2026 Landscape for Kojin Jigyo-nushi

Japan's freelance market has transformed significantly since the COVID-19 pandemic accelerated remote work adoption. With over 1 million registered independent contractors (個人事業主, kojin jigyo-nushi), Japan's freelance sector now spans software engineering, design, content creation, consulting, and creative services across a growing network of digital platforms.

The Platforms Japanese Freelancers Use

Japan has a robust domestic freelance platform ecosystem alongside international options:

  • Lancers (ランサーズ) — Japan's largest freelance marketplace covering writing, design, web development, and consulting
  • Crowdworks (クラウドワークス) — second-largest platform, strong in tech, design, and data entry projects
  • Coconala (ココナラ) — skill marketplace focused on personal services, illustration, and consulting
  • Findy (ファインディ) — high-skill engineer marketplace with premium project rates
  • Upwork & Toptal — used by Japan-based freelancers targeting international clients for higher rates

Key Cities for Japanese Freelancers

Tokyo remains the dominant hub for tech and creative freelancers. Osaka has a strong design and e-commerce ecosystem. Fukuoka is emerging as Japan's "Silicon Valley of the South" — with city-level support for startups and freelancers, lower cost of living, and an English-friendly tech community. Kyoto has a growing creative freelance community in UX, illustration, and content creation.

Growing Sectors for Freelancers in Japan

The sectors experiencing the fastest freelance growth in Japan include:

  • エンジニア (Software Engineers) — especially in React, Python, and iOS/Android development
  • デザイナー (UI/UX Designers) — with domestic and international demand
  • 動画編集者 (Video Editors) — explosive growth from YouTube and TikTok creator ecosystem
  • マーケター (Digital Marketers) — SEO, social media, and paid advertising specialists
  • ライター・翻訳者 (Writers and Translators) — English-Japanese translation especially high-value

Kakutei Shinkoku: The Annual Tax Filing

Kakutei shinkoku (確定申告) is the annual income tax self-assessment filing that Japanese freelancers (kojin jigyo-nushi) must submit each year by March 15. Unlike salaried employees whose taxes are withheld automatically, freelancers calculate their own taxable income — total revenue minus business expenses — and pay the resulting tax.

Allowable deductions include: home office costs (按分,按分計算 for the business-use portion), equipment and software purchases, platform fees, communication costs, and professional development expenses. Tracking these throughout the year in a Notion expense database makes March filing significantly less stressful.

Japan's National Tax Agency (国税庁) e-Tax system allows freelancers to file kakutei shinkoku online using a My Number Card and compatible card reader or smartphone. Notion's annual income summary makes it straightforward to enter figures directly into the e-Tax system.

Frequently Asked Questions: Notion for Japanese Freelancers

What is kakutei shinkoku and how can Notion help?
Kakutei shinkoku (確定申告) is the annual income tax self-assessment filing required for Japanese freelancers (kojin jigyo-nushi). It is due by March 15 each year. Notion helps by tracking all income and expenses throughout the year in an organized database, with rollup totals by category that map directly to the tax filing form — significantly reducing year-end scrambling.
What is Japan's Qualified Invoice System (Invoice Seido)?
Japan's Qualified Invoice System (インボイス制度, Tekikaku Kessai Seikyusho) was implemented in October 2023. It requires freelancers who want to issue invoices that clients can claim as tax deductions to register as Qualified Invoice Issuers (Tekikaku Kessai Jigyosha) and include their registration number on all invoices. Unregistered freelancers' invoices cannot be used by clients for input tax credits.
What are the best freelance platforms in Japan?
The major domestic freelance platforms in Japan are Lancers (ランサーズ) for general freelancing, Crowdworks (クラウドワークス) for tech and design, Coconala (ココナラ) for personal services, and Findy for high-skill engineering projects. For international clients, Japan-based freelancers commonly use Upwork, Toptal, and direct LinkedIn outreach.
Can I use Notion in Japanese?
Yes. Notion fully supports Japanese language input across all platforms — desktop, web, iOS, and Android. You can write page titles, database entries, and notes in Japanese, use Japanese tags and filters throughout your workspace, and mix Japanese and English content in the same database without any issues.
How do Japanese freelancers handle National Health Insurance?
Japanese freelancers pay National Health Insurance (国民健康保険, Kokumin Kenko Hoken) premiums calculated based on prior year income. These are paid monthly and can be tracked in your Notion expense database as a business-related deduction for kakutei shinkoku. National Pension (国民年金, Kokumin Nenkin) is a separate flat monthly payment that is also deductible.
Is Notion popular among Japanese freelancers?
Yes, Notion has a large and active Japanese user community. Japanese freelancers particularly appreciate Notion's ability to combine project management, client records, invoice history, and knowledge management in one tool — reducing the number of separate apps (note apps, task managers, spreadsheets) needed to run a freelance business efficiently.
