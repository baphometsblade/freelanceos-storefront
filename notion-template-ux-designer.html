The Problem

You Design Great Experiences for Clients. What About Your Own Practice?

Freelance UX designers and product designers are often among the most systems-oriented professionals working in any field — yet the practice of running the design business itself is frequently the least organized part of the operation. Research lives separately from projects. Portfolio work never gets prioritized. Client relationships are managed through email memory. The system that serves your clients is excellent. The system for running your own business rarely exists at all.

🎨

Projects tracked in separate Figma files

Research docs in Google Drive, design files in Figma, client feedback in email, invoices in a separate app. No unified view of where each project actually stands across clients, phases, and deadlines. When you have four engagements running simultaneously, the current status of each one lives partially in your email and partially in your head.

🔬

User research findings get lost

Interview notes, usability test recordings, and affinity diagrams from six months ago are buried in folders that nobody can find — including you. Every new project starts research from scratch even when directly relevant findings from past work exist somewhere in your file system. Institutional research memory evaporates between engagements, and the learning from each project doesn't compound the way it should.

📂

Portfolio case studies always "almost ready"

You have strong project work but turning it into portfolio case studies takes a back seat to client deadlines. Your portfolio is always 18 months behind your best work. There is no system that forces a decision about the case study status of every completed project, so excellent work sits invisible to the market while you take on new clients who cannot see your actual capabilities.

💳

Client relationships managed through email

Briefs, feedback threads, contract versions, and invoice follow-ups scattered across email chains for every client. Professional but chaotic. When a client references feedback they gave in a review session three months ago, finding that exact thread requires a specific enough search term to locate the right email in a sea of similarly worded project correspondence.

Design System Component Library Index — Component, Status, Figma Link & Dev Handoff

A component index tracking every element of every design system you are building or maintaining across client projects — component name, component type following atomic design principles (atom, molecule, organism), current status (Draft, Review, Approved, Deprecated), a direct link to the Figma component for immediate access, documentation status indicating whether the component has written usage guidelines, dev handoff status showing whether engineering has received and acknowledged the specifications, and the date last updated so you can identify components that may have drifted from current design standards. For freelance designers managing design systems across multiple client projects simultaneously — a situation that most experienced UX designers find themselves in — this index provides oversight that is otherwise very difficult to maintain when each design system lives in its own Figma file with no meta-view across them.
