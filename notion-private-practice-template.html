Common questions

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about using Notion for private practice administration — and exactly where the boundaries are.

Is Notion HIPAA compliant?
No. Notion does not sign Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) and is not HIPAA-compliant. You must never store Protected Health Information in Notion — no client names linked to clinical data, no session notes, no diagnoses, no treatment plans, no intake forms, no billing records tied to individual clients. All clinical records must remain in a HIPAA-compliant EHR such as SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, Jane App, TheraNest, or comparable platforms. This template is built entirely for the non-clinical business administration side of your practice, and every module is designed with that boundary in mind.
What CAN I track in Notion for my private practice?
You can safely use Notion for any non-clinical, non-identifiable business information. This includes: continuing education hours, certificate links, and license renewal deadlines; referral source contacts (professionals and organizations — not clients); marketing calendar, content drafts, and online presence management; aggregated session volume and revenue trends (not per-client records); professional development goals and training plans; general intake inquiry volume by source (no identifiable prospect information); business expense logs for tax preparation; and practice growth strategy and goal planning.
How do I track CE hours in Notion?
FreelanceOS Pro's CE & License Tracker has a database entry for each training you complete. You log the training name, provider, date, hours earned, CE category (ethics, cultural competence, modality-specific, etc.), and a link to your certificate. Rollup formulas automatically sum your total hours by category so you can see at a glance how you're tracking against state board requirements. Each license gets its own renewal date entry with a calculated countdown. When renewal time comes, your full log is already organized — no frantic folder-diving required.
Can I use this alongside SimplePractice or TherapyNotes?
Yes — and that is exactly how this template is designed to be used. Your EHR handles all clinical records, session notes, intake forms, insurance billing, and client communication. FreelanceOS Pro handles the business layer that EHRs typically don't cover well: CE tracking with certificate storage, referral source relationship management, marketing and content planning, aggregated revenue trends, professional development goals, and practice growth strategy. The two systems are complementary, not competing — they cover completely different domains.
How does the referral source CRM work without tracking clients?
The Referral Source CRM tracks the professionals and organizations who refer clients to you — not the clients themselves. Each entry represents a referral source: a PCP practice, a school counselor, a colleague therapist, an EAP provider. You store their contact information, your last outreach date, the nature of your professional relationship, and general notes about referral volume (e.g., "sends 2–3 referrals per quarter" — not specific client information). This lets you manage referral relationships strategically without ever creating a connection between identifiable client data and your Notion workspace.
I'm a solo LCSW just starting my practice. Is this too much?
FreelanceOS Pro is well-suited for solo practitioners at any stage — including those just starting out. The template is modular, so you start with what's immediately useful (probably the CE tracker, the operations dashboard, and the referral source CRM) and expand into other modules as your practice grows. Early-stage solo practitioners often find the business goal planner and the practice building tools especially valuable — they help you build systematically rather than reactively. You don't need to use every module on day one.
