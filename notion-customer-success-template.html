Red / Yellow / Green Status Logic

Red (0–49): Immediate intervention required. Trigger the escalation playbook, schedule an urgent call, and flag to your CS manager within 24 hours.

Yellow (50–69): Monitor closely. Identify the lowest-scoring signal category and address it before the next QBR. Put on your weekly review list.

Green (70–100): On track. Maintain regular cadence, look for expansion signals, and prepare a strong renewal narrative.

The QBR Workflow: From Prep to Follow-Up

The QBR template turns a 4-hour prep process into a 45-minute fill-in-the-blanks exercise. Here's the full workflow, from pulling data 2 weeks out to logging action items after the meeting.

1

T-14 Days: Pull Data & Open the Template

Duplicate the QBR template for the account. Pull current metrics from your product analytics tool, support platform, and NPS survey into the metrics section. This takes 20 minutes with everything in one Notion workspace.

2

T-10 Days: Build the Narrative

Using the Accomplishments section of the template, pull wins from your Voice of Customer notes and Goal Tracker. The QBR template has prompts to help you frame accomplishments in terms of the customer's original goals, not just product features used.

3

T-7 Days: Identify Risks & Build Asks

Review the health scorecard and escalation log for this account. Document current risks and challenges honestly — it builds trust when you name them before the customer does. Prepare 2–3 clear asks for the coming quarter (resources, executive access, product feedback).

4

T-3 Days: Internal Alignment

Share the Notion page with your AE, Solutions Engineer, and CS Manager. Use the comments to collect their input. Send the agenda to the customer contact and confirm attendees. The template has a dedicated attendees section to track who confirmed on both sides.

5

Day-of: Run the QBR

Use the Notion page as your live agenda. The template has a running notes section for capturing what the customer says in real time — customer quotes, concerns raised, decisions made. This is your source of truth if there's ever a dispute about what was agreed.

6

T+24 Hours: Capture Action Items & Next Steps

Fill in the Action Items table in the template: each action, who owns it (customer or vendor), and due date. Send the completed QBR page as the follow-up email attachment. Every action item links to your task manager for follow-through tracking.

Churn Prevention: Building Early Warning Systems in Notion

The best churn prevention is invisible to the customer — it's the proactive action you took 60 days ago when health started slipping. The template operationalizes early warning signals so prevention happens at the system level, not just when you remember to check.

Early Warning Signals to Track

The health scorecard is designed to surface these signals before they become problems: product login frequency drops below 3x/week, a second support ticket escalation opens within 30 days, NPS drops below 30, the executive sponsor leaves the company, or a success goal is flagged as off-track two quarters in a row.

When Health Drops to Yellow

Schedule a mid-quarter touchpoint within 5 business days. Identify the lowest-scoring signal and address it directly. Document your observation and planned action in the escalation log. Review at your next 1:1 with your CS manager.

Escalation Playbook (Red Accounts)

Flag in escalation log same day. Schedule executive call within 72 hours with your VP CS. Build a Save Plan documenting root cause, actions taken, and success criteria for returning to green. Get AE aligned on account strategy.

Save Playbook

The save playbook template documents the recovery plan: what went wrong, what we're committing to fix, the timeline for improvement, and the metrics that define "saved." Tracked in the escalation log until closed.

Executive Escalation Template

When you need to escalate to the customer's C-suite, the template provides a structure: business impact statement, proposed solution, executive ask, and success definition. Built to be sent directly from your Notion workspace.
