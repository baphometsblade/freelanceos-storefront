Frequently Asked Questions

Is this Notion template built specifically for corporate trainers and L&D professionals?
Yes — every database is designed around the corporate trainer's workflow: managing a program library across multiple clients, tracking session deliveries with facilitator debrief notes, maintaining a participant and cohort database, logging learning outcomes and NPS scores, and running the business operations of a freelance training practice or in-house L&D function. It works for leadership development, skills training, compliance, onboarding, sales enablement, DEI programming, and keynote workshop facilitation.
Can I manage training programs for multiple corporate clients at once?
Yes. The Training Program Library and Delivery Tracker both include a client field so you can maintain programs and delivery records across multiple corporate accounts simultaneously. Filter by client to see all active programs, upcoming delivery dates, NPS scores, and outstanding invoices for a single account without losing context across the others.
How does the learning outcomes and ROI log work?
The Learning Outcomes and ROI Log holds one entry per program delivery with learning objectives met percentage, participant satisfaction NPS, manager-reported behavior change if the client provides follow-up data, business outcome tied to the training, client testimonial, and case study potential flag. Over time this builds the evidence portfolio you reference in proposals to demonstrate that your training produces measurable results — not just high NPS scores.
Can I track content development alongside delivery?
Yes. The Content Development Tracker holds one entry per module or asset — facilitator guide, participant workbook, slide deck, video, e-learning module, job aid — with status from drafting through approved and versioned, last updated date, and version number. When you update a module after a regulation change or client feedback, you version it and know exactly what each client received at each delivery — critical for compliance training where currency of content matters.
