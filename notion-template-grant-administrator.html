Frequently Asked Questions

Is this Notion template built specifically for grant administrators and research administrators?
Yes — every database is designed around the grants administrator's workflow: tracking active award compliance across multiple funders, managing reporting calendars with fiscal and programmatic deadlines, monitoring budget utilization against allowable cost categories, overseeing subrecipient monitoring requirements, and running the business operations of a freelance or contract research administration practice. It's distinct from grant writing templates — this is the administrative post-award compliance side of sponsored programs management.
How does this differ from a grant writer's template?
Grant writers focus on proposal development and funder relationships. Grant administrators focus on post-award compliance: ensuring expenditures are allowable, allocable, and reasonable under Uniform Guidance (2 CFR Part 200) or funder terms and conditions; monitoring subrecipients; submitting financial and programmatic reports on time; managing no-cost extensions; and closing out awards. This template is built for the administrative and compliance side — not the proposal writing side.
Can I manage awards from multiple funders for multiple client organizations?
Yes. The Award Compliance Tracker and Reporting Calendar both include client organization and funder fields, so you can manage awards from NIH, NSF, USDA, foundations, and other funders across multiple client organizations simultaneously. Filter by client, funder, or deadline type to see exactly where your compliance attention is needed without switching tools.
Does this help with Uniform Guidance (2 CFR Part 200) compliance?
The template includes budget monitoring by cost category, subrecipient monitoring checklists aligned with 2 CFR 200.331-200.332 requirements, audit readiness notes, and cost share tracking. It's a business management tool to organize your compliance work — your official documentation and federal financial reports remain in your client's grants management system (eRA Commons, Grants.gov, SAM.gov, or institutional system).
