Per-project revision tracking: client feedback (with timecode references where applicable), round number, what was changed, approval status, hours spent per round. How do motion designers use Notion to manage projects?

Motion designers use Notion to manage the full project lifecycle — from initial brief through concept, storyboard, animation, revision rounds, and final delivery — in a single connected workspace. A project tracker gives you a master database of all active and past projects with the motion type, deliverable format, status, deadline, revision count, and invoice status in one record. Each project links to the client CRM, storyboard library, revision log, and delivery checklist. The board view gives you a visual pipeline of all active projects across every stage — making bottlenecks visible before they become deadline crises, and giving you a clear answer to "what do I need to finish this week" without opening a single file.

Can Notion help motion designers track client revisions?

Yes — revision tracking is one of the highest-value things a freelance motion designer can implement in Notion. The revision log in FreelanceOS Pro captures each round with the round number, date feedback received, feedback channel, detailed notes on requested changes, version file name, and delivery date. Tracking the round count against your contracted revision allowance lets you identify when a project exceeds scope and a change order is warranted. Tracking revision hours per round also reveals which project types or clients consistently require more revision work than the contract covers — the data you need to price future similar projects more accurately and stop absorbing unpaid extra rounds.

How do motion designers organize storyboards and concepts in Notion?

The storyboard and concept library in FreelanceOS Pro stores per-project creative documentation: storyboard frame descriptions, concept notes, art direction references, color palette and typography specs, music and SFX references, and client feedback on concepts before animation begins. Keeping concept approvals in Notion rather than email means you have the record when revision feedback conflicts with what was previously agreed. Over time the concept library becomes a searchable archive of approved creative directions across all past projects — searchable by animation style, client industry, color approach, or mood — that speeds up new project concepting significantly by letting you reference what worked and why.

What is the best Notion setup for tracking software and plugin licenses as a motion designer?

The software and plugin license tracker in FreelanceOS Pro logs every subscription in your motion design stack — After Effects, Cinema 4D, Blender, DaVinci Resolve, RedGiant Universe, Motion Bro, Lottie plugins, and cloud services — with tool name, license type, annual cost, renewal date, and whether it is actively in use. Motion designers accumulate significant plugin stacks that auto-renew without notice. A quarterly review of this tracker identifies licenses you have outgrown, tools replaced by newer alternatives, or subscriptions that have stopped being used but continue billing. It also gives you a complete professional software expense record for tax deductions — no reconstructing from bank statements at year end.
