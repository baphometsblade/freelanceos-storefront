The System

Three Steps. One Sunday Session. Done.

Most meal planning systems fail because they create more work. This one is engineered to reduce it — built around how freelancers actually operate.

1

Add Your Recipes

Populate your recipe database by pasting in links, entering details manually, or importing from any recipe site. Each recipe is a rich Notion page with all the info you need — ingredient list, prep time, macros, and your personal notes.

Takes about 2–3 minutes per recipe. Build your library over time — you only need 10–15 go-to recipes to get full value from the system.
2

Plan the Week

Every Sunday, open the weekly meal planner and drag your recipes into each day. The system shows total prep time so you can balance convenience meals against more involved cooking. Takes 10–15 minutes for a full week.

If you're short on time, use Quick Plan to auto-fill the week from your Favorites Collection. One click, whole week sorted.
3

Generate Your Grocery List

Once your week is planned, your grocery list is ready. Ingredients are aggregated and organized by store section, with quantities consolidated across multiple recipes. Grab your phone, go to the store, and check items off as you go.

No more standing in the produce aisle wondering if you already have garlic at home. One list. One trip. Done.
