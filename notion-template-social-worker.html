Organized Professional Life Without Compromising Privacy

CEU Tracking That's Actually Useful

The CEU Tracker is a database where each row represents one training or continuing education event. Properties include: Training Name, Provider, Credit Hours (number), Category (select from: Clinical Practice, Ethics, Diversity and Cultural Competency, Child Welfare, Supervision, Law and Regulations, Mental Health, Substance Use, or any custom category), Completion Date, Certificate Number, and an Expiration flag for time-sensitive trainings.

A rollup at the top of your renewal dashboard shows total credit hours earned toward your current renewal cycle. Filter by category to see if you have the required ethics hours or the minimum hours in specific content areas that your state board mandates. Color-coded alert properties flag when your renewal deadline is approaching: green if you're on track with more than 90 days remaining, yellow at 60 days, red at 30 days.

No more scrambling through email in the weeks before your renewal deadline. No more wondering whether you have enough ethics hours. The tracker shows you exactly where you stand on every requirement at every point in your renewal cycle — the kind of clarity that turns a stressful biennial task into a routine professional habit.

Supervision That Supports Your Growth

The Supervision Log is built for social workers at every stage — from field placement students logging required hours to licensed clinicians maintaining their own supervision practice. Each entry includes: Date, Supervisor Name, Session Duration, Topics Covered (multi-select from common clinical and professional topics), Goals Set, Action Items Assigned, and a Reflections page for longer-form processing of what was discussed.

A Timeline view shows your supervision frequency over time — invaluable for ensuring you meet minimum supervision hours required by your board, and for demonstrating consistent reflective practice in your own professional documentation. A rollup calculates total supervised hours logged, which you can filter by supervisor or by time period for licensure applications.

When you come prepared to supervision with documented topics and action items from the previous session, your supervisor notices. The structured approach signals professional seriousness and gets more out of the time. Many social workers find that the act of writing down what they want to bring to supervision helps them arrive knowing what they actually need — not just whatever happened to be on their mind that morning.

Fight Burnout With Systems, Not Willpower

The Self-Care Tracker That Doesn't Judge You

The Self-Care section begins with a weekly check-in template. Each check-in records: Stress Level (1-10 scale), Sleep Quality (1-10), Physical Activity this week (Yes/Partially/No), Meaningful Social Connection (Yes/No), Professional Fulfillment (1-10), and a free-text field for anything you want to note about the week. It takes under 3 minutes to complete.

The power is in the accumulation. After 8-10 weeks of weekly check-ins, the trend view shows you patterns that are genuinely difficult to see in real time: the two-week stretch where stress was consistently 8-9, the correlation between lower sleep scores and higher stress, the weeks where professional fulfillment dropped sharply. This data doesn't make your decisions for you, but it gives you actual information about your own state rather than a vague sense that "things have been hard lately."

Social workers are trained to help others recognize warning signs and develop protective factors. This template applies the same logic inward — systematic, non-judgmental data collection that supports informed self-care decisions. The self-care commitment section at the bottom of each check-in is one specific, concrete thing you commit to doing before the next check-in. Small and specific beats large and vague every time.

Resource Directory Without PHI

A searchable resource directory is one of the most practical tools a social worker can build — and one of the most time-consuming to maintain in a spreadsheet. The Resource Directory database holds one entry per organization or resource, with properties for: Resource Name, Category (Housing, Food, Mental Health, Substance Use, Crisis, Transportation, Legal Aid, Childcare, Employment, Medical), Geographic Area, Phone Number, Website URL, Hours of Operation, Eligibility Notes (general), and a Personal Notes field for your direct experience with this resource.

Because none of this information is client-specific — it's agency and program information, not individual client data — it's safe to keep in Notion without PHI concerns. You can share this resource directory with your team via a Notion share link, turning your curated knowledge into a team asset. Filter by category or area for quick referral lookups during the workday without digging through old emails or your memory.

Supporting Your Career, Not Just Your Caseload

Licensure Roadmap for Every Stage

The Career Goals section includes a Licensure Roadmap tracker that works for social workers at every stage: from MSW program students tracking field placement hours, to Associates tracking supervised hours toward full licensure, to licensed clinicians planning toward advanced credentials or ACSW membership.

Track your required supervised hours with a running total, log your exam preparation materials and target exam date, set renewal calendar reminders for each credential you hold, and document your professional development goals for the next 1, 3, and 5 years. The career map makes the path from where you are to where you want to be visible — and visible goals are far more likely to be reached than mental ones.

Privacy reminder: This template is designed with social work ethics in mind. Every database was built around professional development, career management, and personal productivity — not client work. When in doubt about whether a piece of information is appropriate to log here, err on the side of caution and use your organization's approved systems.

Can I store client case notes in this template?

This template is for your professional organization, CEU tracking, and personal productivity only. What CEU categories can I track?
The Category property is fully customizable. Common categories include: clinical practice, ethics, diversity and cultural competency, supervision, law and regulations, child welfare, aging, mental health, substance use treatment, trauma-informed care, and crisis intervention. Add, remove, or rename categories to match exactly what your state board requires for renewal.
Is this suitable for all social work licensure types?
Yes — LCSW, LMSW, LICSW, CSW, ASW, and state-specific license types. The licensure roadmap section and CEU tracker are flexible enough to accommodate any state board's requirements. Customize the license type field, credit hour requirements, and renewal cycle length to match your specific state and license level.
Does Notion have a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement?
Notion does not offer a standard BAA for consumer or team accounts. Enterprise plans may have BAA options available — contact Notion directly if you need to pursue that. For all PHI and client records, use only systems where your organization has a signed BAA in place. This template is explicitly designed to avoid any scenario where PHI would be at risk.
Can I share my resource directory with colleagues?
Yes — because the resource directory contains no client information (only agency names, phone numbers, addresses, and general eligibility notes), it's safe to share. You can share the resource directory page with colleagues via a view-only Notion link, or copy specific resources to a shared team workspace. It becomes even more valuable as a shared team asset.
Is this template useful for self-care and preventing burnout?
Yes — the weekly self-care check-in and trend tracking templates are designed specifically to surface burnout warning signs before they become crises. The trend view in the self-care database shows patterns in your stress, sleep, and fulfillment scores over time that are genuinely difficult to notice in real time. It's not a clinical tool, but it is a practical self-monitoring system built for sustainability in a demanding field.
