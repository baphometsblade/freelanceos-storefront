How to Build a Reusable
Lesson Plan in Notion

Each lesson plan in the database follows a consistent structure. Once it's built, cloning it for a different period, class, or year takes 30 seconds.

1

Write the Objective (SWBAT Format)

Start with a measurable student objective. SWBAT (Students Will Be Able To) forces you to name the observable behavior — not just the topic. If you can't write a clear SWBAT, the lesson isn't ready to teach.

SWBAT identify the three branches of US government and explain one power each branch holds.
2

List Materials and Preparation

Copies needed, tech setup, manipulatives, room arrangement. Anything that requires action before the bell rings. Logged here so next time you teach this lesson, setup takes 5 minutes instead of 20.

3

Build the Procedure: Hook → Instruction → Practice → Closure

Four timed sections with notes on what you'll say, show, or do. The hook section has a prompt: "Why will students care about this today?" — answering it before class almost always improves engagement.

4

Add Differentiation Notes

A dedicated field for scaffolds, extensions, and accommodations. Tier A students get extensions; tier B get graphic organizers; students with IEP flags get their specific accommodations pulled from the Student Notes module automatically.

5

Tag Standard and Link to Unit

Tag the lesson to your standards database (Common Core, NGSS, or custom). Link it to the parent unit in the Unit Planner. Now it appears in both your lesson database and your curriculum map — no double entry.

6

Fill the Reflection Field After Teaching

One text field: what worked, what didn't, and what to change next time. Takes 3 minutes after class. The next time you teach this lesson — or adapt it for a different group — that reflection is the most valuable planning asset you have.

Cloning across classes and periods: Once a lesson is complete in the database, you duplicate the page for each additional period or section. Swap in the period-specific notes (different seating arrangements, pacing differences, behavioral considerations) while keeping the core plan intact. At the end of the year you have a complete lesson archive — not a graveyard of Google Docs — and planning next year's version takes minutes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything teachers ask before getting started.

Can I use Notion to create reusable lesson plan templates?

Yes — Notion's database and template system is purpose-built for this. You create a master lesson plan template with all your standard sections — objective, materials, hook, instruction, practice, closure, reflection — and then use Notion's built-in "New from template" button to generate a new plan with all those fields pre-populated. Fill in the specific content, tag the standard, link to the unit, and you're done.

The real power is the database view. After a year of teaching, you have 100+ lesson plans, all searchable and filterable by subject, grade, standard, or unit. Planning a unit you've taught before takes 20 minutes instead of two hours because you're adapting existing plans, not starting from scratch.

How do I organize lesson plans by subject and grade in Notion?

The FreelanceOS lesson plan template uses a Notion database with dedicated properties for Subject (select), Grade Level (select), Unit (relation), Standard (multi-select), and Lesson Type (lecture, lab, project, review). You can create saved views for each subject-grade combination — so a middle school science teacher gets one click to "7th grade science" and sees only those plans, sorted by unit and date.

If you teach multiple subjects or grade levels, you can also group lessons by Subject or Grade in gallery or board view — turning your lesson database into a visual curriculum library organized exactly the way your brain works. See the Notion teacher template hub for more on setting up a multi-subject workspace.

Does the Notion lesson plan template work for standards-based teaching?

Standards alignment is a first-class feature of the template, not an afterthought. Every lesson has a Standards property where you tag Common Core ELA, Common Core Math, NGSS, or any custom standard you add. Over time, you build a fully tagged curriculum library where you can filter by standard and see exactly how many lessons address it, when they're taught, and at what depth.

For curriculum reviews, observations, or accreditation, you can share a filtered view showing all lessons for a given standard — with dates, units, and reflection notes — in under a minute. No spreadsheet, no separate tracking system, no manual compilation.

Can I use this Notion template to track student grades and assessments?

Module 3 of FreelanceOS Pro is a dedicated Grade and Assessment Tracker. You log assessment scores by student, assignment name, assignment type (quiz, test, project, formative), and unit. The tracker shows each student's running average and lets you filter to see struggling students — those below a threshold you set — before the grading period closes.

Student records link to the Student Notes module, so when you're looking at a student's grades and see a pattern, you can jump directly to their profile to check accommodations, IEP flags, or parent communication history. It's not just a gradebook — it's a connected student management system.

Is this template suitable for college and university instructors, not just K-12?

The system adapts cleanly to higher education: Grade Level becomes course section, state standards become learning outcomes or accreditation criteria, and the Unit Planner becomes a week-by-week syllabus. And the Student Notes module — used in K-12 for IEP flags — becomes a place to track office hours conversations and student academic concerns in a higher-ed context.

How does the substitute teacher folder work in Notion?

The Sub Folder is a dedicated section of your Notion workspace that you maintain on an ongoing basis — updating it whenever your schedule or procedures change. It contains everything a substitute needs: your daily schedule, class-by-class seating charts, behavioral notes for specific students, emergency procedures, and a library of ready-to-go lesson plans for each class that work without any prep from the sub.

You share it as a read-only Notion public link, which you can email or text to a sub in under 30 seconds. The sub opens it on their phone or laptop — no Notion account required.
