How to Set Up Payhip

8 steps to a fully live Payhip store. Follow in order — takes under an hour.

1

Create your account

Go to payhip.com and sign up with your email. Choose the free plan to start — Payhip charges a 5% transaction fee on the free plan, dropping to 2% on Plus ($29/mo) and 0% on Pro ($99/mo). For launch, free is fine.

2

Connect your payment method

Go to Settings → Payments. Connect your PayPal and/or Stripe account. You need at least one connected before you can publish paid products. Payhip supports both simultaneously.

3

Set up your store page

Go to Store Settings. Add your store name (FreelanceOS), a short bio, and your logo. Set the store URL to something clean — payhip.com/freelanceos or similar. This is your public storefront URL.

4

Add FreelanceOS Lite first (free)

Click Add Product → Digital Download. Upload your .zip file, paste the product name and description from this kit, set the price to $0. Free products build your list and create upsell opportunities. Publish this first.

5

Add your paid products

Repeat for each paid product: upload the product file (.zip), paste the name, tagline, and full description from this kit, set the correct price, add your 10 tags. Upload the cover image at 1280×720px for each.

6

Configure post-purchase delivery

Under each product, set the "Thank you" message and confirmation email. Mention the Notion duplication instructions: "Click the link, then click Duplicate in the top-right corner to add it to your Notion workspace." This reduces support requests significantly.

7

Enable affiliates (optional)

Go to Marketing → Affiliates in your Payhip dashboard. Enable the affiliate program, set the commission rate to 30%, and set the cookie duration to 60 days. This matches your existing Gumroad affiliate program and allows Payhip affiliates to promote your products too.

8

Cross-link to your storefront

Add Payhip product links to your GitHub Pages storefront as alternative purchase options. Some buyers prefer Payhip over Gumroad for regional payment reasons. More purchase options = higher conversion rate overall.
