Why Nurses Need This

You're Organized at Work.
Your Career Deserves the Same.

Nurses are meticulous professionals — but most have no system for managing their own career logistics. These four problems sound familiar:

📆

Losing Track of Continuing Education Deadlines

Your license renewal window opens, and you realize you've only completed 8 of 24 required CE hours — with three months to go. You scramble to find accredited courses, paying premium prices for last-minute access. This happens to most nurses who don't have a structured CE tracking system. A dedicated tracker with cycle dates, logged hours, and expiration alerts changes everything.

🔄

No System for Shift Swaps and Schedule Management

Tracking your personal schedule across multiple communication channels — unit group chats, paper request forms, posted schedules — creates confusion and missed swaps. You need a personal shift calendar that's yours: logging your actual hours, tracking overtime, recording swap requests you've made, and keeping a clear view of your schedule regardless of how chaotic the unit board gets.

📓

Career Goals Scattered Across Notebooks and Notes Apps

Your five-year specialty goal is in a notebook from 2022. Your skills development list is in Apple Notes. Your professional accomplishments for your annual review are nowhere to be found. Without a single career home base, it's impossible to make consistent progress toward your professional goals, negotiate raises with confidence, or build a clear picture of your clinical career trajectory.

🪪

Certification Renewal Dates Sneaking Up

ACLS expires in October. Your CCRN was last renewed in 2022. Your Ohio RN license renews every two years — but which two years? Travel nurses holding licenses in multiple compact states face an even more complex web of renewal timelines. Without a centralized license and certification hub with renewal reminders built in, you're always one calendar miss away from a lapsed credential — and the professional consequences that follow.

How It Works

Never Miss a Renewal Deadline Again

The CE and license workflow is the core of the system. Here's how it works for managing your continuing education from first credit to renewal:

1

Set Up Your License Cycle

Duplicate the template and enter your RN license details — state, license number, required CE hours per renewal cycle, cycle start date, and expiration date. Add a row for each license and specialty certification you hold. Set your renewal alert preferences at 90, 60, and 30 days out. The system shows you exactly how many hours you need and how much time you have to get them. This takes about 15 minutes to set up completely, and you only do it once per cycle.

2

Log Your CEs As You Complete Them

Every time you complete a continuing education activity — a webinar, an accredited online course, a conference session, an in-service, or a journal CE — add it to your CE log. Record the course name, provider, date, contact hours earned, and accreditation body. The tracker automatically adds your hours to the correct license cycle and updates your progress bar in real time. No more spreadsheet formulas or mental math — you always know where you stand.

3

Renew With Confidence — On Time, Every Time

When your renewal window opens, your CE tracker shows exactly what you've earned, the complete list of courses for your documentation, and whether you've met your state's specific requirements. No scrambling, no last-minute course buying, no uncertainty about which credits count. Submit your renewal with a full, organized log of everything you've completed — and set up your next cycle in the same session so you start fresh and on track for year two.
