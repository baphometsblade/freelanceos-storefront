FreelanceOS vs Easlo Freelance OS: Which Notion Template Is Right for You? (2026)

Published April 26, 2026  ·  8 min read

If you're searching for a Notion template to run your freelance business, two names keep coming up: FreelanceOS and Easlo's Freelance OS. Both are well-designed, both promise to replace your scattered tools with a single Notion workspace, and both have real users who swear by them.

So which one should you actually buy?

We're obviously biased — we built FreelanceOS — but we're going to give you an honest breakdown. Easlo makes great templates. They've earned their reputation. The question isn't which product is "better" in the abstract; it's which one fits your situation, budget, and workflow.

Feature-by-Feature Breakdown

Client Management

Both templates include a client CRM with contact details, status tracking, and linked records. FreelanceOS connects your client database to projects, invoices, and revenue data using Notion relations and rollups — so clicking into a client shows you everything: active projects, total revenue, outstanding invoices, and communication notes.

Easlo's CRM is similarly structured and includes a deals pipeline, which is useful if you do a lot of outbound prospecting. If most of your work comes through referrals or inbound leads, FreelanceOS's simpler CRM will be more than enough. If you're actively chasing deals, Easlo's pipeline feature has an edge here.

Project & Task Management

Both systems let you create projects, assign them to clients, set deadlines, and track progress through status stages. This is table stakes for any freelance Notion template, and both handle it well.

Easlo additionally separates tasks from projects and includes a more granular task management layer. FreelanceOS keeps projects and tasks in a single tracker, which works better for solo freelancers who don't need the overhead of a multi-level hierarchy. If you manage teams or complex multi-phase projects, Easlo's separation makes more sense.

Invoicing

FreelanceOS includes a full invoice system linked to clients and projects. Track invoice status (Draft, Sent, Paid, Overdue), see outstanding amounts, and your revenue dashboard automatically reflects payment data.

Easlo's Freelance OS includes an invoice tracker as well. Both handle this core freelance need. Neither replaces a dedicated invoicing tool like FreshBooks or Wave for generating PDF invoices — but both give you a centralized view of what's owed and what's been paid.

Revenue Dashboard

This is where FreelanceOS deliberately invests more. The Pro version includes a dedicated revenue dashboard with monthly and quarterly views, income by client, and cash flow tracking. For freelancers who care about understanding their business finances at a glance, this is a meaningful differentiator.

Easlo's template focuses more on OKR tracking and goal-setting. If you're more interested in strategic planning and quarterly objectives, their approach might resonate. But most solo freelancers care more about "How much did I earn this month?" than "Am I hitting my Q2 OKRs."

Weekly Planning

FreelanceOS includes a weekly planner that pulls from your project tracker — a lightweight board view designed for the Monday morning "What do I need to focus on this week?" question. It's simple by design, and it's one of the most-used features based on user feedback.

Easlo's template doesn't include a dedicated weekly planner, though their task management system can serve a similar function with filtered views.

Additional Features in Easlo

To be fair, Easlo's Freelance OS includes meeting notes, OKR tracking, and a deals pipeline. If those are important to your workflow, they add genuine value. Easlo also has a massive catalog of 40+ templates — for $389 (All-Access), you get everything they make.

Pricing: The $50 Difference

Let's talk about the number most people are here for.

  • FreelanceOS Pro: $29 one-time
  • FreelanceOS Lite: Free
  • Easlo Freelance OS: $79 one-time (individual), $199 (team)
  • Easlo All-Access: $389 for 40+ templates

FreelanceOS Pro is 63% less expensive than Easlo's Freelance OS. For a solo freelancer watching every dollar, that $50 difference matters — it's real money that could go toward a month of software, a domain renewal, or simply staying in your pocket.

At $29, FreelanceOS gives you a complete CRM, project tracker, invoice system, revenue dashboard, weekly planner, templates library, and quarterly review framework. At $79, Easlo gives you a CRM, deals pipeline, project and task management, meeting notes, OKRs, and an invoice tracker. The core feature sets overlap significantly.

Try before you buy: FreelanceOS Lite is genuinely free — not a 7-day trial, not a feature-locked teaser. It includes a working project tracker, client list, invoice overview, and weekly planner. Duplicate it, use it for a week, and decide if the system fits your workflow before spending anything.

Who Should Pick FreelanceOS

  • Want to start free — The Lite version lets you test the system with zero risk before deciding whether to upgrade
  • Are a solo freelancer — The entire system is built for one-person businesses. No team features you'll never use
  • Care about revenue visibility — The dedicated revenue dashboard gives you financial clarity most templates skip
  • Prefer simplicity — Five connected databases, clean views, no bloat

Who Should Pick Easlo

  • Need team collaboration — Their $199 team license makes it a better fit for working with contractors or a small team
  • Want a deals pipeline — If outbound sales is core to your business, Easlo's pipeline feature is a genuine advantage
  • Already use other Easlo templates — If you're in the Easlo ecosystem, their All-Access plan makes the math work
  • Want OKR tracking — If you run your freelance business with quarterly objectives and key results, Easlo has this built in
  • Prefer a broader ecosystem — Easlo's 40+ templates cover everything from personal CRMs to habit trackers

The Verdict

Our honest take

Both FreelanceOS and Easlo Freelance OS are capable templates built by people who understand Notion. You won't go wrong with either one.

For solo freelancers who want a focused, affordable system — one that prioritizes revenue tracking, weekly planning, and connected client data — FreelanceOS offers better value at $29 (or free for Lite). You get the features that matter most to a one-person business, without paying for extras you don't need.

If you need team features, a deals pipeline, or you're already invested in the Easlo ecosystem, their Freelance OS is a solid product worth considering.

Either way, stop managing your freelance business across five different apps. Pick one system and commit to it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is FreelanceOS better than Easlo Freelance OS?
It depends on what you need. FreelanceOS is purpose-built for solo freelancers and costs $29 for Pro (with a free Lite tier). Easlo Freelance OS is $79 and part of a larger ecosystem. If you want a focused, affordable freelance system, FreelanceOS is the better value. If you want 40+ templates, Easlo's All-Access plan may suit you.
Does FreelanceOS have a free version?
Yes. FreelanceOS Lite is completely free and includes a project tracker, basic client list, invoice overview, and weekly planner. It's a fully functional system, not a limited trial. Upgrade to Pro ($29) when you want the full CRM, revenue dashboard, and additional modules.
How much does Easlo Freelance OS cost?
Easlo Freelance OS is $79 individual or $199 team. They also offer an All-Access plan at $389 for 40+ Notion templates.
Can I switch from Easlo to FreelanceOS?
Yes. Both run in Notion, so you can use them side by side or migrate gradually. Start with FreelanceOS Lite (free) and move your data over when ready.
