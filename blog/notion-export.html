Notion Export: How to Export Notion Pages, Databases, and Your Full Workspace

Export a single page as PDF or Markdown, export databases as CSV, back up your entire workspace, and understand what Notion's export can and can't do.

July 2026  ·  9 min read
Home › Blog › Notion Export Guide 2026

Exporting from Notion is straightforward once you know where the options live and what each format actually produces. Whether you need a PDF to share with a client, a CSV to analyze your project data, or a full workspace backup, Notion has a path for each. This guide covers all of them — plus the limitations you need to know before you depend on an export for something important.

Why Export from Notion?

There are four main reasons people export from Notion:

  1. 1
    Backup your data
    Notion is cloud-based, which means a lost login, account issue, or workspace deletion could cost you everything. A regular workspace export creates an offline copy of your most critical systems.
  2. 2
    Share outside of Notion
    Not everyone has Notion. When you need to share a proposal, report, or document with a client or collaborator who doesn't use Notion, exporting as PDF gives them a clean, professional version they can open anywhere.
  3. 3
    Migrate to another tool
    If you're moving to Obsidian, Roam Research, Logseq, or another tool that accepts Markdown, Notion's Markdown export is the migration path. For moving databases to Airtable, ClickUp, or Monday, CSV export is the bridge.
  4. 4
    Analyze data elsewhere
    Notion databases hold valuable project and business data, but Notion's analytics capabilities are limited. Export your task database, client database, or financial tracker as CSV and analyze it in Google Sheets, Excel, or a BI tool.

How to Export a Single Notion Page

Exporting a single page is the most common export task. Here is the step-by-step process:

  1. Open the Notion page you want to export.
  2. Click the three-dot menu (…) in the upper right corner of the page.
  3. Select Export from the dropdown menu.
  4. Choose your export format: Markdown & CSV, PDF, or HTML.
  5. Choose whether to include subpages (if the page has nested child pages).
  6. Click Export and the file downloads to your computer.

Choosing the Right Export Format

PDF
Best for: sharing with clients, printing
Exports the page as it appears visually in Notion. Maintains layout, images, and formatting. Best for sharing proposals, reports, and docs with people outside Notion.
Markdown & CSV
Best for: migration, developer workflows
Exports page content as a .md file (readable in any text editor or Markdown-based tool). Databases included as CSV. Best for migrating to Obsidian, Logseq, or any Markdown-based system.
HTML
Best for: full formatting preservation offline
Exports as a self-contained HTML file with inline CSS. Opens in any browser, preserves most Notion formatting including colors. Best for archiving or offline access when PDF isn't suitable.
PDF Export Tip
Before exporting as PDF, switch to a simple page layout (remove any full-width toggles or complex columns that may not render cleanly). Use the Page width option in PDF export settings — "Full page" fits the Notion page width, while "Letter" or "A4" uses standard paper dimensions. For client-facing proposals, A4 or Letter typically looks more professional.

Exporting Notion Databases as CSV

Notion databases can be exported as CSV files, which you can then open in any spreadsheet application. This is especially useful for analyzing your project data, migrating to another project management tool, or creating reports outside of Notion.

  1. Open the Notion database you want to export (table view is easiest, but any view works).
  2. Click the three-dot menu (…) at the top right of the database.
  3. Select Export.
  4. Choose Markdown & CSV as the format. Notion will export both the CSV data and any Markdown content inside database records.
  5. You can also choose whether to include subpages within database records.
  6. Click Export and download the ZIP file. Inside you'll find the CSV file for the database rows.

What the CSV Export Contains

The exported CSV includes one row per database record and one column per property. Text properties export as plain text, select/multi-select properties export the selected option names, date properties export as ISO format dates, and checkbox properties export as TRUE/FALSE values. Relation and rollup properties export the linked page titles as text.

Use Case
Export your Notion task or project database to CSV monthly for an offline backup. Open in Google Sheets to calculate project completion rates, average task duration, or revenue by client — analysis that Notion's native dashboards can't easily provide.

How to Export Your Full Workspace

Exporting your entire Notion workspace creates a full backup of everything — every page, database, and nested subpage — in a single ZIP file. This is the most complete backup option but is only available on paid Notion plans.

  1. Click your workspace name in the upper-left sidebar to open the workspace menu.
  2. Navigate to Settings.
  3. Scroll down to the Export content section (you may need to click on "Settings" in the left sidebar within settings).
  4. Click Export all workspace content.
  5. Choose your format: Markdown & CSV is the most portable option for full backups.
  6. Notion will prepare your export. For large workspaces, this can take several minutes. You'll receive a download link or email when ready.
  7. Download and unzip the file. You'll find a folder structure mirroring your Notion sidebar, with pages as Markdown files and databases as CSV files.

Recommended Backup Schedule

For freelancers and small business owners using Notion as their primary work system, a monthly full workspace export is a reasonable minimum. If you have critical client data, project databases, or financial records in Notion, increase to weekly. Store the export ZIP in an external drive or cloud storage (Dropbox, Google Drive) separate from your Notion account.

Exporting Subpages

When exporting a page that has nested sub-pages (child pages), Notion gives you the option to include those subpages in the export. This option appears in the export dialog as "Include subpages" and is checked by default.

  • Include subpages checked: The export creates a folder structure where the main page is a file and each subpage is a file inside a subfolder named after the parent page. The deeper your nesting, the deeper the folder hierarchy in the export.
  • Include subpages unchecked: Only the top-level page content is exported. All links to subpages become dead links in the exported file. Use this only when you want just the content of a single page without its children.

For full workspace exports, all pages and subpages are always included — there's no option to exclude them.

Export Limitations in Notion

Notion's export functionality has several important limitations that can catch people off guard:

No native Word/DOCX export
Notion cannot export directly to .docx format. The closest alternatives are exporting as HTML and opening it in Word, or using a third-party conversion tool. For clients who require Word documents, this is a meaningful friction point.
PDF export doesn't support all block types perfectly
Embeds (YouTube videos, third-party integrations), some database views, and certain block types may not render correctly or at all in PDF export. Complex column layouts can also shift unexpectedly. Always preview the PDF before sending it to a client.
Markdown export loses Notion-specific formatting
Text colors, background colors, callout block styling, and some Notion-specific elements don't have Markdown equivalents. Callouts export as blockquotes, colors are stripped, and toggles export as collapsible HTML (not all Markdown readers support this). The structure is preserved, but the visual styling is not.
Full workspace export requires a paid plan
The "Export all workspace content" option in Settings is only available on Notion's paid plans (Plus, Business, or Enterprise). Free plan users can export individual pages but cannot do a one-click full workspace backup.
Linked databases export separately
When you use a linked database view (a view of a database that lives on a different page), the export captures the view page but the database data exports from its original location — not the linked view. This means you may need to export both the view page and the source database to get the full picture.

Alternative: Third-Party Export and Sync Tools

For use cases that Notion's native export doesn't cover well, these third-party tools fill the gaps:

Notion2Sheets
Best for: live CSV sync
Syncs a Notion database to Google Sheets in real time. Better than manual CSV export for ongoing analysis — changes in Notion appear in Sheets automatically.
Whalesync
Best for: bidirectional sync
Bidirectional sync between Notion and Airtable, Google Sheets, and other tools. Changes in either direction sync both ways — useful for teams that use multiple platforms.
Super.so / Potion
Best for: publishing as website
Publish your Notion pages as a proper website with custom domain, SEO, and fast loading. Not a traditional export, but a way to make Notion content publicly accessible in a professional format.
Pandoc
Best for: DOCX conversion
Free command-line tool that converts Markdown to DOCX (Word), HTML, PDF, and dozens of other formats. Export from Notion as Markdown, then run Pandoc to convert to Word. Preserves structure well.
Zapier / Make
Best for: automated exports
Automate data flow from Notion to other apps. Trigger on database changes, send data to Google Sheets, create documents in Google Docs, or notify via Slack — without manual exports.
Notion API
Best for: custom integrations
Notion's official API lets developers read and write Notion data programmatically. Build custom export scripts, sync pipelines, or integrations that Notion's native export doesn't support.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I export a Notion page as a PDF?
To export a Notion page as PDF: open the page, click the three-dot menu (…) in the upper right, select Export, and choose PDF from the format options. Notion will generate the PDF with the current page layout. Note that some block types (embeds, certain databases) may not render perfectly in the PDF. For best results, simplify the page layout before exporting.
Can I export a Notion database to CSV?
Yes. Open your Notion database, click the three-dot menu (…) in the upper right of the database, select Export, and choose Markdown & CSV as the format. Notion exports the database rows as a CSV file alongside Markdown files for any page content within the database records. You can then open the CSV in Excel, Google Sheets, or any spreadsheet tool.
How do I export my entire Notion workspace?
To export your full Notion workspace: go to Settings (click your workspace name in the sidebar), navigate to Settings, then scroll to Export all workspace content. Click Export and Notion will prepare a ZIP file containing all your pages as Markdown files and all databases as CSV files. This is only available on paid Notion plans. The export arrives by email or download link and may take a few minutes for large workspaces.
Can I export Notion to Word (DOCX)?
Notion does not natively export to Word format (.docx). Your options are: export as HTML and open it in Word (formatting will be partially preserved), export as Markdown and convert using Pandoc (a free command-line tool), or use a third-party tool like Makepad or Notion2Word that adds DOCX export capability. For most purposes, copy-pasting from Notion into Word is the fastest workaround for occasional use.
Does Notion export preserve formatting?
It depends on the format. HTML export preserves the most formatting — headings, bold, italic, lists, tables, and most block types translate cleanly. PDF export preserves visual layout well but may not render all block types perfectly. Markdown export preserves structure (headings, lists, code blocks) but loses formatting like text colors, background colors, and some Notion-specific elements like callouts (which become blockquotes). CSV export for databases preserves all property values but not the formatting within page content.
