Notion Dark Mode: Complete Guide to Dark Theme in Notion (2026)

Notion's dark mode is one of the most-requested and most-used features in the app. If you spend hours in Notion each day — writing, planning, managing client projects — switching to dark theme can significantly reduce eye strain, especially in low-light environments. And honestly, Notion looks great in dark mode.

This guide covers exactly how to enable dark mode on every platform, how to make it follow your system setting automatically, the best dark Notion templates, and what third-party tools exist if you want to go even darker. Let's get into it.

1. How to Enable Notion Dark Mode

Notion provides dark mode natively across all platforms. Here's how to enable it on each:

Mac Desktop
  1. Open Notion
  2. Click Settings (bottom-left sidebar)
  3. Click My settings
  4. Under Appearance, select Dark

Keyboard shortcut: Cmd + Shift + L

Windows Desktop
  1. Open Notion
  2. Click Settings (bottom-left sidebar)
  3. Click My settings
  4. Under Appearance, select Dark

Same path as Mac — no Mac-only shortcut on Windows

iOS (iPhone / iPad)
  1. Open the Notion app
  2. Tap your profile icon or the ... (three dots)
  3. Tap Settings
  4. Tap Appearance
  5. Select Dark
Android
  1. Open the Notion app
  2. Tap the ... or sidebar menu
  3. Tap Settings
  4. Tap Appearance
  5. Select Dark

The Fastest Way on Mac: Keyboard Shortcut

⌘ Cmd + Shift + L Instantly toggles between light and dark mode in the Notion Mac app. Works from any page or view — no need to open Settings.

Browser (Notion Web App)

When using Notion in a web browser, the dark mode setting works the same way — go to Settings → My Settings → Appearance → Dark. The setting syncs across all your Notion sessions, so setting it once in the browser also applies to the desktop app and vice versa.

2. System-Wide Dark Mode vs. Notion-Specific Dark Mode

Notion gives you two ways to control dark mode:

SettingWhat It DoesBest For
LightAlways shows Notion in light mode, regardless of your OS settingPeople who want white pages in Notion even with dark OS
DarkAlways shows Notion in dark mode, regardless of OS settingPeople who always want dark Notion
Use system settingNotion automatically follows your Mac/Windows/phone appearance settingPeople who switch between light and dark OS modes

The "Use system setting" option is the most popular for people who already use OS-level dark mode (macOS: System Settings → Appearance → Dark; Windows: Settings → Personalization → Colors → Dark). When you flip your OS to dark at sunset, Notion flips with it automatically.

Pro tip: On Mac, use one-switch apps or Schedule Dark Mode (macOS Sonoma+: System Settings → Appearance → Auto) to have your entire Mac — and Notion — go dark at sunset automatically.

4. Notion Dark Mode on Mobile

Dark mode on the Notion mobile app (iOS and Android) is comprehensive — the entire app goes dark, not just the page content. Here's what changes:

  • Navigation bar: Goes dark gray, matching the overall theme
  • Sidebar / page list: Dark background with white page titles
  • Page content area: Dark background, light text, readable callouts
  • Database views: Board, table, gallery, list all adapt to dark
  • Search modal: Dark background with white search input
  • New page dialog: Dark
  • Property editing panels: Dark

On mobile, dark mode is especially valuable because you often view Notion at night or in dim environments. The dark background significantly reduces screen glow on your face during late-night planning sessions.

Auto-follow on mobile: Set Notion to "Use system setting" on mobile, then use your phone's scheduled or automatic dark mode. On iPhone: Settings → Display & Brightness → Automatic. On Android: Settings → Display → Dark theme → Schedule. Notion will follow along automatically every day.

6. Third-Party Notion Dark Mode Tools

If Notion's built-in dark mode isn't dark enough for you, third-party tools can go further — but they come with real trade-offs.

Notion Enhancer (Desktop App)

Notion Enhancer is the most popular unofficial modification tool for the Notion desktop app. It allows custom CSS themes, custom dark backgrounds (you can make it as dark as OLED black), custom accent colors, font changes, and interface tweaks. It's free and open-source.

The trade-off: Notion Enhancer modifies the Notion app files. Every time Notion updates, the enhancer may break until it's updated too. If you rely on it heavily, expect occasional broken periods after Notion auto-updates.

Notion Boost (Browser Extension)

Notion Boost is a Chrome/Firefox extension with features like focus mode, smaller text, wider pages, and some appearance tweaks. It's less focused on dark mode specifically but adds useful UI improvements alongside appearance options.

Custom CSS via Stylus

For the Notion web app, you can use the Stylus browser extension to inject custom CSS. This lets you override Notion's dark mode colors without modifying any app files. It only affects the browser — not the desktop app.

Use third-party tools with caution: Unofficial tools are unsupported by Notion. They may expose you to security risks (always install from official repos only), break when Notion updates, or cause unexpected behavior. For most freelancers, Notion's built-in dark mode is sufficient. Save third-party tools for power users who know the risks.

7. Notion Dark Mode Limitations

There are a few things that dark mode cannot change:

⚠️ What dark mode does NOT affect:

  • Embedded images and photos: Images display with their original colors — Notion doesn't apply a dark filter or overlay to images
  • Embeds (iframes): Embedded content like Google Docs, Figma, YouTube, or Loom may or may not have dark mode — it depends on the embedded service's own settings, not Notion
  • PDFs: Embedded PDFs display with their original white or light backgrounds
  • Cover photos: Database gallery covers and page cover images display normally
  • Videos: Embedded YouTube or Vimeo videos are not affected
  • External links: When you open an external link from Notion, the website you visit has its own light/dark mode setting

For heavy image-based pages (portfolio pages, mood boards, design references), the contrast between bright images and a dark page background can actually look striking and intentional — similar to how art galleries use dark walls.

No Custom Colors or Gradients in Built-In Dark Mode

Notion's built-in dark mode has one fixed dark theme — you cannot adjust how dark the background is, change the accent color, or pick a different dark palette (like OLED-black vs. charcoal). The background is a medium-dark charcoal, not pure black. If you need OLED black or custom color schemes, you'll need a third-party tool like Notion Enhancer.

Notion AI Sidebar

The Notion AI panel and suggestion sidebar fully respect dark mode. AI responses, inline suggestions, and the AI side panel all display in dark theme when dark mode is enabled.

8. Dark Mode and Notion Template Design

If you're building Notion templates that you'll share with others, it's worth designing with dark mode in mind — because a significant portion of Notion users run in dark mode. Here's what looks best:

  • Cover images: Dark gradient covers (dark navy, charcoal, deep purple) look intentional and beautiful in dark mode. Bright white or pastel covers create jarring contrast.
  • Callout colors: Use the darker callout colors (dark gray, dark blue) for a cohesive look. Bright yellow callouts can feel visually jarring in dark mode.
  • Database views: Gallery and board views with dark-toned cover images look premium. Tables look great because the alternating row shading is still visible.
  • Dividers: Use horizontal rule dividers (--- in Notion) to create visual separation — they're subtle in dark mode but effective.
  • Emoji and icons: Emoji page icons look vivid against the dark background and are a great way to add color accents without adding visual noise.
Design tip: Test your template in both light and dark mode before sharing. A template that looks polished in dark mode may have contrast issues in light mode (or vice versa). The best templates look intentional in both.

FAQ: Notion Dark Mode

Does Notion have dark mode?

Yes. Notion has built-in dark mode available on all platforms — Mac, Windows, iOS, Android, and web browser. Go to Settings → My Settings → Appearance → Dark to enable it, or set it to "Use system setting" to follow your OS automatically.

How do I turn on dark mode in Notion?

On Mac and Windows: open Notion, click the Settings icon (bottom left of the sidebar), go to My Settings, find the Appearance section, and select Dark. On Mac, you can also use the keyboard shortcut Cmd+Shift+L to toggle instantly. On mobile: open Notion, tap the menu or profile icon, go to Settings, then Appearance, and select Dark.

Does Notion dark mode work on mobile?

Yes. Notion dark mode works on both iOS and Android. On mobile, the entire interface goes dark including the navigation bar, sidebar, page content, and all database views. It can also be set to automatically follow your phone's system dark mode setting so it switches automatically at sunset.

Can I make Notion darker?

Notion's built-in dark mode has one darkness level — the background is a medium charcoal, not pure black. If you want a darker background (OLED black or custom), you'll need a third-party tool like Notion Enhancer (desktop app) or custom CSS via Stylus (browser). These are unofficial, unsupported tools that may break with Notion updates.

Does Notion follow system dark mode?

Yes. In Settings → Appearance, choose "Use system setting" and Notion will automatically switch between light and dark mode to match your operating system's appearance setting. This works on Mac, Windows, iOS, and Android — and it syncs across devices because the setting is stored in your Notion account.
