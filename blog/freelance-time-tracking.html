Why Freelancers Should Track Time (Even on Fixed-Price Projects)

Most freelancers who price by the project think time tracking doesn't apply to them. This is a costly mistake.

Tracking time on fixed-price projects reveals your effective hourly rate on each project. If you quoted $2,000 for a "two-week project" and it took you 40 hours, your effective rate is $50/hour. If it took 80 hours, you worked for $25/hour. Without data, you'll keep underquoting the same types of projects and wonder why you're always busy but never profitable.

Time tracking data is also powerful for:

  • Scope creep defence: "We're now at 12 hours on revisions — the original scope included 4. I'd like to discuss a change order."
  • Better future quotes: Historical data shows exactly how long each type of project takes you.
  • Client reporting: For hourly clients, detailed time logs are a trust signal, not just a billing mechanism.
  • Understanding your capacity: Knowing how many hours you actually work per week helps you decide whether to take on another project.
Industry data: Freelancers who track time report underestimating project time by an average of 26%. Over a year of projects, this translates to significant revenue leakage. Time data is your single best tool for fixing pricing.

Top 7 Time Tracking Tools for Freelancers

1. Toggl Track Free Tier Editor's Pick

Price: Free up to 5 users / $9/mo Starter Rating: 4.8 / 5

Toggl Track is the most popular time tracker among freelancers, and for good reason. It combines a beautifully simple interface with powerful reporting, a browser extension that auto-tracks your activity, and apps for web, desktop (Mac/Windows/Linux), iOS, and Android. One-click start/stop timer, project tagging, and client-ready reports that you can export as PDF or CSV.

The free plan is genuinely complete for solo freelancers — unlimited time entries, unlimited projects, and 7 days of detailed reports. You only need to upgrade if you want 90-day+ reports or team features.

Strengths

  • Cleanest UX in the category
  • Excellent browser extension
  • Free plan is fully functional
  • Integrates with 100+ tools

Limitations

  • Reports limited to 7 days on free plan
  • No invoicing integration on free tier

2. Clockify Free Forever

Price: Free forever / $3.99/mo Basic for extras Rating: 4.4 / 5

Clockify is the most generous free time tracker available. Unlimited users, unlimited projects, unlimited time tracking, and full reporting — all free, permanently. It's the right choice if you want Toggl-level functionality without any cost concerns. The interface is slightly more complex than Toggl, but the learning curve is manageable in 20 minutes.

Strengths

  • Completely free — no limits
  • Full reporting on free plan
  • Web, desktop, and mobile apps

Limitations

  • More cluttered UI than Toggl
  • Less polished mobile experience

3. Harvest

Price: $12/mo per user (free trial available) Rating: 4.5 / 5

Harvest's signature feature is time-to-invoice: track your hours, then convert them directly to a client invoice with one click. For hourly freelancers, this workflow alone justifies the price. Harvest also has budget alerts (notify you when you've used 80% of a project budget), integrations with Asana, Trello, Basecamp, and Slack, and detailed profitability reports.

Strengths

  • One-click time-to-invoice workflow
  • Budget tracking and alerts
  • Best integrations in the category

Limitations

  • $12/mo is steep for solo freelancers
  • No truly free plan (2 projects limit)

4. Timely

Price: From $9/mo Solo Rating: 4.3 / 5

Timely takes a different approach: instead of manual timers, it uses AI to automatically track what you're working on (apps, websites, documents, meetings) and presents it as a draft timeline you review and approve each day. The result is near-perfect time tracking with minimal friction. Excellent for freelancers who forget to hit the timer. The AI is surprisingly accurate after 2–3 weeks of training.

Strengths

  • Automatic AI-powered time capture
  • No manual timers needed
  • Beautiful timeline interface

Limitations

  • Higher price than manual trackers
  • AI needs 2–3 weeks to calibrate

5. RescueTime

Price: From $6.50/mo Rating: 4.2 / 5

RescueTime is less about billing and more about productivity awareness. It runs silently in the background and categorises your computer activity (productive vs. distracting) without any input from you. Weekly reports show you your true working patterns. Less useful for client invoicing, more useful for understanding whether you're actually working the hours you think you are.

Price: Free with Notion free plan Rating: 4.1 / 5

7. FreshBooks (Time Tracking)

Price: From $17/mo (included with FreshBooks) Rating: 4.4 / 5

FreshBooks includes a time tracker built directly into its invoicing platform. If you already use FreshBooks for invoicing, the time tracker is a no-brainer add-on — track time on a project, then include those hours on the next invoice with one click. Not the best standalone time tracker, but excellent as part of the FreshBooks workflow.

How to Set Up a Simple Time Tracking System in Notion

4-Step Notion Time Log Setup

1

Create a Time Log database. New page → Database (table view). Columns: Project (relation or text), Date, Duration (number, hours), Task Description (text), Client (text), Billable (checkbox).

2

Create a Projects database and link it. Change the Project column to a Relation pointing to your Projects database. Now every time entry is linked to its project for rollups.

3

Add a Rollup on your Projects page. In your Projects database, add a Rollup column that sums the Duration from your Time Log. Now every project shows total hours tracked instantly.

4

Create a filtered view for "This Month." Filter by Date → is within → this month. This shows your monthly time log at a glance — useful for billing and capacity planning.

This simple 4-step setup handles 90% of freelance time tracking needs inside Notion. FreelanceOS Pro includes this system pre-built with additional features like an effective hourly rate calculator and monthly time summaries.

Time Tracking Best Practices

Track in Real Time, Not Retrospectively

Retrospective time logging (filling in what you did at end of day) is consistently 25–40% less accurate than real-time tracking. The brain simply doesn't remember how long tasks actually took. Start your timer before you begin a task. Stop it when you switch. This takes 3 seconds and dramatically improves your data quality.

Use Descriptive Task Names

Instead of "Client work" for 3 hours, log "Homepage design — wireframe to hi-fi" or "Client email and revision review — Project X." Descriptive names make your weekly reports useful and give you data to quote future projects accurately.

Track Non-Billable Time Too

Admin, invoicing, proposals, calls, business development — track all of it, even if it's not billable. This reveals what percentage of your working week is actually client-facing. Many freelancers discover that 30–40% of their week is admin they hadn't accounted for in their rate calculations.

Review Weekly

Spend 10 minutes every Friday reviewing your week's time log. Are projects running over estimate? Are certain clients absorbing disproportionate time? Is your effective hourly rate healthy? Weekly reviews take 10 minutes and prevent small inefficiencies from compounding into profitability problems.

Set Time Budgets per Project

Before starting any project, estimate total hours and enter it as a budget in your time tracking tool (or Notion). Get a visual alert when you hit 75% of budget. This is your early warning system for scope creep and underestimation — you can have a conversation with the client before you've already done the extra work for free.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Should I track time if I charge fixed-price project fees?

Yes — emphatically. Fixed-price freelancers who track time consistently improve their pricing accuracy over time. Your time data reveals your true effective hourly rate on each project type, shows you which clients are most profitable, and gives you the evidence needed to raise rates. Start tracking even if you never send a time-based invoice — the data is for you, not just for billing.

2. What's the best free time tracking tool for freelancers?

Toggl Track for the best user experience (free up to 5 users, 7-day reports), or Clockify if you want completely unlimited features free forever. Both are excellent. Try Toggl first — if you hit the report limits and don't want to upgrade, switch to Clockify.

3. How do I share time reports with clients?

Most time tracking tools (Toggl, Harvest, Clockify) allow you to export time reports as PDF or CSV, or generate a shareable link. Export a weekly or project-based summary, filter by client name, and attach it to your invoice. For hourly clients, this builds trust and reduces invoice disputes.
