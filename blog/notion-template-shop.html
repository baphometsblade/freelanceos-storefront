What to Look for in a Notion Template Shop

Not all Notion template shops are equal. Many sell visually attractive pages that look impressive in screenshots but don't actually function as systems. After purchase, you discover they're just formatted text pages — no databases, no relations, no actual structure. The result: you paid for decoration, not productivity.

A quality Notion template shop sells functional systems: relational databases that connect clients to projects to invoices, multiple view types for different contexts, documentation that explains how to use it, and templates that have been iterated based on real-world use. These are templates that save you 10+ hours of architecture work, not just 10 minutes of formatting.

What to check before buying any Notion template: Does it use relational databases? Are there multiple views (table, board, calendar)? Does it come with setup documentation? Has the seller updated it recently? If the answer to any of these is no, look elsewhere.
