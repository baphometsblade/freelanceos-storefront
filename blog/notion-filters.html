Notion Filters: How to Filter Your Databases to See Exactly What You Need

11 min read • Updated July 2026 • FreelanceOS Team

Master Notion database filters — add filters by any property type, chain AND/OR logic, save filtered views, and build custom dashboards that show you exactly what matters right now.

What Are Notion Filters?

Filters let you show only the database entries that match your criteria — and hide everything else. They don't delete data; they temporarily narrow your view to what's relevant right now.

Example: You have a Tasks database with 200 items. Apply a filter for Status = "In Progress" and Due Date within the next 7 days — now you see only the 12 tasks that need your attention this week. The other 188 are still there, just hidden by the filter.

A few key facts about how Notion filters work:

  • Filters are per-view. The same database can have a "No filter" view, an "In Progress" view, a "This Week" view, and a "Client A Only" view — all looking at the same underlying data.
  • Filters are collaborative. If you add a filter to a shared workspace database view, everyone who opens that view sees the same filter.
  • Filters are non-destructive. Filtered-out entries still exist in the database — they're just hidden in that view.
  • Filters update in real time. When an entry's properties change (e.g., status moves from "In Progress" to "Done"), it automatically drops out of the filtered view.

When to use filters vs. sorts: Use a filter to hide irrelevant entries entirely. Use a sort to reorder visible entries. Combine both to get exactly the right entries in exactly the right order.

How to Add a Filter (Step by Step)

  1. Open any Notion database (Table, Board, Gallery, List, Calendar, or Timeline view)
  2. Click Filter in the toolbar at the top of the database
  3. Click Add filter (or the + button if filters are already active)
  4. Select the property you want to filter by from the dropdown (e.g., Status, Due Date, Assignee)
  5. Choose your filter condition (e.g., "is", "contains", "is before")
  6. Enter or select the filter value
  7. The database updates instantly — click outside the filter panel to close it

When a filter is active, you'll see a Filter indicator in the toolbar with the number of active filters (e.g., "Filter: 2"). Click it to view, edit, or remove filters anytime.

To remove a filter: click Filter → click the × next to any filter condition to delete it.

Text "Contains" vs. "Is"

For text and title properties: "Contains" matches if the text includes your search term anywhere (partial match — great for search-style filters). "Is" requires an exact full match. Use "Contains" for flexible filtering; use "Is" when you need precision.

AND vs OR Filter Logic

When you add multiple filters, Notion lets you control how they combine:

AND Logic (default)

Every condition must be true. An entry only appears if it matches ALL filters simultaneously. Use AND when you want to narrow down results.

Status = "In Progress" AND Assignee = "Me" AND Due Date within next 7 days

Result: Only my in-progress tasks due this week.

OR Logic

Any condition being true is enough. An entry appears if it matches ANY filter. Use OR when you want to broaden results across multiple values.

Status = "In Progress" OR Status = "In Review" OR Status = "Blocked"

Result: All tasks that are actively being worked on, regardless of exact status.

Switching Between AND and OR

  1. Open the Filter panel
  2. Click Add filter rule to add a second condition
  3. Between the filter rows, you'll see an "And" label — click it to toggle it to "Or"
  4. Each pair of conditions can independently use AND or OR, allowing complex logic combinations

Complex filter example: (Status = "In Progress" OR Status = "Review") AND Assignee = "Me" — shows only your tasks that are in progress or in review. Notion supports nested filter groups for exactly this kind of logic.

Filtered Database Views — The Power Move

The real productivity gain from filters isn't ad-hoc filtering — it's saving filters to named views so you never have to set them up again.

A single database can have unlimited views, each with its own filter. Think of views as "lenses" on your data:

How to Save a Filter to a View

  1. Click the + next to the view tabs at the top of the database to create a new view
  2. Name the view (e.g., "This Week", "My Tasks", "In Progress")
  3. Apply your filter(s) — they're automatically saved to this view
  4. Navigate away and come back — your filter is still applied when you return to this view

Now you can switch between views instantly with a single click — your "This Week" view always shows this week's tasks, your "Client A" view always shows Client A's work, without ever manually setting filters again.

Filtering by Formula or Rollup

Formula and rollup properties are filterable — which opens the door to highly custom logic.

Formula Filters

If your formula outputs a boolean (true/false), you can filter to "Is checked" — showing only entries where the formula evaluates to true.

Formula: if(prop("Due Date") < now(), true, false) Filter: "Overdue?" is checked → shows only overdue items

If your formula outputs a number, you can filter with number conditions:

Formula: dateBetween(prop("Due Date"), now(), "days") Filter: "Days Until Due" < 3 → shows tasks due in fewer than 3 days

Rollup Filters

Filter by a rollup's aggregated value — count, sum, average, percent:

Rollup: "# of Tasks" (count of related tasks) Filter: "# of Tasks" > 0 → shows only projects that have at least one related task Filter: "# of Tasks" = 0 → shows projects with no tasks (potential oversight)

Pro tip: Combine a formula property "Is Overdue?" (boolean) with a filter for "is checked" to build an always-up-to-date overdue tracker that requires zero manual maintenance.

Filtering on Relation Properties

Relation filters let you show only entries that are connected to a specific page in another database — perfect for client-scoped or project-scoped views.

How to Filter by Relation

  1. Click Filter → Add filter → select your Relation property (e.g., "Client")
  2. Choose condition: Contains
  3. Search for and select the specific related page (e.g., the "Acme Corp" page in your Clients database)

The database now shows only entries related to Acme Corp.

Relation Filter Use Cases

  • Filter a Tasks database by Client → see only tasks for that client
  • Filter a Deliverables database by Project → see only deliverables for the active project
  • Filter a Budget database by Department → see only one department's expenses
  • Filter a Content Calendar by Campaign → see only content for a specific campaign

Combine relation + status filters for powerful compound views:

Client = "Acme Corp" AND Status = "In Progress" → Acme's active tasks only

When Filters Disappear (and Why)

If your filter seems to have vanished, one of these is usually the cause:

You switched to a different view

Filters are saved per-view. Each view has its own independent filter settings. Switch back to the view where you set the filter — it'll still be there.

The filter cleared a property value

If you filtered by a Select value that no longer exists (because the option was renamed or deleted), Notion may clear the filter automatically. Re-add it with the updated option name.

Someone else removed it

Filters in shared workspace databases are collaborative — any member with edit access can add or remove filters from a view. If your filter keeps disappearing, check with your team, or create a personal copy of the view for your own filtered access.

Personal vs. collaborative filters: On pages you own (in your private workspace), filters are yours alone. On shared workspace databases, filters are visible and editable by everyone with access. For personal-only filtered views of a shared database, duplicate the database as a linked view on your own private page and apply your personal filters there.

Building Dashboards with Linked Views and Filters

This is where filters become genuinely transformative: combine linked database views with different filters on a single dashboard page to create a real-time command center.

How to Build a Filtered Dashboard

  1. Create a new blank Notion page for your dashboard
  2. Type /linked view or /linked database to insert a linked view of an existing database
  3. Select your database from the list
  4. Apply a filter to this linked view (e.g., "Today's Tasks")
  5. Repeat — add more linked views of the same database, each with a different filter
  6. Name each linked view clearly using the View name field

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add a filter to a Notion database?
Click the Filter button at the top of any database view, then click Add filter. Select the property to filter by, choose your condition (is, contains, is before, etc.), and set the value. The database updates instantly as you set conditions. Click outside the filter panel to close it — your filter remains active.
Can I use AND and OR logic in Notion filters?
Yes. Notion supports both AND and OR logic. By default, multiple filters use AND (all conditions must be true simultaneously). To switch specific conditions to OR, click the "And" label between filter rows to toggle it to "Or" — entries matching any of the OR conditions will be shown. You can also create filter groups (click Add filter group) to build complex nested logic.
Do Notion filters apply to everyone who views the database?
Yes — filters saved to a view are collaborative and visible to all workspace members who have access to that view. They are not personal by default. To create a personal-only filtered view, duplicate the database as a linked view on your private page and apply your personal filters to that copy. Only you can see pages in your private workspace.
How do I filter a Notion database by date?
Click Filter → Add filter → select your date property → choose a condition. Relative conditions like "Is within the next 7 days" or "Is within the past month" automatically update each day without manual resetting — ideal for always-current "This Week" or "Overdue" views. For a fixed date range, use "Is after [date]" combined with "Is before [date]".
Why did my Notion filter disappear?
The most common reason: you switched to a different view of the same database. Notion filters are per-view — each view has its own filter settings that don't carry over. Navigate back to the original view and your filter should still be active. Other causes: a team member removed it (collaborative views), or a Select option that the filter referenced was renamed or deleted.
