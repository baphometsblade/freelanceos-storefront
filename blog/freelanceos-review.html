What Is FreelanceOS?

FreelanceOS is a Notion template system built for freelancers who need to manage clients, projects, invoices, and revenue in one place. It's available in two tiers: a Lite version (free) with core functionality, and a Pro version ($29) with the full system including invoicing, revenue dashboards, and advanced templates.

Unlike generic productivity templates, FreelanceOS was designed specifically around how a freelance business actually operates. It doesn't try to be everything — there's no habit tracker or goal-setting section. It focuses entirely on the business operations side: who you're working with, what you're doing for them, what they owe you, and how your revenue is tracking over time.

The template is delivered via Notion's duplication system. You click a link, duplicate it to your own Notion workspace, and it's yours. No login, no subscription, no ongoing fees.

What's Included: A Walkthrough of All 7 Databases

FreelanceOS Pro is built around seven interconnected databases. Here's what each one does and how they connect to each other.

1. Client Database

The foundation of the whole system. Each client gets a record with contact details, status (active, inactive, lead), rate information, and a relation to every project and invoice associated with them. The rollup fields are the key feature here — you can see total billed, total paid, and outstanding balance for any client at a glance without opening a single invoice.

2. Project Pipeline

Every client project lives here. You can view the pipeline as a Kanban board (by stage: proposal, active, review, complete) or as a table filtered by client, deadline, or status. Each project links back to its client and forward to any related invoices. The built-in properties cover deliverables, deadline, hourly vs. fixed price, and a progress indicator.

3. Invoice Tracker

This is where FreelanceOS Pro earns its $29. The invoice tracker is genuinely well-designed — each invoice has a payment status (draft, sent, paid, overdue), due date, amount, and client relation. There's a filtered view that surfaces only overdue invoices, which is the single most useful thing for most freelancers. The formulas automatically calculate days overdue for each unpaid invoice.

4. Revenue Dashboard

Aggregates your income across all clients and invoices. Shows monthly totals via rollup from the invoice tracker, with a view that lets you see revenue by month at a glance. Not a full accounting system — it won't replace your tax software — but for understanding how your business is trending month-over-month, it's exactly what you need.

5. Proposal Storage

A database for storing the proposals you send to clients. You can track status (sent, accepted, rejected, expired) and link accepted proposals to the projects they generated. This is particularly useful for understanding your conversion rate over time — something most freelancers have no idea about.

6. Rate Calculator

A template-driven calculator that helps you figure out what to charge for a project based on your target monthly income, estimated hours, and expenses. It's not a database in the traditional sense — it's more of a guided worksheet. Useful when onboarding new client types where you don't have a baseline rate yet.

7. Weekly Review Template

A recurring template for weekly business reviews. Pre-filled with prompts: what did I ship this week, what invoices need chasing, what's in the pipeline, what should I focus on next week. If you actually use it consistently (the hard part), this is one of the higher-value items in the system because it forces the kind of regular business review most freelancers skip entirely.

Who FreelanceOS Is For

It's for you if:

  • You're actively managing 2 or more clients at any time
  • You invoice clients and want to track payment status without digging through email
  • You want to understand your revenue trends month-over-month
  • You've tried spreadsheets and find them clunky for this kind of work
  • You're already a Notion user or want to consolidate into Notion

It's NOT for you if:

  • You only have one client or work on a single long-term retainer with no juggling
  • You need a time tracker or billing system integrated in the same tool
  • You need accounting features for tax purposes
  • You strongly prefer dedicated apps over all-in-one workspaces
  • You've never used Notion and aren't interested in learning it
