The 80% of Notion Most Users Never Discover

Notion is one of the most powerful productivity tools ever built — and one of the most underused. Most people who sign up for Notion use it as a fancy note-taking app, never discovering the database engine, formula system, AI capabilities, and workflow automation hiding underneath.

A 2025 survey found that the average Notion user uses fewer than 20% of the platform's features. The remaining 80% — linked databases, rollups, relations, sync blocks, template buttons, formulas, Notion AI — are where the real productivity gains live.

This guide walks through 50 specific, actionable tips organized by skill level. Whether you've used Notion for a week or two years, you'll find things in here you didn't know existed. Let's start with the fundamentals and work up to the features that genuinely change how you work.

Beginner Notion Tips (Tips 1–10)

Beginner Level

1Use / slash commands for everything

Type / anywhere on a Notion page to open the block menu. From here you can insert headings, databases, toggle lists, embeds, callouts, dividers, and over 50 other block types. Learning this command eliminates the need to hunt through menus. Master the slash command and you'll build pages 3x faster.

2Toggle blocks keep long pages navigable

Create a toggle block with /toggle. Toggle blocks collapse their children, keeping long pages clean and scannable. Use them to hide detailed notes under a summary header, collapse completed sections, or organize sub-items under parent categories. Toggle lists are essential for any page longer than one screen.

3Duplicate any block with Ctrl+D (Mac: ⌘+D)

Select any block — or multiple blocks — and hit Ctrl+D to duplicate instantly. This works on individual blocks, groups of blocks, or entire pages. Essential for creating repeating structures like weekly review templates or project brief formats without rebuilding from scratch.

4Transform any block into a different type

Click the ⋮⋮ handle that appears to the left of any block, then select "Turn into" to convert it to a different block type. Turn a bullet point into a toggle, a heading into a callout, or a to-do into a numbered list. You can also select multiple blocks and turn them all at once.

5Link to any page with @mention

Type @ followed by a page name to create an inline link to that page. This works anywhere in Notion — inside text, inside database properties, in comments. It creates a clickable link that also creates a bidirectional connection. Use @Today, @Tomorrow, or @[date] to insert date mentions.

6The sidebar Favorites keeps key pages one-click away

Hover over any page in your sidebar and click the ★ star icon to add it to your Favorites section, which appears at the top of the sidebar. Add your most-used pages — your home dashboard, your active project board, your inbox — so you never need to dig through your workspace hierarchy to find them.

7Template buttons auto-insert content on click

Create a template button with /template button. Inside, add any blocks you want inserted when someone clicks it. Now anyone (or future-you) can click once to insert a complete meeting agenda, project brief, weekly review template, or daily journal format. Template buttons are one of Notion's most underused features.

8Notion mobile — the gestures that matter

On mobile, long-press any block to select and drag it. Swipe left on a block to quickly delete or duplicate it. Tap the + button at the bottom to add new blocks. Use the quick-add icons above the keyboard to insert common block types fast. The mobile app supports full database editing — not just viewing.

9Inline comments with Ctrl+Shift+M

Select any text and press Ctrl+Shift+M (Mac: ⌘+Shift+M) to add an inline comment on that specific text. This is different from page-level comments. Use inline comments to annotate specific phrases for review, ask questions about particular points, or leave feedback for collaborators without editing the page itself.

10Synced blocks — one block, multiple pages

Create a synced block with /synced block. Any content you put inside syncs automatically across every page that contains that synced block. Update it once, and it updates everywhere. Perfect for navigation menus, disclaimers, team contact lists, or any content that needs to appear consistently across multiple pages.

Intermediate Notion Tips (Tips 11–20)

Intermediate Level

11Linked databases — same data, different views

Create a linked view of a database anywhere with /linked view. The linked view shows the same data as the original database, but you can apply different filters, sorts, and display settings without affecting the original. One task database can appear as a board on your project page, a timeline on your planning page, and a filtered list on your daily dashboard — all showing the same underlying data.

12Filter by "Me" across any workspace

In any database with an Assignee or Person property, create a filter where that property "is" set to "Me." This dynamically shows only your own tasks, regardless of who else is in the database. Add this filter to your personal dashboard to see all your active work across every project in one view.

13Group by property for instant Kanban

In any database board or table view, click the "Group by" option and select any Select or Status property. Your flat list instantly becomes a grouped Kanban board organized by that property. Group tasks by Priority, Status, Assignee, or Sprint. Switch between groupings to see the same tasks from different angles without creating new views.

14Rollup properties — aggregate from related records

Once two databases are connected with a Relation property, add a Rollup property to calculate aggregates from the related records. Sum all invoice amounts from your Invoices database into your Clients database. Count the number of tasks in each project. Average the completion percentage across all deliverables. Rollups turn relationships into automatic summaries.

15Formulas 101 — dateBetween() for days until due

Add a Formula property to any database. To calculate days until a deadline: dateBetween(prop("Due Date"), now(), "days") — this returns the number of days between now and the due date. Combine with if() to add color logic: if(dateBetween(prop("Due Date"), now(), "days") < 3, "🔴 Urgent", "✅ On Track")

16Multi-select filtering for complex views

In database filters, you can stack multiple conditions with AND/OR logic. Click "Add filter" to add more conditions, then toggle between "And" and "Or" to control how they interact. Example: show tasks where Status is "In Progress" AND Assignee is Me AND Priority is "High" — your actual urgent queue, not everything assigned to you.

17Embed any URL as an inline preview

Paste a URL on its own line in Notion and click "Create embed" to display it as an inline preview rather than a link. Works with Google Docs, Figma files, Loom videos, Google Maps, Airtable bases, Miro boards, and hundreds of other services. Your project page becomes a hub of embedded tools without leaving Notion.

18Gallery view for visual databases

Switch any database to Gallery view to display records as visual cards with image previews. Set a Cover property to control which image appears on each card. Perfect for reading lists (show book covers), design portfolios (show mockup images), product catalogs, or content libraries where visual scanning is faster than reading titles in a list.

19Timeline view for project planning

Switch any database with date properties to Timeline view for a Gantt-like project overview. Map each record's start and end dates onto a visual timeline. You can drag the bars to reschedule items. Add "Dependencies" as a property and visualize what needs to happen in what order. Far more useful than a list of deadlines for planning complex projects.

20Relations + rollups = automatic project summaries

Connect your Projects database to your Tasks database with a Relation property. Add Rollup properties to your Projects database: count total tasks, count completed tasks, then create a formula: round(prop("Completed Tasks") / prop("Total Tasks") * 100) for an automatic completion percentage. Every project now shows its real progress without manual updates.

Power User Notion Tips (Tips 21–30)

Power User Level

21Connect Notion to Zapier for lightweight automation

Notion's API connects to Zapier, which means you can trigger actions when database items are created or updated. Common automations: when a new client is added to your Clients database, create a new project folder in Google Drive. When an invoice status changes to "Paid," send yourself a Slack notification. When a task is marked done, update a summary spreadsheet.

22Sub-items in databases for task hierarchies

In any database, enable "Sub-items" in the database settings. This lets you nest items inside other items — tasks within tasks, sub-projects within projects, sub-goals within goals. Sub-items inherit the parent's database properties and appear indented in table view. The "Dependency" feature (also in database settings) lets you explicitly mark which items must be completed before others.

23Color-coded status properties in board views

In any Select or Status property, click each option to assign a color. These colors appear throughout Notion — as colored dots in table view, as colored column headers in board view, and as colored chips throughout your workspace. Use color deliberately: red for blocked, yellow for in-progress, green for done. Consistent color coding makes status scannable at a glance.

24The dependency pattern — Task A blocks Task B

Enable Sub-items and Dependencies in your database settings, then mark task relationships with "Blocking" and "Blocked by" connections. In Timeline view, Notion will draw visual dependency lines between connected tasks. Use this to catch scheduling conflicts early — if Task A slips and it blocks Task B, you'll see Task B can't start on its planned date before you promised it to a client.

25Advanced formula — conditional status icons

Build visual status indicators with formulas. Example: if(prop("Status") == "Done", "✅ Complete", if(prop("Status") == "In Progress", "⏳ Active", if(dateBetween(prop("Due Date"), now(), "days") < 0, "🔴 Overdue", "⬜ Not Started"))) — this single formula shows four distinct visual states based on status and due date.

26Page icons and covers for visual organization

Every Notion page supports a custom icon (emoji or image) and a header cover image. Use consistent icon conventions throughout your workspace: 📋 for project pages, 👥 for client pages, 💰 for financial pages, 📝 for meeting notes. This visual language makes navigating your workspace far faster than reading titles, especially on mobile.

27Share pages publicly with granular permission control

Click Share on any page and toggle "Share to web" to make it publicly accessible. Choose between "Can view," "Can comment," or "Can edit" for each person or group you share with. For client-facing content, create a dedicated page with project status, deliverables, and feedback — share it publicly or via email invite. This replaces a lot of client email back-and-forth.

28Notion Sites for custom domain pages

Notion Sites (available on Plus and Business plans) lets you publish Notion pages as proper websites with custom domains (yourname.com instead of notion.so/yourname). Use this for client portals, personal portfolios, knowledge bases, or simple microsites. Your Notion page becomes a real URL you can share professionally.

29Team wikis with permission levels

Build a team wiki in Notion by creating a root "Home" page and organizing subpages by department or function. Set page permissions at the section level: your finance pages are visible only to the finance team, your engineering docs only to engineering. Use @here mentions in comments to notify specific groups. The permission hierarchy lets you build a shared knowledge base without showing everything to everyone.

30Export to Markdown for full data portability

Export any Notion page or your entire workspace as Markdown + CSV via Settings → Export. This creates a full backup of your content in an open format that can be imported into Obsidian, Roam, Bear, or any other Markdown-compatible app. Run exports periodically as a backup habit — Notion is reliable, but vendor independence is wise.

Notion AI Tips (Tips 31–40)

Notion AI Level

31AI Autofill — auto-summarize database pages

Add an AI Autofill property to any database. Choose a prompt (e.g., "Summarize this page in 2 sentences" or "List the key action items from this meeting note") and Notion AI will automatically generate that content for every record in your database. Fill your entire meeting notes database with AI-generated summaries in one click.

32"Ask AI about this page" for instant Q&A

Open any Notion page and click the AI icon (or press Space to trigger AI). Choose "Ask AI about this page" and ask any question: "What are the next steps?", "What decisions were made in this meeting?", "What's blocking progress on this project?" Notion AI reads the full page and answers from that context.

33Change tone to rewrite client emails professionally

Select any text, open AI, and choose "Change tone" → Professional, Casual, Direct, or Friendly. This is powerful for client communication: write your first draft fast and direct, then have AI polish it to be professional and appropriately warm. Your client gets well-written communication; you spend 30 seconds instead of 5 minutes crafting the perfect phrasing.

34"Find action items" in messy meeting notes

After any meeting, paste your raw notes into Notion and use AI to "Find action items." Notion AI scans the text and extracts a clean, formatted list of tasks with owners and implied deadlines. This turns 20 minutes of note-editing into a 30-second AI task. Then send the action items to your project database with a few clicks.

35AI Q&A across your entire workspace

Notion AI Q&A searches your entire workspace, not just the current page. Ask "What did the client say about the project deadline?" or "What's our current pricing for logo projects?" — AI will find relevant information across your notes, databases, and pages. This turns your entire workspace into a searchable knowledge base you can query in plain English.

36Draft blog post outlines in seconds

Create a new Notion page, type your blog post title, then use AI to "Draft an outline" or "Write a draft." You get a structured starting point in under 10 seconds. The draft won't be publish-ready, but having structure to react to and edit is far faster than writing from a blank page. Use AI as your first draft machine, not your final editor.

37Translate content to any language

Select any text in Notion and use AI to translate it into any of 100+ languages. Useful for: sending client proposals to non-English speakers, translating meeting notes from multilingual calls, localizing documentation for international team members, or understanding foreign-language documents pasted into your workspace.

38Fix spelling and grammar in one click

Select your entire document (Ctrl+A), then use AI → "Fix spelling and grammar." Notion AI corrects errors throughout the selection while preserving your voice and formatting. More reliable than traditional spell-checkers for nuanced grammar issues, and faster than reading through a document manually before sending it to a client.

39"Continue writing" to break through writer's block

When you're stuck on a section, click at the end of your last paragraph and use AI → "Continue writing." Notion AI will generate the next few sentences or paragraphs based on what you've written so far. Don't use the output verbatim — use it to break the blank-page paralysis and then rewrite it in your voice. The momentum is the point.

40AI-powered Notion Slack integration

Notion AI can be connected to Slack (via the Notion Slack app) to answer questions about your Notion workspace directly inside Slack. Team members can ask "What's the status of the Q3 campaign?" in Slack and get an AI-generated answer sourced from your Notion workspace — without leaving the app they're already in.

Notion Tips for Freelancers Specifically (Tips 41–50)

Freelance-Specific

41Build a client portal in Notion for every project

Create a shared Notion page for each client with: project status, current deliverables, feedback requests, and a file library. Share it publicly or via email invite. Clients can see exactly where their project stands without emailing you for updates. This one change reduces project management overhead by 30–50% for most freelancers.

42Rate card and proposal template database

Store your service tiers, rates, and standard deliverables in a Notion database. When building a proposal, filter to your relevant services and pull them into your proposal template with one click. Your rates are always current, your proposals are consistent, and you never have to remember what you charged for something six months ago.

43Weekly capacity planning with a simple formula

Add an "Estimated Hours" property to your tasks database and a "Deadline" property. Create a view filtering tasks due this week, then add a formula column: prop("Estimated Hours") and enable "Calculate" → "Sum" at the bottom. You'll see your total committed hours at a glance. If the sum exceeds your available hours, you know you're overcommitted before it becomes a problem.

44Feedback tracker for ongoing client relationships

Create a simple Feedback database with properties: Client, Date, Feedback Type (Positive / Constructive / Complaint), Summary, and Status (Acknowledged / Actioned / Closed). Log every piece of client feedback here. Before any renewal conversation, review their feedback history. Patterns in constructive feedback reveal service improvements; patterns in positive feedback reveal your real differentiators.

45Automate your weekly review with a template button

Create a Weekly Review template button on your planning page. Inside: a section for wins, a section for challenges, a linked view of last week's completed tasks, a linked view of next week's upcoming tasks, and a revenue summary rollup. Click once every Friday to generate your review structure pre-populated with real data from your databases.

46Notion as your primary email drafting tool

Write important client emails in Notion first, then copy-paste to your email client. This gives you full formatting, the ability to save drafts indefinitely, version history, and AI editing tools before the email goes out. It also creates a searchable record of what you've communicated with each client. Link email drafts to the relevant client page.

47Monthly income tracking with automatic totals

In your Invoice database, add a filter for "Payment Date is within this month" and enable the Sum calculation on your Amount column. Notion instantly shows your total income for the current month. Create views for each month to compare your income trajectory without any manual calculations. Add a rollup to your annual overview page for a running total.

48Discovery questionnaire as a shared Notion page

Create a discovery questionnaire for new clients as a Notion page with a template button. Share it with the client so they can fill in their answers directly. Their responses live in your workspace, connected to their client record. No more lost email threads hunting for "what did they say their budget was in the first call?"

49Connect your CRM to your calendar via Date properties

In your Clients or Projects database, add Date properties for key milestones: Kickoff Date, Review Date, Deadline, Renewal Date. Use a linked Timeline view on your planning page. You now have a visual calendar of all client commitments across all projects — without any separate calendar app. Add deadline reminders via Zapier if you want push notifications.

50Build a Notion home page dashboard that actually works

Your Notion home page should show exactly what you need to work, nothing more. Include: today's tasks (linked database filtered to today), three active projects with their status, your monthly revenue figure (rollup), and a quick-capture inbox. No more than these four sections. A home page with 15 sections gives you 15 places to look instead of one. Simplicity is the feature.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important Notion features to learn first?
Start with: slash commands, toggle blocks, and basic databases with at least one filter and one sort. These three things unlock 80% of what makes Notion powerful. Once those are comfortable, add Relations and Rollups — they're where the real magic happens for connecting your workspace into a system rather than a collection of disconnected pages.
Are Notion formulas difficult to learn?
Notion formulas follow a JavaScript-like syntax and are genuinely learnable with practice. Start with simple formulas: if(), dateBetween(), and prop() are the core functions you'll use 80% of the time. The Notion documentation has a complete function reference, and there are hundreds of community-shared formulas you can copy and adapt. Don't fear formulas — most useful ones are 1–3 lines.
Is Notion AI worth the extra cost?
For most power users, yes. Notion AI adds $8–10/month to your Notion subscription. If you use it daily for meeting summaries, email drafts, document editing, and workspace Q&A, it saves at least 30–60 minutes per week — which is worth far more than $10/month for most professionals. The AI Autofill feature alone justifies the cost for people with large databases.
How do I stop my Notion workspace from getting messy?
Three habits prevent workspace chaos: 1) Every page belongs to a parent page — no orphaned pages floating in the sidebar. 2) Weekly cleanup: 15 minutes every Friday to archive completed projects and delete draft pages that went nowhere. 3) Never create a new page until you know where it belongs. Structure first, then content. A home page that shows everything important makes buried pages less tempting.
What's the difference between a database page and a regular Notion page?
A regular Notion page is a free-form document — like a Word document with blocks. A database page is a record inside a database — it has structured properties (dates, status, select fields, relations) plus an open content area below. Every database record is a full page with those properties attached. The power comes from filtering, sorting, and querying those properties across hundreds of records.
Can Notion replace project management tools like Asana or Linear?
For individuals and small teams (under 10 people), absolutely — especially with Sub-items, Dependencies, and Timeline view. For larger engineering teams doing complex sprint planning, Linear or Jira still have advantages in speed and GitHub integration. But for freelancers, agencies, content teams, and most small businesses, Notion handles project management extremely well while also being your documentation, wiki, and knowledge base — eliminating the tool-switching tax.
