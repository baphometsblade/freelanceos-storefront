Notion @Mentions: The Complete Guide to Mentioning People, Pages, and Dates

8 min read • Updated July 2026 • FreelanceOS Team

The @ key in Notion does a lot more than tag teammates. Here is the complete guide to all three types of Notion mentions — people, pages, and dates — and how to use them to build a deeply connected workspace.

What Are Notion Mentions?

In Notion, the @ symbol triggers a universal mention menu. Depending on what you type after the @ sign, you can:

  • Mention a person in your workspace — tags them and sends a notification
  • Mention a page — inserts a clickable inline link to any page in your workspace
  • Mention a date — inserts an inline date chip, with optional reminder

All three work the same way: type @ anywhere in a Notion text block, comment, or database property cell, then type to filter the results. Notion shows matching people, pages, and dates in a unified dropdown, ranked by relevance and recency.

What makes Notion mentions powerful: they work anywhere text appears — in paragraphs, bullet points, callout blocks, comments, database properties like Text or Title, and even inside formulas and filters. They are not limited to designated "mention fields" the way some project management tools are.

The Three Types of Notion Mentions

1. @Person Mentions

Typing @ followed by a name searches your Notion workspace members. Select someone from the dropdown to insert their name as a highlighted mention link.

"Please review the proposal before our call — @Sarah Chen can you check the pricing section?"

Person mentions have two purposes in Notion:

  • Notification — the mentioned person receives an in-app notification (and optionally an email) so they know they have been tagged in a specific page or comment
  • Clarity — in meeting notes and project pages, seeing someone's name inline makes it instantly clear who is responsible for what

You can find all pages and comments where you have been @mentioned by clicking the Updates icon (bell) in the Notion sidebar. This gives you a feed of everything you have been tagged in across the workspace.

2. @Page Mentions

Typing @ followed by a page name inserts a clickable inline link to that page — directly in the flow of your text, not as a separate block.

"This project follows the process described in @Brand Guidelines 2026. See also: @Client Onboarding Checklist."

@Page mentions are how you create a web of cross-references in Notion — linking related pages without creating a formal parent-child hierarchy. Some powerful uses:

  • Link a meeting notes page to the relevant project page in one sentence
  • Reference your rate card from inside a proposal page
  • Create a "hub" page that mentions all related project pages, acting as a manual index
  • In database entries (like a task), mention the relevant SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) page directly in the task description

Freelancer tip: In every client project page in Notion, include an @mention of the client's page, the relevant proposal, and the contract. Creates a three-click navigation system between the core documents for every project.

3. @Date Mentions

Date mentions insert an inline date chip — not a database Date property, but a date that appears directly in text.

"Proposal due: @July 18, 2026. First draft complete by @tomorrow. Review meeting: @next Monday."

Shortcut date mentions Notion supports:

  • @today — inserts today's date
  • @tomorrow — inserts tomorrow's date
  • @yesterday — inserts yesterday's date
  • @next week — inserts the date 7 days from today
  • @[any date] — type a date to search and insert a specific date

Date mentions can optionally trigger a reminder. When you click a date mention, a popup allows you to set a reminder notification at the date and time — useful for deadline callouts in meeting notes, project briefs, or weekly planning pages.

How to Use @Mentions: Step-by-Step

Step 1 — Position your cursor in any text area

Click into any text block in Notion — a paragraph, bullet point, callout, heading, numbered list, or even inside a database Title property or Text property cell.

Step 2 — Type @

Press @ on your keyboard. A unified mention menu immediately appears below your cursor, showing recent people, pages, and dates.

Step 3 — Type to filter

Start typing to filter results. Notion searches across all three mention types simultaneously:

  • Type a name to find workspace members
  • Type a page title to find any page in your workspace (including database entries)
  • Type "today", "tomorrow", or a date to insert a date mention

Step 4 — Select with mouse or keyboard

Use arrow keys to navigate the dropdown and Enter to confirm, or click the item with your mouse. The mention is inserted inline in your text.

Step 5 — For date mentions — optionally set a reminder

Click on any inserted date mention to open its settings popup. Toggle Remind to on and set the notification time. Notion will send you a reminder at that date and time — useful for keeping deadline callouts in your meeting notes actually actionable.

@Mentions in Database Properties

Notion mentions work inside database property cells as well as in page body text. Here is how each property type interacts with mentions:

Property TypeMentions Work?Best Use
TitleYesAdd @person or @page to a task title to show context inline in database views
TextYesAdd @page links in a "Notes" text property to cross-reference related pages
PersonBuilt-inThe Person property type is a dedicated people-picker — use it instead of @mentions for formal ownership assignment
DateBuilt-inThe Date property type stores dates natively — use it for structured deadline and scheduling data; use @date mentions only for inline references in text
Select / Multi-selectNoSelect properties use predefined tags, not mentions
FormulaNoFormulas use Notion's formula language, not @mention syntax

The general rule: use dedicated property types (Person, Date) for structured data you want to filter, sort, or roll up. Use @mentions in text properties and page body when you want to create readable cross-references in the flow of writing.

@Mentions in Comments

Comments in Notion are one of the most common places to use @person mentions. You can comment on any Notion page or on any database entry by selecting text and clicking the comment icon, or by clicking the comment icon at the top right of any page.

In comments, @person mentions are especially powerful because they:

  • Send a direct notification to the mentioned person
  • Appear in that person's Updates feed in the sidebar
  • Can generate an email notification depending on the recipient's notification settings
  • Create a permanent, attributable record of who was asked to do what and when
Comment: "@Marcus — can you review the budget section before we send this to the client? Flagging the numbers in section 3 specifically."

This comment @mentions Marcus, sends him a notification, links him directly to the page where the comment was posted, and creates a record of the request — all in one action. No Slack message needed, no context switching, no "I never got that" moments.

How Mentions Create a Connected Notion Workspace

The most powerful use of Notion mentions is not individual tags — it is the cumulative effect of @page mentions across your workspace, which creates a web of navigable cross-references that makes your Notion system feel like a connected knowledge base rather than a collection of isolated pages.

The Hub-and-Spoke Pattern

A common advanced Notion pattern: create a hub page for each major domain (Client Projects, Marketing, Operations) and use @page mentions to create a hand-curated index of the most important child pages. This is more flexible than the Notion sidebar hierarchy because you can mention the same page in multiple hubs and add context around each link.

Meeting Notes Cross-Referencing

In every meeting notes page, @mention the relevant project page and the names of everyone who attended. This creates a bi-directional trail: if you later read a project page and want to find all meetings related to it, you can search for pages that mention that project. Building this habit over months creates a searchable meeting history that is actually navigable.

The @mention Index Property

In a database, add a Text property called "Related Pages" and populate it with @page mentions for each entry. This creates a lightweight relations system without using Notion's formal Relations property type — useful for databases where you want to cross-reference pages without setting up full bidirectional database relations.

Notion Mentions: Tips and Keyboard Shortcuts

  • @mentions work in every text context — paragraphs, bullets, numbered lists, callout blocks, toggle headings, quote blocks, code block captions, and image captions. If it is text in Notion, @ works there.
  • Use [[double brackets]] as an alternative to @page — typing [[ in any text block also opens the page search menu. It creates the same page mention link as @pagename. Many Notion power users prefer [[ because it distinguishes page links from people mentions visually as you type.
  • @mentions survive page renames — if you rename a page, all @page mentions pointing to it automatically update their display text to the new name. You do not need to manually update cross-references.
  • Filter the mention menu by type — after typing @, you can type "people:", "pages:", or "date:" to filter the dropdown to show only that category of mentions. Useful in large workspaces with many pages.
  • Use @date mentions for deadline visibility in meeting notes — instead of writing "the deadline is July 20th" as plain text, write it as a @date mention. The date chip is visually distinct and can trigger a reminder. Dates-as-mentions are harder to miss than dates-as-text.
  • Check your updates feed daily — the bell icon in the Notion sidebar shows all your @mentions across the workspace. Making this a daily habit ensures you never miss an action item that came in via a Notion comment or page mention.
  • @mentions in comments resolve threads — after an @mentioned person completes a task or resolves an issue, they or the commenter can mark the comment thread as "Resolved." Resolved threads can be hidden, keeping the page clean while preserving the history.

Notion Mentions vs. Relations: What Is the Difference?

A common question from Notion users getting more advanced is: should I use an @page mention or a Relation property to connect two pieces of content?

Feature@Page MentionRelation Property
Where it livesInside a text block, inline with your writingAs a dedicated database property column
Bidirectional?No — one-way inline linkYes — Notion creates a link on both sides
Filterable / sortable?NoYes — filter and sort databases by relation values
Works in rollups?NoYes — rollup properties aggregate related page data
Best forReadable cross-references in text, meeting notes, documentationStructured data relationships between databases (tasks to projects, projects to clients)

Use @page mentions for human-readable cross-references in your text. Use Relation properties when you need to build structured, filterable connections between databases that power rollups and linked views.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you mention someone in Notion?
Type @ anywhere in a Notion text block or comment, then start typing the person's name. A dropdown appears showing workspace members. Select the person to insert the mention. If notifications are enabled, the mentioned person receives an in-app and optionally email notification.
How do you link to a page in Notion using mentions?
Type @ in any Notion text block, then type the name of the page you want to link to. The dropdown shows matching pages from your workspace. Select the page to insert a clickable inline mention link. Clicking it navigates directly to that page. You can also use [[double brackets]] as an alternative shortcut for the same page-linking behavior.
What is a date mention in Notion?
A date mention is an inline date reference you insert with @today, @tomorrow, @yesterday, or @[specific date]. It displays as a clickable date chip in your text. You can configure date mentions to send a reminder notification at the specified date and time — useful for deadline callouts in meeting notes or project briefs.
Do Notion @mentions send notifications?
Yes — @person mentions in Notion send a notification to the mentioned person if they have a Notion account and notifications are enabled. They receive an in-app notification (visible in the bell icon sidebar) and, depending on their settings, an email notification. @page and @date mentions do not generate notifications — they are navigation and reference tools, not alerts.
What is the difference between @mention and a linked page in Notion?
A @page mention is an inline text link embedded in the flow of your writing — like a hyperlink in a sentence. A sub-page or linked page appears as its own block in the Notion page hierarchy, like a traditional document link in a navigation structure. Both navigate to the same destination when clicked, but @mentions integrate naturally into text while linked pages appear as standalone entries in your page content blocks.
