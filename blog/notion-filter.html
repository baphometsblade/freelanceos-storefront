Notion Filters: How to Filter, Sort, and Find Anything in Notion

A Notion database without filters is like a spreadsheet where every row is always visible — useful, but overwhelming. Filters are how you transform a raw database into a focused, actionable view that shows exactly what matters right now: your active tasks, this week's deliverables, overdue invoices, or hot prospects.

This guide covers everything about Notion filters: how to add them, how to combine multiple conditions with AND/OR logic, how to filter by date dynamically, how to save filtered views so they're always one click away, and the difference between filtering and searching. By the end, your databases will surface exactly the right information at the right time.

1. What Are Notion Filters?

A Notion filter is a rule you apply to a database view that hides rows not matching the rule. Filters are:

  • Non-destructive: The hidden rows still exist in your database — filters only hide them from the current view. Switch to an unfiltered view and everything is there.
  • View-specific: Filters apply per view. The same database can have a "All Tasks" view with no filters and a "My Active Tasks" view with two filters — the underlying data is the same.
  • Saveable: You can save a filtered view and come back to it anytime. The filters are stored with the view.
  • Dynamic: Date filters like "is within the next week" or "is in the past month" update automatically based on today's date — you don't need to update them manually.

Two types of Notion filters:

  1. Simple filters: A single condition on one property (e.g., Status is "In Progress")
  2. Filter groups with AND/OR logic: Multiple conditions combined with AND (all must match) or OR (any must match) logic — or a mix of both using filter groups

2. Adding Your First Notion Filter

Adding a filter takes three clicks:

Step 1
Open a database in any view (table, board, gallery, list, or calendar)

Filters work in every Notion database view. Make sure you're in a database — not a simple bulleted list.

Step 2
Click the "Filter" button at the top right of the view

The Filter button appears in the database toolbar alongside Sort, Group, and Properties. If you don't see it, hover over the database to reveal the toolbar.

Step 3
Click "Add Filter" → Select a property → Select a condition → Enter a value

The filter applies immediately as you configure it. You'll see the database update in real-time as rows are shown or hidden.

Quick filter tip: After clicking Filter, Notion often suggests common filters based on your database properties. You can click a suggestion to apply it instantly instead of building it from scratch. For example, if your database has a Person property, Notion may suggest "Assigned to me" as a quick filter.

4. Using AND vs. OR Logic in Notion Filters

When you add more than one filter condition, you need to decide whether rows must match ALL conditions or ANY condition. This is the AND vs. OR choice.

AND Logic (Default)

Every condition must be true for a row to show. Adding a second filter to an existing filter panel defaults to AND:

Status is "In Progress"
AND Assigned To contains Me

This shows only tasks that are BOTH In Progress AND assigned to you. A task assigned to you that's "Done" is hidden. A task that's "In Progress" assigned to someone else is also hidden.

OR Logic

Any condition being true is enough for a row to show. To use OR, after adding your first filter, look for the option to change the logic from AND to OR within the filter panel:

Status is "In Progress"
OR Status is "In Review"

This shows tasks that are either In Progress OR In Review — effectively combining two status values into one filter view.

Mixed AND/OR Logic Using Filter Groups

For more complex filtering, Notion supports filter groups — independent groups of conditions combined with OR between groups, and AND within each group:

GROUP 1
Status is "In Progress"
AND Assignee contains Me
OR
GROUP 2
Priority is "Urgent"

This shows: (tasks that are In Progress AND assigned to me) OR (tasks that are Urgent regardless of assignee or status). Filter groups let you build sophisticated views that would be impossible with simple AND/OR alone.

6. Saving Filtered Views in Notion

A filter you have to re-apply every time provides only half the value. Saved views make your filters permanent and one-click accessible — the real power of Notion databases.

How to Save a Filtered View

  1. Set up your filters the way you want them
  2. Click the + button next to the existing view tabs at the top of the database
  3. Choose the view layout (Table, Board, Calendar, Gallery, or List)
  4. Notion creates a new view — add the same filters to this view
  5. Double-click the view tab name and rename it to something descriptive

Now the view is permanently saved with those filters. Click the tab anytime to instantly see the filtered results.

8. Filter vs. Search in Notion

Notion beginners often confuse filters with Notion's search feature. They serve completely different purposes:

FeatureNotion FilterNotion Search (Cmd+P)
What it searchesProperties of rows in a specific databaseContent and titles of all pages in your workspace
ScopeOne database viewEntire workspace
PersistenceSaved with a view, always activeOne-off — clears when closed
What it can matchProperty values, checkboxes, dates, people, statusAny text inside any page or database row
Best forOngoing curated views ("show me active projects")Finding a specific page by keyword ("where did I write about client X?")
Updates automaticallyYes — dynamic date filters always recalculateN/A — you type a new query each time
Use filters for your daily workflows. Use search for one-off lookups. If you find yourself re-typing the same search query regularly, that's a sign you should build a saved filtered view instead.

Filtering Within Search Results

Notion's search (Cmd+P) has its own basic filter options — you can narrow results to a specific database or page. But these are search filters (narrowing search results), not database view filters (hiding rows in a view). Don't confuse the two.

9. Advanced Filter Tips for Power Users

Filter by Formula Output

Formula properties output a value (text, number, boolean, or date) that you can filter on. Create a formula that calculates something (e.g., days until deadline, or a priority score), then filter by that formula's output. For example:

  • Formula: dateBetween(prop("Due Date"), now(), "days") → outputs number of days until due
  • Filter: Formula < 7 → shows only tasks due within 7 days

Filter Linked Databases on a Hub Page

When you embed a linked database view on a hub page (like a client page showing that client's projects), you can add filters that automatically scope it to just that client's records. Use a Relation property filter: "Client contains [current page]" — when set up correctly, each client page shows only their projects.

Sharing Filters with Guests

When you share a Notion page with a guest or the public, they see the database with whichever view is active when they view the page. You can create a specific "Public View" with filters that show only published or approved records, and share only that view. Guests cannot see other views unless they have edit access.

Filter + Group = Ultimate Board View

Combining filters with the Group By option creates powerful Kanban-style boards scoped to exactly what matters. Example: filter by Assignee = Me, then group by Status. You see your personal Kanban board — nobody else's tasks, just yours, organized by stage.

FAQ: Notion Filters

How do I filter a Notion database?

Open a Notion database, click the Filter button at the top right of the view, click Add Filter, select a property, choose a condition (like "contains" or "is"), and enter a value. The filter applies immediately and only shows rows that match the condition. You can add multiple filters and save the view for future use.

How do I use OR in Notion filters?

Click Filter, add your first condition, then click Add Filter again. By default, new filters use AND logic. To switch to OR, look for the "And" text between filter conditions — you can click it to toggle to "Or". For more complex OR logic across groups of conditions, use the Add Filter Group option to create separate filter groups that are combined with OR between groups.

Can I save filtered views in Notion?

Yes. Set your filters, then create a new view by clicking the + next to the view tabs. Add the same filters to that view and rename it descriptively — "This Week", "My Active Tasks", "Overdue Invoices". The filters are permanently saved with the view, so clicking the tab always applies them instantly.

How do I filter by date in Notion?

Click Filter → Add Filter → select your date property → choose a condition: "is before today" for overdue, "is within the next week" for upcoming, "is" for a specific date. The "is within" conditions are dynamic — "next week" always means the next 7 days from today, so the filter stays accurate without manual updating.

What is the difference between Notion filter and search?

Notion filters apply to a specific database view and show/hide rows based on property values — they're persistent, saveable, and update dynamically. Notion search (Cmd+P or Ctrl+P) does full-text search across your entire workspace, finding pages by their content or title. Use filters for ongoing curated views; use search for one-off lookups when you're trying to find a specific page.
