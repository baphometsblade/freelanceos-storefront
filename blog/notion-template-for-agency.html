The Best Notion Template for Agencies in 2026

Published May 2026  ·  Last updated: May 2026  ·  7 min read

Running a small agency on a collection of spreadsheets, Slack threads, and half-remembered client context is a surprisingly common situation. It works until it doesn't — and the point where it stops working is usually when you have more than 4 clients, more than one person delivering work, and a mix of retainer and project billing happening simultaneously.

Notion solves the organization problem better than most dedicated agency tools for a specific type of agency: small (2–15 people), knowledge-work focused (design, marketing, dev, content), and running a mix of project and retainer work. If that's you, this guide covers what you actually need to build in Notion and the template that gets you there fastest.

What Agency Owners Actually Need in a Notion System

The pain points that Notion solves for agencies are specific. Before evaluating any template, be clear about which of these actually apply to your situation:

  • Client visibility across the team: Everyone needs to know the status of every client without asking someone else
  • MRR and retainer tracking: Which clients are on retainer, what are they paying, what's renewing this month
  • Project pipeline: What's in progress, what's in scope review, what's ready to invoice
  • Team workload: Who is at capacity, who has bandwidth, where are the bottlenecks
  • Project timelines: When things are due, what's at risk, what clients need updates
  • Profitability per client: Are you making margin on the accounts you're most invested in

The more of these that apply to you, the more you need a dedicated agency template rather than a general freelance one.

AgencyOS: A Full Walkthrough

AgencyOS is a purpose-built Notion template for small agencies and consultancies. Here's what's inside:

Client Dashboard

The central hub of the system. Each client record contains contact info, account status (active, onboarding, paused, churned), retainer or project type, monthly value, and relations to all active and historical projects. The dashboard view gives you a table showing every client with their MRR value and project count side by side. You can see at a glance if a client has gone quiet, which accounts are most valuable, and which are at risk of churning based on project status.

Project Pipeline

A Kanban board filtered by client and stage. Stages: discovery, scoping, active, QA, review, complete, invoiced. Each project card includes deadline, assigned team member, budget, and billed amount. The pipeline view is designed to mirror how agencies actually track client work — not just a generic task board, but a business pipeline view that connects to client records and invoicing.

MRR Tracker

One of the most valuable features of AgencyOS. The MRR tracker shows which clients are on recurring retainers, what they pay monthly, when contracts renew, and a running total of monthly recurring revenue. This is the number most small agencies don't track carefully enough, and having it in the same workspace as your project management means you can see the business health picture without switching tools.

Pro tip: Use the MRR tracker to set a renewal review date 30 days before each contract end date. This gives you a month to have a proactive conversation about renewal, rate increases, or scope changes — rather than scrambling when a client sends a "what are we doing next quarter?" email with two weeks' notice.

Team Workload View

A filtered database view showing each team member alongside their currently assigned projects, estimated hours, and capacity indicator. This is what separates AgencyOS from simpler freelance templates. When you're leading a team, the most important thing to know each Monday is: who is at capacity, who has room, and where are the bottlenecks that need to be removed. This view answers all three in one place.

Project Timelines

Notion's timeline view lets you build Gantt-style project tracking without a dedicated Gantt tool. AgencyOS uses this for active projects, showing start and end dates, milestones, and dependencies. Client-facing project status becomes a ten-second check instead of a ten-minute dig through Slack history.

Revenue and Profitability

The revenue database tracks billed amounts per project, payment status, and rolls up into per-client and per-month totals. Combined with the MRR tracker, you get a complete picture of both recurring and project revenue. The profitability module lets you input estimated vs. actual hours per project to calculate margin — critical for pricing future work accurately.
