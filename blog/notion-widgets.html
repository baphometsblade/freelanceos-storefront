The Best Notion Widgets for 2026 (Free & Premium)

Clock, weather, countdown timers, Spotify players, GitHub stats, progress bars — the top 12 Notion widgets and exactly how to embed them in your workspace.

June 2026  ·  11 min read
Home › Blog › Notion Widgets 2026

Notion's built-in database views and linked databases are powerful — but they don't do everything. A live clock, a real-time weather widget, a countdown to your project deadline, your Spotify currently playing track — none of these are native to Notion. That's where widgets come in.

Notion supports embedding any publicly accessible URL via its /embed command. An ecosystem of widget services has grown around this capability, building dynamic, auto-updating widgets designed specifically to live inside a Notion page. The result: you can build a genuinely useful dashboard that shows live data without ever leaving Notion.

This guide covers what Notion widgets are, how to add them, the 12 best widgets available in 2026, the three main widget platforms (Indify, Widgetbox, Apption.co), and specific tips for freelancers building productivity dashboards.

What Are Notion Widgets (And How Do They Work)?

Notion widgets are small web applications — hosted externally — that are embedded inside a Notion page using Notion's embed block. The widget runs in an iframe, meaning it's a live web page inside your Notion page. Because it's a live web page, it can update in real time, connect to APIs, and display dynamic content that a static Notion database cannot.

The embed block in Notion supports any publicly accessible URL. Widget services like Indify and Widgetbox take advantage of this by building widget pages that are specifically sized and styled to display well inside Notion's embed blocks. When you add a widget, Notion is simply rendering an external webpage inline — there's no official Notion widget marketplace or API integration. It's elegant in its simplicity.

This also means you can build your own widgets. If you know HTML and CSS, any webpage you host publicly (on GitHub Pages, Netlify, or Vercel for free) can be embedded in Notion. More on that later.

How to Add Any Widget to Notion

1
Create your widget on the widget service (Indify, Widgetbox, etc.). Configure the appearance, colors, and settings.
2
Copy the widget URL from the service. It will look like https://indify.co/widgets/live/clock/abc123.
3
Open your Notion page where you want the widget. Type /embed and press Enter.
4
Paste the widget URL into the embed input field and press Enter. The widget renders immediately.
5
Resize the embed block by dragging the edges. Most widgets have a minimum size — clock widgets look best at 200×200px, weather widgets at 300×200px or wider.
Color Matching Tip

Most widget services let you set a background color and text color. Set the widget background to #0f0f0f or #111111 to match Notion's dark mode, so the widget blends seamlessly into your dashboard instead of looking like a white box stuck on a dark page.

The 3 Best Notion Widget Platforms Compared

Indify
indify.co
The gold standard for Notion widgets. Indify has the largest widget library, the best dark mode support, and the cleanest design. Create an account, configure your widget (colors, font, content), and get an embed URL. Widgets auto-update without requiring page refresh.
  • Best widgets: Clock, Weather, Countdown Timer, Quote of the Day, Progress Bar, Google Calendar
  • Free tier: All widgets available, some limited to 2 widgets per type
  • Pro ($5/month): Unlimited widgets, no Indify branding, priority support
  • Dark mode: Excellent — background color is fully customizable
Widgetbox
widgetbox.app
A simpler alternative to Indify with no account required for basic widgets. Good for quick additions when you don't want to sign up for another service. Widget variety is smaller but the ones it has are well-built. The quote widget and digital clock are particularly clean.
  • Best widgets: Clock, Quote widget, Time tracker
  • Free tier: Most widgets completely free, no account needed
  • Limitation: Fewer widget types than Indify
  • Best for: Users who want a quick embed without an account
Apption.co
apption.co
Focuses on data-driven widgets — year/month progress, time trackers, and statistical displays. The year progress widget alone makes it worth bookmarking. Apption widgets have a minimal aesthetic that blends well with Notion's interface. Most are free with no account required.
  • Best widgets: Year Progress, Month Progress, Day Progress
  • Free tier: Majority of widgets are completely free
  • Design: Minimal — small file size, fast load
  • Best for: Progress and time awareness widgets

Widget Tips for Freelancers

Widgets are most valuable when they surface information you'd otherwise need to switch apps to see. For freelancers, the highest-impact dashboard widget setup is:

  • Google Calendar embed: See today's schedule without opening a new tab. This alone saves 5–10 context switches per day.
  • Countdown timer for active projects: Set one countdown per active client deadline. Deadline visibility reduces the "I thought I had more time" problem.
  • Clock with client time zone: If you work with international clients, showing their time zone at a glance prevents scheduling errors.
  • Monthly income progress bar: A progress bar manually updated to show what percentage of your monthly income target you've hit is genuinely motivating. Update it weekly when you log new invoices.
  • Pomodoro timer: Having your focus timer in the same workspace as your task list removes the friction of switching to a separate app mid-session.
Dashboard Layout Tip

Use Notion's column layout (drag blocks side by side) to place 2–3 widgets in a row at the top of your dashboard. Ideal layout: Clock (left) | Calendar Embed (center, spans 2 columns) | Countdown Timer (right). This gives you time, schedule, and deadline visibility at a single glance before scrolling to your task databases below.

Building a Custom Notion Widget (For Technical Users)

If you know basic HTML and CSS, building a custom widget gives you capabilities no pre-made service can match. Here's the pattern:

  1. Create a simple HTML file with your widget content. Use background: transparent or match Notion's dark background (#0f0f0f).
  2. Host the file on GitHub Pages (free) — push to a GitHub repo, enable Pages in Settings, and get a public URL.
  3. Embed the GitHub Pages URL in Notion using /embed.

Custom widgets you can build this way include: a revenue tracker that reads a public Google Sheets JSON export, a habit streak counter using localStorage, a branded analytics dashboard, or a live social media follower count widget using the relevant platform's public API.

The key limitation: custom widgets using JavaScript need CORS-accessible data sources. Public APIs and Google Sheets published-to-web exports work. Private APIs that require authentication won't work in a simple iframe embed.

Common Widget Mistakes to Avoid

  • Too many widgets: Each embed makes an external request. 2–3 well-chosen widgets is ideal. More than 6 on a single page will noticeably slow it down.
  • White widget on dark page: Always configure the widget background color to match Notion's dark theme. A white box on a dark dashboard looks jarring and unprofessional.
  • Widgets that break when offline: Embeds require an internet connection to load. If you work offline sometimes, don't put critical workflow information only in widgets.
  • Embedding on pages you visit infrequently: Widgets are best on your main dashboard. Embedded on an archive page, they waste load time without adding value.
  • Neglecting mobile: Widgets on the Notion mobile app are hit-or-miss. If you use Notion heavily on mobile, test every widget on your phone before building it into your main workspace.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are Notion widgets?
Notion widgets are embeddable web elements that add dynamic, live-updating content to your Notion pages. Because Notion supports embedding any web URL via the /embed command, third-party services like Indify, Widgetbox, and Apption.co create widgets specifically designed to display inside Notion — showing live clocks, weather forecasts, countdown timers, Spotify players, and more.
How do I add a widget to Notion?
Type /embed in any Notion page, then paste the widget URL from the widget service (like Indify or Widgetbox). Press Enter. The widget renders inline in your Notion page. For best results, create the widget on the service's website first, customize it to match your Notion theme, then copy the embed URL.
What is Indify Notion?
Indify (indify.co) is the most popular Notion widget service. It offers free clock, weather, countdown, quote of the day, Google Calendar, and progress bar widgets specifically designed for Notion. Free tier widgets have some limitations; the Pro plan ($5/month) removes them. The widgets auto-update without requiring page refresh.
Can you embed Spotify in Notion?
Yes. Go to any track, album, or playlist on Spotify Web, click the share icon, select 'Copy embed code', extract the src URL from the iframe code, then paste it into Notion's /embed block. The Spotify mini-player renders directly in your Notion page and plays music without leaving Notion.
Are Notion widgets free?
Most popular Notion widget services have generous free tiers. Indify is free for basic widgets (clock, countdown, weather, quotes). Widgetbox and Apption.co are also free for most widgets. Some advanced features — like removing branding or unlocking premium widget types — require paid plans, typically $4–$8/month.
Do Notion widgets slow down the page?
Embedded widgets do add some load time because each embed makes an external request to load. For dashboards with 4–6 widgets, the performance impact is minor. For pages with 10+ embeds, loading can become sluggish. Best practice: use widgets sparingly on your main dashboard.
Can I make my own Notion widget?
Yes. Any publicly accessible web page can be embedded in Notion. If you know basic HTML and CSS, you can host a simple webpage on GitHub Pages or Netlify and embed its URL in Notion. Custom widgets let you build exactly what you want — a revenue counter, a custom progress bar, or a branded team dashboard element.
What is the best Notion widget for freelancers?
The most useful Notion widgets for freelancers are: a countdown timer (to track project deadlines), a Google Calendar embed (to see your schedule without switching apps), a clock with time zone support (for international clients), and a progress bar for monthly income goals. These keep critical context visible on your dashboard without requiring app switching.
