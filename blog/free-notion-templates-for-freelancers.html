Freelance Project Hub by Thomas Frank

Category: Project management  •  Best for: Visual thinkers who prefer kanban over tables

Thomas Frank's Freelance Project Hub is one of the most duplicated free Notion templates in the ecosystem, and for good reason. It uses a three-stage kanban (Briefing, In Progress, Review/Done) with a linked client database and a task list nested inside each project. The template does one thing exceptionally well: showing you the status of every active deliverable at a glance without opening individual project pages.

What's inside:

  • Kanban project board with customizable stages
  • Linked task database with sub-tasks per project
  • Client relation (connects to a separate client database)
  • Deadline and priority fields with color-coded status
  • Timeline view for seeing all projects on a Gantt-style calendar

The main limitation: no invoice or financial tracking. Pair this with a standalone invoice template (see #5) if you don't need the full FreelanceOS system.

Where most project management templates track projects as a whole, this template tracks individual deliverables. Invoice & Finance (Templates 5–6)

5
Community

Simple Freelance Invoice Tracker

Category: Invoice management  •  Best for: Freelancers who send fewer than 10 invoices per month and want minimal setup

This template strips the invoice database down to its essential components: Invoice Number, Client (text, not a relation), Amount, Date Sent, Date Due, and Status. No formulas, no connections, no rollups. If you are just starting out and need to track whether invoices have been paid without building a full system, this works immediately with zero configuration.

The tradeoff for simplicity is manual maintenance. Without an Is Overdue formula, you have to manually change status to Overdue when payments pass due. Without a Client relation, you cannot see a client's billing history without filtering manually. Think of this as the minimum viable invoice log — useful at low volume, limiting as your client roster grows.

What's included:

  • Single-database invoice log with 6 core fields
  • Paid, Unpaid, and Overdue filter views
  • Running total via a Sum calculation at the bottom of the Amount column

Time Tracking (Templates 7–8)

7
Community

Hourly Freelance Time Logger

Category: Time tracking  •  Best for: Hourly freelancers who bill by the hour and need accurate time records per project

Notion is not a purpose-built time tracker, but this template gets it close enough for most hourly freelancers. Each time entry is a database record with Start Time, End Time, Project (relation), Client (relation), and a Duration formula that calculates hours automatically from Start and End. The billable amount column multiplies Duration by an Hourly Rate property stored on the Project record.

The weekly rollup view shows total hours per project for the current week, which is useful for checking whether you are on track to hit your target hours before a billing cycle closes.

What's included:

  • Time entry database with automatic duration calculation
  • Billable amount formula linked to per-project hourly rate
  • Weekly and monthly grouped views
  • Per-client total hours rollup for billing accuracy

Community

Project Time Budget Tracker

Category: Time budgeting  •  Best for: Fixed-price freelancers who need to track hours against a scoped budget

Fixed-price freelancers have a different time tracking problem than hourly ones: they need to know whether they are over or under the hours they budgeted when they wrote the proposal. This template solves it with two fields per project: Budgeted Hours (set when you scope the project) and Logged Hours (a rollup from linked time entries). A formula field called Hours Remaining subtracts one from the other.

When Hours Remaining goes negative, you know you are out of scope before the client asks why the project is taking so long. This single data point — real-time scope tracking — is worth more than any time tracking app subscription.

What's included:

  • Project database with budgeted vs. logged hours comparison
  • Linked time entry log for recording actual work sessions
  • Hours Remaining formula with color-coded positive/negative indicator
  • Percentage complete field: logged hours divided by budgeted hours

Freelance Content & Deliverable Calendar

Category: Content planning  •  Best for: Content freelancers (writers, social media managers, video editors) managing recurring content for multiple clients

Managing recurring content deliverables across multiple clients is one of the most admin-heavy parts of content freelancing. This template uses a single database with a Calendar view as the primary interface: every piece of content appears as an event on the calendar, color-coded by client. The Status property (Briefed, Writing, In Review, Scheduled, Published) keeps everything moving.

The Recurring toggle property is the most useful feature: mark any deliverable as recurring and it appears in a dedicated view that shows all recurring commitments sorted by day of week. You can see at a glance that you owe Client A a weekly report every Monday, Client B a newsletter every Thursday, and Client C two social posts every weekday.

What's included:

  • Calendar view for deadline visualization

Proposal & Outreach Tracker

Category: Business development  •  Best for: Freelancers actively pitching new clients and tracking their conversion rate

Every freelancer who pitches cold knows the problem: you sent a proposal two weeks ago, forgot to follow up, and the prospect hired someone else. This template fixes the follow-up gap with a database built around the proposal pipeline: Proposal Sent, Follow-up 1 Due, Follow-up 2 Due, Won/Lost, and a Lost Reason dropdown for tracking patterns.

The Win Rate summary at the top of the database is a calculated field showing proposals won divided by total proposals sent (minus those still open). When you can see that you win 30% of proposals over $5,000 but 60% under $2,000, you have data to guide your pricing and positioning decisions.

What's included:

  • Proposal database with 12 properties covering outreach through close
  • Pipeline board view (Drafting, Sent, Negotiating, Won, Lost)
  • Win rate formula and average deal size calculation
  • Lost reasons analysis view for identifying patterns

How to Choose the Right Free Template

With 12 options across six categories, the right choice depends on where your biggest admin pain point is right now. Here is the decision tree:

  • You lose track of clients and what you've promised them: Start with FreelanceOS Lite (Template 1). The client database with last-contacted tracking solves this directly.
  • Invoices go unpaid because you forget to follow up: FreelanceOS Lite again, specifically the invoice tracker with Is Overdue and Days Outstanding formulas.
  • You don't know whether fixed-price projects are profitable: Template 8 (Project Time Budget Tracker) tells you whether you are over or under scope on every project in real time.
  • You miss content deadlines for clients: Template 9 (Freelance Content Calendar) gives you a visual calendar of all client commitments.
  • You're not following up on proposals consistently: Template 10 (Proposal Tracker) automates the follow-up reminder system.
  • You don't know what to charge: Template 12 (Rate Calculator) grounds your pricing in financial reality.
Common mistake: Starting with a productivity template (Template 11) when the real problem is client or financial tracking. Feeling organized is not the same as being organized. Fix your client and invoice tracking first, then layer in planning and focus tools.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are all these Notion templates really free?
Yes. Every template on this list is genuinely free with no email required. Templates 1 and 2 are from FreelanceOS Lite, which is our free tier. The community templates (3–12) are available on the Notion template gallery or directly from their creators.
Do I need a paid Notion plan to use these?
No. All templates on this list work on Notion's free plan. Relation properties, rollups, and formulas are available on the free tier. The only Notion Free limitation that might affect you is the 1,000 block limit on the free plan — if you have a large existing workspace, you may want to check your current usage.
How do I duplicate a Notion template to my workspace?
Open the template page in your browser, click the "Duplicate" button in the top-right corner of the Notion interface (you need to be logged in to your Notion account), and select the workspace you want to copy it into. The entire template including all databases, views, and formulas copies automatically.
