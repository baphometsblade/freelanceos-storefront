What Is the Notion Web Clipper?

The Notion Web Clipper is a free browser extension that lets you save any web page directly to your Notion workspace — without copying and pasting. Install it once, and a small Notion icon appears in your browser toolbar. Every time you find an article, resource, product page, or research source you want to keep, click the icon and it saves to Notion in seconds.

Each clip creates a new page in Notion containing:

  • The page title (automatically extracted from the webpage)
  • The source URL saved as the page's web URL property
  • A text-based extract of the page's readable content
  • The date clipped (via Notion's auto-populated Created time property)

The Web Clipper is available for Chrome, Firefox, and Safari on desktop. On mobile, Notion's share sheet extension provides the same functionality.

The use case: The Web Clipper is a core tool for building a Notion second brain. Every article, case study, tutorial, or inspiration piece you find while browsing goes straight into your Notion resources library — searchable, tagged, and linked to relevant projects.

How to Install the Notion Web Clipper

Chrome (including Brave, Edge, Arc)

Step 1

Open the Chrome Web Store

Go to the Chrome Web Store and search "Notion Web Clipper" — or search Google for "Notion Web Clipper Chrome" and follow the first result to the official Notion listing.

Step 2

Click "Add to Chrome"

Click the blue "Add to Chrome" button and confirm the permission request. The Notion icon (a small 'N') appears in your Chrome toolbar. If you don't see it, click the puzzle piece icon in your toolbar and pin Notion.

Step 3

Sign in to your Notion account

Click the Notion icon in your toolbar. A sign-in prompt appears — use the same email you use for Notion on desktop. Once signed in, you are ready to clip.

Firefox

Go to the Firefox Add-ons page and search "Notion Web Clipper." Click "Add to Firefox," confirm permissions, then click the Notion icon in your toolbar and sign in. Works identically to the Chrome version.

Safari (Mac)

Open the Mac App Store, search "Notion Web Clipper," and install. In Safari, go to Settings → Extensions and enable the Notion Web Clipper. Click the Notion icon in Safari's toolbar and sign in.

How to Use the Notion Web Clipper

Once installed and signed in, the core workflow is three clicks:

Step 1

Browse to the page you want to save

Navigate to any web page — an article, a product page, a GitHub repo, a competitor's website, a recipe, a research paper, anything.

Step 2

Click the Notion icon in your toolbar

A popup appears showing: the page title (editable), your Notion workspace selector (if you have multiple workspaces), and a destination selector where the clip will be saved.

Step 3

Choose your destination and click "Save page"

Select the Notion page or database you want to save to. If you choose a database, it creates a new row. Click "Save page" and the clip appears in Notion within a few seconds.

Choosing the right destination

The destination picker shows your recent pages and lets you search for any page or database. The most efficient setup is to have one dedicated Resources database as your clipping destination. Every clip goes there first — then you tag and organize during your weekly review rather than during capture. This removes decision fatigue from the clipping moment.

Setting Up a Resources Database for Clipping

The Web Clipper is significantly more useful when you clip into a structured database rather than a blank page. Here is the optimal Resources database setup for Notion clippers.

Create a database called "Resources" or "Read Later" with these properties:

  • Title (Text, auto-populated from webpage) — the article or page name
  • URL (URL property) — the source link, auto-populated by the clipper
  • Topic (Multi-select) — tags like SEO, Marketing, Design, Development, Finance, Inspiration
  • Status (Select) — To Read / Reading / Done / Reference
  • Project (Relation → your Projects database) — link this resource to a relevant project
  • Key Insight (Text) — fill in during your review: one sentence on why you saved it
  • Clipped (Created time) — auto-populated, tells you when you saved it

Set up a "To Read" view filtered to Status = "To Read" sorted by Clipped date descending. This becomes your read-later queue. A second "Reference" view filtered to Status = "Reference" holds your evergreen resources library — things you want to keep but have already processed.

Weekly review habit: Once a week, open your "To Read" view and go through unread clips. Either read them now, add a Key Insight and change status to "Reference," or delete them if they are no longer relevant. This keeps your resources database useful instead of a graveyard of unread links.

3 Practical Clipping Workflows

Workflow 1: Research and reference library

1

Find a useful article, tutorial, or case study while browsing

2

Click the Notion clipper → save to Resources database → Status = "To Read"

3

During weekly review: read clip, add Key Insight, change Status to "Reference," add Topic tags

4

Link relevant resources to active projects via the Project Relation property

Workflow 2: Competitor and inspiration tracking

1

Browse competitor websites, design inspiration, or industry benchmarks

2

Clip each page to a dedicated "Competitor Research" or "Inspiration" database with a Category tag

3

Add a "Notes" text property and fill in observations while reviewing

4

Filter the database by Category to pull up all clips for a specific competitor or niche at once

Workflow 3: Content creation research

1

When planning a blog post or video, search for 10-15 relevant sources

2

Clip each source directly to the Notion page for that specific piece of content

3

Open each clipped page in Notion, read it, and use progressive summarization to highlight the key passages

4

Use Notion AI to summarize all clipped sources and draft an outline — turning your research into a first draft

Notion Clipper on Mobile (iPhone + Android)

There is no separate mobile version of the Notion Web Clipper, but the Notion iOS and Android apps add a native share extension that does the same thing.

How to clip on iPhone (iOS)

  1. Open any article or page in Safari (or Chrome, Firefox)
  2. Tap the Share button (the box with an arrow)
  3. Scroll the share sheet and tap Notion (if you don't see it, tap "More" and enable Notion in the share sheet)
  4. A Notion popup appears — choose your destination database or page
  5. Tap "Save" — the page is clipped to Notion

How to clip on Android

  1. Open any page in Chrome or your default browser
  2. Tap the Share button (the three-dot menu or the share icon)
  3. Tap Notion in the share options
  4. Choose your destination and save

The mobile share extension captures the page URL and title. The full article text capture is more limited on mobile than desktop — for reading-focused capture, clip on desktop and read later on mobile using your Notion read-later queue.

Best Notion Web Clipper Alternatives

The official Notion Web Clipper is simple and free but limited in what it captures and how much control you have over saved content. These alternatives offer more power for specific use cases.

Save to Notion
Chrome extension · Free + Paid

A third-party Chrome extension that offers significantly more control over Notion database properties when clipping. You can fill in Select, Multi-select, Date, and other properties directly from the clipper popup — without opening Notion. Best for: power users who clip into structured databases and want to tag content at the moment of capture.

Readwise Reader
Read-later app + Notion sync · $7.99/mo

A dedicated read-later app that captures full articles with excellent formatting. Highlights and annotations sync to Notion automatically via the native Readwise → Notion integration. Best for: heavy readers who want to highlight articles and have those highlights automatically appear in their Notion knowledge base. The cleanest article capture available.

Omnivore
Open-source read-later + Notion sync · Free

A free, open-source read-later app with a Notion integration. Captures full articles cleanly, syncs highlights to Notion, and is free for unlimited use. Best for: users who want Readwise-level capture without the monthly fee. The Notion sync is less polished than Readwise but fully functional.

Notion's native share extension (mobile)
Built-in · Free

For mobile use, the native Notion share sheet extension is built in when you install the Notion app. No additional install needed. Best for: quick mobile saves where you just need the URL and title in a Notion database or page.

Recommendation: For most Notion users, start with the official Web Clipper and "Save to Notion" together — official for simple clips, Save to Notion when you want to tag at capture time. If you do a lot of reading and highlighting, add Readwise Reader as your article layer with Notion as the knowledge base.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Notion Web Clipper?

The Notion Web Clipper is a free browser extension for Chrome, Firefox, and Safari that lets you save web pages directly to any page or database in your Notion workspace. It captures the page URL, title, and a text extract of the content and adds it as a new entry in the destination you choose.

How do I install the Notion Web Clipper?

Go to the Chrome Web Store and search "Notion Web Clipper" (or the Firefox Add-ons directory, or Mac App Store for Safari). Click "Add to Chrome" (or your browser), then click the Notion icon that appears in your toolbar and sign in with your Notion account. Setup takes under two minutes.

Where does the Notion Web Clipper save content?

When you clip a page, the clipper popup lets you choose which workspace page or database to save it to. If you choose a database, it creates a new database row. The page title, URL, and a text extract are saved automatically. The best setup is a dedicated Resources database with a "To Read" view as your default clipping destination.

Does the Notion Web Clipper save the full article text?

It saves a text-based extract of the page content along with the title and URL. The quality of the clip varies by website — pages with complex layouts may only capture a partial summary. For full-article capture with clean formatting, third-party tools like Readwise Reader or Omnivore do a better job and can sync highlights back to Notion.

Is there a Notion clipper for Safari on iPhone?

On iPhone there is no separate extension, but the Notion iOS app adds Notion to the native iOS share sheet. Tap the Share button in Safari and select Notion to save the current page to any page or database in your workspace — it works exactly like the desktop clipper.

What is the best alternative to the Notion Web Clipper?

The best alternatives in 2026 are: "Save to Notion" (Chrome extension with better database property support), Readwise Reader (full-article capture with highlight sync to Notion, $7.99/mo), and Omnivore (free open-source read-later tool with a Notion integration). If you want to capture highlights and annotations rather than just page URLs, Readwise is the most popular choice among Notion power users.
