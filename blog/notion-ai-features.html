Notion AI Features: The Complete Guide to Every Notion AI Tool in 2026

14 min read • Updated July 2026 • FreelanceOS Team

Notion AI has grown far beyond a basic writing assistant. This guide covers every Notion AI feature in 2026 — what each one does, when to use it, and the prompts that actually work.

What Is Notion AI?

Notion AI is an artificial intelligence layer built directly into Notion's workspace. Unlike external AI tools that require copying and pasting content back and forth, Notion AI lives where your work already lives — it can read your pages, query your databases, summarize your meeting notes, and draft new content without ever leaving Notion.

Notion launched its AI features in early 2023 and has expanded them significantly through 2024 and 2025. As of 2026, Notion AI includes:

  • Writing assistant — draft, edit, summarize, and improve content on any page
  • Q&A (Ask AI) — answer questions by searching across your entire workspace
  • AI Autofill — automatically populate database properties using AI
  • Meeting notes AI — transcribe, summarize, and extract action items from meetings
  • Page summarization — condense long pages into key points
  • Translation — translate page content to 20+ languages
  • Tone and style editing — rewrite text to be more professional, casual, concise, or formal

How much does Notion AI cost? Notion AI is $10/member/month (monthly) or $8/member/month (annual). It's an add-on to any Notion plan. All plans including the free tier get a limited number of AI responses before an upgrade is required.

Feature 1: Writing Assistant

The Notion AI writing assistant can generate, edit, and refine any text content directly in your Notion pages. It's the most widely used Notion AI feature and the starting point for most people.

What the Writing Assistant Can Do

GENERATE Draft content from a prompt

Give Notion AI a prompt and it will write a full draft — blog posts, project briefs, client proposals, SOPs, meeting agendas, email drafts, and more — directly in your Notion page.

EDIT Improve writing

Select any existing text and ask AI to improve it — make it clearer, more concise, more professional, or more conversational. The original text is preserved until you accept the suggestion.

EDIT Fix spelling and grammar

Select text and choose "Fix spelling & grammar" to clean up a page before sharing with a client. Works better than most browser spell checkers because it understands context.

EDIT Change tone

Rewrite selected text in a different tone: professional, friendly, casual, direct, diplomatic, confident. Useful for adapting email drafts to different clients or situations.

EDIT Summarize

Paste a long document or select a large block of text, then ask AI to summarize it into key points. Works on meeting notes, research articles, lengthy client briefs — anything too long to read quickly.

EDIT Translate

Select text and ask AI to translate it into any of 20+ languages. Useful for international clients, multilingual documentation, or translating reference materials into English.

Best Prompts for the Writing Assistant

DRAFT A PROPOSAL
"Write a professional project proposal for a freelance web design project. The client is an e-commerce company. The scope includes homepage redesign, 5 product pages, and mobile optimization. The timeline is 6 weeks and the budget is $4,500."
IMPROVE WRITING
"Make this more concise and professional. Remove any filler phrases. Keep the key information but cut the word count by 30%."
CHANGE TONE
"Rewrite this in a warmer, more human tone. It should still be professional, but less formal — like an email from a trusted colleague, not a corporate memo."
GENERATE AGENDA
"Create a meeting agenda for a 45-minute client kickoff call for a brand identity project. Include sections for introductions, project goals, timeline, deliverables, communication preferences, and next steps."

Feature 2: Q&A — Ask AI About Your Workspace

This is the most underused and arguably most powerful Notion AI feature. Q&A lets you ask natural language questions and Notion AI searches across all your workspace pages to find and synthesize the answer.

This turns your Notion workspace into a searchable knowledge base where you don't need to know where to look — you just ask.

What Q&A Can Answer

  • "What were the action items from last Thursday's team meeting?"
  • "What's the current status of the Acme Corp project?"
  • "What did we decide about the pricing strategy?"
  • "Who is our contact at Smith Industries and what's their email?"
  • "What are all the projects with a deadline in July?"
  • "What was the conclusion from the Q2 retrospective?"

How to Use Q&A

Open the AI panel with Cmd/Ctrl + J and type your question in natural language. Q&A searches your workspace and returns a synthesized answer with citations to the source pages. Click any citation to jump directly to the source.

Q&A works best when your Notion workspace is well-organized. Pages with descriptive titles, clear headings, and consistent structure give AI more surface area to search. Messy, unnested pages with poor titles reduce answer quality.

Feature 3: AI Autofill in Databases

AI Autofill is a special property type in Notion databases. When added, it automatically generates a value for the property by reading the page content or other database properties — no manual input required.

What AI Autofill Can Generate

Autofill TypeWhat It GeneratesBest Use Case
SummarizeA 1-3 sentence summary of the page contentTask database, meeting notes, project pages
Key infoBullet list of key facts from the pageResearch database, client brief pages
Action itemsList of action items extracted from the pageMeeting notes database
Custom promptAny output you define (e.g., "Extract the budget from this page")Contract review, proposal analysis
TranslateContent translated to a specified languageMultilingual content databases

Setting Up AI Autofill

  1. Open a Notion database
  2. Click + Add property
  3. Select AI custom autofill (or choose from preset options)
  4. Write your prompt — e.g., "Write a one-sentence summary of this page's main decision or outcome"
  5. Choose when to run: manually (click to generate), automatically on page creation, or automatically when specific properties change
  6. Click Save

Real-world example: A freelancer's meeting notes database has an AI Autofill property with the prompt "List all action items and who is responsible for each." After every meeting, Notion AI reads the notes and populates a clean action item list — without any manual work.

Feature 4: Meeting Notes AI

Notion's meeting notes feature integrates with Google Meet and Zoom to transcribe and analyze meetings directly inside Notion. It's one of the most powerful end-to-end AI workflows available in any productivity tool.

How Meeting Notes AI Works

  1. Create a meeting page in Notion using the built-in Meeting Notes template
  2. Connect your Google Meet or Zoom account in Notion settings
  3. Start your meeting — Notion records and transcribes the call
  4. After the meeting ends, AI automatically processes the transcript to generate: a summary, key decisions, action items with owners, and open questions
  5. The structured output appears in your Notion meeting page within minutes

What Meeting Notes AI Extracts

Meeting Summary

A paragraph-length overview of what was discussed, decisions made, and outcomes reached. Useful for people who weren't in the meeting and for future reference.

Action Items

A bulleted list of specific tasks, with the person responsible for each one, extracted from the conversation. These can be linked to your Tasks database via a Notion relation.

Key Decisions

A list of definitive decisions made during the meeting — the answers to questions that were debated and resolved. Eliminates the "wait, what did we decide about X?" problem.

Open Questions

Questions raised in the meeting that weren't resolved — requiring follow-up before the next meeting. These become your meeting prep for the next session.

Feature 5: Page Summarization

Notion AI can summarize any page in your workspace — condensing a long document, research article, SOW, or meeting transcript into a brief overview of the most important information.

How to Summarize a Page

  • Method 1: Open the page, press Space on an empty block, choose "Summarize"
  • Method 2: Open AI panel (Cmd+J), type "Summarize this page"
  • Method 3: Add an AI Autofill property with a "Summarize" prompt to generate summaries for all pages in a database

Best Use Cases for Page Summaries

  • Summarizing lengthy client briefs before a kickoff call
  • Generating TL;DRs at the top of long SOPs for team members
  • Condensing weekly updates for a dashboard overview
  • Creating abstracts for your research or blog content database

Notion AI Best Practices for Freelancers

1. Keep your page structure clean

Notion AI performs better on well-structured pages with clear headings, concise paragraphs, and organized content. Pages that are walls of unformatted text produce lower-quality AI outputs. Use H2/H3 headings, bullet lists, and toggle blocks to organize your content.

2. Use AI Autofill for your project database summaries

Add an AI Autofill property to your Projects database with the prompt: "Write a 2-sentence status summary of this project based on the page content." It creates an always-current project overview without manual updates.

3. Let AI draft first drafts, then edit

AI is fastest at generating structure and first drafts. Use it to produce a rough draft in 30 seconds, then edit and personalize. This workflow is faster than writing from scratch and fighting blank page syndrome.

4. Use Q&A before searching manually

Before digging through pages to find something, ask Notion AI Q&A. The time saved on a single "find that decision we made" search often justifies the AI subscription cost for an entire month.

5. Specify tone and audience in every prompt

Vague prompts get generic outputs. "Write a client proposal" produces something mediocre. "Write a 3-paragraph project proposal for a mobile app startup, tone: professional but not stiff, audience: non-technical founder" produces something usable. Always include who it's for and what tone it should have.
