Notion vs Trello in 2026: Which Kanban Tool Should You Use?

Pricing, features, kanban comparison, and a clear verdict for freelancers — which board tool is actually worth your time in 2026?

June 2026  ·  11 min read
Home › Blog › Notion vs Trello

Quick verdict: Trello is simpler and great for pure kanban. Notion is more powerful but has a learning curve. For freelancers who need more than a task board — client notes, databases, business docs — Notion wins clearly.

Trello is one of the most recognizable tools in productivity. The kanban board format it popularized — lists of cards that you drag from left to right — is now a standard paradigm across project management software. Simple, visual, and fast to start using.

Notion entered the scene with a completely different approach. Rather than a purpose-built kanban tool, Notion is a flexible workspace where kanban boards are just one of many views you can apply to your data. More powerful, more complex, and more capable of growing with your needs.

This comparison will help you decide which is right for where you are right now — and where you're going.

What Is Trello?

Trello is a visual kanban board tool owned by Atlassian. Cards live in lists (columns), you drag them as work progresses, and you add details, checklists, labels, and due dates to individual cards. That's the core of Trello, and it works beautifully for simple linear workflows.

Trello has added Power-Ups (integrations with external apps) and additional views like Calendar and Timeline on paid plans. But the product's DNA remains "visual board of cards." You can be productive in Trello within ten minutes of signing up — no complex setup required.

The honest limitation: Trello is not a business management system. It can't build relational databases. It can't connect a client record to their projects, invoices, and notes. Each board exists in isolation, and there's no way to build a single source of truth for an entire business within Trello alone.

What Is Notion?

Notion is a flexible all-in-one workspace combining note-taking, databases, wikis, and project management. The fundamental unit is a "page" — which can contain text, tables, databases, kanban boards, or any combination. Databases are relational, meaning a client record can link to all their projects, invoices, and files.

Notion's Board view gives you a kanban layout for any database. You can view your project tasks as a kanban board, then switch to a calendar view for scheduling, then back to a table view for status tracking — all the same data, just displayed differently. This flexibility is Notion's superpower over Trello.

Trello vs Notion for Freelancers

Trello is the right choice if you have one or two clients, straightforward projects, and zero interest in building a business system. It gets you organized in minutes. Dragging a card from "In Progress" to "Done" is genuinely satisfying. For very simple task management, Trello is efficient and perfectly adequate.

Notion is the right choice if you're managing multiple clients, need to connect your projects to client records and invoices, and want your business documentation alongside your task management. Most freelancers outgrow Trello within 3-6 months of taking on multiple clients — at which point migrating to Notion under pressure is more painful than setting it up proactively.

The practical question: are your needs purely "tasks on a board," or do you need "tasks on a board connected to clients, invoices, and business docs"? If it's the latter, Notion is the better investment even if it takes longer to set up.

Moving from Trello to Notion

Migration Steps

  1. Import your Trello boards. In Notion, go to Settings → Import → Trello. Authorize your Trello account. Notion imports your boards as databases, with cards becoming rows and lists becoming status values.
  2. Clean up the import. Raw Trello imports are messy. Delete what you don't need, rename properties, and restructure the database so it matches how you think about your work.
  3. Add a template overlay. The most efficient path is to import your Trello data, then overlay a proper freelance Notion template so your workspace has real structure from day one.
  4. Connect your databases. Once your project database is set up, build a client database and link them. This is the moment Notion becomes more powerful than Trello ever was.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Notion better than Trello?

Notion is more powerful overall, but Trello is simpler and faster to start. Trello wins for pure kanban simplicity. Notion wins for everything else: databases, knowledge base, multiple views, client portals, and all-in-one workspace. For freelancers who need more than task management, Notion is the better long-term investment.

Can I migrate from Trello to Notion?

Yes. Notion has a built-in Trello importer. Go to Settings → Import → Trello and authorize your account. Notion imports your boards as databases. The import takes minutes; cleanup and restructuring takes an hour or two.

Is Trello free?

Trello has a free tier with unlimited boards, cards, and members, limited to one Power-Up per board. The Standard paid plan starts at $5/user/month and adds unlimited Power-Ups, custom fields, and additional views like Calendar and Timeline.

Does Notion have a kanban board?

Yes. Notion's Board view provides a kanban-style layout for any database. You can group cards by status, assignee, priority, or any property, and drag cards between columns just like Trello. Notion's kanban isn't quite as polished as Trello's dedicated board, but it's functionally equivalent for most use cases.
