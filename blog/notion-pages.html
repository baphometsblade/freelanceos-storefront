What is a Notion Page?

At its core, a Notion page is the fundamental unit of Notion — a blank canvas that can hold any kind of content imaginable. Unlike a traditional document in Google Docs or Word, a Notion page is built from individual blocks: paragraphs, headings, bullet lists, code snippets, images, toggles, callouts, tables, databases, and dozens more. You can drag any block to any position, transform it into a different type, and even nest entire pages inside other pages.

Every workspace in Notion is ultimately a collection of pages. Your sidebar is a page tree. Every database row opens as a page. Even the page you're reading a Notion document on — that's a page. Mastering how pages work unlocks the full power of the tool.

Quick Fact

Notion pages were redesigned in 2023 to support a new full-page database view, meaning a single page can function as a document, a project board, and a calendar — all at once. This guide covers everything from the 2024–2025 Notion interface.

Think of a Notion page like a container that can hold anything: prose, structured data, embedded apps, linked references, and even other pages. That flexibility is what makes Notion so powerful — and also what trips up new users who expect it to behave like a simple word processor.

Pages vs Databases vs Blocks — What's the Difference?

Before going further, let's sort out the terminology. These three concepts underpin all of Notion:

📄
Block
The smallest unit. Every piece of content — a paragraph, image, heading, checkbox, toggle — is a block. Blocks live inside pages and can be reordered, transformed, or moved between pages.
🗒️
Page
A container for blocks. Pages can nest inside other pages, creating a hierarchical structure. Every database row is also a page you can open and edit with full block-editing powers.
🗃️
Database
A structured collection of pages-as-rows, each sharing a set of properties (dates, selects, formulas, relations). Databases live inside pages and can be shown in table, board, gallery, calendar, list, or timeline view.
🏗️
Workspace
The top-level container. Every Notion account belongs to one or more workspaces. The sidebar shows all your top-level pages and teamspaces — everything is a page underneath.

The critical distinction to remember: a page is freeform, a database is structured. You write prose on a page. You track projects, clients, or content in a database. But every row of a database is itself a page — so you get the best of both worlds.

How to Create a Notion Page — Step by Step

There are multiple ways to create a new page in Notion. Here are the most useful methods:

1

Click + New Page in the sidebar

The most obvious method. At the bottom of your sidebar, hover and click "+ New Page." A new untitled page appears at the root of your workspace. Type your title and hit Enter to start adding content.

2

Hover over a page in the sidebar and click +

Hover over any existing page in the sidebar. A + icon appears to its right. Click it to create a child page nested directly inside that parent. This is the fastest way to build a hierarchy.

3

Type /page inside any page body

Place your cursor anywhere in an existing page and type /page. Select "Page" from the command menu. An inline page block appears — click it to open and name it. This creates a sub-page without leaving your current view.

4

Turn any block into a page

Select any block (a heading, paragraph, or list item), hover over the six-dot drag handle on the left, and click "Turn into" › "Page." The block's text becomes the page title and it's nested in-place. Extremely useful for converting bullet lists into sub-pages.

5

Use the keyboard shortcut

Press Cmd+N (Mac) or Ctrl+N (Windows) to open a new page in the current workspace section. In the mobile app, tap the compose icon in the bottom bar.

6

Duplicate an existing page as a starting point

Right-click any page in the sidebar (or click its … menu) and choose "Duplicate." The copy appears immediately beneath the original with all content intact. Rename it and adapt — perfect for repeating structures like weekly reviews or client onboarding docs.

Nesting Pages and Page Hierarchy

One of Notion's most powerful features is its ability to nest pages inside pages to any depth. This lets you build a complete knowledge base, wiki, or project structure without any external folder system.

Here's how page hierarchy typically looks in a freelance workspace:

📁 Clients
  ├── 📄 Client A — Acme Corp
  │   ├── 📄 Project Brief
  │   ├── 📄 Meeting Notes
  │   └── 🗃️ Deliverables (database)
  └── 📄 Client B — Bloom Studio
      ├── 📄 Contract & SOW
      └── 📄 Invoices
📁 Projects
  └── 📄 Q3 Content Strategy
📁 Resources
  ├── 📄 Brand Guidelines
  └── 📄 Rate Card

To move a page to a new parent, drag it in the sidebar to the desired location. Drop it on top of another page to make it a child, or between pages to reorder at the same level. You can also use Cmd+Shift+P (Mac) to open the "Move to" dialog and type the destination page name.

Pro Tip

Keep your top-level sidebar clean. Aim for 5–8 top-level pages at most — use these as "category" pages (Clients, Projects, Resources, Archive). Everything else nests inside. A cluttered sidebar is the #1 reason freelancers abandon Notion setups after two weeks.

Inline Sub-Pages vs Sidebar Sub-Pages

When you type /page inside a page body, you create an inline page block. This appears as a link block inside the parent page and also shows up in the sidebar as a child. The parent-child relationship is the same either way — the difference is only how prominently it appears on the page itself. Use inline sub-pages when you want to call attention to child pages as links within a document. Use sidebar sub-pages when you want the hierarchy to exist without cluttering the page body.

Customising Pages: Icons, Covers, Width, and Small Text

Notion pages can be dressed up significantly to aid navigation and make your workspace feel intentional. Here are all the customisation options:

Page Icons

Every page can have an icon that appears in the sidebar, at the top of the page, and in any database cell that links to it. Click "Add icon" at the top of a page to choose from:

  • Emoji — the default and most common option. Fast to scan and universal.
  • Custom upload — upload a PNG, SVG, or JPG. Great for client logos or brand icons.
  • URL — paste a link to any image hosted online.

Cover Images

Click "Add cover" to show a full-width image banner at the top of the page. Options include:

  • Unsplash — search millions of royalty-free photos directly from Notion.
  • Gradients & colours — solid or gradient fills that load fast and look clean.
  • Custom upload — your own images. Notion crops to 100px tall by default; reposition by clicking "Reposition" and dragging.

Full Width Mode

By default, Notion constrains content to a reading-optimised column (~700px wide). For dense pages — dashboards, databases with many properties, reference pages — turn on Full width via the … menu at the top right. Full width is per-page so you can mix narrow (prose) and wide (database) pages in the same workspace.

Small Text Mode

Also in the … menu: Small text compresses the font and spacing, fitting more content on screen. Useful for meeting notes, daily logs, or any page where you scan rather than read. Combine with Full width for maximum information density — this is a favourite setting for project dashboards.

Design Tip

Use consistent emoji categories to make your sidebar instantly scannable. For example: 📁 for top-level folders, 👤 for client pages, 📋 for project pages, 📅 for calendar or time-based pages, and 💰 for financial tracking. Your brain learns the pattern fast.

Types of Notion Pages

While every Notion page uses the same underlying technology, certain patterns emerge based on what you put inside them. Here are the 8 most common page types you'll use:

📝
Document Page
Pure prose — meeting notes, briefs, SOWs, articles. Uses headings, paragraphs, lists, and callouts. The most basic page type and the default starting point.
🗃️
Database Page
A page whose primary content is a full-page database (table, board, etc.). Often used as a "hub" — e.g., your Projects hub or Client CRM. Set to Full width for best results.
📊
Dashboard Page
A command-centre page with multiple linked database views, metrics, and quick-access links. Uses columns, linked views, and callouts. Usually your workspace home page.
📚
Wiki Page
A reference page with links to child pages, structured into sections. Used for company wikis, SOPs, resource libraries. Heavy use of toggles and inline page links.
🗂️
Project Page
A dedicated page per project, combining a project brief doc with linked tasks, deliverables database, meeting notes, and file embeds all in one place.
👤
Client Page
A CRM-style page per client. Contains contact info, linked projects, notes history, contract links, invoice tracker, and communication log — all in one scrollable page.
📅
Daily / Weekly Note
A time-stamped document created fresh each day or week. Includes today's priorities, meeting notes, decisions, and a reflection section. Usually lives in a journal database.
🧩
Template Page
A page structure saved for reuse — either as a database template (appearing in the New button dropdown) or as a standalone page you duplicate manually each time you need it.

How to Embed Content in a Notion Page

The process is the same for any embed type:

  1. Type the relevant slash command (e.g., /video, /pdf, /embed)
  2. Paste the URL of the content when prompted
  3. Hit Enter — Notion renders the embed inline
  4. Drag the resize handle at the bottom of the embed to adjust its height
Heads Up

Not all websites support embedding due to X-Frame-Options headers. If an embed shows a blank box or an error, the site is blocking iframes. Use a screenshot or a link block (/bookmark) as an alternative to show a preview card with title and description.

Video Embeds — Loom, YouTube, and More

Video embeds are especially powerful for freelancers. Embed a Loom walkthrough video directly in your client proposal page. Embed a YouTube tutorial in your team wiki. Embed a Vimeo showreel in your portfolio database entry. Supported video platforms include YouTube, Vimeo, Loom, Streamable, and direct .mp4 file URLs.

Figma Embeds — Live Design Previews

The Figma embed renders a live, scrollable, interactive view of a Figma file or prototype right inside Notion. This is invaluable for project pages: designers can share a Figma link, embed it in the project page, and clients or stakeholders can view the design without needing a Figma account. Notion re-fetches the Figma embed live, so changes in Figma appear in Notion automatically.

@ Mentions and Inline Page Links

Notion's @ mention system is one of its most underrated features. You can reference any page, person, or date directly inside the body of another page, creating a living web of cross-links throughout your workspace.

Mentioning a Page

Type @ anywhere in a page body and start typing the name of another page. Notion searches your workspace and shows matching pages. Click to insert a linked reference — it appears as a tappable link that opens the target page. This is how you cross-reference a client page from a project page, or link your meeting notes back to the relevant project.

Pro Tip

Use @ mentions to create a "Related Pages" section at the bottom of any page. Instead of manually pasting URLs, just @mention each related page by name. If the page is renamed later, the mention updates automatically — zero maintenance.

Mentioning a Person

In a team workspace, @PersonName mentions send a Notion notification to that person and create an attribution reference. Use this in meeting notes, task descriptions, and project pages to tag owners and reviewers. Solo freelancers can skip this, but it's invaluable if you work with a team or contractors.

Mentioning a Date or Reminder

Type @today, @tomorrow, or any specific date like @July 15 to insert an inline date reference. You can also add a reminder — Notion will send you a notification at the specified time. This keeps deadlines visible inside the relevant page without needing a separate calendar app for every item.

Backlinks

Whenever another page mentions or links to a page, Notion shows a Backlinks panel at the top of that page (hidden by default — click "Backlinks" to expand). This creates a bi-directional graph of your workspace, similar to tools like Roam Research or Obsidian. For freelancers, backlinks help you instantly see which projects reference a particular client, which meeting notes reference a specific project, and which resources are cited across your wiki.

Sharing Notion Pages Publicly

Any Notion page can be shared publicly with a single toggle — no export required, no third-party tool needed. Here's how sharing works:

View only (default)
Anyone with the link can read the page. They cannot edit, comment, or duplicate. Best for client-facing deliverables, portfolios, or invoices.
Allow comments
Viewers can leave comments on blocks. Great for client feedback rounds — they don't need a Notion account to comment.
Allow editing
Anyone with the link can edit the page. Use with caution — only for trusted collaborators who don't have a Notion account.
Allow duplication
A "Duplicate to my Notion" button appears at the top of the public page. This is the mechanism used for sharing Notion templates publicly.

How to Share a Notion Page Publicly

  1. Open the page you want to share
  2. Click Share in the top-right corner of the page
  3. Toggle Share to web to ON
  4. Choose your permissions (view, comment, edit, duplicate)
  5. Copy the link and share it — anyone with the URL can access it
Privacy Note

When you share a page publicly, all sub-pages nested inside it are also accessible to anyone with the link — even if they're not linked directly. Be mindful of what's nested inside a page before making it public. Move sensitive sub-pages out first.

Using Shared Notion Pages for Client Deliverables

Freelancers use public Notion pages as a professional alternative to emailing PDFs. Instead of a Word doc attachment, you share a Notion page URL. Clients click and immediately see a well-formatted, always-up-to-date version of the deliverable. If you update the page, they see the latest version automatically — no back-and-forth with "version 3 FINAL.docx" in email threads.

Great use cases for public Notion pages with clients:

  • Project proposals and scope of work documents
  • Design briefs and brand guidelines
  • Content calendars and editorial plans
  • Progress updates and status reports
  • Onboarding wikis for new clients
  • Portfolios and case studies

Exporting Notion Pages (PDF, Markdown, HTML)

Sometimes you need a Notion page in a traditional file format — to send via email, archive offline, or import into another tool. Notion supports three export formats:

Markdown & CSVMigrating to other tools, developer-friendly backup, blog post draftsText blocks become Markdown. Databases export as CSV. Images download as files. Good for version control in Git.

How to Export a Notion Page

  1. Open the page to export
  2. Click … (three-dot menu) at the top right of the page
  3. Click Export
  4. Choose your format: PDF, Markdown & CSV, or HTML
  5. For databases, choose whether to include subpages and content scale
  6. Click Export — the file downloads to your computer
Batch Export Tip

To export your entire workspace as a backup, go to Settings & Members › Settings › Export all workspace content. This creates a full .zip archive of every page in Markdown or HTML format. Do this monthly as an offline backup — especially important for client-facing content.

Duplicating Pages as Templates

One of the most practical uses of Notion pages is as reusable templates. Instead of starting from scratch every time you onboard a new client or kick off a new project, you duplicate a template page and fill it in.

Simple Duplication Method

Build your ideal page structure once. Place it in a Templates folder in your sidebar. When you need a new version, right-click the template page and choose Duplicate. Rename the copy and move it to the right location. Fast, zero-friction.

Database Template Method (More Powerful)

If your pages live inside a database (e.g., a Projects database or Client CRM), you can create official database templates. Click the dropdown arrow next to the blue New button in any database view. Click + New template. Build out the page structure — add properties, headings, sections, checklists, embeds. Now every new database entry created from that template starts with your exact structure pre-populated.

This is how FreelanceOS is built — every project, client, and content entry uses pre-built templates so you never stare at a blank page again. See our full guide to setting up your Notion workspace for the full system.

Template Design Rule

The best template pages are 80% structure, 20% placeholder text. Include all the sections and headings, some example content as a guide, but leave the actual fields empty. Overly-filled templates slow you down at creation time — too empty and you forget what goes where.

Page Views for Databases

When a Notion page contains a database, that database can be displayed in any of six different view types. Each view shows the same underlying data differently — you can switch between them instantly without losing data.

📊
Table View
Spreadsheet-style grid. Best for seeing all properties across many entries. The default view for most databases.
📋
Board View
Kanban columns grouped by a select property (e.g., Status). Ideal for task management and pipeline tracking.
🖼️
Gallery View
Visual card grid using page cover images or a select property as thumbnail. Great for portfolios, mood boards, and resource libraries.
📅
Calendar View
Pages mapped to a date property. Shows what's due when. Perfect for content calendars and deadline tracking.
📈
Timeline View
Gantt-style horizontal bars spanning start/end dates. Invaluable for project scheduling and capacity planning across multiple projects.
📝
List View
A simple flat list of page titles with optional properties shown inline. Compact and fast for quick scanning of large databases.

You can add multiple views to a single database — for example, a Projects database might have a Table view for data entry, a Board view for status tracking, and a Timeline view for scheduling. Click + Add a view at the top left of any database to add new views without affecting existing ones.

Template Pages vs Template Buttons

Notion has two distinct "template" mechanisms and they serve different purposes. Getting them confused is one of the most common beginner mistakes.

Template Pages (Database Templates)

These live inside a database and define the structure of new entries. When someone clicks New in your Projects database, they can choose from your pre-built project page templates. Database templates can include:

  • Pre-filled property values (e.g., Status = "Not started", Priority = "Medium")
  • Pre-structured page body (sections, headings, checklists)
  • Linked database views filtered to show only this entry's related items
  • Embedded blocks like meeting note sections or deliverable checklists

Template Buttons

Template buttons (inserted with /template) are different — they're blocks that live inside any page and generate a set of blocks on demand when clicked. For example, a "Daily Log" template button might create a bulleted list with "Morning priority:", "Afternoon priority:", and "End-of-day reflection:" entries whenever you click it. Each click adds another set. Great for repeating structures within a single page (like a meeting agenda that creates new sections for each meeting).

Frequently Asked Questions About Notion Pages

What is a Notion page?
A Notion page is the core building block of Notion — an infinitely flexible document that can contain any combination of text, images, databases, embeds, and nested sub-pages. Unlike a traditional word-processor document, a Notion page is made up of individual blocks that you can drag, rearrange, and transform freely. Pages live in your sidebar and can be nested inside other pages to form a hierarchical workspace. Every database row is also a page you can open and edit.
How do I create a sub-page in Notion?
You can create a sub-page in three ways. First, click the + icon that appears when you hover over a page in the sidebar to add a child page directly beneath it. Second, type /page anywhere inside an existing page body to insert an inline page block. Third, drag an existing page from the sidebar and drop it on top of another page — Notion will make it a child automatically. Sub-pages appear indented beneath their parent in the sidebar and can be nested to any depth.
Can I share a Notion page publicly?
Yes. Open the page, click Share in the top-right corner, then toggle "Share to web." This generates a public URL that anyone can view without a Notion account. You can also allow visitors to comment, to edit, or to duplicate the page into their own workspace. Note that all sub-pages nested inside a shared page are also publicly accessible, so review what's nested inside before sharing. You can restrict access to specific people or email domains instead of making it fully public.
How do I make a template page in Notion?
There are two ways. First, you can duplicate any page and reuse the copy — simple but manual. Second, inside any database you can click the dropdown arrow next to the blue New button and choose "+ New template." This opens a template editor where you build the page structure once — including properties, sections, linked views, and placeholder content. Every new entry created from that template starts with the same layout pre-filled. Template buttons (inserted with /template) let you trigger block generation within any page for repeating structures like meeting notes or daily logs.
What's the difference between a Notion page and a database?
A Notion page is a freeform document — you write, arrange blocks, and nest content however you like. A Notion database is a structured collection of page-entries that share a set of properties (text, date, select, relation, formula, etc.) and can be viewed as a table, board, gallery, calendar, list, or timeline. Every item inside a database is itself a page that you can open and edit freely. Think of pages as documents and databases as spreadsheets whose rows are also documents. You often combine both: a page that contains multiple database views is a "dashboard page."
Can I embed other apps in a Notion page?
Yes. Notion supports rich embeds for dozens of services. Type /embed and paste a URL to embed almost anything, or use dedicated blocks: /video (YouTube, Vimeo, Loom), /figma (Figma files and prototypes), /pdf (any PDF URL), /github (GitHub Gist), /google maps, /tweet, /framer, and many more. The content renders inline inside the page so you never have to leave Notion to view it. Note that some websites block embedding via X-Frame-Options headers — use a /bookmark block as a preview card fallback for those.
How do I export a Notion page as PDF?
Click the … (three-dot) menu at the top-right of any page and choose Export. In the dialog, select PDF as the format. You can choose to include subpages and set the content scale. Click Export and Notion will generate a PDF download. Note that very large pages or pages with many databases may take a moment to render. Notion Plus and Business plans get higher-quality PDF exports with better font rendering. For bulk exports of your entire workspace, go to Settings & Members › Settings › Export all workspace content.
How do I duplicate a Notion page?
To duplicate a page, hover over it in the sidebar, click the … menu, and select Duplicate. You can also open the page, click … in the top-right page header, and choose Duplicate. The duplicate appears immediately below the original with the same title plus "Copy" appended. All sub-pages, blocks, and database entries inside are copied too. This is the quickest way to create a one-off copy; for repeatable templates use the database template feature instead, which lets you set a page as an official template accessible from the New button dropdown.
