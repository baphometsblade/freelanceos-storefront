Notion vs Evernote in 2026: Which Is Worth Your Money?

Full comparison of features, pricing, and performance — find out which note-taking app is actually better for your workflow in 2026.

June 2026  ·  12 min read
Home › Blog › Notion vs Evernote

Quick verdict: Notion is better for most people in 2026. Evernote peaked in 2015. Notion has better pricing, databases, collaboration, and an ecosystem Evernote can't match. The only reason to stay on Evernote is if you rely on its web clipper or have a decade of notes too painful to migrate.

Evernote was once the default answer for "what app should I use for notes?" In 2013, it was valued at $1 billion and had 100 million users. In 2026, it's a cautionary tale about product stagnation and pricing miscalculation — owned by a European investment firm, with a dramatically reduced free tier and a loyal but shrinking user base.

Notion launched in 2016 and has done almost everything Evernote failed to do: built a modern interface, added databases and collaboration, created a thriving template ecosystem, and maintained a generous free tier. For most note-taking and knowledge management use cases, Notion has replaced Evernote as the default recommendation.

But "most people" isn't everyone. This guide gives you the honest comparison so you can decide whether to migrate or stay.

Overview: Evernote's Decline and Notion's Rise

Evernote's problems started around 2018. The company went through multiple rounds of layoffs, a CEO change, and a significant product slowdown. In 2022, Bending Spoons — an Italian software company — acquired Evernote and immediately cut the free tier to a single device (from two). Pricing increased. Core features were removed from lower tiers. The user backlash was significant.

Meanwhile, Notion was growing rapidly. It added collaboration features, databases, an API, an AI assistant, and a template marketplace that now has tens of thousands of options. The free tier remained generous — unlimited pages and blocks for individuals. Notion became the tool that productivity creators and YouTube channels recommended, and the flywheel of community templates and guides accelerated its adoption.

The result: Evernote is still used by people who've been on it for years and haven't felt pain from the pricing changes. Notion is where new users go and where many Evernote migrants end up.

Why People Are Switching from Evernote to Notion

The migration pattern is real and well-documented across productivity communities. The three main reasons people are leaving Evernote:

  1. Pricing. Evernote's free tier now allows only one device — making it essentially unusable for anyone with both a phone and a computer. The Personal plan at $14.99/month is significantly more expensive than Notion Plus at $10/month. For freelancers and creators who are cost-conscious, the math doesn't work.
  2. Features Notion has that Evernote never will. Evernote is a note-taking app. Notion is a workspace. If you've grown beyond "storing notes" and need client management, project tracking, databases, or connected workflows, Evernote can't grow with you. Notion can.
  3. Product direction uncertainty. Evernote's ownership change and the layoffs that followed created real anxiety about the product's future. Many long-term users decided to migrate proactively rather than wait to be forced to.

The Evernote to Notion Migration Guide

3-Step Migration Process

1
Export from Evernote

In Evernote, go to File → Export Notes. Export as .enex format. You can export individual notebooks or everything at once. For large accounts, export by notebook to keep things manageable.

2
Import into Notion

In Notion, go to Settings → Import → Evernote. Authorize your Evernote account and Notion will import your notebooks directly. Alternatively, you can import the .enex files manually. Notebooks become pages; notes become sub-pages.

3
Restructure and organize

Raw imports are messy. Use this as an opportunity to delete what you don't need and restructure what remains. Convert relevant notebooks into databases for anything that benefits from filtering or sorting. Add a template overlay for ongoing workflows.

Where Evernote Still Beats Notion

Honesty matters: Evernote still has two genuine advantages. The web clipper is class-leading — it can capture full pages, article text only, bookmarks, screenshots, and emails with remarkable fidelity. If your workflow involves heavy web research and clip organization, Evernote's clipper is noticeably better than Notion's.

Search is Evernote's other strength. Its OCR technology can search the text inside images, scanned documents, and even handwritten notes. If you scan a lot of physical documents or screenshot receipts and need to search inside them, Evernote's search capabilities are genuinely superior.

For pure capture-and-search workflows — journalists, researchers, heavy web readers — Evernote still earns its subscription. For everyone else, the value proposition has eroded significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Notion better than Evernote in 2026?

Yes, for most users. Notion has better pricing, databases Evernote lacks entirely, a massive template ecosystem, and strong collaboration features. Evernote's main remaining advantages are its web clipper and document search, but these don't outweigh Notion's advantages for most use cases.

Can I migrate from Evernote to Notion?

Yes. Notion has a built-in Evernote importer. Go to Settings in Notion, select Import, choose Evernote, and authorize your account. Notion will import your notebooks and notes directly. The process takes a few minutes for most accounts.

Is Evernote still worth using in 2026?

For most people, no. Pricing has increased while feature development has stalled. The free tier allows only one device. Notion offers a more generous free tier, better features, and is actively developed. Stay on Evernote only if you rely heavily on its web clipper or have years of notes with complex organization you'd rather not migrate.

What does Notion have that Evernote doesn't?

Notion has relational databases, multiple database views (kanban, calendar, gallery, timeline), a template marketplace with thousands of options, built-in real-time collaboration, and a public API for automation. Evernote has none of these. Evernote is purely a note-taking app; Notion is an all-in-one workspace.
