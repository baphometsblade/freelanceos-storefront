Notion Calendar View: How to Use and Customize Your Calendar in Notion

Updated July 2026 · By FreelanceOS Team
Home › Blog › Notion Calendar View

Notion's Calendar View transforms any database into a visual timeline. Whether you are scheduling content, tracking project deadlines, planning client deliverables, or building a habit tracker, the calendar view lets you see your data arranged by date at a glance — and drag items to reschedule them without opening a single page.

This guide covers how to add a calendar view to any database, how to configure it, how to use it effectively, and how it compares to the standalone Notion Calendar app.

What Is Notion Calendar View?

Calendar View is one of Notion's database layouts, alongside Table, Board, List, Gallery, and Timeline. When you switch a database to Calendar View, Notion arranges your database items (rows) on a monthly grid based on a date property you choose.

Every row in your database that has a date assigned appears as a card on the calendar on that date. Items without a date assigned appear in an "No date" sidebar on the right side of the calendar.

Calendar View is most useful when:

  • You need to visualize when things are due or scheduled
  • You want to reschedule items by dragging them to a new date
  • You are planning content and want to see publish density across a month
  • You are managing multiple deadlines for a client project
  • You are tracking habits, routines, or recurring tasks by date

How to Add a Calendar View to a Notion Database

Step 1
Open your database

Navigate to any Notion database — a table, board, list, or gallery. Your database must have at least one Date property for Calendar View to display items. If it does not have a date property, add one first: click + Add a property in the table header and choose Date.

Step 2
Add the Calendar View

Click the + Add a view button at the top left of your database (next to the existing view tabs). In the view picker, click Calendar. The calendar view is created immediately and added as a new tab alongside your other views.

Step 3
Set the date property

If your database has multiple date properties, Notion uses the first one by default. To change it: click the three-dot menu next to the calendar view name, open View settings, and look for the "Show calendar by" option. Select the date property you want the calendar to use (e.g., "Publish Date" instead of "Created Date").

Quick Start

If your database has no date property, Notion will prompt you to add one when you create the Calendar View. It will create a "Date" property automatically if you confirm.

Navigating the Notion Calendar View

The Calendar View defaults to a monthly grid. Here is how to navigate it:

  • Previous / Next month — use the left and right arrows at the top of the calendar
  • Today button — click "Today" to jump back to the current month
  • No date sidebar — items without a date assigned appear in the right sidebar; drag them onto the calendar to assign a date
  • Click a date cell — clicking an empty area in any date cell creates a new database item with that date pre-filled
  • Click an item card — opens the database item's full page to edit all properties
Drag-and-Drop Scheduling

You can drag any item card from one date to another in the calendar. Notion updates the date property automatically. This makes bulk rescheduling — moving a week of content back by two days, for example — extremely fast.

Configuring Your Calendar View

Changing the Date Property

Click the three-dot menu or the "..." next to the view name. Open View settings. Under "Show calendar by," select which date property controls placement on the calendar. If you have a "Due Date" and a "Created Date" property, you can choose which one the calendar uses.

Adding Properties to Item Cards

By default, calendar items only show the item name. To show additional properties on the card (like status, assignee, or tags), click the three-dot menu, open Properties, and toggle on the properties you want visible on calendar cards. This is especially useful for a content calendar where you want to see the Platform or Content Type on each card.

Filtering Your Calendar View

Click Filter in the top toolbar to add filter conditions. Example: filter by Status = "Scheduled" to only show scheduled content on your content calendar, hiding drafts and ideas. Filters apply per-view — your table view and calendar view can have different filters on the same database.

Grouping in Calendar View

Calendar View supports a limited form of grouping — you can show items with date ranges (items that span multiple days) by using a date property with an end date. Items with a start and end date appear spanning the appropriate days on the calendar, similar to multi-day events in Google Calendar.

Best Use Cases for Notion Calendar View

Content Calendar

See all your scheduled posts, videos, and newsletters on a monthly grid. Drag to reschedule. Filter to show only what is publishing this week. The most common use case for creators.

Project Deadline Tracker

View all project milestones and deliverables by due date. Quickly spot deadline clashes and redistribute workload before it becomes a problem.

Client Deliverable Schedule

For freelancers managing multiple clients: one calendar view filtered to a single client shows all their deliverables for the month at a glance.

Editorial Calendar

Plan blog posts, newsletter issues, and podcast episodes by publish date. See gaps in your schedule and fill them without counting rows in a table.

Habit and Routine Tracker

Log daily habits as database entries with today's date. Switch to Calendar View to see your completion pattern visually across the month.

Invoice and Payment Tracking

Track invoice due dates on a calendar. Filter to "Unpaid" to see outstanding invoices by due date — a simple accounts receivable view without dedicated software.

Notion Calendar View vs. The Notion Calendar App

Notion offers two different "calendar" products, and the distinction matters:

Feature Calendar View (in database) Notion Calendar App
What it is A view layout inside a Notion database A standalone calendar app (formerly Cron) by Notion
Available layouts Monthly only Day, week, month, multi-week
Google Calendar sync No Yes — shows Google Cal events + Notion DB items together
Where you use it Inside Notion, on a page Separate app (Mac, Windows, web)
Best for Visualizing a single database by date Unified personal/work calendar with Notion integration
Cost Included in all Notion plans Free (Notion account required)

For most solo users, the in-database Calendar View is sufficient for content planning and task scheduling. The Notion Calendar app is the better choice if you want to see your Notion tasks alongside Google Calendar meetings in one view.

Tips for Getting the Most from Notion Calendar View

  • Create a dedicated "Publish Date" property — if you use the default "Date" property for multiple purposes, add a specific "Publish Date" or "Due Date" property for your calendar view to keep things clean.
  • Use date ranges for multi-day tasks — when setting a date property, enable "End date" so tasks that span multiple days display correctly as a bar across the calendar.
  • Save a filtered calendar view per project or client — instead of one cluttered calendar, create multiple calendar views with different filters (one for each client, one for each content type).
  • Color-code with tags or status — add a Status or Tag property and enable it in the calendar card properties. You can then visually scan for overdue items, drafts, and published content at a glance.
  • Combine with a Board view — keep a Board view (status-based Kanban) and a Calendar view of the same database. Use Board for workflow management, Calendar for scheduling.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add a calendar view in Notion?
Open any Notion database, click the '+ Add a view' button at the top left, type 'Calendar,' and select Calendar from the list. The calendar view appears immediately using the first Date property in your database. You can change which date property it uses in the view settings.
What is the Notion Calendar app?
Notion Calendar (previously Cron) is a standalone calendar application by Notion that connects your Google Calendar and Notion databases in a unified calendar view. It is separate from the Calendar View inside Notion databases — Notion Calendar is a dedicated app for Mac, Windows, and web that shows Notion database entries alongside your regular calendar events.
Can I see events by week or day in Notion Calendar View?
The built-in Notion Calendar View in databases only shows a monthly grid. You cannot switch it to week or day view within the database. For week and day views, use the Notion Calendar app (formerly Cron), which supports day, week, and month layouts and integrates with Google Calendar.
How do I change what date property a Notion calendar view uses?
Click the three-dot menu or 'View settings' button next to your calendar view name. Look for 'Show calendar by' and select the date property you want to use. Any Date property in the database can be used as the calendar display field.
Can I drag and drop items in Notion Calendar View?
Yes. You can drag database items from one date to another within the calendar view, and Notion will update the date property automatically. This makes it easy to reschedule content or tasks directly on the calendar without opening each item individually.
