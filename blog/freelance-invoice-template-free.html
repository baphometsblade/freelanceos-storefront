Best Free Freelance Invoice Templates in 2026 (Word, Excel, Notion & More)

Getting paid late is one of the top complaints from freelancers — and most of the time, a bad invoice process is the root cause. A professional, clearly formatted invoice reduces payment disputes, gets processed faster by client accounting departments, and signals that you run a real business. The good news: you don't need to pay for invoicing software. There are excellent free options across Word, Excel, Google Docs, Wave, and Notion.

This guide compares the five best free freelance invoice template formats in 2026, explains exactly what to include in every invoice, and shows why the Notion option is the right choice for freelancers who want their invoicing connected to their broader business system.

5 Types of Free Freelance Invoice Templates

1. Microsoft Word Templates

Word invoice templates are the most familiar option. Microsoft offers several free templates built into Word, and dozens more are available on Office.com. You fill in your details, replace the placeholder text, and print or export to PDF.

Pros: Familiar interface, no learning curve, works offline, easy to brand with your logo and colors.

Cons: No auto-calculations — you have to manually add up line items and tax. No invoice history or client tracking. Each invoice is a separate file. Numbering invoices sequentially requires manual effort. You'll end up with a folder of files named "Invoice_AcmeCorp_April_v2_FINAL.docx" within six months.

2. Excel or Google Sheets Templates

Spreadsheet invoice templates solve the auto-calculation problem — formulas handle your subtotals, tax, and totals automatically. Google Sheets templates have the added advantage of being shareable via link rather than requiring an email attachment.

Pros: Auto-calculations for line items and tax, easy to duplicate for new invoices, works in your browser with Google Sheets, free.

Cons: Doesn't look particularly professional when a client opens it. Hard to maintain a master client history — you'd need a separate tab or file for that. Exporting a clean PDF from Sheets requires extra steps. No connection to your project or client data.

3. Google Docs Templates

Google Docs invoice templates look more polished than Sheets and are easy to share with clients via link. The Google Docs template gallery includes several well-designed invoice options that you can customize with your branding.

Pros: Clean, professional look. Easy to share. Free with a Google account. Simple to customize with your logo and brand colors.

Cons: No automation whatsoever — every number is typed manually. No client history. Storing all your invoices as separate Google Docs gets messy fast. The math is on you.

4. Wave (Free Invoicing Software)

Wave is a dedicated free invoicing and accounting tool designed for freelancers and small businesses. It handles recurring invoices, online payment collection, expense tracking, and basic accounting.

Pros: Professional-looking invoices with online payment links. Client history built in. Tracks which invoices are paid, overdue, or pending. Free for invoicing and accounting basics.

Cons: Another tool to log into and maintain alongside your project management and client communication tools. No connection to your Notion workspace where your actual project work lives. The free tier is limited, and online payments charge a processing fee.

5. Notion Invoice Template

A Notion invoice template is a database where each row is an invoice, linked to your client database and project tracker. When you complete a project, you create a new invoice row, fill in the line items, and the formulas calculate the total. The entire invoice history for each client is visible in one filtered view.

Pros: Connected to your clients and projects — no duplicate data entry. Complete invoice history per client. Formulas handle calculations. Visual dashboard shows total revenue, paid invoices, overdue amounts. One workspace for everything.

Cons: Requires initial Notion setup and some familiarity with databases. Exporting as a client-facing PDF requires an extra step (though Notion's print-to-PDF works well for simple invoices).

What Every Freelance Invoice Must Include

Regardless of the format you choose, every professional freelance invoice should contain these 8 elements:

  1. Your name or business name and contact information (email, website, address)
  2. Invoice number — sequential, e.g., INV-2026-047
  3. Invoice date and payment due date (typically Net 14 or Net 30)
  4. Client name and billing address
  5. Itemized list of services with description, quantity, rate, and line total
  6. Subtotal, tax (if applicable), and total amount due
  7. Payment instructions — bank transfer details, PayPal, Stripe link, or other method
  8. Late payment terms — e.g., "Invoices unpaid after 30 days incur 1.5% monthly interest"
