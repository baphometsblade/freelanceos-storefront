Notion Integrations Guide 2026

The 25 Best Notion Integrations in 2026 (With Setup Instructions)

Stop using Notion as an isolated notebook. Connect it to Slack, Gmail, Stripe, Google Calendar, Zapier, and 20 more tools — and turn it into the operating system for your entire business.

June 2026  ·  16 min read
Home › Blog › Best Notion Integrations 2026

Notion is a powerful tool out of the box. But used in isolation — with no connections to the rest of your tool stack — it's an expensive notebook. The real transformation happens when Notion becomes the central hub that everything else flows into and out of.

Integrations are what make that happen. When your Calendly bookings automatically create Notion meeting prep pages, when every Stripe payment logs to your revenue tracker, when your Gmail client emails become Notion CRM entries — Notion stops being a place you go to manually enter data and becomes a live, connected business operating system.

This guide covers all 25 integrations worth setting up in 2026, organized into five categories: communication, productivity, file storage, automation platforms, and sales & business tools. For each one: what it does, how hard it is to set up (Easy / Medium / Technical), free or paid, and the exact steps to connect it.

How to Read This Guide

Easy = native Notion connection, takes 5 minutes. Medium = Zapier or Make, takes 15-30 minutes. Technical = Notion API or self-hosted tools, requires some coding or technical setup. Free tiers are noted — most integrations have usable free options.

Category 1: Communication Integrations

These integrations connect Notion to the tools you use to communicate with clients, teams, and prospects. Getting communication flowing into Notion means you never lose a client message, a meeting note, or a follow-up in another app.

Integration 1 of 25
1. Slack
Easy Free
Connect Slack and Notion to send Notion page updates to Slack channels, and create new Notion pages or database entries directly from Slack messages using the /notion slash command. When a team member updates a project status in Notion, the assigned Slack channel gets a notification. When someone shares a brilliant idea in Slack, it can become a Notion page in one command.

Setup: In Notion, go to Settings & Members → Connections → Slack → Connect. Authorize the Slack workspace. Then configure which Notion pages/databases send notifications to which Slack channels. In Slack, type /notion in any channel to create Notion content without leaving Slack. Total setup: 5-10 minutes.

2. Gmail
Medium Free Tier
Save important client emails to Notion automatically — no manual copy-paste. Use Zapier or Make to create a flow that triggers when you label a Gmail email (e.g., "→ Notion") and creates a Notion database entry with the subject, sender, date, and email body. Perfect for logging client communications in your CRM without leaving your inbox.

Setup: In Zapier, create a Zap with trigger "Gmail — New Labeled Email" (choose your label). Action: "Notion — Create Database Item." Map fields: Subject → Name, From → Client field, Body → Notes. Free tier handles 100 tasks/month — enough for most freelancers. Make offers 1,000 operations/month free.

3. Calendly
Medium Free Tier
When a new meeting is booked in Calendly, automatically create a Notion meeting prep page with the meeting title, attendee names, date/time, and a template of prep questions. Never show up to a client call unprepared again — your Notion meeting page is ready the moment the booking is confirmed.

Setup: In Zapier: Trigger "Calendly — New Invitee." Action "Notion — Create Page." Set the parent to your Meeting Notes database. Map: Event Name → Title, Invitee Name → Attendee, Start Time → Date. Add a fixed text block for your standard prep questions template. Takes about 20 minutes to set up.

4. Zoom
Medium Free Tier
When a Zoom meeting ends, automatically create a Notion meeting notes page pre-populated with the meeting name, participants, date, and duration. You immediately have a structured place to add notes, decisions, and action items — no more hunting for where to put the notes from a call that just finished.

Setup: In Zapier: Trigger "Zoom — Meeting Ended." Action "Notion — Create Database Item" in your Meeting Notes database. Map: Meeting Topic → Title, Start Time → Date, Duration → Duration field, Participants → Attendees. Add your standard meeting notes template as a default body text block.

5. Loom
Easy Free
Embed Loom videos directly in Notion pages — paste any Loom share URL into a Notion page, press Enter, and Notion auto-embeds the video with the title and thumbnail. Perfect for async client updates: record a Loom walkthrough, paste it into your client's Notion project page, and they can watch it with full context right there.

Setup: No configuration needed. Copy your Loom video share URL. In Notion, type /embed and paste the URL, or simply paste the URL on its own line and press Enter when the "Create embed" option appears. The video embeds in seconds. Works with any Loom link — free or paid.

Category 2: Productivity Integrations

Connect Notion to your productivity tools to keep tasks, time tracking, calendar events, and learning all flowing into one central system — without duplicating data entry across multiple apps.

6. Google Calendar
Easy Free
Sync deadlines and events from your Notion database to Google Calendar — so your project deadlines appear alongside personal appointments. Notion's built-in Calendar view (calendar.notion.so) connects directly to Google Calendar for two-way visibility. For true two-way sync where events created in Google Calendar create Notion entries, use Zapier or Make.

Setup (Native): Go to calendar.notion.so and connect your Google Calendar account. Your Notion date properties and Google Calendar events appear side-by-side. For Zapier two-way sync: Trigger "Google Calendar — New Event," Action "Notion — Create Database Item." Map title, date, and description fields. Takes 15 minutes.

7. Todoist
Medium Free Tier
Some people prefer Todoist for mobile task management (better notifications, Siri integration, widget support) but want Notion as their project and knowledge hub. Use Zapier to push tasks from Notion to Todoist when they're marked "ready" — so your Notion project tasks appear in your Todoist inbox for mobile capture and reminders.

Setup: In Zapier: Trigger "Notion — Updated Database Item" (filter: Status = Ready). Action "Todoist — Create Task." Map: Notion task name → Todoist content, due date → Todoist due date. You can also reverse this: completed Todoist tasks mark Notion items done. Takes about 20-30 minutes to set up both directions.

8. Toggl Track
Medium Free Tier
Track your time in Toggl and automatically log summaries to Notion. Use Zapier to create a daily or weekly Zap that pulls total hours by project from Toggl and adds a row to your Notion time log database. Perfect for freelancers who need accurate time records for client billing without manually copying data between apps.

Setup: In Zapier: Trigger "Toggl Track — New Time Entry." Action "Notion — Create Database Item" in your Time Log database. Map: Project → Project, Description → Task, Duration → Hours, Start Date → Date. Run this Zap for every time entry, or use a scheduled Zap that pulls weekly summaries. Toggl's free plan includes Zapier integration.

9. Readwise
Easy Paid ($7.99/mo)
Readwise's native Notion integration is one of the most powerful knowledge tools available. It automatically syncs your Kindle highlights, Instapaper saves, Pocket articles, web clippings (via Readwise Reader), Twitter/X bookmarks, and podcast notes to a Notion database — organized by source, with tags, author, and your personal commentary. This is the fastest way to build a Notion Second Brain that's actually populated with useful content.

Setup: In Readwise, go to Export → Notion → Connect. Authorize Notion and select the database where your highlights should appear. Choose your export frequency (continuous sync is best). From then on, every highlight automatically appears in Notion. Takes 5 minutes. Readwise costs $7.99/month — worth it for heavy readers.

10. Obsidian
Technical Free
Use both Notion (for projects, tasks, collaboration) and Obsidian (for local, graph-linked notes). Export Notion pages to Markdown and import them into your Obsidian vault — useful for writers who want Obsidian's graph view on content they originally created in Notion. This is a manual or script-assisted workflow, not a live sync.

Setup: In Notion: Settings → Export → Export All Workspace Content → Markdown & CSV. Download the export. Move the .md files into your Obsidian vault folder. Use a folder watch script (Python or Node) to automate future exports. Alternatively, use the community Notion-to-Obsidian converter script on GitHub. Not a live sync — best for periodic exports.

Category 3: File Storage Integrations

Keep your files, designs, and code connected to your Notion projects — so everything related to a client or project lives in one place, even when the files themselves are stored elsewhere.

11. Google Drive
Easy Free
Embed Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, and Drive folders directly in Notion pages — they open in a live preview window. For projects: use Zapier to automatically create a Google Drive folder when a new project is added to your Notion Projects database. Every new project gets its own Drive folder, linked from the Notion project page.

Embed (Easy): Copy any Google Drive link → paste into Notion → press Enter → choose "Create embed." The file previews in Notion. Auto-folder creation (Medium): Zapier trigger "Notion — New Database Item" (Projects). Action "Google Drive — Create Folder." Map: Project Name → Folder Name. Link returned URL back to Notion using a second action "Notion — Update Page."

12. Dropbox
Easy Free
Share Dropbox files and folders with clients by embedding shared Dropbox links directly in Notion client pages. When you deliver a project — video files, design assets, print files, audio — the client has a Notion page with all deliverables accessible via embedded Dropbox previews. Cleaner than emailing download links.

Setup: In Dropbox, create a shared link for any file or folder. In Notion, paste the link on a new line and select "Create embed" or use the /embed command. Dropbox paper files also embed natively. For image and video files, Notion shows a preview. For folders, it shows the Dropbox folder interface.

13. GitHub
Easy Free
Paste any GitHub URL (repository, commit, PR, issue, or file) into a Notion page and it automatically renders as a rich preview card showing the repo name, description, stars, and recent activity. For development teams: link specific GitHub issues and PRs to Notion project tasks, so your team can jump from the Notion task to the GitHub code change in one click.

Setup: Paste any GitHub URL into Notion and press Enter — the preview card appears automatically. No OAuth required. For deeper integration (auto-creating Notion tasks when GitHub issues are opened): Zapier trigger "GitHub — New Issue," Action "Notion — Create Database Item." Maps issue title, body, and URL to Notion task fields.

14. Figma
Easy Free
Embed Figma frames, files, or prototypes directly in Notion pages — they appear as a live, interactive preview. Click the embed to open the full file in Figma. Perfect for design teams and freelance designers: your project page in Notion can include the live design mockup, which clients or developers can review without hunting for a Figma link in email.

Setup: In Figma, right-click a frame → Copy/Paste as → Copy link. In Notion, paste the link on an empty line and press Enter → Notion offers "Embed." Select it. The frame renders as a zoomable preview. Clicking "Open in Figma" opens the full design. Works with Figma free plans — viewers don't need a Figma account to see the embed.

15. Airtable
Medium Free Tier
Migrate data between Airtable and Notion, or keep them in sync. For teams transitioning from Airtable to Notion: export Airtable as CSV and import into a Notion database. For ongoing sync: use Zapier or Make to push new Airtable records to Notion (or vice versa) — useful when some teams use Airtable and others use Notion.

Manual Migration: In Airtable, export any table as CSV. In Notion, open your database → Import → CSV → upload the file. Notion maps columns automatically. Live Sync: Zapier trigger "Airtable — New Record," Action "Notion — Create Database Item." Map all relevant fields. Review Make's Airtable → Notion scenario templates for more complex flows.

Category 4: Automation Platforms

These are the connective tissue of your entire Notion integration stack. Automation platforms let you connect Notion to apps that don't have native integrations — and build complex multi-step workflows without writing code.

16. Zapier
Easy Free Tier
Zapier connects Notion to 5,000+ apps via point-and-click automations called Zaps. Trigger: something happens (new Stripe payment, new Calendly booking, new Gmail label). Action: something happens in Notion (create database item, update a property, create a page). Best for: non-technical users who want robust automations without code. Free tier: 100 tasks/month — enough for 3-5 active Zaps with moderate volume.

Setup: Sign up at zapier.com. Click "Create Zap." Search for your trigger app (e.g., Stripe). Choose a trigger event (e.g., "New Payment"). Connect your Stripe account. Choose "Notion" as the action app. Choose action (e.g., "Create Database Item"). Connect your Notion workspace. Map fields. Test. Turn on. Takes 15-20 minutes for a first Zap.

17. Make (formerly Integromat)
Medium Free Tier
Make is more powerful than Zapier for complex, multi-step workflows. Its visual scenario builder shows the entire flow as a diagram — easier to debug than Zapier's linear Zap editor. Make's free plan includes 1,000 operations/month. Better for: iterating over lists of items, conditional branching, error handling, and flows with 5+ steps. Also significantly cheaper than Zapier at scale.

Setup: Sign up at make.com. Click "Create a new scenario." Add modules by clicking the "+" icon — each module is a trigger, action, or filter. Notion modules include: "Watch Database Items" (trigger), "Create a Record," "Update a Record," "Search Records." Connect your Notion account via API key. Build scenarios visually and test with real data before activating.

18. n8n
Technical Free (self-hosted)
n8n is an open-source automation platform you can self-host on a VPS for free — no per-task fees, no usage limits, full data ownership. It has a visual workflow builder similar to Make, 350+ integrations including a full Notion node, and supports JavaScript code nodes for custom logic. Best for developers or technical freelancers who process high volumes of automation tasks.

Setup: Install n8n on a VPS (DigitalOcean, Hetzner, Railway.app). Run via Docker: docker run -it --rm --name n8n -p 5678:5678 n8nio/n8n. Access at localhost:5678. Add the Notion node — provide your Notion API integration token. Build workflows with the Notion node reading/writing databases. n8n Cloud (hosted) starts at $20/month if you don't want to self-host.

19. Notion API
Technical Free
Build your own custom Notion integrations using Notion's official REST API. Read and write to any Notion database, create pages, update properties, and query with filters — all programmatically. Best for: developers who want to build custom tools (a command-line time tracker, a Notion-powered personal CRM dashboard, automated weekly reports), or when no off-the-shelf automation covers your exact use case.

Setup: Go to notion.so/my-integrations → New Integration → Name it → Select workspace → Submit. Copy the Internal Integration Token. In Notion, open the database you want to access → click ··· → Connections → add your integration. Use the REST API at api.notion.com/v1/ with your token in the Authorization header. Community SDKs exist for Python (notion-client), JavaScript (@notionhq/client), and more.

20. IFTTT
Easy Free Tier
IFTTT (If This Then That) is simpler than Zapier — single trigger, single action, no multi-step flows. Fewer Notion-specific actions than Zapier but useful for simple mobile-triggered automations: "If I press a button on my phone widget, add a row to my Notion log." Good for: simple personal automations, iOS/Android widget triggers, basic RSS-to-Notion flows.

Setup: Sign up at ifttt.com. Click "Create." Choose your trigger (the "If This" — e.g., iOS Widget Button press). Choose Notion as the action (the "Then That"). Connect your Notion account. Map to a database. IFTTT free plan includes 5 active Applets. Pro plan ($2.99/month) allows unlimited Applets with more filtering options.

Category 5: Sales & Business Integrations

These integrations make Notion the command center for your business revenue, client pipeline, and sales process — so your financial data and client data are always up to date without manual entry.

21. Stripe
Medium Free Tier
Every time you receive a Stripe payment, automatically log it as a new entry in your Notion Revenue Tracker database — with the amount, customer name, product, date, and transaction ID. Over time, this builds a real-time financial dashboard in Notion: monthly revenue, top products, YTD totals — without ever opening Stripe's dashboard.

Setup: Zapier trigger "Stripe — New Payment." Action "Notion — Create Database Item" in your Revenue Tracker. Map: Amount → Amount, Customer Name → Client, Product → Product, Created → Date, Payment ID → Transaction ID. Add a formula property in Notion that calculates cumulative totals by month. Takes 25 minutes to set up — saves hours of monthly reconciliation.

22. HubSpot
Medium Paid (HubSpot)
Sync new HubSpot contacts and deals to your Notion CRM — so your team has the full client picture in Notion without switching to HubSpot for basic lookups. Use Zapier to push new HubSpot deals (stage = "Qualified") to Notion as new CRM entries, including company, contact, deal value, and stage. Best for teams that use HubSpot as the source of truth but do day-to-day work in Notion.

Setup: Zapier trigger "HubSpot — New Deal" (or "Deal Stage Updated"). Action "Notion — Create Database Item" in your Client CRM database. Map: Deal Name → Client Name, Amount → Contract Value, Stage → Status, Contact Email → Email, Close Date → Expected Date. You can also reverse-push: Notion CRM updates → HubSpot deal stage updates via a second Zap.

23. Gumroad
Medium Free Tier
Log every Gumroad sale to your Notion Revenue Tracker automatically. Track product performance, customer count, revenue by product, and daily/weekly/monthly totals — all in a Notion dashboard that updates in real time as sales come in. Essential for digital product creators selling templates, courses, or ebooks on Gumroad.

Setup: Zapier trigger "Gumroad — New Sale." Action "Notion — Create Database Item." Map: Product Name → Product, Price → Amount, Buyer Email → Customer, Sale Date → Date, Quantity → Quantity. In Notion, add a rollup property to calculate total revenue per product, and a formula to compute running monthly totals. Gumroad's free plan includes Zapier integration.

24. Typeform
Easy Free Tier
When a lead fills out your Typeform intake form, automatically create a new entry in your Notion CRM — with all the form fields (name, company, budget, project description, timeline) mapped to the right Notion properties. Never manually transfer form responses to your CRM again. Typeform has a native Notion integration that works without Zapier.

Native Integration: In Typeform, go to Connect → Notion → Connect. Select the Typeform and the Notion database. Map each question to a Notion property. Test with a submission. This is one of the few native two-app connections that doesn't require Zapier — takes 10 minutes. Typeform free plan includes 10 responses/month; Typeform Basic ($25/month) allows unlimited responses.

25. Calendly + Stripe (Full Pipeline)
Medium Paid (Tools)
The ultimate freelancer automation: when a client books a paid Calendly meeting (Calendly + Stripe integration for payment collection), two things happen automatically: (1) a new Notion meeting prep page is created for the call, and (2) the payment is logged in your Notion Revenue Tracker. The full booking → payment → onboarding pipeline runs without you lifting a finger.

Setup: Enable Calendly + Stripe integration in Calendly settings (requires Calendly Professional $12/month). Then set up two Zapier automations: (1) "Calendly — New Invitee" → "Notion — Create Meeting Prep Page." (2) "Stripe — New Payment" → "Notion — Create Revenue Entry." These two Zaps handle the full flow automatically. Add a third Zap: "Notion — Meeting Status = Completed" → "Notion — Update Revenue Entry marked as fulfilled."

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Notion have native integrations?
Yes. Notion has native integrations with Slack, Google Drive, GitHub, Figma, Jira, Asana, and Google Calendar (via Notion's Calendar feature). These are available directly in Settings > Connections. For everything else — Gmail, Stripe, Calendly, HubSpot — you use Zapier, Make, or the Notion API.
Is Zapier the best way to connect Notion?
Zapier is the easiest way for non-technical users to connect Notion to 5,000+ apps. It's point-and-click with a free tier (100 tasks/month). For more complex flows, Make (formerly Integromat) is more powerful. For technical users who want to self-host and avoid per-task fees, n8n is the best option.
Can Notion connect to Gmail?
Notion doesn't have a native Gmail integration. The best approach is using Zapier or Make to create a Zap/Scenario that triggers when you receive (or label) a Gmail email and creates a Notion database entry. This is great for logging client emails automatically.
How do I use the Notion API?
To use the Notion API: go to notion.so/my-integrations, create a new integration, give it a name and select your workspace. You'll receive an Integration Token. Share the specific Notion pages or databases you want the integration to access. Then use the REST API (or community SDKs for Python, JavaScript) to read and write to those databases using your token.
Can Notion sync with Google Calendar?
Yes. Notion has a built-in Calendar view that can show dates from any Notion database. For two-way sync with Google Calendar (where events created in Google Calendar appear in Notion), use Zapier or Make. Notion's Calendar app (calendar.notion.so) also connects directly to Google Calendar.
Is Readwise worth it for Notion users?
Readwise is extremely valuable for Notion Second Brain users. It automatically syncs your Kindle highlights, Instapaper saves, Pocket articles, and web clippings to a Notion database. Instead of manually transferring book notes, every highlight appears in Notion automatically. The native Readwise → Notion integration takes about 5 minutes to set up.
How do I embed Figma in Notion?
Paste any Figma file URL directly into a Notion page and press Enter — Notion automatically creates a live Figma embed showing your design. Click the embed to open the file in Figma. This works for Figma files, frames, and prototypes. Great for design-development collaboration without leaving Notion.
What's the best Notion integration for freelancers?
The three highest-value Notion integrations for freelancers are: (1) Calendly → Notion via Zapier, which auto-creates meeting prep pages when clients book, (2) Stripe → Notion via Zapier, which logs every payment as a database entry for financial tracking, and (3) Google Calendar ↔ Notion for deadline and schedule sync. These three automations save 3-5 hours per week for most active freelancers.
