Notion Embeds: The Complete Guide to Embedding Content in Notion

Notion's embed blocks let you bring the outside world into your workspace — watch a Loom walkthrough on an SOP page, review a Figma design on a project brief, fill out a Typeform on a client onboarding page, or view Google Maps directions for an in-person meeting. All without leaving Notion.

Notion supports embeds from 500+ services, and new ones are added regularly. This guide covers how to embed content, which services are supported, 8 high-value embed use cases, and the limitations you need to know before relying on embeds in your workflow.

1. What Are Notion Embeds?

A Notion embed block displays external content inline on a Notion page using an iframe. The embedded content is live — a YouTube video actually plays, a Figma design is actually interactive, a Typeform form is actually submittable. You're not viewing a screenshot; you're viewing the real thing.

The content comes from the external service's servers — Notion doesn't host it. This means:

  • The content is always up to date (changes in Figma are reflected in the embed immediately)
  • An internet connection is required to display embeds
  • If the external service goes down, the embed shows an error
  • The external service's own sharing and privacy settings control what's visible

2. How to Embed Content in Notion

There are three ways to add an embed to a Notion page:

Method 1: The /embed Slash Command (Most Reliable)

  1. Click on an empty line in your Notion page
  2. Type /embed and press Enter
  3. Paste the URL of the content you want to embed
  4. Press Enter or click "Embed link"
  5. Notion creates an embed block with the content loaded

Method 2: Paste a URL on an Empty Line

  1. Click on an empty line
  2. Paste a URL (Cmd+V / Ctrl+V)
  3. Notion shows a prompt with three options: "Dismiss", "Create bookmark", or "Create embed"
  4. Select "Create embed" to load the content inline

If you select "Create bookmark" instead, you get a link preview card — not a live embed. See the comparison section below for when to use each.

Method 3: Drag and Drop a Supported File

  1. Drag a PDF, image, or supported file from your computer or Finder/Explorer
  2. Drop it onto the Notion page
  3. For supported file types (PDF, images, audio files), Notion uploads and embeds the file inline
Resize any embed: After embedding, drag the bottom edge of the embed block to adjust its height. Click the left or right edge and drag to adjust width. You can make an embed full-width or column-sized depending on your page layout.

4. Eight High-Value Embed Use Cases for Freelancers

Use Case 1
Embed a Figma Design on a Project Brief Page

Every project in your Notion project tracker can have the active Figma design file embedded directly on the project page. When you open the project to check status, you can also review the latest designs without switching apps. As the design updates in Figma, the embed automatically reflects the newest version.

How to: In Figma, click Share → "Anyone with the link can view" → copy the link. In Notion, type /embed → paste the Figma URL.

Use Case 2
Embed a Loom Video Walkthrough on an SOP Page

Standard operating procedures are more effective when paired with a video walkthrough. Record your Loom, then embed it at the top of the SOP Notion page. Team members or clients see the video first, then scroll down to the written steps. The Loom embed includes a full video player — play, pause, rewind, speed controls.

How to: In Loom, copy the share URL. In Notion, paste on an empty line → select "Create embed".

Use Case 3
Embed a Typeform Intake Form on a Client Onboarding Page

When you share a Notion client hub with a new client, embed your Typeform onboarding questionnaire directly on the page. The client can fill out the form without navigating away from Notion. Responses go to Typeform (and optionally to a Notion database via Zapier). It makes the onboarding experience seamless.

How to: In Typeform, click Share → Link → copy the URL. In Notion, /embed → paste the Typeform URL.

Use Case 4
Embed a Spotify Playlist in Your Focus or Deep Work Template

Add a Spotify embed to your deep work session template. When you open your daily focus page, your go-to focus playlist is right there — hit play without opening another tab. Notion embeds the full Spotify player including playlist cover, track list, and playback controls.

How to: In Spotify web, click "..." on a playlist → Share → Copy link. In Notion, paste the link and select "Create embed".

Use Case 5
Embed a Google Sheet Budget in a Project Tracker

Keep your project financial model in Google Sheets and embed it on the Notion project page. The sheet displays inline and is fully interactive — you can scroll, click cells, and even edit values (if the sheet is shared with edit access). The most useful version: embed the budget view as a read-only snapshot for client-facing project pages.

How to: In Google Sheets, File → Share → "Anyone with the link" → copy the link. In Notion, /embed → paste.

Use Case 6
Embed a Calendly Calendar on a Client Hub Page

Share a client hub in Notion that includes your Calendly booking page embedded at the bottom. Clients can book a call directly from Notion without needing your calendar link separately. The Calendly embed shows your available times and handles booking entirely within the embed.

How to: In Calendly, click Share → Copy link. In Notion, /embed → paste the Calendly URL.

Use Case 7
Embed Google Maps for an In-Person Meeting

When you have an in-person client meeting or event, embed Google Maps with the exact location on your Notion meeting notes page. Everyone with access can see where they're going, get directions, check estimated travel time — all inline. Especially useful for Notion pages shared with a team attending the same meeting.

How to: Search the location on Google Maps → click Share → "Copy link". In Notion, /embed → paste.

Use Case 8
Embed a PDF Portfolio in a Client Pitch Page

Keep your portfolio PDF hosted on Google Drive or Dropbox. When pitching a new client, share a Notion proposal page with the PDF embedded inline. The client sees your work without downloading anything. The PDF is scrollable, zoomable, and professional. You can update the PDF and the embed refreshes automatically.

How to: Upload PDF to Google Drive → Share → "Anyone with the link" → copy the link. In Notion, /embed → paste.

5. Embed Limitations in Notion

Embeds are powerful but not unlimited. Here are the most important limitations to know:

HTTPS Required

The external URL must use HTTPS. HTTP-only URLs cannot be embedded in Notion (modern browsers block mixed content). Most sites use HTTPS by default in 2026, so this rarely causes issues — but if you have an older self-hosted tool on HTTP, it won't embed.

Websites That Block Embedding

Many websites explicitly block being embedded in iframes via the X-Frame-Options: DENY or Content-Security-Policy: frame-ancestors 'none' HTTP header. Notable sites that block Notion embeds:

  • LinkedIn (all pages)
  • Most banks and financial platforms
  • Facebook (most pages)
  • Some Google services (Google Slides and Docs work; Google Search does not)
  • Most e-commerce platforms (Shopify store pages, Amazon product pages)
  • Many government websites

When you try to embed a blocked site, Notion shows a placeholder with a broken embed icon. The only workaround is to use a bookmark instead (which shows a preview card) or to take a screenshot.

Google Drive Sharing Settings

PDFs and files on Google Drive must have sharing set to "Anyone with the link can view". If the file is restricted to specific users or your organization, the embed will show a Google sign-in prompt instead of the content — which is useless for shared Notion pages. Always check sharing settings before embedding Drive content.

Figma Share Settings

Figma designs embedded in Notion must be shared via "Anyone with the link can view" in Figma's share dialog. Designs shared only with specific email addresses will prompt viewers to request access, not load the design inline.

Embeds Don't Work Offline

Unlike native Notion content (text, databases, checklists), embeds require an internet connection to load. If you or your clients need to access Notion pages offline, use text-based content — not embeds — for critical information.

Mobile Embed Behavior

Embeds on the Notion mobile app (iOS/Android) may behave differently from desktop. Complex embeds like Figma or interactive spreadsheets may show as a link rather than a live embed on mobile — it depends on the service's mobile iframe support. Always test embeds on mobile if your Notion pages will be accessed on phones.

6. Notion Embed vs. Linked Preview vs. Bookmark

Notion has three related but different block types for external URLs:

Embed Block

Shows the actual interactive content inline. You can interact with it — play a video, scroll a PDF, fill out a form. Loads in an iframe. Best for: videos, designs, forms, PDFs you want to view or interact with.

Linked Preview

Shows a rich preview card specific to certain apps — GitHub repos show PR counts, Jira tickets show status, Figma files show a thumbnail. More structured than a generic bookmark. Best for: dev tools, project management apps with native Notion integrations.

Bookmark

Shows a URL preview card with the page title, description, and thumbnail image. Clicking opens the URL in a new tab — you're not viewing the content inline. Best for: saving reference links, external documentation, websites you want to read later.

FAQ: Notion Embeds

How do I embed a link in Notion?

There are three ways: (1) Type /embed on an empty line, paste the URL, and press Enter; (2) Paste a URL directly on an empty line — when Notion prompts you, select "Create embed"; (3) Drag and drop a supported file onto the page. For most URLs, the paste method is fastest.

What can you embed in Notion?

Notion supports embeds from 500+ services including YouTube, Vimeo, Loom (video), Figma, Miro, InVision, Marvel (design), Google Docs, Google Sheets, PDFs, Airtable (documents), GitHub Gist, CodePen, CodeSandbox (code), Spotify, SoundCloud (audio), Typeform, Calendly, Google Forms (forms), Twitter/X posts, and Google Maps. Any other HTTPS URL can also be attempted via /embed.

Why won't my Notion embed work?

Common reasons: (1) The website blocks iframe embedding (LinkedIn, most banks); (2) The URL uses HTTP instead of HTTPS; (3) For Google Drive files — the sharing must be "Anyone with the link", not restricted; (4) For Figma — the design must have link sharing enabled. When an embed fails, Notion shows a broken embed icon — hover over it to see the URL and check these common issues.

Can you embed a PDF in Notion?

Yes. Upload a PDF directly by typing /pdf or dragging the file onto the page. Notion uploads and renders it inline as a scrollable document. You can also embed PDFs hosted on Google Drive or Dropbox — just make sure the sharing is set to "Anyone with the link can view" so the embed loads without a login prompt.

What is the difference between Notion embed and bookmark?

A Notion embed shows the actual interactive content inline (a live YouTube player, a working Figma design, a scrollable PDF). A Notion bookmark shows a link preview card with the page title, description, and thumbnail — it's a clickable link, not an interactive embed. Use embeds when you want to interact with content; use bookmarks for saving links to read or visit later.
