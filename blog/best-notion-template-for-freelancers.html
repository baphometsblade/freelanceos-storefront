What Makes a Good Freelance Notion Template?

Before diving in, here's the criteria used to evaluate each template. A great freelance Notion template should:

  • Cover the actual core of freelance operations: clients, projects, invoices, revenue
  • Use relational databases so the pieces connect to each other
  • Have views that surface what you actually need day-to-day (not just tables of everything)
  • Be set up in under an hour and usable from day one
  • Not force you to maintain multiple disconnected systems

With that benchmark in mind, here are the rankings.

#2 — Runner-Up
Easlo Freelance OS
Price: $19  ·  Best for: Solo freelancers wanting a simpler setup

Easlo is one of the most well-known Notion template creators, and his Freelance OS is genuinely good. It covers client management, project tracking, and a basic invoicing view. The design is clean and approachable. It's slightly simpler than FreelanceOS Pro, which can be an advantage if you're new to Notion and don't want to be overwhelmed.

Pros
  • Clean, approachable design
  • Lower price point ($19)
  • Good client and project coverage
Cons
  • Less invoice detail than FreelanceOS
  • No revenue dashboard
  • No rate calculator
Verdict: Solid alternative. Best for freelancers who want something simpler and don't need the full revenue tracking layer.
#3
Thomas Frank Creator Notion System
Price: Free  ·  Best for: Freelancer-creators who also produce YouTube or blog content

Thomas Frank's Notion system is primarily designed for content creators but has been adopted by many freelancers because of its project management depth. It excels at task management and content tracking but lacks dedicated client invoicing and revenue dashboards. If your freelance work overlaps heavily with content creation, it's worth exploring.

Pros
  • Free and comprehensive
  • Excellent task management
  • Well-documented with video walkthroughs
Cons
  • Not built for client billing
  • Broad scope — lots to configure
  • No invoicing module
Verdict: Great free option for creator-freelancers. Doesn't replace a dedicated client management system for active billing freelancers.
#4
Practical Notion Freelance Hub
Price: $15  ·  Best for: Freelancers who want a lean, no-frills system

Practical Notion's Freelance Hub is deliberately minimal — three databases (clients, projects, invoices) connected cleanly with no extra features. It's a good choice if you find richer templates overwhelming. The lack of rollup views and revenue dashboards means you're doing more manual math, but the simplicity reduces setup friction significantly.

Pros
  • Very fast to set up
  • Clean three-database structure
  • Budget-friendly at $15
Cons
  • No revenue dashboard
  • Limited views and filters
  • Manual math required for totals
Verdict: Good for freelancers who want minimal complexity. Outgrown quickly once billing volume increases.
#5
Notion's Official Freelance Template
Price: Free  ·  Best for: Complete Notion beginners who want to start simple

Notion's own template gallery includes a basic freelance template. It covers the fundamentals — a client list, project table, and basic task management. It's functional as a starting point but lacks the relational depth and invoicing features that make a system actually useful for an active freelance business. Treat it as a proof of concept, not a production system.

Pros
  • Completely free
  • Available directly in Notion
  • Zero learning curve
Cons
  • Very basic — no relations or rollups
  • No invoicing or revenue tracking
  • Outgrown quickly
Verdict: Start here if you've never used Notion. Move to a proper template within a month of active use.

AgencyOS is built for a slightly different use case — it's designed for managing multiple clients across a small team with MRR tracking, team workload capacity, and project timeline management. If you're a solo freelancer, most of it won't apply. If you're leading a small team or managing 5+ simultaneous client accounts, it becomes relevant.

Pros
  • Built for multi-client management
  • MRR and team capacity tracking
  • Project timelines
Cons
  • Overkill for solo freelancers
  • Higher price at $49
  • Steeper learning curve

#7
Simple Client Tracker by RedGregory
Price: Free  ·  Best for: Freelancers who only need client contact management

RedGregory's client tracker is a popular free template on the Notion community gallery. It's a single database for client contacts with status tracking — essentially a lightweight CRM. Useful as a standalone contact manager but not a full freelance operating system. Good supplemental tool; not a primary workflow system.

Pros
  • Free, simple, fast to set up
Cons
  • Just one database — not a system
Verdict: Fine as a contact list. Not a freelance business system.
#8
Freelance Business Kit by Notionpages
Price: $12  ·  Best for: Freelancers who want basic project + billing coverage on a budget

A budget-priced template covering clients, projects, and a simple invoice list. Functional without being feature-rich. The visual design is polished for the price point. Lacks rollup views and revenue dashboards that make FreelanceOS Pro worth the extra $17.

Pros
  • Low price, decent coverage
Cons
  • No automated totals or rollups
Verdict: Budget option. Functional but will need augmenting as your business grows.
#9
Invoice OS Free
Price: Free  ·  Best for: Freelancers who only need invoice tracking, nothing else

A single-purpose free template focused entirely on invoice tracking. Does one thing reasonably well — tracks invoice status, due dates, and client info. No project management, no client database with history, no revenue rollups. Useful as a bolt-on to an existing system that lacks invoicing.

Pros
  • Free, focused, fast to use
Cons
  • One-dimensional — not a full system
Verdict: Good supplement to an existing Notion workspace. Not a standalone freelance system.
#10
ALUX Notion Life OS (Freelancer Edition)
Price: $99  ·  Best for: Freelancers who want a full life + business system in one

ALUX's Life OS is a comprehensive life management system that has a freelancing module embedded within it. The business management components are solid, but they come packaged with goal tracking, health logging, reading lists, and other personal development tools. If you want all of that, it's worth the price. If you want just the freelancing system, it's overkill and the $99 price reflects the full suite, not just the business portion.

Pros
  • Comprehensive life + business system
Cons
  • Expensive for just the business portion
  • Heavy — lots to configure and maintain
Verdict: Worth it only if you want the full life OS. Not the right pick if you just want freelance business management.

For most freelancers, a well-built template beats a mediocre DIY system — and the FLASH40 discount on FreelanceOS Pro at $17.40 removes the economic argument for DIY.

Pros

Cons

Verdict: Valid if you love building systems. For most freelancers, the time cost exceeds the purchase price of a quality template.
