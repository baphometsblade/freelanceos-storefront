Organization & Productivity

5 Tools
1

Notion

Free · Plus $10/mo · Best all-in-one workspace

The foundation of the FreelanceOS system. Notion handles your client database, project tracker, notes, invoices, content calendar, and personal productivity in one place. The free tier is genuinely powerful — you don't need paid for solo use. FreelanceOS Pro is built entirely in Notion and unlocks 20+ pre-built templates instantly.

2

Todoist

Free · Pro $4/mo · Best dedicated task manager

If you find Notion's task management too flexible (a real problem), Todoist gives you structure. Natural language input ("Invoice client Monday at 9am"), priority levels, recurring tasks, and clean mobile apps. The free tier handles most freelancers' needs. Integrates with Notion, Google Calendar, and Slack.

3

Toggl Track

Free tier · Starter $10/mo · Best time tracking

One-click time tracking with a browser extension, desktop app, and mobile app. The free tier includes unlimited time entries and basic reports. Essential for hourly billing, scope estimation, and understanding where your time actually goes. See more in our time tracking guide.

4

Calendly

Free · Standard $10/mo · Best booking automation

Replace "when works for you?" emails with a booking link. Calendly syncs with your Google/Outlook calendar, blocks out unavailable times, and sends automatic confirmation and reminder emails. The free tier covers one event type — more than enough for discovery calls. Saves 30+ minutes per client per week on scheduling back-and-forth.

5

Loom

Free (25 videos) · Business $12.50/mo · Best async video

Record your screen + face to explain deliverables, walk through feedback, or give project updates without scheduling a call. Clients love Loom walkthroughs — they feel personal and save everyone time. Free tier allows up to 25 videos of any length. Use it for client onboarding, deliverable presentations, and revision explanations.

Client Management

4 Tools
6

FreelanceOS Pro (CRM + Client Tracker)

$49 one-time · Best Notion-native CRM for freelancers

Built in Notion, FreelanceOS Pro includes a full CRM with pipeline tracking, client profiles, project management, invoice tracking, proposal templates, and more. One purchase, no monthly fees, and it lives in your existing Notion workspace. The closest thing to Salesforce for solo freelancers — without the $150/month price tag or the complexity.

7

HubSpot Free CRM

Free forever · Best traditional CRM for sales-focused freelancers

HubSpot's free tier is genuinely excellent — unlimited contacts, deals pipeline, email tracking, and meeting scheduling. If you do a lot of outbound prospecting and need a proper sales pipeline, HubSpot Free is hard to beat. More robust than a Notion database for pure lead management, but requires more setup and doesn't integrate as naturally into your workflow.

8

Dubsado

$20/mo · Best all-in-one client experience platform

Contracts, invoices, proposals, questionnaires, and client portals in one tool. Dubsado is particularly popular with photographers, designers, and coaches who want a fully branded client experience without stitching together separate tools. The setup time is significant, but once it's running, onboarding a new client takes minutes.

9

HoneyBook

$16/mo · Best for creative service businesses

Similar to Dubsado but with a more polished interface and stronger automation features. HoneyBook handles your entire client lifecycle from inquiry to final payment — proposals, contracts, invoices, meeting scheduling, and client communication in one streamlined platform. Popular with photographers, event planners, and wedding vendors.

Finance & Invoicing

4 Tools
10

Wave

Free forever · Best free accounting for freelancers

Wave handles invoicing, expense tracking, bank reconciliation, and basic accounting — completely free. The invoice templates are professional, automated reminders reduce late payments, and the reports give you a clear picture of your freelance income. Accepts online payments via Stripe integration (fees apply). Essential reading: our freelance invoice guide.

11

Stripe

2.9% + 30¢ per transaction · Best payment processing

The payment processor of choice for most freelancers who accept credit cards online. Create a Stripe payment link in minutes, add it to your invoices, and clients can pay by card instantly. Also enables subscriptions for retainer clients. No monthly fee — you only pay when you process a transaction. Pairs perfectly with Wave for a free invoicing + payment setup.

12

FreshBooks

From $17/mo · Best for time tracking + invoicing together

FreshBooks integrates time tracking directly into invoicing — log hours, and FreshBooks automatically generates the invoice. Great for freelancers who bill hourly and want a single tool that handles both. Also includes expense tracking, project management, and a client portal. More polished than Wave but requires a monthly subscription.

13

QuickBooks Self-Employed

$15/mo · Best for tax preparation

Purpose-built for freelancers and self-employed people. Automatically categorizes income and expenses, tracks mileage, estimates quarterly taxes, and connects directly to TurboTax at tax time. Less polished for invoicing than Wave or FreshBooks, but the tax automation alone saves most freelancers hundreds of dollars at tax season.

Design & Creative

4 Tools
14

Figma

Free tier · Professional $12/mo · Best UI/UX design tool

The industry standard for UI/UX designers and product designers. Browser-based, collaborative in real time, and the free tier allows unlimited personal files. If you do any interface design, wireframing, or prototyping, Figma is non-negotiable. The community has thousands of free UI kits, icon sets, and templates.

15

Canva

Free tier · Pro $14.99/mo · Best for non-designer freelancers

Every freelancer needs to create graphics occasionally — social media posts, proposal covers, presentation decks, email headers. Canva makes this possible without design skills. The free tier has thousands of templates. The Pro tier unlocks brand kits (keep your colors and fonts consistent everywhere), background remover, and magic resize.

16

Adobe Creative Cloud

From $54.99/mo · Best professional creative suite

If you're a creative professional (designer, photographer, videographer), Adobe CC is industry-standard and non-negotiable for certain deliverables. The full suite includes Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, Premiere, and After Effects. Expensive but essential for certain niches. You can deduct this as a business expense.

17

Unsplash / Pexels

Free forever · Best free stock photography

Two of the best free stock photo sites — no attribution required, commercially licensed. Unsplash has a more editorial, artistic feel; Pexels has a broader, more commercial range. Use both for website images, presentation decks, social media posts, and any client work that needs photography you don't have.

Writing & Content

3 Tools
18

Grammarly

Free tier · Premium $12/mo · Best writing quality tool

Every client-facing email, proposal, and deliverable you send reflects on your professionalism. Grammarly catches typos, grammar errors, and unclear sentences in real time — in Google Docs, Gmail, Notion, your browser, everywhere. The free tier catches most errors. Premium adds tone detection, clarity suggestions, and plagiarism checking.

19

Hemingway Editor

Free (web) · $19.99 desktop · Best for clear writing

Paste any text into Hemingway and it highlights sentences that are too complex, passive voice, adverbs to cut, and overall reading grade level. If you write anything for clients — blog posts, website copy, email sequences — Hemingway makes it cleaner and more effective. Use it on your own proposals and emails too.

20

Claude / ChatGPT

Free tiers · Claude Pro $20/mo · Best AI writing assistant

AI writing assistants are now part of every productive freelancer's toolkit. Use them for first drafts, brainstorming, rephrasing, proposal writing, email templates, and research summaries. Don't use AI output as a final deliverable without editing — use it to accelerate your work, not replace your judgment. Claude is particularly strong for nuanced, long-form writing.

Communication

3 Tools
21

Slack

Free tier · Pro $7.25/mo · Best client communication channel

Many clients prefer Slack over email for day-to-day communication — it's faster, less formal, and keeps project conversations organized in channels. If your clients use Slack, get on it. The free tier works well for smaller teams but limits message history. Pro tip: keep one Slack workspace per client to avoid cross-contamination.

22

Zoom

Free (40-min limit) · Pro $15.99/mo · Best video calls

Still the default video call platform for most business clients. The free tier covers 40-minute meetings — enough for most discovery calls and project check-ins. If you frequently run longer client meetings or webinars, the Pro tier removes the time limit. Integrates with Calendly for seamless booking.

23

Loom (repeated for Communication)

Free (25 videos) · Business $12.50/mo · Best async updates

Loom serves double duty — it's both a productivity tool and a communication tool. Use it to send clients progress updates, explain complex feedback, or walk through a deliverable. A 3-minute Loom video replaces a 30-minute Zoom call for most routine project updates. Clients consistently rate Loom walkthroughs as a professional differentiator.

Business Development

2 Tools
24

LinkedIn Premium

$39.99/mo · Best client prospecting platform

LinkedIn is the #1 platform for B2B freelance client acquisition. The free tier handles profile optimization and organic posting. Premium adds InMail credits to reach decision-makers directly, profile view notifications to see who's checking you out, and advanced search filters to find the exact type of client you want. Worth the cost if you're actively building your pipeline.

25

Carrd

Free · Pro $9/yr · Best quick portfolio site

Carrd builds beautiful single-page websites in under an hour. Perfect for a freelance portfolio or landing page — especially if you're not ready to invest in a full website. The Pro tier at just $9/year adds custom domains, forms, and integrations. Use it to build a simple "hire me" page that you can send to prospects before your full site is ready.

Frequently Asked Questions

What tools do freelancers need to get started?

The essential starter stack costs nothing: Notion (workspace + client tracker), Wave (invoicing + accounting), Calendly (booking), Loom (async video), Canva (graphics), and Grammarly (writing). You can run a complete professional freelance business on this free stack from day one.

What is the best project management tool for freelancers?

Notion is the best all-in-one option for most solo freelancers because it combines project tracking, client management, invoicing, and notes in one place. For dedicated project management with clients, Asana's free tier handles up to 10 team members. Trello is the best purely visual option for freelancers who think in kanban boards.

Do freelancers need a CRM?

Yes, once you have more than 3–5 active clients or leads. A CRM prevents leads from falling through the cracks, reminds you to follow up, and gives a clear view of your pipeline. For freelancers, a Notion CRM (like the one in FreelanceOS Pro) or HubSpot's free tier is more than enough.
