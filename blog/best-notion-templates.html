How to Install Any Notion Template (3 Steps)

1
Click the template link — this opens a preview page in your browser showing the template structure and any instructions. If it's a Notion-hosted template, you'll see a "Duplicate" button in the top-right corner.
2
Click "Duplicate" — you'll be prompted to log in to Notion if you aren't already. Select which workspace to duplicate the template into. For paid templates from Gumroad or other platforms, you'll receive a Notion template link after purchase — same process from there.
3
Customize and populate — the template appears in your sidebar. Start by reading any setup instructions (usually on the first page), then rename sections, delete what you don't need, and start adding your real data. Don't change the database structure until you understand how the relations work. Need help architecting a full workspace? Read our complete Notion workspace setup guide.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best free Notion templates?

The best free Notion templates include Notion's built-in task manager, weekly planner, and meeting notes templates. For freelancers, FreelanceOS offers free invoice and proposal templates. For students, the built-in Cornell Notes and assignment tracker templates are solid starting points. Most premium templates also have stripped-down free versions.

How do I install a Notion template?

Installing a Notion template takes 3 steps: (1) Click the template link — this opens Notion in your browser. (2) Click the "Duplicate" button in the top-right corner. (3) Select which workspace to duplicate it to. The template will appear in your sidebar. You can then customize it, rename pages, and add your own data.

Are paid Notion templates worth it?

Yes, if they save you more time than they cost. A $49 template that replaces 10 hours of setup work and ongoing friction is excellent value. The best premium templates are thoroughly tested, include database relations that would take days to build from scratch, and come with documentation or video walkthroughs.

Can I share a Notion template with others?

Yes. Open the page you want to share as a template, click "Share" in the top-right, enable "Share to web", then click "Template" to create a shareable duplicate link. Anyone who clicks the link can duplicate the template into their own workspace.
