Best Freelance Invoice Apps in 2026: Compared + Ranked

Invoicing is not exciting — but slow or broken invoicing eats into your income. The right tool sends professional invoices in under two minutes, accepts payment online, and lets you know who has paid and who is overdue without digging through email. This guide reviews the six most popular options for freelancers in 2026 and points you toward a free alternative if you are not ready to pay a monthly fee.

What to Look For in a Freelance Invoice App

Not all invoice apps are built for freelancers. Many are designed for agencies, accountants, or small businesses with employees. Here are the four things that matter most for solo freelancers:

  • Ease of use: You should be able to create and send an invoice in under two minutes. Anything requiring multiple screens, projects, and settings before you can send is over-engineered.
  • Recurring invoices: If you have retainer clients, automatic recurring invoices save you from doing the same task every month. This is a non-negotiable feature for retainer-based freelancers.
  • Online payment processing: Clients should be able to click a link and pay via card or bank transfer. Apps that charge 3%+ for this are taking a significant cut of your income.
  • Invoice tracking: You need to see at a glance: sent but unpaid, overdue, paid. Automatic payment reminders for overdue invoices are a bonus.

FreshBooks

FreshBooks
From $19/month
The gold standard for freelance invoicing. Beautiful invoices, strong recurring invoice support, automatic payment reminders, and time tracking built in. The most polished experience in its category.
Pros
  • Extremely clean, professional invoice templates
  • Automatic late payment reminders
  • Built-in time tracking (logs directly to invoices)
  • Strong mobile app
  • Excellent customer support
Cons
  • $19/month is steep for light users
  • Client limit on entry-tier plan (5 clients)
  • Payment processing fee: 2.9% + 30¢
  • Some features locked behind higher plans

Wave

Wave
Free (payment processing fees apply)
The most popular free invoicing tool for freelancers. Core invoicing, expense tracking, and basic accounting are completely free. You only pay when clients pay you online (transaction fee). Excellent value for new freelancers and light invoicers.
Pros
  • Completely free for invoicing and accounting
  • No client or invoice limits
  • Connects to bank accounts for expense tracking
  • Recurring invoices available
  • Works for multiple businesses
Cons
  • Credit card fee: 2.9% + 60¢ per transaction
  • Customer support is limited on free plan
  • Interface feels slightly dated vs. FreshBooks
  • Time tracking requires separate integration

Harvest

Harvest
Free (1 user, 2 projects) / $12/month Pro
Built around time tracking first, invoicing second. Excellent if you bill hourly and want tracked time to flow directly into invoices with one click. Less ideal if you do fixed-price project work.
Pros
  • Best-in-class time tracking integration
  • Timer → invoice workflow is seamless
  • Useful project budget reporting
  • Integrates with Basecamp, Slack, Asana
Cons
  • Free plan is very limited (2 projects only)
  • Less suited to fixed-price freelancers
  • No full accounting features
  • Payment processing via Stripe only

AND.CO (by Fiverr)

AND.CO
Free / $18/month Pro
AND.CO bundles invoicing with proposals, contracts, and expense tracking in one place. Strong choice if you want an all-in-one freelance business tool rather than a dedicated invoice app. Acquired by Fiverr — now integrated with the platform.
Pros
  • Invoices + contracts + proposals in one tool
  • Built-in contract e-signing
  • Recurring invoices and time tracking
  • Mobile app with solid UX
Cons
  • Free plan limited to 1 active client
  • Payment processing fee on top of subscription
  • Heavy for freelancers who only need invoicing
  • Fiverr integration may not suit non-Fiverr users

QuickBooks Self-Employed

QuickBooks Self-Employed
From $15/month
Better suited to freelancers who want tax preparation assistance alongside invoicing. Automatically categorizes expenses, estimates quarterly taxes, and exports data for tax filing. Over-engineered purely for invoicing, but valuable for the tax features.
Pros
  • Excellent automated expense categorization
  • Quarterly tax estimates and mileage tracking
  • Direct TurboTax integration
  • Strong brand credibility
Cons
  • Invoice features are basic vs. FreshBooks/Wave
  • No recurring invoices on base plan
  • Price increases quickly with upgrades
  • Overkill for invoicing-only needs

HoneyBook

HoneyBook
From $19/month
Full client management platform with invoicing, contracts, project pipelines, and client communication in one place. Most valuable for service businesses (photographers, event planners, designers) that manage complex client journeys, less ideal for pure invoicing.
Pros
  • Beautiful proposal + invoice + contract workflow
  • Client portal for each project
  • Automated follow-up sequences
  • Strong for visual service businesses
Cons
  • Expensive relative to invoicing-only needs
  • Steep learning curve
  • 3% payment processing fee
  • Platform-dependent workflow may not suit all freelancers
