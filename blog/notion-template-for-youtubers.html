6 Essential Notion Databases for Your YouTube Channel

Not all Notion templates are created equal. Many YouTube templates on the internet are essentially dressed-up to-do lists. A template that actually moves the needle for your channel needs at least these six databases, properly connected:

1. Video Pipeline Database

This is your production command center. Every video lives here from first spark to published and promoted. The key properties: title, status (Idea → Scripting → Filming → Editing → Scheduled → Published), thumbnail status, publish date, and format (long-form, Short, live). The best setups include a Kanban board view for visual production tracking and a timeline view for scheduling.

Pro tip: Add a "Repurpose Status" field so you track which videos have been clipped, transcribed for blog posts, or repurposed as Shorts. Most creators leave this on the table.

2. Script Library

A dedicated database for every script you've written or are working on. Link each script directly to its video in the pipeline database. Include a status field (Draft, Reviewed, Final), word count, and format type. The real power comes from script templates — pre-built frameworks for different video formats that you apply with one click.

3. Content Calendar

A calendar view of your video pipeline, filtered to show scheduled and upcoming uploads. The best setups include a 3-month view so you can see if you're front-heavy in one month and light in another. Add your Shorts and community posts here too so you have a complete picture of what you're publishing across formats.

4. Analytics Log

A database where you manually log performance data for each video after the first 7, 30, and 90 days. The key metrics to track: views, watch time, average view duration, CTR, and subscriber change from that video. Over time, this database becomes your most valuable strategic asset — a record of exactly what works on your specific channel.

5. Sponsor and Brand Deals CRM

Track every brand relationship from first contact to payment received. Properties include: brand name, contact name and email, rate, deliverables, deadline, deal status, and payment status. Link each deal to the video it appears in. This database alone has helped creators catch thousands of dollars in missed follow-ups.

6. SEO and Keyword Research Database

A place to store and organize keywords you're targeting, with estimated search volume, competition level, and which videos you've assigned each keyword to. Building this over time creates a topic cluster map of your niche and helps you see keyword opportunities you haven't covered yet.

How to Build a Video Production Workflow in Notion

A production workflow is the sequence of steps every video moves through before it goes live. Without a defined workflow, each video is its own chaos event. With one, production becomes a repeatable process you can speed up over time.

Here's the production workflow most successful solo creators use in Notion:

  1. Idea capture — Every video idea goes into the pipeline as "Idea" status immediately. Don't evaluate, just capture.
  2. Research and outline — When you're ready to work on a video, move it to "Research." This is where you collect sources, examples, and build the structure.
  3. Scripting — Write the full script in the linked Script Library database, following your chosen template framework.
  4. Recording — Move to "Filming" status. Link your shot list or B-roll notes here.
  5. Editing — Move to "Editing." Add your editor's name if you have one, and a delivery deadline.
  6. Thumbnail and metadata — "Post-Production" stage. Write the title variations, description, tags, and thumbnail direction here, not the night before you publish.
  7. Scheduled — Set the publish date. Move to "Scheduled."
  8. Published and promoted — After going live, log the clip to your repurposing queue and set a 7-day analytics review reminder.

Template tip: Script frameworks to include

  • The Problem-Solution Hook (works for tutorials and explainers)
  • The Curiosity Loop (works for listicles and ranking videos)
  • The Story Hook (works for personal development and motivational content)
  • The Contrarian Take (works for opinion and analysis videos)
  • The Tutorial Framework (intro, step-by-step, recap, CTA)

Tracking YouTube Analytics in Notion

YouTube Studio gives you real-time data. What it doesn't give you is a structured record of historical performance that you can query, filter, and compare across your entire video library.

A Notion analytics log fills that gap. Here's the setup that works:

Create a database with one record per video, linked to your Video Pipeline database. Each record gets a 7-day check-in and a 30-day check-in. The properties to track at each check-in: views, watch time, average view duration, CTR, and subscriber gain from that video.

Over time, sort this database by CTR or watch time to identify your best performers. Filter by video format to see whether Shorts or long-form drives more subscribers. Group by topic to identify your strongest content pillars.

This is the kind of analysis that transforms guessing into a growth strategy. Most YouTubers never do it because they have nowhere to store the data — a Notion template solves that from day one.

Managing Brand Deals with Notion

Brand deals are where many mid-size channels leave significant money on the table. A $500 deal that gets forgotten because you missed a follow-up is a real loss. An $800 deal that could have been $1,200 because you didn't have your rate history is a real loss.

A Notion brand deals CRM prevents both. The minimum viable setup:

  • Brand name and contact info — who you deal with at the company
  • Deal status — Inbound → Negotiating → Contracted → Delivered → Paid
  • Deliverables — exactly what you agreed to create
  • Rate and payment terms — what was agreed, when payment is due
  • Linked video — which video in your pipeline this integration appears in
  • Notes — brand guidelines, talking points, restrictions

Add a "Follow-up Date" field and filter the database to show any deal in the Inbound or Negotiating stage with a follow-up date in the past. That filter alone will recover deals that would otherwise die in your inbox.
