Where to Find Free Notion Templates

Knowing where to look saves hours of searching. These five sources have the best-to-noise ratio for free Notion templates:

1. Notion's Official Template Gallery
Notion maintains its own gallery at notion.so/templates with hundreds of free templates made by both Notion and verified creators. The quality is inconsistent, but filtering by category narrows it quickly. The official first-party templates (made by Notion's team) are reliably good.
Best for: getting started, official templates
2. Reddit r/Notion
The r/Notion subreddit has an active community that regularly shares free templates in posts. Search for "free template" to find threads with community-made systems. These are often creative and unconventional — great for discovering novel setups you would not find elsewhere.
Best for: creative, niche, and community-made templates
3. Gumroad Free Listings
Many template creators list their free products on Gumroad. Search for "Notion template" and filter by free. The download process requires a Gumroad account but the templates are genuinely free. Quality varies widely so read the description carefully before downloading.
Best for: creator-made polished templates
4. Individual Creator Websites
Many Notion creators offer free templates on their own websites as lead magnets. Sites like FreelanceOS, NotionEverything, and Easlo offer free versions of their paid systems. These tend to be higher quality because the creator is invested in demonstrating the value of their premium products.
Best for: high-quality free samples of premium systems
5. Twitter and X
Notion creators frequently post free templates on X (formerly Twitter) as part of growth campaigns. Search "Notion template free" and sort by recent to find new releases. Follow creators like @notionhq and popular template makers for regular free drops.
Best for: new releases, limited-time free offers

How to Evaluate If a Template Is Actually Good

Not all free Notion templates are worth duplicating. Here is a quick checklist for evaluating quality before you commit time to setting one up:

  • Does it use databases, not just pages? A genuinely useful template has linked relational databases, not just a formatted page of text. If you are tracking anything (clients, tasks, projects), you need database functionality.
  • Are views set up correctly? Good templates include multiple views of the same database — at minimum a list view and a board or calendar view. Pre-built views save significant setup time.
  • Is there a guide or instructions section? The best templates include a brief explanation of how to use them. If there are no instructions, the template may be too obvious to need them, or too complex and undocumented.
  • Does it match your workflow? A template built for a creative agency will not fit a solo freelancer perfectly. Look for templates designed for your specific use case, not the closest approximation.
  • Is it maintained? Check when the template was last updated. Notion releases new features regularly and old templates may not take advantage of recent improvements like automation, AI features, or new database views.

The 3 Templates Every Freelancer Should Install Immediately

If you are a freelancer just starting with Notion, do not try to build everything at once. Start with these three templates in order:

01
Client CRM
Your client database is the foundation of your freelance business in Notion. Every other system — projects, invoices, proposals — links back to it. Set this up first. Even a simple client CRM with name, contact info, status, and a notes section will save you hours per month compared to tracking clients in a spreadsheet or email.
02
Project Tracker
Once your clients are in Notion, link every active project to its client. A project tracker with deliverables, deadlines, status, and hours logged gives you a clear picture of your workload in seconds. This is the template that prevents you from taking on too much and missing deadlines.
03
Invoice Log
A simple invoice tracker linked to your project and client databases closes the loop on the business side. You can see at a glance what is paid, what is outstanding, and what is overdue. This is the template that most directly impacts your cash flow and is the most neglected by new freelancers.

Upgrading from Free to Premium: When Is It Worth It?

Free Notion templates cover the basics well. But there comes a point when a premium system makes economic sense. Here are the signals that you are ready to upgrade:

  • You are spending more than an hour a week maintaining your template. A premium system is designed to minimise maintenance. If you are constantly patching and rebuilding, the one-time cost of a premium template pays for itself quickly.
  • Your systems are not connected. Free templates are usually standalone. A premium system connects your CRM to projects, projects to invoices, invoices to finances, and gives you a unified dashboard. When you start noticing that you are updating the same information in multiple places, it is time to upgrade.
  • You have outgrown the template's scope. A free client CRM built for 5 clients starts to break down at 25. Premium templates are designed for scale.
  • Your income has grown enough to justify the cost. If a better system would save you 2 hours per week, and your hourly rate is $50, the system pays for itself in the first week of use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where can I download free Notion templates?
The best places to download free Notion templates are Notion's official template gallery, Reddit's r/Notion community, Gumroad free listings, and dedicated template creator sites like FreelanceOS. Each source has different quality levels — creator sites often have the most polished free options.
How do I know if a free Notion template is actually good?
A good free Notion template should have linked databases (not just static pages), clear instructions, a logical navigation structure, and views that match how you work. Avoid templates that are just formatted pages with no database functionality if you need to track anything over time.
Can I use free Notion templates for my business?
Yes. Free Notion templates are completely free to use for personal and business purposes. When you duplicate a template it becomes your own Notion page and you have full rights to use it however you want.
When should I upgrade from free to a paid Notion template?
Upgrade to a paid Notion template when you find yourself spending hours customising a free template, when the free version is missing key integrations you need, or when your business has grown to the point where a more complete system would save you significant time each week.
