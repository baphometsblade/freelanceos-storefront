50+ Free Notion Templates for 2026

Productivity, Business, Finance, Health & More — Curated and Ready to Duplicate

Updated May 2026  ·  8 min read

Setting up a system from scratch in Notion takes hours — sometimes days if you want it done properly. A well-designed template collapses that setup time to minutes. The problem is that "free Notion template" returns thousands of results, most of which are either too generic to be useful or designed to funnel you into a paid upgrade.

This list is different. Every category below contains templates that are specific enough to actually use, organized by the type of work they support. Whether you're a student tracking your reading list or a freelancer managing five clients, there's something here worth duplicating today.

1. Productivity & Daily Planning

These templates address the most common Notion use case: getting your day organized. The best ones don't just look clean — they enforce a structure that makes it harder to lose track of what matters.

Daily Planner with Time Blocks

A structured day-view with morning intentions, time-boxed work blocks, and an end-of-day capture section for what spilled over.

Best for: Anyone who feels like the day disappears without enough getting done.
Weekly Dashboard

A seven-column kanban with a rolling "this week" task view linked to a project database — so you always see context, not just a disconnected to-do list.

Best for: Knowledge workers managing multiple ongoing projects.
Pomodoro Session Tracker

Logs your 25-minute work sprints with task name, distraction notes, and a session count so you can see where your deep work hours actually went.

Best for: People who lose hours to task-switching and shallow work.
Morning Routine Builder

A checklist-plus-reflection template with habit checkboxes, a daily intention field, and a quick energy/mood score to track patterns over time.

Best for: Building a consistent start-of-day ritual that sticks.
Habit Tracker (Monthly Grid)

A visual grid that shows habit completion across the month at a glance — more motivating than a simple checklist because streaks are visible immediately.

Best for: Tracking 5–10 daily habits without building a full second brain.
Inbox Zero Processing Template

Modeled on GTD's capture-clarify-organize workflow: a raw capture inbox, a triage view, and four buckets (Do, Delegate, Defer, Delete) with filters built in.

Best for: Anyone who has 3,000 unread emails and no system for dealing with them.

2. Project Management

Notion works extremely well for project tracking once you understand its relational database model. The templates below go beyond a simple task list — they connect tasks to projects, projects to goals, and meetings to decisions.

Kanban Board with Status Tracking

A clean four-column board (Backlog, In Progress, Review, Done) with priority tags, due dates, and an assignee field for small-team use.

Best for: Solo projects or two-person teams who want visual workflow management.
Project Timeline Tracker

Uses Notion's timeline view to map deliverables across weeks with milestone markers — a lightweight alternative to Gantt software for most freelance projects.

Best for: Client work with hard deadlines and sequential dependencies.
Bug & Issue Tracker

A filtered database for software bugs, feature requests, and UX issues — with severity ratings, status, and a linked test environment field.

Best for: Solo developers or small dev teams without a full Jira setup.
Sprint Planner (2-Week Cycles)

Structures work into two-week sprints with a backlog, sprint goal, daily standup section, and a retrospective template at the end of each cycle.

Best for: Developers and product teams running lightweight agile processes.
Meeting Notes with Action Items

A structured note template that captures attendees, discussion points, decisions, and action items with assignees — all rolled up into a searchable meeting log.

Best for: Anyone tired of losing follow-up items inside Google Docs.
Decision Log

Documents significant decisions with context, options considered, final choice, and outcome — invaluable when you need to explain a past decision months later.

Best for: Founders, product managers, and team leads who make many consequential calls.

3. Business & Freelancing

The templates in this category are where Notion starts to earn its reputation as a serious business tool. The free options below are solid starting points — though they're individual templates rather than connected systems.

Client CRM (Simple)

A contacts database with client name, status (prospect / active / past), contact info, linked projects, and a notes field per client.

Best for: Freelancers managing up to 10 active clients who don't need full CRM software.
Invoice Tracker

Logs invoices with client, amount, issue date, due date, and payment status — plus a formula that flags overdue invoices automatically.

Best for: Freelancers who get paid project-by-project and need to track outstanding payments.
Lead Pipeline

A simple funnel view (Lead / Contacted / Proposal Sent / Closed Won / Closed Lost) with estimated value and last-contacted date fields.

Best for: Freelancers doing active outreach who want to see their pipeline at a glance.
Proposal Template

A structured Notion page with sections for project overview, scope, timeline, pricing table, and terms — duplicatable for each new proposal.

Best for: Consultants and agencies who send multiple proposals per month.
Time Log

A daily log of hours worked per client and project, with formula-based weekly totals and a client billing summary view.

Best for: Freelancers billing hourly who need simple time tracking without Toggl.
Business Dashboard

A home page that surfaces your key metrics — revenue this month, active clients, overdue invoices, and upcoming deadlines — from linked databases.

Best for: Freelancers who want a single "command center" instead of jumping between tools.

4. Content Creation

Content creation is chaos without structure. These templates impose the right constraints — so you spend more time writing and less time deciding what to work on next.

Editorial Content Calendar

A multi-channel calendar with content type, platform, status, and publish date — filterable by channel so you can see Instagram vs. blog vs. email in isolation.

Best for: Creators publishing across multiple platforms who need a unified content view.
YouTube Video Pipeline

Tracks videos from idea through scripting, recording, editing, and publishing — with a linked idea bank and a thumbnail concept field.

Best for: YouTubers producing 2+ videos per month who need a production workflow.
Newsletter Planner

Organizes editions with a subject line, main idea, links to reference, and a status field — with a past editions archive for repurposing ideas.

Best for: Newsletter writers who want to batch plan 4–8 issues at a time.
Social Media Idea Bank

Captures content ideas with platform tag, format (reel / carousel / thread), idea source, and a status column from draft to published.

Best for: Social media managers who want to stop starting from a blank page every week.
Podcast Episode Planner

Structures each episode with guest info, interview questions, talking points, show notes, and a promotion checklist post-publication.

Best for: Independent podcasters who juggle guest outreach, recording, and publishing.
Brand Style Guide

A Notion page capturing brand colors, typography, tone of voice, logo usage rules, and example copy — shareable with contractors and collaborators.

Best for: Small brands who need brand consistency without a formal design system.

5. Personal Finance

Notion is not a spreadsheet, but for personal finance it's surprisingly capable — especially if you just need visibility rather than automated bank feeds.

Monthly Budget Tracker

Income and expense categories with formula-based totals, a savings rate calculation, and a month-over-month comparison view.

Best for: Anyone who has never tracked their spending and wants to start without complexity.
Freelance Income & Expense Log

Categorized income and business expenses with a tax-year filter — makes quarterly estimated tax calculations far less painful.

Best for: Self-employed people who need a simple record for their accountant.
Net Worth Tracker

A snapshot-based tracker (update monthly) for assets and liabilities, with a running net worth chart using Notion's chart view.

Best for: Anyone who wants to watch their financial position improve over time.
Savings Goals Board

One card per savings goal with target amount, current amount, target date, and a visual progress bar calculated by formula.

Best for: Saving for multiple things simultaneously (emergency fund, trip, equipment).
Subscription Tracker

A database of every subscription with monthly cost, annual cost, renewal date, and a "worth it?" rating — surfaces subscription creep fast.

Best for: Anyone who suspects they're paying for things they no longer use.

6. Learning & Education

Notion is genuinely excellent for self-directed learning. The templates below create structure around the messy process of acquiring new knowledge and actually retaining it.

Book Notes Template

Captures title, author, key ideas, best quotes, personal takeaways, and an action item — one page per book, all searchable in a reading database.

Best for: Readers who finish books and then immediately forget everything in them.
Online Course Tracker

Tracks courses with platform, completion percentage, key learnings per module, and a "what I'll apply" field — stops courses from becoming passive entertainment.

Best for: Professionals investing in skills who want to ensure the learning sticks.
Language Learning Log

Vocabulary database, grammar notes, practice session log with time spent, and a list of native content consumed — structured immersion tracking.

Best for: Self-taught language learners who aren't using an app like Duolingo as their primary method.
Study Planner (Exam Prep)

Topic checklist, daily study sessions logged, past papers tracker, and a confidence rating per topic — narrows your revision to actual weak spots.

Best for: Students or professionals preparing for a certification or exam.
Reading List with Progress

Books categorized by genre and status (Want to Read / Reading / Done), with a year-read field and star rating for quick reference.

Best for: Replacing Goodreads with something you actually control.

7. Health & Fitness

Health tracking in Notion works best when you keep it simple. These templates remove friction — because anything that feels like a chore gets abandoned in week two.

Workout Log

Logs each session with exercise name, sets, reps, weight, and a notes field — filterable by muscle group and searchable for progressive overload tracking.

Best for: Gym-goers who want to track progression without a dedicated fitness app.
Weekly Meal Planner

A seven-day grid with breakfast, lunch, dinner, and a linked shopping list that aggregates ingredients from the week's meals.

Best for: Anyone who spends 20 minutes every evening deciding what to cook.
Sleep Tracker

Logs sleep time, wake time, hours slept, sleep quality rating, and notes on what affected it — reveals patterns most people don't notice.

Best for: People who know they sleep badly but don't know why.
Mental Health Journal

A daily check-in with mood rating, energy level, three things noticed today, and a free-write section — private and structurally simple to maintain.

Best for: Ongoing self-awareness practice, not crisis management.
Fitness Goal Tracker

Defines 3–5 fitness goals with target metrics, current baseline, check-in frequency, and a notes section for obstacle tracking.

Best for: People who set fitness goals in January and abandon them by February.

8. Second Brain & Personal Knowledge Management (PKM)

These are the most powerful templates on this list — and the most commonly abandoned. The key is not to over-engineer your PKM from day one. Start with one template, add the others when you hit a real limitation.

Zettelkasten Note System

Atomic notes with unique IDs, tags, and bi-directional links — plus a literature note layer that connects source material to your own ideas.

Best for: Writers, researchers, and thinkers who want to build a genuine idea network over time.
Reference Database

A structured library for articles, papers, videos, and resources — with tags, a summary field, and a "when to use this" note so you can actually find things later.

Best for: Anyone who saves interesting things and then never finds them again.
Meeting Notes (Linked to Contacts)

Meeting notes that link to a contacts database and project database — so every meeting is associated with a person and a context, not just a floating page.

Best for: Consultants and freelancers who have many client touchpoints per week.
Evergreen Notes

A curated collection of your most durable ideas — thoughts that remain true regardless of context — with a maturity status and link to source notes.

Best for: Writers and researchers building long-term intellectual capital.
Capture Inbox

A single-page fast capture view for thoughts, ideas, links, and tasks — a holding area you process once a day into the rest of your system.

Best for: Anyone who currently uses the Notes app, seven browser tabs, and a sticky note simultaneously.

Where to Find More Free Notion Templates

The templates above are a strong starting point, but the ecosystem is larger than any one list. Here's where to look next:

  • Notion's Template Gallery (notion.so/templates) — the official source, organized by category. Quality varies widely, but there are genuine gems, especially in the personal productivity section.
  • Reddit r/Notion — the most active community for template sharing. The weekly "Share Your Template" threads surface creative approaches you won't find in official galleries. Filter by "top of all time" to find proven fan favorites.
  • FreelanceOS Free Template Page (free-templates.html) — our curated free tier includes a client tracker, daily planner, and income log built specifically for freelancers and solopreneurs. All templates link together as a mini-system.
  • Notion VIP, Thomas Frank Explains on YouTube, and Red Gregory's blog are worth bookmarking for high-quality free community templates with real explanations of how they work.

One honest note: most free templates are standalone pages. They look clean but they don't connect to each other. If you find yourself duplicating five separate templates and manually trying to link them, that's the moment to consider a purpose-built template pack that's already wired together.
