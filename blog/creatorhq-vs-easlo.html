CreatorHQ vs Easlo Content OS: Which Notion Template Is Best for Content Creators? (2026)

Published April 26, 2026  ·  8 min read

If you're a content creator searching for a Notion template to run your content business, two names keep surfacing: CreatorHQ and Easlo's Content OS. Both promise to replace your scattered tools with one Notion workspace. Both have real users who swear by them.

So which one should you actually buy?

We built CreatorHQ, so we're biased — but we'll be honest about where Easlo wins. They make good products. The real question is which fits your workflow and budget.

Feature-by-Feature Breakdown

Content Calendar & Pipeline

Both templates give you a content calendar — table stakes for any creator system. You plan posts, set dates, and track status through production stages.

CreatorHQ goes deeper with a full production pipeline (Idea through Research, Script, Create, Edit, Review, Schedule, Published, Repurpose) plus time tracking per piece and a repurpose tracker. Easlo handles calendaring well. CreatorHQ's edge shows when you want to analyze your production process, not just schedule it.

Idea Capture

CreatorHQ's Idea Vault captures content ideas with priority levels, source tracking, platform tags, and an evergreen flag. Gallery view for browsing, table sorted by priority — built for that 11pm moment when you need somewhere reliable to capture a video idea.

Easlo takes a different approach with pre-built content templates. If "I keep losing content ideas" is your pain point, CreatorHQ is built for that.

Sponsorship & Brand Deal Management

CreatorHQ includes a full sponsorship CRM (Lead through Pitched, Negotiating, Contracted, Delivered, Paid) plus a rate card template and media kit checklist. Easlo includes basic sponsorship tracking. If brand deals are a meaningful revenue stream, CreatorHQ's pipeline gives you more to work with.

Revenue Tracking

CreatorHQ's Revenue Tracker breaks income into seven creator-specific streams with monthly logging, annual goals, and per-platform breakdown. Most creators have income scattered across 4-6 sources — one unified view answers: "Am I actually growing?" Easlo doesn't focus as heavily on revenue tracking.

Analytics & Growth Tracking

CreatorHQ Pro includes an Audience Growth Dashboard (monthly snapshots, growth rates, milestones) plus an Analytics Log with content scoring and an experiment tracker. Document what you tested, what worked, and refer back months later. Easlo focuses more on content management and less on growth analysis.

Where Easlo Has the Edge

To be fair, Easlo comes out ahead in several areas:

  • Affiliate management: Dedicated module for managing links, partners, and performance.
  • Content templates: Pre-built starting structures for blog posts, social captions, video scripts, and more.
  • Broader ecosystem: 40+ templates across every category. The $389 All-Access plan covers your entire Notion workspace.
  • Established brand: Larger user base means more community resources, tutorials, and third-party reviews.

The Verdict

Our honest take

Both CreatorHQ and Easlo Content OS are serious tools. Either one is a massive upgrade over scattered spreadsheets and disconnected apps.

For solo content creators who want depth — idea vault, sponsorship CRM, multi-stream revenue tracking, growth analytics — CreatorHQ delivers more at $29 (or free for Lite). You get the tools that matter most for building a content business without paying $79.

If you need affiliate management, pre-built content templates, or you're already in the Easlo ecosystem, their Content OS is a solid product worth considering.
