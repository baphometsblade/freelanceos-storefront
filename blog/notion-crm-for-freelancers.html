Why Freelancers Need a CRM (Even "Just" a Simple One)

The word "CRM" (Customer Relationship Management) sounds like something Fortune 500 companies use. But the concept is simpler than the jargon suggests: a CRM is just a system that makes sure you never drop the ball on a client relationship.

Without one, here's what happens:

  • You have a great intro call with a prospect. You mean to send a proposal. Life gets busy. Two weeks later you remember — but they've already hired someone else.
  • A past client would happily hire you again for their next project. But you never followed up after the last one, so they found someone else on Google.
  • You're negotiating rates with a new client but you can't remember what you charged their company last time — so you either undercharge again or quote blindly.

Every one of these scenarios represents real money lost. A CRM doesn't just organise your contacts — it turns relationship management from a thing you have to remember into a system that prompts you automatically.

Database Setup: Columns and Properties

Create a new full-page database in Notion called "Clients." Here are all the properties you need:

Name Title — the client's full name or company name
Status Select — Lead / Contacted / Proposal Sent / Negotiating / Active / Completed / Past
Company Text — company name (if Name is a contact person)
Email Email — primary contact email
Phone Phone — optional, useful for higher-touch relationships
Source Select — Referral / Website / LinkedIn / Cold Outreach / Event / Other
Last Contact Date — the last time you spoke or emailed
Next Follow-up Date — the date you committed to reaching out next
Projects Relation — links to your Projects database
Invoices Relation — links to your Invoices database
Total Revenue Rollup — sum of Amount from linked Invoices (Status = Paid)
Notes Text — personal details, preferences, context for next call

Building Your Pipeline and Contact Views

The properties are just raw data. Views are where the intelligence happens. Here are the four views every freelance Notion CRM needs:

Pipeline Board (Kanban)

Group by Status, show all stages. This is your primary view — a visual pipeline that shows where every relationship stands. Drag cards between columns as relationships progress.

Active Clients (Table)

Filter: Status = Active. Sort by most recent Last Contact. Properties shown: Name, Company, Email, Current Project (rollup), Last Contact, Next Follow-up. Your go-to for managing active client relationships.

Follow-ups Due (Table)

Filter: Next Follow-up is on or before today. Sort by Next Follow-up ascending. This is your daily action list — everyone you need to contact today. Open it every morning.

Past Clients (Gallery)

Filter: Status = Past or Completed. Sort by Total Revenue descending. Shows your most valuable past relationships at a glance — your best source of repeat work and referrals.

Formulas That Automate Your CRM

Notion formulas turn a static database into an active system. Here are three to add immediately:

Days Since Last Contact
dateBetween(now(), prop("Last Contact"), "days")

Highlights relationships going cold. Sort by this to find who needs a check-in.

Follow-up Status
if(prop("Next Follow-up") <= now(), "Due Today", if(dateBetween(prop("Next Follow-up"), now(), "days") <= 3, "Due Soon", "Upcoming"))

Creates a status label for each follow-up. Use as a filter or to colour-code your board.

Pipeline Stage Number (for sorting)
if(prop("Status") == "Lead", 1, if(prop("Status") == "Contacted", 2, if(prop("Status") == "Proposal Sent", 3, if(prop("Status") == "Negotiating", 4, if(prop("Status") == "Active", 5, 6)))))

Useful for sorting table views by pipeline stage.

CRM Automation Ideas

Notion's built-in automations (available on all plans) can do real work in your CRM:

  • Auto-set follow-up on status change. When Status changes to "Proposal Sent," automatically set Next Follow-up to 2 days from now.
  • Reminder notification. Trigger a notification when Next Follow-up = today. Works on desktop and mobile.
  • Create new project on status change. When Status changes to "Active," automatically create a linked entry in your Projects database.

For more advanced automation, tools like Zapier or Make can watch your Notion database and trigger actions in other apps — sending a welcome email via Gmail, creating a Calendly booking, or adding the client to a mailing list.

Integrating with Project Management

A CRM alone tracks relationships. Linked to your project management, it tracks work. Here's how to connect them:

  1. Create a "Projects" database with a Relation property pointing to your Clients database.
  2. On each Project page, link to the relevant client. Notion automatically creates a back-link in the Client record.
  3. Add a Rollup on your Client record to show all project names, statuses, and total project values.
  4. In your daily Dashboard page, create a filtered view of Projects due this week — filtered by the current date and sorted by deadline.

Now when you open a client record, you see their full history: every project, every invoice, every note. No more hunting through emails to answer "what did we work on last year?"

The Follow-Up System That Wins Clients

The #1 thing that separates freelancers who consistently land clients from those who don't isn't talent, portfolio, or pricing. It's follow-up. Most deals are won by the person who follows up — because most people don't.

Here's the follow-up cadence to programme into your CRM:

  • After a discovery call: Send a follow-up email within 24 hours. Reference something specific from the conversation. Set next follow-up to 5 days.
  • After sending a proposal: Follow up in 48–72 hours. Not "just checking in" — add value ("I thought of something we didn't discuss..."). Set next follow-up to 7 days.
  • If no response after 2 follow-ups: Send a final "breakup email" that closes the loop gracefully. Some people respond to this who didn't respond to the previous two.
  • For past clients: Schedule a check-in every 90 days. A brief "thinking of you — how is [project we completed] going?" email keeps you top of mind for their next need.

The key: every interaction ends with a specific next action and a date in your CRM. If there's no next date, the relationship goes cold.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Notion be used as a CRM?

Yes — Notion makes an excellent lightweight CRM for freelancers. Its relational databases let you link clients to projects, invoices, and notes. Filtered pipeline views replicate the core functionality of dedicated CRM tools at zero cost. For most solo freelancers, Notion CRM is better than any paid alternative.

Is Notion CRM better than HubSpot for freelancers?

For most freelancers, yes. HubSpot is built for sales teams with dedicated pipelines, email sequences, and lead scoring — features that add complexity without value for a solo operator. Notion CRM is free, integrates with the rest of your freelance business, and takes less time to maintain.

How do I set up a CRM in Notion?

Create a Notion database called Clients with properties for Name, Status, Contact Details, Last Contact Date, Next Follow-up Date, Source, and Total Revenue. Add filtered views for your pipeline stages (Lead, Proposal, Active, Past). Link to a Projects database via Relation property. That's a functional Notion CRM in under an hour — or use a pre-built template to skip the setup entirely.
