Notion Calendar View Explained

Every Notion database can be viewed as a calendar — you just need at least one date property. Switch to calendar view via the "Add a view" menu, then select which date property to use as the primary date. Each entry in your database appears as a card on the calendar on its due date.

The key insight: you do not need a separate calendar database. If you already have a tasks database with due dates, you can add a calendar view to it in under 30 seconds. Same data, completely different interface.

Quick Setup Open any Notion database → click the view selector at the top left → "+ Add a view" → choose "Calendar" → pick your date property. Done.

5 Calendar Use Cases for Freelancers

📋 Content Calendar
Plan and track blog posts, social content, email newsletters by publish date. Filter by channel, status, or content type.
Key properties: Publish Date, Channel, Status, Content Type
📋 Project Timeline
Show deliverable due dates across all active client projects. Spot deadline conflicts before they happen.
Key properties: Deadline, Client, Project, Priority
📋 Editorial Calendar
For writers: plan article topics, research deadlines, draft dates, and publication slots across multiple publications.
Key properties: Topic, Publication, Draft Due, Publish Date
📋 Personal Schedule
Block time for deep work, client calls, admin, and personal commitments. Works alongside your task manager.
Key properties: Date, Time Block, Category, Energy Level
📋 Social Media Planner
Schedule posts across LinkedIn, Instagram, Twitter/X, and TikTok. Track status from draft to published.
Key properties: Scheduled Date, Platform, Status, Caption, Assets

Properties Needed for Each Use Case

Here are the recommended database properties for the most popular calendar setup — the content calendar:

  • Title (title) — Post or piece name
  • Publish Date (date) — Your calendar view will use this
  • Channel (select) — Blog, LinkedIn, Email, Instagram, etc.
  • Status (select) — Idea, Outline, Draft, Review, Scheduled, Published
  • Content Type (select) — Tutorial, Listicle, Case Study, Promotion, etc.
  • Tags (multi-select) — Topic tags for filtering
  • CTA (text) — What action does this piece drive toward?
  • Assets (files & media) — Attach images, drafts, or briefs

For project timelines, replace Channel/Content Type with Client (relation to your client database) and Project (relation to your project database), and add an Owner property to assign tasks to yourself or collaborators.

How to Sync with Google Calendar

Notion does not natively sync bidirectionally with Google Calendar, but there are two practical approaches:

  1. One-way via Notion's native Google Calendar integration: In your Notion workspace settings under Connections, link your Google Calendar account. This lets you view Google Calendar events inside Notion, but Notion items do not push back to Google Calendar.
  2. Two-way sync via Zapier or Make (Integromat): Set up a Zap that creates a Google Calendar event whenever a new item is added to your Notion database with a date. More setup required, but gives you true two-way visibility.
Practical Recommendation For most freelancers, the simplest approach is to keep your client-facing schedule in Google Calendar (for meeting invites and shared access) and use Notion for your content planning and project deadlines. Reference both daily rather than trying to perfectly sync them.
