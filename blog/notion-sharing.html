How to Share a Notion Page: Notion Sharing Settings and Permissions Explained

Share Notion pages with clients, teammates, or the world — with exactly the right permissions. The complete guide to Notion sharing in 2026.

8 min read  ·  July 2026  ·  FreelanceOS Team
Home›Blog›How to Share a Notion Page

Notion's sharing system is flexible and powerful — but it can be confusing if you've never dug into it. You can share a single page with a client, publish a page publicly on the web, give someone full editing access or read-only view, and build an entire client portal that looks professional and stays separate from your private workspace. This guide covers every sharing scenario you'll encounter as a Notion user in 2026.

Key principle: In Notion, sharing is page-level by default. When you share a specific page with someone, they only see that page and its subpages — not your entire workspace. This makes Notion excellent for professional sharing with clients and external collaborators.

How to Share a Notion Page with Someone (Step-by-Step)

1

Open the Share Menu

Open the page you want to share. In the top right corner of the page, click the Share button. The sharing panel will open as a dropdown. This panel contains all sharing options for this specific page.

2

Invite by Email

In the "Invite" field at the top of the panel, type the person's email address. As you type, Notion will suggest people already in your contacts or workspace. Select the correct person or press Enter to add them by email.

Choose a permission level from the dropdown next to their name before clicking Invite. The default is "Can view" — change it to "Can edit" or "Can comment" as appropriate.

Click Invite. They'll receive an email with a link to the page. If they don't have a Notion account, they'll be prompted to create one (free) to access the page.

3

Copy and Send the Link

At the bottom of the sharing panel, click Copy link. This copies the direct link to the page. You can send this link via email, Slack, or any other channel. Note: for guests (people invited by email), the link only works after they've accepted the invitation. For public pages, the link works for anyone without login.

4

Manage Access

To change a person's permissions or remove their access later: open the Share panel again, find their name in the list, click the permission dropdown next to their name, and select a different permission level or "Remove access." Changes take effect immediately.

For client portals: "Can comment" is usually the ideal permission. Clients can read everything, leave feedback via comments, and you get notified — but they can't accidentally edit your content, delete sections, or change any of the page structure.

How to Share a Notion Page Publicly (Share to Web)

Sharing publicly makes a Notion page accessible to anyone with the link — no Notion account required. This is how many people publish Notion pages as websites, public wikis, or free templates.

1

Toggle "Share to web"

Open the Share panel on your page. Find the "Share to web" toggle and switch it on. The page immediately becomes publicly accessible at a notion.site URL. Anyone with this URL can view the page without logging in.

2

Choose Public Options

After enabling Share to web, you'll see two optional toggles:

  • Allow duplicate as template: Allows anyone viewing the page to click "Duplicate" and copy the entire page into their own Notion workspace. Turn this on if you're sharing a template you want others to use.
  • Allow comments: Allows anyone (even without a Notion account) to leave comments on the public page. Generally leave this off unless you specifically want public feedback.
3

Copy the Public Link

Click "Copy web link" to get the public URL. This URL format is: notion.site/[your-workspace-name]/[page-name]. You can share this link anywhere — social media, email, your own website. The page will render in a clean read-only view without the Notion sidebar.

Security note:

When you share a page publicly, all subpages of that page also become publicly accessible via their own links. Make sure your shared page doesn't have subpages containing private information — or move private content out of the shared page's hierarchy before publishing.

How to Build a Notion Client Portal

One of the most practical uses of Notion sharing for freelancers is the client portal — a dedicated page you share with each client that contains everything relevant to their project. It replaces the "where's the latest file?" email and makes you look more professional than 90% of freelancers your client has ever worked with.

Client Portal Page Structure

  • Project Overview — scope, timeline, key milestones, current phase
  • Status Dashboard — simple table or text: current status, last updated, next milestone, overall health (On Track / At Risk)
  • Deliverables — list of all deliverables with links to the latest version, status (Draft / In Review / Approved), and delivery date
  • Open Questions & Decisions — a table of questions needing client input, with status and resolution date when answered
  • Feedback Log — running log of revision requests and how they were addressed
  • Meeting Notes — subpage for each call, with key decisions highlighted
  • Project Brief — the original approved brief, for reference
1

Create the Portal Page

Create a new top-level page in Notion titled "[Client Name] — Project Portal." Build out the sections above. This page should sit inside a "Clients" section of your workspace, not inside a shared workspace that gives access to your whole setup.

2

Share with the Client

Click Share → Invite → type client's email → set to "Can comment" → click Invite. The client receives an email with a link. When they open it, they see a clean project page with only their project — nothing from your workspace is visible.

3

Update It Weekly

Your weekly Friday update (the 5-bullet email from our client communication guide) should also be reflected in the portal's Status Dashboard. This takes 2 minutes to update and means clients who check the portal between emails are always seeing current information.

Frequently Asked Notion Sharing Questions

Does sharing a page expose my whole workspace?

No. Notion sharing is page-level. When you invite a guest (someone not in your workspace) to a page, they see only that page and its subpages. They cannot navigate to other pages in your workspace. Your notes, other clients' portals, and private pages are completely invisible to them.

Can I share a Notion database with someone?

Yes — a database is a type of page in Notion, and it shares the same way. Share a database with a guest and they can see the database and all its entries. Set their permission to "Can view" to prevent them from adding, editing, or deleting database entries. "Can edit" lets them add and edit entries but not change the database structure.

Can someone without a Notion account view a shared page?

Yes — for publicly shared pages (Share to web turned on). Anyone with the link can view a public page without any account. For pages shared by email invite, the person will need to create a free Notion account if they don't already have one. Account creation is free and takes about 60 seconds.

How do I stop sharing a Notion page?

To revoke access from a specific person: Share panel → find their name → click their permission dropdown → Remove access. To unpublish a public page: Share panel → toggle off "Share to web." The page immediately becomes private again, and any existing links to it will show a "not found" error for viewers who aren't invited guests.

What is the difference between Notion permission levels?
Notion has four permission levels: Full access — can edit, share, and delete the page. Can edit — can edit content but cannot change sharing settings or delete. Can comment — can add comments but cannot edit content. Can view — read-only access, no commenting. For client portals, 'Can comment' is often the right choice — clients can give feedback via comments without accidentally editing your content.
