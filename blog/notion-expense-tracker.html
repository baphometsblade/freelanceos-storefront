1. Why Track Expenses in Notion

Most freelancers use one of three approaches to expense tracking: a spreadsheet, a dedicated app like QuickBooks or FreshBooks, or nothing at all. Notion beats all three for solo professionals who are already managing their work in Notion.

A spreadsheet is fine for simple tracking but terrible for attaching receipts, filtering by multiple properties simultaneously, or linking expenses to specific client projects. Dedicated accounting software is powerful but expensive, requires double-entry of data you already have in Notion, and is often overkill until you are earning six figures. Tracking nothing is the most expensive option of all — you miss deductions and face panic every spring.

Notion's database system handles all the core expense tracking needs — categorization, filtering, date-based views, rollups, receipt storage — while keeping everything in the same workspace where you already manage projects and clients. The ability to link an expense to a client project and see total project costs is something neither a spreadsheet nor most accounting apps can do easily.

When to graduate from Notion: Notion is ideal for freelancers earning under $200K annually with straightforward expense structures. If you have employees, inventory, or complex multi-entity accounting needs, a dedicated tool like QuickBooks will serve you better alongside Notion.

4. Monthly and Annual Views

Raw data is only useful when you can view it in meaningful ways. Creating the right saved views in Notion transforms your expense log from a list of entries into a financial reporting tool you can actually use for decision-making.

Essential expense tracker views

  • This month — filter: Year = current year AND Month = current month, sorted by date descending. This is your default working view for logging new expenses.
  • By category — group by Category, show total amount per group. Immediately shows where your money is going and flags categories that are growing unexpectedly.
  • Missing receipts — filter: Has Receipt = unchecked AND Tax Deductible = checked. Find every deductible expense without a receipt before it is too late.
  • Tax deductible — filter: Tax Deductible = checked. Your complete deduction list for the year, filterable by date range for quarterly review.
  • Annual summary — filter by Year, group by Category. Your year-end expense breakdown for tax prep.
  • By payment method — group by Payment Method. Useful for reconciling against bank and credit card statements.

Save each of these as named views on your expense database. Then you can jump from your daily logging view to the tax deductible view or the missing receipts view with a single click, without rebuilding filters each time.

5. Notion Formulas for Expense Tracking

Notion's formula properties unlock several powerful automation-like features in your expense tracker that would require macros or scripts in a traditional spreadsheet.

Month extraction formula

To extract the month number from your Date property for grouping purposes, use:

Formula: month(prop("Date"))

This returns a number from 1 to 12, which you can then group by to create monthly summaries.

Year extraction formula

Formula: year(prop("Date"))

Returns the four-digit year. Filter your views by this property to separate 2024 and 2025 expenses without creating separate databases.

Month-Year label formula

Formula: format(month(prop("Date"))) + "/" + format(year(prop("Date")))

Creates a "6/2025" style label that you can group by for month-by-month views. More readable than month numbers alone.

Running total with rollups

If you create a parent "Monthly Summary" database, you can use rollup properties to pull total expenses per month from your expenses database, sum them, and track month-over-month spending trends. This requires a relation between the two databases but creates a powerful financial dashboard once set up.

6. Receipt Tracking System

Receipts are the proof that your expenses happened. Without them, deductions become harder to defend in an audit. A Notion-based receipt system is simple, mobile-friendly, and keeps receipts attached directly to the expense record rather than in a separate folder that gets disorganized within weeks.

Mobile receipt capture workflow

The fastest receipt workflow for freelancers is to use Notion's mobile app immediately after any expense:

  1. Open Notion mobile app on your phone immediately after paying
  2. Create a new expense entry in your database
  3. Take a photo of the receipt directly within the Files property
  4. Fill in the amount, category, and a brief business purpose note
  5. Check the Has Receipt checkbox

The entire process takes under 90 seconds and results in a perfectly organized, receipt-attached expense entry. Compare this to the alternative: saving the receipt photo to your camera roll, forgetting about it for three weeks, then spending hours at tax time trying to match blurry photos to bank statement lines.

Digital receipt management tips

  • For email receipts, forward them to a dedicated email address and create an expense entry the same day
  • For subscription charges, set a recurring reminder each billing date to log the expense with the downloaded receipt PDF
  • For mileage, use a mileage tracking app like MileIQ and export monthly, then log the total as one expense entry
  • For credit card statements, reconcile them against your Notion tracker monthly rather than waiting until year-end

7. Tax Preparation with Notion

Tax season should be a 2-hour exercise for freelancers with good records, not a multi-week scramble. A well-maintained Notion expense tracker makes this possible by keeping everything organized throughout the year rather than creating a mountain of paperwork each spring.

Year-end export process

  1. Filter your expenses database to: Year = the tax year AND Tax Deductible = checked
  2. Review each entry for completeness — check that business purpose notes are filled in
  3. Use the By Category view to generate a category-by-category total
  4. Export the filtered view as a CSV: click the three-dot menu on the database and choose Export
  5. Share the CSV with your accountant, or use the totals to fill in your Schedule C directly

Quarterly tax planning

If you make quarterly estimated tax payments, your Notion expense tracker helps you calculate them accurately. At the end of each quarter, filter your income tracker for total revenue and your expense tracker for total deductible expenses. Your estimated taxable income is roughly income minus deductible expenses. Apply your effective tax rate to get a ballpark quarterly payment estimate.

8. Advanced Tips and Integrations

Once your basic expense tracker is running smoothly, a few advanced techniques can save additional time and add analytical power.

Linking expenses to projects

The Linked Project relation property lets you assign expenses directly to client projects. This enables two powerful use cases: first, you can calculate the true cost of delivering each project including tools, subcontractors, and direct expenses, which helps you price future similar work more accurately. Second, for client-billable expenses like stock photos or printing costs, you have a clean list to reference when creating reimbursement invoices.

Budget tracking

Create a "Monthly Budget" database with budget targets for each expense category. Use a relation to your expenses database and rollup properties to compare actual spending versus budget for each category in real time. This turns your expense tracker into a proactive budgeting tool rather than a purely retrospective record.

Automated reminders

Use Notion's reminder date property to set a reminder on recurring subscriptions that are coming up for renewal. This prevents surprise charges and gives you time to evaluate whether you still need each tool before being charged for another year.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Notion be used as an expense tracker?

Yes. Notion's database system is excellent for expense tracking. You can log expenses with full categorization, attach receipt images, filter by date and category, and build formula-powered monthly and annual summaries — all in the same workspace where you manage your projects and clients.

Is there a free Notion expense tracker template?

Yes. You can build a Notion expense tracker for free from scratch using the instructions in this guide. The Notion template gallery also has basic options. FreelanceOS includes a professional expense tracker pre-built with tax-ready categories and all views configured, saving several hours of setup time.

What expense categories should freelancers track?

Freelancers should track: software subscriptions, hardware and equipment, home office costs, marketing and advertising, professional development, travel, client meals, professional services, insurance, subcontractors, banking fees, and office supplies. Align categories with your country's standard self-employment deduction classes.

How do I track receipts in Notion?

Add a Files and Media property to your expenses database and upload receipt photos directly to each expense entry from your phone's camera. Add a Has Receipt checkbox property to quickly filter entries with missing receipts — a vital habit before tax time arrives.

Can I use Notion for tax preparation?

Yes. Filter your expense tracker by tax-deductible categories and by the tax year. The By Category view generates a total per category. Export the filtered view as a CSV for your accountant, or use the categorized totals to complete your tax forms directly. It works best when you have maintained the tracker throughout the year rather than reconstructing it at tax time.

How do I calculate monthly expenses in Notion?

Add formula properties that extract the month and year from each expense date. Then create a filtered view grouped by the Month formula property. Notion's group headers show count and sum of numeric properties, giving you a monthly total automatically when you enable summary rows.

What is the difference between Notion and a spreadsheet for expense tracking?

Notion lets you attach receipts directly to expense entries, link expenses to client projects, add rich notes, create multiple filtered views of the same data, and access everything on mobile with an excellent interface. Spreadsheets are better for complex formulas and pivot tables but lack all of these organizational advantages.

How often should I update my expense tracker?

Log expenses immediately using the Notion mobile app when possible. At minimum, update weekly during a brief Friday financial review. The longer you wait, the more likely you are to forget details, lose receipt photos, or miss the business purpose of an expense. Accuracy drops fast with delayed logging.
