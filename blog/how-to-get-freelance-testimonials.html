How to Get Client Testimonials as a Freelancer: Scripts + Timing Guide (2026)

By FreelanceOS  ·  May 2026  ·  9-minute read  ·  Social Proof & Marketing

Most freelancers know testimonials matter. Few actually have a system for collecting them. The result: a portfolio full of work samples but empty of the social proof that converts hesitant prospects into paying clients.

This guide covers the full system — when to ask, how to ask, what format to request, and exactly what scripts to send. By the end, you will have a repeatable process that builds your testimonial library with every project you complete.

Why Timing Is Everything

The single biggest mistake freelancers make is waiting until the project is fully wrapped — invoice paid, files delivered, everything archived — before asking for a testimonial. By that point, the emotional peak of the client relationship has passed.

The best moments to ask are when the client is most satisfied and most emotionally engaged. These moments are predictable:

  • Right after a key milestone: The moment a client sees the first design round they love, or you deliver a piece of copy that exceeds expectations — that is your window.
  • When they report a result: If a client emails you saying "that landing page went live and we saw a 30% increase in signups," respond to that email and ask immediately.
  • At project completion, before the invoice is fully settled: Not after — while the energy is still high and they are reflecting on the work.
  • After a retainer renewal: A client who just renewed is telling you they are happy. Ask them to put that into writing.
Best Practice Set a project checklist item: "Send testimonial request within 48 hours of milestone approval." Put it in your project template so it never falls through the cracks.

The 3 Testimonial Formats

Different formats serve different purposes. Ideally you collect all three over time, but know what each one is best for before you ask:

Most Common
Text Testimonial
2–4 sentences, written by the client or lightly edited from their words. Goes on your website, proposals, and portfolio. Easy to repurpose across channels.
Highest Credibility
LinkedIn Recommendation
Publicly attached to your LinkedIn profile, verifiable by anyone. Takes more client effort but carries high trust weight. Request after long-term engagements.
Highest Conversion
Video Testimonial
1–2 minute video of the client describing the result. Highest conversion lift on landing pages. Ask loyal, extroverted clients. Tools: Loom, Testimonial.to.

For most freelancers, the priority order is: text (always), LinkedIn (for long-term clients and high-value engagements), video (for your 2–3 closest long-term clients).

Exact Scripts to Send

Email Script — After Milestone Approval

Email / DM Script
Subject: Quick favour — would you be up for a testimonial?

Hi [Name],

Really glad [the homepage / the campaign / the rebrand] landed the way it did — your feedback has been great to hear.

Would you be willing to write a short testimonial I could use on my website? It doesn't need to be long — even 2–3 sentences about your experience working together would mean a lot.

If it helps, you could focus on: what you were trying to achieve, what we did together, and what the result was.

You can reply right here and I'll take care of the formatting. No pressure either way — just thought I'd ask while things were fresh!

Thanks,
[Your name]

LinkedIn Recommendation Request

LinkedIn Message
Hi [Name], it's been great working together on [project]. If you ever have a few minutes, I'd really appreciate a LinkedIn recommendation — even a short one. I can send you a request through LinkedIn directly, or if you'd prefer to draft something first and then post it, I'm happy either way. Thanks so much.

Video Testimonial Ask (for close clients)

Email Script — Video Request
Hi [Name],

I have a slightly bigger ask — would you be open to recording a short 60–90 second video about our work together? Just a quick Loom or phone video talking about what you were trying to solve and what happened as a result.

I use these on my website and they're incredibly helpful for giving potential clients a real sense of what working with me is like. No editing needed on your end — raw and conversational is perfect.

If you'd be up for it, I can send you three quick prompts to guide what you say. Totally understand if it's not your thing!

[Your name]

What to Ask Clients to Focus On

The weakest testimonials say things like "Great to work with! Very professional and responsive." These feel generic and do nothing to convert a prospect. The strongest testimonials follow a simple structure:

  1. The situation before: What problem were they trying to solve? ("We were struggling to get leads from our website...")
  2. What happened during: How was the collaboration? ("The process was smooth and [name] asked the right questions...")
  3. The result after: A specific, measurable outcome. ("Within 60 days, our conversion rate went from 1.2% to 3.8%.")
Pro Tip: Guide Without Ghostwriting It is completely acceptable to give clients a short prompt list. "If you're not sure where to start, try answering these three questions: What were you trying to achieve? What did working together look like? What changed as a result?" This makes it easy for them and produces better testimonials for you.
Avoid This Pattern Do not write the testimonial for the client and ask them to approve it. It sounds inauthentic, and experienced prospects can often tell. Guide them with prompts; let them use their own words.

How to Display Testimonials on Your Portfolio

Collecting testimonials is only half the work. Where and how you display them determines whether they actually influence client decisions.

  • Above the fold on your home page: Do not hide testimonials at the bottom. One strong testimonial near the top of your site — especially near a CTA button — increases conversions significantly.
  • On your proposal or pitch deck: Include 1–2 testimonials directly in your proposal, specifically from clients in similar industries to the prospect you are pitching.
  • Near every CTA: On pricing pages, booking forms, and contact pages, place a testimonial directly adjacent to the "hire me" button.
  • In case studies: Pull quotes from testimonials and embed them within case study pages to reinforce the narrative.
  • LinkedIn: Recommendations on your LinkedIn profile appear in search results and on your public profile — highly visible to inbound leads.

Storing Testimonials in Notion

Testimonials only help if you can find them when you need them. Build a simple Notion database with these properties:

  • Client Name (text)
  • Company / Role (text)
  • Project Type (select: design, copy, dev, etc.)
  • Format (select: text, LinkedIn, video)
  • Testimonial Text (text)
  • Date Collected (date)
  • Industry (select)
  • Used In (multi-select: website, proposal, case study)

This lets you filter by industry when building a proposal — instantly find testimonials from clients in the same space as your prospect.
