The Process

Quarterly Marketing Planning in Notion, Step by Step

This is the exact process embedded inside the template — from strategic audit through execution and measurement. Follow it once and it becomes your repeatable marketing operating system.

1

Run Your Quarterly Marketing Audit

Review the previous quarter before planning the next. The template's audit section guides you through channel performance, campaign results, and budget utilization. Answer four questions: What worked? What did not? What did we learn? What are we changing? Prevents expensive repeats.

60–90 minutes
2

Update Your Marketing Strategy Foundation

ICP and positioning evolve as you learn. Open the Marketing Strategy Hub and refresh your ICP, messaging pillars, and positioning based on audit learnings. Every campaign links back to this foundation, so updates here flow through everything that follows.

30–60 minutes
3

Set Quarterly OKRs and Allocate Budget

Define 2–3 marketing objectives with 2–4 measurable key results each. Open the Budget Tracker and allocate spend across channels based on what the audit showed is working. The template surfaces last quarter's actuals so you allocate with data, not instinct.

45–60 minutes
4

Brief Your Campaigns

Create a Campaign Manager entry for each initiative. Fill in the brief template: objective, audience segment, channel, budget, timeline, and success metrics. Link to relevant messaging pillars. Fifteen minutes per campaign brief prevents mid-campaign confusion.

15 min per campaign
5

Populate Your Content Calendar

With campaigns briefed, open the Content Calendar and add the content needed for each. Link pieces to their parent campaign. Channel filters show email, social, blog, and video side by side. The status pipeline tracks every piece from idea through publication.

1–2 hours per quarter
6

Track, Measure, and Report

Update the Channel Performance Tracker weekly — a 20-minute habit. Run a monthly review to spot trends and adjust. At quarter end, the reporting dashboard is pre-populated with your data. The quarterly report takes 30 minutes, not three days.

20 min/week
Metrics & Reporting

Know What to Track. Build a Dashboard That Actually Gets Used.

The biggest reporting mistake is tracking everything. The template is built around a focused set of leading and lagging indicators that tell you whether your marketing is working — before you run out of budget to fix it.

Leading Indicators — Predictive Signals

Content published (weekly) Are you consistent? Output predicts results 6–8 weeks out.
Email list growth rate Growing slower than 2% per month? Fix top-of-funnel before fixing email.
Organic impressions trend Impressions rising before traffic — a 4–6 week signal that SEO is working.
Ad click-through rate CTR predicts CPL. A 2x CTR improvement often means a 40% CPL reduction.
Social share of voice Is your brand appearing in conversations your competitors own?

Lagging Indicators — Outcome Measures

Leads generated per channel Which channels are delivering qualified pipeline? Track monthly by source.
Cost per acquisition (CPA) The number that decides whether to scale or cut a channel. Track per campaign.
Revenue influenced by marketing What percentage of closed deals had a marketing touchpoint in the journey?
Return on ad spend (ROAS) For paid campaigns. Below 2x ROAS? Pause and diagnose before scaling.
Organic traffic growth (QoQ) Quarterly organic growth above 15% indicates a compounding SEO strategy.
