The Solution

A Complete Event Planning Business OS in Notion

FreelanceOS Pro for Event Planners connects your event database, vendor management, budget tracking, run-of-show templates, client CRM, quote comparisons, post-event reviews, and new business pipeline in one relational Notion workspace built for freelance event planners and wedding coordinators.

Event Database — Client, Date, Venue, Budget, Headcount, Status

A master event database where every event in your practice lives — upcoming events, events in planning, events in final execution, and completed events. Each event record holds the client name, event date, venue, event type (wedding, corporate, birthday, milestone), headcount, total budget, current status, and key contact. Filter by upcoming date to see what is happening in the next 90 days, sort by budget to see your highest-value events, or filter by status to separate events in early planning from those entering final execution. Every event is visible and accessible in seconds from one organized database.

Vendor Database — Category, Contact, Pricing, and Booking Status

A comprehensive vendor database organized by service category — catering, florals, photography, videography, AV and lighting, DJ and entertainment, venue, transportation, hair and makeup, and staffing. Each vendor record holds contact info, specialty, price range, availability notes, booking status for current events, past performance notes from previous collaborations, and your personal reliability rating. When you need to book a florist for a new wedding, search your vendor database by category and see all your trusted contacts with pricing and performance history in seconds instead of starting research from scratch and hoping for a good fit.

Event Day Timeline and Run-of-Show Template

A structured run-of-show template you duplicate for every event. The template includes the full event day timeline — vendor arrival and setup schedule, ceremony or program sequence with minute-by-minute timing, AV cue notes, catering and bar service times, program transitions, wrap-up and breakdown sequence, and emergency contact list. Distribute the completed run-of-show to all vendors one week before the event day and arrive knowing that every person involved is working from the same schedule. Professional run-of-show execution is one of the most visible markers of an experienced event planner versus a novice one — and clients notice.

Budget Tracker — Line Items, Quoted vs. Actual, Client Budget vs. Spend

A detailed budget tracker breaking each event's total budget into line items across every spending category — venue, catering per person, open bar, florals, photography, videography, AV and lighting, DJ, transportation, decor, staffing, and miscellaneous. Each line item tracks quoted cost, actual cost, variance, and payment status. See at a glance whether an event is running over budget before it is too late to make adjustments, compare the total quoted versus total actual spend across the full event, and show clients a transparent budget report at any planning checkpoint. Budget surprises on event day are avoidable — they result from not tracking variances throughout the planning timeline.

Client CRM — Proposal Status and Contract Tracking

A client CRM tracking every prospect and active client through the full sales and delivery cycle — initial inquiry, consultation scheduled, proposal sent, contract out for signature, deposit received, planning in progress, final details phase, event completed, and post-event follow-up. See which prospects are waiting on a proposal you owe them, which contracts are out unsigned and need a nudge, and which active clients need attention this week based on upcoming planning milestones. Never lose a booked event to a competitor because a contract signature or deposit request slipped through the cracks during a busy execution week.

Vendor Quote Comparison Template

A side-by-side quote comparison template for presenting vendor options to clients in each service category. Each comparison holds the vendor name, quoted price, specific inclusions, specific exclusions, availability confirmation, past collaboration notes, and your recommendation with rationale. Present clients with a professional, structured comparison rather than forwarding three separate vendor PDFs and asking them to sort it out. Structured quote comparisons speed up vendor selection decisions — one of the most common delays in the event planning timeline — and build client trust by showing them your expertise in evaluating vendor value rather than just price.

Post-Event Debrief and Review Template

A post-event debrief template you complete within 48 hours of each event. Sections cover what went exceptionally well, what went wrong and why, vendor performance ratings, client feedback notes, budget accuracy review, things to do differently next time, and vendor recommendations to update. Consistent post-event debriefs turn every event — including the difficult ones — into operational intelligence that makes your next event better. Planners who document their learnings systematically improve faster than those who finish an event and move on without reflection. This template also gives you content for case studies and testimonial requests while the experience is fresh.

Marketing and Inquiry Pipeline for New Event Business

A new business pipeline tracking every inquiry, consultation, and prospect through your sales process — inquiry source, event type, event date, estimated budget, consultation scheduled, proposal sent, and outcome. Know exactly how many inquiries you have received this month, how many consultations you have completed, your proposal conversion rate, and where most of your bookings are coming from. Track which marketing channels generate your highest-value inquiries and allocate your marketing time and budget accordingly. Event planners with a structured inquiry pipeline capture more revenue from the same lead volume than those who handle inquiries reactively and lose momentum between event seasons.
