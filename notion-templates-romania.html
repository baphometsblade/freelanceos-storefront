Tax Structure

PFA vs SRL: Romania's Two Freelancer Structures

Understanding your options is the foundation of tax planning in Romania. FreelanceOS Pro includes dashboards for both structures.

🇷🇴 Romanian Freelancer Tax Guide (General Overview — Consult a Contabil for Your Situation)

PFA — Persoană Fizică Autorizată

  • Individual entrepreneur / sole trader status
  • Income tax: 10% flat rate on net income
  • CASS (health insurance): 10%, capped at 6x minimum wage × 12
  • CAS (pension): 25% — optional if income under 12x minimum wage/year
  • Annual declaration: D212 filed with ANAF
  • Best for: freelancers under approx. €60,000–80,000/year
  • Simple structure, minimal accounting requirements
  • Registration: Registrul Comerțului in your county

SRL — Societate cu Răspundere Limitată

  • Limited liability company — separate legal entity
  • Micro-enterprise tax: 1% on revenue (up to €500K turnover)
  • Standard profit tax: 16% on profit (above €500K or if opting out)
  • Dividend tax: 8% (reduced from 10% in recent years)
  • Requires: registered office, accounting, quarterly VAT declarations if VAT-registered
  • Best for: higher earners and those building an agency/team
  • More tax-efficient above approx. €60,000–80,000/year income
  • More administrative complexity than PFA

⚠ Romanian tax rules have changed significantly in recent years. Always consult a licensed Romanian contabil (accountant) for advice specific to your income level, activity type, and current ANAF regulations. This is general information only.

Several factors combine to make Romania particularly attractive for tech talent and the companies that employ them: competitive labor costs relative to Western Europe (a senior software developer in Bucharest may earn €40,000–70,000 annually — dramatically less than their Western European counterpart but high within Romania), strong STEM education traditions (technical universities in Bucharest, Cluj-Napoca, and Timișoara produce thousands of engineering graduates annually), excellent English proficiency in the IT sector, and EU membership providing regulatory stability and currency security for international clients.

🇷🇴 Notă în română / PFA Registration Note
Pentru a vă înregistra ca PFA în România, trebuie să depuneți dosarul la Registrul Comerțului din județul dumneavoastră. Sunt necesare: copie CI, cazier fiscal, specimenul de semnătură, dovada sediului profesional și declarațiile de activitate. PFA-urile cu venituri anuale sub plafonul stabilit beneficiază de impozit pe venit de 10% și contribuție la CASS de 10%. Consultați un contabil autorizat pentru sfaturi specifice situației dvs.
English: To register as a PFA in Romania, you must submit your application at the Trade Register (Registrul Comerțului) in your county. Required documents include: national ID copy, tax record clearance (cazier fiscal), signature specimen, proof of professional address, and activity declarations. PFAs with annual income below the established threshold benefit from a 10% income tax and 10% health insurance contribution (CASS). Consult a licensed Romanian accountant (contabil autorizat) for advice specific to your situation, as thresholds and rules change frequently.

FAQ: Notion Templates for Freelancers in Romania

How do Romanian freelancers pay taxes?
Romanian freelancers typically operate as either a PFA or SRL. PFAs pay a flat 10% income tax on net income, plus 10% CASS health insurance (capped) and optionally 25% CAS pension. Annual declaration is filed with ANAF using form D212. SRL micro-enterprises pay 1% on revenue (up to €500K turnover) or 16% on profit, plus 8% dividend tax when withdrawing profits. Rules have changed in recent years — always consult a licensed Romanian contabil for current rates and thresholds specific to your situation.
What is a PFA in Romania?
A PFA (Persoană Fizică Autorizată — Authorized Natural Person) is Romania's sole trader / individual entrepreneur registration for self-employed professionals. It is the simplest way to legally provide services and issue invoices in Romania. Registration is at the Trade Register (Registrul Comerțului) in your county with documents including national ID, tax clearance certificate, signature specimen, and proof of professional address. PFAs pay flat 10% income tax and 10% CASS. Best suited for freelancers earning under approximately €60,000–80,000 per year.
What is the difference between PFA and SRL in Romania?
A PFA is a sole trader structure with simple flat-rate taxation (10% income tax) and minimal accounting requirements. An SRL is Romania's LLC equivalent — a separate legal entity with 1% micro-enterprise revenue tax (for turnover under €500K) and 8% dividend tax. For freelancers earning above €60,000–80,000/year, the SRL structure may be more tax-efficient overall. The SRL requires a registered office, formal accounting, and more administrative overhead. Consult a Romanian contabil to model both options for your specific income level.
How do Romanian IT freelancers receive international payments?
Romanian IT freelancers most commonly use: Revolut (extremely popular in Romania — one of the highest per-capita adoption rates in Europe, with instant EUR/USD-to-RON conversion at near-BNR rates), Wise (formerly TransferWise), PayPal, and traditional Romanian bank SWIFT transfers (Banca Transilvania, BCR, BRD, ING Bank Romania). Revolut is particularly popular for Upwork and Toptal payments due to low conversion fees. Romania has not adopted the Euro as of 2026, so currency conversion and BNR rate tracking matters for ANAF income declarations.
What is Romania's tech hub and why is it important?
Romania has three main tech hubs: Bucharest (capital, largest IT cluster, home to UiPath and Bitdefender), Cluj-Napoca (Transylvania's tech capital with offices for Bosch, Siemens, Deutsche Bank, and Accenture), and Timișoara (western Romania, strong German tech company presence). Romania's IT sector employs approximately 175,000 professionals and contributes over €6 billion annually to the economy. UiPath — founded in Bucharest by Daniel Dines — became Romania's first tech unicorn and listed on the NYSE in 2021, significantly raising Romania's profile in the global tech community.
Is Revolut popular in Romania?
Yes — Romania has one of the highest Revolut adoption rates in Europe. Revolut is extremely popular among Romanian IT professionals and freelancers for receiving international payments and converting EUR/USD to RON at competitive near-BNR interbank rates. The ease of receiving Upwork or Toptal payments in USD/EUR and converting to RON without high traditional bank SWIFT fees has made Revolut the default payment solution for a large segment of the Romanian freelance IT community. Banca Transilvania remains the most popular traditional bank for Romanian freelancers who need a full-service Romanian bank account alongside Revolut.
