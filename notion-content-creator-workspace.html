Editorial Content Calendar

A multi-view content calendar with calendar, timeline, and gallery views. Plan posts weeks ahead, track platform (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, newsletter), status (Idea → Draft → Filmed → Edited → Published), and publish dates. The backbone of a consistent creator business.

Brand Deal CRM

Every brand partnership tracked from outreach through to payment received. Pipeline stages: Prospecting → Proposal Sent → Negotiating → Contracted → Deliverable Due → Invoiced → Paid. Never let a deal slip through cracks or a deliverable go past deadline again.

A capture database for every content idea, regardless of platform or format. Rate ideas by estimated reach, effort, and monetization potential. Filter by platform or content type. When you sit down to plan your next month, your best ideas are already waiting — ranked and ready.

Track revenue by source: brand deals, AdSense, affiliate, digital products, memberships, consulting. Monthly totals calculated automatically. See at a glance which monetization channel is performing and which to double down on — the data most creators guess at.

Cross-Database Relations

Every database in your workspace connects to every relevant other. A brand deal links to the content piece it funds. An idea links to the project it becomes. Frequently Asked Questions

Is this designed specifically for YouTube creators, or does it work for other platforms?

It's platform-agnostic. The content calendar, production pipeline, and idea vault all work for YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, newsletters, podcasts, and blogs — you assign a Platform property to each piece of content and filter accordingly. YouTube creators tend to find the production pipeline particularly valuable, while newsletter writers love the editorial calendar. The brand deal CRM works identically regardless of platform.

Do I need a paid Notion account to use FreelanceOS Pro?

No. All features work on Notion's free plan. The linked databases, filtered views, formula properties, and all six systems in the content creator workspace use standard Notion functionality available at zero cost. You pay once for the FreelanceOS Pro template, never for a recurring Notion subscription unless you separately choose to upgrade Notion for team collaboration or advanced features.

How is this different from a generic Notion content calendar template?

Generic content calendar templates give you a database to schedule posts. FreelanceOS Pro gives you an interconnected business system: your content calendar links to your brand deal CRM, which links to your revenue tracker, which connects back to your idea vault. You can see at a glance which content types are generating the most brand deal income, which ideas have been sitting idle the longest, and what your pipeline looks like for the next 60 days. No generic template comes close to that depth.

Can I use this alongside a social media scheduling tool like Buffer or Later?

Yes. FreelanceOS is your planning and business layer, not a publishing tool. Most creators use FreelanceOS to plan content, track production, manage deals, and log income — then push to Buffer, Later, or YouTube Studio for actual scheduling and publishing. The two tools complement each other perfectly. See also our Notion productivity guide for integrating your full workflow.

What if I'm just starting out and don't have brand deals yet?

FreelanceOS Pro is valuable from day one. The editorial calendar and production pipeline help you publish consistently, which is the foundation for growing any audience. The brand deal CRM will be ready and waiting when your first partnership opportunity arrives — and having a professional tracking system in place signals to brands that you run a real business, which helps you close deals at higher rates. The revenue tracker also works for AdSense and affiliate income from the start.
