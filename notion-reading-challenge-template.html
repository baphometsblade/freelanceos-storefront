The Problem

Goodreads was built for social sharing. Not for serious readers.

If you care about actually remembering what you've read, hitting your reading goal, and building a reading life with intention — you need something better.

📊

Goodreads Has No Private Depth

You can log a book as "read" and leave a star rating, but where do your highlights go? Your reflections? The quote that changed how you think? Goodreads is a social feed, not a personal reading system. Your notes live in a different app, your highlights in another, and the connection between them exists only in your head.

🧠

You Read It. You Don't Remember It.

You've read 30 books this year. Someone mentions a non-fiction concept and you know you read about it — somewhere. But which book? What chapter? What was the actual insight? Without a system to capture key ideas and personal reflections immediately after you finish, most of what you read fades within weeks. The book on your shelf becomes furniture.

📅

January Goal, March Forgotten

You set an ambitious reading challenge on January 1st. By March you've read two books and lost track. By July, you've quietly abandoned the goal. No tracking, no visibility into your pace, no system to tell you "read 1 book this week to stay on track." A goal without a dashboard is just a wish.

🗺️

No Visibility Into Genre Blind Spots

You look at your year-end reading stats and realize: 22 out of 30 books were fantasy or thrillers. You meant to read more classics, more translated fiction, more debut authors — but without visibility into your genre balance, you default to familiar comfort reads every time. Your reading life stays narrower than you want it to be.

Book Notes & Highlights Hub

This is the feature that separates serious readers from casual readers — and it's what Goodreads will never give you. Every book in your database has a linked notes page that belongs to you, styled by you, and completely private.

Feature 04

TBR (To Be Read) List — Your Reading Queue, Actually Organized

Every reader has a TBR that is either embarrassingly long, impossibly disorganized, or both. This template gives your TBR proper structure — not just a pile of titles, but a curated, prioritized, filterable queue you can actually work from.

Log where each book came from: was it a comp title mentioned in another book? A recommendation from your buddy read partner? A BookTok video that convinced you? Over time, you'll learn which recommendation sources produce the books you actually love. Priority levels (High / Medium / Someday) let you separate "must read next" from "sounds interesting, maybe someday." The estimated length field enables a powerful filter: "Under 200 pages" surfaces your quick win reads for those weeks when life is busy and you need something you can actually finish. Filter by genre when you're in a specific reading mood, by recommender when you want to trust a particular source, or by priority when you need to make a decision fast.

Feature 06

Author & Series Tracker + DNF Log

Two features that the reading community genuinely loves — and that no mainstream app handles well.

The Author & Series Tracker links directly to your book database. For every author you've read, see the full list of books you've logged, the titles remaining in their backlist, and — for series — exactly which books you've read and which are next. No more picking up book four thinking it's book two. No more forgetting which Brandon Sanderson Cosmere books you've actually finished.

The DNF (Did Not Finish) Tracker is a beloved feature in the reading community, and for good reason. Life is too short for books you're not enjoying, and DNFing without a record means you'll forget you tried it — and potentially pick it up again. Log every DNF with the title, author, how far you got (percentage or page number), and most importantly: why you stopped. Reasons might include "pacing too slow," "couldn't connect with the narrator," "not the right time for this book" (a reason to revisit later), or simply "not for me." Over time, your DNF log reveals patterns in what you don't enjoy — and that's just as valuable as knowing what you love.

FAQ

Common Questions

How do I set up a reading challenge in this Notion template?
After duplicating the template to your Notion workspace, open the Reading Challenge dashboard and enter your annual goal (e.g., 52 books). The tracker automatically counts completed books and calculates your pace, on-track status, and projected finish date. You can update your goal at any time and the formulas will recalculate instantly. The whole setup takes under five minutes.
How does the template track books I've read?
Every book you finish gets logged in the Book Database with title, author, genre, subgenre, pages, start and finish dates, rating, format (physical, Kindle, audiobook), and who recommended it. The template automatically calculates days to read and pages read totals, and feeds your reading stats across every dashboard. You can add a book in under a minute once you have the details.
Can I use this template for annual reading goals like 52 books in a year?
Yes — the Annual Reading Challenge Tracker is designed specifically for year-long goals. Set any target (20, 52, 100 books), and the template tracks your progress with a visual bar, current pace, estimated completion date, and a weekly reading pace recommendation to stay on track. Many users also run multiple challenge types simultaneously (e.g., annual goal + Popsugar categories).
Does the template include a book notes or highlights database?
Yes. Each book in your database has a linked notes page where you can store key quotes, main ideas, personal reflections, how the book changed your thinking, action items from non-fiction reads, and character notes for fiction. You can paste Kindle highlights directly into the quotes section or write notes by hand. This is the feature that makes this template genuinely better than Goodreads or StoryGraph for serious readers.
How does the genre diversity tracking work?
The Genre Diversity Dashboard uses Notion formulas to count how many books you've read per genre, shows your genre breakdown as a percentage, and flags categories you haven't explored yet. You can set intentional targets for genres like translated fiction, debut authors, classics, or specific decades to diversify your reading. The dashboard updates automatically as you log new books — no manual tracking needed.
Is there a book recommendation system built into the template?
Yes. The TBR (To Be Read) database includes a 'Source of Recommendation' field so you can log where each book came from — a friend, podcast, BookTok, a newsletter, or a comp title from another book. You can filter your TBR by recommender source to see which sources produce your highest-rated reads over time. After a year of data, you'll know exactly whose book recommendations to trust — and whose to skip.
