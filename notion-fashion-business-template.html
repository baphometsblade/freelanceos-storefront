Collection Development

From Concept to Approved Tech Pack — Every Step Tracked

The pre-production workflow in this template takes you from initial concept through design brief, design review, tech pack, sample rounds, and final approval — with every decision documented per style.

1

Concept to Design Brief

Each collection starts with a concept record: season name, inspiration direction, target customer, price positioning, and a mood board link. This brief becomes the reference point for every design decision in the collection. When a style starts drifting off-concept during development, you have the brief to anchor the conversation.

2

Design Review Process

Each design moves through a review checklist: sketch or digital rendering complete, colorways defined, fabric assigned, fit notes added, and design brief alignment checked. Designs that pass review move to tech pack creation. Those that need revision stay in a flagged status with notes on what to address. You always know which styles are ready to move and which are waiting.

3

Tech Pack Checklist Per Style

The tech pack checklist ensures every style going to production has complete documentation: front and back sketch, detail illustrations, measurement spec sheet, material call-out with exact specifications, hardware details, and label placement diagram. One incomplete tech pack can delay an entire production run. The checklist catches gaps before they become problems.

4

Sample Feedback Rounds

Each sample is logged in the production tracker with the date received, round number (first sample, second sample, pre-production sample), fit notes, construction comments, and approval or revision decision. When you have multiple styles in sampling simultaneously, the tracker view shows you exactly where every style stands without emailing the factory for a status update.

Production & Supply Chain

Factory Communication and Quality Control in One Place

The production section goes beyond a simple checklist. It tracks every supplier relationship, production order, QC result, and delivery schedule so you know the status of every style at any moment.

Fabric Supplier Database

Every fabric mill and materials supplier in one database with lead times, minimum order quantities, price per meter, current reliability rating, and past order history. When you are starting a new collection, filter by fiber type and lead time to find the right suppliers for your delivery window. Lead times in your database reflect real experience, not supplier promises.

Factory Communication Log

Every production communication logged by date — purchase order sent, factory acknowledgement received, production start confirmed, mid-production QC visit notes, delivery date updated, and shipment tracking number recorded. When a deadline is missed you have the full communication history ready for a professional conversation with the factory about resolution.

QC Checklist Per Style

A quality control checklist covers construction quality, measurement accuracy, fabric hand and appearance, hardware function, label and hang tag correctness, and packaging standards. Failed QC results are logged with photos linked from your reference folder. Repeated QC failures from a specific factory feed into the supplier performance rating.

Delivery Tracking Per Order

Each production order has a delivery status field with expected ship date, actual ship date, tracking number, and received-at-warehouse confirmation. When styles are arriving across multiple factories and freight shipments, this view tells you exactly what is in transit, what has been received, and what is still outstanding before your market deadlines.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Notion to manage a fashion design business?

Yes — Notion is an excellent tool for fashion designers and brands. Its relational database system lets you connect collections, individual designs, production orders, fabric suppliers, and wholesale buyer relationships into one workspace. The FreelanceOS Fashion Business Template is pre-built for indie designers, small fashion brands, and sustainable fashion companies.

How does the production tracker work?

The Production Tracker has one record per style per season. Each style moves through stages: fabric sourced, pattern made, sample ordered, sample reviewed, bulk production placed, bulk production received, QC passed, and delivery shipped. You can see the entire collection status on a single Kanban board — every style as a card, staged by production phase. No more wondering which styles are in sampling and which are delayed at the factory.

Does the template include a wholesale buyer CRM?

Yes. The Wholesale Buyer and Stockist CRM tracks each buyer or retail account with their contact details, geographic territory, buyer email and phone, current season order status, delivery schedule, reorder history, and relationship notes. You can filter by region, by current season order status, or by buyers due for a follow-up.

Can I track fabric and materials sourcing in this template?

Yes. The Fabric and Materials Sourcing database has one record per material with supplier name, contact, material description, lead time, minimum order quantity, price per meter or unit, and current stock notes. You can link each material record to the designs and production orders that use it, so when a fabric lead time changes you can immediately see which styles are affected.

Does the template include cost and margin tracking?

Yes. The Cost and Margin Sheet tracks COGS per style including fabric cost, trim cost, manufacturing cost, sampling cost, and shipping cost. A formula calculates your cost of goods sold, and you enter your wholesale and retail price to see gross margin percentage. This lets you make pricing decisions based on actual data rather than intuition.

Is this template suitable for sustainable fashion brands?

Yes — the template includes fields specifically for sustainable fashion operations including material certification tracking for GOTS, OEKO-TEX, Bluesign, and other certifications; ethical supplier notes; and transparency documentation links. The fabric sourcing database has a sustainability tier field so you can quickly see which suppliers meet your standards and which are under review.
