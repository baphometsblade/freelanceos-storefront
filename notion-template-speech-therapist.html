Frequently Asked Questions

Questions From Speech Therapists and SLPs

Is Notion HIPAA-compliant for speech therapy records?

No. Notion is not a HIPAA-compliant platform and must not be used to store patient health information (PHI), clinical session notes, assessment data, or any identifiable patient records. All clinical documentation must remain in your certified EMR/EHR system. FreelanceOS Pro for SLPs is designed strictly for administrative workflows, professional development, and resource organisation — areas where HIPAA does not apply because no PHI is stored.

How do speech therapists use Notion for their practice?

SLPs use Notion for the administrative layer of their practice — the parts that don't involve clinical records. This includes scheduling coordination (using client codes rather than names), tracking CPD hours for licence renewal, maintaining a searchable library of intervention activities and materials, logging billing admin follow-ups, and storing parent communication templates. FreelanceOS Pro is built around exactly these use cases.

Can I track my CEU hours in Notion?

Yes — CEU tracking is one of the strongest use cases for Notion in SLP practice. Log each course or conference with the provider, delivery method, hours earned, topic area, completion date, and certificate reference. Create a summary view that shows your total hours by category and renewal period. Many SLPs find that having a running log eliminates the biennial stress of piecing together their CEU record for licence renewal.

How do I build an activity library in Notion?

Create a Notion database where each record is an intervention activity or resource. Tag each record with the area (articulation, language, fluency, AAC), age range, materials needed, source, and a link to the digital version if applicable. Add a description of how you use it in practice. Over time, this becomes a searchable, personal catalogue of everything you've collected across your career — conferences, courses, TpT purchases, and materials you've created yourself.

What's the best Notion setup for private practice SLPs?

Private practice SLPs benefit most from Notion as an administrative hub: a caseload admin scheduler (with client codes, not names), a billing admin tracker for authorisation and invoice follow-up, a CPD log for licence renewal, and a resource library for activities and communication templates. FreelanceOS Pro is designed around these four core modules with additional sections for research and home practice template building.

How do I organise home practice materials in Notion?

Build a library of home practice template structures — one record per programme type (articulation home practice, language home activities, fluency strategies for parents). Each template includes the general structure, activity types, guidance language for caregivers, and frequency recommendations. When you need to create materials for a client, open the relevant template and adapt it. This is far faster than creating home practice documents from scratch each time.

Can I use Notion for parent and caregiver communication?

Yes — Notion is excellent for managing communication template libraries. Store professional letter frameworks, FAQ response templates, home practice guidance structures, and referral letter outlines. When you need to communicate with a family, open the relevant template, adapt it for the situation, and send it. FreelanceOS Pro includes a pre-built template library section specifically for SLP parent and caregiver communication.

How do I track continuing professional development as an SLP?

The FreelanceOS CPD tracker lets you log each development activity with: provider, topic, delivery format, CPD hours earned, date completed, certificate reference, and relevance to your practice area. Create filtered views to see hours by renewal period or professional body requirement category. Export or screenshot the log at renewal time rather than hunting through years of emails and paper certificates. RCSLT, ASHA, and state licensing board requirements all have different categories — the tracker is flexible enough to handle any framework.
