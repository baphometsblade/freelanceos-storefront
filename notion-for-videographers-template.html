The Problem

Running a Video Business Without a System Is Exhausting

Every videographer eventually hits a wall. The projects pile up, the clients multiply, and your "system" becomes a disaster of open browser tabs, scattered Google Docs, and missed deadlines.

🗂️

Projects Without a Production Timeline

You're juggling wedding films, corporate videos, and YouTube series — but there's no single view showing where every project stands in the pipeline. Pre-production bleeds into post, deadlines sneak up, and nothing feels under control.

📷

Gear Inventory Scattered Across Notes

Your camera bodies, lenses, gimbals, audio gear, and lighting rigs are logged across three different apps — or worse, nowhere at all. You forget serial numbers for insurance claims and can't quickly see what gear is available for an upcoming shoot.

📧

Client Revision Requests Lost in Email Threads

Version 3 feedback is buried in a Gmail thread from six weeks ago. Your client swears they asked for something in round 2, you have no way to verify it, and the project scope has quietly expanded beyond the original agreement.

💸

Income That's Irregular and Hard to Track

You know you've been busy, but your bank account doesn't reflect it. Invoices go out late, outstanding payments slip through the cracks, and at tax time you're manually reconstructing a year's worth of project revenue from memory.

FAQ

Everything You Need to Know

Common questions from videographers before they get started.

Does this Notion videographer template work for all video niches? +
Yes. The template is designed for wedding videographers, YouTube creators, corporate video producers, documentary filmmakers, event videographers, and music video directors. The modular system adapts to any video production workflow with customizable project types, client categories, and delivery formats. Whether you shoot one wedding a week or manage a roster of corporate retainer clients, the same system handles both.
Can I track multiple active video projects simultaneously? +
Absolutely. The Project Dashboard uses a Kanban-style pipeline showing all active projects across Pre-Production, In Shoot, Post-Production, Review, and Delivered stages at once. You can filter by client, project type, deadline, or revenue to focus on what matters most. There's no practical limit to how many projects the database can hold — videographers with 10+ concurrent projects use it successfully.
Does this replace project management apps like ClickUp or StudioNinja? +
For most freelance videographers, yes. The template consolidates project tracking, client CRM, shot lists, gear inventory, post-production logging, and invoicing into a single Notion workspace — eliminating the need for 3-4 separate tools and their monthly subscription fees. You save $50-150/month in software subscriptions while getting a system that's more tailored to video workflows than any generic PM tool.
Is this template good for small video agencies? +
Yes. The Client CRM and Business Development modules scale well for agencies handling multiple clients simultaneously. The template supports team collaboration through Notion's shared workspace features, making it easy for multiple editors or producers to access the same project hub. You can assign projects to team members and use Notion's comment system to centralize all production communication.
Can I track equipment rentals with the Gear Inventory module? +
Yes. The Gear Inventory module includes fields for rental value, daily rate, availability status, and rental history so you can track both owned gear and equipment you rent out to other videographers. It also logs serial numbers, warranty dates, purchase prices, and insurance values for every item — making it easy to generate accurate documentation for insurance claims or equipment audits.
Does the template include invoice templates for video projects? +
Yes. The Invoice & Income Tracker module includes a complete invoicing system with project fee fields, milestone payments, retainer tracking, expense logging, outstanding invoice alerts, and monthly income summaries. You can generate invoice data and export it to your preferred invoicing tool, or use the Notion pages directly as invoice records. The system also tracks payment status so outstanding invoices are always visible.
