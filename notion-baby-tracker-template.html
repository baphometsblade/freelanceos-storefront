The New Parent Reality

You're Running on No Sleep and Way Too Many Questions

Before we talk solutions, let's just acknowledge: this season of life is beautiful and absolutely exhausting. You're doing an incredible job. And you deserve better tools.

It's 3:17am. You just finished feeding. You're trying to remember — was that 4oz or 5oz? Was this the left or right side? How long has it been since her last diaper change? Did she sleep for 45 minutes or an hour? And your partner is asking you from across the room because they're up for the handoff and need to know where things stand.

Meanwhile, on your phone: seventeen different apps, a notes app with half-finished entries, and a crumpled sticky note somewhere in the baby bag that you meant to update three days ago.

Was it 4oz or 5oz? Which breast did she nurse on last? When was the last feeding? How many times has she eaten today? These questions shouldn't feel hard — but at 3am on two hours of sleep, everything feels hard.

Everything You Want to Know Before You Buy

Does this Notion baby tracker template include a feeding tracker?
▼
Yes, and it's one of the most detailed features in the whole workspace. The feeding tracker records time, type (breast/bottle/solid), duration in minutes for nursing sessions, ounces consumed from bottle, which side was used for nursing, formula type and brand if applicable, and a free-text notes field. It also automatically calculates daily feeding totals so you can see at a glance how much your baby has eaten each day without doing any math. You can track multiple feeding types as your baby transitions from nursing to bottle to solids, and the database structure means you can filter and sort your feeding history however is most useful to you.
Can I track my baby's sleep with this Notion template?
▼
Absolutely. The sleep log is built to track the full picture of your baby's sleep, not just bedtime and wake time. You can log each individual nap with start and end time, your baby's bedtime and morning wake time, how baby fell asleep (rocking, nursing, self-soothed, in arms), where they slept (crib, bassinet, contact nap, car, stroller), and notes on sleep quality. The template automatically calculates total nighttime sleep hours, total daytime nap hours, and the combined 24-hour sleep total for each day. As weeks pass, you'll naturally start to see sleep patterns and can spot regressions early — information that's genuinely useful when discussing your baby's sleep with a pediatrician or sleep consultant.
How does the milestone tracker work in this Notion baby template?
▼
The milestone tracker is a full Notion database where each milestone gets its own entry. For every milestone, you record the milestone name (e.g., "First Smile", "First Roll", "First Steps"), the exact date it happened, your baby's age in weeks and days so you'll always know precisely how old they were, a notes field where you can write the full story of the moment, and a photo or video attachment field so the memory lives alongside the record. You can categorize milestones by type — motor skills, social/emotional, language and communication, and cognitive — and filter or sort by category to see your baby's developmental journey at a glance. It becomes a beautiful record you'll look back on for years.
Does this template help me track pediatrician visits?
▼
Yes — the pediatrician visit log is one of the most practical parts of the template. For each visit, you record the date and appointment type (newborn, 2-month, 4-month, etc.), your baby's weight, height, and head circumference, the doctor's name, their notes and observations from the visit, any concerns you raised and the responses you received, vaccines given at that appointment, and follow-up action items. There's also a pre-visit question builder — a dedicated field where you can add questions as you think of them in between appointments. Then when you get to the visit, you work through your list and check them off. Parents tell us this is one of the most-used features because it completely eliminates the "I had a whole list of questions and I forgot them all" feeling.
Is there a vaccination record tracker included?
▼
Yes. The vaccination record database logs every vaccine with the vaccine name, date administered, the provider who administered it, the lot number (useful for any recalls), the next due date for that particular vaccine series, and notes on any reactions or side effects observed. You can see at a glance which vaccines have been completed, which are part of a series still in progress, and which upcoming vaccines are on the horizon and when they're due. This means when your partner texts "did we do the 2-month shots?" you can answer instantly and accurately — no hunting for the physical card, no uncertainty, no guessing.
Does this Notion baby tracker include growth charts?
▼
Yes. The growth chart tracker records your baby's weight, height/length, and head circumference at every checkup, along with the percentile they fall in for each measurement. Over time, this builds a complete growth record that you can reference at any appointment, share with family, or use to have informed conversations with your pediatrician. Having the data over multiple visits lets you see trends — consistent growth, any dips or jumps — so you're working from real information rather than memory. The growth tracker also has a doctor's assessment notes field so you can record what your pediatrician said about your baby's growth trajectory at each visit.
