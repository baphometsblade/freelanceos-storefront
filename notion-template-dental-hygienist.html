Never Miss a CE Deadline Again

CE Credit Tracker That Actually Works

The CE Credit Tracker database holds one row per continuing education course. Properties include: Course Name, Provider (dental school, ADHA, Dental Products Shoppe, online CE platform, local study club), Credit Hours (number property), Category (Infection Control, Radiography, Pharmacology, Ethics, Clinical Topics, Local Anesthesia, Nitrous Oxide, Medical Emergencies, Periodontal Therapy, OSHA, or any custom category), Completion Date, Certificate Number, and an Attachment field for uploading your PDF certificate directly to the record.

The renewal dashboard at the top of the CE section uses a rollup formula to calculate total credit hours earned in the current renewal period, compared against your state's required total. A formula property calculates days until renewal and changes color based on urgency: more than 90 days shows green, 60-90 days shows yellow, under 60 days shows red. This simple visual means you always know your position without mentally calculating from memory.

Filter views let you slice your CE data by category — need to check your infection control hours specifically? One filter, instant answer. Preparing for renewal submission? Sort by Completion Date and export as CSV to submit to your board with confidence that the data is complete and accurate. No more hunting through email for the confirmation receipt from a webinar you took 14 months ago.

Multi-Credential Renewal Calendar

Dental hygienists hold multiple credentials simultaneously — and different states require different renewal timelines for each. The credential database tracks every license and certification separately: your RDH state license, CPR/BLS certification, local anesthesia permit, nitrous oxide/conscious sedation permit, coronal polishing certification if applicable, and any specialty certifications like EFDA or public health credentials.

Each credential record includes: Credential Name, Issuing Body, Credential Number, Issue Date, Renewal Date, CE Hours Required for Renewal, CE Hours Completed (linked rollup from the CE tracker), and Status (Active, Needs Renewal, Expired). A calendar view shows all upcoming renewal dates on a visual timeline, making it immediately obvious when credential deadlines cluster together and need advance planning.

The most common credential management mistake dental hygienists make is forgetting about permit renewals that have different cycles from their main RDH license. A local anesthesia permit that expires mid-year in a different year than your RDH renewal is easy to miss when you're only watching one deadline. This tracker puts all of them in one place with individual alerts.

Plan Your Career With Clarity

Beyond the Dental Chair: Career Pathways for RDHs

The dental hygiene profession offers far more career trajectories than many hygienists realize when they graduate. The Career Goals section maps out four major pathways with milestone trackers for each: Clinical Excellence (pursuing periodontal therapy specialization, ADHA active membership, peer mentoring), Dental Education (adjunct faculty positions, ADHE membership, clinical coordinator roles), Dental Sales and Consulting (medical device and dental product rep positions, continuing education facilitation), and Practice Ownership or Management (office management track, business education, financial planning milestones).

Each pathway has a milestone database where you can track specific goals: "Attend ADHA Annual Session," "Complete local anesthesia certification," "Apply for dental hygiene program adjunct position," "Shadow a dental consultant for one day." Mark milestones complete, add target dates, and link relevant resources. The career roadmap stops being a vague aspiration and becomes a trackable plan with visible progress.

The skill development section maps clinical competencies you want to strengthen or develop — laser hygiene therapy, cone beam CT interpretation, motivational interviewing techniques, hard and soft tissue assessment skills. Rate your current proficiency, set a target proficiency level, and link the CE courses you're planning to take to close the gap. Intentional skill development rather than just random CE credit accumulation.

Professional Tools That Work for Your Life

Personal Equipment Management

Many dental hygienists invest significantly in personal instrument kits — and those instruments perform better and last longer when properly maintained and tracked. The Personal Equipment database holds one record per instrument: Instrument Name (e.g., "Gracey 11/12," "Nevi 1," "Barnhart 5/6"), Manufacturer, Purchase Date, Sharpening Count (a number property you increment after each sharpening session), Last Sharpened date, Condition (Excellent/Good/Needs Sharpening/Replace), and a flag for replacement needed.

Filter the database by "Condition = Needs Sharpening" before every sharpening session to know exactly what to pull. Sort by sharpening count to identify instruments nearing end of life before they become a problem chairside. Track your ultrasonic insert replacement schedule and suction tip inventory on the same system for complete equipment awareness going into each shift.
