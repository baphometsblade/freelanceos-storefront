Client Intake & Onboarding

The quality of your initial intake determines the quality of your recommendations. This structure ensures you capture everything relevant before giving any guidance.

Health History & Context

  • Current health conditions (relevant context, not clinical depth)
  • Medications that may affect metabolism or digestion
  • Previous nutrition interventions and outcomes
  • Family history context (obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular)
  • Digestive issues, intolerances, or sensitivities
  • Hormonal context where relevant to goals
  • Relationship with food — any history of disordered eating

Dietary Preferences & Lifestyle

  • Dietary preferences: omnivore, vegetarian, vegan, pescatarian
  • Specific food restrictions and strong dislikes
  • Foods they currently eat regularly and enjoy
  • Cooking ability and time available for food prep
  • Household situation (cooking for family, eating solo)
  • Work schedule and when they typically eat
  • Budget constraints for grocery shopping

Goal Setting Framework

  • Primary goal: weight, energy, digestion, performance, etc.
  • Timeline expectations and milestone checkpoints
  • Previous experience with goal setting — what's worked, what hasn't
  • Readiness and motivation level (1–10)
  • Potential barriers and obstacles they anticipate
  • Support system at home or socially
  • Definition of success at 3, 6, and 12 months

First Session Agenda Checklist

  • Review intake form and clarify any gaps
  • Walk through current eating patterns — a typical day
  • Establish rapport and explain your coaching approach
  • Agree on 1–2 initial focus areas (not everything at once)
  • Set the first week's action steps — specific and achievable
  • Explain how check-ins work and what you expect from them
  • Set the next session date and confirm communication channel

Practice Management — What Goes in Notion vs. What Stays Elsewhere

Notion is an excellent tool for nutrition coaching business management. Here's a clear picture of what belongs here and what should stay in a clinically appropriate system.

Use Notion for these

  • ✓ Client contact details and communication preferences
  • ✓ General dietary preferences and lifestyle context
  • ✓ Meal plan templates and the client's assigned plan
  • ✓ Session notes covering coaching focus and next steps
  • ✓ Progress metrics: weight, energy, adherence, digestion notes
  • ✓ Weekly check-in responses and trend tracking
  • ✓ Educational handouts and general nutrition resources
  • ✓ Group program curriculum and delivery schedule
  • ✓ Business tracking: invoices, packages, referrals, revenue
  • ✓ Content planning and social media pipeline

For registered dietitians and clinical practitioners

  • ⚑ Clinical medical histories requiring HIPAA protection — use your compliant EMR
  • ⚑ Prescription-level dietary interventions — maintain in your clinical system
  • ⚑ Diagnostic assessments or clinical lab interpretations
  • ⚑ Insurance billing and clinical billing records
  • ⚑ Legally protected health information under your jurisdictional requirements
This template is built for coaching practice management. If your scope of practice requires clinical-grade data management, maintain clinical records in appropriate systems. Use FreelanceOS as your operational business layer on top.
