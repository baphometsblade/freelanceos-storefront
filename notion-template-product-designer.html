Frequently Asked Questions

Is this template for product designers specifically, or graphic designers and UX researchers?
This template is specifically for product designers — people who own the end-to-end design of digital products from discovery through delivery. If you design SaaS products, mobile apps, enterprise software, or consumer platforms, this is built for you. FreelanceOS also has separate templates optimized for graphic designers, which covers print and brand design, and UX researchers, which focuses on research-heavy roles distinct from full product ownership.
How does the research repository work?
The Research Repository holds one entry per study — user interviews, usability tests, surveys, card sorts, A/B tests, competitive analyses, diary studies — with date, participants, method, key insights, quotes bank, behavioral observations, design recommendations derived, and linked project. Research from past projects becomes searchable institutional memory you carry forward. Filter by research type, date, or project to pull relevant insights when starting a new feature or redesign.
What is the design decision log and why does it matter?
The Design Decision Log documents each major design choice with context, the alternatives you considered and why you did not choose them, rationale, who approved it, date, a Figma reference link, and the post-launch outcome. It prevents the "why did we design it this way" question from derailing design reviews, makes onboarding new teammates significantly faster, and gives you rich material for portfolio case studies where the reasoning behind decisions is as important as the final design.
Can I use this for freelance product design client management?
Yes. The Design Project Tracker includes invoice status for freelancers. The Freelance Client CRM and Invoice Tracker is explicitly included in the template. You get the full project management and research system that in-house designers use, plus the freelance business operations layer — client contact, billing status, and contract notes — layered on top.
