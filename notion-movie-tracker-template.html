Step-by-Step Setup

How to Build Your Notion Film Database

From blank Notion page to fully operational film tracker in under an hour. Here's how the FreelanceOS movie template is structured — and why each decision was made.

1

Duplicate the Template and Set Up Your Core Database

Once you grab FreelanceOS Pro, duplicate the movie tracker into your Notion workspace. The core Movies database comes pre-loaded with properties: Title, Year, Director, Genre, Country, Language, Runtime, Personal Rating, Craft Rating, Date Watched, Status, and a Notes field. Don't change these right away — live with the defaults for a week first.

Tip: Add your 20 most recent watched films first to get a feel for the structure before building out the full watchlist.
2

Decide What Fields Matter Most to You

The template includes every field a serious film enthusiast might want — but you don't have to fill them all. The essentials are Title, Director, Year, Status, and Rating. The nice-to-haves are Country, Language, Cinematographer, and Screenplay Writer. The obsessive fields (production company, award nominations, aspect ratio) are there if you want them.

Tip: Use the Cinematographer field if you care about visual style — it helps you discover that you keep loving the same DPs without realising it.
3

Add Cover Images for Your Gallery View

The gallery view is what makes the tracker feel like a real film library rather than a spreadsheet. For each movie page, click "Add cover" and paste a poster URL from TMDB (themoviedb.org) — every film has a high-res poster you can right-click and copy. Takes 10 seconds per film and completely changes how the database looks.

Tip: TMDB has a free API if you want to automate poster fetching via Zapier or Make — worth setting up once you've logged 50+ films.
4

Set Up Your Mood and Genre Filtered Views

The template includes pre-built filtered views: "Tonight's Watch" (filters by <120 min, 7+ rating, Not Watched), "Short Films" (<60 min), "Long Films" (>150 min), "Comedy Night," "Director Deep Dives," and "Hidden Gems" (your 8+ rated films most people haven't heard of). Tweak the filters to match your actual taste.

Tip: Create a "Comfort Rewatch" view filtered to your 9–10 rated films for those evenings when you want something guaranteed.
5

Import Your Existing Watchlists

Letterboxd exports your diary as a CSV (Settings → Export Data). IMDb lets you export your ratings list similarly. Import both into Notion via the CSV import feature — the columns map cleanly. Your existing watch history comes with you, and you can clean up duplicates as you go. Most people's full import takes 20–30 minutes.

Tip: Don't try to import everything at once. Import your Letterboxd diary first (your definitive watched history), then fill gaps from IMDb.

Advanced Usage

For the Serious Film Enthusiast

Beyond basic logging — the features that turn your Notion film tracker into a genuine cinephile's toolkit.

🏆

Film Festival Tracking

Add a "Film Festival" multi-select property and tag films with Cannes, Sundance, TIFF, Venice, or any festival you follow. Filter to see your full festival watchlist by year, or track which Cannes Palme d'Or winners you've seen. Great for building a challenge: "watch every Cannes winner of the last decade."

💡

Thematic Lists — "Films That Changed How I See Things"

Create a "Lists" database linked to your movie tracker. Build curated lists: films that changed your worldview, films set in cities you've visited, films you watched during a specific period of your life, films your dad introduced you to. Notion's relation property makes it frictionless to add films to multiple lists simultaneously.

🎥

Director Filmographies — Complete the Career

The Director database shows you exactly how far through a filmmaker's work you are. Choose a director whose work you want to explore (Agnès Varda, Hou Hsiao-hsien, Hirokazu Kore-eda) and use the "Not Watched" filter on their filmography to build an ordered watchlist. Work through a career chronologically rather than at random.

💎

Hidden Gem Tracking — Your Personal Best-Kept Secrets

Add a "Well Known" toggle (derived from box office or IMDB vote count) and filter your 8+ rated films where Well Known is false. The result is your personal hidden gem list — films you loved that most people haven't seen. Easily shareable, endlessly discussable, and the kind of list that makes people want to talk film with you.

🤝

Comparing Ratings With Friends

The "Friends' Picks" linked database lets you log what your film-loving friends recommended, what they rated it, and what you rated it after watching. Over time you build a profile of which friends have taste aligned with yours — and whose recommendations you should perhaps treat with a bit more skepticism.

📊

Year-in-Review Stats

Filter your Watched Log by current year and count: how many films did you watch? What was your average rating? How many countries did you cover? How many films were made before you were born? Notion's formula and rollup properties let you surface surprisingly interesting statistics about your own viewing without any extra setup.
