How to Build Your Notion Film Database

From blank Notion page to fully operational film tracker in under an hour. Here's how the template is structured and why each decision was made.

1

Duplicate the Template Into Your Workspace

After purchasing FreelanceOS Pro, duplicate the movie tracker into Notion. The core Movies database comes pre-loaded: Title, Year, Director, Genre, Country, Language, Runtime, Personal Rating, Craft Rating, Date Watched, Status, Notes. Live with the defaults for a week before customising.

Tip: Add your 20 most recent films first to feel the structure before tackling your full watchlist.
2

Decide Which Fields You Actually Need

Essentials: Title, Director, Year, Status, Rating. Nice-to-haves: Country, Language, Cinematographer. Optional deep-cuts: production company, award nominations, aspect ratio. The template includes everything — use only what adds value to your watching habits.

Tip: Add the Cinematographer field if you care about visual style — you'll quickly discover you keep loving the same DPs.
3

Add Poster Images for Your Gallery View

For each movie page, click "Add cover" and paste a poster URL from TMDB (themoviedb.org). Takes 10 seconds per film and transforms the database from a spreadsheet into a visual film library. The template includes a pre-built Gallery view ready to go.

Tip: TMDB has a free API — worth setting up with Zapier once you've logged 50+ films.
4

Activate Mood and Genre Filtered Views

Pre-built views: "Tonight's Watch" (<120 min, 7+ rating, unwatched), "Short Films," "Long Films," "Comedy Night," "Director Deep Dives," and "Hidden Gems" (8+ rated, lesser-known picks). Tweak filter values to match your actual taste.

Tip: Add a "Comfort Rewatch" view filtered to 9–10 rated films for evenings when you want something guaranteed.
5

Import Your Existing Watchlists

Letterboxd exports your diary as CSV (Settings → Export Data). IMDb does the same for ratings. Import via Notion's CSV import — columns map cleanly. Most full imports take 20–30 minutes. Import Letterboxd first (your definitive history), then fill gaps from IMDb.

Tip: Don't try to be exhaustive immediately. Get your watched history right first, then build the watchlist.

Advanced Usage

For the Serious Film Enthusiast

Beyond basic logging — features that turn your Notion film tracker into a genuine cinephile's toolkit.

🏆

Film Festival Tracking

Tag films with Cannes, Sundance, TIFF, Venice, or any festival you follow. Filter by year or track which Palme d'Or winners you've seen. Build challenges: "watch every Cannes winner of the last decade."

💡

Thematic Lists — "Films That Changed How I See Things"

Link a "Lists" database to your tracker and build curated collections: films that changed your worldview, films set in cities you've visited, films your dad introduced you to. Notion's relation property makes adding films to multiple lists completely frictionless.

🎥

Director Filmographies — Complete the Career

The Director database shows exactly how far through a filmmaker's work you are. Pick a director (Agnès Varda, Kore-eda, Hou Hsiao-hsien) and use the "Not Watched" filter to build a chronological watchlist. Work through a career systematically, not randomly.

💎

Hidden Gem Tracking

Filter your 8+ rated films where "Well Known" is false. The result is your personal hidden gem list — films you loved that most people haven't seen. The kind of list that makes people want to talk film with you.

🤝

Comparing Ratings With Friends

Log what friends recommended, their rating, and yours after watching. Over time you build a profile of which friends have taste aligned with yours — and whose recommendations to treat with mild skepticism.

📊

Year-in-Review Stats

Filter by year: films watched, average rating, countries covered, films made before you were born. Notion's formula and rollup properties surface genuinely interesting viewing statistics with zero extra setup.
