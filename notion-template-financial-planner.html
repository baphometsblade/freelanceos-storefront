Build a Systematic Practice Without the Complexity

Meeting Preparation That Impresses Clients

The meeting notes template is a Notion template button that generates a new structured page for each client meeting. The pre-meeting section captures: scheduled agenda topics, documents or statements you requested in advance, and a pull of open action items from the previous meeting. The during-meeting section has fields for discussion notes, client goals or concerns mentioned, and any decisions made in the meeting. The post-meeting section captures next steps with owner, documents to send, and the next review date.

Critical compliance note: keep these notes general and non-sensitive. This is a preparation and context-capture tool for your own use — not a substitute for the regulated client records your firm requires you to maintain in your CRM. If your firm's compliance guidance restricts what you can document outside approved systems, follow that guidance. The value here is structured preparation, not documentation substitution.

Financial planners who arrive at meetings visibly prepared — with the previous meeting's action items already pulled up, with an agenda already sent — are perceived as more professional and trustworthy regardless of their technical competence. Preparation signals that you take the relationship seriously. This template builds that preparation habit without requiring it to live in your head.

Annual Review Ready Every Time

The Annual Review Checklist is a template button that generates a per-client review preparation page. Different sections cover different review types: comprehensive financial plan review (updated goals, income changes, major life events, risk tolerance check), investment portfolio review (asset allocation, performance vs benchmark, rebalancing needed), insurance review (coverage gaps, beneficiary verification, policy performance), and estate planning check-in (will and trust currency, beneficiary designations, power of attorney adequacy).

Each checklist creates a dated record of what was covered in that year's review cycle. When a client asks "when did we last discuss my life insurance?" you have a logged, dated answer — not a vague memory. When a new compliance requirement appears about documenting what topics were covered in annual reviews, you have a habit already established. The discipline of structured annual reviews consistently produces better client outcomes and better advisor confidence.

Grow Your Practice With Better Systems

Business Development Pipeline That Gets Used

The Business Development Pipeline database tracks every prospect from initial identification to active client relationship. Each prospect record moves through stages: Referred or Identified, Initial Contact Made, Discovery Meeting Scheduled, Discovery Meeting Completed, Proposal or Financial Plan Delivered, Compliance and Paperwork, Active Client. Each stage has a description of what "done" looks like so you never leave a prospect stuck between stages without a clear next action.

Properties per prospect include: Full Name, Referral Source (which COI or client referred them), Estimated AUM or Income Level (general ranges only, no specific financial details), What They're Looking For (retirement, estate planning, business exit, young professional services), Next Action, Next Action Due Date, and your personal Priority level. Filter the pipeline by stage for your weekly pipeline review — a 15-minute Friday ritual that surfaces every warm prospect who's been sitting without follow-up for more than a week.

The weekly pipeline review template generates a structured review page: how many prospects at each stage, who needs a follow-up call this week, who has upcoming meetings, and who has gone cold and needs a different approach or should be moved to inactive. Financial planners who run a systematic pipeline review consistently convert more prospects than those who rely on memory and intent alone.

CE That Doesn't Slip Through the Cracks

The CFP Board requires 30 hours of CE every two years, with at least 2 hours in CFP Board-approved Ethics CE. The CE Tracker database logs each course with: Course Name, Provider, Credit Hours (number property), Delivery Type (in-person live, self-study, exam, nano-learning), CFP Board Topic Category (Financial Planning Process, General Principles, Estate Planning, Tax Planning, Retirement, Insurance, Investment Management, Ethics), Completion Date, and whether the course was Ethics CE (checkbox).

Rollup properties at the top of the CE dashboard show: Total Hours Completed This Cycle, Ethics Hours Completed, and Days Until Renewal. The renewal date alert fires at 90 days out — far enough in advance to schedule any remaining hours without scrambling. CFA holders can create a parallel CE database with Level III-specific categories. ChFC, CLU, and other designation CE requirements get their own database records too, each tracking independently toward their own renewal cycles.

Professional Excellence Beyond the Numbers

Building Your Center of Influence Network

The Professional Network section includes a dedicated COI view — Centers of Influence who refer clients to you or to whom you refer clients. Each COI record tracks: Full Name, Firm, Specialty, How You Met, Referral Source History (general tracking — not client-specific details), Last Contact Date, Contact Frequency Goal, Relationship Quality (Strong, Building, Cold), Referrals Received (number), and Referrals Given (number).

Attorney referrals from estate planning attorneys, CPA referrals from accounting firms, referrals from mortgage brokers and real estate agents — each relationship has a different cultivation cadence and different mutual value proposition. The COI database surfaces who you haven't contacted in 60 or 90 days, who you've been receiving referrals from without reciprocating, and which relationships are warming versus cooling over time.

Most financial planners know intellectually that systematic COI cultivation drives practice growth more reliably than marketing. The gap between knowing and doing is almost always a system gap — there's no structure making the cultivation visible and consistent. This database closes that gap. Monthly: review your COI database, identify who to reach out to this month, and add a calendar event for the specific touchpoint.

Market Research and Planning Knowledge Base

The Financial Planning Library is a database of resources that inform your professional knowledge: economic research reports, articles on planning strategies, tax law updates, product guides, and conference takeaways. Each entry has: Title, Source, Publication Date, Topic Tags (Tax, Estate, Retirement, Insurance, Investment, Business Planning, Behavioral Finance, Economic Outlook), and a Summary or Key Takeaways page.

When a client asks about Roth conversion strategies given expected tax law changes, you don't need to reconstruct your position from memory — you search "Roth conversion" in your library and surface the articles and analysis you've tagged. When you're preparing a presentation for a prospect with a concentrated equity position, your library of resources on concentrated position strategies is one search away. The knowledge you accumulate becomes progressively more valuable as the library grows.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I store client portfolio details or account numbers in this template?
Absolutely not — this template is for professional organization only. All client financial data, account numbers, Social Security numbers, portfolio holdings, and any other Non-Public Personal Information (NPPI) must remain in FINRA and SEC-compliant systems approved by your compliance officer. Never store sensitive client information in Notion or any unvetted third-party application. Consult your compliance officer if you have any questions about what client information is appropriate to document outside your firm's systems.
What CE designations can I track?
Fully customizable — CFP (30 hours per 2-year period, 2 hours ethics required), CFA, ChFC, CLU, RICP, CRPC, CDFA, and any others. Create a separate database record for each designation with its specific requirement and renewal cycle. The CE tracker properties and rollup formulas adapt to whatever credit hour total and renewal period your designation requires.
Does this template handle compliance reporting?
No — this is for personal professional organization only. All regulatory reporting, trade records, client suitability documentation, supervision records, and compliance submissions must go through your firm's approved compliance systems. This template provides personal reminders of compliance training deadlines and firm certification due dates — it is not a compliance system and should not be treated as one.
Is this suitable for fee-only RIAs?
Yes — the practice management sections are compensation-model neutral and work well for fee-only, fee-based, or commission-based advisors. The business development pipeline and COI tracking sections are especially valuable for RIAs building a referral-based practice, since systematic relationship tracking is often where fee-only advisors find the most opportunity for improvement.
Can I use this to track COI relationships?
Yes — the professional network section includes a dedicated COI (Center of Influence) tracking view designed for referral relationship cultivation: estate planning attorneys, CPAs, commercial bankers, mortgage professionals, and other referral partners. Track relationship quality, last contact date, referrals given and received, and contact frequency goals. The system surfaces who you haven't reached out to recently and where reciprocal referral flow may be out of balance.
How does this differ from a CRM like Salesforce or Redtail?
This is a lightweight personal productivity and professional development system, not a CRM. Use it alongside your firm's CRM (Redtail, Salesforce Financial Services Cloud, Wealthbox, Junxure, etc.), not instead of it. This template fills the gaps that compliance-grade CRMs don't address: personal CE tracking, career goal planning, market research organization, and the general business development context that doesn't belong in regulated client records.
What if I'm a sole practitioner without a compliance officer?
Consult a compliance consultant before making decisions about data storage and record-keeping obligations as an independent advisor. Resources like NAPFA (for fee-only advisors), the Investment Adviser Association, and FINRA's BrokerCheck guidance for registered representatives can help you understand your independent obligations. This template does not substitute for professional compliance guidance, and the answer to compliance questions should always come from a qualified compliance professional, not from a productivity system.
