The Problem

Feng Shui Consulting Is Project Work — And Project Work Needs Project Management

Each feng shui assessment is a unique project: a specific property, a specific client goal, a specific school or methodology applied, recommendations delivered, and often a follow-up relationship that extends for years. Without a system to manage these projects, client history gets lost. Here's what an unmanaged feng shui practice looks like.

🏠

Assessment history lives in your email and consultation notes

A past client calls. They'd like a yearly flying star update. You need to know when you assessed their property, which school you applied, what the facing direction was, which remedies you recommended, and what annual chart year you were working in. This information is somewhere in your email, your printed notes, or your memory. A client assessment log that captures this at the time of the consultation means every follow-up call starts with complete professional context instead of a memory scramble.

🧭

Working notes from past assessments not systematically organized

Your feng shui assessment notes are thorough — facing directions, flying star charts, bagua overlay analysis, element cure recommendations, structural observations. But they live in a mix of handwritten notes, Word documents, and PDF reports scattered across your computer. When a client returns for an annual update or renovation consultation, reconstructing your previous analysis adds hours of unnecessary work. A structured assessment reference database makes your prior work instantly searchable and accessible.

🌿

Space clearing tracked separately from your main consulting work

Space clearing — predecessor energy clearing, new space blessing, move-in clearing, post-renovation clearing — often represents 20-30% of a feng shui consultant's service revenue. But if space clearing is treated as an informal side service managed through a separate booking process with no consistent tracking, you're missing revenue data, follow-up opportunities, and the ability to see how clearing services interact with your broader consulting business.

🔄

Annual flying star update clients not systematically contacted

The annual flying star chart changes with each lunar new year. Every client who received a Classical Flying Stars assessment has a natural reason to return for an annual update — but only if you contact them. Without a systematic annual update outreach tracker, you rely on clients to remember to ask. Most don't. This represents thousands in annual recurring revenue that most feng shui consultants leave on the table simply because they lack a system to reach out at the right moment.

Does this template work for Classical feng shui (Flying Stars, Eight Mansions) and BTB feng shui?
Yes. If you work across multiple schools — many practitioners blend Classical compass-based analysis with BTB bagua overlay — you can note which system or combination was applied to each client project. The bagua overlay method field specifically distinguishes three-door bagua entry method (standard BTB) from compass directions (Classical), so your working notes reflect the actual methodology used for each space.
How does the yearly flying star update tracker help me build recurring annual revenue?
For Classical Flying Stars practitioners, the annual flying star chart changes each lunar new year — which means existing clients whose spaces were assessed using Flying Stars need an annual update to understand which sectors are activated, afflicted, or favorable in the new year's chart. This annual update is a natural recurring revenue opportunity: it's a shorter service than a full assessment (typically 30-60 minute consultation or a written report), it requires no site visit for clients who received comprehensive reports, and clients who received a full assessment have a built-in reason to return every year. The yearly update tracker stores each past client's assessment date, which flying star chart year was used, and whether an annual update email has been sent for the current year — so you never miss the opportunity to reach out to your existing client base at the most natural time to do so.

Remote feng shui has expanded the addressable market for most practitioners significantly — clients who previously couldn't access in-person feng shui consultation can now receive a comprehensive analysis via photo and floor plan submission.
