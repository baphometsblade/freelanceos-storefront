Yes — every database is designed around the accessibility consultant's workflow: managing a pipeline of audit projects across WCAG 2.1/2.2 AA/AAA and Section 508 scopes, maintaining a professional reference library of WCAG success criteria and remediation guidance, tracking clients from initial inquiry through ongoing monitoring retainer, and running the business operations of an independent accessibility practice. It works for digital accessibility audits, ADA legal demand response, mobile app accessibility, PDF remediation, document accessibility, and team training engagements.
How does the WCAG findings reference library work?
The Findings & Issue Tracking Reference is your professional knowledge base — organized by WCAG principle (perceivable/operable/understandable/robust), success criterion number and name, common occurrence patterns, and your standard fix guidance. This is your reference library, not a client-specific finding list — the detailed audit report for each client stays in your project files. Use this to build and maintain your professional expertise and reuse guidance across every audit without rewriting it from scratch.
Can I manage both proactive compliance audits and ADA legal demand response projects?
Yes. The Accessibility Audit Project Tracker and Client Pipeline both include engagement type fields that distinguish proactive compliance audits from legal demand response. ADA demand letter response work is tracked separately because it has different urgency, pricing, and capacity implications — you can filter by engagement type to see your legal response pipeline vs. your proactive audit pipeline without mixing the two.
