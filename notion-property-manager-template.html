FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I manage multiple rental properties in one Notion template?
Yes — the template is built for multi-property portfolios. The central Property Portfolio database holds unlimited properties, each linked to its own tenant records, lease agreements, maintenance entries, and income/expense data. Landlords managing 1 to 20+ units use the same template without any modification.
How do I track rent payments and late fees in Notion?
The Rent Collection Tracker has one entry per unit per month with Paid, Late, Outstanding, and Partial status options. A filtered view showing all outstanding and late entries is your monthly collections dashboard. Late fee fields are built in, and rollup formulas calculate total outstanding balances across your portfolio.
Does this Notion template work for Airbnb and short-term rentals?
Yes. The template includes a dedicated Short-Term Rental section with a check-in/check-out calendar, cleaning crew task tracker, guest communication templates, review log, nightly rate history, and occupancy rate dashboard. You can run a mix of long-term and short-term units in the same workspace.
How does the maintenance log work?
Every maintenance request moves through a status pipeline: Reported → Assessed → Scheduled → In Progress → Completed → Invoiced. Each entry captures the property, unit, tenant who reported it, assigned vendor, estimated and actual cost, and photo link. A view filtered to open statuses is your daily work queue.
Can I use this template to prepare my taxes as a landlord?
The Annual Tax Prep Summary aggregates income and expenses per property for the calendar year across all standard Schedule E categories. It is an organizational tool designed to give your accountant a clean, categorized summary. It does not constitute tax advice — please consult a qualified tax professional for your specific situation.
What is included in the tenant database and lease tracker?
The Tenant Database stores full contact details, lease dates, monthly rent, security deposit held, and emergency contacts. The Lease Tracker focuses on renewal dates, rent escalation clauses, notice period requirements, and lease document links. A view sorted by soonest renewal — filtered to leases expiring in the next 90 days — means you never miss a renewal window.
