6 Features That Make This Invoice Tracker Actually Useful

Most Notion invoice templates are just a table with a few columns. This one is built the way a working freelancer actually thinks about money.

🔗

Linked to Your Clients

Each invoice links to a client record in the Client CRM database. Click into any invoice and see who owes you, their contact details, and all past invoices — without leaving the page.

📁

Linked to Projects

Tie every invoice to a specific project so you can see at a glance whether a payment covers phase 1, phase 2, or a retainer. Helps with scoping disputes and invoice numbering.

📊

Revenue Rollup Views

Filter by status to see total outstanding, total paid this month, or all overdue invoices. The database includes a formula property that calculates days overdue automatically.

🏷️

Invoice Number Auto-Tracking

A dedicated invoice number field with a consistent naming convention (INV-001 format) means you always have a clean paper trail for accounting and tax purposes.

💬

Notes + Follow-Up Log

Add payment notes, record when you sent reminders, or paste the client's payment confirmation reference number. Every invoice becomes a mini log of its own lifecycle.

📅

Issue Date + Due Date Tracking

Two separate date fields — when you issued the invoice and when it's due. Notion's date filter lets you build a view that shows everything due in the next 7 days automatically.
