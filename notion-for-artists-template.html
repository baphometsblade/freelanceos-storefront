How It Works

The Complete Commission Management System — Inquiry to Paid

A defined process for every commission means nothing gets lost, revision limits are respected, and payment is confirmed before you deliver the final file.

1

Inquiry Intake

Log every inquiry immediately — client handle, what they want, their budget, and deadline. Capture the key details from the DM into the database while you have them open. Status: Inquiry.

2

Deposit & Brief Confirmation

Once terms are agreed, mark Deposit Received. Log the deposit amount, payment method, agreed price, revision round limit, commercial vs. personal use, and the full brief in the commission page body.

3

WIP Updates

Track your progress notes in the commission record. Schedule update checkpoints as a linked task. Log every WIP image link. Your commission history becomes a professional record if any dispute arises.

4

Revision Tracking

Log each revision round separately. The Revisions Completed field counts against your agreed limit. When the client hits their revision cap, you have a documented record — not just a memory.

5

Delivery & Payment Confirmation

Mark Delivered when you send the final file. Log the delivery link. Move to Paid only when payment is confirmed. The testimonial ask prompt reminds you to request a review while the experience is fresh.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I track art commissions in Notion?
The Commission Pipeline database gives every commission a status: Inquiry, Deposit Received, In Progress, Revision, Delivered, and Paid. Each entry captures the client, price, deposit, deadline, revision round count, delivery link, and payment confirmation. A Kanban view shows your entire workload at a glance, and filtered views surface overdue commissions and commissions awaiting payment.
Can I track my art portfolio and sold pieces in Notion?
Yes — the Portfolio Database gives every piece its own record: title, medium, dimensions, creation date, price, status (For Sale, Sold, NFS), buyer, sale platform, and image link. Filter by status to see your available inventory. The Sales Tracker rolls up revenue per piece and per platform so you always know what's performing best.
Does this template work for digital illustrators as well as traditional artists?
Yes — the template is designed for digital illustrators, painters, photographers, and graphic designers taking commissions. The commission pipeline includes file delivery and commercial licensing fields for digital artists. The portfolio database has fields for physical dimensions and digital format. You can customize the medium and type select options to match your specific practice.
How do I track art sales across Etsy, Society6, and direct sales in Notion?
The Art Sales and Marketplace Tracker consolidates revenue from every platform into one database. Each sale entry has a platform field, product title, sale price, platform fee, and net revenue. Grouped views show total revenue per platform after fees. A Bestsellers view sorted by unit count shows your top-performing pieces across all channels.
Can I use this Notion template to manage exhibition submissions and grant applications?
Yes — the Exhibition and Show Tracker database handles open calls, gallery submissions, grant applications, residencies, and press features in a single pipeline. Each entry captures the opportunity, deadline, status (Researching, Applied, Accepted, Rejected), and response date. A deadline-sorted view ensures you never miss an application window.
What is the difference between the Commission Pipeline and the Client CRM?
The Commission Pipeline is project-focused — one row per commission, tracking it from inquiry to payment. The Client and Collector CRM is person-focused — one row per client, tracking every commission ordered, pieces purchased, preferred style, communication notes, and lifetime spend. They are linked: clicking a client shows all their commissions. Clicking a commission shows the full client record.
