The Problem

Managing Multi-Phase Projects Across Multiple Clients Without a System Is Unsustainable.

Small architecture practices routinely juggle five to fifteen active projects simultaneously, each in a different phase, with different consultants, different permit jurisdictions, and different fee billing milestones. Managing that complexity from memory, email threads, and scattered spreadsheets is how critical deadlines get missed and fee collection falls behind — not from lack of talent, but from lack of operational infrastructure.

🏗️

Projects exist in different phases with no unified view

Without a master project database, knowing the status of every active project across all phases requires asking yourself individually about each one. That mental overhead is exhausting and error-prone — one project's phase milestone quietly slips because it was not visible in a system that demanded your attention.

📋

Permit deadlines tracked by calendar and memory

Permit submission windows, required response deadlines for corrections, and approval expiration dates are some of the highest-stakes time-sensitive items in architectural practice. Managing them across multiple projects and multiple jurisdictions from a calendar alone creates exposure to missed deadlines with significant schedule consequences.

💵

Fee collection trails behind the work

Architects who bill percentage-of-fee by phase often discover they have been delivering work in advance of billing — especially during Construction Documents and CA phases. Without a fee tracker showing billed versus total contracted fee per project, it is easy to reach project completion having invoiced only 80% of the fee you earned.

🏆

Award submissions and portfolio entries stall

Design award submissions, publication opportunities, and portfolio photography are perennial backburner items for busy small practices. Without a tracker keeping these opportunities visible and moving forward, years of excellent work goes unrecognized simply because the submission was never submitted by the deadline.

A Complete Architecture Practice OS in Notion

FreelanceOS Pro for Architects connects your project database, client CRM, consultants directory, permit deadline tracker, fee and invoice tracker, AIA credit log, portfolio and award submission tracker, and business development pipeline in one relational Notion workspace designed for the operational reality of sole-practitioner and small studio architectural practice.

Project Database — Phase, Client, Contract Value, Deadline

A master project database where every active and completed project lives with full context — project name, client, current phase (Schematic Design, Design Development, Construction Documents, Permitting, Construction Administration, Closeout), contract value, key upcoming deadline, and project status. Filter by phase to see every project currently in Construction Documents and identify which consultant deliverables are due this week. Sort by contract value to see your revenue pipeline. Group by client to see all projects for a particular client relationship in one view. The project database replaces the mental overhead of tracking every project's phase status from memory with a systematic, always-current operational view of your entire office — the kind of visibility that lets you manage ten projects without losing track of any of them.

Client CRM — Contact Info, Project History, Referral Source

A client relationship management database linking every client to their project history, contact information, referral source, and relationship notes. When a client calls about their project, you have their full history — what you have built for them, when, at what fee, and who referred them to you — accessible in seconds rather than reconstructed from email searches. The referral source field helps you understand which relationships and channels are driving your best client work, which informs where you invest your business development energy. For repeat clients who have commissioned multiple projects over time, the project history creates a relationship record that demonstrates your understanding of their needs and history together.

Consultants and Subconsultants Database — Structural, MEP, Civil

A directory of every engineering consultant and specialty subconsultant in your professional network — structural engineers, MEP engineers, civil engineers, landscape architects, acoustic consultants, facade specialists, code consultants, and others — with contact information, firm name, areas of expertise, project links, preferred project types, and relationship notes. Link each consultant record to the projects they are involved in so you can see a consultant's full history with your practice. For sole-practitioner architects who rely on a consistent stable of trusted consultants, this directory makes it easy to assemble a project team from known, vetted relationships rather than finding consultants from scratch each time. It also becomes a reference for identifying specialty consultants you have not yet worked with when a project requires expertise outside your usual consultant network.

Permit and Regulatory Deadline Tracker by Jurisdiction

A permit and regulatory deadline tracker for every permit action across every active project — submission date, permit type, jurisdiction, assigned plan checker if known, required corrections response deadline, approval expected date, and actual approval date. For practices working across multiple municipalities with different review processes and timelines, this tracker creates a unified view of every outstanding permit action in the office. Missed permit correction response deadlines can reset the review clock and add weeks or months to a project's schedule, directly affecting the client relationship and your reputation for delivering on commitments. The tracker makes every outstanding permit action visible in one place so nothing gets lost in project-specific folders.

Fee and Invoice Tracking Per Project Phase

A fee and invoice tracker logging every invoice for every project organized by phase — invoice number, phase billed, amount, invoice date, due date, and payment status (Unpaid, Paid, Overdue). For architects billing percentage-of-construction-cost fees with phase-based billing milestones, the tracker shows exactly how much has been billed and collected versus the total contracted fee for each project, what the next phase billing trigger is (phase completion, submittal milestone, etc.), and which invoices are outstanding and overdue. Managing fee collection proactively rather than reactively — invoicing at the right moment in each phase rather than after you realize you have fallen behind — is one of the highest-leverage financial practices in architectural sole practice and small studio operations.

Continuing Education and AIA Credit Tracker

A continuing education log tracking every LU (Learning Unit) activity — course name, provider, AIA LU hours, HSW (Health, Safety, Welfare) credit designation, completion date, and applicable license renewal deadlines. AIA members must earn a minimum number of LUs per reporting cycle including a specific minimum of HSW credits. The tracker shows your running total by credit type at any point in the cycle so you are never scrambling at renewal. State architecture license renewal requirements add jurisdiction-specific requirements — the tracker handles both in one view so you can see your compliance status across all obligations simultaneously rather than tracking AIA and state license requirements separately. Many architects also use this log when applying for AIA Fellowship or other professional recognition that requires demonstrated commitment to continuing education.

Project Photography and Portfolio Submission Tracker

A tracker for every project's post-completion documentation and portfolio activities — photography session scheduled or completed, professional photographer contact, image delivery status, publication submission targets, and submission deadline. Link each portfolio entry to the project record so the complete project lifecycle — from design through photography and publication — is documented in one relational record. Completed projects without professional photography and no award submissions represent value left unrealized. The photography and portfolio tracker keeps these post-completion activities visible and moving forward rather than perpetually deferred because you are already busy with the next project.

Business Development and Award Submission Pipeline

A business development and award submission pipeline tracking every active opportunity — potential client relationships in development, RFQ and RFP responses in preparation, design award submissions in progress, publication inquiries, and speaking or lecture opportunities. Each record shows the opportunity type, status, key deadline, and next action required. For sole-practitioner architects, business development and reputation building through awards and publication are often left to chance rather than managed as the systematic strategic activities they need to be. The pipeline creates visibility into every active opportunity so nothing stalls at a critical moment because it was not in view when decisions about where to spend your time were made.
