FAQ

Questions from Photographers

Everything you want to know before getting started.

Can clients access my Notion workspace? ⌄
Yes — Notion lets you share specific pages as public links. You can give clients read-only access to their project page (shoot details, gallery delivery links, questionnaires) without them seeing the rest of your workspace. Many photographers create a dedicated "Client Portal" page per booking — just the information that client needs, nothing else. No Notion account is required for clients to view a shared page.
How do I track editing deadlines in Notion? ⌄
The Editing Pipeline database uses a status property (Culling → Editing → Color Grading → Exported → Gallery Uploaded → Delivered) plus a Delivery Deadline date field. Filter for sessions in "Editing" status and sort by deadline to see exactly what needs to be done today. A timeline view shows your full editing queue visually. Sessions with past-due deadlines show a red highlight so nothing slips without you noticing.
Can I manage multiple shooters or second photographers? ⌄
Yes. The Shoot Planner database includes a "Team" multi-select field for assigning second shooters and assistants. You can share the specific shoot page directly with collaborators so they see location, timeline, and shot list without accessing your full workspace. Equipment checklists can be built per person. If you run a studio with multiple full-time photographers, each can have their own workspace section while sharing the main client pipeline.
How do I handle retainer or subscription photography clients? ⌄
The Client CRM includes a "Client Type" property (One-Time, Retainer, Corporate). For retainer clients you set up a recurring shoot schedule linked to monthly invoice records. A "Next Shoot Due" date field auto-populates based on your billing cycle. The Marketing Calendar also has a retainer client review cadence to make sure you're regularly demonstrating value — especially important for commercial retainers renewed annually.
Is this better than HoneyBook or 17hats for photographers? ⌄
For photographers who want full customization without $40–$60/month subscription fees, FreelanceOS Pro wins. HoneyBook and 17hats are polished but rigid — you can't add custom fields, link gear to shoots, build a marketing calendar alongside your CRM, or modify the pipeline stages without paying for plan upgrades. FreelanceOS Pro is a $29 one-time cost and you own and customize it forever. The tradeoff is that HoneyBook handles e-signatures natively — you'd use a separate tool like DocuSign or an embedded Google Form for contracts.
How do I track which equipment to bring for each shoot? ⌄
Each Shoot record has a linked Equipment Checklist you build from your inventory database. Select the gear you plan to bring for that specific shoot, check items off as you pack, and uncheck on return to confirm everything made it back. The Equipment Inventory table tracks each item's last maintenance date, serial number, insurance value, and every shoot it's been used on — useful for wear-and-tear tracking and insurance claims.
