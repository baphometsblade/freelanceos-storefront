The Problem

Crystal Businesses Are Complex — Sessions, Retail, Courses, and Sourcing All at Once

Many crystal healing businesses operate three revenue streams simultaneously: healing sessions, retail crystal sales, and education. Managing all three without a unified system means things fall through. Here's what that looks like.

💎

Crystal inventory tracked in a spreadsheet or your memory

Your collection spans hundreds or thousands of specimens — raw stones, tumbled crystals, towers, spheres, clusters, and carved shapes in dozens of varieties. Knowing what you have on hand, what it cost, what it retails for, and when to reorder from your wholesale supplier requires a proper inventory system. A spreadsheet breaks under the complexity. An inventory database purpose-built for crystals — with fields for variety, form, locality, chakra association, and retail versus personal practice — gives you control over what is effectively your largest business asset.

🛍️

Etsy revenue not connected to wholesale buying decisions

Your top three bestsellers this quarter sold out twice. You didn't know until customers messaged asking where their orders went. The connection between your sales performance data and your supplier reorder triggers doesn't exist — because the inventory system and the sales data live in different places. A product sales tracker tied to your inventory that surfaces restock needs before you run out fixes this. Your customers shouldn't discover your stockouts before you do.

🎓

Crystal healing courses tracked informally or not at all

You offer a beginner crystal healing workshop, an intermediate crystal grid certification, and a distance crystal healing course on Teachable. Each has its own enrollment count, revenue total, and launch history — but you've never pulled these together to see which course earns the most per student, which has the best completion rate, and which you should run again. Without a course tracker, your education product pipeline is invisible.

🌍

Supplier relationships managed in DMs and email threads

You source crystals from five wholesale suppliers across the US, Morocco, Brazil, and Madagascar. Each supplier has different MOQs, lead times, quality consistency, and ethical sourcing practices. Managing these relationships — tracking orders placed, amounts spent, supplier quality ratings, and whether they provide mine-of-origin documentation — across a jumble of emails and DMs means you're reacting to problems rather than managing relationships proactively.

Can I use this template if I sell crystals on Etsy but don't offer healing sessions?
Yes. The template is designed for the full range of crystal business models — from pure crystal retail sellers to practitioners who only offer healing sessions, and everyone in between. If you're primarily a crystal shop owner selling raw stones, tumbled crystals, and curated collections on Etsy, the crystal inventory database, product sales tracker, and supplier sourcing network are the most relevant modules for you. If you only offer healing sessions and don't sell physical products, the client session overview, course tracker, and business dashboard are your core modules. The template accommodates any combination of these revenue streams and you can simply leave unused sections empty.
How does the crystal inventory database help me manage both my personal collection and retail stock?
The crystal inventory database has a field that distinguishes between personal healing practice collection and inventory for sale — so you can see everything you own in one database while filtering to show only retail inventory when you need to check stock. For retail crystals, it tracks quantity on hand, cost per unit, retail price, and platform so you can calculate margin and monitor stock levels. For your personal collection, it tracks which crystals you use in which types of healing sessions. The combination gives you a complete picture of your crystal assets — both the tools of your practice and the inventory of your business.
What seasonal trends should I know about crystal product sales?
Crystal retail has consistent seasonal patterns that the product sales tracker is built to capture. Rose quartz spikes dramatically around Valentine's Day (February) — it's the most gifted crystal in the industry. Citrine and pyrite see their highest demand in January (new year, abundance intention setting). Black tourmaline and obsidian spike in October and November as buyers prepare for darker months. Amethyst and clear quartz are year-round bestsellers with relatively flat seasonality. Selenite and angel crystals peak around Christmas and gift-giving seasons. Crystal grid kits and collection sets perform best in early spring and autumn. Tracking these patterns in your sales data over 12 months reveals your specific customer's purchasing behavior, which may differ from industry averages.
Does the supplier sourcing network help me find ethically sourced crystals?
The supplier network has an ethical sourcing field — yes or no and whether the supplier provides mine-of-origin documentation. Ethical sourcing has become a significant differentiator in the crystal market. A growing segment of crystal buyers specifically seek ethically sourced stones from suppliers who can verify country of origin, avoid conflict minerals, and ensure fair labor practices. Tracking which of your suppliers provide mine-of-origin documentation lets you make sourcing decisions that align with your values and communicate honestly with ethically-motivated buyers. The supplier network also tracks minimum order quantities, lead times, quality ratings, and total spend per supplier — so you can identify your most reliable partners and manage your wholesale relationships proactively.
