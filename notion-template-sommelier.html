The Problem

Running a Sommelier Consulting Practice Means Managing Clients, Knowledge, Events, and Business Simultaneously

Freelance sommeliers balance restaurant wine program consulting, private cellar clients, tasting events, education programs, and certification advancement — all while building the deepest wine knowledge in the room. Here is what the unmanaged version looks like.

🧠

Tasting notes scattered across notebooks and apps

You've tasted 300 wines in the past two years for study and client work. Your notes live in three different notebooks, a notes app, and a tasting app you stopped using. When you're preparing a client recommendation for a collector who loves elegant Burgundian-style Pinot Noir from the Willamette Valley, you can't search your own tasting history. A searchable, structured tasting database that accumulates over your career is professional infrastructure — not a luxury.

🍽️

Restaurant wine program details confused across clients

You consult for four restaurants simultaneously. Each has different cuisine, price point, target list size, BTG program, FOH training needs, and seasonal update schedule. Without a per-restaurant record, you arrive at meetings having confused one client's constraints with another's — recommending a $180 Barolo program for a casual neighborhood bistro targeting $45 average bottle price, or forgetting that this restaurant's GM specifically asked to eliminate all California Cabernet last quarter.

📚

Certification study without a structured tracking system

WSET Diploma and CMS Advanced require systematic regional coverage, structured tasting practice, and cumulative study that compounds over months. Without a study tracker that records which regions you've covered in depth, which producers you've tasted, and which theory areas still need attention, study sessions feel productive in the moment but don't necessarily build toward the comprehensive coverage the examination requires.

💼

Revenue by service type unknown

You earn income from restaurant consulting, private cellar consulting, education events, corporate tastings, and virtual wine experiences. You know your total monthly revenue — but you don't know what percentage comes from each service type, which engagements have the best hourly return, or whether your education events are genuinely profitable once preparation time is included. Without income-by-type tracking, decisions about where to grow the practice are guesswork.

The Solution

A Complete Sommelier Consulting OS in Notion

FreelanceOS Pro for Sommeliers connects your client wine profiles, tasting database, education event tracker, restaurant program management, consulting pipeline, and business dashboard in one Notion workspace built for independent wine professionals.

🍷 Client & Wine Preference Profile

Per-client records: client type (restaurant, private collector, corporate, winery, hotel), wine budget per engagement or per month, preferred styles (bold reds, elegant Burgundy-style reds, dry whites, natural wine, Champagne and sparkling, sweet wines, fortified), specific favorites by producer or appellation, avoided styles, cellar target if building a collection, purchasing channels (auction, direct from domaine, wine merchant, importer), and service goals (restaurant wine program, personal cellar, entertaining, education, gifting). The client preference profile is what separates a great recommendation from a generic one — and for consultants managing multiple clients with different palates and objectives, it's the intelligence file you reference before every engagement.

🏆 Wine Knowledge & Tasting Database

Your personal wine library: wine name, vintage, producer, region and appellation, grape variety, ABV, format, source, purchase price, estimated current value, drinking window (drink now, hold through year), tasting notes (aroma, palate, finish — your professional notes), food pairing suggestions, rating (1–100 or 1–5 stars), and cellar location if storing. The tasting notes field is the most professionally valuable field in the template — notes written immediately after tasting capture sensory detail that fades rapidly. Searchable by region, grape, producer, and vintage, this database accumulates into a career-spanning record of your palate development and tasting experience that no app or notebook system can replicate.

📚 Wine Education & Events Tracker

Every tasting and education engagement you run: event name, type (wine tasting, pairing dinner, wine education class, winery tour, corporate wine event, virtual tasting), client or public event, date, venue, attendance capacity, theme (Burgundy vs. Bordeaux, Old vs. New World, natural wine, Champagne masterclass), wines featured, ticket price or consulting fee, total revenue, actual attendance, and post-event survey score. Track education income as a distinct, scalable revenue stream with its own performance metrics — revenue per event, average attendance, repeat engagement rate, and conversion to private consulting clients.

🏥 Restaurant Wine Program Management

Per-restaurant consulting record: restaurant name, cuisine type, price point, target wine program size in SKUs, programming budget, current wine list status, last list update date, by-the-glass program (count and GP margin), bottle sales year-to-date, wine program revenue target, training sessions conducted for FOH staff, and staff education level assessment. Each restaurant is a distinct consulting relationship with its own constraints, objectives, and hospitality culture. Keeping detailed per-client records ensures your consulting is always specific to the client in front of you — not a generic wine list recommendation that happens to land in the right room.

💼 Consulting Engagement Log

Per-engagement records: client name, project type (wine list build, cellar consultation, event, education, import advisory, content creation), start date, deliverable description, contract value, hours worked, invoice status, testimonial received, and case study permission granted. The engagement log tracks your consulting pipeline from prospect to invoiced project — and the hours-worked field enables honest calculation of your effective hourly rate per project type. Some consulting engagements that appear lucrative by contract value turn out to have lower hourly rates than simpler event work. The log makes those comparisons objective rather than intuitive.

💼 Sommelier Business Dashboard

Active consulting clients, restaurant programs managed, education events this quarter, monthly revenue, income by service type (restaurant consulting, private cellar, education events, virtual tastings, content creation, import advisory), certification level and exam prep tracking (WSET 1 through Diploma, CMS Introductory through Master), study log for certification advancement, and annual revenue goal. The certification study tracker integrates with the tasting database and regional study library — so your professional development and your business performance are visible in the same workspace.

How does the tasting database support my professional development?
The wine knowledge and tasting database functions as your personal wine library with structured tasting notes written immediately after tasting while sensory memory is fresh.
