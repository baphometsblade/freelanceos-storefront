Why German Freelancers Choose Notion

Germany has one of Europe's largest and most administratively demanding freelance markets. Here is why tens of thousands of Freiberufler and Selbstständige are switching to Notion.

Germany is home to over 5 million Freiberufler and Gewerbetreibende, making it one of the most active freelance markets in the European Union. The distinction between these two categories matters significantly for invoicing and taxes. A Freiberufler — typically a professional in an intellectual, creative, or scientific field such as software development, design, law, medicine, or journalism — is classified under §18 EStG and is exempt from Gewerbesteuer. A Gewerbetreibender, by contrast, runs a commercial business and must register a Gewerbe, file Gewerbesteuer returns, and comply with additional commercial regulations. This distinction affects how you invoice, how you structure your taxes, and how much administrative overhead you face every quarter.

Remote work has accelerated dramatically across Germany since 2020. The pandemic normalised distributed work, and the German freelance market responded rapidly — with a surge in EU-wide project sourcing, cross-border client contracts, and digital-first service delivery. German freelancers increasingly bill clients in the Netherlands, Sweden, France, and beyond, creating new complexity around EU VAT rules, reverse charge mechanisms, and multi-currency EUR invoicing. Managing this administrative load with spreadsheets alone has become impractical for any serious solo operator.

Notion's flexibility makes it ideal for managing the unique administrative demands of German freelancing. Unlike rigid accounting software, Notion adapts to your workflow — you can build the exact system your practice needs, whether that is a solo IT consultant tracking billable hours per client, a freelance translator managing recurring projects across multiple languages, or a marketing strategist juggling retainer contracts with quarterly reporting deadlines.

German freelancing is famously document-heavy. Between quarterly Umsatzsteuervoranmeldungen, annual Einkommensteuererklärungen, DSGVO compliance records, client contracts, Angebote (quotes), and formal Rechnungen (invoices), the paperwork burden on a solo operator can feel overwhelming. Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from German freelancers about Notion templates, tax records, DSGVO compliance, and invoicing requirements.

Can I use Notion for German tax records?

Yes, Notion can serve as a well-organised repository for tax-related records, helping you track income, expenses, VAT collected, and client correspondence. However, Notion is not an officially approved accounting software under German law (GoBD). Most Steuerberater recommend using it alongside dedicated accounting software like DATEV, Lexoffice, or Fastbill for the official books. Notion excels as a workflow hub and preliminary tracker — it is where you manage your practice day-to-day, not where you submit your official Steuererklärung.

Is Notion DSGVO (GDPR) compliant?

Notion has implemented GDPR-compliant data processing agreements and stores EU user data in EU data centres. It is generally considered usable for processing personal data under GDPR when configured correctly. However, you remain responsible as the data controller for how you store, process, and delete personal data. Use the DSGVO checklist in this template to document your processing activities and ensure proper consent and deletion procedures are followed. This is general guidance only — consult a qualified Datenschutzbeauftragter for your specific situation and industry.

How do German freelancers typically invoice?

German freelancers must include specific mandatory information (Pflichtangaben) on invoices: full name and address, client name and address, tax number (Steuernummer) or VAT ID (Umsatzsteuer-Identifikationsnummer), a unique invoice number, invoice date, date of service delivery, a clear description of services rendered, net amount (Nettobetrag), VAT rate and VAT amount, and the gross total (Bruttobetrag). Kleinunternehmer must additionally include the §19 UStG notice in German. The FreelanceOS Pro template includes pre-formatted invoice templates covering all mandatory fields for both standard and Kleinunternehmer scenarios.

What is the Kleinunternehmerregelung?

The Kleinunternehmerregelung (§19 UStG) is a German tax regulation allowing freelancers with annual revenue below approximately 22,000 EUR to exempt themselves from collecting and remitting Umsatzsteuer (VAT). Instead of charging 19% VAT, they add a mandatory notice to their invoices — typically: "Gemäß §19 UStG wird keine Umsatzsteuer berechnet." This significantly simplifies bookkeeping and quarterly tax filings. However, it also means you cannot reclaim input VAT on your business expenses. If your revenue is likely to exceed 22,000 EUR in the current year, consult your Steuerberater about transitioning to standard VAT registration. The FreelanceOS template includes a Kleinunternehmer invoice template with the correct legal notice and a revenue tracking view to monitor your position relative to the threshold throughout the year.

How do I track VAT in Notion?

The FreelanceOS Germany VAT tracker uses Notion databases to log each invoice with its net amount, applicable VAT rate (7% or 19%), calculated VAT amount, and gross total. Notion formula properties calculate VAT amounts automatically based on the rate you select, eliminating manual calculation errors. A rollup formula on the quarterly summary view aggregates your total VAT collected, so you know exactly what to declare and remit in your Umsatzsteuervoranmeldung before the filing deadline (generally the 10th of the month following the quarter end). Always verify your figures with your Steuerberater before submission — particularly if you have complex situations involving EU clients, partial exemptions, or mixed VAT rates on a single invoice.

Can I use Notion with DATEV?

Notion and DATEV do not integrate natively, and DATEV is primarily designed to be used by licensed Steuerberater and their clients through professional accounting workflows. However, many German freelancers use Notion very effectively alongside DATEV-based accounting. A common workflow: use Notion for day-to-day project management, client tracking, invoice logging, and preliminary expense recording, then periodically export or summarise data (monthly or quarterly) to hand to your Steuerberater, who enters the official records in DATEV. Lexoffice, Fastbill, and Papierkram all offer more direct DATEV-export capabilities for the official accounting layer and may be worth using in tandem with your Notion workspace.

The FreelanceOS Pro template includes a multi-currency project tracker designed for exactly this situation. For USD or GBP clients, you log the invoiced amount in the original currency alongside the EUR equivalent calculated at the exchange rate on the invoice date — which is the figure that matters for German tax reporting. The template includes a dedicated exchange rate field per invoice entry, and a formula property calculates the EUR equivalent automatically once the rate is entered. This gives you a clean EUR-denominated income view for tax purposes, while preserving the original currency amounts for client-facing records and your own reference. The quarterly financial summary aggregates all income in EUR regardless of original billing currency.
