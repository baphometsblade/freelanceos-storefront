Freiberufler vs. Gewerbetreibende: The Key German Distinction

Freiberufler (Liberal Professions)

The category of Freiberufler under §18 of the Einkommensteuergesetz (EStG) covers professionals in recognised liberal professions: doctors, lawyers, tax advisors, architects, engineers, journalists, translators, scientists, and — crucially — IT consultants, designers, and writers. As a Freiberufler, you register directly with the Finanzamt using the Fragebogen zur steuerlichen Erfassung. You do not need to register with the Gewerbeamt, you are exempt from Gewerbesteuer (trade tax), and your administrative burden is comparatively lower.

If you work in software development, UX design, copywriting, photography, or similar creative and technical fields, you are almost certainly a Freiberufler. However, the exact classification can sometimes be ambiguous — consulting a Steuerberater when you start is worth the investment.

Gewerbetreibende (Commercial Enterprise)

If your activities fall outside the recognised liberal professions — for example, if you run a trading business, operate as a coach in certain contexts, or run an agency with commercial activities — you are classified as a Gewerbetreibende. This requires additional registration with the Gewerbeamt, filing a Gewerbeanmeldung, and paying Gewerbesteuer if your annual profits exceed €24,500. The complexity is higher, and a Steuerberater from day one is strongly recommended.

German Tax Obligations for Freelancers

Umsatzsteuer (VAT) and Umsatzsteuervoranmeldung

Once your annual turnover exceeds the Kleinunternehmerregelung threshold of €22,000, you must register for Umsatzsteuer (German VAT). The standard rate is 19% for most professional services, with a reduced rate of 7% applying in specific sectors. You must then file Umsatzsteuervoranmeldungen (VAT pre-notifications) monthly or quarterly via the ELSTER portal, and an annual Umsatzsteuererklärung.

VAT on international B2B services to other EU businesses is typically handled via the reverse charge mechanism — you issue the invoice without VAT and the client self-assesses. For non-EU international clients, no VAT applies.

Kleinunternehmerregelung: The Under-€22,000 Option

The Kleinunternehmerregelung (§19 UStG) allows freelancers with annual turnover below €22,000 (in the previous year) and below €50,000 (projected for the current year) to opt out of VAT. This means you issue invoices without VAT, don't charge clients 19%, and don't file Umsatzsteuervoranmeldungen. However, you also cannot claim input VAT on your business purchases. For freelancers with mostly business clients who can reclaim VAT anyway, the Kleinunternehmerregelung is often not beneficial — the calculation depends on your specific situation.

Einkommensteuer for German Freelancers

All self-employed income in Germany is subject to Einkommensteuer (income tax). The basic exemption (Grundfreibetrag) for 2026 is €11,784. Progressive rates from 14% to 42% apply above that, with a top rate of 45% (Reichensteuer) above approximately €277,000. German freelancers also pay Kirchensteuer (church tax, 8–9% of income tax) if they are church members, and Solidaritätszuschlag (solidarity surcharge) in certain higher income brackets.

The annual Einkommensteuererklärung is due by 31 July if filed without a Steuerberater (31 May for paper), or up to 28 February of the following year with professional assistance. Vorauszahlungen (advance tax payments) are due quarterly.

German Invoice Requirements: Rechnungspflichtangaben

German law requires invoices to contain specific mandatory information (Rechnungspflichtangaben) under §14 UStG. For VAT-registered freelancers, every invoice must include:

  • Your full legal name and address
  • The full name and address of your client
  • Your Steuernummer (tax number) or Umsatzsteuer-Identifikationsnummer (VAT ID)
  • The date of invoice issuance
  • A unique, sequential invoice number (Rechnungsnummer)
  • A description of the services rendered
  • The date or period of the service
  • The net amount, VAT rate, and VAT amount
  • The gross total

Do I need to charge VAT (Umsatzsteuer) on my freelance invoices in Germany?

It depends on your annual turnover. Under the Kleinunternehmerregelung, if your previous year's turnover was below €22,000 and you expect the current year below €50,000, you can issue invoices without VAT. Above those thresholds, you must register for Umsatzsteuer (19%), charge it on invoices, and file regular Umsatzsteuervoranmeldungen via ELSTER. What is the difference between Freiberufler and Gewerbetreibende?
