The Problem

Running a coaching practice without a system means leaving clients and revenue on the table

Most coaches are excellent at the actual coaching — but running the business side is a different skill set entirely. Here's what gets in the way.

📓

Session notes scattered everywhere

Homework assigned in a notebook, goals discussed over email, follow-up questions jotted on a napkin. By session five you're spending the first ten minutes piecing together what you discussed last time — and clients notice.

👁

No visibility into who needs a check-in

You know intellectually that clients who go quiet sometimes need a nudge — but with no central system, "check in on Marcus" lives in your head and gets bumped by whatever's urgent that day. Clients slip through the cracks right before they would have re-signed.

📉

Business development happens by accident

When you're fully booked the pipeline goes cold. When clients finish you scramble. There's no consistent system for tracking discovery calls, following up on proposals, or nurturing warm leads — so revenue peaks and valleys feel unpredictable.

🔁

Recreating materials for every client

You've run versions of the same core framework with a dozen clients but the worksheets, exercises, and session structures live in different files and folders each time. Onboarding a new client means assembling the same materials again from scratch instead of clicking duplicate.

How It Works

Go from setup to running your practice like a business in three steps

The Coach OS is designed for fast onboarding. Most coaches have their client roster populated and their first session prepped within two hours of duplicating the template.

1

Set up your client roster

Duplicate the template into your Notion workspace and start by adding your current clients to the Client Roster & CRM. Enter their coaching type, package, start date, session count, and referral source. In 30–60 minutes you'll have a complete picture of your current client base — something most coaches have never seen in one place. Then customize the engagement status labels and property options to match your exact packages and coaching terminology.

2

Prep each session in advance with the Session Prep Board

Before each client session, open their session prep page and spend 5–10 minutes reviewing the goals from last time, the homework you assigned, and the questions you wanted to explore. Add your agenda for today's session. This single habit — which the board makes almost effortless — is what separates coaches clients rave about from coaches clients forget. The board also surfaces who has sessions coming up this week so nothing slips through on busy days.

3

Track revenue and business development in a weekly review

Set aside 20–30 minutes every Friday for a business review ritual. Open the Revenue Tracker, log the week's income, and check outstanding balances. Then open the Business Development Pipeline and move any prospects through their current stage — follow up with anyone who hasn't responded, and add any new leads from the week. Finally, check the Goals Dashboard to see if you're on track for your quarterly target. This weekly habit is how coaches go from reactive to intentional about growing their practice.
