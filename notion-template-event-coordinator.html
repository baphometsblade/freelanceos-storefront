FAQ

Questions From Event Coordinators & Event Planners

How do event coordinators use Notion to manage events?

Event coordinators use Notion to manage every aspect of the event planning process in a single connected workspace. A Notion event tracker gives you a master database of all your events — corporate conferences, weddings, product launches, charity galas — with event type, date, venue, guest count, budget, status, and client contact all in one record. Because Notion is relational, each event links directly to the vendors booked for that event, the budget tracker for that event's spend, the run-of-show timeline, and the guest list. You can filter by event date to see what is coming up this month, by status to see which events are in the vendor booking phase, or by event type to compare your corporate work against your wedding work — giving you a clear operational picture of your entire planning business.

Can Notion help event planners track vendor contracts and budgets?

Yes — vendor and budget management is where Notion delivers significant practical value for event coordinators. The vendor CRM in FreelanceOS Pro stores every vendor across every category (venue, catering, AV, photography, floristry, entertainment, transportation, staffing, rentals) with contact details, price quoted vs. booked, contract status, deposit paid, payment due dates, cancellation policy, and performance notes. These vendor records link to specific events so you can pull up any event and see every booked vendor with payment status at a glance. The budget tracker captures estimated vs. actual spend per vendor category and flags categories running over budget — eliminating missed deposit deadlines and lost track of outstanding invoices in the days before an event.

How do I create a run-of-show timeline in Notion?

A run-of-show in Notion works as a structured database where each record is a scheduled block in the event day: time, activity, responsible person or vendor, location, duration, setup requirements, and contingency notes. View it as a sorted table to see every activity chronologically, or filter by responsible vendor to give each team member a personalised view of only their moments. The run-of-show links to vendor records so you reach any vendor contact in one click mid-event. You can update the run-of-show in real time from mobile and have changes immediately visible to your team — far more practical than a static PDF printed on event morning that becomes outdated the moment anything changes.

What is the best way to manage guest lists and RSVPs in Notion?

The guest database in FreelanceOS Pro stores each guest with name, company or relationship to host, RSVP status, dietary restriction, table or room assignment, VIP status, plus-one, and special requirements. Filter by RSVP status to instantly see outstanding responses. Filter by dietary restriction to produce the catering brief. Sort by table assignment to verify seating coverage before the final count goes to the venue. The guest database links to the event record so confirmed headcount and dietary breakdown flow directly through to the final numbers for your venue and catering team — no re-entering data across multiple files on deadline day.
