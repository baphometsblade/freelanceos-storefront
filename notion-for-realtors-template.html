Most real estate business comes from people who already know you. The agents who win consistently are the ones with a system for staying top of mind — not just with active buyers, but with everyone in their sphere.

  • Sphere of Influence Database — every person in your network categorized by relationship type, last contact date, and referral history
  • Follow-up Cadence Templates — 3-day, 7-day, 30-day, and 90-day follow-up sequences for different lead types
  • Open House Attendee Log — name, contact, interest level, buyer/seller, timeline, and follow-up date captured at every open house
  • Past Client Anniversary System — closing anniversary dates in a calendar view with a touchpoint reminder and referral ask prompt
  • Lead Temperature Tracking — Hot / Warm / Cold status on every contact with last-touch date so nothing ages out unnoticed

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I use Notion as a real estate agent CRM?
The Client Pipeline database is your core CRM — one row per client with status, lead source, budget, target area, key dates, agent notes, and linked showing records. Buyers move through Lead → Active Search → Under Contract → Closed. Every linked showing note, offer, and document attaches to the client record so their full file is one click away at any point in the transaction.
How do I manage showing notes for multiple buyers in Notion?
The Showing Notes Database has one entry per showing with the property address, client, date, your full notes, pros, cons, and a rating. It links to the client record, so clicking any buyer shows every property they've toured in chronological order. A Gallery view grouped by client lets you compare multiple properties side by side before a buyer meeting.
Does this Notion template include a transaction checklist for closing?
Yes — the Transaction Checklist module is a 30-step closing process database with every milestone from executed contract to post-closing follow-up. Each step has status, responsible party, and due date. The checklist is templated so you duplicate it for each new transaction. You'll never miss a contingency deadline again.
How do I track my real estate leads and their sources in Notion?
The Lead Source Tracker records every lead with source type, date received, status, and ultimate outcome. Grouped views show your leads by source so you can see which channels generate closings — not just contacts. Over time this data tells you exactly where to invest your marketing dollars and which relationships are most valuable to maintain.
Can I track my commission income and business goals in Notion?
Yes — the Commission Income Tracker records each closed transaction with sale price, commission side, GCI, brokerage split, team split if applicable, and net commission. Rollup formulas calculate your total GCI and transaction volume for any time period. An annual goal vs. actual view shows your progress against your GCI target — your personal business dashboard separate from your brokerage's reporting.
Is this Notion realtor template appropriate for any brokerage or state?
Yes — the template is brokerage-agnostic and not specific to any state's contract forms or legal requirements. The transaction checklist steps, status labels, and timeline fields are fully customizable. The template is a personal organizational tool and does not constitute legal or brokerage compliance advice. Always follow your brokerage's policies and your state's licensing requirements.
