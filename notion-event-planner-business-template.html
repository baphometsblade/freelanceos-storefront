Timeline System

Building a Master Timeline That Works Backwards from the Contract

The biggest mistake event planners make is building their timeline forward from today. The FreelanceOS approach works backwards from the event date — so you always know what must happen by when.

1

Lock the Event Date First

Enter the event date in the pipeline record. Every milestone in the timeline builder auto-calculates its due date based on weeks-out rules. 12 weeks: venue confirmed and deposit paid. 8 weeks: catering menu locked. 6 weeks: final vendor contracts signed. 4 weeks: guest count confirmed. 2 weeks: run-of-show draft sent. 1 week: final call with all lead vendors.

2

Build the Day-Of Run-of-Show

The run-of-show database has time-slot entries with fields for the time, activity, lead responsible (planner, venue, vendor, or client), location on site, and notes. You build the minute-by-minute schedule starting from load-in and vendor arrivals through the final toast and breakdown. Every vendor sees only their relevant rows.

3

Create the Shareable Client Version

Duplicate the run-of-show database and hide your internal margin notes and planner commentary. Share a filtered Notion view with the client that shows only the guest-facing timeline — ceremony start, cocktail hour, dinner service, first dances, cake cutting, and departure. Clients love seeing this in Notion without needing to download anything.

4

Update Once, Propagate Everywhere

When the ceremony start shifts by 30 minutes, you update a single time entry in the run-of-show and the cascade ripples through every linked record. Vendor callsheet, client timeline view, and venue coordination notes all reflect the change. No more hunting through six documents.

After every event, log a 1–5 rating for each vendor with notes on punctuality, communication, quality, and any client feedback. Over time you build an objective performance record that informs every future recommendation. A vendor who consistently scores 5 gets first call. Exclusive Agreements & Referrals

Track which vendors have exclusive agreements with specific venues, which vendors you have referral arrangements with, and which ones require you to use their specific sub-vendors. Budget Management

Client Budget Tracking That Protects Your Profitability

The Event Budget Tracker separates what the client approved from what you actually spent — and keeps the deposit schedule visible so nothing slips through the cracks.

Client-Facing Budget View

Show clients a clean summary of their approved budget by category without exposing your cost breakdowns or margins. The template includes a client-safe filtered view you can share as a Notion link or screenshot for approval sign-off.

Deposit & Payment Tracking

Every budget line item has fields for deposit paid date, deposit amount, balance due date, and final payment received. A formula flags any overdue balances in red so you always know who owes you money before you pick up the phone.

Scope Change Documentation

When a client upgrades the floral arrangement or adds a photo booth at 6 weeks out, log the scope change with the original approval, the new item, the cost delta, and the client's written sign-off. This protects you if disputes arise about what was and was not included.

Final Reconciliation Report

After the event, the budget tracker generates a final reconciliation summary: total budget approved, total actual spend, variance by category, total collected, and outstanding balance. Send this to clients as the closing document before issuing the final invoice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I manage my event planning business in Notion?

Yes — Notion is an excellent tool for event planners. Its relational database system lets you connect client records, vendor contacts, budgets, timelines, and invoices so every piece of information is one click away. The FreelanceOS Event Planner Template is pre-built for the full event lifecycle, from initial inquiry to final invoice reconciliation.

Does this template include a vendor management system?

Yes. The Vendor and Supplier CRM includes contact details, pricing tiers, availability notes, past event performance ratings, exclusive agreements, and referral tracking. You can filter by vendor category — caterers, florists, photographers, AV, rentals — and quickly pull up your preferred vendor network for any event type.

How does the event budget tracker work?

Each event has its own budget database with categories for every expense type. For each line item you record the client-approved budget, the actual cost, any deposit paid, and balance due. Formulas calculate the variance between budget and actuals automatically, so you can see at a glance if any category is running over. A client-facing summary view can be shared without exposing your internal margin notes.

Can I use this for both weddings and corporate events?

Absolutely. The template includes event-type views so you can filter your pipeline by event category. Wedding events include the sentimental detail fields and vendor coordination notes that matter for high-stakes personal events, while corporate event views emphasize ROI metrics, brand consistency notes, and AV and tech requirements. You can run both types simultaneously without them interfering with each other.

Does the template include a day-of run-of-show builder?

Yes. The Master Timeline Builder has two layers: a planning milestone tracker that works backwards from the event date and a minute-by-minute day-of run-of-show database. You can duplicate the run-of-show as a shared Notion page for vendors or present it as a clean summary for clients on the day itself.
