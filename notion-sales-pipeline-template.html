What to Track at Each Stage

Prospect: Company, contact, ICP fit score, source, date added. Exit criteria: contact identified, initial research complete.

Qualified: BANT or MEDDIC qualification notes, budget range confirmed, decision-maker identified. Exit criteria: confirmed they have budget, a real problem, and decision authority.

Discovery: Pain points documented, impact quantified, timeline established. Exit criteria: discovery notes page complete, problem-solution fit confirmed.

Demo/Proposal: Demo delivered or proposal sent, proposal value, decision criteria confirmed. Exit criteria: decision criteria mapped, evaluation timeline set.

Negotiation: Redlines received, internal approval needed, legal status. Exit criteria: all objections addressed, terms agreed in principle.

Building Your Outbound Prospecting System in Notion

Most salespeople treat outbound as a series of one-off actions. The template turns it into a repeatable system: a defined ICP, segmented prospecting lists, tracked outreach sequences, and a structured way to handle replies.

1

Define Your ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

The ICP page in the template captures your target firmographics (company size, industry, geography, tech stack, growth stage) and buyer persona (title, department, key pain points, trigger events). This is your filter for every prospecting decision. Revisit it quarterly as you learn what closes.

2

Build Segmented Prospecting Lists

Create separate filtered views in the Lead Database for each ICP segment: by industry, by company size, by trigger event (new funding, new hire, product launch). This lets you send hyper-relevant outreach because your message is written for that specific segment, not the general list.

3

Track Outreach Sequences

The Lead Database has a field for sequence step (Step 1 / Step 2 / Step 3 / Responded / Not Interested / Converted). Filter by sequence step to see who needs a second touchpoint this week. Track which step in the sequence generates the most replies to optimize your cadence over time.

4

Cadence Management

The Follow-up Queue view sorted by next-contact date shows your outbound cadence in a single list. Monday morning: open the queue, work through everyone who's due for outreach today. The discipline of checking one view every morning replaces the chaos of checking multiple lists.

5

Reply Handling & Conversion

When a prospect replies positively, move them from Lead Database to Deal Pipeline with one click. The account research you built during prospecting carries over to the deal page. Your discovery call has better questions because you already know their situation. The pipeline starts pre-loaded.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Notion replace Salesforce or HubSpot for B2B sales?

For solo sellers, small teams, and founders doing their own sales, Notion can absolutely replace a paid CRM. It gives you deal tracking, contact management, pipeline visibility, and revenue forecasting without the $80–200/month price tag. Where Notion falls short is in email sequence automation, call recording integration, and AI-powered lead scoring — features you need at 50+ reps but rarely at 1–10.

Does this Notion sales template work for SDRs doing high-volume outbound?

Yes. The outbound prospecting system module is built for SDR workflows: ICP-based prospect lists, outreach sequence tracking, reply handling, and cadence management. It handles high-volume outbound effectively, though automated email sending still requires a dedicated sequencer tool like Outreach or Apollo.

How do I track follow-ups so nothing falls through the cracks?

The Follow-up Queue module is a filtered database view of every deal with a next-action date, sorted chronologically. Open this each morning to see exactly who you need to contact today. You can also set Notion reminders on individual deal pages to get notified before a follow-up is due. This single view eliminates the "deals stalling in the pipeline" problem most sales reps face.

Can I use this template to forecast revenue?

Yes. The Revenue Forecast Dashboard uses weighted pipeline values — deal value multiplied by close probability by stage — to produce a rolling quarterly forecast. You see your current quarter's committed, best-case, and pipeline totals without ever opening a spreadsheet. Refresh it in under 5 minutes.

Is this template good for a founder doing their own B2B sales?

This is the most popular use case. Founders doing their own sales need pipeline visibility and follow-up discipline without the overhead of a full CRM. The template gives you a deal pipeline, discovery notes, proposal tracker, and forecast in about 30 minutes of setup — all in one workspace alongside your other business operations in Notion.
