🔍 Availability Checklist
No API needed — here's how to manually check your name in under 5 minutes:
1
Google "[your name]" and check the first page If nothing comes up, you have a clean slate. If a business exists, check how similar they are and whether you operate in the same market.
2
Search Instagram, LinkedIn, and Twitter/X for the handle Aim for the same handle across all platforms. If @yourname is taken, try @yourname_ or @yournameofficial. Consistency matters more than perfection.
3
Check the .com domain on Namecheap.com If your .com is taken, try [yourname]hq.com, [yourname]studio.com, or go[yourname].com as fallbacks before switching TLDs.
4
Check trademark databases — USPTO.gov (US), IPO (UK), IP Australia A registered trademark in your industry means you need to pick a different name — even if the registrant isn't in your location.
💡 5 Tips for Choosing Your Freelance Business Name
1
Keep it under 3 words. Shorter names are easier to remember, look better in an email signature, and are less likely to get garbled when clients pass your name along verbally. "Rivera Design" beats "Alex Rivera Independent Creative Design Studio" every time.
2
Check social handles immediately. Before you fall in love with a name, run a quick search on Instagram, LinkedIn, and Twitter. If @yourname is taken everywhere, it's worth reconsidering — brand consistency across platforms matters.
3
Avoid trendy words that will date your brand. Words like "AI", "Digital", "Solutions", and "Synergy" feel generic and overused. Unless it genuinely captures what you do uniquely, pick something that will still feel fresh in 10 years.
4
Say it out loud 10 times. If it's hard to pronounce, clients won't remember it. Read it to a friend over the phone and see if they can spell it back correctly. If they can't, simplify.
5
Get the .com — not .io, .co, or .design. Alternative TLDs are trendy, but .com still converts better for service businesses because clients default to typing .com when they can't remember your exact address. Tweak the name before settling for a lesser extension.
