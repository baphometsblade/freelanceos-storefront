Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use this for both fixed-price projects and ongoing retainers?

Yes. The project tracker includes a Project Type property (Fixed / Retainer / Hourly) and the pipeline stages work for both. Retainer clients typically sit in the "Active" stage permanently, with monthly milestone entries tracking deliverables. Fixed-price projects move through the full pipeline to Completed and then Invoiced. Both models are well-supported out of the box, and you can customize the stages further in five minutes.

How does this compare to using Asana or ClickUp?

Asana and ClickUp are excellent for teams but have significant gaps for solo freelancers: they don't connect client data to projects, they don't link project completion to invoice creation, and they require separate subscriptions for the full feature set. FreelanceOS Pro costs $29 once, includes client CRM, project management, invoicing, and revenue tracking in one connected workspace, and works on Notion's free plan. See our full comparison page for a detailed breakdown.

What does "linked invoice creation" actually mean in practice?

When you move a project to the Invoicing stage in the kanban board, the Invoice Pipeline database has a linked entry waiting with the client name and project name pre-populated via Notion's relation property. You add the amount, set the due date, and mark it as Sent — three fields, no re-entering data you've already recorded. Most FreelanceOS users say they went from invoicing 3–5 days after project completion to invoicing the same day.

Does this work if I subcontract work to other freelancers?

It works well for small subcontracting setups. You can add a "Subcontractor" property to each project or deliverable, track their costs in the expense section, and calculate your margin on each project. For larger teams with multiple contractors needing their own views and edit access, you'd want Notion's paid plan for team collaboration features. For most solopreneurs with one or two occasional contractors, the free plan setup in FreelanceOS is sufficient.

How long does setup realistically take?

Most freelancers complete initial setup in 45–90 minutes. That includes duplicating the template, entering your 5–10 current clients into the CRM, creating your active projects in the kanban board, and logging any outstanding invoices. The setup guide included with Pro walks through every step with screenshots. After the initial setup, ongoing maintenance is 5–10 minutes per new project and 2–3 minutes per invoice — less time than any alternative system.
