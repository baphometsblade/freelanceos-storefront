Managing a life together is harder than it should be

You're not disorganized — you just don't have a system built for two people. Here's what couples deal with before they have a shared workspace.

🔄

"Wait — who was supposed to handle that?"

Household tasks drift through texts and mental lists until something falls through the cracks. The dishwasher repair, the utility bill, the vet appointment — nobody tracked it and now you're both frustrated.

💸

Joint finances with no shared view

You each pay some bills, split others, and maintain separate accounts — but neither of you has a clear picture of joint spending, savings progress, or whether you're on track for the goals you talked about last year.

🗓️

Relationship goals set in January, gone by March

You had great ideas — travel more, save for a house, start a shared hobby — but with no system to track them together, they quietly disappear under the weight of day-to-day life. They never get updated, reviewed, or celebrated.

💔

Date nights that never actually happen

You both want them. You both know they matter. But without a real plan, it takes one busy week to turn into one busy month. Nobody pulls up a list of ideas and suddenly Netflix is just… easier.

8 modules built for every part of life together

Each module is a self-contained Notion system. Use them all or just the ones that matter most to you right now — the workspace grows with your relationship.

Household Command Center

The central hub for running your home together. Every recurring task, chore, and home responsibility — assigned, tracked, and never forgotten again. Customize the division of labor so both partners always know what's on their plate without asking.

  • Recurring task tracker with customizable frequency (daily, weekly, monthly, seasonal)

Joint Finances Hub

A complete view of your shared financial picture — without forcing complete transparency if you prefer financial independence. Track joint expenses, build shared savings goals, manage debt payoff plans, and run a monthly budget review together.

  • Shared income tracker — combine household income for budgeting purposes
  • Joint expense log with categories (housing, groceries, subscriptions, utilities)
  • Savings goals with visual progress bars and target dates
  • Debt payoff planner with avalanche or snowball method views
  • Monthly budget template with planned vs. actual spend
  • Optional privacy toggle — individual finances can stay separate
  • Bill due-date calendar so nothing gets missed or late-fee'd

Stop setting goals in separate journals and forgetting about them by February. This module creates a living, shared vision for your life together — with space for the individual goals that matter to each of you alongside the ones you're building toward as a couple.

  • 5-year vision document — a shared place for big-picture life direction
  • Individual goals section — each partner's personal goals visible to both
  • Weekly goals check-in template to review and re-prioritize together
  • Goals archive — celebrate what you've accomplished, not just what's next
  • Milestone tracker with estimated timelines and owners
  • Goal categories: financial, travel, health, career, relationship, home

Common questions

Everything you're wondering about

Answers to the questions we hear most from couples before they get started.

Do we both need a Notion account to use this template?
Yes, you'll each need a free Notion account. Once you have accounts, one partner duplicates the template into their workspace and then shares the workspace with the other using Notion's share link. Notion's free plan fully supports sharing a workspace between two people — so there's no extra cost beyond the one-time $29 template purchase. Both partners get full access and can edit in real time.
Can we keep some things private even in a shared workspace?
Absolutely. The template is designed with privacy in mind. Notion lets you keep individual pages private while sharing others. The Joint Finances Hub has a specific "individual finances" section that's meant to stay separate — you can track shared savings goals together while keeping your personal salary, individual accounts, or private spending in a locked personal page. We include setup guidance for structuring private vs. shared sections.
Is this template a good fit for couples who just moved in together?
It's one of the best use cases for this workspace. The Household Command Center and Home & Moving Planner modules were specifically designed for the transition of moving in together — covering utility setup, chore division from day one, furniture decisions, and service provider contacts. Couples who just moved in together consistently say it eliminated the biggest friction points in the first 90 days: unclear task ownership, shared expenses confusion, and competing visions for the home.
Does this template work for long-distance couples?
Yes — Notion is fully cloud-based, so both partners access the same workspace from anywhere in the world. Long-distance couples find the Shared Goals Board and Date Night Planner especially useful for staying connected — having concrete plans and shared milestones to look forward to makes a real difference. The Memory Log is also popular for documenting visits and shared experiences across the distance.
Does the couples template include wedding planning features?
The template includes a Family Planning Notes section and a life milestones tracker that works well for early engagement planning, initial budgeting conversations, and big-picture wedding vision. For a complete dedicated wedding planning system — vendor tracker, guest list management, detailed budget breakdown, seating chart, and a full wedding day timeline — we recommend our separate Notion Wedding Planner template, which is purpose-built for the full planning journey.
Can two co-parents use this for co-parenting coordination?
Yes. The Family Planning section, shared family calendar, household task tracker, and health & wellness modules all work well for co-parenting scenarios. Many co-parents use the Household Command Center for scheduling school pickups, extracurricular activities, and pediatric appointments. The Joint Finances section works for tracking shared child-related expenses like school fees, medical co-pays, and activity costs — with clear record-keeping both parties can reference.
